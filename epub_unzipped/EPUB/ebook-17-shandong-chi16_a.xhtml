Lao Shan 崂山
崂山

%0532

A quick ride 28km east from Qingdao, an arresting jumble of sun-bleached granite and hidden freshwater springs rises over the sea. It’s easy to understand why Lao Shan (Láo Shān) has attracted spiritual pilgrims throughout the centuries. These days it’s a great place to escape the city and recharge.
从青岛向东行驶 28 公里，海面上耸立着一片迷人的阳光漂白花岗岩和隐藏的淡水泉水。很容易理解为什么老山 （Láo Shān） 几个世纪以来一直吸引着精神朝圣者。如今，这里是逃离城市和充电的好地方。

In his quest for immortality, Emperor Qin Shi Huang ascended these slopes (with the help of a litter party of course), and in the 5th century Buddhist pilgrim Faxian landed here returning from India with a complete set of Buddhist scriptures. Lao Shan has its share of religious sites, but it is most steeped in Taoist tradition. Adepts of the Quanzhen sect, founded near Yantai in the 12th century, cultivated themselves in hermitages scattered all over the mountain.
为了追求长生不老，秦始皇登上了这些山坡（当然是在垃圾党的帮助下），在 5 世纪，佛教朝圣者法显带着一套完整的佛经从印度返回来到这里。老山有其宗教场所，但最有道教传统。12世纪在烟台附近创立的全真派的行家们在遍布山上的隐居中修炼。

Paths wind past ancient temples (and ruins), bubbling springs trickling into azure pools, and inscriptions left by Chinese poets (and German alpinists).

MAKING COPIES
制作复印件

For millennia, everything from imperial decrees to poetry, religious scriptures and maps were preserved by carving them into stone. This was done either as an inscription (yin-style) or a relief (yang-style). Copies were made by applying ink to the stone and pressing rice paper onto it, or by tamping a damp sheet of paper into the crevices and allowing it to dry, before patting ink onto the paper’s surface. Over time, even stone would wear and the clearest, best-made prints became works of art themselves. Unfortunately, this prompted unscrupulous collectors to damage carvings to ensure they had the very best copy. These are some of the gouges and scratches you see in many of the most prized tablets and stelae.
几千年来，从圣旨到诗歌、宗教经典和地图，一切都是通过雕刻在石头上保存下来的。这要么是铭文（阴式）还是浮雕（阳式）。复印件的方法是将墨水涂在石头上并将宣纸压在上面，或者将一张湿纸夯实到缝隙中并使其干燥，然后再将墨水拍在纸的表面上。随着时间的推移，即使是石头也会磨损，最清晰、制作最好的版画本身就变成了艺术品。不幸的是，这促使不道德的收藏家损坏雕刻品，以确保他们拥有最好的复制品。这些是您在许多最珍贵的石板和石碑中看到的一些凿痕和划痕。

History
历史

Starting life as a defence outpost and fishing village, Yantai’s name literally means ‘Smoke Terrace’: wolf-dung fires were lit on the headlands during the Ming dynasty to warn villagers of Japanese marauders. Yantai was thrust under the international spotlight in the late 19th century when the Qing government, reeling from defeat in the Opium War, signed over the city to the British and French, who established a treaty port here, when it was known as Chefoo (Zhifu). The rest of the eight-nation alliance followed with outposts, which remained until the province was captured by the Japanese in WWII. After the war, China kept Yantai’s ports (ice-free in winter) open for foreign trade.

DALIAN–YANTAI TUNNEL

Two decades in the making, the blueprints are almost done for China’s next epic infrastructure project: a ¥220 billion tunnel 100ft under the bottom of the Bo Sea that will trace Yantai’s coastline before curving north to Dalian in Liaoning province.
经过二十年的酝酿，中国下一个史诗般的基础设施项目的蓝图几乎已经完成：一条耗资 2200 亿日元的隧道，位于薄海海底 100 英尺处，该隧道将沿着烟台的海岸线延伸，然后向北弯曲到辽宁省的大连。

At 123km long, the Dalian–Yantai tunnel will be longer than the two previous recordholders combined – the Seikan Tunnel (between Honshu and Hokkaido in Japan) and the ‘Chunnel’ (between Britain and France). More importantly, it will cut a seven-hour boat ride or a 1400km road trip down to a 40-minute high-speed-train ride or a quick drive, linking China’s north and south like never before.
大连-烟台隧道全长 123 公里，将比之前两个纪录保持者——青函隧道（日本本州和北海道之间）和“春内尔”隧道（英国和法国之间）的总和还要长。更重要的是，它将把7小时的乘船或1400公里的公路旅行缩短到40分钟的高铁或快速驾驶，以前所未有的方式连接中国的南北。

The tunnel will actually be three – one channel each for cars, trains and maintenance – plus many more vertical ventilation pipes bored through the ocean floor. Building it will rely on engineering that hasn’t been tested before, and critics point out that the tunnel could disturb two active fault lines. Architects counter with the argument that it will all be accounted for in the plans. The tunnel was originally slated to open before 2020, but the go-ahead for construction has still yet to be met with final approval, and once started it will take an estimated 10 years to complete.
这条隧道实际上将是三条——各一条通道用于汽车、火车和维护——以及更多穿过海底的垂直通风管道。建造它将依赖于以前未经测试的工程，批评者指出，这条隧道可能会扰乱两条活动断层线。建筑师反驳说，这一切都将在计划中得到考虑。该隧道原定于 2020 年之前开通，但施工许可尚未获得最终批准，一旦开工，预计需要 10 年才能完工。

Penglai 蓬莱
Penglai 蓬莱

%0535 / Pop 450,000

The city of Penglai ( Pénglái) has long been connected with the Taoist legend of the Eight Immortals Crossing the Sea. Though the exact location of the legendary land of Penglai, where the Four Immortals were said to live, is disputed, Penglai City in Shandong (about 75km northwest of Yantai) enjoys an understandable connection to the legend, especially thanks to the 1000-year-old Penglai Pavilion, which sits on a mountain overlooking the Bo Sea.
蓬莱城（蓬莱）长期以来一直与道教传说中的八仙过海有关。尽管传说中四仙居住的蓬莱之地的确切位置存在争议，但山东蓬莱市（烟台西北约 75 公里）与传说有着可以理解的联系，尤其是这要归功于拥有 1000 年历史的蓬莱亭，它坐落在一座俯瞰博海的山上。

蓬莱阁旁边，但在其售票景区之外，是一片安静的沙滩 ，非常适合划桨。要到达那里，请左转离开蓬莱亭景区，然后左转沿钟楼北路下。

Penglai PavilionHISTORIC SITE
蓬莱阁历史遗址

(蓬莱阁, Pénglái Gé; ¥120; h7am-5pm; g4, 5 or 7)
（ 蓬莱阁 ，Pénglái Gé;120 日元; h 上午 7 点至下午 5 点; g 4、5 或 7）

About 75km northwest of Yantai, perched on a bluff overlooking the waves, the 1000-year-old Penglai Pavilion is closely entwined with Chinese mythology and the Taoist legend of the Eight Immortals Crossing the Sea. The entrance to the ticketed scenic area is at the south end, and the route up to the pavilion passes the grounds of an ancient naval base and a series of temples. The pavilion itself is unassuming as its restored exterior is rather similar to surrounding structures.
烟台西北约75公里处，坐落在俯瞰海浪的悬崖上，拥有千年历史的蓬莱阁与中国神话和道教传说的八仙过海紧密相连。售票景区的入口在南端，通往展馆的路线经过一座古老的海军基地和一系列寺庙。展馆本身并不起眼，因为它经过修复的外观与周围的建筑非常相似。

Inside is a collection of prized inscriptions left by famous visitors since the Song dynasty, and a beautiful modern rendering of the Eight Immortals by Zhou Jinyun. There are many versions of the story, but in this one the immortals, who came from different walks of life, shared drinks at the pavilion before crossing the Bo Sea using unique superpowers.
里面收藏了宋代以来著名游客留下的珍贵碑文，以及周锦云对八仙的精美现代渲染图。这个故事有很多版本，但在这个故事中，来自各行各业的神仙们在亭子里喝酒，然后使用独特的超能力渡过博海。

Close to the pavilion, and still within the ticketed scenic area, you can zip across the bay by cable car (索道, suǒ dào; one way/return ¥30/50; h8am-5.10pm) for cliffside walks overlooking the Bo and Yellow Seas. The park also contains museums (open 7.30am to 5.30pm) dedicated to ancient shipbuilding, regional relics and Qi Jiguang, a local-born Ming-dynasty general who battled pirates.
靠近展馆，仍在售票景区内，您可以乘坐缆车 （ 索道 ，suǒ dào;单程/往返 ¥30/50; h 上午 8 点至下午 5.10 点） 的悬崖边步道，俯瞰博海和黄海。公园内还设有博物馆 （上午 7：30 至下午 5：30 开放），专门展示古代造船、地区文物和当地出生的明代将军戚继光，曾与海盗作战。

If you arrive after a heavy rain, keep an eye out for mirages at sea that have appeared every few years. Long ago, this earned Penglai a reputation as a gateway to immortal lands and compelled Emperor Qin Shi Huang to send ships in search of islands of immortality further east.
如果您在大雨后抵达，请留意每隔几年出现一次的海上海市蜃楼。很久以前，这为蓬莱赢得了通往仙地门户的声誉，并迫使秦始皇派船前往更东的地方寻找长生不老的岛屿。
