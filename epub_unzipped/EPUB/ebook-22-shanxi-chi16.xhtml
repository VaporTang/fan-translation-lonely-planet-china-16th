Why Go?
为什么要去？

Waist-deep in handsome history, Shanxi (山西, Shānxī) is home to an impressive roll call of must-see, ancient sights. Most travellers start with the walled city of Pingyao. Basing yourself here and jumping to the town’s surrounding sights is practically all you need: you’ll encounter time-worn temples, sprawling Ming- or Qing-dynasty courtyard residences, some of the warmest people in the Middle Kingdom and the opportunity for a day trip to the dizzying mountain cliffs and gorges of Mian Shan.
山西（ 山西 ，Shānxī）拥有悠久的历史，拥有令人印象深刻的必看古迹。大多数旅行者的起点都是平遥城。在这里扎根并跳到小镇周围的景点几乎是您所需要的：您会遇到陈旧的寺庙、庞大的明代或清代四合院、中国一些最热情的人民，并有机会前往面山令人眼花缭乱的山崖和峡谷进行一日游。

Travellers shouldn’t overlook Datong, a forward-thinking city with a brand-new city wall and a great looking old town, but it’s the astonishing cave sculptures at Yungang, outside town, that give expression to the province’s other great source of magic: a rich vein of Silk Road Buddhist heritage, which splendidly litters the rolling mountain fastness of Wutai Shan with temples. Add the time-warp walled village, Guoyu, and the still-inhabited cave dwellings of Lijia Shan, and you could find yourself staying longer than planned.
旅行者不应忽视大同，这是一座具有前瞻性的城市，拥有全新的城墙和美丽的老城区，但城外云港令人惊叹的洞穴雕塑表达了该省的另一个伟大魔力来源：丝绸之路佛教遗产的丰富脉络，在五台山连绵起伏的山脉上绚丽地散布着寺庙。再加上时光扭曲的围墙村落 Guoyu 和 Lijia Shan 仍然有人居住的窑洞，您可能会发现自己停留的时间比计划的要长。

History
历史

Though home to the powerful state of Jin, which split into three in 403 BC, Shanxi really only rose to greatness with the Tuoba, a clan of the Xianbei people from Mongolia and Manchuria who made Datong their capital during the Northern Wei dynasty (AD 386–534). Eventually the Tuoba were assimilated, but as China weakened following the Tang collapse, the northern invaders returned; most notable were the Khitan (907–1125), whose western capital was also in Datong.
虽然山西是强大的晋国的所在地，晋国在公元前 403 年分裂为三个，但山西实际上是在拓跋氏的推动下崛起的，拓跋是来自蒙古和满洲的鲜卑氏族，在北魏（公元 386-534 年）以大同为首都。最终拓跋人被同化了，但随着中国在唐朝崩溃后衰弱，北方侵略者又回来了;最著名的是契丹（907-1125），其西都也在大同。

After the Ming regained control of northern China, Shanxi was developed as a defensive outpost, with an inner and outer Great Wall constructed along the northern boundaries. Local merchants took advantage of the increased stability to trade, eventually transforming the province into the country’s financial centre with the creation of China’s first banks in Pingyao.
明朝重新控制中国北方后，山西被发展为防御前哨，沿北界修建了内外长城。当地商人利用日益稳定的贸易机会，最终在平遥创建了中国第一家银行，将该省转变为该国的金融中心。

Today Shanxi is best known for its many mines; the province contains one-third of all China’s coal deposits and parts of it are heavily polluted, while Datong has experienced an astonishing, if controversial, facelift.
今天，山西以其众多的矿山而闻名;该省拥有中国三分之一的煤炭储量，其中部分煤炭污染严重，而大同则经历了惊人的整容，尽管有争议。

Language
语言

Jin is spoken by most Shanxi people. The main difference from Mandarin is its final glottal stop, but it also features complex grammar-induced tone shifts. Most locals also speak Mandarin.
大多数山西人都说金语。与普通话的主要区别在于它的最后声门塞音，但它也具有复杂的语法引起的声调变化。大多数当地人也说普通话。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

There are airports at Datong and Taiyuan, with flights to cities across China. Taiyuan and Pingyao are connected to Beijing and other cities by high-speed rail, while long-distance buses run to neighbouring provinces and further afield.
大同和太原都有机场，有飞往中国各地城市的航班。太原和平遥通过高铁与北京和其他城市相连，而长途巴士则开往邻近省份和更远的地方。

Modern railway lines and roads split Shanxi on a northeast–southwest axis, so getting between Datong, Taiyuan and Pingyao is no problem, with long-distance buses taking up the slack.
现代铁路线和公路将山西分成一条东北-西南轴线，因此往返于大同、太原和平遥之间没有问题，长途巴士填补了这一空缺。

Datong 大同

Datong (Dàtóng) today is fascinating, and charming to boot. Come night-time – with red lanterns swinging in the breeze and wind chimes tinkling on the illuminated city walls – the old town evokes Datong’s past glories as an ancient capital. No matter that most of this has been recreated from scratch: an estimated ¥50 billion has been ploughed into a colossal renovation of the old quarter. The city wall has been rebuilt in its entirety, enclosing a retinue of renovated (or newly built) sights. But it’s beyond the wall where Datong really comes into its own. The town is the gateway to the awe-inspiring Yungang Caves, one of China’s most outstanding Buddhist treasures, as well as a launch pad to the photogenic Hanging Monastery, the world’s oldest wooden pagoda, crumbling earthen sections of the Great Wall and onward trips to sacred Wutai Shan.
今天的大同（Dàtóng）令人着迷，也很迷人。到了晚上，红灯笼在微风中摇曳，风铃在灯火通明的城墙上叮当作响，老城区让人想起大同过去作为古都的辉煌。尽管其中大部分是从头开始重建的：估计已经投入了 500 亿日元对老城区进行大规模翻修。城墙已经全部重建，包围着一群经过翻修（或新建）的景点。但大同真正发挥作用的地方是墙外。该镇是通往令人敬畏的云冈洞的门户，云冈石窟是中国最杰出的佛教瑰宝之一，也是通往上镜的悬寺、世界上最古老的木塔、摇摇欲坠的长城土片以及前往神圣的五台山的出发点。

History
历史

Datong has long held a strategic position on the edge of the Mongolian grasslands, first rising to greatness as the capital of the Tuoba. A federation of Turkic-speaking nomads who united northern China (AD 386–534) and converted to Buddhism, the Tuoba were eventually assimilated into Chinese culture (like most other invaders). The Tuoba established the Northern Wei dynasty, whose greatest achievement here was the Yungang Caves, a collection of sublime 5th-century Buddhist carvings that capture a quiet, timeless and evocative beauty. The dynastic capital was later moved to Luoyang in Henan province and the rock carving continued at the astonishing Longmen Grottoes. During the Ming dynasty (1368–1644), the walls were rebuilt and 13 military garrisons were stationed here.
大同长期以来一直占据蒙古草原边缘的战略要地，首先作为拓跋的首都而崛起。拓跋人是联合中国北方（公元 386-534 年）并皈依佛教的突厥语游牧民族联盟，最终被中国文化同化（与大多数其他入侵者一样）。拓跋人建立了北魏王朝，北魏王朝在这里最大的成就是云冈石窟，这是一组崇高的 5 世纪佛教雕刻，捕捉到了一种安静、永恒和令人回味的美丽。王朝首都后来迁至河南省洛阳，石刻在令人惊叹的龙门石窟继续进行。明朝（1368-1644）时期，城墙被重建，13个军事驻军驻扎在这里。

Much of Datong’s old town has been recreated in an astonishingly ambitious program commenced by former city mayor Geng Yanbo, which aimed to restore its ancient grandeur and charm. Generally, the rebuild looks good, but it involved a huge and deliberate loss of the former city to recreate something better looking.
大同老城区的大部分地区都是在前市长耿彦波发起的一项雄心勃勃的计划中重建的，旨在恢复其古老的宏伟和魅力。总的来说，重建看起来不错，但它涉及对前城市的巨大和故意的损失，以重建一些更好看的东西。

Yungang Caves 云冈石窟

One of China’s most supreme examples of Buddhist cave art, the 5th-century Yungang Caves (Yúngāng Shíkū; %0352 302 6230; Dec-Feb ¥80, Mar-Nov ¥125; h8.30am-5.30pm 1 Apr–15 Oct, to 4.50pm 16 Oct–31 Mar) are magnificent. With 51,000 ancient statues and celestial beings, they put everything else in the Shanxi shade. Carved by the Turkic-speaking Tuoba, the Yungang Caves drew their designs from the Indian, Persian and Greek influences that swept along the Silk Road. Work began in AD 460, continuing for 60 years before all 252 caves, the oldest collection of Buddhist carvings in China, had been completed.
中国佛教石窟艺术最杰出的典范之一，5 世纪云冈石窟 （Yúngāng Shíkū; % 0352 302 6230;12-2 月 80 日元，3-11 月 125 日元; h 4 月 1 日至 10 月 15 日上午 8：30 至下午 5：30,10 月 16 日至 3 月 31 日下午 4：50） 非常壮观。他们拥有 51000 尊古代造像和天人，把其他的都放在山西的阴凉处。云冈石窟由讲突厥语的拓跋人雕刻而成，其设计灵感来自丝绸之路沿线席卷的印度、波斯和希腊的影响。工程始于公元 460 年，持续了 60 年，直到中国最古老的佛教雕刻收藏的 252 个洞穴全部完工。

Pass through the visitor centre and a recreated temple on a lake before arriving at the caves. You may find some caves shut for restoration and this is done on a rotational basis. Despite weathering, many of the statues at Yungang still retain their gorgeous pigment. The caves that are deeply recessed, in particular, have been well protected from the outside weather, although the penetration of water from above is a constant hazard.

Some 70km northeast of Datong, Zhenghong (正宏堡村长城, Zhènghóng Bǎo Cūn Cháng Chéng; Zhènghóngbǎo Village, 正宏堡村) is a sleepy farming village just off the highway, set within the ruins of a bǎo (fort) that once served the Great Wall. The fort was one of more than 70 dotting the strategic plains north of Datong. Keep going through the village and you’ll reach the Wall proper, which scythes eastwards through untamed canyon country. Opportunities for hiking and canyoning abound, but exercise caution and take water. No public transport, but a return taxi should cost ¥300.
正宏堡村长城（正宏堡村长城 ，Zhènghóng Bǎo Cūn Cháng Chéng;正宏堡村 ） 是一个沉睡的农村，位于高速公路旁，坐落在曾经服务于长城的堡垒废墟内。该堡垒是分布在大同北部战略平原上的 70 多个堡垒之一。继续穿过村庄，您将到达长城，它向东穿过未开化的峡谷国家。远足和峡谷漂流的机会比比皆是，但要小心并取水。没有公共交通工具，但往返出租车的费用为 300 日元。

A good place to see some raw sections of the Great Wall is Deshengbao (得胜堡), a 16th-century walled fort almost on the border with Inner Mongolia that is now a small farming village. The fort’s north and south gates are still standing, as are parts of its walls. Walk through the village (many of its houses are built out of Great Wall bricks) to the north gate and beyond it you’ll see wild wall – 10m-high sections of it. To get to Deshengbao, take a minibus to Fengzhen (丰镇; ¥17, one hour) from opposite Tónghé Dàfàndiàn, next to Datong train station. There are about 10 buses a day between 7.15am and 3.45pm. The bus will drop you at the turn-off for Deshengbao, from where it’s a 1km walk to the south gate. Heading back, return to the highway and flag down any Datong-bound bus.
得胜堡是观赏长城一些原始部分的好地方，这是一座 16 世纪的城墙堡垒，几乎与内蒙古接壤，现在是一个小农村。堡垒的南北门仍然矗立着，部分城墙也是如此。穿过村庄（许多房屋都是用长城砖建造的）到北门，再往北走，你会看到野墙——10 米高的部分。要前往德胜堡，请从大同火车站旁的 Tónghé Dàfàndiàn 对面乘坐小巴到丰镇 （17 日元，一小时）。早上 7.15 至下午 3.45 之间每天约有 10 辆巴士。巴士将在前往德胜堡的岔路口下车，从那里步行 1 公里即可到达南门。返回，返回高速公路并拦下任何开往大同的巴士。

Yong’an Temple
浑源永安寺

Yong’an Temple (Yǒng’ān Sì; 200m south of Heshun Beilu, 和顺北路南200米; ¥20; h8am-5pm) at the foothills of Heng Shan (恆山) only unlocks its halls when there are visitors and for a good reason – this poised and serene 13th-century temple is the home of some of the most precious Yuan dynasty coloured frescoes in China. You don’t have to be an expert to appreciate the 200m of paintings on the interior walls of the main hall: gorgeous, fragile and surprisingly vivid, quietly telling their religious and secular stories in the half-light.

The temple sells exquisitely printed scrolls of the frescoes with descriptions. Scroll booklets (in English or Chinese) are ¥50 each; and there’s a large deluxe box-set with several metres of scrolls that sells for over ¥9000.
寺庙出售印刷精美的带有描述的壁画卷轴。卷轴小册子（英文或中文）每本50日元;还有一套大型豪华套装，里面有几米长的卷轴，售价超过 9000 日元。

Buses depart for Hunyuan (浑源) from Datong’s main bus station (¥31, two hours). A cab ride from downtown Hunyuan to the temple costs ¥50.
巴士从大同的主要汽车站出发前往浑源 （31 日元，两小时）。从浑源市中心到寺庙的出租车费用为 50 日元。

Hanging Monastery 悬空寺
悬空寺

Built precariously into the side of a cliff, the Buddhist Hanging Monastery (Xuánkōng Sì; ¥115; h8am-7pm summer, 8.30am-5.30pm winter) is made all the more stunning by its long support stilts. The halls hug the contours of the cliff face, connected by rickety catwalks and narrow corridors, which can get very crowded in summer. It’s a sight to behold.
佛法悬空寺 （Xuánkōng Sì;¥115; h 夏季上午 8 点至晚上 7 点，冬季上午 8.30 至下午 5.30） 因其长支撑高跷而更加令人惊叹。大厅紧贴悬崖面的轮廓，由摇摇欲坠的走道和狭窄的走廊连接起来，在夏天会变得非常拥挤。这是一个值得一看的景象。

Combine your trip to the monastery with a visit to the riveting five-storey Yingxian Muta (应县木塔; Yingxian Xijie, 应县西街; ¥50; h7.30am-6.30pm summer, 8am-5.30pm winter), the world’s tallest (67m) and China’s oldest wooden pagoda. Constructed with red pine without a single nail, it’s often referred to as simply mùtǎ (the Wooden Pagoda). The clay Buddhist carvings it houses, including an 11m-high Sakyamuni on the 1st floor, are as old as the pagoda itself. Visitors can only climb to the first two levels, but there are photos of the higher floors to the side of the pagoda.
将您的寺院之旅与参观引人入胜的五层楼的应县木塔 ; 应县西街 ;¥50; h 夏季上午 7.30 至下午 6.30，冬季上午 8.30 至下午 5.30）， 世界上最高的（67 米）和中国最古老的木塔。它由红松建造而成，没有一根钉子，通常简称为 mùtǎ（木塔）。它收藏的泥土佛教雕刻，包括一楼 11 米高的释迦牟尼，与宝塔本身一样古老。游客只能爬到前两层，但宝塔一侧有较高楼层的照片。

Many buses departing for Hunyuan (浑源) from Datong’s main bus station (¥31, two hours) come to the monastery. If you want to go on to Yingxian Muta, there are frequent buses from Hunyuan (¥14, one hour), or shared taxis make the run from the monastery car park for ¥50 per person (when full). Hourly buses return to Datong until 6pm, or you can travel onto Taiyuan (¥90, 3½ hours, last bus 2.30pm).
许多从大同的主要汽车站（31 日元，两小时）出发前往浑源的巴士来到寺院。如果您想继续前往迎县木塔，有频繁的巴士从浑源出发（14 日元，一小时），或者从寺院停车场乘坐共享出租车，每人 50 日元（满员时）。每小时一班的巴士返回大同直至下午 6 点，或者您可以前往太原（90 日元，3 小时半，末班车下午 2.30）。

Wutai Shan 五台山

The mountainous, monastic enclave of Wutai Shan (Wǔtái Shān, Five Terrace Mountains) is Buddhism’s sacred northern range and the said to be earthly abode of Manjusri (文殊, Wénshū), the Bodhisattva of Wisdom. Chinese students sitting the ferociously competitive gāokǎo (university entrance) exams troop here for a nod from the learned Bodhisattva, proffering incense alongside saffron-robed monks and octogenarian pilgrims.
五台山（Wǔtái Sān，五台山）的山区寺院飞地是佛教的神圣北部山脉，据说是智慧菩萨文殊菩萨的尘世居所。 参加竞争激烈的大学入学考试的中国学生聚集在这里，向博学的菩萨点头致敬，与身穿藏红花长袍的僧侣和八十多岁的朝圣者一起上香。

A powerful sense of the divine holds sway in Wutai Shan, and the port-walled monasteries – the principal sources of spiritual power – find further amplification in the sublime mountain scenery.
五台山强大的神圣感占据着主导地位，而港口围墙的寺院——精神力量的主要来源——在壮丽的山景中得到了进一步的放大。

The forested slopes overlooking the town eventually give way to alpine meadows where you’ll find more temples and great hiking possibilities. Wutai Shan is also famed for its mysterious rainbows, which can appear without rain and are said to contain shimmering mirages of Buddhist beings, creatures and temple halls.
俯瞰城镇的森林山坡最终让位于高山草甸，在那里您会发现更多的寺庙和绝佳的徒步旅行机会。五台山还以其神秘的彩虹而闻名，彩虹可以在不下雨的情况下出现，据说包含佛教众生、生物和寺庙大厅的闪闪发光的海市蜃楼。

Climate
气候

Wutai Shan is at high altitude and powerful blizzards can sweep in as late as May and as early as September; check ahead to ensure the roads are passable. Winters are freezing with snow; summer months are the most pleasant, but always pack a jacket, as well as suitable shoes or boots for rain, as temperatures fall at night. If you are climbing up the peaks to see the sunrise, warm coats can be hired.
五台山地处高海拔，雪最晚5月，早9月即可席卷而来;提前检查以确保道路可通行。冬天积雪寒冷;夏季是最宜人的月份，但随着夜间气温下降，一定要带上夹克，以及合适的鞋子或靴子以防雨。如果您要爬上山峰看日出，可以租用保暖外套。

Enclosed within a lush valley between the five main peaks is an elongated, touristy town called Taihuai (台怀), but everyone simply calls it Wutai Shan. It’s here that you’ll find the largest concentration of temples, as well as all the area’s hotels and tourist facilities. An 18th-century complex of courtyards, Qiao Family Compound (乔家大院, Qiáojiā Dàyuàn; www.qjdywhyq.com; ¥138; h8am-7pm) is a fine example of a traditional private residence in China. Once home to a celebrated merchant, it’s an austere yet elegant maze of doorways and courtyards that lead onto a seemingly infinite number of rooms (over 300). The complex is famous as the set of director Zhang Yimou’s tragedy Raise the Red Lantern (大红灯笼高高挂; 1991) starring Gongli. If you have seen and enjoyed the film, a visit is a must.

Pingyao (Píngyáo) is China’s best-preserved ancient walled town. If you have any China mileage under your belt you’ll appreciate the town’s age-old charms. While some ‘ancient’ cities may rustle together an unconvincing display of old city walls, sporadic temples or the occasional ragged alley thrust beneath apartment blocks, Pingyao has managed to keep its beguiling narrative largely intact: red-lantern–hung lanes set against silhouettes of imposing town walls, ancient towers poking into the north China sky, and an entire brood of creaking temples. In recent years, shops on the main commercial streets have developed a penchant for disco lighting, light-box menus and noise-making costumed jesters. But outside these central areas, the ancient city is little changed.
平遥（平遥）是中国保存最完好的古城墙。如果您有任何中国里程，您一定会欣赏到这座小镇古老的魅力。虽然一些“古老”城市可能会沙沙作响，展示着古老的城墙、零星的寺庙或偶尔出现在公寓楼下的破烂小巷，但平遥却设法在很大程度上保持了其迷人的叙事完好无损：红灯笼悬挂的小巷与雄伟的城墙的轮廓相映成趣，古老的塔楼伸入华北的天空，以及一整群吱吱作响的寺庙。近年来，主要商业街上的商店对迪斯科灯光、灯箱菜单和制造噪音的盛装小丑产生了浓厚的兴趣。但在这些中心区域之外，古城几乎没有什么变化。

Pingyao is also a living and breathing community where the 30,000-odd locals who reside in the old town hang laundry in courtyards, career down alleyways on bicycles and sun themselves in doorways.

Pingyao’s most famous food is Pingyao beef (平遥牛肉, píngyáo niúròu), a reddish cured meat that tastes like corned beef. It’s eaten sliced as a cold dish, or in a stir-fry with potatoes or bell peppers. This supremely peaceful 550-year-old village, hugging a hillside with terraces of crops running up it, has hundreds of cave dwellings (窑洞, yáodòng) scaling 11 levels. Once home to more than 600 families, most with the family name Li, today’s population is much depleted at around 45. Some stone paths and stairways that twist up the hill date from Ming times; note the rings on some walls that horses were tied to and the ornate stone, brick and wood carvings.
这个拥有 550 年历史的极其宁静的村庄，紧邻山坡，山坡上长满了农作物，拥有数百座窑洞 （ 窑洞 ，yáodòng），高达 11 层。曾经有 600 多个家庭，其中大多数姓李氏，如今人口已大幅减少，约为 45 人。一些蜿蜒上山的石板路和楼梯可以追溯到明朝时代;请注意一些墙上拴着马匹的环以及华丽的石头、砖块和木雕。

Guoyu 郭峪古城
Guoyu 郭峪古城

The atmospheric walled village of Guoyu (Guōyù Gǔchéng) is the highlight of a trip to this part of Shanxi. Although there’s now an entrance fee (¥35), there’s little tourist paraphernalia; just the genuine charm of a historic and still inhabited Ming dynasty settlement. The crumbling remains of this one-time fort’s south gate and some of its old walls still stand sentry at the entrance to the village close to the road. Walk 200m and it’s as if you’ve stepped back in time. Narrow alleys and stone streets run past courtyard houses, where the locals sit and chatter in their native dialect.
大气的围墙村果榆（Guōyù Gǔchéng）是山西这一地区之旅的亮点。虽然现在有入场费（35 日元），但旅游用具很少;只是一个历史悠久且仍然有人居住的明朝定居点的真正魅力。这座曾经的堡垒南门的摇摇欲坠的遗迹和一些旧城墙仍然矗立在靠近道路的村庄入口处。步行 200 米，仿佛时光倒流。狭窄的小巷和石头街道穿过四合院，当地人坐在那里用他们的母语喋喋不休。

Guoyu’s oldest building is Tāngdì Miào (汤帝庙), a 600-year-old Taoist temple. It’s well preserved with nine rooms and an operatic stage; the latter has Cultural Revolution–era paintings with slogans exhorting the locals to work harder. In the room on the left of the main worship hall (where you’ll find a few disconcertingly life-like statues of deities), there are also Communist propaganda slogans on the walls. The temple was a government building during the Cultural Revolution. It’s also worth looking inside the former courtyard residence of Minister Chen’s grandfather at 1 Jingyang Beilu (景阳北路1号). Admission is included with the village entrance.
国玉最古老的建筑是汤帝庙 ，一座拥有 600 年历史的道教寺庙。它保存完好，有九个房间和一个歌剧舞台;后者有文化大革命时期的画作，标语劝告当地人更加努力地工作。在主礼拜堂左侧的房间里（你会发现一些栩栩如生的神像），墙上还挂着共产主义的宣传标语。该寺庙是文化大革命期间的政府大楼。陈部长祖父位于景阳北路 1 号的旧四合院住宅也值得一看。入场费包含在村庄入口处。

To get here, catch one of the frequent buses headed to Prime Minister Chen’s Castle (¥15, 1½ hours, 6.30am to 6pm) from Jincheng’s East Station. Guoyu is a 10-minute walk south of the castle. Return transport is scarce, so it’s best to take a minibus to the small town of Beiliu (北留; ¥5, 15 minutes), then catch an ordinary bus back to Jincheng (¥12).
要到达这里，请从晋城东站乘坐前往陈丞相府的频繁巴士（15 日元，1 小时半，早上 6：30 至下午 6 点）。果峪位于城堡以南步行 10 分钟处。回程交通稀缺，最好乘坐小巴到北留（ 北留 ，5 日元，15 分钟），然后乘坐普通巴士返回晋城（12 日元）。

This well-preserved, magnificent Ming dynasty castle is the former residence of Chen Tingjing, prime minister under Emperor Kangxi in the late 17th century, and coauthor of China’s most famous dictionary. The Chen family rose to prominence as senior officials in the 16th century and the castle walls were originally constructed to keep revolting peasants out. While it may have some tourist trappings today, it remains a riveting maze of battlements, courtyards, gardens and stone archways.
这座保存完好、宏伟的明代城堡是 17 世纪末康熙皇帝时期的宰相陈廷敬的故居，也是中国最著名的词典的合著者。陈氏家族在 16 世纪作为高级官员而声名鹊起，城墙最初是为了将起义的农民拒之门外而建造的。虽然今天它可能有一些旅游景点，但它仍然是一个由城垛、庭院、花园和石拱门组成的引人入胜的迷宫。
