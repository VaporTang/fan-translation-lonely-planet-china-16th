Why Go?
为什么要去？

Known as the ‘Vegas of China’, Macau is indeed an epicentre of gambling and glitz. While luxury entertainment here is world-class, the city has much more to offer than that. Macau was a Portuguese colony for 300 years, a heritage marked by a wonderful cultural hybridity that manifests itself in all aspects of life: Chinese temples stand on maritime-themed Portuguese tiles; the sound of Cantonese permeates streets with Portuguese names; and when you’re hungry, it could be Chinese dim sum, pastéis de nata (Portuguese egg tarts) or Macanese minchi (ground meat stir-fried with potatoes) that come to the rescue.
澳门被誉为“中国的拉斯维加斯”，确实是赌博和浮华的中心。虽然这里的豪华娱乐活动是世界一流的，但这座城市提供的远不止于此。澳门作为葡萄牙殖民地已有 300 年的历史，其文化融合体现在生活的方方面面：中国寺庙矗立在以海洋为主题的葡萄牙瓷砖上;粤语的声音弥漫在带有葡萄牙名字的街道上;当你饿了时，中式点心、pastéis de nata（葡萄牙蛋挞）或土生葡人 minchi（碎肉和土豆炒）可以来拯救你。

Macau Peninsula holds the Unesco World Heritage–crowned old city centre. Further south are the former islands of Taipa and Coloane, joined together by a strip of reclaimed land named Cotai. Taipa has lovely Macanese houses and quaint boutiques; Cotai hosts the new megacasinos; while Coloane is a laid-back village with shipyards and beaches.
澳门半岛拥有被联合国教科文组织列为世界遗产的旧城中心。再往南是前氹仔岛和路环岛，由一条名为路氹城的填海地连接在一起。氹仔有可爱的土生葡人房屋和古色古香的精品店;路氹城拥有新的大型赌场;而路环是一个悠闲的村庄，拥有造船厂和海滩。

History
历史

Portuguese galleons first visited southern China to trade in the early 16th century, and in 1557, as a reward for clearing out pirates, they obtained a leasehold for Macau. The first Portuguese governor of Macau was appointed in 1680, and as trade with China grew, so did Macau. However, after the Opium Wars between the Chinese and the British, and the subsequent establishment of Hong Kong, Macau went into a long decline.
葡萄牙大帆船于16世纪初首次到中国南方进行贸易，1557年，作为清除海盗的奖励，他们获得了澳门的租赁权。1680 年任命了第一位葡萄牙澳门总督，随着与中国贸易的增长，澳门也随之增长。然而，在中英鸦片战争以及随后的香港建立之后，澳门进入了长期的衰落。

In 1999, under the Sino–Portuguese Joint Declaration, Macau was returned to China and designated as a Special Administrative Region (SAR). Like Hong Kong, the pact ensures Macau a ‘high degree of autonomy’ in all matters (except defence and foreign affairs) for 50 years. The handover, however, did not change Macau socially and economically as much as the termination of the gambling monopoly in 2001. Casinos mushroomed, redefining the city’s skyline, and tourists from mainland China surged, fattening up the city’s coffers.
1999 年，根据《中葡联合声明》，澳门归还中国并被指定为特别行政区（SAR）。与香港一样，该协议确保澳门在所有事务（国防和外交事务除外）上拥有 50 年的“高度自治权”。然而，回归并没有像 2001 年结束赌博垄断那样改变澳门的社会和经济。赌场如雨后春笋般涌现，重新定义了这座城市的天际线，来自中国大陆的游客激增，使这座城市的金库变得肥大。

Yet the revenue boost, coupled with government policies (or the lack thereof), also led to income inequality and a labour shortage. Macau residents are also increasingly critical of their chief executive’s pro-Beijing stance.
然而，收入增长，加上政府政策（或缺乏政策），也导致了收入不平等和劳动力短缺。澳门居民也越来越批评行政长官的亲北京立场。

The government tightened its anti-moneylaundering laws in 2017, which will subject casinos to stricter controls and regulations from 2020.
政府在 2017 年收紧了反洗钱法，从 2020 年起，赌场将受到更严格的控制和监管。

Language
语言

Cantonese and Portuguese are the official languages of Macau, though not many people actually speak Portuguese. English and Mandarin are reasonably well understood, though the former is harder to find here than in Hong Kong.
粤语和葡萄牙语是澳门的官方语言，但真正说葡萄牙语的人并不多。英语和普通话相当容易理解，尽管前者在这里比在香港更难找到。

You’ll find the lion’s share of Macau’s museums, churches, gardens and interesting architecture on the peninsula. In 2005 Unesco recognised this wealth by adding the Historic Centre of Macau, comprising eight squares and 22 historic buildings, to its World Heritage list. oRuins of the Church of St PaulRUINS
o 圣保罗教堂遗址 RUINS

(大三巴牌坊, Ruinas de Igreja de São Paulo; map Google map; Calçada de São Paulo; g8A, 17, 26, disembark at Luís de Camões Garden) F
（ 大三巴牌坊 ， Ruinas de Igreja de São Paulo; 地图谷歌地图 ;圣保罗卡尔萨达; g 8A、17、26，在 Luís de Camões 花园下船） F

The most treasured icon in Macau, the towering facade and stairway are all that remain of this early-17th-century Jesuit church. With its statues, portals and engravings that effectively make up a ‘sermon in stone’ and a Biblia pauperum (Bible of the poor), the church was one of the greatest monuments to Christianity in Asia, intended to help the illiterate understand the Passion of Christ and the lives of the saints.
作为澳门最珍贵的标志，高耸的外墙和楼梯是这座 17 世纪早期耶稣会教堂的全部遗迹。教堂的雕像、门户和版画有效地构成了“石头布道”和 Biblia pauperum（穷人的圣经），是亚洲最伟大的基督教纪念碑之一，旨在帮助文盲了解基督的受难和圣徒的生活。

The church was designed by an Italian Jesuit and built by early Japanese Christian exiles and Chinese craftsmen between 1602 and 1640. It was abandoned after the expulsion of the Jesuits in 1762 and a military battalion was stationed here. In 1835 a fire erupted in the kitchen of the barracks, destroying everything, except what you see today. At the top is a dove, representing the Holy Spirit, surrounded by stone carvings of the sun, moon and stars. Beneath the Holy Spirit is a statue of the infant Jesus, and around it, stone carvings of the implements of the Crucifixion (the whip, crown of thorns, nails, ladder and spear). In the centre of the third tier stands the Virgin Mary being assumed bodily into heaven along with angels and two flowers: the peony, representing China, and the chrysanthemum, representing Japan. To the right of the Virgin is a carving of the tree of life and the apocalyptic woman (Mary) slaying a seven-headed hydra; the Japanese kanji next to her read: ‘The holy mother tramples the heads of the dragon’. To the left of the central statue of Mary, a ‘star’ guides a ship (the Church) through a storm (sin); a carving of the devil is to the left. The fourth tier has statues of four Jesuit doctors of the church: (from left) Blessed Francisco de Borja; St Ignatius Loyola, the founder of the order; St Francis Xavier, the apostle of the Far East; and Blessed Luís Gonzaga.
该教堂由一位意大利耶稣会士设计，由早期日本基督教流亡者和中国工匠于 1602 年至 1640 年间建造。1762年耶稣会士被驱逐后，它被废弃，一个军事营驻扎在这里。1835 年，军营的厨房发生了一场大火，烧毁了一切，除了你今天看到的。顶部是一只代表圣灵的鸽子，周围环绕着日月星辰的石雕。圣灵下面是婴儿耶稣的雕像，周围是钉十字架工具（鞭子、荆棘冠冕、钉子、梯子和长矛）的石雕。在第三层的中央，圣母玛利亚与天使和两朵花一起被带入天堂：代表中国的牡丹和代表日本的菊花。圣母的右边是生命树的雕刻，世界末日的女人（玛丽）杀死了一只七头九头蛇;她旁边的日语汉字写着：“圣母践踏龙头”。在中央玛利亚雕像的左侧，一颗“星星”引导一艘船（教会）穿过风暴（罪恶）;左边是魔鬼的雕刻。第四层有教堂四位耶稣会医生的雕像：（左起）真福弗朗西斯科·德·博尔哈;圣依纳爵·洛约拉，该骑士团的创始人;远东使徒圣方济各泽维尔;和真福路易斯·冈萨加。

(鄭家大屋, Caso do Mandarim; map Google map; %853 2896 8820; www.wh.mo/mandarinhouse; 10 Travessa de António da Silva; h10am-5.30pm Thu-Tue; g18, 28B) F

Built around 1869, the Mandarin’s House, with over 60 rooms, was the ancestral home of Zheng Guanying, an influential author-merchant whose readers included emperors, Dr Sun Yatsen and Chairman Mao. The compound features a moon gate, tranquil courtyards, exquisite rooms and a main hall with French windows, all arranged in that labyrinthine-style typical of certain Chinese period buildings. There are guided tours in Cantonese on weekends.
郑官府建于 1869 年左右，拥有 60 多个房间，是郑冠英的祖居，郑冠英是一位颇具影响力的作家兼商人，其读者包括皇帝孙中山博士和毛主席。该大院设有月亮门、宁静的庭院、精致的房间和带落地窗的主厅，所有这些都布置在中国某些时期建筑典型的迷宫式风格中。周末有粤语导览。

oChurch of St JosephCHURCH
o 圣约瑟夫教堂 CHURCH

(聖若瑟聖堂, Capela do Seminario São José; map Google map; Rua do Seminário; h9am-6pm; g9, 16, 18, 28B)
（ 圣若瑟圣堂 ，Capela do Seminario São José; 地图谷歌地图 ;塞米纳里奥街; h 上午 9 点至下午 6 点; g 9、16、18、28B）

St Joseph’s, which falls outside the tourist circuit, is Macau’s most beautiful model of tropicalised baroque architecture. Consecrated in 1758 as part of the Jesuit seminary, it features a scalloped canopy and a staircase leading to the courtyard from which you see the arresting white-and-yellow facade of the church and its dome. The latter is the oldest dome ever built in China. The interior, with its three altars, is lavishly ornamented with overlapping pilasters and attractive Solomonic ‘spiral’ columns.
圣若瑟大教堂位于旅游线之外，是澳门最美丽的热带巴洛克建筑典范。它于 1758 年作为耶稣会神学院的一部分落成，拥有扇形天篷和通往庭院的楼梯，从那里您可以看到教堂及其圆顶引人注目的白黄外墙。后者是中国有史以来建造的最古老的圆顶。内部有三个祭坛，装饰着重叠的壁柱和迷人的所罗门式“螺旋”柱。

This charming building, founded in the 19th century, was the country retreat of the late tycoon Robert Ho Tung, who purchased it in 1918. The islands of Taipa and Coloane are one big island these days. Land reclamation has created a connecting strip known as Cotai (conflation of ‘Coloane’ and ‘Taipa’). Head to Taipa for dining and shopping in the narrow streets of old Taipa Village. Visit Cotai to gamble and gawk at the biggest casinos on earth. Go south to Coloane for beachy getaways and long Portuguese lunches.
如今，氹仔岛和路环岛是一个大岛。填海造就了一条称为路氹城的连接带（“路环”和“氹仔”的合并）。前往氹仔老氹仔旧城区的狭窄街道上用餐和购物。前往路氹城，在地球上最大的赌场赌博和凝视。向南前往路环，享受海滩度假和丰盛的葡萄牙午餐。

Chopsticks in the Sea
海里的筷子

Stilt houses and shipyards (or skeletons of them) dot coastal Rua dos Navegantes. These huts of colourful corrugated metal, extending like chunky chopsticks into the harbour, were once landing spots for houseboats. Dried seafood shops occupy some of them, such as Tong Kei Dried Fish at Largo do Cais, the square near the lovely old Coloane pier (Ponte Cais de Coloane).
高脚屋和造船厂（或它们的骨架）点缀在沿海的 Rua dos Navegantes。这些五颜六色的波纹金属小屋像粗筷子一样伸向港口，曾经是船屋的登陆点。海鲜干店占据了其中一些，例如位于 Largo do Cais 的 Tong Kei Dried Fish，这是可爱的旧路环码头 （Ponte Cais de Coloane） 附近的广场。

Lai Chi Vun Village
荔枝云村

A slope on the right of the Servicos de Alfangega building at the square connects to Lai Chi Vun Village (荔枝碗村, ‘Lychee Bowl Village’), an area synonymous with Macau’s old shipbuilding industry. The village has sparked much recent debate among conservation advocates who believe the shipyards should be kept and restored, developers who think otherwise, and the government who agreed to stop tearing down the structures. You can see the cavernous cadavers of a dozen shipyards lined up on the left side of the road. Their gates are often open during the day and if you like atmospheric abandoned spaces, you can enter for a peek and to take pictures, but beware of dogs.
广场上 Servicos de Alfangega 大楼右侧的斜坡与荔枝碗村相连，荔枝碗村是澳门老造船业的代名词。该村最近引发了保护倡导者、认为应该保留和修复造船厂的开发商以及同意停止拆除这些建筑的政府之间的许多争论。你可以看到十几家造船厂的海绵状尸体排列在路的左边。他们的大门通常在白天打开，如果你喜欢大气的废弃空间，你可以进去偷看和拍照，但要小心狗。

Shipbuilding in Macau
澳门造船业

Shipbuilding, the crafting of wooden fishing junks and dragon boats, as well as their demolition, was one of Macau’s oldest and most important industries. The city was a port for four centuries, one built by seafarers and fisherfolk. At its heyday in the 1940s and ‘50s, Macau’s shipbuilders’ skills were much sought after and there were close to 10,000 fishermen and over 30 shipyards in the city, mostly in the coastal villages. But the industry began declining from the 1970s, through the ‘80s and ‘90s, to the early 2000s when the last vessels were made. This was due to various factors – the market shifting to mainland China where production costs were cheaper, the popularity of metal boats, which were less time-consuming to make, and water pollution in the Pearl River Delta.
造船、木制渔船和龙舟的制作以及拆除，是澳门最古老、最重要的产业之一。这座城市在四个世纪的时间里一直是一个港口，由海员和渔民建造。在1940年代和50年代的鼎盛时期，澳门的造船工人技能备受追捧，该市有近10,000名渔民和30多家造船厂，其中大部分位于沿海村庄。但从 1970 年代开始衰落，到 80 年代和 90 年代，再到 2000 年代初，最后一批船只被制造出来。这是由于多种因素造成的，包括市场转移到生产成本较低的中国大陆、制造时间较少的金属船的流行以及珠江三角洲的水污染。

Conservation & Repurposing
保护和再利用

The Macau government bulldozed two shipyards in the 2010s, then halted due to a public outcry. There’s been much talk of revitalising the shipyards into a museum, inns and boutiques, and everything in between. At the time of writing, the site is awaiting heritage classification. It is expected that at least some of the shipyards will be restored and revitalised. In the meantime, the docks remain forlorn witnesses to an important slice of Macau’s modern history.
澳门政府在 2010 年代推平了两家造船厂，然后因公众强烈抗议而停止。人们一直在谈论将造船厂振兴为博物馆、旅馆和精品店，以及介于两者之间的一切。在撰写本文时，该地点正在等待遗产分类。预计至少部分造船厂将得到修复和振兴。与此同时，码头仍然是澳门现代历史重要片段的凄凉见证。

Macau’s gambling industry goes back to the 16th century when migrant labourers from China would gather to play a game called ‘fan tan’ in makeshift stalls on the streets and alleys near the harbour. But it wasn’t until the 19th century when neighbour Hong Kong surpassed the port of Macau in importance that the government legalised gambling to boost revenue, and the city gained a reputation for being a gambling centre.
澳门的赌博业可以追溯到 16 世纪，当时来自中国的农民工聚集在海港附近的大街小巷的临时摊位上玩一种名为“fan tan”的游戏。但直到 19 世纪，邻国香港的重要性超过了澳门港口，政府才将赌博合法化以增加收入，这座城市赢得了赌博中心的声誉。

Rise of the Megacasinos
超级赌场的崛起

Since the early 2000s, megacasinos boasting celebrity-chef restaurants, top-tier fashion boutiques and rooms with excessive luxuries have been sprouting in the city, especially in the Cotai area. The change began when casino mogul Stanley Ho’s monopoly ended in 2002 and Las Vegas operators set up shop in competition. The trend peaked in 2014 when Macau’s gaming revenue surpassed all of the world’s major gambling jurisdictions combined.
自 2000 年代初以来，拥有名厨餐厅、顶级时尚精品店和过度奢华客房的大型赌场在该市如雨后春笋般涌现，尤其是在路氹城地区。当赌场大亨何鸿燊的垄断于 2002 年结束，拉斯维加斯运营商在竞争中开设商店时，这种变化就开始了。这一趋势在 2014 年达到顶峰，当时澳门的博彩收入超过了全球所有主要博彩司法管辖区的总和。

However, at around the same time, the industry suffered a blow from Beijing’s anticorruption crackdown. The move targeted the ultra-wealthy, many of whom happened to be VIPs at Macau’s casinos. Gaming revenue plummeted for two years, but in August 2016 things started looking up again and in 2018 Macau’s gaming revenue more than tripled that of Las Vegas.
然而，大约在同一时间，该行业遭受了北京反腐打击的打击。此举针对的是超级富豪，其中许多人恰好是澳门赌场的贵宾。博彩收入连续两年暴跌，但2016年8月情况再次好转，2018年澳门的博彩收入是拉斯维加斯的两倍多。

There are currently more than 40 casinos in Macau, with more in the pipeline. Macau Peninsula has most of the older, smaller casinos, while the Cotai area is home to the new breed of casino-hotel-shopping-entertainment behemoths.
目前澳门有 40 多家赌场，还有更多赌场正在筹备中。澳门半岛拥有大多数较旧、规模较小的赌场，而路氹城地区则是新型赌场-酒店-购物-娱乐巨头的所在地。

Indoor Smoking Ban
室内禁烟令

A welcome change for many, and a very visible one too, is that Macau’s casinos are no longer the smoke-filled halls they once were where players had to squint to see their cards. Smoking is now prohibited in all indoor areas in Macau, and while casinos (and the airport) are allowed to have smoking lounges, the law now requires new lounges of a higher standard to better protect casino employees from secondhand smoke.
对许多人来说，一个可喜的变化，也是一个非常明显的变化，那就是澳门的赌场不再是曾经那个烟雾缭绕的大厅，玩家必须眯着眼睛才能看到他们的牌。澳门所有室内区域现在都禁止吸烟，虽然赌场（和机场）允许设有吸烟室，但法律现在要求新建更高标准的休息室，以更好地保护赌场员工免受二手烟的侵害。

Casino Culture
赌场文化

Table games are the staple at casinos here – mostly baccarat, then roulette and a dice game called dai sai (big small). You’ll hardly hear any whooping and clunking – slot machines make up only 5% of total casino winnings (versus Vegas’ 60%). Drunks are also hard to come by, as Chinese players believe that booze dulls their skill. Over 80% of gamblers and 95% of high rollers come from mainland China. The latter play inside members-only rooms where the total amount wagered on any given day can exceed a small country’s GDP.
桌面游戏是这里赌场的主要内容——主要是百家乐，然后是轮盘赌和一种名为 dai sai（大）的骰子游戏。您几乎听不到任何呜呜声和叮当声——老虎机仅占赌场总奖金的 5%（而拉斯维加斯为 60%）。醉酒者也很难找到，因为中国球员认为酒精会削弱他们的技术。超过 80%的赌徒和 95%的豪赌客来自中国大陆。后者在会员专用房间内进行游戏，任何一天的投注总额可能超过一个小国家的 GDP。

For recreational players, the only thing to watch out for is harassment by tip hustlers – scam artists who hang around tables acting like your new best friend. They may steal your chips, nag you for a cut or try to take you to a casino that will tip them for bringing clients.
对于休闲玩家来说，唯一需要提防的是小费骗子的骚扰——骗子在桌子周围闲逛，表现得像你最好的新朋友。他们可能会偷你的筹码，唠叨你分一杯羹，或者试图带你去赌场，因为他们会给他们带来客户的小费。

BUS
总线

Macau is an easy gateway by land into mainland China. Simply take bus 3A, 27 or AP1 (MOP$6) to the Border Gate (關閘, Portas do Cerco; 6am to 1am) and walk across. A second – and much less busy crossing – is the Cotai Frontier Post (路氹邊檢大樓; 24 hours) on the causeway linking Taipa and Coloane, which allows visitors to cross the Lotus Flower Bridge by shuttle bus (MOP$5) to Hengqin in Zhuhai. Buses 15 (MOP$6, every 30 to 35 minutes, 6am to 8pm) and 50 (MOP$6, every 12 to 26 minutes, 7.30am to 10.15pm) will drop you off at the crossing.
澳门是陆路进入中国大陆的便捷门户。只需乘坐 3A、27 或 AP1 路（澳门币 6 元）到关闸（关闸 ，Portas do Cerco，早上 6 点至凌晨 1 点），然后步行穿过。第二个不那么繁忙的过境点是路氹边检大楼 ，位于连接氹仔和路环的堤道上，游客可以乘坐穿梭巴士（澳门币 5 元）穿过莲花桥前往珠海横琴。15 路巴士（澳门币 6 元，每 30 至 35 分钟一班，上午 6 时至晚上 8 时）及 50 路巴士（澳门币 6 元，每 12 至 26 分钟一班，上午 7 时 30 分至晚上 10 时 15 分）会在路口下车。

If you want to travel further afield in China, buses run by Kee Kwan Motor Road Co (歧關車路有限公司; %853 2893 3888; h9am-9.30pm) leave the bus station at the Border Gate. Buses for different locations in Guangzhou (about MOP$90, three hours) depart every 30 minutes or so. There are also a number of buses to Zhongshan (MOP$50, one hour).
如果您想在中国更远的地方旅行，可以乘坐由 Kee Kwan Motor Road Co（ 歧关车路有限公司 % 853 2893 3888; h 上午 9 点至晚上 9 点 30 分） 在边境口岸离开巴士站。广州市不同地点的巴士（约澳门币 90 元，三小时）每 30 分钟左右一班。还有多辆前往中山的巴士（澳门币 50 元，一小时）。

In 2018 the long-anticipated Hong Kong–Zhuhai–Macau Bridge opened to traffic, reducing transit times and costs for getting around the Pearl River Delta. A 24-hour licensed shuttle-bus service (HK$65 to HK$70, every five to 15 minutes, 6am to midnight and every 15 to 30 minutes overnight) operates daily from Macau Port to Hong Kong Port, where you can pick up local buses to the airport and city.
