Why Go?
为什么要去？

Unfurling up to Russia in the north, Heilongjiang (黑龙江, Hēilóngjiāng), meaning ‘Black Dragon River’, is one of China’s most beautifully rugged provinces. Forests, lakes, mountains and the dormant volcanoes of Wudalian Chi beckon well beyond the capital Harbin (Hā’ěrbīn), an architecturally diverse city with a distinctly cosmopolitan feel.
黑龙江（黑龙江 ，Hēilóngjiāng），意为“黑龙江”，北至俄罗斯，是中国最美丽崎岖的省份之一。森林、湖泊、山脉和五大连池的休眠火山远远超出了首都哈尔滨（哈尔滨），这是一座建筑多元化的城市，具有明显的国际化感觉。

Of course, it gets cold – sub-Arctic cold – in China’s northernmost province, but Harbin is a winter playground, hosting a world-renowned ice-sculpture festival and a brand-new indoor ski centre. The province also boasts some of China’s most established ski slopes, which are increasingly popular as the country prepares for the Winter Olympics in 2022.
当然，在中国最北端的省份，天气会很冷——亚北极的寒冷，但哈尔滨是一个冬季游乐场，举办着世界著名的冰雕节和一个全新的室内滑雪中心。该省还拥有中国一些最成熟的滑雪场，随着该国为 2022 年冬奥会做准备，这些滑雪场越来越受欢迎。

From Mohe, China’s most northerly city, you can access the remote Beijicun and Beihongcun for bragging rights to say you have stood at the very top of the Middle Kingdom.
从中国最北端的城市漠河出发，您可以到达偏远的北集村和北红村，吹嘘自己站在了中国的最顶端。

History
历史

Heilongjiang forms the northernmost part of Dongbei, the region formerly known as Manchuria. Its proximity to Russia has long meant strong historical and trade links with its northern neighbour. In the mid-19th century, Russia annexed parts of Heilongjiang, while in 1897 Russian workers arrived to build a railway line linking Vladivostok with Harbin. By the 1920s well over 100,000 Russians resided in Harbin alone.
黑龙江是东北的最北端，该地区以前被称为满洲。长期以来，它靠近俄罗斯，这意味着与其北部邻国有着牢固的历史和贸易联系。19世纪中叶，俄国吞并了黑龙江部分地区，1897年俄国工人抵达，修建了一条连接符拉迪沃斯托克和哈尔滨的铁路线。到 1920 年代，仅哈尔滨就有超过 100,000 名俄罗斯人居住。

Heilongjiang was occupied by the Japanese between 1931 and 1945. After the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) took power in 1949, relations with Russia grew steadily frostier, culminating in a brief border war in 1969. Sino-Russian ties have much improved in recent years and the two sides finally settled on the border in July 2008, after 40-odd years of negotiation.
黑龙江在 1931 年至 1945 年间被日本占领。1949 年中国共产党 （CCP） 掌权后，与俄罗斯的关系逐渐冷淡，最终在 1969 年爆发了一场短暂的边境战争。近年来，中俄关系有了很大改善，经过 40 多年的谈判，双方终于在 2008 年 7 月就边界问题达成了协议。

In 2016 China relaxed its one-child policy further in Heilongjiang, allowing overseas returnees to have up to three children in this ageing province.
2016年，中国进一步放宽了黑龙江省的独生子女政策，允许海外归国者在这个老龄化的省份生育最多三个孩子。

Climate
气候

The region experiences long, freezing winters, with temperatures dropping below -30°C. Short summers are warm and humid, especially in the south and east. Temperatures in the mid- to high 30°Cs are possible and afternoon showers are common.
该地区经历漫长而寒冷的冬季，气温降至 -30°C 以下。 短暂的夏季温暖潮湿，尤其是在南部和东部。气温可能在 30°C 中高，下午阵雨很常见。

Home to the wondrous Harbin Ice & Snow Festival, and the world’s largest indoor ski facility, the capital of Heilongjiang (Hā’ěrbīn), located on the Songhua River, is a stylish city and a highlight of any trip through Dongbei.
黑龙江省首府黑龙江省会（哈尔滨）是奇妙的哈尔滨冰雪节的举办地，也是世界上最大的室内滑雪设施，位于松花江畔，是一座时尚的城市，也是东北之旅的亮点。

哈尔滨的俄罗斯和犹太遗产最好在夏季在蓝天下漫步，但冬天才是哈尔滨真正炎热的时候，因为游客们会穿上地毯去参观一个完全由冰组成的霓虹灯覆盖的零度以下主题公园。

At its peak in the 1920s and ’30s, Harbin was home to 120,000 Russians and roughly 50 synagogues and churches.
在 1920 年代和 30 年代的鼎盛时期，哈尔滨拥有 120,000 名俄罗斯人和大约 50 座犹太教堂和教堂。

Although much has since been lost to development (and the Red Guards in the 1960s), the legacy of concession-era Harbin can be still be felt, particularly along the cobbles of Zhongyang Dajie map Google map (中央大街, Central Street, Walking Street) F. A pedestrian-only zone bustling with day-trippers, the avenue and parts of its surrounding streets are lined with former department stores, restaurants and cinemas dating back to the early 20th century. Some are imposing, others distinctly dilapidated, but the mix of architectural styles, from art nouveau to eclecticism and baroque, is fascinating.
尽管此后发展（以及 1960 年代的红卫兵）失去了很多东西，但租界时代哈尔滨的遗产仍然可以感受到，特别是沿着中阳大街的鹅卵石地图 Google map（ 中央大街 ，中央大街，步行街） F .这条大道及其周围部分街道是步行区，挤满了一日游游客，两旁林立着可追溯到 20 世纪初的前百货公司、餐馆和电影院。有些气势磅礴，有些则明显破旧不堪，但从新艺术运动到折衷主义和巴洛克风格的建筑风格的混合令人着迷。

The two streets running parallel to the east, Shangzhi Dajie and Zhaolin Jie, are also home to a dwindling number of old buildings, including the magnificent Church of St Sophia. To the west, Tongjiang Jie reveals two former synagogues and the Turkish Mosque.

Further afield, Hongjun Jie, heading south from the train station, and parts of Dongdazhi Jie are home to several faded old structures including a handful of places of worship and former consulates. 再往前走，从火车站向南行驶的红军街和东大直街的部分地区，有几座褪色的旧建筑，包括一些礼拜场所和前领事馆。郭里大街 ，从东大至街向南延伸至士课街教堂（士课街教堂，Shì Kè Jiē Jiàotáng;211 Shike Jie， 士课街 211 号 ）， F 是另一条前俄罗斯大道，拥有一些幸存的遗产。

The Temple of Bliss (极乐寺, Jílè Sì; 9 Dongdazhi Jie, 东大直街9号; ¥10; h8am-5pm; g53) sits off a pedestrian-only street reachable by bus 53 (¥1) or taxi (¥12) from the Zhongyang Dajie area 5km away. The red-brick Russian Orthodox Church of St Sophia, with its distinctive green onion dome and roosting pigeons, is Harbin’s most famous landmark. Built in 1907 and expanded in 1932, it was the largest Orthodox church in the Far East and the centre of spiritual life for 100,000 Russian settlers. After surviving the Cultural Revolution it was used as a warehouse for a department store. Declared a protected landmark in the 1990s, the church was under renovation at the time of print due to damage caused by the resident pigeons; it is not known when it might reopen.
红砖建造的俄罗斯东正教圣索菲亚教堂，拥有独特的葱穹顶和栖息的鸽子，是哈尔滨最著名的地标。它建于 1907 年，并于 1932 年扩建，是远东最大的东正教教堂，也是 100,000 名俄罗斯定居者的精神生活中心。在文化大革命中幸存下来后，它被用作百货公司的仓库。该教堂在 1990 年代被宣布为受保护地标，由于留鸽造成的损坏，在印刷时正在进行翻修;目前尚不清楚何时会重新开放。

Japanese Germ Warfare Experimental BaseMUSEUM

(侵华日军第731部队遗址, Qīnhuá Rìjūn Dì 731 Bùduì Yízhǐ; Xinjiang Dajie, 新疆大街; h9-11am & 1-3.30pm Tue-Sun) F

This museum is set in the notorious Japanese Germ Warfare Experimental Base (Division 731). Between 1939 and 1945, prisoners of war and civilians were frozen alive, subjected to vivisection or infected with bubonic plague, syphilis and other virulent diseases. Three to four thousand people died here in the most gruesome fashion. The museum includes photos, sculptures and exhibits of the equipment used by the Japanese. There are extensive English captions and an audio guide is available for ¥15.
该博物馆位于臭名昭著的日本细菌战实验基地（第 731 师）。1939年至1945年间，战俘和平民被活活冻死、活体解剖或感染腺鼠疫、梅毒等剧性疾病。三四千人以最可怕的方式死在这里。博物馆包括日本人使用过的设备的照片、雕塑和展品。有大量的英文字幕和语音导览，价格为 15 日元。

The base is in the south of Harbin and takes over an hour to reach by bus. From the small bus station (通江湿地公交枢纽站, Tōngjiāng Shīdì Gōngjiāo Shūniǔ Zhàn) at the north end of Tongjiang Jie (通江街), catch bus 348 (¥2). Get off at the temporary stop called Yiliu’er Zhong (一六二中). From there it’s a 700m walk northeast to the base. If you get lost, just ask the locals the way to qīsānyī (731). Note that Chinese people can be, understandably, uncomfortable talking about this museum.
基地位于哈尔滨南部，乘坐巴士需要一个多小时才能到达。从通江街北端的小公交枢纽站（通江湿地公交枢纽站 ，Tōngjiāng Shīdì Gōngjiāo Shūniǔ Zhàn） 出发，乘坐 348 路公交公交车（2 日元）。在名为 Yiliu'er Zhong （ 一六二中 ） 的临时站点下车。从那里向东北步行 700 米即可到达基地。如果你迷路了，只需向当地人询问前往 qīsānyī （731） 的路。请注意，可以理解的是，中国人可能会不愿意谈论这个博物馆。

THE GREAT CATS
伟大的猫科动物

As with many of the world’s powerful wild creatures, size did not give the Amur (Siberian) tiger much of an advantage during the 20th century. The largest feline in the world, topping 300kg for males and capable of taking down a brown bear in a fair fight, was no match for the poachers, wars, revolutions, railway construction and economic development in its traditional territory in Russia, China and Korea. These days it’s believed that fewer than 500 of the great cats still prowl the wilds of Russia, with a fraction of these settling in Heilongjiang and Jilin provinces in China, and fewer still in neighbouring North Korea.
与世界上许多强大的野生动物一样，体型并没有给东北虎（西伯利亚）带来太大的优势。世界上最大的猫科动物，雄性体重超过 300 公斤，能够在公平的战斗中击倒一只棕熊，无法与俄罗斯、中国和韩国传统领土上的偷猎者、战争、革命、铁路建设和经济发展相提并论。如今，据信只有不到 500 只大型猫科动物仍在俄罗斯的荒野中徘徊，其中一小部分定居在中国的黑龙江省和吉林省，而在邻国朝鲜的则更少。

It’s a dismal figure, and in 1986 the Chinese government set about boosting numbers by establishing the world’s largest tiger breeding centre in Harbin. The majority of tigers are still in captivity, which makes any wild sighting a cause for celebration. In early 2019, a family of tigers was filmed in Jilin province in the Hunchun area, further evidence that the cats are expanding their range south – back into traditional Chinese territory.
这是一个令人沮丧的数字，1986 年，中国政府着手在哈尔滨建立了世界上最大的老虎繁殖中心，以增加数量。大多数老虎仍处于圈养状态，这使得任何野生目击事件都值得庆祝。2019年初，在吉林省珲春地区拍摄了一只老虎，进一步证明这些猫正在向南扩展活动范围，回到中国传统领地。

In 2020 a 14,600-sq-km national park will be established, bridging the Jilin and Heilongjiang borders – and dedicated to tiger preservation. It’s the clearest evidence yet that the Amur might yet have a future in China.
2020年，将建立一个占地14,600平方公里的国家公园，连接吉林和黑龙江边境，致力于老虎保护。这是迄今为止最明确的证据，表明阿穆尔河可能在中国还有未来。

Northeastern China is home to several nature reserves established to protect endangered species of wild cranes. Zhalong Nature Reserve (扎龙自然保护区, Zhālóng Zìrán Bǎohùqū; ¥75; h8am-5.30pm) near Qiqiha’er is the most accessible and most visited of these sanctuaries. Four of the species that migrate here are on the endangered list: the extremely rare red-crowned crane, the white-naped crane, the Siberian crane and the hooded crane.
中国东北地区拥有多个自然保护区，旨在保护濒危野生鹤类。 扎龙自然保护区 （ 扎龙自然保护区 ，Zhālóng Zìrán Bǎohùqū;¥75; h 早上 8 点至下午 5 点 30 分） 位于齐齐哈尔附近，是这些保护区中交通最便利、访问量最大的。该保护区是约 260 种鸟类的家园，其中包括几种稀有鹤。迁徙到这里的四种物种被列入濒危名单：极其罕见的丹顶鹤、白枕鹤、西伯利亚鹤和冠鹤。

Hundreds of birds arrive in April and May, rear their young from June to August and depart in September and October. Unfortunately, a significant percentage of the birds you can see live are in captivity and are periodically released so that visitors can take photos.
该保护区包括约 2100 平方公里的湿地，这些湿地位于从俄罗斯北极延伸到东南亚的鸟类迁徙路径上。数百只鸟在四月和五月抵达，在六月至八月抚养幼崽，并在九月和十月离开。不幸的是，您能看到的活鸟中有很大一部分是圈养的，并且会定期放生，以便游客可以拍照。
