Why Go?
为什么要去？

Guangdong’s unique culture and natural beauty fly under the radar and have yet to be discovered by many travellers, so you may have a plethora of sublime sights (not to mention great dim sum) all to yourself.
广东独特的文化和自然美景在雷达下飞行，尚未被许多旅行者发现，因此您可能会独享大量壮丽的景点（更不用说美味的点心了）。

Northern Guangdong (广东, Guǎngdōng) is home to some wild and wondrous landscapes. In the blue pine forests of Nanling, the music of waterfalls and windswept trees boom-erangs in your direction. If it’s Unesco-crowned heritage you’re after, Kaiping’s flamboyant watchtowers and the stylised poses of Cantonese opera will leave you riveted. What’s all the fuss about Hakka culture? Find out in Meizhou and Chaozhou.
广东（Guǎngdōng）拥有一些野生而奇妙的景观。 在南岭的青松林中，瀑布和被风吹过的树木的音乐向你的方向轰隆作响。如果您追求的是联合国教科文组织加冕的遗产，开平华丽的瞭望塔和粤剧的风格化姿势会让您着迷。客家文化有什么大惊小怪的？在梅州和潮州找出答案。

Historically Guangdong was the starting point of the Maritime Silk Road and the birthplace of revolution. On the scenic byways of the Pearl River delta, you’ll uncover the glory of China’s revolutionary past, while on the surfbeaten beaches of Hailing Island, an ancient shipwreck and its treasures await.
历史上，广东是海上丝绸之路的起点，也是革命的发源地。在珠江三角洲风景秀丽的小路上，您将发现中国革命历史的辉煌，而在海陵岛海浪饱满的海滩上，一艘古老的沉船及其宝藏等待着您。

History
历史

Guangdong has had contact with the outside world for nearly two millennia. Among the first outsiders to arrive were the Romans, who appeared in the 2nd century AD. By the Tang dynasty (618–907), a sizeable trade with the Middle East and Southeast Asia had developed.
广东与外界接触近两千年。第一批到达的外来者包括公元 2 世纪出现的罗马人。到了唐朝（618-907），与中东和东南亚的大规模贸易已经发展起来。

The first Europeans to settle here were the Portuguese in 1557, followed by the Jesuits who established themselves in Zhaoqing. The British came along in the 17th century and by 1685 merchant ships from the East India Company were calling at Guangzhou. In 1757 an imperial edict gave the cohong, a local merchants’ guild, a monopoly on China’s trade with foreigners, who were restricted to Shamian Island. Trade remained in China’s favour until 1773, when the British shifted the balance by unloading 1000 chests of Bengal opium in Guangzhou. Addiction spread in China like wildfire, eventually leading to the Opium Wars.
第一批在这里定居的欧洲人是 1557 年的葡萄牙人，随后是耶稣会士，他们在肇庆定居。英国人于 17 世纪出现，到 1685 年，东印度公司的商船停靠广州。1757 年，一项圣旨授予当地商人行会 cohong 垄断中国与外国人的贸易权，外国人仅限于沙面岛。贸易一直对中国有利，直到 1773 年，英国人在广州卸下了 1000 箱孟加拉鸦片，从而改变了平衡。毒瘾在中国像野火一样蔓延，最终导致了鸦片战争。

In the 19th century Guangdong was a hotbed of reform and revolt. Among the political elites who sowed revolutionary ideas here was Sun Yatsen, who later became the first president of the Republic of China.
19世纪的广东是改革和起义的温床。在这里播下革命思想的政治精英中，有孙中山，他后来成为了中华民国的第一任总统。

The 20th century saw Guangdong serve as the headquarters of both the Nationalist and Communist Parties, and endure great suffering during the Cultural Revolution. After the implementation of the ‘open door’ policy in 1978, it became the first province to embrace capitalism. The province’s continued economic success has made it a leading export centre for consumer goods.
20世纪，广东是国共两党的总部，在文化大革命期间遭受了巨大的苦难。1978年实施“门户开放”政策后，它成为第一个接受资本主义的省份。该省持续的经济成功使其成为领先的消费品出口中心。

Language
语言

The vast majority of the people of Guangdong speak Cantonese, a dialect distinct from Mandarin and also spoken in Hong Kong. Though it enjoys a less exalted status than the national dialect, Cantonese is older and better suited than Mandarin for the reading of classical poetry, according to many scholars. In 1911 Cantonese lost out to Mandarin by a mere one vote to become the national language of China.
广东绝大多数人说粤语，这是一种与普通话不同的方言，在香港也使用。许多学者认为，尽管粤语的地位不如民族方言那么崇高，但粤语比普通话更古老，更适合阅读古典诗歌。1911年，粤语仅以一票之差输给普通话，成为中国的国语。

Guangzhou (Guǎngzhōu), once better known internationally as Canton, has been China’s busiest trading centre for centuries. Despite breakneck redevelopment up to and after the 2010 Asian Games, much of the metropolis still hums along at a pleasantly sedate pace, where narrow, leafy streets conceal temples and mosques, pockets of colonial-era heritage, traditional dim sum eateries, distinctive qílóu shophouses and Lingnan architecture. Equally, you can embrace modernity via the 21st-century architectural landmarks of the showpiece Zhujiang New Town, such as Zaha Hadid’s Opera House and the slim-waisted Canton Tower, rising up over the Pearl River, which cuts a lazy course through the city.
广州（Guǎngzhōu）曾经在国际上更广为人知的名字是广州，几个世纪以来一直是中国最繁忙的贸易中心。尽管在 2010 年亚运会之前和之后进行了迅猛的重建，但大都市的大部分地区仍然以令人愉悦的平静节奏嗡嗡作响，狭窄、绿树成荫的街道隐藏着寺庙和清真寺、殖民时代的遗产、传统的点心餐厅、独特的七楼店屋和岭南建筑。同样，您可以通过珠江新城的 21 世纪建筑地标来拥抱现代感，例如扎哈·哈迪德的歌剧院和耸立在珠江上、穿过城市的慵懒路线的细腰广州塔。

History
历史

Guangzhou’s history is one dominated by trade and revolution. Since the Tang dynasty, it had been China’s most important southern port and the starting point for the Maritime Silk Road, a trade route to the West. It became a trading post for the Portuguese in the 16th century, and later for the British.
广州的历史是一段以贸易和革命为主导的历史。自唐代以来，它一直是中国最重要的南方港口，也是通往西方的贸易路线海上丝绸之路的起点。它在 16 世纪成为葡萄牙人的贸易站，后来成为英国人的贸易站。

After the fall of the Qing dynasty in 1911, the city was a stronghold of the republican forces led by Sun Yatsen and, subsequently, a centre of activity for the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) led by Mao Zedong.
1911 年清朝灭亡后，这座城市是孙中山领导的共和军的据点，随后成为毛泽东领导的中国共产党（CCP）的活动中心。

During the post-1949 years of China’s self-imposed isolation, the Canton Trade Fair was the only platform on which China did business with the West.
在1949年后中国自我封锁的岁月里，广交会是中国与西方做生意的唯一平台。

In 2010 Guangzhou held the Asian Games, which meant a wide-ranging facelift for the city, from improved transport networks to new landmark buildings and cultural institutions.
2010年，广州举办了亚运会，这意味着这座城市进行了广泛的改造，从改善交通网络到新的地标性建筑和文化机构。

KAIPING’S BIZARRE TOWERS
开平奇塔

Scattered across Kaiping’s 20km periphery are diāolóu – multistorey watchtowers and fortified residences displaying a flamboyant mix of European, Chinese and Moorish architectural styles. The majority were built in the early 20th century by some of the villagers who made a fortune working as labourers overseas. They brought home fanciful architectural ideas they’d seen in real life and on postcards, and built the towers as fortresses to protect their families from bandits, flooding and Japanese troops.
开平 20 公里外围散布着 diāolóu——多层瞭望塔和防御工事住宅，展示了欧洲、中国和摩尔人建筑风格的华丽融合。大多数是由一些在海外做劳工发家致富的村民在 20 世纪初建造的。他们把在现实生活中和明信片上看到的奇特建筑理念带回家，并将塔楼建造成堡垒，以保护他们的家人免受土匪、洪水和日本军队的侵害。

The oldest diāolóu were communal watchtowers built by several families in a village. Each family was allocated a room within the citadel, where all its male members would go to spend the night to avoid being kidnapped by bandits. These narrow towers had sturdy walls, iron gates and ports for defence and observation. The earliest diāolóu were also watchtowers, but ones equipped with searchlight and alarm. They are located at the entrances to villages.
最古老的 diāolóu 是由一个村庄的几个家庭建造的公共瞭望塔。每个家庭在城堡内都分配了一个房间，所有男性成员都会去那里过夜，以避免被土匪绑架。这些狭窄的塔楼有坚固的城墙、铁门和防御和观察的港口。最早的 diāolóu 也是瞭望塔，但配备了探照灯和警报器。它们位于村庄的入口处。

More than 60% of diāolóu, however, combined residential functions with defence. Constructed by a single family, they were spacious and featured a mix of decorative motifs. As the builders had no exposure to European architectural traditions, they took liberties with proportions, resulting in outlandish buildings that seem to have leapt out of an American folk-art painting or a Miyazaki animation.

Bāguà Villages 八卦村
八卦村

Two villages, exceptional for their shape and feng shui, make great excursions from Zhaoqing. These ‘bāguà villages’ (bāguà cūn) are designed according to bāguà, an octagon-shaped Taoist symbol with eight trigrams representing different phases in life.
两个村庄的形状和风水都非常出色，是肇庆的绝佳游览地点。这些“ 巴瓜村”（bāguà cūn）是根据巴瓜设计的，巴瓜是一种八角形的道教符号，有八个卦，代表人生的不同阶段。

Around 15km southeast of downtown Zhaoqing, Xiangang village (蚬岗镇, Xiǎngǎng Zhèn), founded in the Ming dynasty, is large and lively and has a market at its entrance. Its 16 ancestral halls, some opulent, are open only on the first and 15th days of the lunar month. Board bus 308 (¥12, one hour) at Qiaoxi Bus Station (桥西汽车站, Qiáoxī Qìchē Zhàn) in Zhaoqing to get here.
肇庆市中心东南约 15 公里处，始建于明代的仙岗镇 （ 蚬岗镇 ，Xiǎngǎng Zhèn），面积大，热闹非凡，入口处设有市场。它的 16 座祠堂，有些很华丽，只在农历月初一和十五日开放。在肇庆桥西汽车站乘坐 308 路公交车（12 日元，一小时）即可到达。

At 700-year-old Licha village (黎槎村, Líchá Cūn; ¥25; h8.15am-5.30pm), 21km southeast of Zhaoqing, houses, many with wok-handle roofs and bas-relief sculptures, radiate from a taichi (a symbol of yin and yang) on a central terrace, turning the village into a maze. Most residents have emigrated to Australia; only the elders remain. Bus 315 leaves for Licha (¥12, 40 minutes, every 15 minutes) from behind Zhaoqing’s Qiaoxi Bus Station.
在拥有 700 年历史的利查村 （ 黎槎村 ，Líchá Cūn;25 日元; h 上午 8 时 15 分至下午 5 时 30 分） 位于肇庆东南 21 公里处，房屋中许多都有炒柄屋顶和浅浮雕，从中央露台上的太极拳（阴阳的象征）中辐射出来，将村庄变成了一个迷宫。大多数居民已移民到澳大利亚;只剩下长辈们。315 路公交车从肇庆桥溪汽车站后面开往荔枝（12 日元，40 分钟，每 15 分钟一班）。

Here you can sample the famous Dinghu Vegetarian Dish (鼎湖上素, Dǐnghú Shàngsù; ¥68), supposedly an invention by monks around here. LINGNAN CULTURE
岭南文化

Lingnan (岭南, Lǐngnán) culture is an important part of broader Cantonese culture and it manifests itself most notably in food, art, architecture and Cantonese opera. Culturally Lingnan was a hybrid and a late bloomer that often went on to reverse-influence the rest of the country. Its development was also fuelled by the ideas of the revolution to end feudalism. Boundaries between refined and pedestrian are relaxed and there’s an open-mindedness towards modernity.
岭南文化是广大粤语文化的重要组成部分，最显著地体现在美食、艺术、建筑和粤剧方面。在文化上，岭南是一个混合体和大器晚成的人，经常继续对全国其他地区产生反向影响。它的发展也受到结束封建主义的革命思想的推动。精致和行人之间的界限是放松的，对现代性持开放态度。

Place
地方

Lingnan, literally South of the Ranges, refers to the region to the south of the five mountain ranges that separate the Yangzi River (central China) from the Pearl River (southern China). Traditionally, Lingnan encompassed several provinces, but today it’s become almost synonymous with Guangdong.
岭南，字面意思是山脉之南，是指将扬子江（中国中部）与珠江（中国南部）分开的五座山脉以南的地区。传统上，岭南包括多个省份，但如今它几乎已成为广东的代名词。

People
人

The term ‘Lingnan’ was traditionally used by men of letters on the Yangzi side as a polite reference to the boonies, where ‘mountains were tall and emperors out of sight’, as a Chinese saying goes. These northerners regarded their southern cousins as less robust (physically and morally), more romantic and less civilised. But being far-flung had its benefits. Lingnan offered refuge to people not tolerated by the Middle Kingdom; and played host in various diasporas in Chinese history to migrants from the north, such as the Hakkas in Meizhou. This also explains why some Cantonese words are closer in pronunciation to the ancient speech of the Chinese.
“岭南”一词传统上被扬子一侧的文学家用来礼貌地指代“山高，皇帝看不见”，正如中国谚语所说。这些北方人认为他们的南方表亲不那么强壮（在身体和道德上）、更浪漫、更不文明。但距离遥远也有其好处。岭南为中国不容忍的人提供庇护;并在中国历史上的各种侨民中接待了来自北方的移民，例如梅州的客家人。这也解释了为什么一些粤语单词在发音上更接近中国人的古语。

Lingnan School of Painting (1900–50)
岭南画派 （1900–50）

The Lingnan painters were an influential lot who ushered in a national movement in art in the first half of the 20th century.
岭南画家是20世纪上半叶引领全国性艺术运动的一群颇具影响力的画家。

Traditionally Chinese painters were literati well versed in calligraphy, poetry and Confucian classics. These scholar-artists would later become imperial bureaucrats, and as they were often stationed somewhere far away from home, they expressed their nostalgia by recreating the landscapes of their childhood villages from memory.
传统上，中国画家是精通书法、诗歌和儒家经典的文人。这些文人艺术家后来成为帝国官僚，由于他们经常驻扎在离家很远的地方，他们通过记忆中重现童年村庄的风景来表达他们的怀旧之情。

The founding masters of the Lingnan School of Painting, however, studied abroad, where they were exposed to Japanese and European art. China, during the Qing dynasty, was being carved up by Western powers. Sharing the ideals of the revolutionaries, these artists devoted themselves to a revolution in art by combining traditional techniques with elements of Western and Japanese realist painting.
然而，岭南画派的创始大师们却出国留学，接触到了日本和欧洲的艺术。清朝时期的中国正在被西方列强瓜分。这些艺术家分享了革命者的理想，将传统技法与西方和日本现实主义绘画元素相结合，致力于艺术革命。

The New National Painting, as it came to be called, featured a bolder use of colours, more realism and a stronger sense of perspective – a style that was more accessible to the citizenry of China’s new republic than the literati painting of the past.
后来被称为“新国画”，其特点是更大胆的色彩运用、更现实主义和更强的透视感——这种风格比过去的文人画更容易为中国新共和国的公民所接受。

Lingnan Architecture
岭南建筑

The Lingnan school of architecture is one of three major schools of modern Chinese architecture, alongside the Beijing and Shanghai schools. It was founded in the 1950s, though earlier structures exhibiting a distinctive local style had existed since the late Ming dynasty (1600s). The features of the Lingnan school are lucidity, openness and an organic incorporation of nature into built environments.
岭南建筑学派是中国现代建筑的三大学派之一，与北京画派和上海画派并驾齐驱。它始建于 1950 年代，但自明朝晚期（1600 年代）以来就已经存在具有独特当地风格的早期建筑。岭南学派的特点是清晰、开放和将自然有机地融入建筑环境。

ANCIENT
古代

Examples of this style of architecture include schools, ancestral halls and temples of the Ming and Qing dynasties. The Chen Clan Ancestral Hall in Guangzhou and Zumiao in Foshan are prime illustrations of this style.
这种建筑风格的例子包括明清时期的学校、祠堂和寺庙。广州的陈氏宗祠和佛山的祖庙就是这种风格的典范。

Vernacular Lingnan-style houses are more decorative than their austere northern cousins. The ‘wok-handle’ houses (锅耳屋, guō’ěr wū) in Licha village near Zhaoqing have distinctive wok-handle-shaped roofs that also serve to prevent the spread of fire. You’ll also see in Licha village bas-relief sculpting and paintings (浮雕彩画, fúdiāo cǎihuà), intricate and colourful, above windows or doors, portraying classical tales, birds, flowers and landscapes.
乡土岭南风格的房屋比它们朴素的北方表亲更具装饰性。肇庆附近荔枝村的锅耳屋（锅耳屋 ，guō'ěr wū）有独特的炒锅柄形屋顶，也起到了防止火势蔓延的作用。在利查村，您还会看到窗户或门上方错综复杂、色彩缤纷的浅浮雕和绘画，描绘了古典故事、鸟类、花卉和风景。

MODERN
摩登

Excellent examples of this style of architecture, appearing in the late Qing dynasty, are the Xiguan houses on Enning Road in Guangzhou, with their grey bricks and stained-glass windows. These windows were products of the marriage between Manchurian windows (满洲窗, mǎnzhoū chuāng), simple contraptions consisting of paper overlaid with wood, and coloured glass introduced to Guangzhou by Westerners. It’s said that when a foreign merchant presented the empress dowager with a bead of coloured glass, she was so dazzled by its beauty that she reciprocated with a pearl. Panxi Restaurant in Guangzhou and Yuyin Mountain Villa have Manchurian windows embedded with coloured glass.
这种建筑风格的优秀例子出现在晚清时期，是广州恩宁路的西关房屋，灰砖和彩色玻璃窗。这些窗户是满洲窗（ 满洲窗 ，mǎnzhoū chuāng）与西方人引入广州的彩色玻璃组成的简单装置，由纸覆盖木头组成的简单装置结合的产物。据说，当一位外国商人向太后赠送一颗彩色玻璃珠时，太后被它的美丽所迷惑，以一颗珍珠作为回报。广州的攀溪餐厅和玉阴山庄的满洲窗嵌入了彩色玻璃。

CONTEMPORARY
同时代

The garden-restaurants and garden-hotels that proliferated between the 1950s and 1990s are examples of contemporary architecture. These indoor Edens were fashioned after the private Lingnan-style gardens of wealthy families, such as Liang Garden in Foshan, which together with the imperial gardens of Peking and the scholars’ gardens of Jiangnan, constituted the three main types of Chinese gardens.
这些室内伊甸园是仿照佛山梁园等富家私有岭南式园林而建的，与北京的皇家园林、江南的士大夫园林一起构成了中国园林的三大类型。

Cantonese Opera
粤剧

Cantonese opera is a regional form of Chinese opera that evolved from theatrical forms of the north and neighbouring regions. Like Peking opera, it involves music, singing, martial arts, acrobatics and acting. There’s elaborate face painting, glamorous period costumes and, for some of the roles, high-pitched falsetto singing. But compared to its northern cousin, it tends to feature more scholars than warriors in its tales of courtship and romance.
粤剧是中国戏曲的一种地方形式，由北方及邻近地区的戏剧形式演变而来。与京剧一样，它涉及音乐、歌唱、武术、杂技和表演。有精心制作的面部彩绘、迷人的古装，对于某些角色来说，还有高亢的假声演唱。但与它的北方表亲相比，它在求爱和浪漫的故事中往往以学者多于战士为特色。

You don’t have to understand or even like Cantonese opera to appreciate it as an important aspect of Cantonese culture – there’s no shortage of related attractions, such as Bahe Academy and Luányú Táng in Guangzhou, a festival, and a props speciality shop in Chaozhou.
你不必了解甚至喜欢粤剧，就能将其视为粤剧文化的一个重要方面——这里不乏相关的景点，例如广州的八河书院和銮榆堂 、节日和潮州的道具专卖店。

Cantonese Cuisine
粤菜

There’s a saying ‘Good food is in Guangzhou’ (食在广州, shí zài Guǎngzhoū). Regional bias aside, Cantonese food is very good. The most influential of the eight major regional cuisines of China, it’s known for complex cooking methods, an obsession with freshness and the use of a wide range of ingredients.
有句话说“美食在广州”（ 食在广州 ，shí zài Guǎngzhoū）。撇开地域偏见不谈，粤菜非常好。它是中国八大地方美食中最具影响力的菜系，以复杂的烹饪方法、对新鲜度的痴迷和使用多种食材而闻名。

Many Cantonese dishes depend on quick cooking over high heat – these require skills (versus patience over a stew) that are less common in other regional cuisines. Dishes such as sweet-and-sour pork, crab shell au gratin and tempura-style prawns show an open-mindedness to foreign ideas.
许多粤菜都依赖于高温快速烹饪——这需要技巧（而不是炖菜的耐心），而这在其他地区美食中不太常见。糖醋肉、蟹壳焗烤和天妇罗式大虾等菜肴显示出对外国思想的开放态度。

When it comes to haute cuisine, even northern cooks would acknowledge the superiority of their Cantonese colleagues in making the best of expensive items such as abalone. Also, much of the costliest marine life to grace the Cantonese table, such as deep-sea fish and large prawns, simply doesn’t grow in inland rivers.
说到高级料理，即使是北方厨师也会承认他们的粤菜同事在充分利用鲍鱼等昂贵食物方面的优势。此外，粤菜餐桌上许多最昂贵的海洋生物，如深海鱼和大虾，根本不生长在内河中。
