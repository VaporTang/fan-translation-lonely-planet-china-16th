Cuisine
厨房

To the Chinese, food is life. Dining is the cherished high point of the daily social calendar and often the one occasion to stop work and fully relax. The only problem is knowing where to begin: the sheer variety on offer can have your head spinning and your tummy quivering.
对中国人来说，食物就是生命。用餐是日常社交日历中珍贵的高潮，也是停下工作、充分放松的唯一机会。唯一的问题是知道从哪里开始：提供的种类繁多会让你头晕目眩，肚子颤抖。

Noodles
面条

Marco Polo may have nicked the recipe to make spaghetti (so they say), but he didn’t quite get the flavouring right. Noodles range across an exciting spectrum of tastes, from the mouth-numbing dàndan miàn (spicy noodles) through to the super-salty zhájiàng miàn (fried sauce noodles).
马可·波罗可能已经修改了制作意大利面的配方（他们是这么说的），但他并没有完全掌握调味方法。面条的口味范围很广，从令人垂涎欲滴的 dàndan miàn（辣面）到超咸的 zhájiàng miàn（炸酱面）。

Dim Sum
点心

Dim sum is steamed up across China, but like the Cantonese dialect it’s best left to the masters of the south to get it right. Hong Kong, Macau and Guangzhou should be your first stops – they set the dim sum benchmark.
点心在中国各地都有，但就像粤语一样，最好留给南方的主人来做。香港、澳门和广州应该是您的第一站——它们设定了点心的标杆。

Dumplings
饺子

Set your compass north and northeast for the best jiǎozi (northern-style boiled dumplings) – leek, pork, lamb, duck, egg, prawn or crabmeat wrapped in an envelope of dough. If you like them crispy, get guōtiē (fried). Shanghai’s interpretation is xiǎolóngbāo – scrummy and steamed.
将指南针向北和东北方向设置，寻找最好的饺子 （ 北方式水饺）—— 韭菜、猪肉、羊肉、鸭肉、鸡蛋、虾或蟹肉包裹在面团中。如果你喜欢它们酥脆，就买 guōtiē（油炸）。上海的诠释是 xiǎolóngbāo——scrummy 和 steamed。

Peking Duck
北京烤鸭

Purists insist you must be in Beijing for true Peking duck roasted to an amber hue over fruit-tree wood. You might as well take their advice as that’s where you’ll find the best Peking-duck restaurants.
纯粹主义者坚持认为，你必须在北京才能品尝到真正的北京烤鸭，烤成琥珀色，在果树木上。您不妨听从他们的建议，因为在那里您可以找到最好的北京烤鸭餐厅。

Hotpot
火锅

An all-weather meal, hotpot is ideal for banishing the bitter cold of a northern winter, while in steaming Chongqing, old folk devour the spiciest variety in the height of summer.
火锅是一顿全天候的饭菜，是驱散北方冬季严寒的理想选择，而在热气腾腾的重庆，老人们在盛夏时节会吃到最辣的火锅。

Hiking
远足

If you’re keen to escape the cities into the great outdoors, China’s dramatic variety of landscapes is the perfect backdrop for bracing walks – whether island-hopping in Hong Kong, exploring the foothills of the Himalayas, or trekking through gorges in Yunnan province.
如果您热衷于逃离城市，到户外活动，那么中国多样的风景是健美散步的完美背景——无论是在香港跳岛游、探索喜马拉雅山麓，还是徒步穿越云南省的峡谷。

Tiger Leaping Gorge, Yunnan
云南虎跃峡

The mother of all southwest China’s treks, this magnificently named Yunnan route is at its most picturesque in early summer. It’s not a walk in the park, so plan ahead and give yourself enough time.
作为中国西南地区所有徒步旅行之母，这条名字宏伟的云南路线在初夏最为美丽。这不是在公园散步，所以要提前计划并给自己足够的时间。

Hong Kong’s Outlying Islands & New Territories
香港离岛及新界

A whopping 70% of Hong Kong is hiking territory, so fling off your Gucci loafers, lace up your hiking boots and go from island to island or make a break for the New Territories, where fantastic hiking trails await.
香港高达 70% 的地区是远足地区，所以脱下你的 Gucci 乐福鞋，系好登山靴，从一个岛到另一个岛，或者去新界休息一下，那里有美妙的远足小径等待着你。

Ganden to Samye, Tibet
甘丹到西藏三野

You’ll need four to five days for this glorious high-altitude hike connecting two of Tibet’s most splendid monasteries. The landscape is beautiful, but the trek requires both physical and mental preparation (plus a Tibet travel permit).
您需要四到五天的时间才能完成这次连接西藏两座最辉煌寺院的壮丽高海拔徒步旅行。风景很美，但徒步旅行需要身心准备（加上西藏旅行证）。

Huangshan, Anhui
安徽黄山

Sooner or later you’ll have to hike uphill, and where better than on China’s most beautiful mountain. The steps may be punishing, but just focus on the scenery – even if the fabled mists are nowhere to be seen, the views are simply breathtaking.
迟早你必须徒步上坡，还有哪比在中国最美丽的山上更好呢？台阶可能会很痛苦，但只要专注于风景——即使传说中的薄雾无处可寻，景色也令人叹为观止。

Yangshuo, Guangxi
广西阳朔

Yangshuo’s karst topography is truly astonishing. Base yourself in town, give yourself three or four days, and walk your socks off (or hire a bike). Adventurous types can even try rock climbing.
阳朔的喀斯特地形确实令人惊叹。在城里扎根，给自己三四天时间，然后脱掉袜子（或租一辆自行车）。喜欢冒险的人甚至可以尝试攀岩。

Temples
鬓

Divided between Buddhist, Taoist and Confucian faiths, China’s temples are places of introspection, peace and absolution. Find them on mountain peaks, in caves, on side streets, hanging from cliffsides or occupying the epicentre of towns, from Tibet to Beijing and beyond.
中国的寺庙分为佛教、道教和儒家信仰，是内省、和平和赦免的地方。在山峰、洞穴、小巷、悬挂在悬崖边或占据城镇中心，从西藏到北京等地，都可以找到它们。

Temple of Heaven, Beijing
天坛，北京

It’s not really a temple, but let’s not quibble. Beijing’s Temple of Heaven was where Ming and Qing emperors communicated with the gods, encapsulating the Confucian desire for symmetry and order, and harmony between heaven and earth.
它不是真正的寺庙，但我们不要狡辩。北京的天坛是明清皇帝与神灵交流的地方，概括了儒家对称秩序、天地和谐的渴望。

Puning Temple, Chengde
承德普宁寺

On a clear day this temple stands out sharply against the hills around Chengde, while in the Mahayana Hall towers the colossal statue of Guanyin, a 22m-high, multiarmed embodiment of Buddhist benevolence – perhaps China’s most astonishing effigy.
在晴朗的日子里，这座寺庙在承德周围的山丘上显得格外醒目，而大乘殿中耸立着巨大的观音像，这是一尊 22 米高、多臂的佛教慈悲化身——也许是中国最令人惊叹的雕像。

Confucius Temple, Qufu
曲阜孔庙

This is China’s largest and most important Confucius temple. The Shandong sage has had an immeasurable influence on the Chinese persona through the millennia – visit the town where it began and try to put his teachings in perspective.
这是中国最大、最重要的孔庙。几千年来，这位山东圣人对中国人的形象产生了不可估量的影响——参观它开始的小镇，并试图正确看待他的教义。

Labrang Monastery, Xiahe
夏河拉卜楞寺

Rustling up a Tibet travel permit can be a hassle, so pop down instead to this gargantuan Tibetan monastery in the scenic southwest corner of Gansu. Its aura of devotion is amplified by the nonstop influx of Tibetan pilgrims and worshippers.
办理西藏旅行证可能会很麻烦，所以可以前往这座位于甘肃风景秀丽的西南角的巨大藏族寺院。它的虔诚光环因不断涌入的西藏朝圣者和礼拜者而放大。

Jokhang Temple, Lhasa
拉萨大昭寺

Tibet’s holiest place of worship, the Jokhang Temple in Lhasa is a place of pilgrimage for every Tibetan Buddhist at least once in their lifetime.
拉萨大昭寺是西藏最神圣的礼拜场所，是每个藏传佛教徒一生中至少朝圣一次的地方。

Festivals
节日

China is a nation of hard workers and entrepreneurs, but considerable energy is reserved for its festivals and celebrations. Festivals can be religious, fun-filled, commemorative or seasonal. Locals don their best clothes and get seriously sociable. Join in and be part of the party.
中国是一个勤奋工作和企业家的国家，但为节日和庆祝活动保留了相当多的精力。节日可以是宗教的、充满乐趣的、纪念性的或季节性的。当地人穿上他们最好的衣服，变得非常善于交际。加入并成为聚会的一部分。

Dragon Boat Festival
端午节

Commemorating the death of the celebrated 3rd-century-BC poet and statesman Qu Yuan, dramatic dragon boat races can be seen in May or June churning up the waterways in Shanghai, Hong Kong, Tianjin and other cities.
为了纪念公元前 3 世纪著名的诗人和政治家屈原的逝世，5 月或 6 月，上海、香港、天津和其他城市的水道上都可以看到戏剧性的龙舟比赛。

Ice & Snow Festival, Harbin
冰雪节，哈尔滨

The arctic temperatures may knock the wind from your lungs, but in January the frost-bitten capital of Heilongjiang province twinkles with an iridescent collection of carved ice sculptures.
北极的气温可能会让你的肺部感到不舒服，但在一月份，这个饱受冻伤的黑龙江省首府闪烁着彩虹色的冰雕。

Third Moon Fair, Dali
第三届月会，大理

One of China’s many ethnic minority festivals, usually held in April, this Bai fair commemorates the appearance of Guanyin, the Bodhisattva of Mercy, to the people of the Nanzhao kingdom.
这个白集是中国众多少数民族节日之一，通常在四月举行，是为了纪念观音菩萨向南诏国人民的出现。

Spring Festival
春节

China’s most commercially driven and full-on celebration takes the entire nation by storm at midnight on the first day of the first lunar month. The fuse is lit on a nationwide arsenal of fireworks.
中国最具商业化和最全面的庆祝活动在农历正月初一午夜席卷全国。导火索点燃在全国范围内的烟花库上。

Monlam (Great Prayer) Festival, Xiahe
Monlam（大祈祷）节，夏河

The highlight of this Buddhist festival (in February or March), celebrated across Tibet, is easiest to witness in the monastic town of Xiahe, where a host of activities include the unfurling of a huge thangka (sacred painting) on the hillside.
这个佛教节日（二月或三月）的重头戏在西藏各地庆祝，在寺院小镇夏河最容易见证，那里的一系列活动包括在山坡上展开一幅巨大的唐卡 （圣画）。
