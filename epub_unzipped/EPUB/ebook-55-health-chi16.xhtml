In China

Health
健康

China is a reasonably healthy country in which to travel. Some health issues should be noted, though pre-existing medical conditions and accidental injury (especially traffic accidents) account for most life-threatening problems while travelling in China.
中国是一个相当健康的旅行国家。应注意一些健康问题，尽管在中国旅行时，原有的医疗状况和意外伤害（尤其是交通事故）是大多数危及生命的问题。

Outside of the major cities, medical care is often inadequate, and food and waterborne diseases are common. Malaria is still present in some parts of the country, and altitude sickness can be a problem, particularly in Tibet.
在大城市之外，医疗服务往往不足，食物和水传播疾病很常见。疟疾在该国的一些地区仍然存在，高原反应可能是一个问题，尤其是在西藏。

In case of accident or illness, it’s best just to get a taxi and go to hospital directly.
万一发生意外或生病，最好直接打车去医院。

Advice here is a general guide only and does not replace the advice of a doctor trained in travel medicine.
此处的建议仅是一般指南，不能取代受过旅行医学培训的医生的建议。

Health Insurance
健康保险

AEven if you are fit and healthy, don’t travel without health insurance – accidents happen.
A 即使您身体健康，也不要在没有健康保险的情况下旅行——事故时有发生。

ADeclare any existing medical conditions you have (the insurance company will check if your problem is pre-existing and will not cover you if it is undeclared).
A 申报您现有的任何医疗状况（保险公司将检查您的问题是否已经存在，如果未申报，则不承保您）。

AYou may require extra cover for adventure activities such as rock climbing or skiing.
A 您可能需要额外的保险才能进行攀岩或滑雪等冒险活动。

AIf you’re uninsured, emergency evacuation is expensive; bills of more than US$100,000 are not uncommon.
如果您没有保险， A 紧急疏散费用很高;超过 100,000 美元的账单并不少见。

AEnsure you keep all documentation related to any medical expenses you incur.
A 确保保留与您产生的任何医疗费用相关的所有文件。

CORONAVIRUS
冠状病毒

In December 2019, a new virus surfaced in a food market in Wuhan, the capital of Hubei province. Officially named Covid-19, the virus spread worldwide, despite Wuhan and other cities in Hubei province going into lockdown in late January 2020. The virus causes a range of range of respiratory illnesses which can lead to death, especially in the elderly and vulnerable. At the time of writing, almost 3.5 million people had died from contracting Covid-19. China closed its borders to foreign visitors in March 2020; at the time of writing, only a restricted number of foreigners could enter China with stringent conditions attached to the visa application system, including the provision of a document of health (issued if evidence is supplied of negative nucleic acid and IgM antibody tests for Covid-19, taken no more than 48 hours before travel to China) or proof of vaccination with a Chinese-made vaccine, followed by two weeks quarantine upon arrival in China. For the latest information on the virus, including safety precautions and travel advice, visit www.who.int/emergencies/diseases/novel-coronavirus-2019..
2019 年 12 月，湖北省省会武汉市的一个食品市场出现了一种新型病毒。该病毒的正式名称为 Covid-19，尽管武汉和湖北省其他城市于 2020 年 1 月下旬进入封锁状态，但该病毒仍在全球范围内传播。该病毒会导致一系列呼吸道疾病，可能导致死亡，尤其是在老年人和弱势群体中。在撰写本文时，已有近 350 万人死于 Covid-19。中国于 2020 年 3 月对外国游客关闭边境;在撰写本文时，只有有限数量的外国人可以进入中国，但签证申请系统附带了严格的条件，包括提供健康证明（如果提供 Covid-19 核酸和 IgM 抗体检测阴性的证据，在前往中国前不超过 48 小时进行）或中国制造的疫苗接种证明， 抵达中国后进行两周的隔离。有关该病毒的最新信息，包括安全预防措施和旅行建议，请访问 www.who.int/emergencies/diseases/novel-coronavirus-2019..

Vaccinations
疫苗

Specialised travel-medicine clinics stock all available vaccines and can give specific recommendations for your trip. The doctors will consider your vaccination history, the length of your trip, activities you may undertake and any underlying medical conditions, such as pregnancy.
专门的旅行医学诊所备有所有可用的疫苗，并可以为您的旅行提供具体建议。医生会考虑您的疫苗接种史、旅行时间、您可能进行的活动以及任何潜在的医疗状况，例如怀孕。

AVisit a doctor six to eight weeks before departure, as most vaccines don’t produce immunity until at least two weeks after they’re given.
A 在出发前六到八周去看医生，因为大多数疫苗要到接种后至少两周才会产生免疫力。

AAsk your doctor for an International Certificate of Vaccination (otherwise known as the ‘yellow booklet’), listing all vaccinations received.
A 向您的医生索取国际疫苗接种证书（也称为“黄册子”），其中列出了所有接受的疫苗接种。

AThe only vaccine required by international regulations is for yellow fever.
A 国际法规要求的唯一疫苗是黄热病疫苗。

Proof of vaccination against yellow fever is only required if you have visited a country in the yellow-fever zone within the six days prior to entering China. If you are travelling to China directly from South America or Africa, check with a travel clinic as to whether you need a yellow-fever vaccination.
只有在进入中国前六天内到过黄热病区国家时，才需要提供黄热病疫苗接种证明。如果您直接从南美洲或非洲前往中国，请咨询旅行诊所是否需要接种黄热病疫苗。

Recommended Vaccinations
推荐的疫苗接种

The World Health Organization (WHO) recommends the following vaccinations for travellers to China:
世界卫生组织 （WHO） 建议前往中国的旅客接种以下疫苗：

Adult diphtheria and tetanus (ADT) Single booster recommended if you’ve not received one in the previous 10 years. Side effects include a sore arm and fever. An ADT vaccine that immunises against pertussis (whooping cough) is also available and may be recommended by your doctor.
成人白喉和破伤风 （ADT） 如果您在过去 10 年内没有接种过一次加强针，建议您接种一次加强针。副作用包括手臂酸痛和发烧。也可以接种免疫百日咳（百日咳）的 ADT 疫苗，您的医生可能会推荐。

Hepatitis A Provides almost 100% protection for up to a year; a booster after 12 months provides at least another 20 years’ protection. Mild side effects such as a headache and sore arm occur in 5% to 10% of people.
甲型肝炎提供长达一年的几乎 100% 保护;12 个月后的加强针至少可提供 20 年的保护。5% 至 10% 的人会出现头痛和手臂酸痛等轻微副作用。

Hepatitis B Now considered routine for most travellers. Given as three shots over six months, though a rapid schedule is also available. There is also a combined vaccination with hepatitis A. Side effects are mild and uncommon, usually a headache and sore arm. Lifetime protection results for 95% of people.
乙型肝炎现在被认为是大多数旅行者的例行公事。在六个月内注射三针，但也可以快速安排。还有与甲型肝炎联合接种的疫苗。副作用轻微且不常见，通常是头痛和手臂酸痛。95% 的人获得终身保护。

Measles, mumps and rubella (MMR) Two doses of MMR is recommended unless you have had the diseases. Occasionally a rash and a flu-like illness can develop a week after receiving the vaccine. Many adults under 40 require a booster.
麻疹、腮腺炎和风疹 （MMR） 除非您患有这些疾病，否则建议服用两剂 MMR。有时，在接种疫苗一周后会出现皮疹和流感样疾病。许多 40 岁以下的成年人需要加强针。

Typhoid Recommended unless your trip is less than a week. The vaccine offers around 70% protection, lasts for two to three years and comes as a single shot. Tablets are also available, though the injection is usually recommended as it has fewer side effects. A sore arm and fever may occur. A vaccine combining hepatitis A and typhoid in a single shot is now available.
伤寒推荐，除非您的旅行少于一周。该疫苗提供约 70% 的保护，可持续两到三年，并且是单针。也可以使用片剂，但通常建议注射，因为它的副作用较少。可能会出现手臂酸痛和发烧。一种将甲型肝炎和伤寒结合在单针中的疫苗现已上市。

Varicella If you haven’t had chickenpox, discuss this vaccination with your doctor.
水痘如果您没有患过水痘，请与您的医生讨论这种疫苗接种。

The following immunisations are recommended for travellers spending more than one month in the country, or those at special risk:
建议在该国停留超过一个月的旅客或有特殊风险的旅客接种以下疫苗：

Influenza A single shot lasts one year and is recommended for those over 65 years of age or with underlying medical conditions such as heart or lung disease.
流感单次注射持续一年，建议 65 岁以上或患有心脏病或肺部疾病等基础疾病的人接种。

Japanese B encephalitis A series of three injections with a booster after two years. Recommended if spending more than one month in rural areas in summer, or more than three months in the country.
日本乙型脑炎两年后连续三次注射加强剂。如果夏季在农村地区度过一个多月，或在乡下度过三个月以上，则推荐使用。

Pneumonia A single injection with a booster after five years is recommended for all travellers over 65 years of age or with underlying medical conditions that compromise immunity, such as heart or lung disease, cancer or HIV.
肺炎建议所有 65 岁以上或患有损害免疫力的基础疾病（例如心脏病或肺病、癌症或艾滋病毒）的旅行者在五年后进行一次加强针注射。

Rabies Three injections in all. A booster after one year will then provide 10 years’ protection. Side effects are rare – occasionally a headache and sore arm.
狂犬病总共注射了三针。一年后的加强针将提供 10 年的保护。副作用很少见——偶尔会出现头痛和手臂酸痛。

Tuberculosis A complex issue. High-risk adult long-term travellers are usually recommended to have a TB skin test before and after travel, rather than vaccination. Only one vaccine is given in a lifetime. Children under five spending more than three months in China should be vaccinated.
结核一个复杂的问题。通常建议高危成年长期旅行者在旅行前后进行结核病皮肤测试，而不是接种疫苗。一生只接种一种疫苗。在中国生活超过三个月的五岁以下儿童应接种疫苗。

Pregnant women and children should receive advice from a doctor who specialises in travel medicine.
孕妇和儿童应接受专门从事旅行医学的医生的建议。

Tips for Taking Medications to China
带药去中国的小贴士

In China you can buy some medications over the counter without a doctor’s prescription, but not all, and in general it is not advisable to buy medications locally without a doctor’s advice. Fake medications and poorly stored or out-of-date drugs are also common, so try to bring your own.
在中国，您可以在没有医生处方的情况下在柜台购买一些药物，但不是全部，一般来说，在没有医生建议的情况下，不建议在当地购买药物。假药和储存不当或过期的药物也很常见，因此请尽量自备。

APack medications in their original, labelled containers.
A 将药物包装在原始的贴有标签的容器中。

AIf you take any regular medication, bring double your needs in case of loss or theft.
如果您服用任何常规药物， A 请携带双倍的需求，以防丢失或被盗。

ATake a signed and dated letter from your physician describing your medical conditions and medications (using generic names).
A 从您的医生那里拿一封签名并注明日期的信，描述您的医疗状况和药物（使用通用名称）。

AIf carrying syringes or needles, ensure you have a physician’s letter documenting their medical necessity. If you have a heart condition, bring a copy of an ECG taken just prior to travelling.
A 如果携带注射器或针头，请确保您有医生的信函，证明其医疗必要性。如果您患有心脏病，请携带旅行前拍摄的心电图副本。

Availability & Cost of Health Care
医疗保健的可用性和成本

Good clinics catering to travellers can be found in major cities. They are more expensive than local facilities, but you may feel more comfortable dealing with a western-trained doctor who speaks your language. These clinics usually have a good understanding of the best local hospital facilities and close contacts with insurance companies should you need evacuation. As a rough idea of cost, the private section of a large hospital in Beijing dedicated to foreigners will charge about ¥500 upfront for a consultation, plus another ¥500 for an X-ray. Waiting times are roughly an hour for each step.
在主要城市可以找到为旅行者提供服务的优质诊所。它们比当地设施更贵，但与会说您的语言的受过西方培训的医生打交道可能会让您感到更舒服。这些诊所通常对当地最好的医院设施有很好的了解，并在您需要疏散时与保险公司密切联系。作为费用的粗略概念，北京一家专门为外国人提供的大型医院的私家科会收取约 500 日元的前期咨询费用，另外还有 500 日元的 X 光检查费用。每步的等待时间大约是一个小时。

Infectious Diseases
传染病

Dengue
登革热

This mosquito-borne disease occurs in some parts of southern China. There is no vaccine so avoid mosquito bites – the dengue-carrying mosquito bites day and night, so use insect-avoidance measures at all times. Symptoms include high fever, severe headache and body ache. Some people develop a rash and diarrhoea. There is no specific treatment – just rest and paracetamol. Do not take aspirin.
这种蚊媒疾病发生在中国南方的一些地区。没有疫苗，所以要避免蚊虫叮咬——携带登革热的蚊子日夜叮咬，所以要时刻采取避虫措施。症状包括高烧、剧烈头痛和身体疼痛。有些人会出现皮疹和腹泻。没有特定的治疗方法——只有休息和扑热息痛。不要服用阿司匹林。

Hepatitis A
甲型肝炎

A problem throughout China, this food- and waterborne virus infects the liver, causing jaundice (yellow skin and eyes), nausea and lethargy. There is no specific treatment for hepatitis A; you just need to allow time for the liver to heal. All travellers to China should be vaccinated.
这种食物和水传播的病毒感染肝脏，导致黄疸（皮肤和眼睛发黄）、恶心和嗜睡。甲型肝炎没有特定的治疗方法;你只需要留出时间让肝脏愈合。所有前往中国的旅客都应接种疫苗。

Hepatitis B
乙型肝炎

The only sexually transmitted disease that can be prevented by vaccination, hepatitis B is spread by contact with infected body fluids. The long-term consequences can include liver cancer and cirrhosis. All travellers to China should be vaccinated.
乙型肝炎是唯一可以通过接种疫苗预防的性传播疾病，通过接触受感染的体液传播。长期后果可能包括肝癌和肝硬化。所有前往中国的旅客都应接种疫苗。

Japanese Encephalitis
日本脑炎

Formerly known as ‘Japanese B encephalitis’, this is a rare disease in travellers, though vaccination is recommended if you’re in rural areas for more than a month during summer, or if spending more than three months in the country. No treatment is available; one-third of infected people die, while another third suffer permanent brain damage.
以前称为 “日本乙型脑炎”，这是一种旅行者罕见的疾病，但如果您在夏季在农村地区停留超过一个月，或者在该国停留超过三个月，建议接种疫苗。没有可用的治疗方法;三分之一的感染者死亡，另外三分之一遭受永久性脑损伤。

Malaria
疟疾

Malaria has been nearly eradicated in China; it is not generally a risk for visitors to the cities and most tourist areas. It is found mainly in rural areas in the southwestern region bordering Myanmar, Laos and Vietnam, principally Hainan, Yunnan and Guangxi. More limited risk exists in the remote rural areas of Fujian, Guangdong, Guizhou and Sichuan. Generally medication is only advised if you are visiting rural Hainan, Yunnan or Guangxi.
疟疾在中国几乎被根除;对于前往城市和大多数旅游区的游客来说，这通常不会构成风险。主要分布于缅甸、老挝和越南接壤的西南部农村地区，主要是海南、云南和广西。福建、广东、贵州和四川等偏远农村地区的风险较为有限。一般来说，只有在您访问海南、云南或广西农村时才建议服药。

To prevent malaria:
预防疟疾：

AAvoid mosquitoes and take antimalarial medications (most people who catch malaria are taking inadequate or no antimalarial medication).
A 避免蚊子并服用抗疟药物（大多数感染疟疾的人服用的抗疟药物不足或没有服用）。

AUse an insect repellent containing DEET on exposed skin (natural repellents such as citronella can be effective, but require more frequent application).
在裸露的皮肤上 A 使用含有避蚊胺的驱虫剂（香茅等天然驱虫剂可能有效，但需要更频繁地使用）。

ASleep under a mosquito net impregnated with permethrin.
A 睡在浸有氯菊酯的蚊帐下。

AChoose accommodation with screens and fans (if it’s not air-conditioned).
A 选择带屏幕和风扇的住宿（如果没有空调）。

AImpregnate clothing with permethrin in high-risk areas.
A 在高风险区域用氯菊酯浸渍衣服。

AWear long sleeves and trousers in light colours.
A 穿浅色长袖和长裤。

AUse mosquito coils.
A 使用蚊香。

ASpray your room with insect repellent before going out for your evening meal.

水

In general, you should never drink the tap water in China, even in five-star hotels. Boiling water makes it safe to drink, but you might prefer to buy bottled mineral water.
一般来说，你永远不应该喝中国的自来水，即使是在五星级酒店。开水可以安全饮用，但您可能更愿意购买瓶装矿泉水。

RABIES
狂犬病

An increasingly common problem in China, this fatal disease is spread by the bite or lick of an infected animal, most often a dog. Seek medical advice immediately after any animal bite and commence post-exposure treatment. The pre-travel vaccination means the post-bite treatment is greatly simplified.
这种致命的疾病在中国越来越普遍，是通过受感染动物（最常见的是狗）的咬伤或舔舐传播的。任何动物咬伤后立即寻求医疗建议并开始暴露后治疗。旅行前疫苗接种意味着咬伤后的治疗大大简化。

Follow these instructions if an animal bites you:
如果动物咬伤您，请按照以下说明进行作：

AGently wash the wound with soap and water, and apply an iodine-based antiseptic.
A 用肥皂和水轻轻清洗伤口，并涂抹碘基防腐剂。

AContact your insurance company to locate the nearest clinic stocking rabies immunoglobulin and vaccine. Immunoglobulin is often unavailable outside of major centres, but if you have had a bite that has broken the skin, it’s crucial that you get to a clinic that has immunoglobulin as soon as possible.
A 请联系您的保险公司，找到最近的备有狂犬病、免疫球蛋白和疫苗的诊所。免疫球蛋白通常在主要中心之外无法获得，但如果您被咬伤导致皮肤破裂，尽快前往有免疫球蛋白的诊所至关重要。

SCHISTOSOMIASIS (BILHARZIA)
血吸虫病（BILHARZIA）

This disease occurs in the central Yangzi River (Cháng Jiāng) basin, carried in water by minute worms that infect certain varieties of freshwater snail found in rivers, streams, lakes and, particularly, behind dams. The infection often causes no symptoms until the disease is well established (several months to years after exposure); any resulting damage to internal organs is irreversible. Effective treatment is available.
这种疾病发生在扬子江中部（Cháng Jiāng）流域，由微小蠕虫在水中携带，这些蠕虫感染了河流、溪流、湖泊中发现的某些淡水蜗牛，特别是在水坝后面。感染通常不会引起任何症状，直到疾病确立（接触后数月至数年）;由此造成的任何内脏器官损伤都是不可逆转的。有效的治疗是可用的。

TYPHOID
伤寒

Typhoid is a serious bacterial infection spread via food and water. Symptoms include headaches and a high and slowly progressive fever, perhaps accompanied by a dry cough and stomach pain. Vaccination is not 100% effective, so still be careful what you eat and drink. All travellers spending more than a week in China should be vaccinated against typhoid.
伤寒是一种通过食物和水传播的严重细菌感染。症状包括头痛和高烧并缓慢进行，可能伴有干咳和胃痛。疫苗接种并非 100% 有效，因此仍然要注意饮食。所有在中国停留超过一周的旅客都应接种伤寒疫苗。

Traveller’s Diarrhoea
旅行者腹泻

Between 30% and 50% of visitors will suffer from traveller’s diarrhoea within two weeks of starting their trip. In most cases the ailment is caused by bacteria and responds promptly to treatment with antibiotics.
30% 至 50% 的游客将在开始旅行后两周内患上旅行者腹泻。在大多数情况下，这种疾病是由细菌引起的，并且对抗生素治疗会迅速做出反应。

Treatment consists of staying hydrated – rehydration solutions such as Gastrolyte are best. Antibiotics such as norfloxacin, ciprofloxacin or azithromycin will kill the bacteria quickly. Loperamide is just a ‘stopper’ and doesn’t cure the problem; it can be helpful, however, for long bus rides. Don’t take loperamide if you have a fever, or blood in your stools. Seek medical attention if you do not respond to an appropriate antibiotic.
治疗包括保持水分——胃溶剂等补液溶液是最好的。诺氟沙星、环丙沙星或阿奇霉素等抗生素会迅速杀死细菌。洛哌丁胺只是一个“塞子”，不能治愈问题;但是，对于长途乘坐公共汽车来说，它可能会有所帮助。如果您发烧或便血，请勿服用洛哌丁胺。如果您对适当的抗生素没有反应，请就医。

AEat only at busy restaurants with a high turnover.
A 只在营业额高的繁忙餐厅用餐。

AEat only freshly cooked food.
A 只吃新鲜烹制的食物。

A 避免吃自助餐里的食物。

APeel all fruit, cook vegetables and soak salads in iodine water for at least 20 minutes.

ADrink bottled water.

Traditional Chinese Medicine
中医

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) views the human body as an energy system in which the basic substances of qì (气, vital energy), jīng (精, essence), xuè (血, blood) and tǐyè (体液, body fluids, blood and other organic fluids) function. The concept of yīn (阴) and yáng (阳) is fundamental to the system. Disharmony between yin and yang, or within the basic substances, may be a result of internal causes (emotions), external causes (climatic conditions) or miscellaneous causes (work, exercise, stress etc). Treatment includes acupuncture, massage, herbs, diet and qì gōng (气功), which seeks to bring these elements back into balance. Treatments can be particularly useful for chronic diseases and ailments such as fatigue, arthritis, irritable bowel syndrome and some chronic skin conditions.
中医将人体视为一个能量系统，其中气（ 气，生命能量）、 精 （ 精 ，精气）、 血 （ 血 ，血液 ）和体液 （ 体液 、体液、血液和其他有机体液）等基本物质发挥作用。 阴 （ 阴 ）和阳 （ 阳）的概念是该系统的基础。阴阳之间或基本物质内部的不和谐可能是由内因（情绪）、外因（气候条件）或杂因（工作、运动、压力等）造成的。治疗包括针灸、按摩、草药、 饮食和气功 ，旨在使这些元素恢复平衡。治疗对于慢性疾病和疾病特别有用，例如疲劳、关节炎、肠易激综合征和一些慢性皮肤病。

Be aware that ‘natural’ does not always mean ‘safe’ – there can be drug interactions between herbal medicines and western medicines. If using both systems, ensure you inform each practitioner what the other has prescribed.
请注意，“天然”并不总是意味着“安全”——草药和西药之间可能存在药物相互作用。如果同时使用这两个系统，请确保告知每位从业者对方的处方。

Environmental Hazards
环境危害

AIR POLLUTION
空气污染

Air pollution is a significant problem in many Chinese cities, and many locals wear face masks. People with underlying respiratory conditions should seek advice from their doctor prior to travel to ensure they have adequate medications in case their condition worsens. Take treatments such as throat lozenges and cough-and-cold tablets.
空气污染是中国许多城市的一个重大问题，许多当地人都戴着口罩。患有基础呼吸系统疾病的人应在旅行前寻求医生的建议，以确保他们有足够的药物以防病情恶化。服用润喉糖和止咳感冒片等治疗方法。

ALTITUDE SICKNESS
高原反应

There are bus journeys in Tibet, Qinghai and Xinjiang where the road goes above 5000m. Acclimatising to such extreme elevations takes several weeks at least, but most travellers come up from sea level very fast – a bad move! Acute mountain sickness (AMS) results from a rapid ascent to altitudes above 2700m. It usually commences within 24 to 48 hours of arriving at altitude, and symptoms include headache, nausea, fatigue and loss of appetite (feeling much like a hangover).
西藏、青海和新疆有公交车，公路超过 5000 米。适应如此极端的海拔至少需要几周时间，但大多数旅行者很快就从海平面上来了——这是一个糟糕的举动！急性高山病 （AMS） 是由于快速上升到 2700 米以上的海拔而引起的。它通常在到达海拔高度后 24 至 48 小时内开始，症状包括头痛、恶心、疲劳和食欲不振（感觉很像宿醉）。

If you have altitude sickness, the cardinal rule is that you must not go higher as you are sure to get sicker and could develop one of the more severe and potentially deadly forms of the disease: high-altitude pulmonary oedema (HAPE) and high-altitude cerebral oedema (HACE). Both are medical emergencies and, as there are no rescue facilities similar to those in the Nepal Himalaya, prevention is the best policy.
如果您患有高原反应，基本规则是您一定不能走得更高，因为您肯定会病情加重，并且可能会患上一种更严重和可能致命的疾病形式：高原肺水肿 （HAPE） 和高原脑水肿 （HACE）。两者都是医疗紧急情况，由于没有类似尼泊尔喜马拉雅山的救援设施，预防是最好的政策。

AMS can be prevented by ‘graded ascent’. It is recommended that once you are above 3000m you ascend a maximum of 300m daily, with a rest day every 1000m. You can also use a medication called Diamox as a prevention or treatment for AMS, but you should discuss this first with a doctor experienced in altitude medicine. Diamox should not be taken by people with a sulphur-drug allergy.
AMS 可以通过“分级上升”来预防。建议一旦您超过 3000 米，您每天最多攀登 300 米，每 1000 米休息一天。您也可以使用一种名为 Diamox 的药物来预防或治疗 AMS，但您应该先与在高原医学方面经验丰富的医生讨论这个问题。对硫磺药物过敏的人不应服用 Diamox。

If you have altitude sickness, rest where you are for a day or two until your symptoms resolve. There is no way of predicting who will suffer from AMS, but certain factors predispose you to it: rapid ascent, carrying a heavy load, and having a seemingly minor illness such as a chest infection or diarrhoea. Make sure you drink at least 3L of non-caffeinated drinks daily to stay well hydrated.
如果您患有高原反应，请在原地休息一两天，直到症状消失。无法预测谁会患上 AMS，但某些因素会使您容易患上 AMS：快速上升、负重以及患有看似轻微的疾病，例如胸部感染或腹泻。确保每天至少喝 3 升不含咖啡因的饮料，以保持充足的水分。

HEAT EXHAUSTION
中暑

Dehydration or salt deficiency can cause heat exhaustion. Take time to acclimatise to high temperatures, drink sufficient liquids and avoid physically demanding activity.
脱水或缺盐会导致中暑。花时间适应高温，喝足够的液体，避免体力要求较高的活动。

Salt deficiency is characterised by fatigue, lethargy, headaches, giddiness and muscle cramps. Salt tablets may help, but adding extra salt to your food is better.
缺盐的特征是疲劳、嗜睡、头痛、头晕和肌肉痉挛。盐片可能会有所帮助，但在食物中添加额外的盐会更好。

HYPOTHERMIA
低温

Be particularly aware of the dangers of trekking at high altitudes or simply taking a long bus trip over mountains. In Tibet it can go from being mildly warm to blisteringly cold in minutes – blizzards can appear from nowhere.
要特别注意在高海拔地区徒步旅行或只是乘坐长途巴士翻山越岭的危险。在西藏，天气可能会在几分钟内从温和的温暖变成酷冷的天气——暴风雪可能会不知从何而来。

Progress from very cold to dangerously cold can be rapid due to a combination of wind, wet clothing, fatigue and hunger, even if the air temperature is above freezing. Dress in layers – silk, wool and some artificial fibres are good insulating materials. A hat is important, as a lot of heat is lost through the head. A strong, waterproof outer layer (and a space blanket for emergencies) is essential. Carry basic supplies, including food containing simple sugars, and fluid to drink.
即使气温高于冰点，由于风、湿衣服、疲劳和饥饿的结合，从非常寒冷到危险的寒冷也会迅速发展。多穿几层衣服——丝绸、羊毛和一些人造纤维是很好的绝缘材料。帽子很重要，因为头部会散失大量热量。坚固、防水的外层（以及紧急情况下的太空毯）是必不可少的。携带基本用品，包括含有单糖的食物和饮用的液体。

Symptoms of hypothermia are exhaustion, numb skin (particularly the toes and fingers), shivering, slurred speech, irrational or violent behaviour, lethargy, stumbling, dizzy spells, muscle cramps and violent bursts of energy.
体温过低的症状是疲惫、皮肤麻木（尤其是脚趾和手指）、颤抖、言语不清、非理性或暴力行为、嗜睡、绊倒、头晕、肌肉痉挛和精力剧烈爆发。

To treat mild hypothermia, first get the person out of the wind and/or rain, remove their clothing if it’s wet, and replace it with dry, warm clothing. Give them hot liquids – not alcohol – and high-calorie, easily digestible food. Early recognition and treatment of mild hypothermia is the only way to prevent severe hypothermia, a critical condition that requires medical attention.
要治疗轻度体温过低，首先让患者远离风和/或雨，如果衣服湿了，请脱掉衣服，换上干燥、温暖的衣服。给他们热液体——而不是酒精——和高热量、易消化的食物。及早识别和治疗轻度体温过低是预防严重体温过低的唯一方法，这是一种需要医疗护理的危急情况。

INSECT BITES & STINGS
昆虫叮咬和蜇伤

Bedbugs don’t carry disease, but their bites are very itchy. Treat the itch with an antihistamine.
臭虫不会携带疾病，但它们的叮咬非常痒。用抗组胺药治疗瘙痒。

Lice inhabit various parts of the human body, most commonly the head and pubic areas. Transmission is via close contact with an affected person. Lice can be difficult to treat, but electric lice combs/detectors can be effective (pick one up before travelling). Otherwise you may need numerous applications of an antilice shampoo such as permethrin. Pubic lice (crab lice) are usually contracted from sexual contact.
虱子栖息在人体的各个部位，最常见的是头部和。传播是通过与受影响的人密切接触。虱子可能很难治疗，但电虱梳/探测器可能很有效（旅行前捡起一个）。否则，您可能需要多次使用防虱洗发水，例如氯菊酯。阴虱（蟹虱）通常是通过性接触感染的。

Ticks are contracted by walking in rural areas, and are commonly found behind the ears, on the belly and in armpits. If you have had a tick bite and experience symptoms such as a rash, fever or muscle aches, see a doctor. Doxycycline prevents some tick-borne diseases.
蜱虫在农村地区通过行走而感染，常见于耳后、腹部和腋窝。如果您被蜱虫叮咬并出现皮疹、发烧或肌肉酸痛等症状，请去看医生。多西环素可预防一些蜱传疾病。
