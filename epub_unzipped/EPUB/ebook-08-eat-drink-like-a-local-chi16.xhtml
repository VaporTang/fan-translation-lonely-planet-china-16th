Eat & Drink Like a Local
像当地人一样吃喝

Half of China travel revolves around food; so does half of Chinese life. The catalyst for enjoyment, meals are occasions for pleasure and entertainment, to clinch deals, strike up new friendships and rekindle old ones. Put China on a plate: all you need is a visa, a pair of chopsticks and an adventurous palate.
中国一半的旅行围绕着美食;一半的中国人的生活也是如此。用餐是享受的催化剂，是快乐和娱乐的场合，是达成交易、建立新友谊和重燃旧友谊的场合。把中国放在盘子里：你只需要一个签证、一双筷子和一个冒险的味觉。

The Year in Food
美食之年

China’s vast size and climatic disparities means there’s no shortage of interesting bites year-round, but there are some seasonal specialities for your diary:
中国幅员辽阔，气候差异很大，一年四季都不乏有趣的美食，但也有一些时令特色菜可供您的日记阅读：

Spring (March–May) The best season for bright-red xiǎolóngxiā (小龙虾) – spicy crayfish, sold in street-food markets. The Lantern Festival sees steaming bowls of yuánxiāo (元宵), a sweet dessert of soft balls made from glutinous rice flour. Bamboo shoots (笋; sǔn) spring up on the menu.
春季 （3 月至 5 月）鲜红小龙虾（小龙虾 ）的最佳季节——辣小龙虾，在街头食品市场出售。 元宵节会看到热气腾腾的元宵 ，这是一种由糯米粉制成的软球甜点。竹笋（ 笋 ; 桑） 在菜单上弹出。

Summer (June–August) Watermelons, hami melons and grapes slake a national thirst. Zòngzi (粽子) – sweet parcels of glutinous rice wrapped in vine leaves – are devoured during the Dragon Boat Festival in June.
夏季 （6 月至 8 月）：西瓜、 哈米瓜和葡萄可以满足全国的口渴。粽子 （ 粽子 ）——用藤叶包裹的糯米甜包——在六月端午节期间被吞噬。

Autumn (September–October) October is a tip-top month to corner hairy crabs (大闸蟹; dàzháxiè) with your chopsticks in Shanghai. The land is awash with moon cakes for the eponymous festival in September. Lotus roots (藕; ǒu) are in season.
秋季 （9 月至 10 月）十月是大闸蟹（大闸蟹 ;dàzháxiè）在上海用筷子。这片土地上到处都是九月同名节日的月饼。莲藕（ 藕 ;ǒu）是当季的。

Winter (November–February) Street sellers roast up warming tángchǎo lìzi (糖炒栗子) – sweetened, roast chestnuts – and baked sweet potatoes (烤红薯; kǎohóngshǔ) steam from every street corner.
冬季 （11 月至 2 月）：街头小贩烤糖炒栗子（糖炒栗子 ）和烤红薯（ 烤红薯 ;kǎohóngshǔ）从每个街角冒出蒸汽。

STREET FOOD
街头小吃

Snacking your way around China is a fine way to sample the different flavours of the land while on the move. Most towns have a street market or a night market (夜市; yèshì), a great place for good-value snacks and meals. You can either take it away or park yourself on a wobbly stool and grab a beer.
在中国各地吃零食是在旅途中品尝这片土地不同风味的好方法。大多数城镇都有街头市场或夜市（ 夜市 ;yèshì），是享用物超所值小吃和餐点的好地方。你可以把它拿走，也可以把自己停在摇摇晃晃的凳子上喝杯啤酒。

Street markets such as Kaifeng’s boisterous night market abound with choices you may not find in restaurants. Vocal vendors will be forcing their tasty creations on you, but you can also see what other people are buying and what’s being cooked up. All you have to do is join the queue and point.
开封热闹的夜市等街头市场充满了您在餐馆里找不到的选择。有声的供应商会把他们的美味佳肴强加给你，但你也可以看到其他人在买什么以及正在制作什么。您所要做的就是加入队列并指向。

Real Chinese Food
真正的中国菜

Because the nation so skilfully exported its cuisine abroad, your very first impressions of China were probably via your taste buds. Chinatowns the world over teem with the aromas of Chinese cuisine, ferried overseas by China’s versatile and hard-working cooks; Sundays often see diners flocking to them for ‘yum cha’ and feasts of dim sum. Chinese food is indeed a wholesome and succulent point of contact between an immigrant Chinese population and everyone else.
因为这个国家如此巧妙地将美食出口到国外，你对中国的第一印象可能是通过你的味蕾。世界各地的唐人街都弥漫着中国美食的香气，这些香气由中国多才多艺、勤奋的厨师运往海外;周日，食客经常蜂拥而至，享用“yum cha”和点心盛宴。中国菜确实是华人移民与其他人之间有益健康、多汁的接触点。

Despite the widening of overseas Chinese culinary horizons responding to the growth of Chinese tourism abroad and a wider diaspora of Chinese living in the West, what you see – and taste – abroad remains a wafer-thin slice of a very hefty and wholesome pie. Chinese cuisine in the West was traditionally lifted from the cookbook of an emigrant community that originated mainly from China’s southern seaboard. In a similar vein, the sing-song melodies of Cantonese were the most familiar of China’s languages in Chinatowns, even though the dialect finds little traction in China beyond Hong Kong, Macau, Guangdong, parts of Guangxi and KTV parlours nationwide. You may be hard-pressed to avoid dim sum and cha siu in your local Chinatown, but finding more ‘obscure’ specialities from elsewhere in China may still be a challenge (albeit not as hard as it once was), though you will pay a hefty premium for the experience. Nothing beats coming to China, however, to dine on regional delicacies on home turf.
尽管随着华人海外旅游业的增长和居住在西方的华人侨民的扩大，海外华人烹饪视野不断扩大，但你在国外看到和品尝到的仍然是一个非常庞大而有益健康的馅饼中的薄片。西方的中国菜传统上是从主要起源于中国南部沿海的移民社区的食谱中提取的。同样，粤语的歌声旋律是唐人街中最熟悉的中国语言，尽管这种方言在香港、澳门、广东、广西部分地区和全国的 KTV 厅之外，在中国几乎没有受到关注。您可能很难避免在当地的唐人街吃点心和茶烧 ，但从中国其他地方找到更多“默默无闻”的特色菜可能仍然是一个挑战（尽管不像以前那么难），尽管您将为这种体验支付高昂的溢价。然而，没有什么比来到中国在本土享用当地美食更好的了。

To get an idea of the size of its diverse menu, remember that China is not that much smaller than Europe. Just as Europe is a patchwork of different nation states, languages, cultural traditions and climates, China is also a smorgasbord of dialects, languages, ethnic minorities and extreme geographic and climatic differences, despite the common Han Chinese cultural glue.
要了解其多样化菜单的规模，请记住，中国并不比欧洲小多少。正如欧洲是不同民族国家、语言、文化传统和气候的拼凑而成一样，尽管汉族文化有着共同的粘合剂，但中国也是方言、语言、少数民族以及极端地理和气候差异的大杂烩。

The sheer size of the land, the strength of local culture, and differences in geography and altitude mean there’s little in common between the cuisines of Xinjiang and Tibet, even though they are adjacent to each other. Following your nose (and palate) around China is one of the exciting ways to journey the land, so pack a sense of culinary adventure along with your travelling boots.
土地幅员辽阔，当地文化的浓厚，地理和海拔的差异，意味着新疆和西藏的美食几乎没有共同之处，尽管它们彼此相邻。跟随您的鼻子（和味觉）环游中国是环游这片土地的令人兴奋的方式之一，因此请带上您的旅行靴来体验美食冒险。

Local Specialities
当地特产

China’s diverse regional cooking styles have undergone a long process of evolution, influenced by climate, the distribution of crop and animal varieties, topography, proximity to the sea, the influence of neighbouring nations and cultures and the arrival of ingredients and flavours from overseas. It’s almost impossible to know where to start breaking it all down, but naturally seafood is prevalent in coastal regions of China, while in the landlocked and mighty northwest there is a dependence on meat such as beef and lamb. In between those two poles is as varied a selection of regional cuisine as you could imagine.
中国多样化的地区烹饪风格经历了漫长的演变过程，受到气候、农作物和动物品种分布、地形、邻近大海、邻国和文化的影响以及来自海外的食材和风味的影响。几乎不可能知道从哪里开始分解，但自然海鲜在中国沿海地区很普遍，而在内陆和强大的西北地区则依赖牛肉和羊肉等肉类。在这两极之间，有您能想象到的各种地方美食。

Eight Schools of Cooking
八大烹饪流派

Another crucial ingredient in the evolution of China’s wide-ranging regional cuisines is history. The flight of the Song court south of the Yangzi River (Cháng Jiāng) from northern Jurchen invaders in the 12th century helped develop China’s major regional cuisines. This process was further influenced by urbanisation, itself made possible by the commercialisation of agriculture and food distribution. This led to the emergence of the restaurant industry and the further consolidation of regional schools. Further impetus came from the merchants and bureaucrats who travelled the land, and from improved transport options such as the Grand Canal, which allowed for the shipping of ingredients and recipes between Beijing in the north and Hangzhou further south.
中国广泛地方美食演变的另一个重要因素是历史。12 世纪，宋朝宫廷在扬子江以南（Cháng Jiāng）逃离了女真北部的侵略者，这有助于中国主要地方美食的发展。这一过程进一步受到城市化的影响，而城市化本身是由于农业和粮食分配的商业化而成为可能。这导致了餐饮业的出现和地区学校的进一步整合。进一步的推动力来自在这片土地上旅行的商人和官僚，以及改善的交通选择，例如大运河，它允许在北部的北京和更南的杭州之间运输食材和食谱。

Many Chinese regions lay claim to their own culinary conventions, which may overlap and cross-pollinate. The cooking traditions of China’s ethnic minorities aside, Han cooking has traditionally been divided into eight schools (中华八大菜系; zhōnghuá bādàcàixì):
中国许多地区都声称拥有自己的烹饪惯例，这些习俗可能会重叠和异花授粉。撇开中国少数民族的烹饪传统不谈，汉族烹饪传统上分为八个流派（ 中华八大菜系 ;zhōnghuá bādàcàixì）：

Chuān (川; Sichuan cuisine)
川 （ 川 ;川菜）

Huī (徽; Anhui cuisine)
徽 （ 徽 ;安徽菜）

Lǔ (鲁; Shandong cuisine)
卢 （ 鲁 ;山菜）

Mǐn (闽; Fujian cuisine)
闽 （ 闽 ;闽菜）

Sū (苏; Jiangsu cuisine)
苏 （ 苏 ;江苏菜）

Xiāng (湘; Hunan cuisine)
湘 （ 湘 ;湘菜）

Yuè (粤; Cantonese/Guangdong cuisine)
粤 ; 粤菜/粤菜）

Zhè (浙; Zhejiang cuisine)
浙 ; 浙菜）

Although each school is independent and well defined, it is possible to group these eight culinary traditions into northern, southern, western and eastern cooking.
尽管每个流派都是独立且明确的，但可以将这八种烹饪传统分为北方、南方、西方和东方烹饪。

A common philosophy lies at the heart of Chinese cooking, whatever the school. Most vegetables and fruits are yin foods, generally moist and soft, possessing a cooling effect while nurturing the feminine aspect. Yang foods – fried, spicy, or with red meat – are warming and nourish the masculine side. Any meal should harmonise flavours and achieve a balance between cooling and warming foods.
无论在哪个学校，中国烹饪的核心都存在着一个共同的理念。大多数蔬菜和水果都是阴性食物，一般湿润柔软，具有清凉作用，同时滋养女性的一面。阳性食物——油炸、辛辣或红肉——可以温暖并滋养男性的一面。任何一餐都应该协调口味，并在冷却和加热食物之间取得平衡。

TIPPING
小费

Tipping is never done at cheap restaurants in mainland China. Smart, international restaurants will encourage tipping, but it is not obligatory and it’s uncertain whether wait staff receive their tips at the end of the night.
在中国大陆的廉价餐馆从不给小费。聪明的国际餐厅会鼓励给小费，但这不是强制性的，也不确定服务员是否会在晚上结束时收到小费。

Hotel restaurants automatically add a 15% service charge; some high-end restaurants may do the same.
酒店餐厅自动加收15%服务费;一些高档餐厅可能会这样做。

Travel Your Taste Buds
旅行你的味蕾

China is such a gourmand’s paradise, you won’t know when to stop. In the north, fill up on a tasty dish of wontons (húntún) stuffed with juicy leeks and minced pork, or Mongolian hotpot (Ménggǔ huǒguō), a hearty brew of mutton, onions and cabbage.
中国就是这样一个美食家的天堂，你不知道什么时候该停下来。在北方，您可以享用美味的馄饨 （húntún）， 里面塞满了多汁的韭菜和猪肉末，或者蒙古火锅 （Ménggǔ huǒguō）， 这是一种由羊肉、洋葱和卷心菜制成的丰盛啤酒。

Chefs from China’s arid northwest might slide a bowl of noodles topped with sliced donkey meat (lǘròu huáng miàn) under your nose, or pop sizzling lamb kebabs (kǎo yángròu) between your fingers. Stop by Xi’an for warming servings of mutton broth and shredded flatbread (yángròu pàomó). A dish of Lanzhou hand-pulled noodles (lā miàn) is a meal in itself.
来自中国干旱西北地区的厨师可能会将一碗面条放在你的鼻子下面，上面放着驴肉片 （lǘròu huáng miàn）， 或者在你的手指之间放上铁板羊肉串 （kǎo yángròu）。 在习安停下来，享用温暖的羊肉汤和切碎的大饼 （yángròu pàomó）。 一盘兰州手拉面 （lā miàn） 本身就是一顿饭。

In case you’re pining for something sweet, head to Shanghai for delicious honey-smoked carp (mìzhī xūnyú). Here you can also dine on more savoury helpings of steaming xiǎolóngbāo dumplings, which require considerable dexterity to consume without meat juices jetting to all compass points.
如果您想吃甜食，可以前往上海品尝美味的蜜熏鲤鱼 （mìzhī xūnyú）。 在这里，您还可以品尝到更美味的蒸饺子，这些饺子需要相当大的灵活性才能食用，而肉汁不会喷射到所有罗盘点。

Cleanse your palate with a glass of heady Shaoxing yellow wine (Shaoxing huángjiǔ) or the more delicate flavours of Dragonwell tea (lóngjǐng chá). It may not exactly give you wings, but a dose of Huangshan braised pigeon (Huangshan dùngē) will definitely give you the stamina to clamber up the misty inclines of Huangshan.
用一杯令人陶醉的绍兴黄酒 （绍兴 huángjiǔ） 或更精致的龙井茶 （lóngjǐng chá） 来净化您的味觉。它可能不会给你翅膀，但一剂黄山红烧鸽子（黄山嘟嘟） 绝对会给你爬上黄山迷雾缭绕的斜坡的耐力。

Some like it hot, and little comes hotter than the fiery flavours of Sichuan. Begin with mouth-numbing mapo tofu (mápó dòufu), followed by the celebrated spicy chicken with peanuts (gōngbǎo jīdīng). If smoke isn’t now coming out of your ears, fish smothered in chilli (shuǐzhǔ yú) should have you breathing fire. Alternatively, test your mettle with a volcanic Chongqing hotpot.
有些人喜欢热的，没有什么比四川的火辣味道更辣的了。从令人口腔麻木的麻婆豆腐 （mápó dòufu） 开始，然后是著名的花生辣鸡 （gōngbǎo jīdīng）。 如果现在没有烟雾从你的耳朵里冒出来，辣椒闷鱼 （shuǐzhǔ yú） 应该会让你喷火。或者，用火山重庆火锅来测试你的勇气。

In the south relax with morning dim sum in Guangzhou or a bowl of Cantonese snake soup (shé gēng) in one of the city’s boisterous night markets. While in Macau taste the Macanese dish porco à alentejana, a mouthwatering casserole of pork and clams.
在南方，您可以在广州享用早晨点心，或在该市热闹的夜市之一享用一碗粤式蛇汤 （shé gēng） 以放松身心。在澳门期间，您可以品尝土生菜 porco à alentejana， 这是一种令人垂涎欲滴的猪肉和蛤蜊砂锅菜。

Northern Cooking
北方烹饪

Northern Chinese cuisine ranges across a vast area that includes Beijing, Shandong, northeastern (Manchurian) and Shanxi cuisine, cooking up the most time-honoured and most central form of Chinese dining.
中国北方美食遍布广阔的地区，包括北京、山东、东北（满洲）和山西美食，是中国餐饮中历史最悠久、最核心的形式。

In this dry northern Chinese wheat belt, the accent falls on millet, sorghum, maize, barley and wheat rather than rice (the lush south takes care of that). At home in the harsh and hardy winter climate, northern cooking is rich, wholesome and rather salty. Filling breads – such as mántou (馒头) or bǐng (饼; flatbreads) – are steamed, baked or fried, while noodles may form the basis of any northern meal and filling dumplings (饺子; jiǎozi) are widely eaten – usually boiled, but also fried. You can always find rice if you want it!
在这片干燥的中国北方小麦带，重点落在小米、高粱、玉米、大麦和小麦上，而不是水稻（郁郁葱葱的南方照顾了这一点）。在严酷而耐寒的冬季气候中，北方烹饪丰富、有益健康且相当咸。馅饼——如馒头 （mántou）或饼（bǐng）——可以蒸、 烤或炸，而面条可以构成任何北方餐和馅饺子（ 饺子 ;jiǎoz） 被广泛食用——通常是煮的，但也油炸的。如果你想要米饭，你总能找到它！

Beijing was the principal capital through the Yuan, Ming and Qing dynasties, so imperial cooking is a chief characteristic of the northern school. Peking duck is Beijing’s signature dish, served with typical northern ingredients – pancakes, spring onions and fermented bean paste. You can find it across China, but it’s only true to form in the capital, roasted in ovens fired up with fruit-tree wood.
北京在元、明、清三代都是主要首都，因此御制烹饪是北方学派的主要特征。北京烤鸭是北京的招牌菜，搭配典型的北方食材——煎饼、葱和豆沙。你可以在中国各地找到它，但它只在首都形成，在用果树木燃烧的烤箱中烘烤。

With China ruled from 1644 to 1911 by non-Han Manchurians, the influence of northeast cuisine (东北菜; dōngběi cài) has naturally permeated northern cooking, infusing it with rich and hearty stews, dense breads, preserved foods and dumplings.
随着中国从 1644 年到 1911 年由非汉满洲人统治，东北菜（ 东北菜 ;dōngběi cài）自然地渗透到北方烹饪中，融入了丰富丰盛的炖菜、浓密的面包、腌制食品和饺子。

Meat roasting is also more common in the north than in other parts of China. Meats in northern China are braised until falling from the bone, or slathered with spices and barbecued until smoky. Pungent garlic, chives and spring onions are used with abandon and also employed raw. Muslim Uyghur cuisine from the desert northwest is another principal cuisine, based around lamb, beef, chicken and breads and represented in restaurants across China.
烤肉在北方也比中国其他地区更为普遍。中国北方的肉类被红烧至从骨头上掉下来，或者涂上香料烧烤至烟熏味。辛辣的大蒜、细香葱和葱被放弃使用，也可以生吃。来自沙漠西北部的穆斯林维吾尔菜是另一种主要菜系，以羊肉、牛肉、鸡肉和面包为主，在中国各地的餐馆都有代表。

The nomadic and carnivorous diet of the Mongolians also permeates northern cooking, most noticeably in the Mongolian hotpot and the Mongolian barbecue. Milk from nomadic herds of cattle, goats and horses has also crept into northern cuisine – as yogurts (suānnǎi), for example.
蒙古人的游牧和肉食性饮食也渗透到北方烹饪中，最明显的是蒙古火锅和蒙古烧烤。来自牛、山羊和马的游牧牛群的牛奶也已潜入北方美食中——例如酸奶（suānnǎi）。

Southern Cooking
南方烹饪

The southern Chinese – particularly the entrepreneurial and itinerant Cantonese – historically spearheaded successive waves of immigration overseas, leaving aromatic constellations of Chinatowns around the world. Consequently, much of the world most often associates this school of cooking with China.
华人南方人——尤其是富有创业精神和流动性的广东人——历来引领了连续一批海外移民浪潮，在世界各地留下了芳香四溢的唐人街星座。因此，世界上大部分地区最常将这种烹饪流派与中国联系在一起。

Typified by Cantonese (粤菜; yuècài) cooking, southern cooking avoids the richness and saltiness of northern cooking to instead coax subtle aromas to the surface. The Cantonese believe good cooking does not need much flavouring and the xiān (natural freshness) of ingredients marks culinary perfection. A near-obsessive attention to freshness of ingredients in southern cuisine ensues.
以粤菜（ 粤菜 ;yuècài）烹饪，南方烹饪避免了北方烹饪的浓郁和咸味，而是将微妙的香气哄到表面。广东人认为，好的烹饪不需要太多的调味，食材的天然新鲜度标志着烹饪的完美。随之而来的是对南方美食中食材新鲜度的近乎痴迷的关注。

The hallmark Cantonese dish is dim sum (点心; Mandarin: diǎnxīn). Yum cha (literally ‘drink tea’) – another name for dim sum dining – in Guangzhou and Hong Kong can be enjoyed on any day of the week. Dishes, often in steamers, are wheeled around on trolleys so you can see what’s available to order. Well-known dim sum dishes include guōtiē (a kind of fried dumpling), shāomài (a kind of open pork dumpling), chāshāobāo (pork-filled bun) and chūnjuǎn (spring rolls).
标志性的粤菜是点心（ 点心 ;普通话：diǎnxīn）。在广州和香港，Yum cha（字面意思是“喝茶”）——点心餐饮的别称——可以在一周中的任何一天享用。菜肴通常装在蒸锅中，在手推车上推来转去，这样你就可以看到可以点什么。著名的点心菜肴包括 guōtiē（一种煎饺子）、shāomài（一种开放式猪肉饺子）、chāshāobāo（猪肉包）和春卷（chūnjuǎn）。

Fujian (闽菜; mǐncài) cuisine is another important southern cooking style, with its emphasis on light flavours and, due to the province’s proximity to the East China Sea, seafood.
福建（ 闽菜 ;mǐncài）美食是另一种重要的南方烹饪风格，强调清淡的口味，并且由于该省靠近东海，因此以海鲜为特色。

Hakka cuisine from the disparate and migratory Hakka folk (Kèjiāzú) is another feature of southern Chinese cooking, as is the food of Cháozhōu in eastern Guangdong.
来自异地和迁徙客家民（Kèjiāzu）的客家菜是中国南方烹饪的另一个特色，广东省的茶州美食也是如此。

Sparkling paddy fields glitter across the south – the humid climate, plentiful rainfall and well-irrigated land means that rice has been farmed here since the Chinese first populated the region during the Han dynasty (206 BC–AD 220). It’s the staple of southern dishes.
波光粼粼的稻田在南方闪闪发光——潮湿的气候、充足的降雨和灌溉良好的土地意味着自汉代（公元前 206 年至公元 220 年）中国人首次定居该地区以来，这里就一直在种植水稻。它是南方菜肴的主食。

Western Cooking
西餐

The cuisine of landlocked western China, a region heavily dappled with ethnic shades and contrasting cultures, welcomes the diner to the scarlet end of the culinary spectrum. The trademark ingredient here is the fiercely hot red chilli, a potent firecracker of flavour that floods dishes with an allpervading spiciness. Aniseed, coriander, garlic and peppercorns are chucked in for good measure to add extra pungency and bite.
中国西部内陆的美食，一个充满民族色彩和对比鲜明文化的地区，欢迎食客来到烹饪光谱的猩红色一端。这里的标志性成分是炽热的红辣椒，这是一种浓郁的鞭炮，让菜肴充满无处不在的辛辣味。茴香、香菜、大蒜和胡椒粒被很好地加入，以增加额外的辛辣味和咬合力。

The trademark cuisine of the western school is fiery Sichuan (川菜; chuāncài) food, renowned for its eye-watering peppery aromas, and marked by the use of ‘flower pepper’ (huājiāo), a numbing, peppercorn-like herb that floods the mouth with an anaesthetising fragrance in a culinary effect termed málà (numb and hot). The delicious sour cabbage fish soup (酸菜鱼; suāncàiyú) features wholesome fish chunks in a spicy broth. The Chongqing hotpot is a force to be reckoned with, but must be approached with a stiff upper lip (and copious amounts of liquid refreshment). If you want a hotpot pitched between spicy and mild, select a yuanyang hotpot (yuānyāng huǒgūo), a vessel divided yin-yang style into two different compartments for different soup bases. Sichuan restaurants are everywhere in China – swarming around train stations, squeezed away down food streets, or squished into street markets.
西派的标志性美食是火辣四川（ 川菜 ; 川卡） 食物，以其令人眼花缭乱的胡椒香气而闻名，并以使用“花椒”（huājiāo）为标志，这是一种麻木的、类似胡椒的草本植物，在一种称为 málà（麻木和热）的烹饪效果中充满麻醉香味。美味的酸菜鱼汤（ 酸菜鱼 ;suāncàiyú）在辛辣的肉汤中加入有益健康的鱼块。重庆火锅是一股不可忽视的力量，但必须上唇僵硬（和大量的液体茶点）来接近。如果你想要一个介于辛辣和温和之间的火锅，可以选择鸳鸯火锅（yuānyāng huǒgūo），这是一种将阴阳风格分为两个不同隔间的容器，用于不同的汤底。四川餐馆在中国随处可见——蜂拥而至的火车站周围，挤在美食街上，或者挤进街头市场。

Dishes from Hunan (湘菜; xiāngcài) are similarly pungent, with a heavy reliance on chilli. Unlike Sichuan food, flower pepper is not employed and instead spicy flavours are often sharper, fiercer and more to the fore.
来自湖南的菜肴（ 湘菜 ;xiāngcài）同样辛辣，严重依赖辣椒。与川菜不同，花椒不使用，而是辣味通常更尖锐、更猛烈、更突出。

In the far west, Tibetan cuisine includes tsampa (porridge of roasted barley flour), bö cha (yak-butter tea), momos (dumplings filled with vegetables or yak meat), thugpa (noodles with meat), thenthuk (fried noodle squares) and shemdre (rice, potato and yak-meat curry).
在遥远的西部，藏菜包括糌粑 （烤大麦粉粥）、bö cha（牦牛油茶）、momos（蔬菜或牦牛肉馅饺子）、thugpa（肉面条）、thenthuk（炒面块）和 shemdre（米饭、土豆和牦牛肉咖喱）。

Eastern Cooking
东方烹饪

The eastern school of Chinese cuisine lies in a fertile region, slashed by waterways and canals, glistening with lakes, fringed by a long coastline and nourished by a subtropical climate. Generally more oily and sweeter than other Chinese schools, the eastern school revels in fish and seafood, reflecting its geographical proximity to major rivers and the sea. Fish is usually qīngzhēng (清蒸; steamed) but can be stir-fried, pan-fried or grilled. Hairy crabs (dàzháxiè) are a Shanghai speciality between October and December, devoured with soy, ginger and vinegar and downed with warm Shaoxing wine – the crab increases the body’s yin (coldness), so yang (warmth) is added by imbibing lukewarm rice wine.
中国菜的东方流派地处肥沃的地区，水道和运河环绕，湖泊波光粼粼，海岸线绵长，亚热带气候滋养。东方学派通常比其他中国学校更油腻、更甜，陶醉于鱼类和海鲜，这反映了其地理位置靠近主要河流和海洋。鱼通常是清蒸 ，但也可以炒、煎或烤。大闸蟹（dàzháxiè）是上海的特产，在 10 月至 12 月期间，用酱油、生姜和醋一起食用，然后用温热的绍兴酒蘸上温热的酒——大闸蟹可以增加身体的阴（寒），所以喝温黄酒可以增加阳（温暖）。

As with Cantonese food, freshness is a key ingredient in the cuisine, and sauces and seasonings are only employed to augment essential flavours. Stir-frying and steaming are also used, the latter with Shanghai’s famous xiǎolóngbāo – steamer buns filled with nuggets of pork or crab swimming in a scalding meat broth. Learning how to devour these carefully without the meat juice squirting everywhere and scalding the roof of your mouth (or blinding your neighbour) requires dexterity.
与粤菜一样，新鲜度是菜肴的关键成分，酱汁和调味料仅用于增强基本风味。还使用炒菜和蒸煮，后者搭配上海著名的 xiǎolóngbāo——蒸笼子里装满了猪肉块或螃蟹块，在滚烫的肉汤中游动。学习如何小心地吞噬这些，而不会让肉汁喷到各处并烫伤你的上颚（或弄瞎你的邻居），需要灵巧。

Jiangsu (苏菜; sūcài) – one of the core regions of the eastern school – is famed as the ‘Land of Fish and Rice’, a tribute to its abundance of food and produce. A culture of epicurism and gastronomic enjoyment prevails here.
江苏（ 苏菜 ; 苏卡伊 ）– 东方学派的核心地区之一 – 被誉为“鱼米之乡”，这是对其丰富的食物和农产品的致敬。这里盛行着伊壁尔主义和美食享受的文化。

South of Jiangsu, Zhejiang (浙菜; zhècài) cuisine is another cornerstone of eastern cooking. With a lightness of flavour, Anhui (徽菜; huīcài) cuisine puts less emphasis on seafood. Braising and stewing of vegetables and wildlife from its mountainous habitats is a pronounced feature of this regional cuisine.
江苏以南，浙江（ 浙菜 ;zhècài）美食是东方烹饪的另一个基石。味道清淡，安徽（ 徽菜 ; 惠卡伊 ）美食不太强调海鲜。炖煮来自山区栖息地的蔬菜和野生动物是这种当地美食的一个显着特点。

China’s best soy sauce is also produced in the eastern provinces, and the technique of braising meat using soy sauce, sugar and spices was perfected here. Meat cooked in this manner takes on a dark mauve hue auspiciously described as ‘red’, a colour associated with good fortune.
中国最好的酱油也产自东部省份，用酱油、糖和香料炖肉的技术在这里得到了完善。以这种方式烹制的肉呈现出深紫红色调，被吉祥地描述为“红色”，这种颜色与好运有关。

How to Eat & Drink
如何吃喝

When dining together the Chinese tend to eat in a communal fashion, with all dishes arriving on the table at roughly the same time and everyone sharing from each plate, rather than ordering their own dish. Tables often tend to be large and banquet style, to accommodate large numbers of diners. In this case, dishes may be laid on a lazy Susan, to be revolved. Diners will tend to fill the bowls of their neighbours or guests, out of courtesy.
当一起用餐时，中国人倾向于以公共方式用餐，所有菜肴大致同时上桌，每个人都从每个盘子里分享，而不是点自己的菜。餐桌通常偏大且呈宴会风格，以容纳大量食客。在这种情况下，盘子可能会放在懒惰的苏珊身上，进行旋转。出于礼貌，食客往往会把邻居或客人的碗装满。

Large Chinese restaurants are often bright and flooded with light, rather than concocting a darker, more subdued dining environment, though more romantic restaurants can be found.
大型中餐馆通常明亮且光线充足，而不是营造更黑暗、更柔和的用餐环境，但可以找到更浪漫的餐厅。

Table Manners
餐桌礼仪

Chinese meals are generally relaxed affairs with no strict rules of etiquette. Meals can commence in a Confucian vein before spiralling into total Taoist mayhem, fuelled by incessant toasts with báijiǔ (a white spirit) or beer.
中餐一般都是轻松的活动，没有严格的礼仪规则。用餐可以以儒家风格开始，然后进入彻底的道教混乱，由不停地用白酒 （一种白酒）或啤酒祝酒。

Meals typically unfold with one person ordering on behalf of a group. When a group dines, a selection of dishes is ordered for everyone to share rather than individual diners ordering a dish just for themselves. It is common practice and not impolite (unless messy) to use your own chopsticks to serve yourself straight from each dish. Soup may appear midway through the meal, or at the end. Rice often arrives at the end of the meal; if you would like it earlier, just ask. Chinese diners will often slurp their noodles quite noisily, which is not considered impolite.
用餐通常由一个人代表团体点餐。当团体用餐时，会订购精选菜肴供每个人分享，而不是个别食客只为自己点一道菜。使用自己的筷子直接从每道菜中为自己上菜是一种常见的做法，而不是不礼貌的（除非凌乱）。汤可能会在用餐中途或结束时出现。米饭通常在饭后端上;如果您想早点，请询问。中国食客经常会大声地啜饮面条，这并不被认为是不礼貌的。

It is good form to fill your neighbours’ tea cups or beer glasses when they are empty. To serve yourself tea or any other drink without serving others first is bad form. Appreciation to the pourer is indicated by gently tapping the middle finger on the table.
当邻居的茶杯或啤酒杯空了时，最好把它们倒满。不先为他人服务而为自己端茶或任何其他饮料是不好的形式。用中指轻轻敲击桌子来表示对倒酒者的赞赏。

When your teapot needs a refill, signal this to the waiter by simply taking the lid off the pot.
当您的茶壶需要补充时，只需取下茶壶的盖子即可向服务员发出信号。

Don’t insist on paying for the bill if someone else is tenaciously determined to pay – usually the person who invited you to dinner. By all means offer to pay, but then raise your hands in mock surrender when resistance is met. To pay for a meal when another person is determined to do so is to make them lose face.
如果其他人顽强地决心支付账单——通常是邀请您共进晚餐的人，请不要坚持支付账单。无论如何，都要提出付款，但当遇到阻力时，请举手模拟投降。当另一个人下定决心这样做时，支付一顿饭的费用，就是让他们丢脸。

Chinese toothpick etiquette is similar to that found in other Asian nations: one hand excavates with the toothpick, while the other hand shields the mouth.
中国的牙签礼仪与其他亚洲国家相似：一只手用牙签挖掘，另一只手遮住嘴巴。

DESSERTS & SWEETS
甜点和糖果

The Chinese do not generally eat dessert, but fruit – typically watermelon (xīguā) or oranges (chéng) – often concludes a meal. Ice cream can be ordered in some places, but in general sweet desserts (tiánpǐn) are consumed as snacks and are seldom available in restaurants.
中国人通常不吃甜点，但水果——通常是西瓜 （xīguā） 或橙子（chéng）——通常可以结束一顿饭。有些地方可以点冰淇淋，但一般来说，甜点 （tiánpǐn） 是作为零食食用的，很少在餐馆里买到。

When to Eat
什么时候吃

Breakfast (早饭; zǎofàn) Gets going from around 6am and is generally light, simple and over and done with in a flash. Brekkie may simply be a bowl of rice porridge (粥; zhōu) or its watery relative, rice gruel (稀饭; xīfàn). Pickles, boiled eggs, steamed buns, fried peanuts and deep-fried dough sticks (油条; yóutiáo) are also popular, washed down with warm soy milk. Breakfast at your Chinese hotel often looks like this. Coffee can be hard to find down the side streets, unless you can stumble across a cafe.
早餐 （ 早饭 ; 佐法安 ）从早上 6 点左右开始，通常很轻、简单，很快就完成了。Brekkie 可能只是一碗粥（ 粥 ;zhōu）或其水亲戚稀饭（稀饭 ;xīfàn）。咸菜、煮鸡蛋、馒头、炸花生和油条（ 油条 ;yóutiáo）也很受欢迎，用温热的豆浆冲下去。中国酒店的早餐通常是这样的。在小街上很难找到咖啡，除非你偶然发现一家咖啡馆。

Lunch (午饭; wǔfàn) Usually eaten early, from around 11.30am, either self-cooked or a takeaway at home, or in a street-side restaurant. Lunch is taken pretty seriously, but can be rushed into a short break for harried white-collar staff in big cities. It won’t ever be skipped, however. Some restaurants will close at around 2.30pm to take a break before reopening around 5pm.
午餐 （ 午饭 ;wǔfàn）通常早点吃，从上午 11.30 左右开始，要么自己做饭，要么在家里外卖，或者在街边餐馆吃。午餐是相当认真的，但对于大城市忙碌的白领来说，午餐可以匆忙休息一下。然而，它永远不会被跳过。一些餐厅将在下午 2.30 左右关门休息一下，然后在下午 5 点左右重新开业。

Dinner (晚饭; wǎnfàn) The social meal of the day, when everyone stops work (下班; xiābān) and gets together (聚一聚; jùyijù) to let their hair down or have some fun (热闹一下; rènàoyixià). Dinner kicks off around 6pm – slightly later than in the old communist days when everyone would typically down tools at 5pm and reach for their chopsticks. If you’re dining out, it can go on to the early hours, but it’s also not unusual for local restaurants to close their kitchens by 9pm, so check ahead and plan to eat earlier than you usually might.
晚餐 （ 晚饭 ; 温芬 ）一天中的社交聚餐，当每个人都停止工作时（ 下班 ; 夏班） 聚一聚 ;jùyijù）放下头发或玩得开心（ 热闹一下 ;rènàoyixià）。晚餐在下午 6 点左右开始——比旧共产主义时代稍晚，当时每个人通常会在下午 5 点放下工具并伸手去拿筷子。如果您外出就餐，可能会持续到凌晨，但当地餐馆在晚上 9 点之前关闭厨房的情况也并不少见，因此请提前检查并计划比平时更早吃饭。

VEGETARIANISM
素食主义

If you’d rather chew on a legume than a leg of lamb, it can be hard to find truly vegetarian dishes. China’s history of famine and poverty means the consumption of meat has always been a sign of status, and is symbolic of health and wealth. Eating meat is also considered to enhance male virility, so vegetarian men raise eyebrows. Partly because of this, there is virtually no vegetarian movement in China, although Chinese people may forgo meat for Buddhist reasons. For the same reasons, they may avoid meat on certain days of the month but remain carnivorous at other times.
如果您宁愿咀嚼豆类而不是羊腿，那么很难找到真正的素食菜肴。中国的饥荒和贫困历史意味着食用肉类一直是地位的标志，是健康和财富的象征。吃肉也被认为可以增强男性阳刚之气，所以素食男性扬眉吐气。部分原因在于，中国几乎没有素食运动，尽管中国人可能会出于佛教原因放弃肉食。出于同样的原因，它们可能会在每月的某些日子避免吃肉，但在其他时间仍然是肉食性的。

You will find that vegetables are often fried in animal-based oils. Vegetable soups are often made with chicken or beef stock, so simply choosing ‘vegetable’ items on the menu is ineffective. A dish that you are told does not contain meat may still mean it is riddled with tiny pieces of meat. In Beijing and Shanghai you will, however, find a generous crop of vegetarian restaurants to choose from, alongside outfits such as Element Fresh, which has a decent range of healthy vegetarian options.
你会发现蔬菜通常是用动物油油炸的。蔬菜汤通常用鸡肉或牛肉汤制成，因此仅仅在菜单上选择“蔬菜”是无效的。一道菜被告知不含肉，可能仍然意味着它充满了小块肉。然而，在北京和上海，您会发现大量的素食餐厅可供选择，此外还有 Element Fresh 等餐厅，它有一系列健康的素食选择。

Out of the large cities, your best bet may be to head to a sizeable active Buddhist temple or monastery, where Buddhist vegetarian restaurants are often open to the public. Buddhist vegetarian food typically consists of ‘mock meat’ dishes created from tofu, wheat gluten, potato and other vegetables. Some of the dishes are almost works of art, with vegetarian ingredients sculpted to look like spare ribs or fried chicken. Sometimes the chefs go to great lengths to create ‘bones’ from carrots and lotus roots.
在大城市之外，最好的选择可能是前往一座相当活跃的佛教寺庙或寺院，那里的佛教素食餐厅经常向公众开放。佛教素食通常包括由豆腐、小麦面筋、土豆和其他蔬菜制成的“模拟肉”菜肴。有些菜肴几乎是艺术品，素食食材被雕刻成排骨或炸鸡的样子。有时，厨师们会不遗余力地用胡萝卜和莲藕制作“骨头”。

If you want to say ‘I am a vegetarian’ in Chinese, the phrase to use is ‘wǒ chī sù’ (我吃素).
如果你想用中文说“我是素食主义者”，可以使用“wǒ chī sù”（ 我吃素 ）。

Where to Eat
去哪里吃饭

Chinese eateries come in every conceivable shape, size and type, from shabby, hole-in-the-wall noodle outfits with flimsy PVC furniture, blaring TV sets and well-worn plastic menus, to gilded, banquet-style restaurants where elegant cheongsam-clad waitresses show you to your seat, straighten your chopsticks and bring you a warm hand towel and a gold-embossed wine list. In between are legions of very serviceable midrange restaurants serving cuisine from across China.
中餐馆有各种可以想象的形状、大小和类型，从破旧的破旧面条装、脆弱的 PVC 家具、刺耳的电视机和破旧的塑料菜单，到镀金的宴会式餐厅，优雅的旗袍女服务员带您入座，伸直筷子，为您带来温暖的手巾和金色压花酒单。中间是大量服务丰富的中档餐厅，供应来自中国各地的美食。

As dining in China is such a big, sociable and often ostentatious affair, many Chinese banqueting-style restaurants have huge round tables, 1000-candle-power electric lights and precious little sense of intimacy or romance. Over-attentive and ever-present staff may add to the discomfort for foreigners, but remember that they are trying their best.
由于在中国用餐是一件盛大、善于交际且经常炫耀的事情，许多中式宴会式餐厅都有巨大的圆桌、1000 盏烛电灯和珍贵的亲密感或浪漫感。过度细心和无时无刻不在的工作人员可能会增加外国人的不适，但请记住，他们正在尽力而为。

Cāntīng (餐厅), Cānguǎn (餐馆), Fànguǎn (饭馆) or occasionally Fàndiàn (饭店; this term also used to mean a ‘hotel’) or Jiǔdiàn (酒店; also often means a ‘hotel’) Restaurants, serving pretty much everything, but often with a speciality, eg Chuāncài (川菜; Sichuan food), Yuècài (粤菜; Cantonese food) or Dōngběicài (东北菜; northeast food).
Cāntīng（ 餐厅 ）、Cānguǎn（ 餐馆 ）、Fànguǎn（ 饭馆 ）或偶尔 Fàndiàn（ 饭店 ;这个术语也曾经表示“酒店”）或 Jiǔdiàn（ 酒店 ，通常也表示“酒店”）餐厅，几乎供应所有东西，但通常有特色菜，例如 Chuāncài（ 川菜 ;川菜）、粤菜（粤菜 ;粤菜）或东北菜 。

Miànguǎn (面馆) Noodle bar, serving all manner of noodles, either dry or in soup.
Miànguǎn （ 面馆 ） 面条吧，供应各种面条，有干面条还是有汤面条。

Jiǎoziguǎn (饺子馆) Serves dumplings, either boiled (水饺; shuǐjiǎo) or fried (煎饺; jiānjiǎo).
饺子馆 供应饺子，水饺（水饺 ; 舒教 ）或油炸（ 煎饺 ;jiānjiǎo）。

Shāokăodiàn (烧烤店) Barbecue or grilled-meat restaurants.
Shāokăodiàn （ 烧烤店 ） 烧烤或烤肉餐厅。

Sùcàiguǎn (素菜馆) Vegetarian restaurant (often found in Buddhist temples).
素菜馆 素食餐厅 （常见于佛教寺庙）。

Yèshì (夜市) Night market; most towns will have one. Point at what you want, grab a wobbly stool, tuck your elbows in and enjoy.
夜市夜市; 大多数城镇都会有一个。指着你想要的东西，拿起一个摇摇晃晃的凳子，把你的肘部收起来，享受吧。

Kāfēiguǎn (咖啡馆) Cafe, serving coffee and usually baked goodies, too. Will normally have an English menu, but not always.
Kāfēiguǎn （ 咖啡馆 ） 咖啡厅，供应咖啡和通常的烘焙食品。通常会有英文菜单，但并非总是如此。

Xīcāntīng (西餐厅) Western restaurant.
Xīcāntīng （ 西餐厅 ） 西餐厅。

Drinking
喝

China’s most popular drink is tea (茶; chá), consumed across the nation. When you sit down in a restaurant, you will often be presented with a cup.
中国最受欢迎的饮料是茶（ 茶 ;chá），在全国范围内消费。当你在餐厅坐下时，你经常会得到一个杯子。

The Chinese were the first to cultivate tea, and the art of brewing and drinking it has been popular since Tang times (AD 618–907). An old Chinese saying identifies tea as one of the seven basic necessities of life, along with firewood, oil, rice, salt, soy sauce and vinegar. Tea is to the Chinese what fine wine is to the French: a beloved beverage savoured for its fine aroma, distinctive flavour and pleasing aftertaste.
中国人是最早种植茶叶的人，自唐代（公元 618-907 年）以来，茶叶的冲泡和饮用艺术就一直很流行。中国有句古话说，茶是七大生活必需品之一，与柴、油、米、盐、酱油、醋并列。茶之于中国人，就像美酒之于法国人一样：一种深受人们喜爱的饮料，因其细腻的香气、独特的风味和令人愉悦的回味而备受喜爱。

China has three main types of tea: green tea (绿茶; lǜ chá), black tea (红茶; hóng chá) and wūlóng (乌龙茶; a semifermented tea, halfway between black and green tea). In addition there are other variations, including jasmine (茶水; cháshuǐ) and chrysanthemum (菊花茶; júhuā chá). Some famous regional teas of China are Fujian’s tiě guānyīn, pú’ěrh from Yunnan and Zhejiang’s lóngjǐng tea. Eight-treasure tea (bābǎo chá) consists of rock sugar, dates, nuts and tea combined in a cup; it makes a delicious treat.
中国茶主要有三种类型：绿茶（绿茶 ;lǜ chá）、 红茶（红茶 ;Hóng chá） 和乌龙茶（乌龙茶 ，一种半发酵茶，介于红茶和绿茶之间）。此外，还有其他变种，包括茉莉花（ 茶水 ; 查舒） 菊花茶 ;júhuā chá）。 中国一些著名的地方茶有福建的 tiě guānyīn、云南的 pú'ěrh 和浙江的龙井茶。八宝茶 （bābǎo chá） 由冰糖、枣、坚果和茶混合在一个杯子中组成;它是一种美味的享受。

Alcohol in China
中国的酒精

China drinks more beer than any other nation – almost twice as much as the US – though its huge population is to blame for that statistic. Per capita, the Chinese drink less than half of what their US counterparts consume. Most drinking is done with meals and is an important, if not crucial, part of many business deals.
中国喝的啤酒比任何其他国家都多——几乎是美国的两倍——尽管其庞大的人口是这一统计数据的罪魁祸首。中国人的人均饮酒量不到美国人饮酒量的一半。大多数饮酒都是在吃饭时完成的，即使不是关键的，也是许多商业交易的重要组成部分。

Beer
啤酒

If tea is the most popular drink in China, then beer (啤酒; píjiǔ) is surely second. Many towns and cities have their own brewery and label, though a remarkable feat of socialist standardisation ensures a striking similarity in flavour and strength. You can drink bathtubs of the stuff and still navigate a straight line. If you want your beer cold, ask for liáng de (凉的); if you want it truly arctic, call for bīngzhèn de (冰镇的).
如果说茶是中国最受欢迎的饮料，那么啤酒（ 啤酒 ; 皮酒） 肯定是第二位。许多城镇都有自己的啤酒厂和品牌，尽管社会主义标准化的非凡壮举确保了风味和强度的惊人相似性。你可以喝浴缸里的东西，仍然可以直线航行。如果你想喝冰镇的啤酒，请要求 liáng de（ 凉的 ）;如果你想要真正的北极，请致电 Bīngzhèn de （ 冰镇的 ）。

The best-known beer is Tsingtao, made with Lao Shan mineral water, which lends it a sparkling quality. It was originally a German beer, since the town of Qingdao (formerly spelled ‘Tsingtao’) was once a German concession. The Chinese inherited the brewery, which dates to 1903, along with Bavarian brewing methods. Look out too for various forms of dark beer (黑啤; hēipí), brewed by Tsingtao and other breweries.
最著名的啤酒是青岛啤酒，由老挝山矿泉水酿制而成，赋予其起泡的品质。它最初是一种德国啤酒，因为青岛镇（以前拼写为“青岛”）曾经是德国的租界。中国人继承了这家可追溯至 1903 年的啤酒厂以及巴伐利亚的酿造方法。还要留意各种形式的黑啤酒（ 黑啤 ;hēipí），由青岛和其他啤酒厂酿造。

Several foreign beers are also brewed in China and there’s a growing market for craft brews in the wealthier cities. If you crave variety, many of the bars we list should have a selection of foreign imported beers; prices will be high, however.
中国也酿造了几种外国啤酒，在较富裕的城市，精酿啤酒市场不断增长。如果您渴望多样性，我们列出的许多酒吧都应该有精选的外国进口啤酒;然而，价格会很高。

Spirits
精神

The word ‘wine’ gets rather loosely translated – many Chinese ‘wines’ are in fact spirits. Maotai, a favourite of Chinese drinkers, is a very expensive spirit called báijiǔ made from sorghum (a type of millet) and used for toasts at banquets. The cheap alternative is Erguotou, distilled in Beijing but available all over China; look out for the Red Star (Hongxing) brand. Báijiǔ ranges across the alcohol spectrum, from milder forms to around 65% proof. Milder rice wine is intended mainly for cooking rather than drinking, but can be drunk warm like sake.
“葡萄酒”这个词的翻译相当宽松——许多中国的“葡萄酒”实际上都是烈酒。茅台酒是中国饮酒者的最爱，是一种非常昂贵的烈酒，称为白酒 ，由高粱（一种小米）制成，用于宴会上的祝酒。便宜的替代品是二锅头，在北京蒸馏，但在中国各地都有售;留意红星（鸿星）品牌。 白酒的酒精范围从温和到酒精度数的 65% 左右。较温和的米酒主要用于烹饪而不是饮用，但可以像清酒一样温热饮用。

Whiskey, red wine and cocktails are largely status symbols and consumed as such.
威士忌、红酒和鸡尾酒在很大程度上是身份的象征，并因此消费。
