Why Go?
为什么要去？

A zip – and an entire world – away from Shanghai, wellirrigated Jiangsu (江苏, Jiāngsū) spills over with as much charm and history as the waters that flow through its shimmering canals. The province, which owed its historical wealth to silk and salt production, boasts the Grand Canal as well as elaborate waterways that thread through this Yangzi River (Cháng Jiāng) region. It’s known throughout China for its cute canal towns, enchanting gardens and sophisticated opera and folk arts.
江苏（江苏 ，该省的历史财富归功于丝绸和盐的生产，拥有大运河以及贯穿扬子江（Cháng Jiāng）地区的精致水道。它以其可爱的运河小镇、迷人的花园以及精致的戏曲和民间艺术而闻名于中国。

Tourists descend on Suzhou all four seasons of the year, but kick-start your day early, go slightly off the main streets, and you’ll see the old-world charm and have the place to yourself. In the lovely provincial capital and university town of Nanjing there’s a lot that remains relatively undiscovered by outsiders: Ming-dynasty heritage, leafy boulevards, superb museums and some fantastic restaurants.
一年四季都有游客来到苏州，但要早点开始新的一天，稍微偏离主要街道，你会看到旧世界的魅力，并拥有属于自己的地方。在美丽的省会和大学城南京，有很多东西仍然不为外人所发现：明代遗产、绿树成荫的林荫大道、一流的博物馆和一些一流的餐厅。

History
历史

Jiangsu was a relative backwater until the Song dynasty (960–1279), when it emerged as an important commercial centre as trading routes were opened up by the Grand Canal. In particular, the south of the province flourished: the towns of Suzhou and Yangzhou played an important role in silk production, overseen by a large mercantile class.
江苏在宋代（960-1279 年）之前一直是一个相对偏远的地区，当时随着大运河开辟贸易路线，江苏成为重要的商业中心。特别是该省南部的繁荣：苏州和扬州镇在丝绸生产方面发挥了重要作用，由庞大的商业阶层监督。

Prosperity continued through the Ming and Qing dynasties, and with the incursion of westerners into China in the 1840s, southern Jiangsu opened up to western influence. During the catastrophic Taiping Rebellion (1851–64), the Taiping established Nanjing as their quasi-Christian capital, naming it Tianjing (天京, Tiānjīng, Heavenly Capital).
繁荣持续到明清两代，随着 1840 年代西方人入侵中国，苏南向西方的影响开放。在灾难性的太平天国运动（1851-64 年）期间，太平天国将南京定为准基督教首都，并将其命名为天京（ 天京 ，天京，天都）。

Today, proximity to Shanghai guarantees southern Jiangsu a fast-growing economy and rapid development, although northern Jiangsu still lags behind.
如今，靠近上海保证了苏南经济的快速增长和快速发展，尽管苏北仍然落后。

Jiangsu is well connected to all major cities in China. There are numerous flights daily from Nanjing to points around the country, as well as frequent bus and train connections. Getting to Jiangsu from Shanghai is very easy, as high-speed rail connections head to Nanjing and Suzhou.
江苏与中国所有主要城市的交通十分便利。每天有许多航班从南京飞往全国各地，还有频繁的巴士和火车连接。从上海前往江苏非常方便，因为高铁连接前往南京和苏州。

Jiangsu has a comprehensive bus system that allows travellers to get around within the province without difficulty, but travelling by train is the most straightforward and speedy way to travel between the major towns.
江苏拥有完善的公交系统，旅客可以在省内轻松出行，但乘坐火车是往返主要城镇最直接、最快捷的方式。

Many visitors only pass through handsome Nanjing (Nánjīng; literally ‘Southern Capital’) when travelling from Shanghai to Beijing (or vice versa), but the capital of Jiangsu, lying on the lower stretches of the Yangzi River, boasts a rich and impressive historical heritage. It’s also one of the cleanest and best-looking cities in China.
许多游客在从上海到北京（反之亦然）时只会经过美丽的南京（南井，字面意思是“南方首都”），但江苏省会位于扬子江下游，拥有丰富而令人印象深刻的历史遗产。它也是中国最干净、最好看的城市之一。

The major attractions are the echoes of the city’s brief, former glory as the nation’s capital during its Ming-dynasty apogee and then as the capital of the Republic of China. A magnificent city wall still encloses most of Nanjing, and elegant republican-era buildings dot the centre.
主要景点是这座城市在明朝时期作为国家首都和中华民国首都的短暂辉煌的回声。宏伟的城墙仍然包围着南京的大部分地区，优雅的民国时代建筑点缀在市中心。

The famous university town’s atmosphere is both cultured and relaxed, with wide, tree-lined boulevards and excellent museums, in a fine landscape of lakes, forested parks and rivers. The countless wutong trees afford glorious shade on sunny days and lend the city a very leafy complexion, although summer temperatures can be suffocating.
这座著名的大学城的氛围既文化又轻松，拥有宽阔的绿树成荫的林荫大道和一流的博物馆，拥有湖泊、森林公园和河流的美丽景观。无数的梧桐树在阳光明媚的日子里提供了灿烂的阴凉处，为这座城市增添了绿树成荫的肤色，尽管夏季的气温可能会令人窒息。

History
历史

During the Qin dynasty (221–207 BC), Nanjing prospered as a major administrative centre. The city was razed during the Sui dynasty (AD 589–618), but later enjoyed some prosperity under the long-lived Tang dynasty (618–907), before slipping once more into obscurity.
秦朝（公元前 221-207 年）期间，南京作为主要行政中心而繁荣。这座城市在隋朝（公元 589-618 年）被夷为平地，但后来在长寿的唐朝（618-907 年）下享受了一些繁荣，然后再次默默无闻。

In 1356 a peasant rebellion led by Zhu Yuanzhang against the Mongol Yuan dynasty was successful and in 1368 Nanjing became capital under Zhu’s Ming dynasty, but its glory was short-lived. In 1420 the third Ming emperor, Yongle, moved the capital back to Beijing. From then on Nanjing’s fortunes variously rose and declined as a regional centre, but it wasn’t until the 19th and 20th centuries that the city returned to the centre stage of Chinese history.
1356年，朱元璋领导的反抗蒙古元的农民起义取得了成功，1368年南京成为朱明王朝的首都，但其辉煌是短暂的。1420年，明朝第三位皇帝永乐将首都迁回北京。从那时起，南京作为区域中心的命运起起落落，但直到 19 世纪和 20 世纪，这座城市才重新回到中国历史的中心舞台。

The first Opium War ended in Nanjing when the 1842 Treaty of Nanking – the first of the ‘unequal treaties’ – was signed, opening several Chinese ports to foreign trade, forcing China to pay a huge war indemnity, and ceding Hong Kong to Britain.
第一次鸦片战争在南京结束，1842 年《南京条约》——第一个“不平等条约”——签署，向中国开放了几个港口进行对外贸易，迫使中国支付巨额战争赔款，并将香港割让给英国。

Not long after, Nanjing became the capital of the Taiping Rebellion, which controlled most of southern China. The Rebellion ended in 1864 after the Qing army, British army and various foreign mercenaries surrounded the city. They laid siege for seven months, before finally capturing it and killing the Taiping defenders.
不久后，南京成为了和平天国起义的首都，太平天国控制了中国南方的大部分地区。1864年，清军、英军和各种外国雇佣军包围了这座城市，叛乱结束了。他们围困了七个月，最终占领了它并杀死了太平天国的守军。

The Republic of China was established in Nanjing (then ‘Nanking’) in 1912 and Sun Yatsen was chosen as the first president.
1912年，中华民国在南京（当时的“南京”）成立，孙中山被选为第一任总统。

The Kuomintang made Nanjing the capital of the Republic of China from 1927 to 1937. This period is sometimes called the ‘Golden Decade’, a period that saw the new Nationalist Government attempt to position Nanjing as a modern capital through economic growth and some major construction projects, including the Sun Yatsen Mausoleum.
国民党在1927年至1937年期间将南京定为中华民国首都。这一时期有时被称为“黄金十年”，在这一时期，新国民政府试图通过经济增长和一些重大建设项目（包括中山陵）将南京定位为现代化首都。

The capital was shifted inland to Chongqing as Japanese troops advanced in 1937. When the troops reached Nanjing, they massacred 200,000 to 300,000 civilians in six horrific weeks, known as the ‘Rape of Nanking’. Lingering at the edge of living memory, the invasion still looms large in the identity of the city today.
1937 年，随着日军的推进，首都迁至内陆重庆。当军队到达南京时，他们在可怕的六周内屠杀了 20 万至 30 万平民，被称为“南京大屠杀”。这次入侵萦绕在人们记忆的边缘，至今仍对这座城市的身份隐约可见。

THE RAPE OF NANJING
南京的强奸

In 1937, with the Chinese army comparatively weak and underfunded and the Japanese army on the horizon, the invasion and occupation of Nanjing (or Nanking, as it was known then) appeared imminent. As it packed up and fled, the Chinese government encouraged the people of Nanjing to stay and the city gates were locked, trapping more than half a million citizens inside.
1937年，随着中国军队相对虚弱、资金不足，日军在即，对南京（或当时的南京）的入侵和占领似乎迫在眉睫。当它收拾行李逃跑时，中国政府鼓励南京人民留下来，城门被锁上，超过 50 万市民被困在里面。

What followed in Nanjing was six weeks of brutality to an extent unwitnessed in modern warfare. According to journalists and historians such as Iris Chang and Joshua Fogel, between 200,000 and 300,000 Chinese civilians were killed, either in group massacres or individual murders, during Japan’s occupation of the city. Within the first month at least 20,000 women between the ages of 11 and 76 were raped. Women who attempted to refuse or children who interfered were often bayoneted or shot.
随后在南京发生的残酷行为持续了六周，其程度在现代战争中是前所未有的。根据记者和历史学家的说法，在日本占领香港期间，有 200,000 至 300,000 名中国平民在集体屠杀或个人谋杀中丧生。在第一个月内，至少有 20,000 名年龄在 11 至 76 岁之间的妇女被强奸。试图拒绝的妇女或干涉的儿童经常被刺刀或枪杀。

MING CITY WALLS
明城墙

Unlike the lost city walls of Beijing and Shanghai, an impressive two-thirds of Nanjing’s fabulous city wall is still in place today. Measuring more than 25km, Nanjing’s imposing, five-storey Ming bastion is the longest city wall ever built.
与北京和上海失落的城墙不同，南京神话般的城墙的三分之二至今仍在，令人印象深刻。南京雄伟的五层明朝堡垒全长超过 25 公里，是有史以来最长的城墙。

Built between 1366 and 1393, by more than one million labourers, the layout of the wall is unusual for its time; rather than taking a square format, the wall zigzags around Nanjing’s hills and rivers, accommodating the landscape. Averaging 12m high and 7m wide at the top, the fortification was built of bricks supplied from five Chinese provinces. Each brick was stamped with the place it came from, the overseer’s name and rank, the brick-maker’s name and sometimes the date. This was to ensure that brick-makers were held accountable for poorly made bricks. Many of these stamps are still intact today.
这座城墙建于 1366 年至 1393 年间，由超过 100 万工人建造，其布局在当时是不寻常的。城墙没有采用方形形式，而是曲折地环绕着南京的丘陵和河流，适应了景观。防御工事平均高 12 米，顶部宽 7 米，由中国五个省份提供的砖块建造而成。每块砖都印有它的来源、监督者的姓名和军衔、制砖者的名字，有时还印有日期。这是为了确保制砖商对制作不良的砖块负责。其中许多邮票至今仍完好无损。

QIXIA MOUNTAIN
栖霞山

Founded by the Buddhist monk Ming Sengshao during the Southern Qi dynasty, Qixia Temple (栖霞寺, Qīxiá Sì; %8576 6979; 88 Qixia Jie, 栖霞街88号; Jan-Sep ¥25, Oct-Dec ¥40; h7am-5.30pm; g207) on Qīxiá Mountain (栖霞山, Qīxiá Shān), 22km northeast of Nanjing, is one of China’s largest Buddhist seminaries. Relics believed to be part of Gautama Buddha’s skull were unveiled and interred here. The mountain’s maple trees are a major draw in spring when the hills are splashed in crimson and bronze.
栖霞寺 （ 栖霞寺 ，Qīxiá Sì; % 8576 6979;88 栖霞街 88 号 ;1 月至 9 月 25 日元，10 月至 12 月 40 日元; h 上午 7 点至下午 5.30; g 207） 位于南京东北 22 公里处的栖霞山 ，是中国最大的佛教神学院之一。据信是乔达摩佛头骨一部分的舍利被揭开并埋葬在这里。山上的枫树是春天的主要吸引力，当山丘被深红色和青铜色溅起时。

The two main halls are the Maitreya Hall, with a statue of the Maitreya Buddha sitting cross-legged at the entrance; and the Vairocana Hall, housing a 5m-tall statue of the Vairocana Buddha.
两座大殿分别是弥勒殿 ，入口处有一尊弥勒佛像盘腿而坐;毗卢遮那大厅 ，里面有一尊 5m 高的毗卢遮那佛像。

Behind Qixia Temple is the Thousand Buddha Cliff (千佛岩, Qiānfó Yán). Several grottoes housing stone statues are carved into the hillside, the earliest of which dates as far back as the Qi dynasty (AD 479–502); others are from the Tang, Song, Yuan and Ming dynasties. There is also a small stone pagoda, Sheli Pagoda (舍利塔, Shèlì Tǎ), which was built in AD 601 and rebuilt during the late Tang period. The upper part has engraved sutras and carvings of Buddha; around the base, each of the pagoda’s eight sides depicts tales from the life of Sakyamuni.
栖霞寺后面是千佛岩 （qiānfó yán）。山坡上雕刻着几个石像石窟，其中最早的石窟可以追溯到齐朝（公元 479-502 年）;其他则来自唐、宋、元、明三代。还有一座小石塔舍利塔 （ 舍利塔 ，Shèlì Tǎ），始建于公元 601 年，晚唐时期重建。上部刻有佛经和佛像雕刻;在底座周围，宝塔的八个面都描绘了释迦牟尼生平的故事。

Continue northwards to admire lovely views in the scenic area behind the temple. The steep path meanders via an array of pavilions and rocky outcrops: it’s serene, so consider bringing lunch and spending time here.
继续向北行驶，欣赏寺庙后面风景区的美景。陡峭的小路蜿蜒穿过一系列凉亭和岩石露头：这里很宁静，所以可以考虑带上午餐并在这里度过时光。

Historically, Suzhou (Sūzhōu) was synonymous with high culture and elegance, and generations of artists, scholars, writers and high society in China were drawn by its exquisite art forms and the delicate beauty of its gardens. Like all modern Chinese towns, Suzhou has unfortunately endured much destruction of its heritage and its replacement with largely arbitrary chunks of modern architecture.
从历史上看，苏州（苏州）是高雅文化和优雅的代名词，中国一代又一代的艺术家、学者、作家和上流社会都被其精美的艺术形式和园林的精致之美所吸引。不幸的是，与所有现代中国城镇一样，苏州的遗产遭受了大量破坏，并被大部分任意的现代建筑所取代。

The city does still have enough pockets of charm to warrant two to three days’ exploration on foot. And the gardens, Suzhou’s main attraction, are a symphonic combination of rocks, water, trees and pavilions that reflects the Chinese appreciation of balance and harmony. History
历史

Dating back some 2500 years, Suzhou is one of the oldest towns in the Yangzi Basin. With the completion of the Grand Canal during the Sui dynasty (AD 589–618), Suzhou began to flourish as a centre of shipping and grain storage, bustling with merchants and artisans.
苏州的历史可以追溯到大约 2500 年前，是扬子盆地最古老的城镇之一。随着隋朝（公元 589-618 年）大运河的建成，苏州开始作为航运和粮食储存中心而繁荣起来，商人和工匠熙熙攘攘。

By the 14th century Suzhou had become China’s leading silk-producing city. The town’s winning image as a ‘Garden City’ or a ‘Venice of the East’ came from its medieval blend of woodblock guilds and embroidery societies, whitewashed housing, cobbled streets, tree-lined avenues and canals. Local women were considered the most beautiful in China, largely thanks to the mellifluous local accent, and the city was home to a variety of rich merchants and bookish scholars, many of whom established the garden residences that the city is known for today.
到 14 世纪，苏州已成为中国领先的丝绸生产城市。该镇作为“花园城市”或“东方威尼斯”的获胜形象来自于中世纪木刻行会和刺绣社团、粉刷成白色的房屋、鹅卵石街道、绿树成荫的大道和运河。当地妇女被认为是中国最美丽的，这在很大程度上要归功于悦耳的当地口音，这座城市是各种富商和书生的家园，其中许多人建立了今天这座城市闻名的花园住宅。

In 1860 Taiping troops took the town without a blow and in 1896 Suzhou was opened to foreign trade, with Japanese and other international concessions. Since 1949 much of the historic city, including its city walls, has vanished. A conscious beautification of the city has set the tone for the last decade or so, and areas such as Pingjiang Lu and Shantang Jie suggest the Suzhou of old.
1860 年，太平军不费吹灰之力就占领了该镇，1896 年苏州对外贸易开放，并获得了日本和其他国际租界。自 1949 年以来，这座历史名城的大部分地区，包括城墙，都消失了。有意识的城市美化为过去十年左右的城市定下了基调，平江路和山塘街等地区让人联想到古老的苏州。

GRAND CANAL
大运河

The world’s longest canal, the Grand Canal (大运河, Dàyùnhé) once meandered for almost 1800km from Beijing to Hangzhou, and is a striking example of China’s engineering prowess. Sections of the canal have been silted up for centuries and today perhaps half of it remains seasonally navigable.
世界上最长的运河大运河（大运河 ，Dàyùnhé）曾经从北京蜿蜒到杭州近 1800 公里，是中国工程实力的典范。运河的部分地区已经淤积了几个世纪，今天也许有一半仍然可以季节性通航。

The Grand Canal’s construction spanned many centuries. The first 85km were completed in 495 BC, but the mammoth task of linking the Yellow River (Huáng Hé) and the Yangzi River (Cháng Jiāng) was undertaken between AD 605 and 609 by a massive conscripted labour force during Sui times. It was developed again during the Yuan dynasty (1271–1368). The canal enabled the government to capitalise on the growing wealth of the Yellow River Basin and to ship supplies from south to north.
大运河的建设跨越了许多世纪。最初的 85 公里于 公元前 495 年完成，但连接黄河（幻河）和扬子河（长江）的艰巨任务是在公元 605 年至 609 年间由隋代大量征召劳动力承担的。它在元朝（1271-1368）再次发展。运河使政府能够利用黄河流域不断增长的财富，并将物资从南运到北。

In Suzhou, one of the best ways to view the canal is around Precious Belt Bridge Park. To get there, take bus 529 to the corner of Taihu Donglu and Dangui Lu (太湖东路丹桂路) and walk across the plaza. The modern bridge (跨河景观桥, Kuà Hé Jǐngguān Qiáo) at the end of the plaza crosses the Grand Canal, where you’re likely to see cargo boats that continue to ferry the ancient trade route. Across this bridge is the park; from here, it’s a 10-minute walk through pleasant gardens to reach the ancient Precious Belt Bridge (宝带桥, Bǎodài Qiáo; g529), which boasts 53 arches, and was once a Tangdynasty construction, although the current bridge actually dates from the Ming.
在苏州，观赏运河的最佳方式之一是在宝贝带桥公园周围。前往那里，乘坐 529 路公交车到太湖东路丹桂路的拐角处，步行穿过广场。广场尽头的现代桥（ 跨河景观桥 ，Kuà Hé Jǐngguān Qiáo）横跨大运河，在那里您可能会看到货船继续在古老的贸易路线上航行。这座桥对面就是公园;从这里步行 10 分钟穿过宜人的花园，即可到达古老的宝带桥 （ 宝带桥 ，Bǎodài Qiáo; g 529）， 拥有 53 个拱门，曾经是唐代建筑，尽管现在的桥实际上可以追溯到明代。

Dating from the Ming dynasty, the canal town of Mudu (Mùdú) was once the haunt of wealthy officials, intellectuals and artists, and even the Qing Emperor Qianlong visited six times. Today the village has become an extension of Suzhou as the city develops outwards. While it’s not as picturesque as other canal towns, it’s easy to reach from Suzhou (the metro runs here) and offers a glimpse of traditional Jiangsu canal-town heritage architecture in its bridges, ancient residences and gardens.
运河小镇木都（木都）可追溯至明代，曾是富官、知识分子和艺术家的出没之地，就连清朝皇帝乾隆也曾六次出没。如今，随着城市向外发展，该村已成为苏州的延伸。虽然它不像其他运河城镇那样风景如画，但从苏州（地铁在这里运行）很容易到达，并且可以在桥梁、古民居和花园中一睹传统的江苏运河小镇遗产建筑。

Across Quanfu Bridge and 400m east of the old town, the Temple of the God of Wealth is housed in the former residence of Shen Wansan, Zhouzhuang’s 14th-century ‘local boy made good’. The temple includes a small museum on Wansan’s life and entry is through a series of fun coin-shaped arches.
横跨泉府桥，在老城区以东 400 米处，财神庙位于周庄 14 世纪“当地小子”沈万三的故居内。寺庙包括一个关于万山生平的小型博物馆，通过一系列有趣的硬币形拱门进入。

A lovely day trip only 25km east of Suzhou, the charming and petite canal town of Luzhi (Lùzhí) has a lovely assortment of streets, alleys and centuries-old humpbacked bridges to explore.
迷人而娇小的运河小镇潞支（鹿子）是苏州以东仅 25 公里处的一日游，这里有各种可爱的街道、小巷和拥有数百年历史的座头桥可供探索。

You will only need to buy a ticket (¥78) if you wish to enter any of the sights (8am to 5pm), all of which can be safely missed. If you’re visiting in a group, a leisurely 20- or 40-minute boat ride (¥150 or ¥240) is much better value. Rides depart from the tourist centre.
如果您想进入任何景点（上午 8 点至下午 5 点），您只需购买门票（78 日元），所有这些景点都可以安全地错过。如果您结伴前往，悠闲地乘坐 20 或 40 分钟的船（150 日元或 240 日元）会更划算。游乐设施从游客中心出发。

Tacked onto the old town’s east, Luzhi Cultural Park (甪直文化园, Lùzhí Wénhuà Yuán) F is a huge, faux-Ming-dynasty complex. Admission is free and the exhibition halls, ponds, pavilions and opera stage make it an interesting place to amble.
甪直文化园（甪直文化园 Lùzhí Wénhuà Yuán） F 紧贴在老城区的东边，是一个巨大的、仿明代建筑群。免费入场，展厅、池塘、凉亭和歌剧舞台使其成为一个有趣的漫步场所。

Most people head back to Suzhou after a few hours of sightseeing. Stay the night, however, and you’ll find that evenings are relaxed and tranquil (if a little dark).
大多数人在观光几个小时后返回苏州。然而，过夜，你会发现夜晚是轻松宁静的（如果有点黑）。

老城西边的苏州芦芝华美达酒店 （ % 0512 8098 1777;甫澄北路 18 号， 甫澄北路 18 号 ;d ¥650-800; n a W s ） 是一个非常舒适的选择，尽管不是在预算端。

Luzhi is chock-full of traditional restaurants and smart cafes, perfect for enjoying a beer or coffee near the canal. 鹿芝遍布传统餐厅和时尚咖啡馆，非常适合在运河附近享用啤酒或咖啡。餐厅特色菜包括河蛤、虾、猪蹄和煮熟的软荺荺（ 菱角 ，língjiǎo，像荸荠），随处可见。

LUZHI’S LùDUāN
LUZHI'S LùDUāN

The 6m-tall statue guarding the entrance to Luzhi’s old town may resemble a one-horned qílín (scaly mythical creature with deer’s hooves) but is actually of a lùduān, an auspicious truth-seeking beast that can travel 9000km in a single day. Lùduān always know when a lie is told and can compel people to speak the truth. Telling the two mythological creatures apart is easy – lùduān have claws, while qílín have hooves.
守卫着鹿枝老城口的 6 米高的雕像，可能看起来像一个独角的奇林（长着鹿蹄的鳞片神话生物），但实际上是鲁端 ，一种吉祥的求真兽，一天可以行进 9000 公里。Lùduān 总是知道什么时候说谎，并且可以迫使人们说真话。区分这两种神话生物很容易——lùduān 有爪子，而 qílín 有蹄子。

Luzhi and the word lùduān are spelled with the same first character, 甪, which is also the shape that the town’s river makes on a map if you squint.
Luzhi 和 lùduān 这个词的拼写是相同的第一个字符 甪 ，这也是该镇的河流眯着眼睛在地图上形成的形状。
