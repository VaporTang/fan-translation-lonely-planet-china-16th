People of China
中国人民

The stamping ground of roughly one-fifth of humanity, it would be wrong to assume China is largely homogenous. Even though Han Chinese – the majority ethnic type in this energetic and bustling nation – constitute over nine-tenths of the population, you only have to travel a bit further and turn a few more corners to come face-to-face with a diverse group of indigenous people that make an invaluable contribution to the language and culture of modern China.
作为大约五分之一人类的踩踏地，假设中国在很大程度上是同质的，那就错了。尽管汉族——这个充满活力和繁华的民族的大多数民族——占人口的十分之九以上，但你只需要走得更远一点，再转几个弯，就能与为现代中国的语言和文化做出宝贵贡献的多元化土著人民面对面。

Ethnicity
种族

Han Chinese
汉族

Han Chinese (汉族, Hànzú), the predominant clan among China’s 56 recognised ethnic groups, make up the lion’s share of China’s people – 92% of the total figure. When we think of China – from its writing system to its visual arts, calligraphy, history, literature, language and politics – we tend to associate it with Han culture.
汉族（汉族 ，Hànzú）是中国 56 个公认民族中的主要氏族，占中国人口的最大比例，占中国人口的 92%。当我们想到中国时——从它的文字系统到视觉艺术、书法、历史、文学、语言和政治——我们倾向于将其与汉文化联系在一起。

Distributed throughout China, the Han Chinese are, however, predominantly concentrated along the Yellow River, Yangzi River and Pearl River basins. Taking their name from the Han dynasty, the Han Chinese themselves are not markedly homogenous. China was ruled by non-Han Altaic (Turk, Tungusic or Mongolian) invaders for long periods, most demonstrably during the Yuan dynasty (Mongols) and the long Qing dynasty (Manchu), but also under the Jin, the Liao and other eras.
然而，汉人分布在中国各地，主要集中在黄河、扬子江和珠江流域沿岸。汉人的名字取自汉代，汉人本身并没有明显的同质性。中国长期由非汉族阿尔泰（突厥、通古斯或蒙古）入侵者统治，最明显的是元朝（蒙古人）和长清王朝（满清），但也在金、辽和其他时代。

The Han Chinese display further stark differences in their rich panoply of dialects, which fragments China into a frequently baffling linguistic mosaic, though the promotion of Mandarin has blurred this considerably. The common written form of Chinese using characters (汉字, Hànzi – or ‘characters of the Han’), however, binds all dialects together.
汉人在丰富的方言方面表现出进一步的明显差异，这使得中国分裂成一个经常令人费解的语言马赛克，尽管普通话的推广大大模糊了这一点。然而，汉字（ 汉字 ，Hànzi – 或“汉字”）的常见书面形式将所有方言联系在一起。

The Naxi created a written language more than 1000 years ago using an extraordinary system of pictographs – the only hieroglyphic language still in use today.
纳西族在 1000 多年前使用非凡的象形文字系统创造了一种书面语言——这是今天仍在使用的唯一象形文字语言。

The Non-Han Chinese
非汉族华人

A glance at the map of China reveals that the core heartland regions of Han China are central fragments of modern-day China’s huge expanse. The colossal regions of Tibet, Qinghai, Xinjiang, Inner Mongolia and the three provinces of the northeast (Manchuria – Heilongjiang, Jilin and Liaoning) are all historically non-Han regions, some areas of which remain essentially non-Han today.
纵观中国地图，就会发现中国汉的核心腹地地区是现代中国幅员辽阔的中心碎片。西藏、青海、新疆、内蒙古和东北三省（满洲-黑龙江、吉林和辽宁）等广大地区在历史上都是非汉族地区，其中一些地区今天基本上仍然是非汉族地区。

Many of these regions are peopled by some of the remaining 8% of the population – China’s 55 other ethnic minorities, known collectively as shǎoshù mínzú (少数民族, minority nationals). The largest minority groups in China include the Zhuang (壮族, Zhuàng zú), Manchu (满族, Mǎn zú), Miao (苗族, Miáo zú), Uyghur (维吾尔族, Wéiwú’ěr zú), Yi (彝族, Yí zú), Tujia (土家族, Tǔjiā zú), Tibetan (藏族, Zàng zú), Hui (回族, Huízú), Mongolian (蒙古族, Ménggǔ zú), Buyi (布依族, Bùyī zú), Dong (侗族, Dòng zú), Yao (瑶族, Yáo zú), Korean (朝鲜族, Cháoxiǎn zú), Bai (白族, Bái zú), Hani (哈尼族, Hāní zú), Li (黎族, Lí zú), Kazakh (哈萨克族, Hāsàkè zú) and Dai (傣族, Dǎi zú). Population sizes differ dramatically, from the sizeable Zhuang in Guangxi to small numbers of Menba (门巴族, Ménbā zú) in Tibet. Ethnic labelling can be quite fluid: the roundhouse-building Hakka (客家; Kèjiā) were once regarded as a separate minority, but are today considered Han Chinese. Ethnic groups also tell us a lot about the historic movement of peoples around China – the Bonan minority, found in small numbers in a few counties of Qinghai and Gansu, are largely Muslim but show marked Tibetan influence and are said to be descended from Mongol troops once stationed in Qinghai during the Yuan dynasty.
其中许多地区居住着剩余的 8% 的人口——中国其他 55 个少数民族，统称为 shǎoshù mínzú（ 少数民族 ，少数民族）。中国最大的少数民族包括壮族（壮族 、Zhuàng zú）、满族（满族 、Mǎn zú）、 苗族（苗族 、Miáo zú）、维吾尔族（Wéiwú'ěr zú）、彝族（ 彝族 、Yí zú）、土家族（ 土家族、Tǔjiā zú）、藏族（ 藏族 、Zàng zú）、回族（ 回族 、Huízú）、蒙古族（ 蒙古族 、Ménggǔ zú）、 布依族（Bùyī zú）、 侗族（Dòng zú）、瑶族（Yáo zú）、朝鲜族（朝鲜族 ， Cháoxiǎn zú）、白族 （Bái zú）、哈尼族（Hāní zú）、黎族 （Lí zú）、哈萨克族（Hāsàkè zú）和傣族（Dǎi zú）。 人口规模差异很大，从广西规模庞大的壮族到西藏少量的门巴族（门巴族 ，Ménbā zú）。种族标签可能非常不稳定：圆屋建筑客家（ 客家 ;克家）曾经被视为一个独立的少数民族，但今天被认为是汉族。少数民族也告诉我们很多关于中国各地民族的历史流动——渤南少数民族在青海和甘肃的几个县中少量存在，他们主要是穆斯林，但显示出明显的藏族影响，据说是元朝曾经驻扎在青海的蒙古军队的后裔。

China’s minorities tend to cluster along border regions, in the northwest, the west, the southwest, the north and northeast of China, but are also distributed throughout the country. Some groups are found in just one area (such as the Hani in Yunnan). Others, such as the Muslim Hui, live all over China.
中国的少数民族往往集中在边境地区，在中国的西北、西部、西南、北部和东北部，但也分布在全国各地。有些群体只出现在一个地区（例如云南的哈尼族）。其他人，如穆斯林回族，居住在中国各地。

Wedged into the southwest corner of China between Tibet, Myanmar (Burma), Vietnam and Laos, fecund Yunnan province alone is home to more than 20 ethnic groups, making it one of the most ethnically diverse provinces in the country.
位于中国西南角，位于西藏、缅甸、越南和老挝之间，仅云南省就拥有 20 多个民族，是该国种族最多元化的省份之一。

Despite Manchu culture once ruling over China during the Qing dynasty (1644–1911), possibly fewer than 50 native speakers of the Manchu language survive today, though the closely related Xibo language is spoken by around 20,000 descendants of Xibo tribes resettled in Xinjiang in China’s northwest in the 18th century.
尽管满族文化曾经在清朝（1644-1911 年）统治中国，但今天幸存下来的满语母语者可能只有不到 50 人，尽管密切相关的西博语被大约 20,000 名在中国西北部新疆定居的西博部落后裔使用。

David Eimer’s The Emperor Far Away: Travels at the Edge of China (2014) is a riveting journey through China’s periphery, from the deserts of Xinjiang and the mountains of Tibet to the tropical jungles of Xishuangbanna and the frozen wastes of far-northern Heilongjiang.
大卫·艾默尔（David Eimer）的《 遥远的皇帝：中国边缘的旅行 》（2014）是一次穿越中国外围的引人入胜的旅程，从新疆的沙漠和西藏的山区，到西双版纳的热带丛林和黑龙江极北的冰冻荒原。

The Chinese Character
汉字

Shaped and sculpted by Confucian principles, the Chinese are thoughtful and discreet, subtle but also pragmatic. Conservative and sometimes introverted, they tend to favour dark clothing over bright or loud colours, while their body language is typically reserved and undemonstrative, yet their minds are alert and attentive.
中国人以儒家原理塑造和塑造，深思熟虑、谨慎、微妙但又务实。他们保守，有时内向，倾向于喜欢深色衣服而不是明亮或鲜艳的颜色，而他们的肢体语言通常是矜持和不显露的，但他们的头脑是警觉和专注的。

The Chinese can be both delightful and mystifyingly contradictory. One moment they will give their seat to an elderly person on the bus or help someone who is lost, and the next moment they will entirely ignore an old lady who has been knocked over by a motorbike.
中国人既令人愉快，也可能神秘地矛盾。前一刻他们会给公交车上的老人让座或帮助迷路的人，下一刻他们就会完全忽略一个被摩托车撞倒的老太太。

Particularly diligent, the Chinese are inured to the kind of hours that would prompt a workers’ insurrection elsewhere. This is partly due to a traditional culture of hard work, but is also a response to insufficient social-security safety nets and an anxiety regarding economic and political uncertainties. The Chinese impressively save much of what they earn, emphasising the virtues of prudence and caution. Despite this restraint, however, wastefulness can be breathtaking when ‘face’ is required: mountains of food are often left on restaurant dining tables, particularly if important guests are present.
中国人特别勤奋，习惯了那种会在其他地方引发工人起义的工作时间。这部分是由于勤劳的传统文化，但也是对社会保障安全网不足和对经济和政治不确定性的焦虑的回应。令人印象深刻的是，中国人将他们赚到的大部分钱都存起来，强调谨慎和谨慎的美德。然而，尽管有这种限制，但当需要“面子”时，浪费可能会令人叹为观止：餐厅餐桌上经常会留下堆积如山的食物，尤其是在有重要客人在场的情况下。

Chinese people can be deeply generous. Don’t be surprised if a person you have just met on a train invites you for a meal in the dining carriage. They will almost certainly insist on paying, grabbing the bill from the waiter either with impressive subterfuge or with blinding alacrity. If you do spot what is going on, they will tenaciously resist your attempts to help out.
中国人可以非常慷慨。如果您刚刚在火车上认识的人邀请您在餐车厢用餐，请不要感到惊讶。他们几乎肯定会坚持付款，要么用令人印象深刻的诡计，要么以令人眼花缭乱的敏捷从服务员那里抢账。如果你确实发现了正在发生的事情，他们会顽强地抵制你提供帮助的尝试。

The Chinese are also an exceptionally dignified people. They are proud of their civilisation and history, their written language and their inventions and achievements. This pride rarely comes across as arrogance, however, and can be streaked with a lack of self-assurance. The Chinese may, for example, be very gratified by China’s new-found world status, but may squirm at the mention of food safety or pollution.
中国人也是一个异常有尊严的民族。他们为自己的文明和历史、书面语言以及发明和成就感到自豪。然而，这种骄傲很少给人一种傲慢的印象，并且可能带有缺乏自信的痕迹。例如，中国人可能对中国新获得的世界地位感到非常满意，但一提到食品安全或污染，他们可能会感到不安。

The modern Chinese character has been shaped by recent political realities, and while Chinese people have always been reserved and circumspect, in today’s China they can appear even more prudent. Impressive mental gymnastics are performed to detour contentious domestic political issues, which can make the mainland Chinese appear complicated, despite their reputation for being straightforward.
现代汉字是由近代政治现实塑造的，虽然中国人一直以来都是矜持和谨慎的，但在今天的中国，他们可以显得更加谨慎。令人印象深刻的心理体是为了绕开有争议的国内政治问题，这可能会让中国大陆显得复杂，尽管他们以直率著称。

China’s ‘One-Child Policy’
中国的“独生子女政策”

Disbanded in 2016, the ‘one-child policy’ (actually a misnomer) was railroaded into effect in 1979 in a bid to keep China’s population to one billion by the year 2000 (a target it failed to meet); the population of China is actually expected to peak at around 1.5 billion in the next decade. In January 2016 the regulation was officially amended to a two-child policy.
2016年解散的“独生子女政策”（实际上用词不当）于1979年生效，目的是到2000年将中国人口保持在10亿（它未能实现这一目标）;实际上，中国人口预计将在未来十年达到15亿左右的峰值。2016年1月，该条例正式修订为二孩政策。

The policy was harshly implemented at first, but rural revolt led to a softer stance. Nonetheless it generated considerable bad feeling between local officials and the rural population. All non-Han minorities were exempt from the one-child policy; Han Chinese parents who were both single children could have a second child and this was later expanded to all couples if at least one of them was a single child. Rural families were allowed to have two children if the first child was a girl, but some had upwards of three or four kids. Additional children often resulted in fines, with families having to shoulder the cost of education themselves, without government assistance. Official stated policy opposed forced abortion or sterilisation, but allegations of coercion continued as local officials strived to meet population targets. In 2014 the film director Zhang Yimou was fined US$1.2 million for breaking the one-child policy.
该政策起初实施严厉，但农村起义导致立场更加软弱。尽管如此，这在地方官员和农村居民之间产生了相当大的不愉快情绪。所有非汉族少数民族都不受一胎化政策的约束;都是单身子女的汉族父母可以生育第二个孩子，如果其中至少有一个是单身子女，这后来扩大到所有夫妇。如果第一个孩子是女孩，农村家庭可以生两个孩子，但有些家庭有三四个孩子以上。额外的儿童往往会导致罚款，家庭不得不自己承担教育费用，而没有政府援助。官员表示，政策反对强迫堕胎或绝育，但随着地方官员努力实现人口目标，有关胁迫的指控仍在继续。2014年，电影导演张艺谋因违反独生子女政策被罚款120万美元。

Families who abided by the one-child policy often went to horrifying lengths to ensure their child was male, with female infanticide, sex-selective abortion and abandonment becoming commonplace. In parts of China this resulted in a serious imbalance of the sexes – in 2010, 118 boys were born for every 100 girls. In some provinces the imbalance has been even higher. It was estimated that by 2020 Chinese men could potentially outnumber women by 35 million.
遵守独生子女政策的家庭经常不遗余力地确保他们的孩子是男性，杀害女婴、性别选择性堕胎和遗弃变得司空见惯。在中国部分地区，这导致了严重的性别失衡——2010年，每100个女孩生118个男孩。在一些省份，不平衡程度甚至更高。据估计，到2020年，中国男性的数量可能比女性多3500万。

Another consequence of the one-child policy has been a rapidly ageing population, with more than a quarter of the populace predicted to be over the age of 65 by 2050. The 2016 abolition of the one-child policy has sought to adjust these profound imbalances.
独生子女政策的另一个后果是人口迅速老龄化，预计到2050年，超过四分之一的人口将超过65岁。2016 年废除独生子女政策试图调整这些深刻的不平衡。

China has a high literacy rate of 96.4% for the whole population, a huge improvement on a literacy rate of just 78% in 1990.
中国的全人口识字率高达 96.4%，比 1990 年仅 78% 的识字率有了巨大进步。

Women in China
中国女性

Equality & Emancipation
平等与解放

Growing up in a Confucian culture, women in China traditionally encountered great prejudice and acquired a far lowlier social status to men. The most notorious expression of female subservience was foot binding, which became a widespread practice in the Song dynasty. Female resistance to male-dominated society could sometimes produce inventive solutions, however – discouraged from reading and writing, women in Jiangyong county (Hunan) once used their own invented syllabic script (partly based on Chinese) called nǚshū (女书) to write letters to each other (which men found incomprehensible).
在儒家文化中长大的女性，传统上，女性会遇到很大的偏见，并且获得了比男性低得多的社会地位。女性顺从最臭名昭著的表现形式是缠足，这在宋代成为一种普遍的做法。女性对男性主导社会的抵抗有时可以产生创造性的解决方案——由于不愿阅读和写作，湖南江永县的女性曾经使用自己发明的音节文字（部分基于汉语）称为女书 ）互相写信（男性认为难以理解）。

Women in today’s China officially share complete equality with men, though, as with other nations that profess sexual equality, the reality is often far different. Chinese women do not enjoy strong political representation and the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) remains a largely patriarchal organisation. Iconic political leaders from the early days of the CCP were men and the influential echelons of the party persist as a largely male domain. There has never been a female member of the Standing Committee of the Communist Party’s Politburo.
然而，在当今的中国，女性在官方上与男性完全平等，但与其他自称性别平等的国家一样，现实往往大不相同。中国女性没有强大的政治代表性，中国共产党（CCP）仍然是一个主要的父权制组织。中共早期的标志性政治领导人都是男性，而党内有影响力的梯队仍然以男性为主。从来没有一个女性的中共政治局常委。

Chairman Mao famously said that women ‘hold up half the sky’ and the Communist Party after 1949 tried to outlaw old customs and put women on equal footing with men. It abolished arranged marriages and encouraged women to get an education and join the workforce. By the 1970s China had the world’s largest female workforce. Women were allowed to keep their maiden name upon marriage and leave their property to their children. In its quest for equality during this period, however, the Communist Party seemed to ‘desexualise’ women, fashioning instead a kind of idealised worker/mother/peasant paradigm.
毛主席有句名言，女人“撑半边天”，1949年后的共产党试图取缔旧习俗，让女人与男人平等。它废除了包办婚姻，鼓励妇女接受教育并加入劳动力市场。到 1970 年代，中国拥有世界上最大的女性劳动力。妇女在结婚后可以保留婚前姓氏，并将财产留给子女。然而，在这一时期寻求平等的过程中，共产党似乎将女性“去性化”，而是塑造了一种理想化的工人/母亲/农民范式。

China has almost 90 cities with populations of five to 10 million people, and more than 170 cities with between one and five million people.
中国有近90个城市人口在5至1000万之间，人口在1至500万之间的城市有170多个。

Chinese Women Today
今天的中国女性

According to the 2017 Hurun Report, 49 of the world’s 78 self-made female billionaires are in China. High-profile, successful Chinese women are very much in the public eye, but the relative lack of career opportunities for females in other fields also suggests a continuing bias against women in employment.
根据2017年胡润百富榜，全球78位白手起家的女性亿万富翁中，有49位在中国。备受瞩目的成功中国女性在公众视野中非常受关注，但女性在其他领域的职业机会相对缺乏，这也表明女性在就业中持续存在偏见。

Women’s improved social status today has meant that more women are putting off marriage until their late 20s or early 30s, choosing instead to focus on education and career opportunities. This has been enhanced by the rapid rise in house prices, further encouraging women to postpone marriage (and having children) till a later age. Premarital sex and cohabitation before marriage are increasingly common in larger cities and lack the stigma they had 20 years ago. A survey by Chinese sexologist Li Yinhe revealed that 15.5% of Chinese people had sex before marriage in 1989, but by 2014 that figure had leapt to 71%.
如今，女性社会地位的提高意味着越来越多的女性将结婚推迟到 20 多岁或 30 岁出头，而是选择专注于教育和职业机会。房价的快速上涨加剧了这种情况，进一步鼓励女性将结婚（和生育）推迟到晚年。婚前性行为和婚前同居在大城市越来越普遍，并且没有 20 年前的耻辱感。中国性学家李银河的一项调查显示，1989 年有 15.5% 的中国人婚前发生性行为，但到 2014 年，这一数字已跃升至 71%。

In a sign of growing confidence among the female workforce, a young Beijing woman won the first-ever gender discrimination lawsuit in China in 2014. However, feminist activism remains an uphill struggle. After thousands of students and individuals signed #MeToo petitions in 2018 demanding action against sexual harassment, many of the petitions were deleted by censors on social media. Intense political pressure is applied to women’s right activists, partly out of fear that these rights may cross over to more general human rights concerns and partly because the Chinese state seeks to suppress and weaken all forms of organised advocacy.
2014 年，一名年轻的北京女性赢得了中国有史以来第一起性别歧视诉讼，这表明女性劳动力的信心不断增强。然而，女权主义激进主义仍然是一场艰苦的斗争。2018 年，数千名学生和个人签署了 #MeToo 份请愿书，要求采取行动打击性骚扰，但许多请愿书在社交媒体上被审查人员删除。对女权活动人士施加了巨大的政治压力，部分原因是担心这些权利可能会蔓延到更普遍的人权问题，部分原因是中国政府试图压制和削弱一切形式的有组织的倡导。
