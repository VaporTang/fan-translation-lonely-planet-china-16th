Why Go?
为什么要去？

As the birthplace of Mao Zedong, Communist Party cadres might wax lyrical about the sacred standing of Hunan (湖南; Húnán) in the annals of Chinese history, but it’s the province’s dramatic scenery that is the real draw. A magnificent landscape of isolated mountain ranges and jagged peaks envelops more than 80% of the province. The most astonishing example is found at the phantasmagorical Zhangjiajie, one of China’s most surreal national parks. Here, as in other parts of the province, geological marvels thrust up majestically from green vales fed by tributaries in the fertile Yangzi River basin.
作为毛泽东的出生地，共产党干部可能会对湖南（ 湖南 ;胡南）在中国历史史册上，但该省的壮丽景色才是真正的吸引力。孤立的山脉和锯齿状的山峰的壮丽景观笼罩着全省 80% 以上的地区。最令人惊讶的例子是在梦幻般的张家界，这是中国最超现实的国家公园之一。在这里，与该省其他地区一样，地质奇观从肥沃的扬子江流域支流注入的绿色山谷中雄伟地拔地而起。

People have long made a home amid Hunan’s natural wonders, taming the rocky slopes into terraces of lush fields, and their distinctive cultures live on in charming villages and towns, the most alluring being the historic riverside settlement of Fenghuang.
长期以来，人们一直在湖南的自然奇观中安家落户，将岩石山坡驯服成郁郁葱葱的田野梯田，他们独特的文化在迷人的村庄和城镇中得以延续，其中最诱人的是历史悠久的凤凰河畔聚落。

History
历史

During the Ming and Qing dynasties, Hunan was one of the empire’s granaries, transporting vast quantities of rice to the embattled north. By the 19th century, land shortages and feudalism caused widespread unrest among farmers and hill-dwelling minorities. These economic disparities galvanised the Taiping Rebellion in the 1850s, and ensured widespread support by the 1920s for the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) and Hunan’s Mao Zedong.
在明清时期，湖南是帝国的粮仓之一，将大量的大米运往四面楚歌的北方。到了 19 世纪，土地短缺和封建主义在农民和山区少数民族中引起了广泛的骚乱。这些经济差距激发了 1850 年代的太平天国运动，并确保了到 1920 年代对中国共产党 （CCP） 和湖南毛泽东的广泛支持。

Changsha 长沙
长沙 长沙

%0731 / Pop 4.3 million
% 0731 / 人口 430 万

For three millennia, this city on the Xiang River (湘江, Xiāng Jiāng) flourished steadily as a centre of agriculture and intellect. In the 1920s it was still so well preserved that British philosopher Bertrand Russell is said to have compared it to a medieval town, but not long after, the Sino-Japanese War and a massive fire in 1938 gave Changsha (Chángshā) an irreversible facelift, leaving little of its early history. These days it’s a modern, energetic city, known mainly for sights relating to Mao Zedong, but with its magnolia-lined streets and riverine aspect, it’s a pleasant enough stopover.
三千年来，这座湘江畔的城市作为农业和知识中心稳步繁荣。在 1920 年代，它仍然保存完好，据说英国哲学家伯特兰·罗素 （Bertrand Russell） 将其比作中世纪小镇，但不久之后，甲午战争和 1938 年的一场大火使长沙 （Chángshā） 进行了不可逆转的整容，几乎没有留下它的早期历史。如今，它是一座现代化、充满活力的城市，主要以与毛泽东有关的景点而闻名，但凭借其木兰花成荫的街道和河流景观，这是一个足够愉快的中途停留地。

旧城墙和天心阁历史遗址

(map Google map; 古城墙、天心阁, Gǔchéngqiáng, Tiānxīn Gé; 3 Tianxin Lu, 天心路3号; park free, pavilion ¥32; h7.30am-5.30pm; mNanmenkou)

The old city walls, which once stretched for 9km around ancient Changsha, were built of rammed earth in 202 BC, reinforced with stone in AD 1372 (during the Ming dynasty), damaged by the Taiping in 1852 and finally demolished in 1928, save for this imposing 251m-long section. You can enter Tianxin Park for free and wander around the old wall, but you have to pay to climb up on top of it, and to visit the attractive Tianxin Pavilion atop it.
旧城墙曾环绕古长沙 9 公里，建于公元前 202 年，由夯土建造，公元 1372 年（明朝）用石头加固，1852 年被太平天国破坏，最终于 1928 年拆除，除了这段 251 米长的雄伟部分。你可以免费进入天心公园，在老墙上漫步，但爬上墙顶，参观顶部迷人的天心亭，必须付费。

A reflective 32-year-old Mao immortalised it in ‘Changsha’, probably his best-regarded poem, after standing at its southern tip and looking west towards Yuelu Mountain one autumn day. MAO: THE GREAT HELMSMAN
毛：伟大的舵手

Mao Zedong was born in the village of Shaoshan in 1893, the son of ‘wealthy’ peasants. Mao worked beside his father on the 8-hectare family farm from age six and was married by 14.
毛泽东1893年出生于韶山村，是“富家”农民的儿子。毛从6岁起就在父亲身边在占地8公顷的家庭农场工作，14岁结婚。

At 16, he convinced his father to let him attend middle school in Changsha. In the city, Mao discovered Sun Yatsen’s revolutionary secret society. When the Qing dynasty collapsed that year, Mao joined the republican army but soon quit, thinking the revolution was over.
16岁时，他说服父亲让他在长沙上中学。在城里，毛发现了孙中山的革命秘密社团。当年清朝灭亡时，毛加入了共和军，但很快就退出了，认为革命已经结束。

At the Hunan County No 1 Teachers’ Training School, Mao began following the Soviet socialism movement. He put an ad in a Changsha newspaper ‘inviting young men interested in patriotic work to contact me’, and among those who responded were Liu Shaoqi, who would become president of the People’s Republic of China (PRC), and Xiao Chen, who would be a founding member of the Chinese Communist Party (CCP).
在湖南县第一师范学校，毛开始关注苏联社会主义运动。他在长沙一家报纸上刊登了一则广告，“邀请对爱国工作感兴趣的年轻人与我联系”，做出回应的人包括即将成为中华人民共和国国家主席的刘少奇和即将成为中国共产党创始成员的肖陈。

Mao graduated in 1918 and went to work as an assistant librarian at Peking University, where he befriended more future major CCP figures. By the time he returned to teach in Changsha, Mao was active in communist politics. Unlike orthodox Marxists, Mao saw peasants as the lifeblood of the revolution. The CCP was formed in today’s Xīntiāndì area in Shanghai in 1921, and soon included unions of peasants, workers and students.
1918年，毛学毕业，到北京大学担任图书馆员助理，在那里他结识了更多未来的中共主要人物。当他回到长沙任教时，毛已经活跃在共产主义政治中。与正统的马克思主义者不同，毛将农民视为革命的命脉。中共于 1921 年在今天的上海新天地地区成立，很快包括农民、工人和学生工会。

In April 1927, following Kuomintang leader Chiang Kaishek’s attack on communists, Mao was tasked with organising what became the ‘Autumn Harvest Uprising’. Mao’s army scaled Jinggang Shan, on the border with Jiangxi province, to embark on a guerrilla war. The campaign continued until the Long March in October 1934, a 9600km retreat that ended up in Yan’an in Shanxi province. Mao emerged from the Long March as the CCP leader.
1927 年 4 月，国民党领导人蒋介石对共产党人的攻击后，毛受命组织后来的“秋收起义”。毛的军队翻越了与江西省接壤的井冈山，开始了游击战。战役一直持续到 1934 年 10 月的长征 ，撤退 9600 公里，最终到达山西省延安。毛从长征中脱颖而出，成为中共领导人。

Mao forged a fragile alliance with the Kuomintang to expel the Japanese, and from 1936 to 1948 the two sides engaged in betrayals, conducting a civil war simultaneously with WWII. Mao’s troops eventually won, and the PRC was established on 1 October 1949.
毛与国民党结成了脆弱的联盟，驱逐日本人，从1936年到1948年，双方进行了背叛，与二战同时进行内战。毛的军队最终获胜，中国于 1949 年 10 月 1 日成立。

As chairman of the PRC, Mao embarked on radical campaigns to repair his warravaged country. In the mid-1950s he began to implement peasant-based and decentralised socialist developments. The outcome was the ill-fated Great Leap Forward in the late 1950s and the chaos of the Cultural Revolution (1966–76;).
作为中国主席，毛开始了激进的运动，以修复他饱受战争蹂躏的国家。1950 年代中期，他开始实施以农民为基础的权力下放的社会主义发展。结果是 1950 年代后期命运多舛的大跃进和文化大革命的混乱（1966-76 年;

China saw significant gains in education, women’s rights and average life expectancy under Mao’s rule; however, by most estimates, between 40 million and 70 million people died during that era of change, mainly from famine. Five years after Mao’s death, Deng Xiaoping famously announced Mao had been 70% right and 30% wrong in an effort, some say, to tear down Mao’s cult of personality. Yet today Mao remains revered as the man who united the country, and he is still commonly referred to as the ‘Great Leader’, ‘Great Teacher’ and ‘supremely beloved Chairman’. His image hangs everywhere – in public places, schools, taxis, restaurants and living rooms – but exactly what he symbolises now is the question that China grapples with today.
在毛泽东的统治下，中国在教育、妇女权利和平均预期寿命方面取得了显着进步;然而，根据大多数人的估计，在那个变革时期有 4000 万至 7000 万人死亡，主要是死于饥荒。毛去世五年后，邓小平宣布毛有 70% 是对的，30% 是错的，有人说，是为了推翻毛的个人崇拜。然而，今天，毛仍然被尊为统一国家的人，他仍然被普遍称为“伟大的领袖”、“伟大的老师”和“无上敬爱的主席”。他的形象无处不在——公共场所、学校、出租车、餐馆和客厅——但他现在所象征的正是中国今天正在努力解决的问题。

A ZHANGJIAJIE ITINERARY

If you’re stuck for ideas, the following itinerary takes in the park’s main highlights, and can be done in one long day (figure 10 to 12 hours) or two leisurely days.
如果您没有想法，以下行程将介绍公园的主要亮点，可以在一整天（图 10 到 12 小时）或两天悠闲的时间内完成。

Enter the park at Wulingyuan and take the shuttle to the Ten-Mile Gallery, where the monorail (tram) is located. Since the monorail doesn’t go very far, we recommend starting your hike here, alongside the tracks. After the monorail ends is when the steps start and the crowds disperse – count on three very sweaty hours to the top, with lots of Instagrammable overlooks along the way.

Once you reach Tianzi Shan, stop for a snack and the various viewpoints, then board a bus to the next area, Fields in the Sky. Here an electric cart (¥50) will take you out to a Tujia settlement, where you can look down on an inconceivably placed rapeseed field. There are two viewpoints here; the lower one is unmarked, so keep an eye out for the path.

Return to the bus stop and take the shuttle to Yangjiajie. From here, a path descends into a valley, then climbs back up the other through a series of narrow fissures in the rock and past another Tujia settlement, eventually reaching the top of a pinnacle, which must be ascended via a steel ladder. Don’t skip this area, unless you are afraid of heights. Figure on two hours for the return trip.
返回公交站，乘坐班车前往杨家界。从这里开始，一条小路下降到一个山谷，然后爬上另一条小路，穿过岩石中的一系列狭窄裂缝，经过另一个土家族聚落，最终到达尖峰的顶部，必须通过钢梯登上。不要跳过这个区域，除非你恐高。回程的两小时。

The next stop is Yuanjiajie, from where you can follow a 90-minute path to some of the park’s most famous vistas, including the No 1 Highest Natural Bridge and the Heavenly Pillar.
下一站是袁家界，从那里您可以沿着 90 分钟的小径到达公园一些最著名的景色，包括 1 号最高天然桥和天柱 。

From here, you can take the shuttle to the Bailong Elevator (the world’s highest outdoor lift; ¥72), descend to the valley floor, and then board another shuttle to take you all the way back to the park entrance at Wulingyuan. Be forewarned that the queues to board the final shuttle can be very long (up to an hour) at the end of the day, but you don’t have much of a choice, unless you are returning to Zhangjiajie city instead of Wulingyuan.
从这里乘坐班车到百龙电梯 （世界上最高的室外电梯，72 日元），下降到谷底，然后乘坐另一班班车，一路回到武陵源的公园入口。请注意，在一天结束时，登上最后班车的队列可能会很长（长达一个小时），但您别无选择，除非您返回张家界市而不是武陵源。

If you’d rather enter the park at the slightly quieter Forest Park entrance instead, you can follow Golden Whip Stream to either the elevator or the steps climbing up the cliff at Qiānlǐ Xiānghuì (千里相会), which leads to Yuanjiajie. 如果您更愿意从稍微安静的森林公园入口进入公园，您可以沿着金鞭溪乘坐电梯或爬上千里相会悬崖的台阶，通往袁家界。从这里出发，您可以反向旅行，但请记住，您必须在武陵源离开公园。

You begin by descending through a beautiful flat valley called Moon Valley (月之谷, Yuè Zhi Gǔ), surrounded by cliffs, before climbing to a vast cave called Cathedral Gate (教堂们, Jiàotáng Mén), after which you thread through a bamboo forest to make your way towards a 1km-long subterranean cave.

Once a frontier town, Fenghuang (Fènghuáng) marked the boundary between the Han civilisations of the central plains and the Miao (苗), Tujia (土家) and Dong (侗) minorities of the southwest mountains. Protective walls went up in the Ming dynasty, but despite the implications Fenghuang prospered as a centre of trade and cultural exchange. Its diverse residents built a breathtaking riverside settlement of winding alleys, temples and rickety stilt houses, which these days attract tourists by the bucketload. Do try to stay overnight – the town is bursting with accommodation options, and the sight of an illuminated Fenghuang at night is quite awesome.
凤凰曾经是一个边陲城镇，标志着中原汉族文明与西南山区苗族、土家族和侗族的边界。明代建立了防护墙，但尽管有这些影响，凤凰作为贸易和文化交流中心而繁荣起来。其多元化的居民建造了一个令人叹为观止的河边定居点，由蜿蜒的小巷、寺庙和摇摇欲坠的高脚屋组成，如今吸引了大量游客。一定要尽量过夜——镇上有很多住宿选择，晚上灯火通明的凤凰景象非常棒。

沿着村庄的主要街道（80 日元）漫步，沿着台阶下降到码头和脚下的游河，是一次迷人的游览。下山途中的历史建筑包括观音阁 （ 观音阁 ，Guānyīn Gé;No 105） 和铜柱馆 ;No 15）。 然而，亮点是芙蓉镇瀑布 （ 芙蓉镇瀑布 ，Fúróng Zhèn Pùbù），它将村庄分开，可以通过垫脚石穿过远处，可以欣赏到小镇最迷人的景色，尤其是在日落时分，灯光开始闪烁。准备好相机。

There are numerous hotels and guesthouses in and around the old town where you can find a double room with shower for the night for around ¥150 to ¥250. Hotels in the new town go for about ¥100.
老城区及其周边地区有许多酒店和宾馆，你可以找到一间带淋浴的双人间，晚上的价格约为150日元至250日元。新城的酒店价格约为100日元。

There are a few restaurants as you walk down the main steps to the river, with tables overlooking the waterfall, which serve up a variety of local and regional dishes. Expect to pay around ¥20 to ¥40 per dish.
当您沿着主要台阶走到河边时，有几家餐馆，桌子可以俯瞰瀑布，供应各种当地和区域菜肴。预计每道菜的费用约为 20 至 40 日元。

Buses (¥30, 90 minutes) run hourly from the bus station in Furong Zhen to Zhangjiajie between 7.30am and 5pm. It’s a dusty 2km walk from the bus station (汽车站, qìchēzhàn) to the old town (古镇, gǔzhèn). Unfortunately, the green carts whisking visitors around near the old town go to the tour bus parking lot, not the bus station.
