Why Go?
为什么要去？

Synonymous with the Silk Road, the slender province of Gansu (甘肃, Gānsù) flows east to west along the Hexi Corridor, the gap through which goods and ideas once streamed between China and Central Asia. The constant flow of commerce left Buddhist statues, beacon towers, forts, chunks of the Great Wall and ancient trading towns in its wake. Gansu offers an entrancingly rich cultural and geographic diversity. Historians immerse themselves in Silk Road lore, art aficionados swoon before the wealth of Buddhist paintings and sculptures, while adventurers hike through desert rockland, ascend sand dunes and tread along high-mountain paths well worn by Tibetan nomads. The ethnic diversity is equally astonishing: throughout the province, the local Hui Muslims act as though the Silk Road lives on; in Xiahe and Langmusi a pronounced Tibetan disposition holds sway, while other minority groups such as the Bao’an and Dongxiang join in the colourful minority patchwork.
甘肃省是丝绸之路的代名词，狭长的甘肃省（ 甘肃 ，Gānsù）沿着河西走廊东西流淌，河西走廊曾经是中国和中亚之间商品和思想流动的缺口。不断的商业流动留下了佛像、烽火台、堡垒、长城大块和古老的贸易城镇。甘肃拥有令人着迷的丰富文化和地理多样性。历史学家沉浸在丝绸之路的传说中，艺术爱好者沉迷于丰富的佛教绘画和雕塑，而冒险家则徒步穿越沙漠岩地，攀登沙丘，沿着藏族游牧民族经常穿过的高山小路行走。种族多样性同样令人惊讶：在整个省内，当地的回族穆斯林表现得好像丝绸之路还在继续;在夏河和朗木寺，明显的藏族性格占据主导地位，而其他少数民族，如宝安族和东乡族，也加入了丰富多彩的少数民族拼凑。

History & People
历史与人物

Although the Qin dynasty had a toehold on eastern Gansu, the first significant push west along the Hexi Corridor came with the Han dynasty. An imperial envoy, Zhang Qian (Chang Ch’ien), was dispatched to seek trading partners and returned with detailed reports of Central Asia and the route that would become known as the Silk Road. The Han extended the Great Wall through the Hexi Corridor, expanding their empire in the process.
尽管秦朝在甘肃东部站稳了脚跟，但沿着河西走廊向西推进的第一次重大推进是汉朝的。一位皇帝使者张骞（常钱）被派去寻找贸易伙伴，并带回了关于中亚和后来被称为丝绸之路的路线的详细报告。汉人通过河西走廊延伸了长城，并在此过程中扩大了他们的帝国。

Travellers and merchants from as far as the Roman Empire entered the Middle Kingdom along the Silk Road, and as trade grew, so did the small way-stations set up along its route; these grew into towns and cities that form the major population centres of modern Gansu. The streams of traders from lands east and west also left their mark in the incredible diversity of modern Gansu, and Buddhism found itself conveyed into China along these mercantile routes. The Buddhist grottoes at Mogao, Maijishan and elsewhere are testament to the great flourishing of religious and artistic schools along the Silk Road.
远至罗马帝国的旅行者和商人沿着丝绸之路进入中王国，随着贸易的发展，沿线设立的小驿站也随之增加;这些城市发展成为构成现代甘肃主要人口中心的城镇。来自东西方土地的商人潮也在现代甘肃令人难以置信的多样性中留下了印记，佛教发现自己通过这些商业路线传播到中国。莫高、麦积山和其他地方的佛教石窟见证了丝绸之路沿线宗教和艺术流派的蓬勃发展。

The mixing of cultures in Gansu eventually led to serious tensions, which culminated in the Muslim rebellions of 1862 to 1877. The conflict left millions dead and virtually wiped out Gansu’s Muslim population. Ethnic tensions have never fully left the province, as the pro-Tibetan demonstrations in Xiahe in 2008 and 2012 illustrate.
甘肃的文化交融最终导致了严重的紧张局势，最终导致了1862年至1877年的穆斯林叛乱。这场冲突造成数百万人死亡，甘肃的穆斯林人口几乎被消灭。民族紧张局势从未完全离开该省，正如 2008 年和 2012 年夏河的亲藏示威活动所说明的那样。

Though remote from the investment banks and manufacturing hubs along the east coast of China, Gansu is not a poor province and a palpable air of prosperity can be felt in towns such as Dunhuang. Gross Domestic Product has been growing at a higher rate than the already blistering national average and massive investments in wind energy are fuelling the transformation of both the natural and urban landscapes.
甘肃虽然远离中国东海岸的投资银行和制造业中心，但甘肃并不是一个贫穷的省份，在敦煌等城镇可以感受到明显的繁荣气息。国内生产总值的增长速度一直高于本已飙升的全国平均水平，对风能的大规模投资正在推动自然和城市景观的转变。

Gansu is home to a large collection of minority peoples, including the Hui, Mongols, Tibetans and Kazaks, though the dominant group is the Han.
甘肃是大量少数民族的家园，包括回族、蒙古族、藏族和哈萨克族，但主要群体是汉族。

LANZHOU 兰州
LANZHOU 兰州

%0931 / Pop 2.9 million
% 0931 / 人口 290 万

At China’s cartographic bullseye, Lanzhou (Lánzhōu) marks the halfway point for overlanders trekking across the country. Growing up on a strategic stretch of the Yellow River (黄河, Huáng Hé), and sitting between competing Chinese and Central Asian empires, Gansu’s elongated capital city frequently changed hands, reflected today in its mix of ethnic groups and cultures. These days, Lanzhou is perhaps most well known for its favourite food – Lanzhou beef noodles (牛肉拉面, niúròu lāmiàn) – and with several excellent night markets, this is an excellent place to sample the delights of Chinese Silk Road fare.
在中国的制图靶心，兰州（兰州）标志着陆路人徒步穿越全国的中途。甘肃长大于黄河（黄河 ，Huáng Hé）的战略地带，位于中国和中亚帝国之间，这座狭长的首都经常易手，如今反映在其民族和文化的混合中。如今，兰州最出名的可能是其最受欢迎的食物——兰州牛肉面（ 牛肉拉面 ，niúròu lāmiàn）——这里有几个一流的夜市，是品尝中国丝绸之路美食的绝佳场所。

The alluring monastic town of Xiahe (Xiàhé) attracts an astonishing band of visitors: backpack-laden students, insatiable wanderers, shaven-headed Buddhist nuns, Tibetan pilgrims in their most colourful finery, camera-toting tour groups and dusty, itinerant beggars. Most visitors are rural Tibetans, whose purpose is to pray, prostrate themselves and seek spiritual fulfilment at holy Labrang Monastery, around which Xiahe has grown up.
迷人的峡河（下河）寺院小镇吸引了一群惊人的游客：背着背包的学生、贪得无厌的流浪者、剃光头的佛教尼姑、穿着最鲜艳的服装的西藏朝圣者、拿着相机的旅行团和尘土飞扬的流动乞丐。大多数游客是农村藏人，他们的目的是在神圣的拉卜楞寺祈祷、匍匐并寻求精神上的满足，夏河就是在拉卜楞寺长大的。

In an arid mountain valley at 2920m above sea level, Xiahe has a certain rhythm about it and visitors quickly tap into its fluid motions. The rising sun sends pilgrims out to circle the 3km kora (pilgrim path) that rings the monastery. Crimson-clad monks shuffle into the temples to chant morning prayers. It’s easy to get swept up in the action, but some of the best moments come as you set your own pace, wandering about town or in the splendid encircling mountains.
在海拔 2920 米的干旱山谷中，夏河有一定的节奏感，游客很快就能感受到它流畅的运动。初升的太阳派出朝圣者绕着修道院周围 3 公里长的 kora（朝圣之路）转一圈。身穿深红色衣服的僧侣拖着脚步走进寺庙，念诵晨祷。人们很容易被卷入其中，但当你设定自己的节奏，在城镇中漫步或在壮丽的环绕山脉中时，一些最美好的时刻就会出现。

Labrang Monastery covers much of Xiahe and a ticket to the monastery includes the main buildings on a guided tour. Several other buildings have their own admission fees, and tickets for those can be purchased at small ticket offices or from attendants at the entrances. 拉卜楞寺覆盖了夏河的大部分地区，寺院的门票包括在导游的带领下参观的主要建筑。其他几座建筑也有自己的入场费，门票可以在小型售票处或入口处的服务员处购买。一般来说，修道院建筑的开放时间为上午 8 点至下午 5 点，但您可以从清晨开始在场地漫步，许多朝圣者在黎明前就开始步行科拉 ，这是加入的好时机。

With its succession of squeaking prayer wheels (3km in total), hawks circling overhead and the throb of Tibetan longhorns resonating from the surrounding hills, Labrang is a monastery town unto itself. Many of the chapel halls are illuminated in a yellow glow by flickering yak-butter lamps, their strong-smelling fuel scooped from voluminous tubs. Even if Tibet is not on your itinerary, the monastery sufficiently conveys the mystique of its devout persuasions, leaving indelible impressions of a deeply sacred domain.
拉卜楞有一连串吱吱作响的转经轮（总共 3 公里），鹰在头顶盘旋，藏长角鸟的悸动声从周围的山丘中回荡，拉卜楞本身就是一座寺院小镇。许多教堂大厅都被闪烁的牦牛油灯照亮，它们从大浴缸中舀出气味浓烈的燃料。即使西藏不在您的行程中，寺院也充分传达了其虔诚教义的神秘感，给人留下了深刻神圣的印象。

In addition to the chapels, residences, golden-roofed temple halls and living quarters for the monks, Labrang is also home to six tratsang (monastic colleges or institutes), exploring esoteric Buddhism, theology, medicine, astrology and law. No photography is allowed within the temple halls, with a ¥500 fine in some places.
除了小教堂、住宅、金顶寺庙大厅和僧侣生活区外，拉卜楞还拥有六所寺院或研究所，探索深奥的佛教、神学、医学、占星术和法律。寺庙大厅内禁止拍照，部分地方可处以 500 日元的罚款。

Labrang Monastery was founded in 1709 by Ngagong Tsunde (E’angzongzhe in Chinese), the first-generation Jamyang (a line of reincarnated Rinpoches or living Buddhas ranking third in importance after the Dalai and Panchen lamas), from nearby Ganjia. The monastery is one of the six major Tibetan monasteries of the Gelugpa order (Yellow Hat sect of Tibetan Buddhism). The others are Ganden, Sera and Drepung monasteries near Lhasa; Tashilhunpo Monastery in Shigatse; and Kumbum Monastery outside Xining in Qinghai.
拉卜楞寺由第一代蚕阳（转世仁波切或活佛，仅次于达赖和班禅喇嘛）于 1709 年创建，来自附近的甘家。该寺院是格鲁派（藏传佛教黄帽教派）的六大藏族寺院之一。其他是拉萨附近的甘丹寺、 色拉寺和哲蚌寺 ;日喀则的 Tashilhunpo 寺 ;青海西宁郊外的昆姆寺 。

At its peak, Labrang housed nearly 4000 monks, but their ranks greatly declined during the Cultural Revolution. Modern Labrang is again such a popular destination for young disciples that numbers are currently capped at 1800 monks, with about 1600 currently in residence, drawn from Qinghai, Gansu, Sichuan and Inner Mongolia.
在鼎盛时期，拉卜楞收容了近 4000 名僧侣，但在文化大革命期间，他们的队伍大大下降。现代拉卜楞再次成为年轻弟子的热门目的地，目前僧侣人数上限为 1800 人，目前居住的僧侣人数约为 1600 人，来自青海、甘肃、四川和内蒙古。

Main Buildings

The only way to visit the interior of the most important buildings is on a tour (no photos allowed inside buildings), which generally includes the Institute of Medicine, the Manjushri Temple, the Serkung (Golden Temple) and the main Prayer Hall (Grand Sutra Hall) – an amazing sight, steeped in mystery and where you may be treated to the spectacle of lamas chanting in the courtyard outside – plus a museum of relics and yak-butter sculptures. English-language tours (per person ¥40) leave the monastery’s ticket office (拉卜楞寺售票处, Lābǔléng Sì Shòupiàochù; h8am-5pm mid-Apr–mid-Oct, 9am-4pm mid-Oct–mid-Apr) around 10.15am and 3.15pm most days, and although there is plenty to see, they can feel a bit rushed. Following the 3km inner kora (pilgrim path) encircling Labrang Monastery is perhaps the best approach to grasping the giant temple’s layout, scale and significance. The kora is lined with long rows of squeaking prayer wheels, whitewashed chörtens (Tibetan stupas) and chapels. LINXIA & ITS MINORITY COMMUNITIES
临夏及其少数民族社区

Linxia (临夏; population 250,000) is a centre of Chinese Islam settled by ancient Silk Road Muslims and now populated by their descendants. At one time known as Hezhou (河州, Hézhōu), the city, which holds a strategic location at the junction of the Silk Road and several north–south trade routes meant it was, for centuries, an important commercial centre. These crossing roads brought Muslim teachers from Central Asia and further afield, some of whom stayed and went on to turn Linxia into an important centre for Chinese Islamic scholarship, particularly Sufism. The city itself is occasionally used by travellers (and monks) to break up the trip between Xiahe and Lanzhou or Qinghai. Linxia is home to more than 80 mosques and gǒngběi (拱北, Sufi master’s shrine complexes) dotted all over town.
临夏 （ 临夏 ，人口 250,000）是中国伊斯兰教的中心，由古代丝绸之路穆斯林定居，现在由他们的后裔居住。这座城市曾经被称为河州（河州 ，Hézhōu），位于丝绸之路和多条南北贸易路线的交汇处，地理位置优越，这意味着几个世纪以来它一直是重要的商业中心。这些十字路口带来了来自中亚和更远地区的穆斯林教师，其中一些人留下来，并继续将临夏变成中国伊斯兰学术，特别是苏菲主义的重要中心。这座城市本身偶尔会被旅行者（和僧侣）用来打断夏河和兰州或青海之间的旅行。临夏拥有 80 多座清真寺和拱北（拱北 ，苏菲派大师的神社建筑群）遍布全镇。

Spilling over a ridge high above Linxia and home to both Hui and Dongxiang minorities, the little market town of Suonanba (锁南坝, Suǒnánbà; population 12,000) has a single street that’s a hive of activity, with locals trading livestock and occasional shepherds shooing flocks about.

该镇有时也以周边县的名字命名为东乡（ 东乡 ，东乡）。东乡人讲阿尔泰语，据信是 13 世纪来自中亚的移民的后裔，他们在忽必烈征服中东后被强行迁移到中国。

Dahejia (大河家, Dàhéjiā; population 4500), with sweeping views over the Yellow River, towering red cliffs and (in summer) verdant green terraces, is a kaleidoscope of colour. The Bao’an are famed for producing knives and share cultural traits with the Hui and Dongxiang. Their Mongol roots are evident during summer festivals, when it is possible to see displays of wrestling and horse riding.
大河家（大河家 ，Dàhéjiā，人口 4500 人）拥有一览无余的黄河景色、高耸的红色悬崖和（夏季）青翠的绿色梯田，是万花筒般的色彩。周边地区居住着大量保安族 ，他们是讲蒙古语的穆斯林。宝安人以生产刀具而闻名，与回族和东乡族有着共同的文化特征。他们的蒙古血统在夏季节日期间很明显，届时可以看到摔跤和骑马的表演。

Straddling the border between Sichuan and Gansu, Langmusi (Lángmùsì; Taktsang Lhamo in Tibetan) is an expanding alpine Amdo Tibetan village nestled among steep grassy meadows, evergreen forests of slender pine trees, crumbling stupas, piles of mani stones, and snow-clad peaks. 横跨川甘边境的朗木斯（Lángmùsì;藏语为 Taktsang Lhamo）是一个不断扩大的高山安多藏族村庄，坐落在陡峭的草地、细长的松树常绿森林、摇摇欲坠的佛塔、成堆的摩尼石和白雪皑皑的山峰之间。这个村庄是一个令人愉快的地方，周围环绕着无数红白相间的寺院建筑、飘扬的经幡，以及黄昏时僧侣诵经的迷人声音。

Smack-dab in the middle of the Hexi Corridor, the chilled-out city of Zhangye (Zhāngyè) has a relaxed atmosphere that belies its historical status as an outpost connecting Central Asia to the Chinese empire via the Silk Road. You approach Jiayuguan (Jiāyùguān) through the forbidding and desiccated lunar landscape of north Gansu. It’s a fitting setting, as Jiayuguan marks the symbolic end of the Great Wall, the western gateway of China proper and, for imperial Chinese, the beginning of the back of beyond. One of the defining points of the Silk Road, a Ming dynasty fort was erected here in 1372 and Jiayuguan came to be colloquially known as the ‘mouth’ of China, while the narrow Hexi Corridor, leading back towards the nèidì (内地, inner lands), was dubbed the ‘throat’.
您通过甘肃北部令人生畏和干燥的月球景观接近嘉峪关（嘉玉关）。这是一个合适的环境，因为嘉峪关标志着长城的象征性终点，是中国本土的西方门户，对于中国帝国来说，是彼岸的起点。1372 年，这里建立了一座明朝的堡垒，这是丝绸之路的标志性点之一，嘉峪关被通俗地称为中国的“口”，而狭窄的河西走廊则通向内地（ 内地，内地），被称为“喉咙”。

In the town itself, you’ll need some imagination to conjure up visions of the Silk Road, as the modern city of Jiayuguan is a place of straight roads, identikit blocks and manufacturing. But Jiayuguan Fort and the Great Wall sites outside town are an essential part of Silk Road lore and most certainly worth a visit.
在小镇本身，您需要一些想象力来想象丝绸之路，因为现代城市嘉峪关是一个笔直的道路、识别街区和制造业的地方。但嘉峪关炮台和城外的长城遗址是丝绸之路传说的重要组成部分，绝对值得一游。

One of the classic images of western China, this huge fort once guarded the narrow pass between the snowcapped Qilian Shan peaks and the Hei Shan (Black Mountains) of the Mazong Shan range.
这座巨大的堡垒是中国西部的经典形象之一，曾经守卫着白雪皑皑的祁连山峰和马宗山脉黑山之间的狭窄通道。

Built in 1372, it was named the ‘Impregnable Defile Under Heaven’. Although the Han Chinese often controlled territory far beyond here, this was the last major stronghold of imperial China – the end of their ‘civilised world’, beyond which lay only desert demons and the barbarian armies of Central Asia.
它建于1372年，被命名为“天下坚不可摧的污秽”。尽管汉人经常控制着远远超出这里的领土，但这是中国帝国的最后一个主要据点——他们“文明世界”的尽头，除了沙漠恶魔和中亚的蛮族军队之外。

Towards the eastern end of the fort is the Gate of Enlightenment (光化楼, Guānghuá Lóu) and on the west side is the Gate of Conciliation (柔远楼, Róuyuǎn Lóu), from where exiled poets, ministers, criminals and soldiers would have ridden off into oblivion. Each gate dates from 1506 and has 17m-high towers with upturned flying eaves and double gates that would have been used to trap invading armies. On the inside are horse lanes with steps leading up to the top of the inner ramparts where you can wander around. Back on ground level, you can also see a former stage for performances, opposite a sizeable Guandi Temple (Guāndì Miào). Note the wall of the Wenchang Pavilion next to the Guandi Temple, which is still covered in a large, yellow and flaking Maoist slogan from the Cultural Revolution.
堡垒的东端是启蒙之门 （ 光化楼 ，Guānghuá Lóu），西侧是和解之门 （ 柔远楼 ，Róuyuǎn Lóu），流亡的诗人、大臣、罪犯和士兵都会从那里被遗忘。每座城门的历史可追溯至 1506 年，有 17 米高的塔楼，带有上翘的飞檐和双层城门，用于困住入侵军队。里面是马道，有台阶通向内城墙的顶部，您可以在那里闲逛。回到地面，您还可以看到一个以前的表演舞台 ，对面是一座相当大的关帝庙 （Guāndì Miào）。请注意关帝庙旁边文昌阁的墙壁，墙上仍然挂着文革时期的黄色和剥落的毛派标语。

The fertile Dunhuang (Dūnhuáng) oasis has for millennia been a refuge for weary Silk Road travellers. Most visitors stayed long enough only to swap a camel; but some stayed, building the forts, towers and cave temples that are scattered over the surrounding area. These sites, along with some dwarfing sand dunes and desertscapes, make Dunhuang a magnificent place to visit.
几千年来，肥沃的敦煌绿洲一直是疲惫的丝绸之路旅行者的避难所。大多数游客停留的时间足够长，只是为了交换一只骆驼;但有些人留下来，建造了散布在周边地区的堡垒、塔楼和石窟寺庙。这些遗址，加上一些矮小的沙丘和沙漠景观，使敦煌成为一个壮丽的旅游胜地。

Despite its remoteness, Dunhuang’s per-capita income is among the highest in China, thanks to a push into wind and solar energy production. The town is thoroughly modern, but has maintained its distinctive desert-sanctuary ambience – with clean, tree-lined streets, slow-moving traffic, bustling markets, budget hotels, cafes and souvenir shops.
尽管地处偏远，敦煌的人均收入却是中国最高的，这要归功于风能和太阳能生产的推动。该镇非常现代化，但保留了其独特的沙漠保护区氛围——干净、绿树成荫的街道、缓慢的交通、熙熙攘攘的市场、经济型酒店、咖啡馆和纪念品商店。

Though relatively small, it’s a clean and terrific walking town with wide footpaths and narrow alleys opening up into squares, markets and the lives of ordinary citizens.
虽然相对较小，但它是一个干净而美妙的步行小镇，宽阔的人行道和狭窄的小巷通向广场、市场和普通市民的生活。

History
历史

Wealthy traders and important officials were the primary donors responsible for creating new caves, as caravans made the long detour past Mogao to pray or give thanks for a safe journey through the treacherous wastelands to the west. The traditional date ascribed to the founding of the first cave is AD 366.
富有的商人和重要官员是负责建造新洞穴的主要捐助者，商队长途跋涉经过莫高，祈祷或感谢穿越险恶的西部荒原。第一个洞穴的传统建立日期是公元 366 年。

The caves fell into disuse for about 500 years after the collapse of the Yuan dynasty and were largely forgotten until the early 20th century, when they were ‘rediscovered’ by a string of foreign explorers.
元朝崩溃后，这些洞穴在大约 500 年内被废弃，直到 20 世纪初才被一系列外国探险家“重新发现”。

Northern Wei, Western Wei & Northern Zhou Caves
北魏、西魏和北周石窟

These, the earliest of the Mogao Caves, are distinctly Indian in style and iconography. All contain a central pillar, representing a stupa (symbolically containing the ashes of the Buddha), which the devout would circle in prayer. Paint was derived from malachite (green), cinnabar (red) and lapis lazuli (blue), expensive minerals imported from Central Asia.
这些石窟是最早的莫高窟，在风格和图像上具有明显的印度特色。所有柱子都有一根中央柱子，代表一座佛塔（象征性地包含佛陀的骨灰），虔诚的人会绕着它祈祷。油漆来自孔雀石（绿色）、朱砂（红色）和青金石（蓝色），这些都是从中亚进口的昂贵矿物。

The art of this period is characterised by its attempt to depict the spirituality of those who had transcended the material world through their asceticism. The Wei statues are slim, ethereal figures with finely chiselled features and comparatively large heads. The northern Zhou figures have ghostly white eyes.
这一时期的艺术特点是试图描绘那些通过禁欲主义超越物质世界的人的灵性。魏氏雕像是苗条、空灵的人物，五官轮廓分明，头部相对较大。北周人物有鬼魅般的白眼睛。

Sui Caves
隋洞

The Sui dynasty (AD 581–618) was short-lived and very much a transition between the Wei and Tang periods. This can be seen in the Sui caves at Mogao: the graceful Indian curves in the Buddha and Bodhisattva figures start to give way to the more rigid style of Chinese sculpture.
隋朝（公元 581-618 年）是短暂的，很大程度上是魏唐时期之间的过渡。这在莫高窟的隋石窟中可见一斑：佛菩萨像中优美的印度曲线开始让位于中国雕塑中更僵化的风格。

The Sui dynasty began when a general of Chinese or mixed Chinese–Tuoba origin usurped the throne of the northern Zhou dynasty and reunited northern and southern China for the first time in 360 years.
隋朝始于一位中国或中国拓跋混血的将军篡夺了北周王朝的王位，并在 360 年来首次统一了中国南北。

Tang Caves
唐洞

The Tang dynasty (AD 618–907) was Mogao’s high point. Painting and sculpture techniques became much more refined, and some important aesthetic developments, notably the sex change (from male to female) of Guanyin and the flying apsaras, took place. The beautiful murals depicting the Buddhist Western Paradise offer rare insights into the court life, music, dress and architecture of Tang China.
唐朝（公元 618-907 年）是莫高的鼎盛时期。绘画和雕塑技术变得更加精致，一些重要的美学发展，特别是观音和飞天的性别变化（从男性到女性）。描绘佛教西方天堂的美丽壁画提供了对唐朝中国宫廷生活、音乐、服饰和建筑的难得见解。

Some 230 caves were carved during the religiously diverse Tang dynasty, including two impressive grottoes containing enormous, seated Buddha figures. Originally open to the elements, the statue of Maitreya in cave 96 (believed to represent Empress Wu Zetian, who used Buddhism to consolidate her power) is a towering 35.5m tall, making it the world’s third-largest Buddha. The Buddhas were carved from the top down using scaffolding, the anchor holes of which are still visible.
在宗教多元化的唐朝，大约有 230 个石窟被雕刻出来，其中包括两个令人印象深刻的石窟，里面有巨大的坐佛像。第 96 窟的弥勒佛像（据信代表武则天皇后，她利用佛教巩固了自己的权力）原本是向自然开放的，高 35.5 米，是世界第三大佛像。佛像是用脚手架自上而下雕刻而成的，脚手架的锚孔仍然清晰可见。

后唐石窟

Following the Tang dynasty, the economy around Dunhuang went into decline, and the luxury and vigour typical of Tang painting began to be replaced by simpler drawing techniques and flatter figures. The mysterious Western Xia kingdom, which controlled most of Gansu from 983 to 1227, made a number of additions to the caves at Mogao and began to introduce Tibetan influences.
随着唐代，敦煌周边的经济开始衰落，唐画典型的奢华和活力开始被更简单的绘画手法和更修平的人物所取代。神秘的西夏王国在 983 年至 1227 年期间控制了甘肃大部分地区，对莫高窟的洞穴进行了多次扩建，并开始引入西藏的影响。

This Han dynasty military post was one of the two most important gates marking the end of the Chinese empire along the ancient Silk Road. Today, a dusty museum chronicles some of the site’s artefacts, but the real draw is the crumbling beacon tower atop Dundun Hill (墩墩山, Dūndūnshān), where a modern viewing platform offers generous vistas of the surrounding Taklamakan Desert.

SILK ROAD RAIDERS

In 1900 the Taoist priest and self-appointed guardian of the Mogao Grottoes, Wang Yuanlu, discovered a hidden library (in cave 17, alongside the Taoist Sanqing Gong Taoist temple), filled with tens of thousands of immaculately preserved manuscripts and paintings, dating as far back as AD 406.
1900 年，道士、自封的莫高窟守护者王元禄发现了一个隐藏的图书馆（位于第 17 窟，与道教三清公道观并列），里面藏有数以万计保存完好的手稿和绘画，其历史可以追溯到公元 406 年。

It’s hard to describe the exact magnitude of the discovery, but stuffed into the tiny cave were texts in rare Central Asian languages, military reports, music scores, medical prescriptions, Confucian and Taoist classics, and Buddhist sutras copied by some of the greatest names in Chinese calligraphy – not to mention the oldest printed book in existence, the Diamond Sutra (AD 868). In short, it was an incalculable amount of original source material regarding Chinese, Central Asian and Buddhist history.
很难描述这一发现的确切规模，但在这个小洞穴里塞满了稀有的中亚语言文本、军事报告、乐谱、医疗方、儒家和道家经典，以及中国书法界一些最伟大人物抄写的佛经——更不用说现存最古老的印刷书籍《 金刚经》（公元 868 年）。简而言之，这是有关中国、中亚和佛教历史的原始原始材料数量无法估量。

Word of the discovery quickly spread and Wang Yuanlu, suddenly the most popular bloke in town, was courted by rival archaeologists Aurel Stein and Paul Pelliot, among others. Following much pressure to sell the cache, Wang Yuanlu finally relented and parted with an enormous hoard of treasure. On his watch close to 20,000 of the cave’s priceless manuscripts were whisked off to Europe for the paltry sum of £220.
这一发现的消息迅速传开，王元禄突然成为镇上最受欢迎的家伙，受到了竞争对手考古学家奥雷尔·斯坦因和保罗·佩利奥特等人的追捧。在巨大的压力下出售藏匿处，王元禄终于心软了，并放弃了大量的宝藏。在他的监督下，洞穴中近 20,000 份无价的手稿以微不足道的 220 英镑的价格被运往欧洲。

Today, Chinese intellectuals – bitter at the sacking of the caves – deride Stein, Pelliot and others for making off with what they consider to be national treasures. Defenders of the explorers point out that had the items been left alone, there is a chance they could have been lost during the ensuing civil war or the Cultural Revolution.
今天，中国知识分子——对洞穴被洗劫一空感到痛苦——嘲笑斯坦因、佩里奥特和其他人拿走他们认为是国宝的东西。探险家的捍卫者指出，如果这些物品不理会，它们有可能在随后的内战或文化大革命中丢失。

o 麦积山石窟

(麦积山石窟, Màijīshān Shíkū; Maijishan, 麦积山; grottoes ¥90, scenic area alone ¥25; h8.30am-4.30pm)
（ 麦积山石窟 ， Màijīshān Shíkū;麦积山， 麦积山 ;石窟¥90，景区单¥25; h 上午 8：30 至下午 4：30）

Set among wild, green mountains southeast of Tianshui, the grottoes of Maijishan hold some of the most famous Buddhist rock carvings along the Silk Road. The cliff sides of Maijishan are covered with 221 caves holding more than 7800 sculptures carved principally during the Northern Wei and Zhou dynasties (AD 386–581). The rock face rises in a steep ascent, with the hundreds of grottoes connected by a series of constructed walkways clinging to the sheer cliff.
麦积山石窟坐落在天水东南部的狂野青山之间，拥有丝绸之路沿线一些最著名的佛教石刻。麦积山的悬崖两侧覆盖着 221 个洞穴，里面有 7800 多件雕塑，这些雕塑主要雕刻在北魏和周朝（公元 386-581 年）。岩壁陡峭地上升，数百个石窟由一系列紧贴陡峭悬崖的人工人行道连接起来。

Within the hard-to-miss Sui dynasty trinity of Buddha and two Bodhisattvas is the largest statue on the mountain: the cave’s central effigy of Buddha tops out at 15.7m. During restoration works on the statue in the late 1980s, a handwritten copy of the Sutra of Golden Light was discovered within the Buddha’s fan.
在不容错过的隋朝佛两菩萨三位一体中，是山上最大的佛像：洞穴中央的佛像最高高 15.7 米。在 1980 年代后期对佛像进行修复工作时，在佛陀的扇子中发现了一本手写的《金光经》。

Vertigo-inducing catwalks and steep stairways cling to the cliff face, affording close-ups of the art. It’s not certain just how the artists managed to clamber so high; one theory is that they created piles from blocks of wood reaching to the top of the mountain before moving down, gradually removing them as they descended.
令人眩晕的走道和陡峭的楼梯紧贴悬崖面，提供艺术特写镜头。目前还不确定艺术家们是如何爬得这么高的;一种理论认为，他们用木块堆成堆，到达山顶，然后向下移动，在下降时逐渐将其移除。

A considerable amount of pigment still clings to many of the statues – a lot of which are actually made of clay rather than being hewn from rock – although you frequently have to climb up steps to peer at them through tight mesh grills with little natural illumination; sometimes it can be hard to see much, but other statues are as clear as day. Most of the more impressive sculptures decorate the upper walkways, especially at cave 4.
许多雕像上仍然粘着相当多的颜料——其中很多实际上是由粘土制成的，而不是从岩石上凿成的——尽管你经常不得不爬上台阶，通过几乎没有自然光的紧网状格栅窥视它们;有时很难看到太多，但其他雕像却像白昼一样清晰。大多数更令人印象深刻的雕塑都装饰着上层走道，尤其是在第 4 窟。

A new visitor centre, complete with post office and rather spooky face recognition technology, has gone up 3km from the grottoes, meaning that you still have to walk this distance uphill to get to the statuary, or you can take one of the sightseeing buses (观光车, guānguāng chē; ¥15) that regularly depart from the road just beyond the ticket office.
一个新的游客中心，配有邮局和相当诡异的人脸识别技术，已经从石窟上升了 3 公里，这意味着您仍然需要步行这么远的上坡才能到达雕像，或者您可以乘坐定期从售票处对面的道路出发的观光车（观光车 ，guānguāng chē;15 日元）。

An English-speaking guide charges ¥50 for a group of up to five. It may be possible to view normally closed caves (such as cave 133) for an extra fee of ¥500 per group.
英语导游最多五人收费 50 日元。可以参观通常关闭的洞穴（例如 133 号洞穴），但每组额外收费 500 日元。

The admission ticket includes entry to Ruiying Monastery (瑞应寺, Ruìyìng Sì), at the base of the mountain, which acts as a small museum of selected statues. Across from the monastery is the start of a trail to a botanic garden (植物园, zhíwùyuán), which allows for a short cut back to the entrance gate through the forest.
门票包括进入位于山脚下的瑞应寺 （ 瑞应寺，Ruìyìng Sì），该寺是一个小型博物馆，收藏了精选的雕像。寺院对面是通往植物园 （ 植物园 ，zhíwùyuán）的小径的起点，可以穿过森林走捷径回到入口大门。

The grottoes are the highlight of the much larger Maijishan Scenic Area (麦积山景区, Màijīshān Jǐngqū), which has considerable hiking potential if you want to make a day of it, passing waterfalls, observation terraces, mountains, valleys and temples. One activity is climbing Xiāngjí Shān (香积山); for the trailhead, head back towards the visitor centre where the sightseeing bus drops you off and look for a sign to the hill down a side road to the left. You can purchase a cheaper ticket (¥25) for just the scenic area if you wish, but it will not permit you to visit the grottoes.
石窟是更大的麦积山景区（麦积山景区 ，Màijīshān Jǐngqū）的亮点，如果您想度过一天，这里有相当大的徒步旅行潜力，途经瀑布、观景台、山脉、山谷和寺庙。其中一项活动是攀登香积山 ;对于步道起点，返回游客中心，观光巴士会在那里下车，然后沿着左侧的小路寻找通往山上的标志。如果您愿意，您可以购买更便宜的门票（25 日元），但不允许您参观石窟。

If you’re hungry, there’s a huge collection of stalls serving noodles and other local dishes near the ticket office. Most dishes are priced at ¥10.
如果您饿了，售票处附近有大量摊位供应面条和其他当地菜肴。大多数菜肴的价格为 10 日元。

The bus terminates at the Maijishan ticketing office. At the time of writing, the road to the grottoes was being much widened to accommodate a greater volume of vehicle traffic to the sight.
