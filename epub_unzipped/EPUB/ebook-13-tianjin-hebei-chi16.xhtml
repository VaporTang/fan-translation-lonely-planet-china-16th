Why Go?
为什么要去？

Though you’ll often be darting through it at 350km/h on one of China’s phenomenal high-speed trains, Hebei (河北, Héběi) is in fact a slow-moving panorama of brown earth and fields of corn and wheat. Cosmopolitan Tianjin (天津) may put on a dazzling show, but the true charms of this region are its time-worn, earthy textures and its deep-rooted historical narrative.
虽然你经常会乘坐中国非凡的高速列车以 350 公里/小时的速度穿过它，但河北 （ 河北 ， Héběi） 实际上是一幅缓慢移动的棕色土地和玉米和小麦田的全景。国际化的天津（ 天津 ）可能会上演一场令人眼花缭乱的表演，但这个地区真正的魅力在于其陈旧的朴实质感和根深蒂固的历史叙事。

Hebei offers the ideal chance to disengage from Beijing’s modernity and frantic urban tempo and experience a more timeless China without having to travel too far. Wander through ancient settlements and walled towns, skirt the wild edges of the former Manchuria and journey to the majestic 18th-century summer retreat of the Qing emperors in Chengde.
河北提供了一个理想的机会，可以摆脱北京的现代性和疯狂的城市节奏，体验更永恒的中国，而无需走太远。漫步在古老的定居点和有围墙的城镇中，绕过前满洲的荒凉边缘，前往承德雄伟的 18 世纪清朝皇帝避暑胜地。

There are temples to explore, rarely visited stretches of the Great Wall, and remote towns and villages whose ancient rhythms and rural seclusion make them the perfect retreats for those prepared to venture slightly off the beaten track.
这里有可供探索的寺庙、人迹罕至的长城，以及偏远的城镇和村庄，其古老的节奏和乡村的隐居使它们成为那些准备远离人迹罕至的地方冒险的人的完美度假胜地。

Forever being compared to Beijing (if anything, it’s more like Shanghai), the former foreign concession port of Tianjin (Tiānjīn) is a large, booming, yet laid-back city, with a pleasant river promenade and some charming, European-flavoured neighbourhoods. History
历史

Tianjin rose to prominence as a grain-storage point during the Mongol Yuan dynasty. The city found itself at the intersection of both inland and port navigation routes, and by the 15th century the town had become a walled garrison.
天津在蒙古元朝时期作为粮食储存点而声名鹊起。这座城市发现自己处于内陆和港口航行路线的交汇处，到 15 世纪，该镇已成为一个有围墙的驻军。

During the foreign concession era, the British and French settled in, joined by the Japanese, Germans, Austro-Hungarians, Italians and Belgians between 1895 and 1900. Each concession was a self-contained world, with its own prison, school, barracks and hospital. During the Boxer Rebellion, the foreign powers levelled the walls of the old Chinese city.
在外国租界时代，英国人和法国人定居，1895 年至 1900 年间，日本人、德国人、奥匈帝国人、意大利人和比利时人也加入其中。每个租界都是一个独立的世界，有自己的监狱、学校、军营和医院。义和团运动期间，列强夷平了中国老城的城墙。

Tianjin was established as a municipality of China in 1927.
天津市于1927年作为中国直辖市成立。

The Tangshan earthquake of 1976 killed nearly 24,000 people in the Tianjin area. The city was badly rocked, but escaped the devastation that virtually obliterated nearby Tangshan, where 240,000 people died. The disaster is portrayed in the multiaward-winning 2010 film Aftershock.
1976 年的唐山大地震，天津地区造成近 2.4 万人死亡。这座城市受到严重震动，但幸免于难，几乎摧毁了附近的唐山，那里有 240,000 人死亡。这场灾难在 2010 年屡获殊荣的电影 《余震》 中得到描绘。

Tianjin doesn’t have many top-drawer sights, though its museums are good. The main attraction here is to leisurely wander through its neighbourhoods: Wudadao, the Treaty Port area, the Italian Concession, the Old Town and the river promenades all make for extremely pleasant strolls, with interesting architecture to spot and cute cafes to rest up in.
天津没有很多顶级景点，尽管它的博物馆很好。这里的主要景点是悠闲地漫步在街区：五大道、条约港区、意大利租界、老城区和河滨长廊都是非常愉快的漫步场所，有有趣的建筑可供观赏，还有可爱的咖啡馆可供休息。

Tianjin MuseumMUSEUM
天津博物馆 MUSEUM

(天津博物馆, Tiānjīn Bówùguǎn; www.tjbwg.com; Tianjin Cultural Centre, 62 Pingjiang Dao, 天津文化中心，平江道62号; h9am-4.30 Tue-Sun; mWenhua Zhongxin) F
（ 天津博物馆 ， Tiānjīn Bówùguǎn;www.tjbwg.com;天津文化中心， 天津文化中心，平江道 62 号 h 9am-4.30 Tues-Sun; m 文华忠信） F

This impressive five-floor museum has over 200,000 pieces in its collection, ranging from oracle bones and an excellent inkstone exhibit to various artefacts and documents related specifically to the city’s historical development. The 3rd floor, which focuses on Tianjin’s development as a modern city from the Opium War onwards, is a highlight.
这座令人印象深刻的五层博物馆收藏了超过 200,000 件藏品，从甲骨文和精美的硯台展览到与城市历史发展相关的各种文物和文献。三楼重点介绍了天津从鸦片战争开始作为现代化城市的发展，是一大亮点。

Part of a landscaped park complex called Wénhuà Zhōngxīn (文化中心, Cultural Centre) that includes a shopping centre with places to eat and a handful of other free-to-enter museums, this area is south of the city centre by Wenhua Zhongxin metro station; turn right out of Exit K and the museum is the central one of three on the other side of the lake, with Tianjin Art Museum to its left and Tianjin Natural History Museum, inside an all-glass stingray-shaped building, to its right.
该地区是名为 Wénhuà Zhōngxīn（ 文化中心 ，文化中心）的景观公园综合体的一部分，该综合体包括一个带餐饮场所的购物中心和一些其他免费进入的博物馆，位于市中心以南的文华中信地铁站旁;从 K 出口右转，博物馆是湖对岸的三个中心之一，左边是天津美术馆 ，右边是天津自然博物馆 ，位于一座全玻璃黄貂鱼形状的建筑内。

Treaty Port AreaARCHITECTURE
条约港区建筑

(map Google map; Jiefang Beilu, 解放北路; mJinwan Guangchang)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ;解放北路 m Jinwan Guangchang）

South of the station across Liberation Bridge was the British concession. The rebuilt riverside facade is an impressive sight at night, but walk further south along Jiefang Beilu to see original, imposing, hundred-year-old European buildings, which once housed the city’s international banks. Names are posted on plaques outside each building; many still house banks today. One building of particular note is the former Qing dynasty post office (109 Jiefang Beilu, 解放北路109号) F, which now has a historic stamp collection on display.
解放桥对面的车站以南是英国租界。重建的河边立面在夜间令人印象深刻，但沿着解放北鹿再往南走，可以看到原始的、雄伟的、拥有百年历史的欧洲建筑，这些建筑曾经是这座城市的国际银行的所在地。名字张贴在每栋建筑外的牌匾上;今天，许多银行仍然设有银行。其中一座特别值得注意的建筑是前清代邮局 （ 解放北路 109 号 F ）， 现在展出了历史悠久的邮票收藏。

The centrepiece of the Wudadao neighbourhood, Minyuan Plaza was rebuilt in 2012 on the site of a 90-year-old stadium that was originally designed by former British Olympian Eric Liddell. It now functions as a giant park of sorts – you can still run laps if you want – with a visitors centre, two small museums (of limited interest), and a host of cafes and restaurants. It’s particularly lively in the evening.
民园广场是五大道街区的中心，于 2012 年在一座拥有 90 年历史的体育场旧址上重建，该体育场最初由前英国奥运选手埃里克·利德尔 （Eric Liddell） 设计。它现在就像一个巨大的公园——如果你愿意，你仍然可以跑几圈——有一个游客中心、两个小型博物馆（兴趣有限）以及许多咖啡馆和餐馆。晚上特别热闹。

Monastery of Deep CompassionBUDDHIST TEMPLE
大悲寺佛教寺庙

(大悲禅院, Dàbēi Chányuàn; 40 Tianwei Lu, 天纬路40号; ¥5; h9am-6pm Apr-Oct, to 4pm Nov-Mar)

Tianjin’s most important Buddhist temple – signposted as ‘Dabei Temple’ on street signs – was built in three stages from 1436 to 1734. While most of the architecture has since been rebuilt, it’s a very large and active place and an enthralling place to wander. Don’t miss the huge, multi-armed statue of Guanyin – whose eyes seem to follow you around – standing in the Great Compassion Hall in a side courtyard. Admission includes three incense sticks; pick them up to the right of the Hall of Heavenly Kings.

Confucius TempleCONFUCIAN TEMPLE
孔庙 CONFUCIAN TEMPLE

Tianjin’s quiet Confucius Temple is actually a two-for-one, with the provincial temple on the east side (dating from 1436) and the county temple on the west side (dating from 1734). Although almost everything has been rebuilt, the county side has some exhibits on the history and main tenets of Confucianism, all set to qín music playing beneath the cypress trees.

ERIC LIDDELL

Olympic champion, rugby international and devout Christian, Scotsman Eric Liddell is best known as the subject of the 1981 Oscar-winning film Chariots of Fire, but few know about his connection to Tianjin. He was born here in 1902 before being educated in Scotland; he then embarked on a short but astonishing sporting career. He was capped seven times by the Scotland rugby union team and won gold in the 400m at the 1924 Paris Olympics. Famously, he pulled out of his favoured event – the 100m – because, as a Christian, he refused to run on a Sunday. The old canal-side market town of Yangliuqing, 20km west of Tianjin, has been spruced up for tourists and is now home to an attractive collection of courtyards, temples, alleyways and squares, the centrepiece of which is the Shi Family Residence (石家大院, Shí Jiā Dàyuàn; 47 Yangliuqing Guyi Jie, 杨柳青估衣街47号; ¥25; h9am-5pm Apr-Oct, to 4.30pm Nov-Mar). ZHAOZHOU BRIDGE
赵州大桥

China’s oldest bridge still standing, Zhaozhou Bridge (赵州桥, Zhàozhōu Qiáo; Zhaoxian County; ¥40) has spanned the Jiao River (Jiǎo Hé) for 1400 years. As the world’s first segmental arch bridge (ie its arch is a segment of a circle, as opposed to a complete semicircle), it predates other bridges of its type throughout the world by 800 years. In fine condition, and part of a riverside park, it is 50.82m long and 9.6m wide, with a span of 37m.
中国现存最古老的桥梁—— 赵州桥 （Zhàozhōu Qiáo;昭县;¥40）横跨焦河（交河）已有 1400 年的历史。作为世界上第一座节段拱桥（即它的拱门是圆的一段，而不是一个完整的半圆），它比世界上其他同类桥梁早了 800 年。它状况良好，是河滨公园的一部分，长 50.82 米，宽 9.6 米，跨度 37 米。

Its streets littered with temple remains, the part-walled town of Zhengding (Zhèngdìng) is an appetising – albeit incomplete – slice of old China. Popularly known as Dafo Temple (大佛寺, Dàfó Sì), or ‘Great Buddha Temple’, the complex contains an astonishing array of Buddhist statuary, housed in some stunning temple halls. 考虑到它的历史——近 1500 年的历史——我们认为这是中国北方最令人印象深刻的寺庙之一。这当然是正定的明星景点。该建筑群俗称大佛寺（大佛寺 ，Dàfó Sì），或“大佛寺”，包含一系列令人惊叹的佛教雕像，位于一些令人惊叹的寺庙大厅中。这座寺庙的历史可以追溯到公元 586 年，经过大量修复，与灵墙被中山东麓隔开。

The time-worn bridge out the front constitutes a handsome historical prelude. You are greeted in the first hall by the jovial Milefo (the laughing Buddha). The four Heavenly Kings flanking him in pairs are disconcertingly vast.
前面那座陈旧的桥段构成了一段漂亮的历史前奏。在第一个大厅里，欢快的 Milefo（笑佛）迎接您。成对夹在他两侧的四位天王，浩瀚得令人不安。

Beyond the ruined Hall of Sakyamuni’s Six Teachers is the Manichaean Hall (摩尼殿, Móní Diàn), an astonishingly voluminous hall flagged in smoothed stone, with amazing carpentry overhead, a huge gilded statue of Sakyamuni and faded Ming frescoes detailing Buddhist tales. At the rear of the hall is a distinctly male statue of the goddess Guanyin, seated in a lithe pose with one foot resting on her/his thigh (a posture known as lalitásana) and surrounded by luóhàn (disciples freed from the cycle of rebirth).
在废墟的释迦牟尼六师殿之外是摩尼堂 （ 摩尼殿 ，Móní Diàn），这是一座巨大的大殿，用光滑的石头标记，头顶上有令人惊叹的木工，一尊巨大的镀金释迦牟尼雕像和褪色的明朝壁画，详细介绍了佛教故事。大殿的后方有一尊明显的男性观音女神雕像，她以轻盈的姿势坐着，一只脚放在她/他的大腿上（这种姿势被称为 lalitásana），周围环绕着 luóhàn（从轮回中解脱出来的弟子）。

The Buddhist Altar behind houses an unusual, bronze, Ming-dynasty two-faced Buddha, gazing north and south. Signs say ‘no touching’ but it’s evident that its fingers and thumb have been smoothed by legions of worshippers. There are two halls behind the Buddhist Altar. On the left is the Revolving Library Pavilion (转轮藏阁, Zhuānlúnzàng Gé), which contains a highly unusual revolving, octagonal, wooden bookcase for the storing of sutras, and some stele on the back of snarling bìxì (a mythical, tortoise-like dragon). Opposite stands the Pavilion of Kindness, containing a fabulous, 7.4m-high statue of Maitreya (the future Buddha), one hand aloft.
后面的佛坛供奉着一尊不寻常的明代青铜双面佛，朝北望南。标志上写着“禁止触摸”，但很明显，它的手指和拇指已经被大批崇拜者抚平了。佛坛后面有两个大殿。左边是转轮藏阁（转轮藏阁 ，Zhuānlúnzàng Gé），里面有一个非常不寻常的旋转八角形木制书柜，用于存放佛经， 以及咆哮的 bìxì（一种神话中的龟状龙）背面的一些石碑。对面是仁慈阁 ，里面有一尊神话般的 7.4 米高的弥勒佛（未来的佛陀），一只手高举着。

The immense Pavilion of Great Benevolence (大悲阁, Dàbēi Gé) contains Longxing Temple’s real drawcard: a 21.3m-tall, bronze colossus of Guanyin. Cast in AD 971 and sporting a third eye, the effigy is wonderful, standing on a magnificently carved base from the Northern Song. Examine the carvings, which include myriad characters and musicians, including Buddhist angels (apsaras).
巨大的大悲阁（Dàbēi Gé）包含龙兴寺真正的吸引力：一个 21.3 米高的青铜观音巨像。这尊雕像铸于公元 971 年，拥有第三只眼，非常美妙，矗立在北宋雕刻精美的底座上。检查雕刻品，其中包括无数的人物和音乐家，包括佛教天使（apsaras）。

Circumambulated by worshippers, the Hall of Vairocana at the rear of the complex contains a four-faced Buddha (the Buddha of four directions), crowned with another four-faced Buddha, upon which is supported a further set. The entire statue and its base contain 1072 statues of Buddha in all.
在礼拜者的环绕下，建筑群后部的毘罗遮那大厅有一尊四面佛（四方佛），顶着另一尊四面佛，上面支撑着另一组。整个雕像及其底座共有 1072 尊佛像。

The gardens right at the back contain scattered temple remains, including some very old stele and a triple-arched stone páilou (decorative archway), dating from 1591.
后面的花园里散落着寺庙遗迹，包括一些非常古老的石碑和一座可追溯到 1591 年的三拱石 páilou（装饰拱门）。

Hidden in the hills near the Hebei–Shanxi border is the peaceful little settlement of Yujiacun (Yújiācūn). Nearly everything, from the houses to furniture inside, was originally made of stone – hence its nickname, Stone Village (石头村; Shítou Cūn).

Chengde 承德
Chengde 承德

%0314 / Pop 457,000
% 0314 / 人口 457,000

Built on the banks of the Wulie River and surrounded by forested hills, Chengde (Chéngdé) is a small, pleasant city that just happens to have an extraordinary history.
承德（Chéngdé）建在五列河畔，周围环绕着森林覆盖的山丘，是一座宜人的小城市，恰好有着非凡的历史。

This was the summer playground of the emperors of the Qing dynasty; beginning with Emperor Kangxi, the Qing Court would flee here to escape the torpid summer heat of the Forbidden City (and occasionally the threat of foreign armies), as well as to be closer to the hunting grounds of their northern homelands.
这里是清朝皇帝的避暑游乐场;从康熙皇帝开始，清廷会逃到这里，以躲避紫禁城的酷暑（偶尔还有外国军队的威胁），并靠近北方故乡的狩猎场。

The Bìshǔ Shānzhuāng (Fleeing-the-Heat Mountain Villa) is a grand imperial palace and the walled enclosure it lies within houses China’s largest regal gardens. Beyond the grounds is a remarkable collection of politically chosen temples, built to host dignitaries such as the sixth Panchen Lama. The Imperial Villa, the gardens and the eight temples are all, quite rightly, Unesco-protected.
Bìshǔ Shānzhuāng（避暑山庄）是一座宏伟的皇宫，它所在的围墙内拥有中国最大的富丽堂皇的花园。场地之外是一系列非凡的政治选择寺庙，这些寺庙是为了接待六世班禅喇嘛等政要而建造的。皇家别墅、花园和八座寺庙都受到联合国教科文组织的保护。

History
历史

In 1703, when an expedition passed through the Chengde valley, Emperor Kangxi was so enamoured with the surroundings that he had a hunting lodge built, which gradually grew into the summer resort. Jehol (or Rèhé, 热河, Warm River; named after a hot spring here) – as Chengde was then known – grew in importance and the Qing court began to spend more time here, sometimes up to several months a year, with some 10,000 people accompanying the emperor on his seven-day expedition from Beijing.
1703 年，一次远征队经过承德河谷，康熙皇帝对周围的环境非常着迷，他建造了一座狩猎小屋，逐渐发展成为避暑胜地。热河（或热河 ，温暖的河，以这里的温泉命名）——当时的承德——变得越来越重要，清廷开始在这里度过更多的时间，有时一年长达几个月，大约有 10,000 人陪同皇帝从北京出发进行为期 7 天的远征。

The emperors also convened in Jehol with the border tribes – undoubtedly more at ease here than in Beijing – who posed the greatest threats to the Qing frontiers: the Mongols, Tibetans, Uyghurs and, eventually, the Europeans. The resort reached its peak under Emperor Qianlong (1735–96), who commissioned many of the outlying temples to rawe visiting leaders.
皇帝们还在热河与边境部落会面——无疑，这里比北京更自在——他们对清朝边境构成最大威胁：蒙古人、藏人、维吾尔人，最终是欧洲人。该度假村在乾隆皇帝（1735-96 年）的领导下达到顶峰，乾隆皇帝委托许多边远寺庙来访的领导人。

Emperor Xianfeng died here in 1861, permanently warping Chengde’s feng shui and tipping the Imperial Villa towards long-term decline.

1Eight Outer Temples
1 八座外庙

Skirting the northern and eastern walls of Bìshǔ Shānzhuāng, the Eight Outer Temples (外八庙; wài bā miào) were, unusually, designed for diplomatic rather than spiritual reasons. Some were based on actual Tibetan Buddhist monasteries, but the emphasis was on appearance: smaller temple buildings are sometimes solid, and the Tibetan facades (with painted windows) are often fronts for traditional Chinese temple interiors. The surviving temples and monasteries were all built between 1713 and 1780; the prominence given to Tibetan Buddhism was as much for the Mongols (fervent Lamaists) as the Tibetan leaders.
环绕着毕树山庄的北墙和东墙，八外寺（ 外八庙 ;wài bā miào）不同寻常的是，是出于外交原因而不是精神原因而设计的。有些是根据真实的藏传佛教寺院改编的，但重点是外观：较小的寺庙建筑有时是坚固的，而藏式外墙（带有彩绘窗户）通常是中国传统寺庙内部的正面。幸存的寺庙和修道院均建于 1713 年至 1780 年间;藏传佛教的突出地位对蒙古人（狂热的喇嘛教徒）和西藏领袖来说一样重要。

Three of these temples are currently closed to the public (and have been for a long time): Puren Temple map Google map (普仁寺, Pǔrén Sì), Pushan Temple map Google map (溥善寺, Pǔshàn Sì) and Guanyuan Temple (广缘寺, Guǎngyuán Sì; off Puning Lu, 普宁路).
其中三座寺庙目前不对公众开放（并且已经关闭了很长时间）： 普仁寺地图谷歌地图 （ 普仁寺 ，Pǔrén Sì）、 蒲山寺地图谷歌地图 （ 溥善寺 ，Pǔshàn Sì） 和观源寺 （ 广缘寺 ，Guǎngyuán Sì; off Puning Lu， 普宁路 ）。

With its squeaking prayer wheels and devotional intonations of its monks, this is Chengde’s only active Buddhist temple. It was built in 1755 in anticipation of Qianlong’s victory over the western Mongol tribes in Xinjiang. Supposedly modelled on the earliest Tibetan Buddhist monastery (Samye), the first half of the temple is distinctly Chinese, with Tibetan buildings at the rear.
凭借吱吱作响的转经轮和僧侣虔诚的语调，这是承德唯一活跃的佛教寺庙。它建于1755年，以期乾隆战胜新疆的西部蒙古部落。据说这座寺庙的前半部分仿照最早的藏传佛教寺院（三叶寺），具有明显的中国风格，后面是藏族建筑。

台阶顶部的露台上是矮小的大乘殿 。两侧是佛塔和方形的街区状藏式建筑，装饰着迷人的水龙头。一些建筑已被改建为商店，而另一些则是坚固的，纯粹用于装饰目的。

The mind-bogglingly vast gilded statue of Guanyin (the Buddhist Goddess of Mercy) towers within the Mahayana Hall. Hewn from five different kinds of wood (pine, cypress, fir, elm and linden), Guanyin has 42 arms, with each palm bearing an eye and each hand holding instruments, skulls, lotuses and other Buddhist devices. Tibetan touches include the pair of hands in front of the goddess, below the two clasped in prayer, the right one of which holds a sceptre-like dorje (vajra in Sanskrit), a masculine symbol, and the left a dril bu (bell), a female symbol. On Guanyin’s head sits the Teacher Longevity Buddha. To the right of the goddess stands a huge male guardian and disciple called Shàncái, opposite his female equivalent, Lóngnǚ (Dragon Girl). Occasionally, tourists are allowed to climb up to the 1st-floor gallery for a closer inspection of Guanyin.
偶尔，游客可以爬到一楼的画廊，仔细观察观音。

The sleepy hamlet of Jimingyi (Jīmíngyì) is a delightful surprise to find amid the scruffy northern Hebei countryside. This walled town, established during the Yuan dynasty (1206–1368), is China’s oldest surviving post station, a historic reminder of a system that endured for 2000 years and enabled the officials in the Forbidden City to keep in touch with their far-flung counterparts around China. Whipped by dust storms in the spring and with archaic, fading Mao-era slogans still visible on walls, Jimingyi sees few visitors and feels much further from the gleaming capital than the 140km distance would suggest.
在河北北部略显粗犷的乡野之间，邂逅宁静的鸡鸣驿村 (Jīmíngyì)，绝对是一个令人愉快的惊喜。这座有城墙的古城始建于元朝（1206-1368年），是中国现存最古老的驿站。它是一个历史的见证，见证了那个延续了两千年的邮驿系统，正是这个系统让身在紫禁城的官员们能够与遍布中国各地、远在天边的同僚们保持联系。

走进这座小建筑，祈求巨额财富，或参观 2012 年发现的奇妙明朝壁画。它描绘了一座古老的银行（ 票号 ，piàohào）;如果你仔细观察，你还会看到五个外国人和一个麒麟（麒麟 ，一种中国神话中的动物）正在前往中国做生意的路上。

Taishan TempleTAOIST TEMPLE
泰山寺道观

(泰山行宫, Tàishān Xínggōng; Near the East Gate)
（ 泰山行宫 ， Tàishān Xínggōng;东门附近）

This temple is dedicated to Bixia, the goddess of Tai Shan. The paintings here, Jimingyi’s largest collection of Ming murals, depict the life of the goddess. They were whitewashed – some say for protection – during the Cultural Revolution. A professor from Qinghua University helped to uncover them; you can still see streaks of white in places.
这座寺庙供奉着泰山女神碧霞。这里的画作是鸡鸣义最大的明代壁画收藏，描绘了女神的生活。它们在文化大革命期间被粉饰——有人说是为了保护。清华大学的一位教授帮助发现了它们;你仍然可以在某些地方看到白色条纹。

4Sleeping & Eating
4 睡眠和饮食

Sleeping options are limited to simple kè zhàn (客栈; guesthouses), with most either on or just off Yicheng Yijie (驿城一街), the road running between the East Gate (东门, Dōng Mén) and West Gate (西门, Xī Mén). Expect to pay around ¥100 for a room.
睡眠选择仅限于简单的客栈 ， 大多数位于驿城一街（驿城一街 ）上或就在驿城一街附近，驿城一街是东门（ 东门，东门）和西门（ 西门 ，西门，西门）之间的道路。预计一个房间需要支付大约 100 日元。

Village guesthouses all double up as restaurants, with simple home-cooked meals. Bear in mind that they eat donkey meat (驴肉; lǘròu) in these parts.
乡村宾馆都兼作餐厅，提供简单的家常饭菜。请记住，他们吃驴肉（驴肉 ;lǘròu）在这些部分。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

TRAIN
火车

Jimingyi can be reached from the fast-developing mining town of Xia Huayuan (下花园, Xià Huāyuán), 4km away. The easiest way to Xia Huayuan is to hop on a train from Beijing Main (¥23.50, 2½ hours, 7.14am) or Beijing West (¥21.50, three hours, 10.24am, 2.53pm and 3.30pm). Same-day tickets can be bought easily at the stations. Once at Xia Huayuan, turn right out of the train station and either walk to Jimingyi (3km) or flag down one of the very frequent small buses that run to Shacheng (沙城, Shāchéng), via Jimingyi (¥3).
从快速发展的矿业小镇下花园（ 下花园 ，Xià Huāyuán）可以到达鸡鸣驿，距离酒店有 4 公里。前往夏花园最简单的方法是从北京主线（23.50 日元，21/2 小时，早上 7 点 14 分）或北京西站（21.50 日元，三小时，上午 10 点 24 分、下午 2 点 53 分和下午 3 点 30 分）乘坐火车。当日车票可以在车站轻松购买。到达夏花园后，从火车站右转，步行到鸡鸣驿（3 公里），或者乘坐一辆非常频繁的小巴，经鸡鸣驿开往沙城（沙城 ，沙城）（3 日元）。

Trains back to Beijing from Xia Huayuan leave at 8.06am (four hours; Beijing Main), 11.45am (2½ hours; Beijing West) and 4.33pm (three hours; Beijing West).
从夏花园返回北京的火车于上午 8 点 06 分出发（4 小时;北京主线），上午 11 点 45 分（2 小时半;北京西部）和下午 4 点 33 分（三个小时;北京西部）。

Note, the Beijing to Zhangjiakou high-speed train line, due to open in 2020, will stop at the new Xia Huayuan North Station (下花园北站, Xià Huāyuán Běizhàn) and will use Beijing North Train Station, which is beside Xizhimen subway station. This new station is further away still from Jimingyi, but presumably local buses will link it either to Jimingyi, or at least to the old train station or Shacheng.
请注意，北京至张家口的高铁线将于 2020 年开通，将停靠新下花园北站 ，并将使用西直门地铁站旁的北京北站。这个新车站距离鸡鸣驿还较远，但据推测，当地巴士将将其连接到鸡鸣驿，或者至少连接到旧火车站或沙城。

BUS
总线

Taking a bus from Beijing offers more flexibility than the train, though it’s a bit more of a pain. First, head to Zhuxinzhuang metro station at the end of Line 8. Come out of Exit B2, cross the main road and turn left. Then walk about 200m to find bus 899, which terminates at Xia Huayuan (¥24, with travel card ¥16, 2¼ hours). Buses leave every hour on the half hour from 5.30am to 5.30pm.
从北京乘坐公共汽车比乘坐火车更灵活，尽管它有点痛苦。首先，前往 8 号线终点的竹信庄地铁站。从 B2 出口出来，穿过主干道左转。然后步行约 200 米即可找到 899 路公交车，终点站为下花园（¥24，凭旅行卡¥16,21/4 小时）。巴士在早上 5：30 至下午 5：30 的半小时每小时一班。

There are no buses from the bus station in Xia Huayuan to Jimingyi, so it’s best to get to the train station and catch the Shacheng bus from there. Local bus 5 goes to the train station, but it’s very infrequent. It’s only a 1.5km walk, though; turn left out of the bus station and walk all the way along Xiyuan Lu (西苑路). Turn left at the end, onto Gonglu Jie (公路街), then take the second right (still Gonglu Jie) and the train station is soon on your right.
从夏花园的汽车站到鸡鸣驿没有公交车，所以最好到火车站，从那里搭乘沙城公交车。当地 5 路公交车到火车站，但班次很少。不过，步行仅 1.5 公里;从汽车站出来左转，沿着西苑路一路走。尽头左转，进入 Gonglu Jie（ 公路街 ），然后在第二个路口右转（仍然是 Gonglu Jie），火车站很快就在您的右边。

A taxi from Xia Huayuan to Jimingyi is around ¥30.
从夏花园到鸡鸣义的出租车费用约为 ¥30。

Return buses from Xia Huayuan to Beijing leave on the hour every hour. The last one is at 5pm.
从夏花园到北京的回程巴士每小时整点发车。最后一个是下午 5 点。
