Why Go?
为什么要去？

Many travellers find themselves drifting into Hubei (湖北, Húběi) through the Three Gorges, the precipitous marvel that’s a continuation of Chongqing’s uniquely lush and hilly landscape. This classic trip down the Yangzi is the perfect introduction to Hubei’s natural beauty.
许多旅行者发现自己通过三峡漂流到湖北 ，这是重庆独特的郁郁葱葱的丘陵景观的延续。这次经典的扬子河之旅是对湖北自然美景的完美介绍。

Sliced by rivers and dappled with lakes, Hubei’s western regions are dominated by stunning mountain scenery, from the national parks of Shennongjia and Enshi to the historic Taoist retreat of Wudang Shan. In 1944 the only variety of redwood growing outside of the USA, the dawn redwood, was discovered in these mountains.
湖北西部地区被河流分割，湖泊斑驳，以迷人的山景为主，从神农架和恩施国家公园到历史悠久的武当山道教隐居地。1944 年，在这些山区发现了唯一生长在美国以外生长的红木品种，黎明红杉。

While eastern Hubei is better known for its industrial belt rather than scenic beauty, the region’s central location has ensured it played a key role in China’s history. Vestiges spanning thousands of years, from the ancient state of Chu to the modern Wuchang Uprising – which led to the downfall of dynastic China – are woven into the province’s very fabric.
虽然湖北东部以其工业带而不是风景而闻名，但该地区的中心位置确保了它在中国历史上发挥了关键作用。从古代楚国到现代武昌起义（导致中国王朝垮台）的数千年遗迹都融入了该省的肌理中。

History
历史

The Hubei area first came to prominence during the Eastern Zhou (700–221 BC), when the powerful Chu kingdom, based in present-day Jingzhou, was at its height. Hubei again became pivotal during the Three Kingdoms (AD 220–280). The Chinese classic The Romance of the Three Kingdoms (Sān Guó Yǎnyì) makes much reference to Jingzhou. The mighty Yangzi River (Cháng Jiāng) ensured prosperous trade in the centuries that followed, especially for Wuhan, China’s largest inland port and stage of the 1911 uprising, which led to the fall of the Qing and the creation of the Republic of China.
湖北地区在东周（公元前 700-221 年）首次崭露头角，当时强大的楚国（位于今荆州）正处于鼎盛时期。湖北在三国时期（公元 220-280 年）再次成为举足轻重的地区。中国经典《 三国演义 》 中大量提到荆州。浩瀚的扬子江（Cháng Jiāng）确保了随后几个世纪的繁荣贸易，特别是对于中国最大的内陆港口和 1911 年起义的舞台武汉来说，这场起义导致了清朝的灭亡和中华民国的建立。

Language
语言

Hubei has two dialects of northern Mandarin – southwest Mandarin and lower-mid Yangzi Mandarin – while in the southeast many people speak Gan, a dialect from Jiangxi.
湖北有两种北部普通话方言——西南普通话和中下扬子普通话——而在东南部，许多人说赣语，一种来自江西的方言。

Wuhan (Wǔhàn) has matured from the sprawling convergence of three independent cities to central China’s main industrial and commercial centre. While there’s not much in the way of cultural sites, Wuhan is a major transport hub so you may find yourself here for a night or two.
武汉（武汉）已经从三个独立城市的庞大交汇处发展成为中国中部的主要工商业中心。虽然武汉没有太多文化遗址，但武汉是一个主要的交通枢纽，所以你可能会在这里住一两晚。

The Yangzi and Han rivers open up the densely packed streets, flowing by parks, lakes and a concession-era entertainment district in Hankou, the pick of the three former cities. This is not a place of penny postcards, but for those travelling through the middle of the Middle Kingdom, it’s a good place to get your urban fix.
扬子江和汉江打通了密密麻麻的街道，流淌着公园、湖泊和汉口的一个租界时代的娱乐区，汉口是前三座城市的首选。这不是一个一分钱明信片的地方，但对于那些穿越中国中部的人来说，这里是了解城市的好地方。

Hubei Provincial MuseumMUSEUM
湖北省博物馆 MUSEUM

(湖北省博物馆, Húběi Shěng Bówùguǎn; www.hbww.org; 156 Donghu Lu, 东湖路156号; h9am-5pm Tue-Sun; mDongting) F
（ 湖北省博物馆 ，Húběi Shěng Bówùguǎn;www.hbww.org;156 Donghu Lu， 东湖路 156 号 h 周二至周日上午 9 点至下午 5 点; m 洞庭） F

The highlights of Hubei’s Provincial Museum are excavations from the Tomb of Marquis Yi of Zeng (c 433 BC): there are bronze wares, weaponry and musical instruments – including one of the world’s largest, a remarkable five-tonne set of 65 double-tone bronze bells. Half-hour chime bell performances (¥30) are given thrice daily (10.30am, 2pm and 3pm) during the week, with an extra performance at 11.30am on the weekend. Other exhibits here include Palaeolithic fossils and Neolithic bronzes and pottery. Passport required.
湖北省博物馆的亮点是曾侯乙墓（约公元前 433 年）的出土：有青铜器、武器和乐器，其中包括世界上最大的 5 吨重的 65 个双色铜钟。工作日每天三次（上午 10：30、下午 2 点和下午 3 点）进行半小时钟声表演（30 日元），周末上午 11：30 额外表演。这里的其他展品包括旧石器时代的化石以及新石器时代的青铜器和陶器。需要护照。

The museum is by the enormous East Lake (东湖, Dōng Hú). Bus 411 runs here from the Yellow Crane Tower. Line 8 on the metro will eventually stop here too.
博物馆位于广阔的东湖（东湖，东湖）旁。411 路公交车从黄鹤楼出发。地铁 8 号线最终也将停在这里。

Yellow Crane TowerHISTORIC SITE
黄鹤楼历史遗迹

(map Google map; 黄鹤楼, Huánghè Lóu; Wuluo Lu, 武珞路; ¥70; h8am-6pm, to 5pm winter)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 黄鹤楼 ， Huánghè Lóu;Wuluo Lu， 武珞路 ; ¥70; h 上午 8 点至下午 6 点，冬季至下午 5 点）

Wuhan’s mythical crane, immortalised in the 8th-century poetry of Cui Hao, has long flown, but the city landmark remains perched atop Snake Hill. The tower has been rebuilt often since the original was constructed in AD 223, and today’s five-storey, yellow-tiled version is a 1980s remake of the Qing tower that burned down in 1884.
武汉神话中的鹤在 8 世纪崔浩的诗歌中永垂不朽，它早已飞翔，但这座城市地标仍然栖息在蛇山顶上。自公元 223 年原塔建成以来，这座塔经常被重建，今天的五层黄瓦版本是 1980 年代对 1884 年被烧毁的清塔的改造。

Bus 411 runs between the tower and the provincial museum. The Dongtinmen stop on metro Line 7 will get you close, while Line 5 will eventually run directly here.
411路公交车往返于塔楼和省博物馆之间。地铁 7 号线的东田门站将让您近距离到达，而 5 号线最终将直接运行到这里。

The Hankou Bund is a roughly 4km stretch of curated park running parallel to the Yangzi where locals gather to amble or gossip. There are some tea houses and bars, a few historic buildings, mahjong and chess boards, and some green areas. Mostly, though, it’s locals escaping the concrete and posing for photos, especially around sunrise and sunset.
汉口外滩是一个约 4 公里长的精心策划的公园，与扬子平行，当地人聚集在这里闲逛或闲聊。有一些茶馆和酒吧，一些历史建筑，麻将和棋盘，还有一些绿地。不过，大多数情况下，是当地人逃离混凝土并摆姿势拍照，尤其是在日出和日落前后。

贵元寺 BUDDHIST TEMPLE

(map Google map; 归元寺, Guīyuán Sì; 20 Cuiweiheng Lu, 翠微横路20号; ¥10; h7.30am-5pm; mZhongjiacun)

An afternoon at this revered 350-year-old Buddhist temple can fluctuate between serenity and chaos, depending on the tour buses. The former capital of the Chu kingdom from 689 to 278 BC, Jingzhou (Jīngzhōu) is an old city even by Chinese standards, and an easy stopover for those travelling by bullet train between Wuhan and Yichang. There are not many places in the world quite like Wudang Shan (Wǔdāng Shān), the wellspring of the gentle art of taichi. Misty clouds cover Taoist courtyards where masters and disciples make slow, flowing moves in unison, while travellers make the long, sweat-soaked ascent up thousands of stone steps. The ‘No 1 Taoist Mountain in the Middle Kingdom’ is more than a place of pilgrimage, however; its flora contains elixirs for natural health remedies sold on every second precipice and its mountain views rival any in China. Press away from the crowds and you’ll find unmarked paths to moss-strewn temples and ethereal splendour.
世界上没有多少地方能像武当山（Wǔdāng Shān）那样，它是温柔太极拳艺术的源泉。薄雾缭绕的云层覆盖着道教庭院，师徒们齐心协力地缓慢而流畅地移动，而旅行者则汗流浃背地爬上数千级石阶。然而，“中国第一道山”不仅仅是一个朝圣地;它的植物群中含有自然健康疗法的灵丹妙药，每隔一个悬崖上就会出售，其山景可与中国的任何山景相媲美。远离人群，您会发现通往长满青苔的寺庙和空灵辉煌的无标记小路。

Wudang Shan attracts a diverse array of climbers, from Taoist pilgrims with knapsacks and porters shouldering paving slabs and sacks of rice, to tired parents piggybacking young kids and bright-eyed octogenarians hopping along. THE BIRTH OF TAICHI
太极拳的诞生

Zhang Sanfeng (张三丰), a semilegendary Wudang Shan monk from the 10th or 13th century (depending on what source you read), is reputed to be the founder of the martial art tàijíquán (literally ‘Supreme Ultimate Boxing’) or taichi. Zhang had grown dissatisfied with the ‘hard’ techniques of Shaolin Boxing and searched for something softer and more elusive. Sitting on his porch one day, he became inspired by a battle between a huge bird and a snake. The sinuous snake used flowing movements to evade the bird’s attacks. The bird, exhausted, eventually gave up and flew away. Taichi is closely linked to Taoism, and many priests on Wudang Shan practise some form of the art.
张三丰是 10 世纪或 13 世纪的半传奇武当山僧（取决于您阅读的来源），被认为是武术 tàijíquán（字面意思是“至尊终极拳”）或太极拳的创始人。张对少林拳的“硬”技术越来越不满意，并寻找更柔软、更难以捉摸的东西。有一天，他坐在门廊上，从一只巨大的鸟和一条蛇之间的战斗中获得了灵感。蜿蜒的蛇用流畅的动作来躲避鸟的攻击。这只鸟筋疲力尽，最终放弃并飞走了。太极拳与道教密切相关，武当山的许多僧侣都练习某种形式的道术。

Hubei’s natural beauty is on show in the northeast of the province, especially around the vast, misty wilderness area known as Shennongjia (Shénnóngjià). Golden snub-nosed monkeys, giant salamanders, 3000m peaks that hold snow through spring, and lower-elevation wetlands come together to form an enchanting and uniquely Chinese nature reserve. Oh, and lest we forget, this is also the supposed stamping ground of none other than yěrén (野人), China’s very own Bigfoot.
湖北省的自然美景在该省的东北部得到了体现，尤其是在被称为神农架（Shénnóngjià）的广阔、云雾缭绕的荒野地区周围。金丝猴、巨型蝾螈、3000 米的山峰，春天积雪，海拔较低的湿地，共同构成了一个迷人而独特的中国自然保护区。哦，别忘了，这里也是所谓的野人 ，中国自己的大脚怪。

While it’s not easy to get to, even the sputtering bus ride here is a show-stopper, making it an interesting detour for the intrepid. Remember that transportation is infrequent, so you’ll need to spend at least two nights here in order to have one full day in the park. The town of Muyu (木鱼, Mùyú; elevation 1216m) is the main jumping-off point, where you can find a number of hotels, restaurants and basic provisions.
虽然到达并不容易，但即使是在这里的巴士之旅也是一个令人兴奋的景点，对于勇敢的人来说，这是一个有趣的弯路。请记住，交通不频繁，因此您需要在这里至少住两晚才能在公园里度过一整天。木鱼（Muyu，Mùyú;海拔 1216 米）是主要的起点，在那里你可以找到许多酒店、餐馆和基本设施。

There are no official taxis here and no local buses. The only way to get to and around the park is to hire a private driver: you can hire an entire minivan (¥400 to ¥500 for the day) or you can join up with a group and split the cost (¥100), which in Chinese is called pīnchē (拼车). Wedged into Hubei’s southwestern border region is a picturesque tableau of crumpled green mountains and terraced tea fields, as well as the occasional giant cliff face that’s high enough to catch a rock climber’s eye. Seat of the Tujia and Miao Autonomous Prefecture, Enshi (Ēnshī) is a low-key town whose main claim to fame is the Grand Canyon. OK, not that Grand Canyon, but instead a swath of impressive cliffs that tower over some lovely countryside below.
夹在湖北西南边境地区的是风景如画的风景，包括皱巴巴的绿色山脉和梯田茶田，以及偶尔出现的巨大悬崖面，其高度足以吸引攀岩者的眼球。土家族苗族自治州的所在地恩施（Ēnshī）是一个低调的小镇，其主要出名的地方是大峡谷。好吧，不是那个大峡谷，而是一片令人印象深刻的悬崖，耸立在下面一些美丽的乡村之上。

While the area can’t compare to the grander landscapes of Zhangjiajie or Shennongjia, it’s still beautiful, and the location along a main rail line makes for a convenient summer getaway for those in the sweltering cities of Chongqing and Wuhan.
虽然该地区无法与张家界或神农架的壮丽景观相提并论，但它仍然很美丽，而且位于主要铁路线沿线的位置为重庆和武汉等闷热城市的人们提供了便利的夏季度假胜地。

Encompassing sheer limestone cliffs and a series of waterfalls cascading down the side of a narrow river gorge, Enshi has some marvellous scenery. It’s divided in two sections: the lower gorge (云龙地缝, Yúnlóng Dìfèng; translated on signs as ‘Yunlong Crack’; elevation 1034m) and the upper clifftop area, Seven Star Village (七星寨, Qīxīng Zhài; elevation 1704m). The river gorge is relatively short with several lovely waterfalls, while the clifftop is more of a hike, eventually opening up to far-reaching views over the valley floor.
恩施拥有陡峭的石灰岩悬崖和一系列从狭窄的河谷一侧倾泻而下的瀑布，拥有一些奇妙的风景。它分为两部分： 下峡谷（ 云龙地缝 ，Yúnlóng Dìfèng，在标志上翻译为“云龙裂缝”，海拔 1034 米）和上悬崖顶地区七星寨 （ 七星寨 ，Qīxīng Zhài，海拔 1704 米）。河谷相对较短，有几个美丽的瀑布，而悬崖顶更像是徒步旅行，最终可以欣赏到谷底的远景。

Ticket prices are quite confusing for the uninitiated – you can choose to visit both areas or just one, with optional extras like the cable car adding to the final price. There are additional add-ons inside the park too, such as a long series of escalators descending from the upper section to the final bus stop – this costs an extra ¥30, though you can choose to walk instead. We recommend visiting both areas if you have a full day, and while the cable car is nice, you can save about ¥100 by taking the bus instead and walking from the last bus stop – note that you will need to change buses to do this.
对于外行来说，票价相当令人困惑——您可以选择参观两个区域或只参观一个区域，缆车等可选附加服务会添加到最终价格中。公园内还有额外的附加设施，例如从上层下降到终点巴士站的长长自动扶梯--这需要额外支付30日元，但你可以选择步行。如果您有一整天的时间，我们建议您参观这两个地区，虽然缆车很好，但乘坐巴士从最后一个巴士站步行可以节省约 100 日元 - 请注意，您需要换乘巴士才能做到这一点。

Now for the caveat: this is a popular destination that was not designed with big crowds in mind. The lower gorge in particular is very narrow and subject to excruciatingly long bottlenecks for the better part of an hour – get there as early (or late) as possible to avoid this.
现在需要注意的是：这是一个受欢迎的目的地，在设计时并没有考虑到大量人群。尤其是下峡谷非常狭窄，在一个小时的大部分时间里都会遇到极长的瓶颈——尽早（或晚点）到达那里以避免这种情况。

The upper gorge will take about 2½ hours to walk and can also get quite crowded. The first hour or so is just OK, and it’s not until you reach Ancient Elephant Mountain that the views really start to impress. The iconic formation here is A Stick of Incense (一炷香, Yī Zhù Xiāng), a slender and supremely photogenic limestone spire.
上峡谷步行大约需要 2 个半小时，而且也会变得非常拥挤。第一个小时左右还可以，直到你到达古象山，景色才真正开始令人印象深刻。这里的标志性结构是一炷香（一炷香 ，Yī Zhù Xiāng），这是一座细长且极其上镜的石灰岩尖顶。

All told, you should expect to spend about five to seven hours here. There are plenty of vendors serving simple meals (fried potatoes are a speciality) and drinks.
总而言之，您应该在这里度过大约五到七个小时。有很多摊贩提供简单的饭菜（炸土豆是特色菜）和饮料。

Enshi Grand Canyon is 50km northwest of town; buses (¥25, 1½ hours) leave regularly from the bus station (across from the train station) from 6.30am to 5.30pm – it’s a very scenic ride. The last bus back is at 6pm.
恩施大峡谷位于城镇西北 50 公里处;巴士（25 日元，1 小时半）从早上 6：30 到下午 5：30 从巴士站（火车站对面）定期出发——这是一次风景优美的旅程。最后一班车回程是下午 6 点。
