Martial Arts of China
中国武术

Unlike Western fighting arts – savate, kick-boxing etc – Chinese martial arts are deeply impregnated with religious and philosophical values. And, some might add, a morsel or two of magic. Many eminent exponents of gōngfū (功夫) – better known in the west as kung fu – were devout monks or religious recluses who drew inspiration from Buddhism and Taoism and sought a mystical communion with the natural world. These were not leisurely pursuits, but were closely entangled with the meaning and purpose of life.
与西方格斗艺术（萨瓦特、跆拳道等）不同 ， 中国武术深深地浸透着宗教和哲学价值观。而且，有些人可能会补充一点魔法。功夫（功夫 ）的许多杰出倡导者——在西方更广为人知的功夫——都是虔诚的僧侣或宗教隐士，他们从佛教和道教中汲取灵感，寻求与自然世界的神秘交流。这些都不是悠闲的追求，而是与人生的意义和目的紧密纠缠在一起。

Styles & Schools
风格和学校

China lays claim to a bewildering range of martial-arts styles, from the flamboyant and showy, inspired by the movements of animals (some legendary) or insects (such as Praying Mantis Boxing), to schools more empirically built upon the science of human movement (eg wing chun). On the outer fringes lie the esoteric arts, abounding with metaphysical feats, arcane practices and closely guarded techniques.
中国拥有一系列令人眼花缭乱的武术风格，从受动物（一些传说中的）或昆虫的动作（如螳螂拳击）启发的华丽和艳丽的武术，到更实证地建立在人体运动科学之上的流派（例如咏春拳）。在外围是深奥的艺术，充斥着形而上学的壮举、神秘的实践和严密保护的技术。

Many fighting styles were once secretively handed down for generations within families and it is only relatively recently that outsiders have been accepted as students. Some schools, especially the more obscure styles, have been driven to extinction partly due to their exclusivity and clandestine traditions.
许多战斗风格曾经在家庭内部秘密传承，直到最近才被接纳为学生。一些流派，尤其是更晦涩难懂的风格，已经濒临灭绝，部分原因是它们的排他性和秘密传统。

Some styles also found themselves divided into competing factions, each laying claim to the original teachings and techniques. Such styles may exist in a state of schism, while other styles have become part of the mainstream – the southern Chinese martial art of wing chun in particular has become globally recognised, largely due to its associations with Bruce Lee.
一些风格还发现自己分为相互竞争的派系，每个派系都声称拥有原始的教义和技术。这些风格可能存在于分裂状态，而其他风格已成为主流的一部分——尤其是中国南方武术咏春拳，已经获得全球认可，这主要是因为它与李小龙的联系。

Unlike Korean and Japanese arts such as taekwondo or karate-do, there is frequently no international regulatory body that oversees the syllabus, tournaments or grading requirements for China’s individual martial arts. Consequently students of China’s myriad martial arts may be rather unsure of what level they have attained and it is often down to the individual teacher to decide what to teach students, and how quickly.
与跆拳道或空手道等韩国和日本艺术不同，中国通常没有国际监管机构来监督中国个人武术的教学大纲、比赛或分级要求。因此，中国无数武术的学生可能相当不确定自己已经达到了什么水平，通常由老师个人决定教学生什么以及教多快。

Several Chinese styles of gōngfū (kung fu) include drunken sets, where the student mimics the supple movements of an inebriate.
几种中国风格的功夫 （功夫）包括醉酒套装，学生模仿醉酒者的柔顺动作。

Hard School
硬学校

Although there is considerable blurring between the two camps, Chinese martial arts are often distinguished between hard and soft schools. Typically aligned with Buddhism, the hard or ‘external’ (外家, wàijiā) school tends to be more vigorous, athletic and concerned with the development of power. Many of these styles are related to Shaolin boxing and the Shaolin Temple in Henan province.
尽管两个阵营之间存在相当大的模糊，但中国武术往往有硬派和软派的区别。通常与佛教结盟，硬派或“外家”（ 外家 ，wàijiā）学派往往更有活力、运动能力更强，并且关注权力的发展。其中许多风格与少林拳和河南省少林寺有关。

Shaolin boxing is forever associated with Bodhidharma, an ascetic Indian Buddhist monk who visited the Shaolin Temple and added a series of breathing and physical exercises to the Shaolin monks’ sedentary meditations. The Shaolin monks’ legendary endeavours and fearsome physical skills became known throughout China and beyond.
少林拳永远与印度佛教僧侣菩提达摩联系在一起，他参观了少林寺，并在少林僧人的久坐禅修中增加了一系列呼吸和体育锻炼。少林僧侣的传奇努力和可怕的身体技能在中国乃至其他地方广为人知。

Fujian white crane is a southern Chinese fighting style invented by a woman called Fang Qiniang, who based the art’s forms and strategy of attack and defence on careful observation of the bird’s movements.
福建白鹤是一种中国南方的格斗方式，由一位名叫方七娘的女士发明，她根据对鸟类运动的仔细观察，发明了这种艺术的形式和进攻和防御策略。

Soft School
软校

Usually inspired by Taoism, the soft or ‘internal’ Chinese school (内家, nèijiā) develops pliancy and softness as a weapon against hard force. Taichi (tàijí quán) is the best-known soft school, famed for its slow and lithe movements and an emphasis on cultivating qì (energy). Attacks are met with yielding movements that smother the attacking force and lead the aggressor off balance. Other soft schools include the circular moves of bagua zhang and the linear boxing patterns of xíngyì quán, based on five basic punches – each linked to one of the five elements of Chinese philosophy – and the movements of 12 animals.
通常受到道教的启发，软派或“内家”中国学派（ 内家 ，nèijiā）发展出柔韧和柔软，作为对抗硬力的武器。太极拳（tàijí quán）是最著名的软流派，以其缓慢而轻盈的动作和强调培养气（能量 ）而闻名。攻击会遇到屈服的动作，扼杀攻击部队并导致侵略者失去平衡。其他软流派包括八卦掌的圆圈动作和辛义拳的线性拳法，基于五种基本拳法——每种拳法都与中国哲学的五要素之一有关——以及 12 种动物的动作。

Zhang Sanfeng, the founder of taichi, was supposedly able to walk more than 1000 li (around 560km) a day; others say he lived for more than 200 years!
太极拳的创始人张三丰据说每天能够步行 1000 多里 （约 560 公里）;还有人说他活了 200 多年！

Forms
形式

Most students of Chinese martial arts – hard or soft – learn forms (套路; tàolu), a series of movements linked together into a pattern, which embody the principal punches and kicks of the style. In essence, forms are unwritten compendiums of the style, to ensure passage from one generation to the next. The number and complexity of forms varies from style to style: taichi may only have one form, though it may be very lengthy (the long form of the Yang style takes around 20 minutes to perform). Five ancestors boxing has dozens of forms, while wing chun only has three empty-hand forms.
大多数中国武术学生——无论是硬武还是软武——学习形式（ 套路 ;tàolu），一系列动作连接在一起形成一个模式，体现了该风格的主要拳打脚踢。从本质上讲，形式是风格的不成文纲要，以确保从一代传到下一代。形式的数量和复杂程度因风格而异：太极拳可能只有一种形式，尽管可能非常长（阳式的长形式大约需要 20 分钟才能演奏）。五祖拳有几十种形式，而咏春拳只有三种空手形式。

Qigong
气功

Closely linked to both the hard and especially the soft martial-arts schools is the practice of qigong (气功, qìgōng), a technique for cultivating and circulating qì (energy) around the body. Qì can be developed for use in fighting to protect the body, as a source of power or for curative and health-giving purposes.
与硬武术流派，尤其是软武术流派密切相关的是气功（气功 ，qìgōng），这是一种在身体周围培养和循环气（气 ）的技术。 气可以用于战斗以保护身体，作为力量的源泉或用于治疗和有益健康的目的。

Qì can be developed in a number of ways – by standing still in fixed postures, or with gentle exercises, meditation and measured breathing techniques. Taichi itself is a moving form of qigong cultivation, while at the harder end of the spectrum a host of qigong exercises aim to make specific parts of the body impervious to attack.
气可以通过多种方式发展——通过以固定姿势静止不动，或者通过温和的练习、冥想和有节制的呼吸技巧。太极拳本身是一种移动的气功修炼形式，而在更难的一端，一系列气功练习旨在使身体的特定部位不受攻击。

The linear movements and five punches of the internal Chinese martial art, body-mind boxing (xíngyì quán), possibly evolved from spear-fighting techniques.
中国武术的线性动作和五拳，身心拳 （xíngyì quán）， 可能是从枪术演变而来的。

Bagua Zhang
张八卦

One of the more esoteric and obscure of the soft Taoist martial arts, bagua zhang (八卦掌, bāguà zhǎng; eight trigram boxing, also known as pa-kua) is also one of the most intriguing. The bagua zhang student wheels around in a circle, rapidly changing direction and speed, occasionally thrusting out a palm strike.
八卦掌 （ 八卦掌 ，八卦拳 ，又称八卦拳）也是最有趣的武术之一。 八卦张学生转了一圈，快速变换方向和速度，偶尔伸出一掌击。

Bagua zhang draws its inspiration from the trigrams (an arrangement of three broken and unbroken lines) of the classic Book of Changes (Yìjīng or I Ching), the ancient oracle used for divination. The trigrams are typically arranged in circular form and it is this pattern that is traced out by the bagua zhang exponent. Training commences by just walking the circle so the student gradually becomes infused with its patterns and rhythms.
八卦章的灵感来自经典《 易经 》的卦（三条断线和不断线的排列），易经是用于占卜的古代神谕。卦通常以圆形形式排列，正是这种模式由八卦张指数追踪出来。训练从绕圈开始，让学生逐渐融入它的模式和节奏。

A hallmark of the style is the exclusive use of the palm, not the fist, as the principal weapon. The palm can transmit a lot of power and is also better protected than the fist as it is cushioned by muscle.
这种风格的一个标志是专门使用手掌而不是拳头作为主要武器。手掌可以传递大量的力量，并且由于受到肌肉的缓冲，因此也比拳头受到更好的保护。

The student must become proficient in the subterfuge, evasion, speed and unpredictability that are hallmarks of bagua zhang. Force is generally not met with force, but deflected by the circular movements cultivated in students through their meditations upon the circle. Circular forms – arcing, twisting, twining and spinning – are the mainstay of all movements, radiating from the waist.
学生必须精通八卦掌的诡计、逃避、速度和不可预测性，这些都是八卦掌的标志。力通常不会遇到力，而是被学生通过对圆圈的冥想培养的圆周运动所偏转。圆形形式——弧形、扭曲、缠绕和旋转——是所有运动的主要运动，从腰部辐射。

Despite being dated by historians to the 19th century, bagua zhang is quite probably a very ancient art. Beneath the Taoist overlay, the movements and patterns of the art suggest a possibly animistic or shamanistic origin, which gives the art its timeless rhythms.
尽管八卦掌被历史学家追溯到 19 世纪，但它很可能是一种非常古老的艺术。在道教的覆盖下，艺术的动作和图案暗示着可能是万物有灵论或萨满教的起源，这赋予了艺术永恒的节奏。

BOOKS ON CHINESE MARTIAL ARTS
中国武术书籍

If you can track down a copy, John F Gilbey’s The Way of a Warrior is a tongue-in-cheek, expertly written and riveting account of the Oriental fighting arts and their mysteries.
如果你能找到一本，约翰·吉尔贝的《 战士之道》 是一部半开玩笑、写得专业、引人入胜的东方格斗艺术及其奥秘的描述。

For metaphysical pointers, soft-school adherents can dip into Laotzu’s terse but inspiring The Classic of the Way and Its Power.
对于形而上学的指导，软学派的追随者可以深入研究老子简洁但鼓舞人心的《 道及其力量的经典 》。

A couple of very useful books (both in print at the time of writing) on bagua zhang include Pa-Kua: Chinese Boxing for Fitness & Self-Defense (Robert Smith; Blue Snake Books) and A Short Guide to Baguazhang Circle Walking (Edward Hines). If you can get hold of the magnificent short story ‘Pop Songs and Pa-kua’ (in The Way of a Warrior by John F Gilbey aka Robert Smith; North Atlantic Books), do so.
关于八卦掌的几本非常有用的书（在撰写本文时均已印刷），包括《 八卦：中国拳击健身与自卫》（ 罗伯特·史密斯;蓝蛇书）和八卦掌环行简略指南 （爱德华·海因斯）。如果你能拿到宏伟的短篇小说《流行歌曲和 Pa-kua》（在约翰·吉尔比又名罗伯特·史密斯的《 战士之道 》中;北大西洋图书），这样做。

Praying mantis master Fan Yook Tung once killed two stampeding bulls with an iron-palm technique.
螳螂大师范玉彤曾用铁掌法杀死两头踩踏的公牛。

Wing Chun
咏春拳

Conceived by a Buddhist nun from the Shaolin Temple called Ng Mui, who taught her skills to a young girl called Wing Chun (詠春), this is a fast and dynamic system of fighting that promises quick results for novices. Wing chun (yǒng chūn) was the style that taught Bruce Lee how to move and, though he ultimately moved away from it to develop his own school, wing chun had an enormous influence on the Hong Kong fighter and actor.
由少林寺的一位名叫吴梅的佛教尼姑构思，她将自己的技能传授给一个名叫咏春的年轻女孩，这是一种快速而动态的格斗系统，有望为新手带来快速的结果。咏春拳（yǒng chūn）是教会李小龙如何移动的风格，尽管他最终放弃了它来发展自己的流派，但咏春拳对这位香港拳手和演员产生了巨大的影响。

Wing chun emphasises speed over strength and evasion – rapid strikes and low kicks are its hallmark techniques. Forms are simple and direct, dispensing with the pretty flourishes that clutter other styles.
咏春拳强调速度而不是力量和躲避——快速打击和低踢是它的标志性技术。形式简单而直接，摒弃了杂乱无章的其他风格的漂亮华丽。

The art can perhaps best be described as scientific. There are none of the animal forms that make other styles so exciting and mysterious. Instead wing chun is built around its centre-line theory, which draws an imaginary line down the human body and centres all attacks and blocks along that line. The line runs through the sensitive regions: eyes, nose, mouth, throat, heart, solar plexus and groin and any blow on these points is debilitating and dangerous.
这门艺术也许可以用科学来形容。没有一种动物形态能让其他风格如此令人兴奋和神秘。相反，咏春拳是围绕其中心线理论建立的，该理论在人体上画了一条假想的线，并沿着这条线将所有攻击和格挡都集中在中心。这条线穿过敏感区域：眼睛、鼻子、嘴巴、喉咙、心脏、太阳神经丛和腹股沟，对这些部位的任何打击都会使人衰弱和危险。

The three empty hand forms – which look bizarre to non-initiates – train arm and leg movements that both attack and defend this line. None of the blocks stray beyond the width of the shoulders, as this is the limit of possible attacks, and punches follow the same theory. Punches are delivered with great speed in a straight line, along the shortest distance between puncher and punched. All of this gives wing chun its distinctive simplicity and economy of movement.
三种空手形态——对于非新手来说看起来很奇怪——训练手臂和腿部的动作，既攻击又防守这条线。没有一个块会偏离肩膀的宽度，因为这是可能攻击的极限，并且拳头遵循相同的理论。冲头沿着冲孔机和冲孔机之间的最短距离沿直线以极快的速度进行。所有这些都赋予了咏春拳独特的简单性和运动经济性。

Iron shirt (tiěshān) is an external kung fu (gōngfū) qigong training exercise that circulates and concentrates the qì (energy) in certain areas to protect the body from impacts during a fight.
铁衫（tiěshān）是一种外功夫（gōngfū）气功训练练习，可循环并将气 （能量）集中在某些区域，以保护身体在战斗中免受冲击。
