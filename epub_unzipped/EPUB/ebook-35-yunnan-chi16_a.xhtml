JADE DRAGON SNOW MOUNTAIN
玉龙雪山

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (玉龙雪山, Yùlóng Xuěshān; ¥100; hcable cars 7am-6pm) soars to some 5596m, and the massif’s thirteen peaks dominate the skyline of the surrounding regions. Undeniably beautiful, the national park is also undeniably overcrowded – a classic case of over-tourism, compounded by a lack of any nature trails or hiking areas whatsoever. If you’re looking for pristine nature, consider Tiger Leaping Gorge or a day hike out of Baisha. If you just want to get up close and personal with peaks, though, visit Jade Dragon Snow Mountain.
玉龙雪山（玉龙雪山 ，Yùlóng Xuěshān;100 日元; h 缆车上午 7 点至下午 6 点） 飙升至约 5596 米，地块的 13 座山峰占据了周边地区的天际线。不可否认，国家公园美丽，但不可否认的是，它也人满为患——这是过度旅游的典型案例，再加上缺乏任何自然小径或远足区。如果您正在寻找原始的自然风光，可以考虑虎跳峡或从白沙出发的一日徒步旅行。不过，如果您只是想近距离接触山峰，请参观玉龙雪山。

Buses (¥15, one hour) from Lijiang arrive at a parking area where you can purchase tickets for the various cable cars and chairlifts that ascend the mountain, but before you even arrive to the service centre you’ll need to pony up ¥100 for the entrance fee. This grants you entrance to the service centre and surrounding Dry Sea Meadow (干海子, Gānhǎizi), but not much more.
从丽江出发的巴士（15 日元，一小时）到达停车场，您可以在那里购买上山的各种缆车和缆车的车票，但在到达服务中心之前，您需要支付 100 日元的入场费。这样，您就可以进入服务中心和周围的干海草甸 （ 干海子 ，Gānhǎizi），但仅此而已。

This is also where the Impression Lijiang (印象丽江, Yìnxiàng Lìjiāng; Yulong Snow Mountain National Scenic Area, 玉龙雪山国家级风景区; ¥280; h1pm) show is held, a mega song-and-dance performance. Note that if you are going to the performance you will also have to pay the park admission fees. Most independent travellers skip this, but those booking an organised tour may find it included in the package price.
这里也是印象丽江 （ 印象丽江 ，Yìnxiàng Lìjiāng;玉龙雪山国家级风景区 ;¥280; h 下午 1 点） 举行大型歌舞表演。请注意，如果您要去观看演出，您还需要支付公园入场费。大多数自由行旅客都会跳过这一点，但那些预订有组织的旅游的人可能会发现它包含在套餐价格中。

From the bus drop-off, a counter on the right sells tickets for separate cable cars to Yak Meadow (牦牛坪, Máoniú Píng) and Spruce Meadow (云杉坪, Yúnshān Píng). Cable-car trips for both Yak Meadow (¥45) and Spruce Meadow (¥40) also require a bus transfer (¥20 round-trip); you could conceivably walk it, but the road to the Yak Meadow cable car is 20km from the visitor centre. Just below the Spruce Meadow cable car station (and served by bus) are the Blue Moon Valley (蓝月谷, Lányuè Gǔ) and the White Water River (白水河, Báishuǐ Hé) that runs through it. In summer, when crowds for the cable cars are long (up to four hours’ wait), many travellers just do the trip to the lake and Yak Meadow or Spruce Meadow.
从巴士下车站出发，右边的柜台出售前往牦牛坪 （Máoniú Píng）和云杉草甸 （ 云杉坪 ）的单独缆车票。牦牛草甸（45 日元）和云杉草甸（40 日元）的缆车之旅也需要乘坐巴士（往返 20 日元）;你可以想象步行，但通往 Yak Meadow 缆车的路距离游客中心 20 公里。云杉草甸缆车站（有巴士服务）的正下方是蓝月谷（蓝月谷 ，Lányuè Gǔ）和流经它的白水河 （ 白水河 ，Báishuǐ Hé）。在夏季，缆车的人群很长（等待长达四个小时），许多旅行者只是去湖边和牦牛草甸或云杉草甸旅行。

If you have the time to wait for it, the main draw of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain is Glacier Park (冰川公园, Bīngchuān Gōngyuán). Ride up nearly 3km of cable car to an elevation of 4506m, where a 200m wooden pathway gets visitors right up next to the mountain’s largest glacier. Buy cable car (¥120) and bus transfer (¥20 round-trip) tickets from a separate ticket office east of where buses from Lijiang drop passengers, across from the Impression Lijiang theatre. In the high season, expect to wait an hour or more for a bus transfer to the cable-car station and then a further three to four hours for the cable car itself. It can get cold and windy at the top in midsummer; you’ll see plenty of domestic tourists wearing rented down jackets, and you’d be well advised to pack your own warm clothes for the trip.
如果你有时间等待，玉龙雪山的主要景点是冰川公园（冰川公园 ，Bīngchuān Gōngyuán）。 乘坐近 3 公里的缆车到达海拔 4506 米的地方，一条 200 米的木制小路将游客带到山上最大的冰川旁边。在丽江巴士下车处以东的单独售票处购买缆车（120 日元）和巴士接送（往返 20 日元）票，丽江印象剧院对面。在旺季，预计需要等待一个小时或更长时间才能乘坐巴士前往缆车站，然后再等待三到四个小时才能乘坐缆车本身。盛夏时节，山顶会变得寒冷多风;您会看到很多国内游客穿着租来的羽绒服，建议您自己带上保暖的衣服。

Buses (¥15, one hour) returning to Lijiang leave every 20 minutes until 5pm from a parking lot just between the Glacier Park ticket office and the Impression Lijiang theatre. The line is not numbered, so look for Yulong Xueshan (玉龙雪山, Yùlóng Xuěshān) in Chinese.

Torch FestivalCULTURAL
火炬节文化

(火把节, Huǒbǎ Jié; hJul)

The torch festival is also celebrated by the Bai in the Dali region and the Yi all over the southwest. The origin of this festival can be traced back to the Nanzhao kingdom, when the wife of a man burned to death by the king eluded the romantic entreaties of the monarch by leaping into a fire.
大理地区的白族和西南各地的彝族也庆祝火把节。这个节日的起源可以追溯到南诏国，当时一个被国王烧死的男人的妻子，为了躲避君主的浪漫恳求，跳入了火中。

These days, flaming torches are paraded through the streets to much merriment, for three days from the 24th day of the sixth lunar month.
这些天，从农历六月二十四开始，为期三天，人们在街上挥舞着火把，欢呼雀跃。

Sanduo FestivalCULTURAL
三多节文化

(三朵节, Sānduǒ Jié; hApr)
（ 三朵节 ，Sānduǒ Jié; h 4 月）

Held on the 13th day of the third lunar month, this festival sees Naxi people offering a sacrifice – normally a goat – to their patron saint Sanduo. The biggest celebration takes place at the Sanduo Temple (三朵寺, Sānduǒ Sì) on Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, where there’s a big market and much revelry.
这个节日在农历三月十三举行，纳西族人向他们的守护神三多献上祭品——通常是山羊。最大的庆祝活动在玉龙雪山的三朵寺（三朵寺 ，Sānduǒ Sì）举行，那里有一个大市场，有很多狂欢。

An interesting local historical story has it that the original Naxi chieftain, whose former home is the Mu Family Mansion, would not allow the old town to be girded by a city wall because drawing a box around the Chinese character of his family name would change the character from mù (木, ‘wood’) to kùn (困, ‘surrounded’ or ‘hard-pressed’).

Baba (粑粑, bābā) is the Lijiang local speciality – thick Naxi flatbreads of wheat, served plain or stuffed with meat, vegetables or sweets. There are always several Naxi items on menus, including the famous ‘Naxi omelette’ (baba with a layer of eggs and tomato cooked on top) and ‘Naxi sandwich’ (goat’s cheese, tomato and fried egg between two pieces of baba). Try locally produced qīng méi jiǔ (a plum-based wine with a 500-year history) – it tastes like a semisweet sherry.

THE NAXI
纳西族

Lijiang has been the homeland of the 300,000-odd Naxi (纳西, Nàxī; na-see; also spelled Nakhi and Nahi) minority for about the last 1400 years. The Naxi descend from ethnically Tibetan Qiang tribes and lived until recently in matrilineal families. Since local rulers were always male it wasn’t truly matriarchal, but women still seemed to run the show.
丽江一直是 300,000 多万纳西族（ 纳西 ，Nàxī;na-see;也拼写为 Nakhi 和 Nahi）大约在过去 1400 年里少数民族。纳西族是藏族羌族部落的后裔，直到最近才生活在母系家庭中。由于地方统治者总是男性，所以并不是真正的母系制，但女性似乎仍然在主导。

The Naxi matriarchs maintained their hold over the men with flexible arrangements for love affairs. The azhu (friend) system allowed a couple to become lovers without setting up joint residence. Both partners would continue to live in their respective homes; the boyfriend would spend the nights at his girlfriend’s house but return to live and work at his mother’s house during the day. Any children born to the couple belonged to the woman, who was responsible for bringing them up. The man provided support, but once the relationship was over, so was the support. Children lived with their mothers and no special effort was made to recognise paternity. Women inherited all property and disputes were adjudicated by female elders.
纳西族的女族长通过灵活的爱情安排来保持对男人的控制。azhu（朋友）制度允许一对夫妇在不设立共同住所的情况下成为恋人。双方将继续住在各自的家中;男朋友会在女友家过夜，但白天回到母亲家生活和工作。这对夫妇所生的任何孩子都属于负责抚养他们的妇女。男人提供了支持，但一旦关系结束，支持也随之结束。孩子们与母亲住在一起，没有特别努力承认亲子关系。妇女继承了所有财产，纠纷由女性长辈裁决。

There are strong matriarchal influences in the Naxi language. Nouns enlarge their meaning when the word for ‘female’ is added; conversely, the addition of the word for ‘male’ will decrease the meaning. For example, ‘stone’ plus ‘female’ conveys the idea of a boulder; ‘stone’ plus ‘male’ conveys the idea of a pebble.
纳西语有很强的母系影响。当添加“女性”一词时，名词会扩大其含义;相反，添加“男性”一词会减少含义。例如，“石头”加上“女性”传达了巨石的概念;“石头”加上“男性”传达了鹅卵石的概念。

RELIGIOUS SITES AROUND LIJIANG
丽江周边宗教场所

There are a number of historic monasteries around Lijiang, all Tibetan in origin and belonging to the Karmapa (Red Hat) sect. Most were extensively damaged during the Cultural Revolution and there’s not much monastic activity nowadays.
丽江周边有许多历史悠久的寺院，都是起源于藏族的，属于噶玛巴（红帽）教派。大多数在文化大革命期间遭到严重破坏，如今寺院活动不多。

Jade Peak Monastery (玉峰寺, Yùfēng Sì; %888 519 0164; Yufeng Si Lu, 玉峰寺路; ¥17; h8am-5.30pm), one of the region’s last functional Tibetan monasteries, is on a hillside about 6km past Baisha. The monastery sits at the foot of Yùlóng Xuěshān (5500m) and was established in 1756. The monastery’s main attraction nowadays is the Camellia Tree of 10,000 Blossoms (Wànduǒ Shānchá). Ten thousand might be something of an exaggeration, but locals claim that the tree produces at least 4000 blossoms between February and April. A monk on the grounds risked his life to keep the tree secretly watered during the Cultural Revolution.
玉峰寺 （ 玉峰寺 ，Yùfēng Sì; % 888 519 0164;玉峰寺路，玉峰寺路 ; ¥17; h 上午 8 点至下午 5 点 30 分） 是该地区最后的西藏寺院之一，位于白沙约 6 公里处的山坡上。该寺院坐落在玉龙雪山（5500m）脚下，建于 1756 年。如今，寺院的主要景点是万花山茶树 （Wànduǒ Shānchá）。一万朵可能有点夸张，但当地人声称这棵树在二月至四月期间至少开出 4000 朵花。在文化大革命期间，场地里的一位僧侣冒着生命危险偷偷给这棵树浇水。

Lijiang is also famed for its temple frescoes, most of which were painted during the 15th and 16th centuries by Tibetan, Naxi, Bai and Han artists; many were restored during the later Qing dynasty. They depict various Taoist, Chinese and Tibetan Buddhist themes and can be found on the interior walls of temples in the area. Remember, though, that the Cultural Revolution caused havoc around here so many are desecrated in various ways.
丽江还以其寺庙壁画而闻名，其中大部分是藏族、纳西族、白族和汉族艺术家在 15 世纪和 16 世纪绘制的;许多在清朝后期被修复。它们描绘了各种道教、中国和藏传佛教主题，可以在该地区寺庙的内墙上找到。但请记住，文化大革命在这里造成了严重破坏，因此许多人以各种方式被亵渎。

The gorge hike is not to be taken lightly. Even for those in good physical shape, it can be a workout and tough on the knees. The path is alarmingly narrow in places, making it sometimes dangerous. When it’s raining (especially in July and August), landslides and swollen waterfalls can block the paths, in particular on the low road. (The best time to come is May and the start of June, when the hills are afire with plant and flower life.)
峡谷徒步旅行不容掉以轻心。即使对于那些身体状况良好的人来说，这也可能是一种锻炼，并且对膝盖很有压力。这条路有些地方非常狭窄，有时很危险。下雨时（尤其是七月和八月），山体滑坡和暴涨的瀑布会阻塞道路，尤其是在低矮的道路上。（最好的时间是五月和六月初，此时山上充满了植物和花卉。

A few people – including a handful of foreign travellers – have died in the gorge. During the past decade, there have also been cases of travellers being assaulted on the trail. It’s safer in all ways not to do the hike alone – though it is rare that anybody reports serious problems.
一些人——包括少数外国旅行者——在峡谷中死亡。在过去的十年中，也发生了旅行者在步道上遭到袭击的案例。从各个方面来说，不独自徒步旅行都更安全——尽管很少有人报告严重的问题。

SHANGRI-LA: FACT & FICTION
香格里拉：事实与虚构

At first it seemed like a typically overstated tourist campaign: ‘Shangri-la Found’. Only they weren’t kidding. In November 1997 ‘experts’ had established with ‘certainty’ that the fabled ‘Shangri-la’ of James Hilton’s 1933 bestseller Lost Horizon was, indeed, in Deqin County.
起初，这似乎是一个典型的夸张的旅游活动：“香格里拉发现”。只是他们不是在开玩笑。1997 年 11 月，“专家”确定詹姆斯·希尔顿 1933 年畅销书《 失落的地平线》 中传说中的“香格里拉”确实在德钦县。

Hilton’s novel (later filmed by Frank Capra and starring Ronald Coleman and Jane Wyatt) tells the story of four travellers who are hijacked and crash-land in a mountain utopia ruled by a 163-year-old holy man. This ‘Shangri-la’ is in the Valley of the Blue Moon, a beautiful fertile valley backed by a perfect pyramid peak, Mt Karakul.
希尔顿的小说（后来由弗兰克·卡普拉拍摄，罗纳德·科尔曼和简·怀亚特主演）讲述了四名旅行者被劫持并迫降在一位 163 岁圣人统治的山区乌托邦的故事。这家“香格里拉”位于蓝月谷，这是一个美丽肥沃的山谷，背靠完美的金字塔山卡拉库尔山。

The claim is based primarily on the fact that Deqin’s Kawa Karpo peak resembles the ‘pyramid-shaped’ landmark of Mt Karakul. Also, the county’s blood-red valleys with three parallel rivers fit a valley from Lost Horizon.
该说法主要基于这样一个事实，即德钦的卡瓦卡尔波峰类似于卡拉库尔山的“金字塔形”地标。此外，该县血红色的山谷和三条平行的河流适合失落地平线的山谷。

One plausible theory is that Hilton, writing the novel in northwest London, based his descriptions of Shangri-la on articles by Joseph Rock that he had read in National Geographic magazine, detailing Rock’s expeditions to remote parts of Lijiang and Deqin. Hilton’s invented place name ‘Shangri-la’ may have been a corruption of the word Shambhala, a mystical Buddhist paradise.
一种合理的理论是，希尔顿在伦敦西北部写这部小说时，对香格里拉的描述是基于他在 《国家地理》 杂志上读到的约瑟夫·洛克的文章，详细介绍了洛克对丽江和德钦偏远地区的探险。希尔顿发明的地名“香格里拉”可能是香巴拉这个词的讹误，香巴拉是一个神秘的佛教天堂。

After Deqin staked its claim to the name Shangri-la, rival bids popped up around Yunnan. Cízhōng in Wēxī County pointed out that its Catholic churches and Tibetan monasteries live side by side in the valley. Meanwhile, Dàochéng, just over the border in Sichuan, had a strong bid based around the pyramid peak of its mountain Channa Dorje and the fact that Rock wrote about the region in several articles.
在德勤对香格里拉这个名字提出要求后，云南各地出现了竞争对手的竞标。渭西县慈雄指出，其天主教堂和藏族寺院并排居住在山谷中。与此同时，位于四川边境的道城以其禅那多杰山的金字塔峰以及洛克在几篇文章中写到该地区的事实而进行了强烈的竞标。

Cynics have had a field day with this and the resulting hijacking of the concept, part of which was to establish tourism as an industry to replace logging, which had been banned.
愤世嫉俗者对此以及由此导致的对这一概念的劫持进行了实地考察，其中一部分是将旅游业确立为一种产业，以取代已被禁止的伐木业。

Shangri-la is at its heart surely a metaphor. As a skinny-dipping Jane Wyatt says in the film version of the book: ‘I’m sure there’s a wish for Shangri-la in everyone’s heart…’
香格里拉的核心无疑是一个隐喻。正如电影版书中瘦身的简·怀亚特所说：“我相信每个人的心中都有一个对香格里拉的愿望......”

Deqin (Déqīn) lies in some of the most ruggedly gorgeous scenery in China. Snugly cloud-high at an average altitude of 3550m, it rests in the shadow of one of China’s most magical mountains, Kawa Karpo (梅里雪山, Méilǐ Xuěshān); at 6740m, it is Yunnan’s highest peak and straddles the Yunnan–Tibet border.
德琴（德琴）位于中国一些最崎岖、最美丽的风景中。它位于平均海拔 3550 米的云层中，坐落在中国最神奇的山脉之一卡瓦卡波山 （ 梅里雪山 ，Méilǐ Xuěshān） 的阴影下;它海拔 6740 米，是云南最高峰，横跨云藏边境。

More than 80% of locals are Tibetan, though two dozen other minorities also live here, including one of the few settlements of non-Hui Muslims in China. The town of Deqin itself is unattractive and mostly a staging post to the nearby mountains of Meili Snow Mountain National Park (梅里雪山国家公园, Méilǐ Xuěshān Guó Jīa Gōng Yuǎn) in surrounding Deqin county; all of which are incorporated by the Díqìng Tibetan Autonomous Prefecture (迪庆藏族自治州, Díqìng zàngzú zìzhìzhōu).

Beyond the town, the villages of Feilaisi, Mingyong and Yubeng serve the needs of travellers with guesthouses and restaurants.

Tumbling off the side of Kawa Karpo peak is the 12km-long Mingyong Glacier. At over 13 sq km, it is not only the lowest glacier in China (around 2200m) but also an oddity – a monsoon marine glacier, which basically translates as having an ecosystem that couldn’t possibly be more diverse: tundra, taiga, broadleaf forest and meadow. Sadly, this natural wonder is also retreating at an alarming – and increasing – rate.
从 Kawa Karpo 峰的一侧倾泻而下的是 12 公里长的明永冰川。它面积超过 13 平方公里，不仅是中国最低的冰川（约 2200 米），而且还是一个奇怪的现象——季风海洋冰川，基本上翻译过来就意味着拥有一个再多样化的生态系统：苔原、针叶林、阔叶林和草地。可悲的是，这个自然奇观也在以惊人的速度消退，而且越来越快。

The mountain, representing the embodiment of the warrior god Kawa Karpo, has been a pilgrimage site for centuries and you’ll still meet a few Tibetan pilgrims, some of whom circumambulate the mountain over seven days in autumn. Surrounding villages are known as ‘heaven villages’ because of the dense fog that hangs about in spring and summer.
这座山代表着武神卡瓦噶尔波的化身，几个世纪以来一直是朝圣地，您仍然会遇到一些西藏朝圣者，其中一些人在秋天绕山 7 天。周围的村庄被称为“天堂村”，因为春夏两季雾气弥漫。

The way to the glacier leads up from Mingyong’s central square. The visitor centre sells tickets (¥75 round-trip) for electric carts to shuttle you 3.6km up to the start of the walk; you can also follow the same road on foot. From the end of the cart road it’s a steady uphill of 2.5km via a new staircase that rises nearly 500 vertical metres – the old horse path is visible below, but there are only a few spots where it’s accessible. At the top is Lotus Temple (莲花庙, Liánhuā Miào), which offers incredible views of the glacier framed by prayer flags and chörten (Tibetan stupas). It’s home to a single monk who lives here for a month at a stretch without leaving the mountain.
通往冰川的路从明永的中央广场向上延伸。游客中心出售电动推车票（往返 75 日元），可接送您 3.6 公里到达步行起点;您也可以沿着同一条路步行。从马车路的尽头开始，通过一个垂直高度近 500 米的新楼梯，这是一条 2.5 公里的稳定上坡路——下面可以看到旧的马道，但只有少数几个地方可以到达。山顶是莲花庙 （ 莲花庙 ，Liánhuā Miào），从这里可以欣赏到经幡和藏佛塔的冰川壮丽景色，这里住着一位单身僧侣，他在这里住了一个月，足不出山。

Mingyong village consists of only a couple of hotels, restaurants and shops. You can overnight in the very basic Renqin Hotel (仁钦酒店, Rénqīn Jiǔdiàn; %139 8871 4330; Mingyong; d ¥80; W). From Deqin, irregular private minibuses to Mingyong leave from the bridge near the market at the top end of town (¥20 per person), or you can hire your own for around ¥150. If you’re coming from Yubeng, you could also hike a high trail to Mingyong from Xidang in around three hours if you hoof it.
明永村只有几家酒店、餐馆和商店。你可以在非常基本的仁钦酒店 （ 仁钦酒店 ，Rénqīn Jiǔdiàn; % 139 8871 4330;明永;d 80 日元; W ）。从德勤出发，不定期的私人小巴从城镇顶端市场附近的桥上出发（每人 20 日元），或者您可以自己租用，费用约为 150 日元。如果您从雨蚡出发，如果您骑着蹄子，您还可以在大约三个小时内从西当徒步到明勇。

The road from Deqin descends into the dramatic Mekong Gorge. Six kilometres before Mingyong the road crosses the Mekong River and branches off to Xidang. There is a checkpoint here where you will need to show your national park ticket or buy one if you haven’t already.
从德勤出发的道路下降到壮观的湄公河峡谷。在明永前六公里处，这条路穿过湄公河，分支到西当。这里有一个检查站，您需要出示您的国家公园门票，或者如果您还没有购买，请购买一张。

YUBENG & KAWA KARPO HIKES
YUBENG & KAWA KARPO 徒步旅行

The principal reason to visit Deqin is the chance to hike to the foot of Kawa Karpo. The main destination is Yubeng (雨崩, Yǔbēng) village, from where you can make day hikes to mountain meadows, the Ice Lake and the fabulous Sacred Waterfall.
参观德钦的主要原因是有机会徒步前往卡瓦卡波山脚下。主要目的地是雨崩村，从那里您可以徒步一日游到山地草甸、冰湖和神话般的圣瀑布。

The five- to six-hour (10km) trek to Yubeng starts at the Xidang Hot Spring (西当温泉, Xīdāng Wēnquán), about 6km uphill past Xidang village. You’ll pay the ¥55 entry fee for Yubeng here if you haven’t already. Jeeps (¥200 per person, one hour) make the trip from the hot spring to Yubeng – they use the same road as hikers and don’t slow down to pass, so remain vigilant if you’re on foot.
前往雨崩的 5 到 6 小时（10 公里）徒步旅行从西当温泉（西当温泉 ，Xīdāng Wēnquán）开始，经过西当村上坡约 6 公里。如果您还没有支付 55 日元的 Yubeng 入场费，请在这里支付。吉普车（每人 200 日元，一小时）从温泉到雨崩——它们与徒步旅行者使用同一条路，并且不会减速通过，因此如果您步行，请保持警惕。

While it is possible to overnight at simple guesthouses in Xidang (from ¥30), it’s actually more efficient to overnight in Feilaisi, from which shared vans will deposit you directly at the hot spring. The drive from Feilaisi to Xidang takes 1½ hours.
虽然可以在西当的简单宾馆过夜（30 日元起），但实际上在飞来寺过夜更有效率，共享面包车将直接将您送到温泉。从飞来寺到西当的车程需要 1 个半小时。

Yubeng consists of two sections. You first arrive in ‘Upper Yubeng’, which contains more guesthouses, then the trail continues another 1km to ‘Lower Yubeng’. Booking ahead at peak periods is a good idea, but in other seasons the abundance of rooms here rarely get full. Dorm beds in Upper Yubeng are available from ¥40 and private rooms start at ¥120. Of special note is the Interval Time Guesthouse (间隔时光, Jiàngé Shíguāng; %180 0887 8300; Upper Yubeng; dm/d ¥50/220; W), which has knowledgable staff and decent food. Lower Yubeng is slightly cheaper (from ¥35 for dorm beds, or ¥120 for private rooms) with the Mystic Waterfall Lodge (神瀑客栈, Shénpù Kèzhàn; %887 841 1082; Lower Yubeng; dm/d ¥35/120; W) of similar quality to other options, but in the nicest location right at the bottom of the village. Guides in the village cost around ¥200 per day. Supplies (food and water) are pricey in Yubeng, so stock up in Feilaisi.
Yubeng 由两个部分组成。您首先到达包含更多宾馆的“上雨貲”，然后小径继续行驶 1 公里到达“下谛蚌”。在高峰期提前预订是个好主意，但在其他季节，这里的大量房间很少会满员。上裕皲的宿舍床位起价为 40 日元，私人房间起价为 120 日元。特别值得一提的是间隔时光 （ 间隔时光 ，Jiàngé Shíguāng; % 180 0887 8300;上雨貲;dm/d ¥50/220; W ），拥有知识渊博的员工和体面的食物。下玉崩（Mystic Waterfall Lodge）（ 神瀑客栈 ，Shénpù Kèzhàn; % 887 841 1082;下雨貲;dm/d ¥35/120; W ） 质量与其他选择相似，但位于村庄底部最好的位置。村里的导游费用约为每天 200 日元。雨貔的物资（食物和水）价格昂贵，所以在飞来寺囤货。

From Yubeng village there are loads of trekking possibilities, though only the two main hikes are signposted. It’s a five- to six-hour round-trip (11.6km) on foot or horseback (¥250) to the Ice Lake (冰湖, Bīng Hú) from Upper Yubeng, passing through rhododendron and pine forests and through the nearly pristine Xiaonong mountain pasture – site of the base camp for ill-fated Japanese climbing expeditions in 1991 and 1996.
从 Yubeng 村出发，有很多徒步旅行的可能性，尽管只有两条主要的徒步旅行有路标。从上雨蚡步行或骑马（250 日元）往返 5 至 6 小时（11.6 公里）到达冰湖 ，穿过杜鹃花林和松树林，穿过近乎原始的小农山牧场——1991 年和 1996 年命运多舛的日本登山探险队的大本营。

From Lower Yubeng a 10.8km trail leads up to the Sacred Waterfall (神瀑, Shénpù), roughly a four- to five-hour round-trip, along a gentler slope through another beautiful forested valley. Another small lake sits high in the mountains here (around 4350m), but it’s inaccessible while snow remains and even in ideal conditions you’ll need a guide and a very early start.
从下玉崩出发，一条 10.8 公里的小径通向神瀑布 （ 神瀑 ，Shénpù），往返大约需要 4 到 5 个小时，沿着一个较缓的斜坡穿过另一个美丽的森林山谷。另一个小湖坐落在高山上（约 4350 米），但在积雪的情况下无法进入，即使在理想的条件下，您也需要向导和早点出发。

Leaving the village, many travellers hike 14km to Nínóng (尼农) village by the Mekong River, a two- to four-hour downhill trek that includes a lovely one-hour section along a rocky path with steep drops down to the river below – if you are prone to vertigo, head back to Xidang instead. Vans run to Deqin and Feilaisi from Ninong for ¥30 per person or ¥200 for the whole van. If you are stuck, walk 6km to Xidang, where there’s more transport.
离开村庄后，许多旅行者徒步 14 公里到达湄公河边的尼农村，这是一条两到四个小时的下坡徒步旅行，其中包括沿着岩石小路的一段可爱的 1 小时路段，陡峭的落差一直延伸到下面的河流——如果你容易眩晕，请返回西荡。面包车从尼农开往德勤和飞来寺，每人 30 日元或整辆 200 日元。如果您被困住了，可以步行 6 公里到达西荡，那里有更多的交通。

该地区徒步旅行的其他选择包括传奇的 Kawa Karpo kora， 这是一次为期 12 天的梅里雪山朝圣之旅。不过，其中一半在西藏自治区，所以你需要许可证才能这样做，而且你肯定需要一个导游。

Nujiang Valley 怒江大峡谷
怒江大峡谷 怒江大峡谷

The 320km-long Nujiang Valley (Nùjiāng Dàxiágǔ) is one of Yunnan’s best-kept secrets. The Nujiang (known as the ‘Salween’ in Myanmar; its name in Chinese means ‘Raging River’) is the second-longest river in Southeast Asia and a Unesco World Heritage Site.
全长 320 公里的怒江谷 （Nùjiāng Dàxiágǔ） 是云南保存最完好的秘密之一。怒江河（在缅甸被称为“萨尔温河”，中文名称的意思是“汹涌的河流”）是东南亚第二长的河流，被联合国教科文组织列为世界遗产。

Sandwiched between the Gaoligong mountains and Myanmar to the west, Tibet to the north and the imposing Biluo Mountain to the east, the gorge holds nearly a quarter of China’s flora and fauna species, and half of China’s endangered species. The valley also has a mix of Han, Nu, Lisu, Drung and Tibetan nationalities, and even the odd Burmese trader. And it’s simply stunning – all of it.
峡谷西夹在高黎贡山脉和缅甸之间，北与西藏相接，东与雄伟的碧螺山相接，拥有中国近四分之一的动植物物种和一半的中国濒危物种。山谷中还有汉族、怒族、傈僳族、德族和藏族，甚至还有奇怪的缅甸商人。这简直令人惊叹——所有这一切。

Like other parts of rural Yunnan, change is coming to the Nujiang Valley. The local government is touting investment opportunities in everything from truffle farms to stone quarries, and the main towns – Liuku and Fugong especially – are booming, with new apartment blocks and shops appearing and Han migrants arriving in numbers. Though the region has long been kept remote by poor road access, a new highway all the way to Gongshan was nearing completion at the time of research.

The main reason to come to the Nujiang Valley is to visit isolated, friendly Bingzhongluo (Bǐngzhōngluò), a farming village set in a beautiful, wide and fertile bowl and populated primarily by Tibetan and Nu communities. Xishuangbanna Region
西双版纳大区

North of Myanmar and Laos, Xishuangbanna (西双版纳, Xīshuāngbǎnnà) is the Chinese approximation of the original Thai name of Sip Sawng Panna (12 Rice-Growing Districts). Better known as Bǎnnà, the area has become China’s mini-Thailand, attracting tourists looking for sunshine, water-splashing festivals and jungle treks. Xishuangbanna Dai Autonomous Prefecture, as it is known officially, is subdivided into the three counties of Jinghong, Menghai and Mengla.
在缅甸和老挝北部，西双版纳（西双版纳 ，Xīshuāngbǎnnà）是泰语原名 Sip Sawng Panna（12 个水稻种植区）的中文近似音。该地区更广为人知的名字是 Bǎnnà，已成为中国的迷你泰国，吸引着寻求阳光、泼水节日和丛林徒步旅行的游客。西双版纳傣族自治州，正式称为西双版纳傣族自治州，细分为景洪、勐海、勐腊三个县。

Banna is home to some of the best food in all China. Sour and spicy Dai food in particular is excellent, drawing on both Chinese and Southeast Asian influences. Dai dishes include barbecued fish, eel or beef cooked with lemongrass or served with peanut and tomato sauce. The region’s fertile earth guarantees superb fruit and vegetables too.
版纳是全中国最好的食物的故乡。酸辣傣类食物尤其出色，受到中国和东南亚的影响。傣族菜肴包括用柠檬草烹制的烤鱼、鳗鱼或牛肉，或配以花生和番茄酱。该地区肥沃的土地也保证了优质的水果和蔬菜。

Environment
环境

Xishuangbanna has myriad plant and animal species, although scientific studies have shown the tropical rainforest areas of Banna are now acutely endangered. The jungle areas that remain contain a handful of tigers, leopards and golden-haired monkeys. The number of elephants has doubled to 250, up 100% from the early 1980s; the government now offers compensation to villagers whose crops have been destroyed by elephants, or who assist in wildlife conservation. In 1998 the government banned the hunting or processing of animals, but poaching is hard to control.
西双版纳拥有无数的动植物物种，尽管科学研究表明版纳的热带雨林地区现在已经濒临灭绝。剩下的丛林地区有少量老虎、豹子和金毛猴。大象的数量翻了一番，达到 250 头，比 1980 年代初增加了 100%;政府现在向农作物被大象破坏的村民或协助野生动物保护的村民提供补偿。1998年，政府禁止狩猎或加工动物，但偷猎很难控制。

该地区的百万人口中约有三分之一是傣族人;另外三分之一左右是汉族，其余的都是少数民族的集团，包括哈尼族、傈僳族和瑶族，以及鲜为人知的山地部落，如艾尼族（哈尼族的一个亚群）、吉诺族、卜朗族、拉祜族和佤族。

Climate
气候

The region has two seasons: wet and dry. The wet season is between May and October, when it rains ferociously, although not every day and only in short bursts. From September to April there is less rainfall, but thick fog often descends during the late evening and doesn’t lift until 10am or even later.

Water-Splashing Festival (泼水节, Pōshuǐjié; hmid-Apr) Celebrated at the same time as in Thailand and Laos, the three-day water-splashing festival washes away the dirt, sorrow and demons of the old year and brings in the happiness of the new. 开门节 （ 傣族开门节 ，Dǎizú Kāiménjié; h 10 月） 农耕季节以傣族的开门节结束，届时每个人都再次放下头发庆祝丰收。

HIKING IN XISHUANGBANNA
西双版纳徒步旅行

Hikes around Xishuangbanna used to be among the best in China – you’d be invited into a local’s home to eat, sleep and drink mǐjiǔ (rice wine). Growing numbers of visitors have changed this in many places, while rubber and banana plantations are having an increasingly deleterious effect on the environment.
西双版纳周围的徒步旅行曾经是中国最好的徒步旅行之一——你会被邀请到当地人的家中吃饭、睡觉和喝米酒 。越来越多的游客改变了许多地方的这种情况，而橡胶和香蕉种植园对环境的有害影响越来越大。

It’s still possible to find villages that see very few foreigners and remain pristine, but they are remote. Don’t expect to roll up in Jinghong and the next day to be in a village that hasn’t seen a foreigner before. And don’t automatically expect a welcome mat and a free lunch because you’re a foreigner, either.
仍然有可能找到很少有外国人并且保持原始状态的村庄，但它们很偏远。不要指望在景洪卷起来，第二天就来到一个没见过外国人的村子。也不要因为你是外国人而自动期待迎宾垫和免费午餐。

If you do get invited into someone’s home, try to establish whether payment is expected. If it’s not, leave an offering or modest gift (ask at the backpacker cafes to find out what’s considered appropriate), even though the family may insist on nothing.
如果您确实被邀请到某人的家中，请尝试确定是否需要付款。如果不是，请留下一份供品或适度的礼物（询问背包客咖啡馆，了解什么被认为是合适的），即使家人可能什么都不坚持。

Also, take care before heading off. It’s a jungle out there – literally – so go prepared, and make sure somebody knows where you are and when you should return. In the rainy season, you’ll need to be equipped with proper hiking shoes and waterproof gear. You’ll need water purification tablets, bottled water or a water bottle able to hold boiled water, as well as snacks and sunscreen. Seriously consider taking a guide. You won’t hear much Mandarin on the trail, let alone any English. Expect to pay around ¥300 per day.
另外，出发前要小心。那里是一片丛林——字面意思——所以要做好准备，并确保有人知道你在哪里以及你什么时候应该回来。在雨季，您需要配备合适的登山鞋和防水装备。您需要净水片、瓶装水或能够盛开水的水瓶，以及零食和防晒霜。认真考虑带上导游。在路上你不会听到太多普通话，更不用说任何英语了。预计每天支付约 300 日元。

京洪的湄公河咖啡馆可以安排徒步旅行和导游。 美美咖啡馆不组织徒步旅行，但活页夹中有很多细节，因此您可以找到自己的路线，包括前往张朗、不浪山和满麦村的自助徒步旅行信息，以及景洪附近的一些一日徒步旅行选择。
