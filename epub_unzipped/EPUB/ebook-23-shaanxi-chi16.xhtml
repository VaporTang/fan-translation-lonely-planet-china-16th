Why Go?
为什么要去？

Shaanxi (陕西; Shǎnxī) is where it all began for China. As the heartland of the Qin dynasty (秦朝), whose warrior emperor united much of China for the first time, Shaanxi was the cradle of Chinese civilisation and the fountainhead of Han culture. Xi’an marked the beginning and end of the Silk Road and was a buzzing capital long before anyone knew of Beijing and its Forbidden City.
陕西（ 陕西 ;Shǎnxī）是中国一切的开始。陕西是秦朝的心脏地带，秦朝的武皇首次统一了中国大部分地区，陕西是中华文明的摇篮和汉文化的源泉。习安标志着丝绸之路的起点和终点，早在人们知道北京及其紫禁城之前，它就已经是一个热闹的首都。

Shaanxi’s archaeological sites make it an essential destination. Around Xi’an there’s an excavated Neolithic village and royal graves, including the tomb of Qin Shi Huang and his Army of Terracotta Warriors, one of the world’s foremost heritage sites. The city of Xi’an is also an emergent travellers’ hub, with hotels, restaurants, museums, ancient pagodas, and a marvellous city wall and Muslim Quarter.
陕西的考古遗址使其成为一个必不可少的目的地。习安周围有一个出土的新石器时代村庄和皇家坟墓，包括秦始皇和他的兵马俑军的陵墓，这是世界上最重要的遗产之一。习安市也是一个新兴的旅游中心，拥有酒店、餐厅、博物馆、古塔，以及奇妙的城墙和穆斯林区。

Rural areas contain fascinating villages barely touched by modernity as well as mountains that once sheltered hermits and sages.
农村地区有迷人的村庄，几乎没有现代感，还有曾经庇护隐士和圣贤的山脉。

History
历史

Around 3000 years ago, the Zhou people of the Bronze Age moved out of their Shaanxi homeland, conquered the Shang and became dominant in much of northern China. Later the state of Qin, ruling from its capital Xianyang (near modern-day Xi’an), became the first dynasty to unify much of China. Subsequent dynasties, including the Han, Sui and Tang, were based in Xi’an, then known as Chang’an, which was abandoned for the eastern capital of Luoyang (in Henan) whenever invaders threatened.
大约3000年前，青铜时代的周人离开了陕西的家园，征服了商朝，并在中国北方的大部分地区占据主导地位。后来，秦国从其首都咸阳（靠近今习安）统治，成为第一个统一中国大部分地区的王朝。随后的王朝，包括汉、隋和唐，都以习安为基地，当时称为长安，每当入侵者受到威胁时，长安就会被废弃为东都洛阳（河南）。

Shaanxi remained the political heart of China until the 10th century. However, when the imperial court shifted eastward, the province’s fortunes began to decline. Rebellions and famine were followed in 1556 by the deadliest earthquake in history, when an estimated 830,000 people died (the unusually high death toll was attributed to the fact that millions were living in cave homes, which easily collapsed in the quake). The extreme poverty of the region ensured that it was an early stronghold of the CCP (Chinese Communist Party).
直到 10 世纪，陕西一直是中国的政治中心。然而，当朝廷东移时，该省的运势开始走下坡路。1556 年，叛乱和饥荒之后发生了历史上最致命的地震，当时估计有 830,000 人死亡（异常高的死亡人数归因于数百万人住在洞穴房屋中，这些洞穴房屋很容易在地震中倒塌）。该地区的极端贫困确保了它是中国共产党（CCP）的早期据点。

Language
语言

Locals like to joke that Xi’an’s dialect is the ‘real’ standard Mandarin – after all, the city was one of the ancient capitals of China. More pedantic linguists, however, prefer to classify the Shaanxi dialect as part of the central Zhongyuan Mandarin group. Generally speaking, the Mandarin spoken in and around Xi’an is pretty clear to students of the language. The Jin Chinese dialect is also spoken in some parts of the province.
当地人喜欢开玩笑说，习安的方言是“真正的”标准普通话——毕竟，这座城市是中国的古都之一。然而，更迂腐的语言学家更愿意将陕西方言归类为中央中元普通话组的一部分。一般来说，西习安及其周边地区的普通话对于学习该语言的学生来说是相当清楚的。该省的一些地区也使用晋语方言。

Once the terminus of the Silk Road and a melting pot of cultures and religions, as well as home to emperors, courtesans, poets, monks, merchants and warriors, the glory days of Xi’an (Xī’ān; pronounced ‘see-an’) may have ended in the early 10th century, but a considerable amount of ancient Chang’an, the former city, survives behind the often roaring, modern city. Xi’an’s Ming-era city walls remain intact, vendors of all descriptions still crowd the narrow lanes of the warren-like Muslim Quarter, and there are enough places of interest to keep even the most amateur historian riveted.
曾经是丝绸之路的终点站和文化和宗教的大熔炉，也是皇帝、、诗人、僧侣、商人和武士的家园，习安（Xī'ān，发音为“see-an”）的辉煌岁月可能在 10 世纪初结束，但相当多的古长安，这座曾经的城市，在经常咆哮的背后幸存下来， 现代城市。习安的明代城墙完好无损，各种类型的摊贩仍然挤满了沃伦般的穆斯林区的狭窄小巷，有足够多的名胜古迹，即使是最业余的历史学家也会着迷。

Most people only spend two or three days in Xi’an; dynastic enthusiasts could easily stay busy for a week. Must-sees include the Army of Terracotta Warriors, the Tomb of Emperor Jingdi, the Xi’an City Walls and the Muslim Quarter, but try to set aside time for its pagodas and museums, plus a side trip to nearby Hua Shan.
大多数人在习安只待两三天;王朝爱好者可以轻松地忙碌一周。必看景点包括兵马俑军、景帝陵、习安城墙和穆斯林区，但尽量留出时间参观宝塔和博物馆，并顺便去附近的华山 。

(map Google map; 西安城墙, Xī’ān Chéngqiáng; ¥54; h8am-8.30pm Apr-Oct, to 7pm Nov-Mar)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 西安城墙 ， Xī'ān Chéngqiáng; ¥54; h 4 月至 10 月上午 8 点至晚上 8 点 30 分，11 月至 3 月晚上 7 点）

Xi’an is one of the few cities in China where the imposing old city walls still stand. Built in 1370 during the Ming dynasty, the magnificent 12m-high walls are surrounded by a dry moat and form a rectangle with a perimeter of 14km. Most sections have been restored or rebuilt, and it is possible to walk the walls in their entirety in a leisurely four hours (or around two hours by bike, or at a slow jog).
习安是中国为数不多的雄伟的古城墙仍然屹立不倒的城市之一。建于明代1370年，12米高的宏伟城墙涸的护城河环绕，形成一个长14公里的长方形。大部分路段都经过修复或重建，可以在悠闲的四个小时内（或骑自行车或慢跑约两个小时）走完整个城墙。

You can cycle from the South, North, West and East gates, with rental costing ¥45 for 100 minutes (¥200 deposit), while the truly lazy can be whisked around in a golf cart for ¥200. Access ramps are located inside the major gates, with the exception of the South Gate, where the entrance is outside the walls; there’s another entrance inside the walls beside the Forest of Stelae Museum. En route, you get to look out over modern-day Xi’an. From this vantage point it’s clear that the city is a hotchpotch of the old and new, with the new vastly in the ascendancy. Every now and then a slice of old Xi’an, such as Guangren Temple, appears and you are rewarded with a bird’s-eye view.
您可以从南门、北门、西门和东门骑行，租金为 100 分钟 45 日元（押金 200 日元），而真正懒惰的人可以乘坐高尔夫球车 200 日元。入口坡道位于主要大门内，但南门除外，其入口位于墙外;石碑森林博物馆旁边的墙内还有另一个入口。在途中，您可以眺望现代的习安。从这个有利位置可以清楚地看出，这座城市是新旧的大杂烩，新事物占据了上风。时不时地会出现一片古老的习安，比如广仁寺 ，你会得到鸟瞰的奖励。

To get an idea of Xi’an’s former grandeur, consider this: the Tang city walls originally enclosed 83 sq km, an area seven times larger than today’s city centre.
要了解习安昔日的宏伟，请考虑一下：唐城墙最初占地 83 平方公里，面积是今天市中心的七倍。

Muslim QuarterHISTORIC SITE
穆斯林区历史遗址

(map Google map; 回族区; mZhonglou)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 回族区 m Zhonglou）

The backstreets leading north from the Drum Tower have been home to the city’s Hui community (non-Uyghur Chinese Muslims) for centuries, perhaps as far back as the Ming dynasty or further still. The narrow lanes are full of butcher shops, sesame-oil factories, smaller mosques hidden behind enormous wooden doors, men in white skullcaps and women with their heads covered in coloured scarves. It’s a great place to wander and is especially atmospheric at night.
几个世纪以来，从鼓楼向北通向的后街一直是该市回族社区（非维吾尔族中国穆斯林）的家园，也许可以追溯到明朝或更远的地方。狭窄的小巷里到处都是肉店、芝麻油厂、隐藏在巨大木门后面的小型清真寺、戴着白色头巾的男人和头上戴着彩色围巾的女人。这是一个漫步的好地方，晚上尤其大气。

Bigger than many temples in China, the Great Mosque is a gorgeous blend of Chinese and Islamic architecture and one of the most fascinating sacred sites in the land. The present buildings are mostly Ming and Qing, though the mosque was founded in the 8th century. Arab influences extend from the central minaret (cleverly disguised as a stumpy pagoda) to the enormous turquoise-roofed Prayer Hall (not open to visitors) at the back of the complex, dating from the Ming dynasty.
大清真寺比中国的许多寺庙都要大，是中国和伊斯兰建筑的华丽融合，是这片土地上最迷人的圣地之一。现在的建筑大多是明清两代建筑，尽管清真寺建于 8 世纪。阿拉伯的影响从中央尖塔（巧妙地伪装成一座矮小的宝塔）延伸到建筑群后面巨大的绿松石屋顶祈祷厅（不对游客开放），其历史可追溯至明朝。

While the Bell Tower originally held a bell that was rung at dawn, the Drum Tower, standing at the foot of the smoky and uproarious street of Beiyuanmen, marked nightfall. Guangren TempleBUDDHIST TEMPLE
广仁寺 BUDDHIST TEMPLE

(map Google map; 广仁寺, Guǎngrén Sì; Guangren Si Lu, 广仁寺路; h8am-5.30pm; mSajinqiao or Yuxiangmen) F
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 广仁寺 ，Guǎngrén Sì;广仁寺路 h 8am-5.30pm; m 萨金桥或玉香门） F

The sole Tibetan Buddhist temple in the entire province, Guangren Temple dates from the early 18th century, but was largely rebuilt in the 20th century. As a sacred Tibetan Buddhist place of worship, the temple hums with mystery and spiritual energy. Perhaps the most valuable object in the temple resides in the final hall, a golden representation of Sakyamuni that rests upon a Tang dynasty pedestal. There is only one other like it, housed at the Jokhang Temple in Lhasa.
广仁寺是全省唯一的藏传佛教寺庙，其历史可追溯至 18 世纪初，但在 20 世纪进行了大部分重建。作为藏传佛教的神圣礼拜场所，这座寺庙弥漫着神秘和精神能量的嗡嗡声。也许寺庙中最有价值的物品位于最后的大厅，这是位于唐朝基座上的释迦牟尼的金色雕像。只有一个类似的，位于拉萨的大昭寺。

Housed in Xi’an’s Confucius Temple, this museum holds more than 1000 stone stelae (inscribed tablets), including the nine Confucian classics and some exemplary calligraphy. East of Xi’an
西习安以东

oArmy of Terracotta WarriorsHISTORIC SITE
o 兵马俑军历史遗址

(兵马俑, Bīngmǎyǒng; www.bmy.com.cn; adult/student Mar-Nov ¥150/75, Dec-Feb ¥120/60; h8.30am-6pm Mar-Nov, to 5.30pm Dec-Feb, last admission 1hr before close)
（ 兵马俑 ，Bīngmǎyǒng;www.bmy.com.cn;成人/学生 3 月至 11 月 ¥150/75,12 月至 2 月 ¥120/60; h 3 月至 11 月上午 8：30 至下午 6 点，12 月至 2 月下午 5：30，最后入场时间为闭馆前 1 小时）

The Terracotta Army isn’t just Xi’an’s premier sight: it’s one of the most famous archaeological finds in the world. This subterranean life-size army of thousands has silently stood guard over the soul of China’s first unifier for more than two millennia. Either Qin Shi Huang was terrified of the vanquished spirits awaiting him in the afterlife or, as most archaeologists believe, he expected his rule to continue in death as it had in life.
兵马俑不仅是习安的首要景点，也是世界上最著名的考古发现之一。这支地下真人大小的数千人大军，两千多年来一直默默守护着中国第一个统一者的灵魂。要么秦始皇害怕来世等待着他的被征服的灵魂，要么正如大多数考古学家所认为的那样，他希望他的统治在死后继续下去，就像在生前一样。

Whatever the case, the guardians of his tomb – who date from the 3rd century BC – today offer some of the greatest insights we have into the world of ancient China.
无论如何，他坟墓的守护者——他们的历史可以追溯到公元前 3 世纪——今天为我们提供了一些关于古代中国世界的最伟大的见解。

The discovery of the army of warriors was entirely fortuitous. In 1974 peasants drilling a well uncovered an underground vault that eventually yielded thousands of terracotta soldiers and horses in battle formation. Throughout the years the site became so famous that many of its unusual attributes are now well known; in particular the fact that no two soldier’s faces are alike.
战士军队的发现完全是偶然的。1974 年，农民在钻井时发现了一个地下拱顶，最终产生了数千名兵马俑，排成战斗队形。多年来，该遗址变得如此出名，以至于其许多不寻常的属性现在已广为人知。特别是没有两个士兵的脸是一样的。

The on-site wrap-around theatre gives a useful primer on how the figures were sculpted. You can also employ a guide (low/high season ¥150/200) or try the audio guide (¥40, plus ¥200 deposit), although the latter is somewhat useless, being difficult to understand and not very compelling.
现场的环绕式剧院为人物的雕刻方式提供了有用的入门知识。您也可以聘请导游（淡季/旺季 150/200 日元）或尝试语音导览（40 日元，另加 200 日元押金），尽管后者有些没用，难以理解，也不是很引人注目。

After this, visit the site in reverse, which enables you to build up to the most impressive pit for a fitting finale.
之后，反向访问该站点，这使您能够建立最令人印象深刻的坑，以获得合适的结局。

Start with the smallest pit, Pit 3, containing 72 warriors and horses; it’s believed to be the army headquarters due to the number of high-ranking officers unearthed here. It’s interesting to note that the northern room would have been used to make sacrificial offerings before battle. In the next pit, Pit 2, containing around 1300 warriors and horses, you can examine five of the soldiers up close: a kneeling archer, a standing archer, a cavalryman and his horse, a mid-ranking officer and a general. The level of detail is extraordinary: the expressions, hairstyles, armour and even the tread on the footwear are all unique.
从最小的坑 3 开始，里面有 72 名战士和马匹;由于这里出土的高级军官数量众多，据信它是陆军总部。有趣的是，北边的房间是用来在战斗前献祭的。在下一个坑， 即 2 号坑 ，里面有大约 1300 名战士和马匹，您可以近距离观察五名士兵：一名跪姿弓箭手、一名站立弓箭手、一名骑兵和他的马匹、一名中级军官和一名将军。细节水平非同寻常：表情、发型、盔甲甚至鞋类的胎面都是独一无二的。

The largest pit, Pit 1, is the most imposing. Housed in a building the size of an aircraft hangar, it is believed to contain 6000 warriors (only 2000 are on display) and horses, all facing east and ready for battle. The vanguard of three rows of archers (both crossbow and longbow) is followed by the main force of soldiers, who originally held spears, swords, dagger-axes and other long-shaft weapons. The infantry were accompanied by 35 chariots, though these, made of wood, have long since disintegrated.
最大的坑，1 号坑 ，是最雄伟的。它坐落在一座飞机库大小的建筑内，据信有 6000 名战士（仅展出 2000 人）和马匹，全部朝东，随时准备战斗。三排弓手（包括弩和长弓）的先锋队后面是士兵主力，他们原本手持长矛、剑、匕首斧等长轴武器。步兵有 35 辆战车陪同，尽管这些战车由木头制成，但早已解体。

Almost as extraordinary as the soldiers is a pair of bronze chariots and horses unearthed just 20m west of the Tomb of Qin Shi Huang. These are now on display, together with some of the original weaponry and a mid-ranking officer you can see up close, in a huge modern museum called the Qin Shi Huang Emperor Tomb Artefact Exhibition Hall (秦始皇帝陵文物陈列厅, Qǐnshǐhuángdìlíng Chénliètīng).
几乎与士兵一样非凡的是，在秦始皇陵以西仅 20 米处出土的一对青铜战车和马匹。这些武器现在与一些原始武器和一名中级军官一起展出，您可以在一个名为秦始皇帝陵文物陈列厅 （ 秦始皇帝陵文物陈列厅，Qǐnshǐhuángdìlíng Chénliètīng）的巨大现代博物馆中展出。

THE MAN BEHIND THE ARMY
军队背后的人

Qin Shi Huang (秦始皇; 259–210 BC), China’s first emperor and creator of the Terracotta Army, has gone down in history as the sort of tyrant who gives tyrants a bad name. It might be because he outlawed Confucianism, ordered almost all its written texts to be burnt and, according to legend, buried alive 460 of its leading scholars.
秦始皇（秦始皇 ，公元前 259-210 年）是中国第一位皇帝，也是兵马俑的创造者，他作为那种给暴君带来恶名的暴君而载入史册。这可能是因为他取缔了儒家思想，下令烧毁几乎所有的书面文本，并且根据传说，活埋了 460 名儒家的主要学者。

Or perhaps it was his enslaving of hundreds of thousands of people to achieve his (admittedly monumental) accomplishments during his 36 years of rule (which began when he was just 13).
或者，也许是他在 36 年的统治期间（从他年仅 13 岁开始）中奴役了数十万人，以取得他（诚然是巨大的）成就。

In recent years, there have been efforts by the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) to rehabilitate him, by emphasising both his efforts to unify China and the far-sighted nature of his policies. A classic overachiever, he created an efficient, centralised government that became the model for later dynasties; and he standardised measurements, currency and, most importantly, writing. He also built more than 6400km of new roads and canals and, of course, he conquered six major kingdoms before turning 40.
近年来，中国共产党一直在努力为他平反，强调他为统一中国所做的努力和政策的远见性。作为一个典型的超额成就者，他创建了一个高效、中央集权的政府，成为后来王朝的典范;他标准化了测量、货币，最重要的是，书写。他还修建了 6400 多公里的新道路和运河，当然，他在 40 岁之前征服了六个主要王国。

Nevertheless, he remains a hugely controversial figure in Chinese history, but also one whose presence permeates popular culture. The first emperor pops up in video games, in literature and on TV shows. He’s also been the subject of films by both Chen Kaige and Zhang Yimou (The Emperor and the Assassin and Hero), while Jet Li played a thinly disguised version of him in the 2008 Hollywood blockbuster The Mummy: Tomb of the Dragon Emperor.
尽管如此，他仍然是中国历史上一个极具争议的人物，但他的存在也渗透到流行文化中。第一位皇帝出现在电子游戏、文学和电视节目中。他也是陈凯歌和张艺谋（《皇帝与刺客 》和《 英雄 》）电影的主题，而李连杰在 2008 年好莱坞大片《 木乃伊：龙皇之墓 》中扮演了他的伪装版本。

There are three ways up the mountain (adult/student ¥180/90) to the North Peak (北峰; Běi Fēng), the first of five summit peaks. 上山有三条路（成人/学生¥180/90）到北峰 （ 北峰 ;Běi Fēng），五座山顶山峰中的第一座。其中两个选择从山的东麓，即北峰缆车终点站开始。如果您不喜欢攀登，第一种选择很方便：奥地利制造的缆车 （ 北峰索道 ，Běifēng Suǒdào;单程/往返 ¥80/160; h 早上 7 点至晚上 7 点） 将在八分钟内将您安静地（除了船上的公告）带到风景优美的北峰，尽管在繁忙时段您可能需要排队一个多小时。

The second option is to work your way to the North Peak under the cable-car route. This takes two sweaty hours, and two sections of 50m or so are literally vertical, with nothing but a steel chain to grab onto and tiny chinks cut into the rock for footing. 第二种选择是在缆车路线下前往北峰。这需要两个小时的汗流浃背，两个 50 米左右的部分实际上是垂直的，除了一条钢链可以抓住，在岩石上切出微小的缝隙作为立足点。这就是为什么这条路线被称为“士兵之路”。

The third option is the most popular, but it’s still hard work, taking between three and five hours. A 6km path leads to the North Peak from the village of Hua Shan, at the base of the mountain (the other side of the mountain from the cable car). It’s pretty easy for the first 4km, but after that it’s all steep stairs.
第三种选择是最受欢迎的，但仍然很辛苦，需要三到五个小时。一条 6 公里的小路从山脚下的华山村通向北峰（缆车的另一边）。前 4 公里很容易，但之后都是陡峭的楼梯。

Mizhi (米脂, Mǐzhǐ) is famed as the home town of Li Zicheng, proto-communist and would-be emperor, as well as for the alleged beauty of its women.
米脂 （Mǐzhǐ） 以原始共产主义者和准皇帝李自成的故乡而闻名，并因其所谓的女性美丽而闻名。

Way off the tourist circuit about 70km south of Yulin, it’s a sleepy place with a small presence of the Hui ethnic minority; you will be the sole foreigner in town and possibly the only visitor of any description. There are attractive terraced fields in the surrounding hills and some of the local population still live in caves (窑洞, yáodòng) and homes carved out of the hillsides, while the small old quarter, with its narrow alleys and dilapidated courtyard homes, is a fascinating place to wander.
它位于榆林以南约 70 公里处的旅游线路附近，是一个沉睡的地方，少数回族人口很少;您将是镇上唯一的外国人，也可能是唯一任何描述的访客。周围的山丘上有迷人的梯田，一些当地居民仍然住在山坡上凿出的洞穴和房屋中，而小老城区则拥有狭窄的小巷和破旧的四合院房屋，是一个迷人的漫步之地。

The principal sight in Mizhi is the Li Zicheng Palace (李自成行宫, Lǐ Zìchéng Xínggōng; Xinggong Lu, 行宫路; ¥20; h8am-5pm), a well-preserved and compact palace built in 1643 at the height of Li’s power. Set against a hillside, there’s a statue of the man himself, pavilions housing exhibits about Li and notable Mizhi women, and a pagoda. There’s also a fine theatre where music performances and plays were held, sometimes for three days at a time, to celebrate Li’s victories. To reach the palace, walk east on Xinggong Lu. It’s a 10- to 15-minute walk from the bus station.

Thanks to extensive coal mining and the discovery of natural gas fields nearby (though you’ll see wind farms as well as oil wells on the way up from Yan’an), Yulin (Yúlín), a one-time garrison town on the fringes of Inner Mongolia’s Mu Us Desert, is booming.
