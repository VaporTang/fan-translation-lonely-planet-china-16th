Why Go?
为什么要去？

China’s largest province, Xinjiang (新疆, Xīnjiāng) is the homeland of the Muslim Uyghurs and a fast-changing region where ancient and modern push up against each other in surprising ways. High-speed railways cross the Martian-like landscapes, linking cities in hours rather than days, and the regional capital Ürümqi is a forest of high-rise apartments and glass skyscrapers; yet, in the Silk Road oases of Kashgar, Hotan and Turpan, life goes as it has for centuries, based around the mosque, the teahouse and the bazaar.
新疆是中国最大的省份，是穆斯林维吾尔人的故乡，也是一个瞬息万变的地区，古代和现代以令人惊讶的方式相互竞争。高速铁路穿过火星般的景观，在几小时而不是几天内连接城市，地区首府乌鲁木齐是高层公寓和玻璃摩天大楼林立的森林;然而，在喀什、和田和吐鲁番等丝绸之路绿洲，生活与几个世纪以来一样，以清真寺、茶馆和集市为基础。

Despite the military and police presence here due to years of unrest, Xinjiang offers extraordinary natural beauty and fascinating Central Asian history and culture. This province of ‘Chinese Turkestan’ offers insights into China’s past and its unsettled multicultural present, and glimpses of some of the most sublime landscapes on earth.
尽管由于多年的动乱，这里有军警驻扎，但新疆拥有非凡的自然美景和迷人的中亚历史和文化。这个“中国突厥斯坦”省份提供了对中国过去及其动荡的多元文化现在的深入了解，并瞥见了地球上一些最崇高的景观。

History
历史

By the end of the 2nd century BC, the expanding Han dynasty had pushed its borders west into what is now Xinjiang. Military garrisons protected the fledgling trade routes, as silk flowed out of the empire in return for the strong Ferghana horses needed to fight nomadic incursions from the north. Chinese imperial rule waxed and waned over the centuries, shrinking after the collapse of the Han and reasserting itself during the 7th-century Tang, though central control was tenuous at best. A Uyghur kingdom based at Khocho (now known as the ruined city of Gaochang in Turpan) thrived from the 8th century and oversaw the Central Asian people’s transformation from nomads to farmers and from Manichaeans to Buddhists.
到公元前2世纪末，不断扩张的汉朝已将其边界向西推进到现在的新疆。军事驻军保护了刚刚起步的贸易路线，因为丝绸流出帝国，以换取对抗来自北方的游牧入侵所需的强壮的费尔干纳马匹。几个世纪以来，中国帝国的统治起起落落，在汉朝崩溃后萎缩，并在 7 世纪唐朝重新确立了自己的地位，尽管中央控制充其量是微不足道的。一个以霍乔（现称为吐鲁番高昌废墟城）为基地的维吾尔王国从 8 世纪开始繁荣发展，并监督了中亚人民从游牧民族到农民、从摩尼教徒到佛教徒的转变。

It was during Kharakhanid rule in the 10th to 12th centuries that Islam took hold in Xinjiang. In 1219, Ili (modern Yining), Hotan and Kashgar fell to the Mongols and their various successors controlled the whole of Central Asia until the mid-18th century, when the Manchu army marched into Kashgar.
正是在 10 世纪至 12 世纪喀喇汗王朝统治期间，伊斯兰教在新疆扎根。1219年，伊犁（今伊宁）、和田和喀什落入蒙古人手中，他们的各个继承者控制了整个中亚地区，直到18世纪中叶，满清军队进军喀什。

In 1865, a Kokandi officer named Yaqub Beg seized Kashgaria, proclaimed a short-lived independent Turkestan, and made diplomatic contacts with Britain and Russia. The Manchu army eventually returned and two decades later Kashgaria was formally incorporated into China’s newly created Xinjiang (New Frontier) province. With the fall of the Qing dynasty in 1911, Xinjiang came under the chaotic and violent rule of a succession of Muslim and Chinese warlords, over whom the Kuomintang (the Nationalist Party) had very little control. In the 1930s and 1940s there was an attempt in both Kashgar and Ili to establish an independent republic of East Turkestan, but both were short-lived.
1865年，一位名叫雅库布·贝格的科坎迪军官占领了喀什噶尔，宣布短暂独立的土耳其斯坦，并与英国和俄罗斯进行了外交接触。满清军队最终卷土重来，二十年后喀什噶尔正式并入中国新成立的新疆（新疆）省。随着 1911 年清朝的灭亡，新疆陷入了一系列穆斯林和中国军阀的混乱和暴力统治之下，国民党（国民党）对他们几乎没有控制权。在 1930 年代和 1940 年代，喀什和伊犁都曾试图建立一个独立的东突厥斯坦共和国，但都昙花一现。

Since 1949 the Chinese government’s main social goal in Xinjiang has been to keep a lid on ethnic separatism, dilute local culture, and flood the region with Han Chinese. Economically, the ‘Develop the West’ campaign has used the region’s oil resources to ramp up the local economy. But this has led to an increase in Han settlers, which has exacerbated ethnic tensions. In a telling statistic, Uyghurs once composed 90% of Xinjiang’s population; today they make up less than 50%. But despite the unease and resentment of the native population, Xinjiang remained peaceful throughout the 1990s and early 2000s as China began to open up to the outside world.
自1949年以来，中国政府在新疆的主要社会目标是遏制民族分裂，淡化当地文化，并让该地区充满汉人。在经济上，“发展西部”运动利用该地区的石油资源来促进当地经济。但这导致了汉族定居者的增加，加剧了民族之间的紧张局势。在一个有说服力的统计数据中，维吾尔人曾经占新疆人口的90%;今天，它们所占比例不到 50%。但是，尽管当地居民感到不安和怨恨，随着中国开始对外开放，新疆在整个 1990 年代和 2000 年代初仍然保持和平。

However, in 2008 street protests and bomb attacks rocked the province, and in 2009 communal violence between Han and Uyghur civilians in central Ürümqi led to around 200 deaths and 1700 injuries, according to Chinese police reports. Protests, riots and terrorist acts have continued to simmer ever since. In 2014 a knife attack at a train station in Kunming (Yunnan province) that killed 29 and injured 143 was blamed on Uyghur separatists. The next month, two attackers stabbed people at the Ürümqi train station before setting off vest explosives. A few weeks later, a suicide car and bomb attack on a market in Ürümqi ended with 31 killed and 90 injured. As a direct result, the Chinese authorities launched a huge security crackdown, the ‘Strike Hard Campaign against Violent Terrorism’, the results of which can still be seen on any street corner in the province. Hundreds of Uyghurs were sentenced to long jail terms and dozens were executed. In 2016 Chen Quanguo, known for his hardline policies, became party secretary in Xinjiang. Since then, reports have circulated of unverified numbers of Uyghurs, Kazakhs, Hui, Kyrgyz and other ethnic and religious minorities being sent to government-run ‘Vocational Education and Training Centers’ for re-education.
然而，根据中国警方的报告，2008 年街头抗议和炸弹袭击震撼了该省，2009 年乌鲁木齐市中心汉族和维吾尔族平民之间的社区暴力导致约 200 人死亡、1700 人受伤。从那以后，抗议、骚乱和恐怖行为继续酝酿。2014年，云南省昆明市火车站发生持刀袭击事件，造成29人死亡、143人受伤，被归咎于维吾尔族分裂分子。接下来的一个月，两名袭击者在乌鲁木齐火车站刺伤了人们，然后引爆了背心炸药。几周后，乌鲁木齐一个市场发生自杀式汽车和炸弹袭击，造成 31 人死亡、90 人受伤。直接结果是，中国当局发起了一场大规模的安全镇压，即“严打反暴力恐怖主义运动”，其成果至今仍在省内任何一个街角都能看到。数百名维吾尔人被判处长期监禁，数十人被处决。2016年，以强硬政策著称的陈全国出任新疆党委书记。此后，有报道称，未经证实的维吾尔人、哈萨克人、回族、吉尔吉斯斯坦和其他少数民族和宗教少数民族被送往政府运营的“职业教育培训中心”接受再教育。

The current situation remains tense. As long as Uyghur resentment continues to be fuelled by what they view as economic marginalisation, cultural restrictions, ethnic discrimination and outright oppression, violence looks likely to remain a threat in the province. However, apart from the heavy police and security measures present, including checkpoints, metal detectors and other security scans, Xinjiang remains open to travellers.
目前的局势仍然紧张。只要维吾尔人的怨恨继续被他们认为的经济边缘化、文化限制、民族歧视和公然压迫所加剧，暴力似乎仍可能成为该省的威胁。然而，除了严密的警察和安全措施，包括检查站、金属探测器和其他安全检查外，新疆仍然对旅行者开放。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

You can fly between Xinjiang and most large Chinese cities, as well as to several Central Asian capitals and a couple of cities further afield, including Moscow, Tehran, Dubai, Seoul and Vienna.
您可以在新疆和中国大多数大城市之间飞行，也可以飞往几个中亚首都和更远的几个城市，包括莫斯科、德黑兰、迪拜、首尔和维也纳。

There are overland border crossings with Pakistan (Khunjerab Pass), Kyrgyzstan (Irkeshtam and Torugart Passes) and Kazakhstan (Nur Zholy, Alashankou, Tacheng and Jimunai). The remote Qolma (Kulma) Pass to Tajikistan appears officially closed to foreigners, though since 2017 a number of travellers, including cyclists, have reported transiting through this crossing. There are no buses to this pass. The rest of these are served by chartered car or bus; Alashankou is China’s only rail link to Central Asia.
与巴基斯坦（洪其拉布山口）、吉尔吉斯斯坦（伊尔克什塔姆山口和托鲁加特山口）和哈萨克斯坦（努尔佐利、阿拉山口、塔城和吉穆奈）有陆路过境点。通往塔吉克斯坦的偏远库尔马（库尔马）山口似乎正式对外国人关闭，但自 2017 年以来，包括骑自行车者在内的许多旅客报告说通过该过境点过境。没有前往该山口的巴士。其余的则由包车或巴士提供服务;阿拉山口是中国通往中亚的唯一铁路。

Heading back into mainland China, the obvious route is the high-speed train line following the Silk Road through Gansu.
回到中国大陆，最明显的路线是沿着丝绸之路穿过甘肃的高铁线。

8Getting Around
8 四处走走

Xinjiang is enormous (slightly bigger than Iran), and getting around usually takes time, money or both. The bus is the standard way to get around, and while bus journeys are cheap, new laws forbidding drivers to drive overnight and constant document checks along the way have increased travel times over the past few years. Buses are often sleepers, which means you’re on either the top (cheaper) or bottom (pricier) bunk, arranged in three lines down the middle of the bus. On-board entertainment usually includes kung fu–film marathons cranked to the hilt. Shared taxis run along many of the bus routes, taking up to half as long, and costing twice as much as buses. Shared taxis only depart when full.
新疆幅员辽阔（比伊朗稍大），四处走动通常需要时间、金钱或两者兼而有之。公共汽车是出行的标准方式，虽然公共汽车旅行很便宜，但在过去几年中，禁止司机通宵驾驶的新法律和沿途不断检查文件增加了旅行时间。公共汽车通常是卧铺，这意味着您坐在顶部（更便宜）或底部（更昂贵）的铺位上，在公共汽车中间排列成三排。船上的娱乐活动通常包括功夫电影马拉松比赛。共享出租车沿着许多公交路线运行，所需时间是公交车的一半，费用是公交车的两倍。共享出租车仅在客满时出发。

In 2015 the Ürümqi–Lanzhou high-speed railway line opened, massively cutting travel times across Eastern Xinjiang. In the west of the province, train journeys are not nearly as fast, but they’re a lot of fun on overnight sleepers – a great way to meet locals.
2015年，乌鲁木齐至兰州高铁线开通，大大缩短了穿越东疆的旅行时间。在该省西部，火车旅行没有那么快，但过夜卧铺却很有趣——这是结识当地人的好方法。

Flying around the province can save a lot of time and tickets are often discounted by up to 60%. Be aware that flights can sometimes be cancelled for lack of passengers or due to bad weather, but if you have more money than time, this is a good way to claw some back.
在全省飞行可以节省大量时间，机票通常可享受高达 60% 的折扣。请注意，航班有时可能会因缺乏乘客或恶劣天气而取消，但如果您的钱多于时间，这是收回一些钱的好方法。

SAFE TRAVEL
安全旅行

Due to the unrest and terrorism that has been a rare – but recurring – feature of life in Xinjiang since 2008, and which most recently peaked again in 2014, the province today has a very visible military and police presence, which can unnerve some. The main function of these patrols is to deter public protests in the cities, and to check the passports of anyone travelling by train or bus. It’s essential that you carry your passport with you at all times; you’re simply asking for trouble otherwise. The main annoyance for travellers is the sheer amount of time that just getting into a train station can take, or the number of stops a long-distance bus is obliged to make, during which Chinese citizens will be required to disembark to have their IDs scanned. As foreign passports cannot be scanned in this way, you may or may not be required to disembark yourself; await instructions at each checkpoint. Note that you may not bring cigarette lighters, a pocket knife, scissors or anything that could potentially be used as a weapon, however seemingly innocuous, onto any bus or train.
自2008年以来，新疆的动乱和恐怖主义一直是新疆生活中罕见但反复出现的特征，最近一次是在2014年再次达到顶峰，因此今天该省的军警存在非常明显，这可能会让一些人感到不安。这些巡逻队的主要职能是阻止城市中的公众抗议活动，并检查任何乘坐火车或公共汽车旅行的人的护照。始终随身携带护照非常重要;否则你只是自找麻烦。旅行者的主要烦恼是进入火车站可能需要花费大量时间，或者长途巴士必须停靠的次数，在此期间中国公民将被要求下车扫描身份证件。由于外国护照无法以这种方式扫描，您可能需要或可能不需要自行下机;在每个检查点等待指示。请注意，您不得将打火机、小刀、剪刀或任何可能用作武器的东西带上任何公共汽车或火车，无论这些物品看似无害。

CENTRAL XINJIANG
中疆

Bound by deserts and mountain ranges, much of present-day Central Xinjiang would have been completely familiar to Silk Road traders on the Northern Route to Kashgar. Today the largest and most important city in the region is Xinjiang’s capital, Ürümqi, though for travellers the ancient cities around Turpan, the Tian Shan mountains, and the Buddha caves of Kuqa are the bigger draws.
今天的中疆大部分地区被沙漠和山脉所束缚，对于通往喀什的北线丝绸之路商人来说，这里的大部分地区都是完全熟悉的。如今，该地区最大、最重要的城市是新疆的首府乌鲁木齐，但对于游客来说，吐鲁番周围的古城、天山山脉和库车的佛窟是更大的吸引力。

Ürümqi 乌鲁木齐

%0991 / Pop 3.1 million
% 0991 / 流行 310 万

In Xinjiang’s capital, Ürümqi (Wūlǔmùqí), high-rise apartments form a modern skyline that will soon dash any thoughts of spotting wandering camels and ancient caravanserais. The vast majority of its inhabitants are Han Chinese, and the city is one of the least typical of Xinjiang, though glimpses of the distant Tian Shan mountains provide a taste of the extraordinary landscapes awaiting you elsewhere.
在新疆首府乌鲁木齐（乌鲁木奇），高层公寓构成了现代化的天际线，很快就会打破任何发现流浪骆驼和古老商队旅馆的想法。这里的绝大多数居民都是汉族，这座城市是新疆最不典型的城市之一，尽管瞥见遥远的天山山脉可以让您领略到其他地方等待着您的非凡景观。

As a fast-growing Central Asian hub, the city does business with traders from Beijing to Baku and plays host to an exotic mix of people. Indeed, it’s hard to imagine where else in the world you’ll see Chinese, Arabic, Latin and Cyrillic script so commonly side-by-side. This truly is Central Asia.
作为一个快速发展的中亚中心，这座城市与从北京到巴库的贸易商都有业务往来，并接待了异国情调的人群。事实上，很难想象世界上还有什么地方会如此频繁地看到中文、阿拉伯文、拉丁文和西里尔文并排出现。这确实是中亚。

Ürümqi is not a historic city, but its museum is excellent and there are some atmospheric Uyghur districts. Most travellers pass through the provincial capital at some point, and many find their stay to be surprisingly enjoyable.
乌鲁木齐不是一座历史名城，但它的博物馆非常好，有一些大气的维吾尔区。大多数旅行者都会在某个时候经过省会，许多人发现他们的逗留出奇的愉快。

To Kyrgyzstan

There are two passes into Kyrgyzstan: the Torugart Pass, which leads to Naryn and then Bishkek in the north; and the Irkeshtam Pass, which goes to Osh in the south.
进入吉尔吉斯斯坦有两条山口：托鲁加特山口，通向纳林，然后通往北部的比什凯克;以及通往南部奥什的伊尔克什塔姆山口。

Going through the Irkeshtam Pass and on to Osh is straightforward, and there may be sleeper buses available from Kashgar’s international bus station (国际汽车站; Guójì Qìchēzhàn; %138 9916 8559, 138 9914 0624). Crossing the Torugart Pass involves more red tape, for which you will need a travel agency’s help. You will also need to have arranged transport on the Kyrgyz side, which travel agents can organise in Naryn or Bishkek. The pass is open year-round to foreigners (weather permitting), though taxis cannot go here; only cars with permits allowing them to make the journey may pass. To Pakistan
前往巴基斯坦

Trips from Kashgar to Sost in Pakistan are done in two stages – there is no direct bus. Take a bus to Tashkurgan from Kashgar’s local bus station, and then stay overnight at Tashkurgan, before continuing the following morning on a minibus to Sost (which runs only Monday to Friday).
从喀什到巴基斯坦索斯特的行程分两个阶段进行——没有直达巴士。从喀什当地巴士站乘坐巴士前往塔什库尔干，然后在塔什库尔干过夜，然后第二天早上继续乘坐小巴前往索斯特（仅周一至周五运行）。

Officially, the border opens daily between 1 April and 1 November. However, the border can open late or close early depending on conditions at the Khunjerab Pass, and is always closed on weekends. The Chinese customs and immigration formalities are dealt with beforehand at Tashkurgan (3km down the road towards Pakistan). Then it’s 126km to the last checkpost at Khunjerab Pass, the actual border, where your documents are checked again before you head into Pakistan. Pakistan immigration formalities are performed at Sost. Pakistani visas are not available to tourists on arrival (and visas are difficult to get in Beijing), so arrive in China with a visa obtained in your home country. Check the current situation as this could change.
正式规定，边境在 4 月 1 日至 11 月 1 日期间每天开放。然而，根据红其拉甫山口的情况，边境可以延迟开放或提前关闭，并且在周末始终关闭。中国海关和移民手续事先在塔什库尔干（通往巴基斯坦的公路 3 公里处）办理。然后 126 公里即可到达真正的边境 Khunjerab Pass 的最后一个检查站，在您前往巴基斯坦之前，您的文件将在那里再次检查。巴基斯坦移民手续在 Sost 办理。巴基斯坦签证不适用于游客落地（在北京很难获得签证），因此请持在本国获得的签证抵达中国。检查当前情况，因为这可能会发生变化。

Tian Chi Lake 天池
天池 天池

The rugged Tian Shan range was well known to travellers along the northern Silk Road, who had to traverse its southern edge if they had any hope of making progress. Modern travellers have it far easier and plan trips into the mountains for fun, especially to stunning Tian Chi Lake (Tiān Chí; literally ‘heavenly lake’). This high-altitude lake is extremely popular with domestic tourists, but you can still escape the worst of the crowds, who either stick to the paved paths on the northern end or ride overpriced boats across the lake.
崎岖的天山山脉为丝绸之路北部的旅行者所熟知，如果他们有任何进展的希望，就必须穿越其南部边缘。现代旅行者更容易计划去山区旅行，尤其是去迷人的天池湖（Tiān Chí，字面意思是“天堂般的湖”）。这个高海拔湖泊非常受国内游客欢迎，但你仍然可以逃离最糟糕的人群，他们要么坚持北端的铺砌小路，要么乘坐价格过高的船只过湖。

Two thousand metres up in the Tian Shan range is Tian Chi, a small, long steely-blue lake nestled below the view-grabbing 5445m Peak of God (博格达峰, Bógédá Fēng). Turpan 吐鲁番

%0995 / Pop 637,000

Turpan (Tǔlǔfān) is China’s Death Valley. At 154m below sea level, it’s the second-lowest depression in the world and the hottest spot in China. In July and August, temperatures soar above 40°C and even 50°C, forcing the local population to sleep on their roofs and visiting tourists into a state of semi-torpor.
吐鲁番（Tǔlǔfān）是中国的死亡谷。它位于海平面以下 154 米处，是世界第二低的低压，也是中国最热的地区。7 月和 8 月，气温飙升至 40°C 甚至 50°C 以上，迫使当地居民睡在屋顶上，来访的游客陷入半昏睡状态。

Despite the heat, the ground water and fertile soil of the Turpan depression has made this a veritable oasis in the desert, evidenced by the nearby centuries-old remains of ancient cities, imperial garrisons and Buddhist caves. The city itself has a mellow vibe to it, and recovering from a day’s sightseeing over a cold Xinjiang beer under the grape vines on a warm summer evening is one of the joys of travelling through the province.
尽管天气炎热，吐鲁番洼地的地下水和肥沃的土壤使这里成为名副其实的沙漠绿洲，附近有数百年历史的古城遗迹、帝国驻军和佛教石窟就是明证。这座城市本身就有一种柔和的氛围，在温暖的夏日傍晚，在葡萄藤下喝着冰镇的新疆啤酒，从一天的观光中恢复过来，是游览该省的乐趣之一。

虽然比高昌的类似遗址要繁忙得多，但这些遗址绝对是两者中最令人印象深刻的，主要是由于幸存的建筑数量之多，而且位置优越，位于山坡上，四面八方的视野开阔。该遗址位于吐鲁番以西 10 公里处。没有公共交通工具;乘坐出租车（30 日元）需要 20 分钟，也可以骑自行车。

XINJIANG’S CLIMATE
新疆的气候

Xinjiang’s climate is one of extremes. Turpan is the hottest spot in the country – temperatures of 54°C have been recorded in the summer months – while the Tarim and Jungar Basins aren’t much cooler. Spring (April and May) is a much better time to travel temperature-wise, though frequent sandstorms can sometimes obscure the landscape. Winters (November to March) see the mercury plummet below 0°C throughout the province, although March is a good time to catch some festivals. Late May, June, September and (especially) October are the best times to visit.
新疆的气候是极端气候之一。吐鲁番是该国最热的地方——夏季的气温为 54°C——而塔里木盆地和准噶尔盆地也凉爽多少。春季（四月和五月）是温度较好的旅行时间，尽管频繁的沙尘暴有时会遮挡景观。冬季（11 月至 3 月）全省的气温降至 0°C 以下，尽管 3 月是庆祝一些节日的好时机。五月下旬、六月、九月和（尤其是）十月是游览的最佳时间。

然而，面对这些变化，喀什的精神依然存在。维吾尔工匠和工匠仍然像几个世纪以来一样锤打和凿凿，商人在喧闹的集市上讨价还价，驴车仍然在狭窄的小巷中蹒跚而行。不要错过这座城市的周日牲畜市场，无论有多少旅游巴士驶来，它仍然是一个迷人的景象。

oShipton’s ArchNATURAL FEATURE

（ 阿图什天门 ，Ātúshí Tiānmén;图舒克·塔格;¥45; h 上午 10 点至晚上 7 点 30 分）

This extraordinary natural rock arch (the rather prosaic Uyghur name means simply ‘mountain with a hole in it’) is one of the tallest on earth. The first westerner to describe it was Eric Shipton, the last British consul-general in Kashgar, during his visit to the region in 1947. Successive expeditions attempted to find it without success until a team from National Geographic rediscovered the arch in 2000. Located 80km northwest of Kashgar, it’s a half-day excursion.
这座非凡的天然岩石拱门（维吾尔语相当平淡无奇的意思是“有洞的山”）是地球上最高的拱门之一。第一个描述它的西方人是埃里克·希普顿 （Eric Shipton），他是英国最后一位驻喀什总领事，他在 1947 年访问该地区时。连续的探险队试图找到它，但没有成功，直到 2000 年国家地理的一个团队重新发现了这座拱门。位于喀什西北 80 公里处，为期半天的短途旅行。

The first part of the trip involves an hour’s drive towards the Irkeshtam Pass, followed by another 20km ride and then a 45-minute hike through a sublimely lunar landscape, hemmed in on all sides by sheer cliffs. At times you’ll be scrambling through the narrowest part of the gorge over small ladders and staircases, until your final ascent to the arch itself, which takes you up a long wooden staircase. Kashgar-based tour operators can arrange a day trip with guide for ¥600 to ¥800, or you can simply take a taxi and walk from the car park yourself, as the route is well signposted. Bring sturdy shoes, a sun hat and water.

Kashgar Old TownOLD TOWN
喀什老城区 OLD TOWN

(map Google map; 喀什老城区, Kāshí Lǎo Chéngqū)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 喀什老城区 ， Kāshí Lǎo Chéngqū）

The Old Town is the soul of Kashgar, and as such the government has spent much of the past two decades knocking it down block by block and building a modern replacement. Yet it’s still possible to see some of the remaining alleyways: check out the neighbourhood near Donghai Lake in the eastern part of the city. Around Jiefang Lu there are also alleys lined with Uyghur workshops and adobe houses that have withstood the passage of time.
老城区是喀什的灵魂，因此，政府在过去二十年的大部分时间里都花了大量时间将其逐个街区拆除，并建造了一个现代化的替代品。然而，仍然可以看到一些剩余的小巷：看看城市东部东海湖附近的街区。解放路周围还有一条小巷，两旁是维吾尔族作坊和经受住岁月流逝的土坯房。

Where they exist, houses range in age from 50 to 500 years old and the lanes twist haphazardly through neighbourhoods where Kashgaris have lived and worked for centuries. It’s a great place for strolling, peeking through gates, chatting to the locals and admiring the craftspeople as they bang on tin and chase copper.
在它们存在的地方，房屋的年龄从 50 年到 500 年不等，小巷随意蜿蜒穿过喀什噶尔人生活和工作了几个世纪的社区。这里是散步、透过大门窥视、与当地人聊天以及欣赏工匠敲锡和追逐铜的好地方。

Traditional houses in Kashgar, rarely more than two storeys high, are built with poplar timber and mud bricks. Walls are very thick but usually unadorned on the outside. The inner courtyards and balconies, however, are decorated with woodcarvings and hangings.
喀什的传统房屋很少超过两层楼高，都是用杨木和泥砖建造的。墙壁很厚，但外面通常没有装饰。然而，内部庭院和阳台装饰有木雕和挂饰。

At the eastern end of Seman Lu stands a 10m-high section of the Old Town walls, which are at least 500 years old. Much of the rest of the Old Town has now been enclosed by modern walls.
在色满路的东端，矗立着一段 10 米高的旧城墙，至少有 500 年的历史。老城区的大部分其他地方现在都被现代城墙包围。

Abakh Hoja MausoleumTOMB
阿巴赫霍贾陵墓

(香妃墓, Xiāngfēimù, Abakh Hoja Maziri; Afaq Khoja Mausoleum; Haohan Village; 浩罕村; ¥30; hdawn-dusk)
（ 香妃墓 ， Xiāngfēimù， Abakh Hoja Maziri;Afaq Khoja 陵墓;浩瀚村; 浩罕村 ;¥30; h 黎明-黄昏）

This 3-hectare mausoleum complex was built by the Khoja family, who ruled the region in the 17th and 18th centuries. Widely considered the holiest Muslim site in Xinjiang, it’s a major pilgrimage destination and a beautiful piece of Islamic architecture, located on the northeastern outskirts of town.
这座占地 3 公顷的陵墓建筑群由 17 世纪和 18 世纪统治该地区的 Khoja 家族建造。它被广泛认为是新疆最神圣的穆斯林圣地，是一个主要的朝圣目的地，也是一座美丽的伊斯兰建筑，位于该镇的东北郊。

Founded as a religious school by Yusuf Khoja, the mausoleum was built in 1640 with further halls and mosques being added over the next three centuries. Not only does it house the remains of Yusuf, but dozens of Khoja family members are also interred here. These include Yusuf’s son Abakh Hoja, a famed 17th-century Sufi and political leader (and after whom the mausoleum is named), and according to legend, Iparhan, Abakh Hoja’s granddaughter.
这座陵墓由优素福·霍贾 （Yusuf Khoja） 创立，是一所宗教学校，建于 1640 年，在接下来的三个世纪里又增加了更多的大厅和清真寺。这里不仅安放着优素福的遗体，还埋葬着数十名霍贾家族成员。其中包括优素福的儿子阿巴赫·霍贾（Abakh Hoja），他是 17 世纪著名的苏菲派和政治领袖（陵墓也以他的名字命名），以及据传说，阿巴赫·霍贾的孙女伊帕尔汉（Iparhan）。

Known to the Chinese as Xiang Fei, the ‘Fragrant Concubine’ of the Emperor Qianlong, Iparhan remains a potent symbol of the Chinese–Uyghur divide. To the Han, she was the beloved but homesick concubine of the Emperor and thus a symbol of national unity. To the Uyghur she was a resistance leader (or the wife of one) who was captured and taken to Beijing where she died (and was likely buried) broken-hearted, or was killed by the Emperor’s mother.
伊帕尔罕被中国人称为向妃，乾隆皇帝的“香妃”，至今仍是中国维吾尔族分歧的有力象征。对汉人来说，她是皇帝心爱但又想家的妃子，因此是国家统一的象征。对维吾尔人来说，她是一名抵抗运动领袖（或维吾尔人的妻子），被俘虏并被带到北京，在那里她心碎地死去（很可能被埋葬），或者被皇帝的母亲杀害。

The mausoleum complex has an irregular design so all the mosques face Mecca at a slightly different angle. South are the main stone gate, with its striking blue tiles, and the High and Low Mosques. These mosques form a typical pairing in Uyghur religious architecture and are known as summer and winter mosques: their open-sided and closed structures, respectively, allow for prayer during the different seasons. Note the wooden and painted columns here, carved in 1926, with their muqarnas capitals (a traditional Persian design likened to hanging stalactites).
陵墓建筑群的设计不规则，因此所有清真寺都以略有不同的角度面向麦加。南面是主石门，上面铺着醒目的蓝色瓷砖，还有高清真寺和低清真寺。这些清真寺是维吾尔族宗教建筑中的典型组合，被称为夏季清真寺和冬季清真寺：它们的开放式和封闭式结构分别允许在不同的季节进行祈祷。请注意这里雕刻于 1926 年的木制和彩绘柱子，带有 muqarnas 柱头（一种传统的波斯设计，比作悬挂的钟乳石）。

The Great Mosque is in the west of the complex, while the small Green Mosque (which also has summer and winter halls) is in the north. The domed mausoleum, the tallest structure in the complex, is east, and is surrounded by a graveyard wall with four colourful towers in the corners. The most striking feature, however, is the exterior of mismatched tiles, the result of piecemeal repairs over the years.
大清真寺位于建筑群的西侧，而小型绿色清真寺（也有夏季和冬季大厅）位于北部。圆顶陵墓是建筑群中最高的建筑，位于东边，周围环绕着墓地墙，角落里有四座五颜六色的塔楼。然而，最引人注目的特征是瓷砖的外部不匹配，这是多年来零碎维修的结果。

WHICH TIME IS IT?
现在是几点？

All of China officially runs on Beijing time (Beijing shíjiān). Xinjiang, several time zones removed from Beijing, however, unofficially runs duelling clocks: while the Chinese tend to stick to the official Beijing time, the locals set their clocks to unofficial Xinjiang time (Xīnjiāng shíjiān), two hours behind Beijing time. Thus 9am Beijing time is 7am Xinjiang time. Most government-run services, such as banks, post offices, bus stations and airlines, run on Beijing time, generally operating from 10am to 1.30pm and from 4pm to 8pm to cater to the time difference. Uyghurs will often automatically use Xinjiang time – a political statement as much as anything else – so always double-check which clock they’re using when making plans!
中国全境正式运行为北京时间（ 北京时健）。 然而，新疆是远离北京的几个时区，但却非正式地使用决斗时钟：中国人倾向于坚持使用官方北京时间，而当地人则将时钟设置为非官方的新疆时间（Xīnjiāng shíjiān），比北京时间晚两个小时。因此，北京时间上午 9 点是新疆时间早上 7 点。大多数政府运营的服务，如银行、邮局、汽车站和航空公司，都按北京时间运行，通常从上午 10 点到下午 1 点 30 分，以及下午 4 点到晚上 8 点，以配合时差。维吾尔人经常会自动使用新疆时间——这是一种政治声明，就像其他任何事情一样——所以在制定计划时一定要仔细检查他们使用的时钟！

Karakoram Highway 中巴公路
喀喇昆仑公路 中巴公路

The Karakoram Hwy (KKH; Zhōngbā Gōnglù) over the Khunjerab Pass (4693m) is one of the world’s most spectacular roads and China’s gateway to Pakistan. For centuries this route was used by caravans plodding down the Silk Road. Khunjerab means ‘valley of blood’ as local bandits used to take advantage of the terrain to slaughter merchants and plunder their wares. Upgraded roads make the going much easier than it once was, though a proposed railway linking China to Pakistan has yet to be developed.
喀喇昆仑高速公路（KKH;Zhōngbā Gōnglù）横跨红其拉甫山口（4693 米）是世界上最壮观的公路之一，也是中国通往巴基斯坦的门户。几个世纪以来，这条路线一直被沿着丝绸之路蹒跚而行的商队使用。Khunjerab 的意思是“血谷”，因为当地土匪过去常常利用地形屠杀商人并掠夺他们的商品。升级后的道路使交通比以前容易得多，尽管连接中国和巴基斯坦的拟议铁路尚未开发。

The main (and practically the only) town between Kashgar and Pakistan is Tashkurgan, a surprisingly modern and sprawling town with a devastatingly beautiful mountain setting. This is where the bus journey to Pakistan begins, and it’s also as far as most travellers not entering Pakistan itself are able to go.
喀什和巴基斯坦之间的主要（也是几乎唯一的）城镇是塔什库尔干，这是一个令人惊讶的现代化和庞大的小镇，拥有极其美丽的山区环境。这是前往巴基斯坦的巴士之旅的起点，也是大多数不进入巴基斯坦的旅客能够到达的地方。

SOUTHERN SILK ROAD

The Silk Road east of Kashgar splits into two threads in the face of the Taklamakan Desert, the second-largest sandy desert in the world. This off-the-grid journey takes you far into the Uyghur heartland, as well as deep into the ancient multiethnic heritage of the region. You’re as likely to come across a centuries-old tiled mosque as you are the ruins of a Buddhist pagoda from the 4th century.

THE SILK ROAD
丝绸之路

Nomadic trading routes across Asia and Europe had existed for thousands of years but what we now call the Silk Road, an intercontinental network connecting the East and West, began to take shape in the 2nd century BC. At the time, the Mediterranean had already been linked to Central Asia by Alexander the Great (and his Roman successors), and China, in its need to defend itself from marauding Xiongnu, was about to do its part.
横跨亚洲和欧洲的游牧贸易路线已经存在了数千年，但我们现在所说的丝绸之路，一个连接东西方的洲际网络，在公元前 2 世纪开始形成。当时，亚历山大大帝（及其罗马继承者）已经将地中海与中亚联系在一起，而中国为了保护自己免受匈奴的掠夺，即将尽自己的一份力量。

In 138 BC the Emperor Wudi sent envoy Zhang Qian to negotiate an alliance with the Yuezhi, a Central Asian people being driven west by the Xiongnu. On his return (after much hardship which included being kidnapped twice), Zhang piqued the emperor’s interest with tales of wealthy neighbouring kingdoms, powerful horses, and trade of Chinese goods, including silk, that had already reached these regions. Over the next two centuries, the Han experienced endless setbacks as they sought to defeat the Xiongnu and secure safe passage from Gansu through Xinjiang, but eventually formal trade with Central Asia was established.
公元前138年，武帝派使者张骞与被匈奴赶往西方的中亚民族月氏谈判结盟。在回国后（经过多次艰辛，包括两次被绑架），张氏用富裕的邻国、强壮的马匹以及已经到达这些地区的地方贸易中国商品（包括丝绸）的故事激起了皇帝的兴趣。在接下来的两个世纪里，汉人在试图击败匈奴并确保从甘肃通过新疆的安全通道时经历了无尽的挫折，但最终与中亚建立了正式的贸易。

Owing as much to continuous political instability as geographical challenges, there was never any one route that goods travelled along, much less a single road; the name Silk Road was coined in 1877 by German geographer Ferdinand von Richtofen. The loose, fragile and often dangerous network of ancient times had its Chinese start in Chang’an (modern Xi’an) and from there proceeded up the Hexi Corridor to Dunhuang. At this great oasis town it split north and south to circumvent the unforgiving Taklamakan Desert. The routes then met again at Kashgar, where they once more split to cross the high, snowy Pamir, Karakorum and Tian Shan Mountains to connect with Samarkand (and eventually Iran and Constantinople), India and the Russian Volga.
由于持续的政治不稳定和地理挑战，货物从来都没有一条路线，更不用说一条道路了;丝绸之路这个名字是由德国地理学家费迪南德·冯·里希托芬于 1877 年创造的。古代松散、脆弱且常常危险的网络始于中国的长安（今习安），然后从那里沿着河西走廊到达敦煌。在这个伟大的绿洲小镇，它分为南北，以绕过无情的塔克拉玛干沙漠。随后，两条路线在喀什再次会合，在那里它们再次分道扬镳，穿越高高的白雪皑皑的帕米尔山脉、喀喇昆仑山脉和天山山脉，与撒马尔罕（最终是伊朗和君士坦丁堡）、印度和俄罗斯伏尔加河相连。

Despite the distances that goods could reach, almost all trade was small scale and local: large caravan teams were rare unless travelling as official envoys, and goods were seldom carried more than a few hundred kilometres by any one group. An average day’s journey was 15km to 20km and traders often made lengthy stops at oasis towns to plan their next stage.
尽管货物可以到达很远的距离，但几乎所有的贸易都是小规模的本地贸易：除非作为官方使节旅行，否则大型商队很少见，而且任何一个团体很少运送货物超过几百公里。平均一天的行程为 15 公里至 20 公里，贸易商经常在绿洲城镇停留很长时间以计划下一阶段。

In addition to silk, which was often used as currency, goods included spices, nuts, fruit, metals, leather products, chemicals, glass, paper, precious gems, gold, ivory, porcelain and exotic animals, including the powerful Ferghana horse much prized by the Chinese. It was the exchange of ideas, technology and culture, however, that is the true legacy of the Silk Road.
除了经常用作货币的丝绸外，商品还包括香料、坚果、水果、金属、皮革制品、化学品、玻璃、纸张、珍贵宝石、黄金、象牙、瓷器和奇异动物，包括中国人非常珍视的强大费尔干纳马。然而，思想、技术和文化的交流才是丝绸之路的真正遗产。

Buddhism entered China via the Silk Road, and later allowed Chinese monks to travel to Gandhara and India for direct study and the gathering of primary texts. In copying Buddhist cave art, which originated in India, the Chinese created some of the finest examples in locations such as Mogao and Kizil. Going in the other direction, fine Chinese tricoloured pottery had influence across Central Asia, the Middle East and Europe.
佛教通过丝绸之路进入中国，后来允许中国僧侣前往犍陀罗和印度进行直接学习和收集原始经典。在复制起源于印度的佛教洞穴艺术时，中国人在莫高窟和克孜勒等地创造了一些最好的例子。另一方面，精美的中国三色陶器在中亚、中东和欧洲产生了影响。

The heyday of Silk Road trade began in the 6th century under the stable but militarily strong Tang dynasty. Chang’an became one of the most cosmopolitan capitals in the world, with an estimated 5000 foreigners, including Indians, Turks, Iranians, Japanese and Koreans permanently settled there. Trade declined and then stabilised under Mongolian rule of China, but by the 14th century, sea routes were supplanting the slow and still dangerous overland routes. By the 16th century, the Silk Road network had reverted to obscure local trading and never recovered its former importance.
丝绸之路贸易的鼎盛时期始于公元 6 世纪，在稳定但军事强大的唐朝统治下。长安成为世界上最国际化的首都之一，估计有 5000 名外国人永久定居在那里，包括印度人、土耳其人、伊朗人、日本人和韩国人。在蒙古对中国的统治下，贸易下降然后稳定下来，但到了 14 世纪，海路正在取代缓慢且仍然危险的陆路。到了 16 世纪，丝绸之路网络又恢复了晦涩难懂的当地贸易，再也没有恢复到以前的重要性。

In June 2014, at the behest of China, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan, Unesco listed the 5000km Tian Shan Corridor of the Silk Roads as a World Heritage Site. The designation highlights not just the obvious pagodas, palaces, cave art and remains of the Great Wall (including beacon towers and forts), but also the caravanserai and way stations that provided relief and lodging for traders. Hopefully in the coming years the listing will encourage more conservation and research and not be seen as a licence for unfettered tourist development.
2014年6月，应中国、哈萨克斯坦和吉尔吉斯斯坦的要求，联合国教科文组织将5000公里的丝绸之路天山走廊列为世界遗产。该名称不仅突出了明显的宝塔、宫殿、洞穴艺术和长城遗迹（包括烽火台和堡垒），还突出了为商人提供救济和住宿的商队旅馆和中转站。希望在未来几年，该清单将鼓励更多的保护和研究，而不是被视为不受限制的旅游开发许可证。

Kanas Lake Nature Reserve 喀纳斯湖自然保护区

Stunning Kanas Lake (哈纳斯湖, Hānàsī Hú) is a long finger of water surrounded by soaring mountain peaks nestled in the southernmost reaches of the Siberian taiga ecosystem, pinched in between Mongolia, Russia and Kazakhstan. Most of the local inhabitants are Kazakh or Tuvan, though Chinese tourists (and the occasional foreigner) descend on the place in droves during the summer months.
令人惊叹的喀纳斯湖（ 哈纳斯湖 ，Hānàsī Hú）是一片长长的水，周围环绕着高耸的山峰，坐落在西伯利亚针叶林生态系统的最南端，夹在蒙古、俄罗斯和哈萨克斯坦之间。大多数当地居民是哈萨克人或图瓦人，尽管中国游客（偶尔还有外国人）在夏季成群结队地来到这里。

Many visitors come hoping for a cameo by the Kanas Lake Monster, China’s Nessie, who has long figured in stories around yurt campfires to scare the kids. She appears every year or two, bringing loads of journalists and conspiracy hounds.
许多游客前来都希望喀纳斯湖水怪客串中国的尼斯湖水怪，它长期以来一直出现在蒙古包篝火周围的故事中，以吓唬孩子们。她每隔一两年就会出现一次，带来大量的记者和阴谋猎犬。

The whole area is only easily accessible from April to October, with ice and snow making transport difficult at other times. The gorgeous autumn colours peak around mid-September.
整个地区只有在 4 月至 10 月才可轻松到达，其他时间冰雪使交通困难。绚丽的秋色在九月中旬左右达到顶峰。

Yining 伊宁

Located on the historic border between the Chinese and Russian empires, Yining (Yīníng; Yili or Gulja) has long been subject to a tug-of-war between the two sides. The city was occupied by Russian troops between 1872 and 1881, and in 1962 there were major Sino–Soviet clashes along the Ili River (Yīlí Hé). There are no unmissable sights here but it’s a pleasant, little-visited stop en route to Sayram Lake, or a good place to break an overland journey to Kazakhstan.
位于中国和俄罗斯帝国的历史边界，伊宁（Yīníng;伊犁或古力加）长期以来一直受到双方之间的拉锯战。该市于 1872 年至 1881 年间被俄罗斯军队占领，1962 年伊犁河沿岸发生了中苏重大冲突。这里没有不容错过的景点，但它是前往塞拉姆湖途中一个令人愉快的、鲜有人迹罕至的站点，或者是结束前往哈萨克斯坦的陆路旅行的好地方。

In 2014 the stadium in Yining was the site of a mass trial in which 55 Uyghurs were charged with terrorist activities. At least one death sentence was handed down. A similar mass trial was held in 1997 and is the subject of Nick Holdstock’s book The Tree That Bleeds (2011).
2014 年，伊宁体育场是一场大规模审判的地点，55 名维吾尔人被指控从事恐怖活动。至少判处了一项死刑。1997 年举行了类似的大规模审判，这是尼克·霍尔德斯托克 （Nick Holdstock） 的著作《 流血的树》（The Tree That Bleeds，2011 年）的主题。
