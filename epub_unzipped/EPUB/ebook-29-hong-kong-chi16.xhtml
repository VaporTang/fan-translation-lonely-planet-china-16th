Why Go?
必去理由

Like a shot of adrenaline, Hong Kong quickens the pulse. Skyscrapers march up jungle-clad slopes by day and blaze neon by night across a harbour criss-crossed by freighters and motor junks. Above streets teeming with traffic, five-star hotels stand next to ageing tenement blocks.
香港，宛如一针强心剂，瞬间便能让你的脉搏随之狂跳。白日里，摩天大楼如军队般列队攀上丛林密布的山坡；入夜后，璀璨的霓虹又将天际点亮，映照着货轮与机动帆船往来穿梭的维多利亚港。在车水马龙的街道之上，五星级酒店与斑驳老旧的唐楼正并肩而立。

The very acme of luxury can be yours, though enjoying the city need not cost the earth. The HK$2.50 ride across the harbour must be one of the world’s best-value cruises. A meander through a market offers similarly cheap thrills. You can also escape the crowds – just head for one of the city’s many country parks.
在香港，顶级奢华触手可及，但享受这座城市的成本却远非天价。仅需$2.5港元即可横渡维多利亚港，这趟旅程堪称全球性价比最高的航游体验。漫步于某个本地市集，同样能带给你不少平价的惊喜。你也可以选择避开喧嚣，城中为数众多的郊野公园便是绝佳的去处。

It’s also a city that lives to eat, offering diners the very best of China and beyond. Hong Kong, above all, rewards those who grab experiences by the scruff of the neck, who’ll try that stinky tofu, explore half-deserted villages or stroll beaches far from neon and steel.
这也是一座为“吃”而生的城市，为食客们献上中国乃至世界各地的顶级珍馐。最重要的是，香港会慷慨回报那些主动出击、敢于体验的探索者——那些敢于尝试臭豆腐、寻访半荒的村落，或是在远离霓虹与钢铁的海滩上漫步的人。

History
历史

Until European traders started importing opium into the country, Hong Kong was an obscure backwater in the Chinese empire. The British developed the trade aggressively and by the start of the 19th century traded this ‘foreign mud’ for Chinese tea, silk and porcelain.
在欧洲商人开始向中国输入鸦片之前，香港不过是中华帝国版图上一个默默无闻的角落。后来，英国人将这项贸易越做越大，到19世纪初，他们就用这种“洋泥”来换取中国的茶叶、丝绸和瓷器。

China’s attempts to stamp out the opium trade gave the British the pretext they needed for military action. Gunboats were sent in. In 1841 the Union flag was hoisted on Hong Kong Island and the Treaty of Nanking, which brought an end to the so-called First Opium War, ceded the island to the British crown ‘in perpetuity’.
中方试图肃清鸦片贸易的举动，恰好给了英国人渴望已久的军事借口。于是，炮舰出动了。1841年，米字旗在香港岛上空升起；而那份宣告了所谓“第一次鸦片战争”结束的《南京条约》，则将这座岛屿“永久”地割让给了英国王室。

At the end of the Second Opium War in 1860, Britain took possession of Kowloon Peninsula, and in 1898 a 99-year lease was granted for the New Territories.
1860年，第二次鸦片战争尘埃落定，英国顺势将九龙半岛也收入囊中。到了1898年，他们又通过一纸99年的租约，将“新界”纳入治下。

Through the 20th century Hong Kong grew in fits and starts. Waves of refugees fled China for Hong Kong during times of turmoil. Trade flourished, as did British expat social life, until the Japanese army crashed the party in 1941.
整个20世纪，香港的发展之路可谓磕磕绊绊。每当中国内地陷入动荡，便有成批的难民涌入这片避风港。贸易与日俱增，英国侨民的社交生活也同样多姿多彩——直到1941年，日本军队的不请自来，彻底搅乱了这场派对。

By the end of WWII Hong Kong’s population had fallen from 1.6 million to 610,000. But trouble in China soon swelled the numbers again as refugees (including industrialists) from the communist victory in 1949 increased the population beyond two million. This, together with a UN trade embargo on China during the Korean War and China’s isolation in the next three decades, enabled Hong Kong to reinvent itself as one of the world’s most dynamic ports and manufacturing and financial-service centres.
二战结束时，香港的人口已从160万骤降至61万。但中国内地的时局动荡很快又让这个数字再度膨胀：1949年，为躲避战乱，大批难民（包括众多实业家）涌入香港，使其人口一举突破两百万大关。这一波移民潮，加上朝鲜战争期间联合国对华的贸易禁运，以及中国在随后三十年里近乎与世隔绝的状态，共同造就了一个千载难逢的历史机遇。香港抓住了它，得以脱胎换骨，一跃成为全球最具活力的港口、制造业及金融服务中心之一。

In 1984 Britain agreed to return what would become the Special Administrative Region (SAR) of Hong Kong to China in 1997, on condition it would retain its free-market economy and its social and legal systems for 50 years. China called it ‘One country, two systems’. On 1 July 1997, in pouring rain, outside the Hong Kong Convention & Exhibition Centre, the British era ended.
1984年，中英双方达成协议：英国将于1997年将香港归还中国，条件是这片日后成立的“香港特别行政区”（SAR）可在50年内保留其自由市场经济与社会及法律制度。中方称此构想为“一国两制”。1997年7月1日，香港会议展览中心外，大雨滂沱。英属时代，在此落下了帷幕。

In the years that have followed, Hong Kong has weathered major storms – an economic downturn, the outbreak of the SARS virus and increasing political activism among Hong Kong’s youth. A deluge of tourists from mainland China, which now make up nearly 70% of the territory’s visitor numbers, and soaring rents are changing the urban fabric of Hong Kong. A nagging mistrust of the government hangs in the air.
回归后的岁月里，香港经历了一场又一场的风暴——经济衰退、SARS病毒的爆发，以及本地年轻人中日益高涨的社会运动。与此同时，来自中国内地的游客如潮水般涌入（如今已占访港旅客总数的近七成），与不断飙升的租金一道，正在深刻地改变着香港的城市肌理。空气中，始终弥漫着一种对政府挥之不去的疑虑。

Two decades on from the handover, many Hong Kongers are worried about what they see as government attempts to destabilise the ‘one country, two systems’ principle of Chinese rule. From his seat in Beijing, President Xi Jinping has stepped up interventions in Hong Kong affairs. In June 2019 the government tried to push through a controversial bill that would allow the Chinese authorities to extradite criminal suspects from Hong Kong to the mainland. Civil rights activists, journalists, lawyers and businesses all condemned the bill as a threat to Hong Kong’s political autonomy. It is estimated that up to two million people took to the streets to prevent the bill from being passed. Protest was met by police force, which was heavily criticised. After initially refusing to address public outrage, Chief Executive Carrie Lam suspended the bill and then formally withdrew it in September 2019. However, at the time of writing protests had continued to escalate, lasting several months and causing widespread disruption on Hong Kong Island, in Kowloon and at the international airport. After years of increasing political antipathy, the extradition bill appears to have rallied the Hong Kong people and united them to speak out for democracy. Neither side look willing to give in, thousands of protestors have been arrested, and police have become increasingly heavy-handed, resorting to tear gas, pepper spray and even petrol bombs.
回归二十余年后，许多香港人对于他们眼中所谓“动摇‘一国两制’原则”的举动深感忧虑。国家主席习近平也加强了对香港事务的干预。2019年6月，特区政府试图强行通过一项备受争议的法案，该法案将允许中国内地当局从香港引渡犯罪嫌疑人。民权活动家、记者、律师乃至商界人士一致谴责，称其严重威胁香港的政治自治。据估计，多达两百万人走上街头，以阻止法案通过。抗议活动遭到了警方的武力应对，而警方的做法也备受诟病。在最初拒绝回应公众的怒火后，行政长官林郑月娥先是宣布暂缓法案，并最终于2019年9月正式将其撤回。然而，在本书撰稿之际，抗议活动仍在持续升级，已蔓延数月之久，并在香港岛、九龙甚至国际机场造成了大规模的混乱。在历经多年日渐加剧的政治对立后，这部引渡法案似乎已将香港人凝聚起来，让他们为民主大声疾呼。双方似乎都无意让步，数千名抗议者被捕，而警方的手段也愈发强硬，动用了催泪瓦斯、胡椒喷雾，甚至汽油弹。

In February 2020, Beijing hardliner and Xi Jinping ally Xia Baolong was appointed director of China’s Hong Kong and Macau Affairs Office, stoking fears that Beijing intends to continue tightening its grip on the region. At the time of press, Hong Kongers’ appetite for protest had been quelled by the fight to contain coronavirus (COVID-19). However resentment towards Beijing still ripples beneath the surface and the battle for Hong Kong looks far from over: seek an update on the current situation with your local foreign office before visiting.
2020年2月，被外界视为北京鹰派人物的夏宝龙，被任命为中国国务院港澳事务办公室主任。此举激起千层浪，许多人担忧北京意图继续收紧对香港的控制。在本书付印之际，为遏制新冠病毒（COVID-19）的传播，港人的抗议势头已暂时平息。然而，对北京的怨气仍在暗流之下涌动，香港的这场抗争也远未画上句号。因此，我们建议您：在到访前，请务必向本国的外事部门查询香港的最新情况。

1Central District
1中环

The minted heart of Asia’s financial hub is crammed with corporate citadels, colonial relics and massive monuments to consumerism. It’s where you’ll find the stock exchange, the Mandarin Oriental, Prada and world-class restaurants and bars, all housed in a compelling mix of modern architecture and heritage digs. Dynamic day and night, Central and its raucous bedfellows, Lan Kwai Fong and Soho, are where well-heeled Hong Kongers come to work and play.
作为亚洲金融枢纽那颗“新鲜出炉”的心脏，这里遍布着壁垒森严的企业巨塔、殖民时代的优雅遗迹，以及一座座为消费主义打造的宏伟丰碑。在这里，香港交易所、文华东方酒店、普拉达旗舰店，连同世界顶级的餐厅与酒吧，全都和谐共存于现代建筑与历史古迹的迷人组合之中。无论昼夜，中环与其两个热闹非凡的“亲密伙伴”——兰桂坊和苏豪区——都同样充满活力，是香港那些衣香鬓影的精英们工作与享乐的首选之地。

o 山顶缆车缆车

(map Google map; %852 2522 0922; www.thepeak.com.hk; Lower Terminus, 33 Garden Rd, Central; adult/child & senior return HK$52/23, one way HK$37/14; h7am-midnight; mCentral, exit J2)
（ 地图 谷歌地图 ; % 852 2522 0922;www.thepeak.com.hk;中环花园路 33 号下总站;成人/小童及长者来回港币 52 元/23 元，单程港币 37 元/14 元; h 早上 7 点至午夜; m 中环，J2 出口）

This cable-hauled funicular railway has been scaling the 396m ascent to the highest point on Hong Kong Island since 1888. A ride on this clanking tram is a classic Hong Kong experience, with vertiginous views over the city as you ascend the steep mountainside. It’s become so popular that the whole experience is being upgraded with larger trams and a bigger lower terminus. A five-month service suspension in 2020 should allow work to be completed in 2021.
自1888年起，这条依靠缆索牵引的铁路便一直攀爬396米的高度，将乘客送往香港岛的最高点——太平山顶。伴随着缆车“哐当哐当”的声响，沿着陡峭的山坡向上攀升，令人目眩的城市风光在眼前徐徐展开——这绝对是香港最经典的体验之一。由于实在太受欢迎，整个缆车体验迎来了全面升级，包括换上了载客量更大的车厢和扩建了山下的总站。按原计划，在2020年暂停服务五个月后，这项工程已于2021年完工。

The Peak Tram runs every 10 to 15 minutes from 7am to midnight; using an Octopus card to pay for the ticket will help reduce your queuing time. Note that the ticket office will push the fare that includes entry to the Sky Terrace 428 viewing deck (HK$84/99 per one way/return).
山顶缆车由早上 7 时至午夜，每 10 至 15 分钟一班;使用八达通卡支付门票将有助于减少您的排队时间。请注意，售票处将推销包括进入 Sky Terrace 428 观景台的票价（单程/往返港币 84/99 元）。

The long-awaited reinvention of Hong Kong’s Central Police Station as a heritage and arts complex was completed in May 2018. It’s impressive and vast, with two courtyards and more than a dozen blocks that were formally dormitories, an armoury, prison cells and Hong Kong’s central magistracy. Visits should start in the Barrack Block, where a gallery explores the site’s history. Other highlights include the Life in Victorian Prison exhibition in Block 12 and the JC Contemporary art gallery fronting the upper Prison Yard.
期待已久的香港中区警署改建为文物及艺术综合体的工程已于 2018 年 5 月完成。它令人印象深刻，面积广阔，有两个庭院和十几个街区，这些街区是正式的宿舍、军械库、牢房和香港的中央裁判官。参观应该从军营街区开始，那里的画廊探索了该遗址的历史。其他亮点包括位于 12 号街区的维多利亚监狱生活展览和位于上层监狱院子前面的 JC 当代艺术画廊。

HSBC BuildingNOTABLE BUILDING
汇丰大厦著名建筑

(滙豐銀總大廈; map Google map; www.about.hsbc.com.hk/hsbc-in-hong-kong; 1 Queen’s Rd, Central; mCentral, exit K) F
（ 滙豐銀總大廈 ; 地图谷歌地图 ;www.about.hsbc.com.hk/hsbc-in-hong-kong;中环皇后路 1 号; m 中环，K 出口） F

This remarkable building, designed by British architect Sir Norman Foster in 1985, has stood the test of time – more than 30 years on, its magnetism can still be felt in Central. On completion it was the world’s most expensive building and considered an engineering marvel, reflecting Foster’s wish to break the mould of previous bank architecture. The ground floor is an inviting two-storey walk-through public space, housing an exhibition of HSBC’s Hong Kong history and architecture.
这座非凡的建筑由英国建筑师诺曼·福斯特爵士于 1985 年设计，经受住了时间的考验——30 多年过去了，中环仍然能感受到它的吸引力。竣工后，它是世界上最昂贵的建筑，被认为是一个工程奇迹，反映了福斯特打破以前银行建筑模式的愿望。一楼是一个迷人的两层步行式公共空间，设有汇丰香港历史和建筑展览。

Underfoot, look out for the illuminated street map of Central Hong Kong, which shows how land reclamation has changed the blueprint of this area; when HSBC’s new headquarters opened in 1986 it stood on the waterfront.
脚下，留意香港中环的灯光街道地图，它展示了填海如何改变了该地区的蓝图;1986 年汇丰银行的新总部开业时，它矗立在海滨。

Don’t miss the pair of bronze lions guarding the harbourside entrance of the building. Called Stephen (left) and Stitt (right), they’re named after HSBC managers from the 1920s. Both bear shrapnel scars from the Battle of Hong Kong. Rub their paws for luck.
不要错过守卫大楼海港入口的一对青铜狮子。他们分别叫斯蒂芬（左）和斯蒂特（右），以 1920 年代汇丰银行经理的名字命名。两人都带有香港保卫战的弹片伤痕。揉搓他们的爪子以求好运。

Hong Kong Chinese, irreverent as always, call the 52-storey glass and aluminium structure the ‘Robot Building’. From the ground floor, escalators rise to the main banking hall. This level is more ordinary than you might expect, but it’s worth ascending to gaze at the cathedral-like atrium and the natural light filtering through its windows.
香港华人一如既往地不敬，称这座52层高的玻璃和铝结构为“机器人大厦”。从一楼，自动扶梯上升到主银行大厅。这个楼层比你想象的要普通，但值得上去凝视大教堂般的中庭和透过窗户的自然光。

1The Peak & Northwest Hong Kong Island
1 山顶及香港岛西北部

You’ll find many of Hong Kong’s most intriguing sights around the Peak and in the neighbourhoods below it, from quirky museums and historic buildings to fragrant temples.
您会在山顶周围和山顶下方的社区找到许多香港最迷人的景点，从奇特的博物馆和历史建筑到芬芳的寺庙。

oVictoria PeakVIEWPOINT
o 太平山顶观景台

(維多利亞山頂; map Google map; %852 2849 0668; www.thepeak.com.hk; combined Peak Tram Sky Pass adult/child return HK$99/47, one way HK$84/38; h24hr; g15 from Central, below Exchange Sq, dPeak Tram Lower Terminus)
（ 维多利亚山顶 ; 地图谷歌地图 ; % 852 2849 0668;www.thepeak.com.hk;山顶缆车 Sky Pass 成人/儿童往返 HK$99/47，单程 HK$84/38; h 24 小时; g 中环 15 路，交汇广场下方， d 山顶缆车下总站）

Standing at 552m, Victoria Peak is the highest point on Hong Kong Island. It is also one of the most visited spots by tourists, and it’s not hard to see why. Sweeping views of the metropolis, verdant woods and easy but spectacular walks are all reachable in just eight minutes from Central via Hong Kong’s 125-year-old, gravity-defying Peak Tram. Predictably, it’s become a money-making circus with restaurants and two shopping malls, but there’s still magic up here if you can get past that.
太平山顶海拔 552 米，是香港岛的最高点。它也是游客访问量最大的景点之一，原因不难理解。从中环经香港拥有 125 年历史的反重力山顶缆车，只需 8 分钟即可到达大都会一览无余的景色、青翠的树林和轻松而壮观的步道。可以预见的是，它已经成为一个赚钱的马戏团，拥有餐馆和两个购物中心，但如果你能克服这一点，这里仍然有魔力。

The Peak Tram’s upper terminus spits you out at the Peak Tower. Ascend to Level 5 and you’ll reach the Sky Terrace 428, so named because it stands at 428 metres above sea level. In Hong Kong terms, this is the top of the world and you’ll be greeted with panoramic 360-degree views of Hong Kong’s forest of skyscrapers, the harbour and Kowloon beyond (if you can dodge the Instagrammers and official photographer touting for business in front of the best framed view).
山顶缆车的上层终点站将您带到山顶凌霄阁。登上 5 层，您将到达 Sky Terrace 428，因其海拔 428 米而得名。用香港的话来说，这是世界之巅，迎接您的是香港摩天大楼林、海港和九龙的 360 度全景（如果您能避开 Instagram 用户和官方摄影师在最佳框架前兜售业务的话）。

If you’re not bothered about the highest point, and even if you are, it’s worth seeking out the Lions View Point Pavilion – a far more charming lookout with a cute Chinese pagoda and gate, bristling with lion statues. This is where elderly locals come to sit and enjoy the view. It’s a signposted two-minute walk from the tram’s upper terminus.
如果您不关心最高点，即使您关心，也值得寻找狮子观景台亭 ——一个更迷人的观景台，有一座可爱的中国宝塔和大门，上面挂满了狮子雕像。这是当地老年人坐下来欣赏风景的地方。从有轨电车的上层终点站步行两分钟即可到达路标。

Some 500m to the northwest of the upper terminus, up steep Mt Austin Rd, is the site of the old governor’s summer lodge, which was burned to the ground by Japanese soldiers during WWII. The beautiful gardens still remain, however, and have been refurbished with faux-Victorian gazebos, sundials, benches and stone pillars. They are open to the public; it takes about 30 minutes to get up here and your reward is that it’s blissfully peaceful. Head past the gardens and you’ll find a second lookout point with island and sea views.
在上终点站西北约 500 米处，沿着陡峭的奥斯汀山路 （Mt Austin Rd） 向上，是旧总督的避暑别墅所在地，该小屋在二战期间被日本士兵烧毁。然而，美丽的花园仍然存在，并用人造维多利亚时代的凉亭、日晷、长凳和石柱进行了翻新。它们向公众开放;到达这里大约需要 30 分钟，您的奖励是这里非常宁静。经过花园，您会发现第二个观景台，可欣赏岛屿和海景。

For longer walks, including the 3.5km Morning Trail, pick up maps from the Hong Kong Visitor Centre in the disused tram beside the Peak Tower, or download the Enjoy Hiking Hong Kong app.
如需长途步行，包括 3.5 公里的晨间径 ，可以在山顶凌霄阁旁废弃的电车上从香港游客中心领取地图，或下载 Enjoy Hiking Hong Kong 应用程序。

One of Hong Kong’s oldest temples and a declared monument, atmospheric Man Mo Temple is dedicated to the gods of literature (‘Man’), holding a writing brush, and of war (‘Mo’), wielding a sword. Built in 1847 during the Qing dynasty by wealthy Chinese merchants, it was, besides a place of worship, a court of arbitration for local disputes when trust was thin between the Chinese and the colonialists.
文武庙是香港最古老的寺庙之一，也是法定古迹，大气的文武庙供奉着手持毛笔的文学之神（“人”）和挥舞着剑的战争之神（“墨”）。它于 1847 年清朝时期由中国富商建造，除了礼拜场所外，还是中国人与殖民者之间信任薄弱时地方纠纷的仲裁法院。

Pak Sing Ancestral HallTEMPLE
白星祠 TEMPLE

(廣福祠; Kwong Fuk Ancestral Hall; map Google map; 42 Tai Ping Shan St, Sheung Wan; h8am-5pm; g26)
（ 广福祠 ;广福祠堂; 地图谷歌地图 ;上环太平山街 42 号; h 上午 8 点至下午 5 点; g 26）

In the 19th century many Chinese who left home in search of better horizons died overseas. As it was the wish of traditional Chinese to be buried in their home towns, this temple was built in 1856 to store corpses awaiting burial in China, and to serve as a public ancestral hall for those who could not afford the expense of bone repatriation. Families of the latter have erected 3000 memorial tablets for their ancestors in a room behind the altar.
在19世纪，许多离开家乡寻找更好视野的中国人死在了海外。由于中国传统人希望葬在家乡，这座寺庙建于 1856 年，用于存放在中国等待埋葬的尸体，并作为公共祠堂，供那些无力承担骨头遣返费用的人使用。后者的家庭在祭坛后面的一个房间里为他们的祖先竖立了 3000 块纪念碑。

1Wan Chai & Northeast Hong Kong Island
1 湾仔及香港岛东北部

Wan Chai and its environs might have fewer traditional sights than Kowloon and Central, but this claustrophobic, east-west jumble of high-rise prosperity is the sight, whether ogled from the upper deck of a tram, or at street level losing yourself in the crowds. The section of Wan Chai between Queen’s Rd East and Johnston Rd, where the original coastline was, is rich with ancient and modern heritage, while the new harbour-front, to the north on reclaimed land, has monuments to culture, commerce and sovereignty. You’ll find scenic stretches of manicured green amid the urban jungle, along with temples wreathed in incense, and one or two good museums and galleries. You just have to get out there and explore.
湾仔及其周边地区的传统景点可能比九龙和中环少，但无论是从电车的上层甲板凝视，还是在街道上迷失在人群中，这种幽闭恐怖、东西向的高层建筑繁华是一道风景线 。 湾仔介乎皇后大道东和庄士敦道之间的一段，原有海岸线，拥有丰富的古今遗产，而北面填海的新海滨则有文化、商业和主权的古迹。您会在城市丛林中发现风景优美的修剪整齐的绿色，以及被熏香笼罩的寺庙，以及一两个不错的博物馆和画廊。你只需要走出去探索。

(香港公園; map Google map; %852 2521 5041; www.lcsd.gov.hk/en/parks/hkp; 19 Cotton Tree Dr, Admiralty; h6am-11pm; c; mAdmiralty, exit C1)

A hillside oasis squeezed between Central and Admiralty, Hong Kong Park was built in 1991 over part of the former Victoria Barracks. Kowloon has most of Hong Kong’s major museums, its busiest and most beguiling Taoist temple complex, and some of the liveliest street markets. Most visitors arrive in Yung Shue Wan, a counterculture haven popular with expats.
大多数游客抵达榕树湾，这是一个深受外籍人士欢迎的反主流文化天堂。
