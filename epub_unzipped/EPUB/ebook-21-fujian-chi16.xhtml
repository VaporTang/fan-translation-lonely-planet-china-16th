Why Go?
为什么要去？

Fujian (福建, Fújiàn) is an attractive coastal province with a long seafaring history. As a significant stop on the maritime Silk Road, its cities developed an easy cosmopolitan outlook and visitors are surprised by the traces of the wider world in its architecture, food, language and people.
福建（ 福建 ，Fújiàn）是一个迷人的沿海省份，拥有悠久的航海历史。作为海上丝绸之路上的重要一站，其城市发展出轻松的国际化景观，游客对其建筑、美食、语言和人民的更广阔世界的痕迹感到惊讶。

Xiamen is the star attraction for visitors, with its long seaside promenade and easy access to little Gulang Yu, a hip island enclave just offshore. Many travellers also pass through the area en route to the Taiwanese island of Kinmen.
厦门是游客的明星景点，拥有长长的海滨长廊，可轻松前往近海的时尚岛屿飞地小鼓浪屿。许多旅客在前往台湾金门岛的途中也会经过该地区。

Away from the coast, the Unesco World Heritage–listed tǔlóu (roundhouses) rise out of the countryside and for generations have housed traditional Hakka and Fujianese communities. Further north, the hill station of Wuyi Shan offers year-round hiking opportunities and a memorable river cruise on bamboo rafts.
远离海岸，被联合国教科文组织列为世界遗产的圆屋（ 圆屋）从乡村拔地而起，世代相传，一直是传统的客家和福建社区的所在地。再往北，武夷山的山站全年提供徒步旅行机会和乘坐竹筏进行难忘的河流巡游。

History
历史

The coastal region of Fujian, known in English as Fukien or Hokkien, has been part of the Chinese empire since the Qin dynasty (221–207 BC), when it was known as Min. Sea trade transformed the region from a frontier into one of the centres of the Chinese world. During the Song and Yuan dynasties the coastal city of Quanzhou was one of the main ports on the maritime Silk Road, which transported not only silk but also other textiles, precious stones, porcelain and a host of other valuables. The city was home to more than 100,000 Arab merchants, missionaries and travellers.
福建沿海地区，英文称为福建或福建，自秦朝（公元前221-207年）以来一直是中华帝国的一部分，当时它被称为闽。海上贸易使该地区从边疆转变为中国世界的中心之一。在宋元时期，沿海城市泉州是海上丝绸之路上的主要港口之一，不仅运输丝绸，还运输其他纺织品、宝石、瓷器和许多其他贵重物品。这座城市是超过 100,000 名阿拉伯商人、传教士和旅行者的家园。

Despite a decline in the province’s fortunes after the Ming dynasty restricted maritime commerce in the 15th century, the resourcefulness of the Fujian people proved itself in the numbers heading for Taiwan, Singapore, the Philippines, Malaysia and Indonesia. Overseas links that were forged continue today, contributing much to the modern character of the province.
尽管在 15 世纪明朝限制海上贸易后，该省的命运有所下降，但福建人民的足智多谋在前往台湾、新加坡、菲律宾、马来西亚和印度尼西亚的人数中得到了证明。建立的海外联系一直延续到今天，为该省的现代特征做出了巨大贡献。

Language
语言

Fujian is one of the most linguistically diverse provinces in China. Locals speak variations of the Min dialect, which includes Taiwanese. Min is divided into various subgroups – you can expect to hear Southern Min (Mǐnnán Huà) in Xiamen and Quanzhou, and Eastern Min (Dōng Mǐn) in Fuzhou. Using Mandarin is not a problem.
福建是中国语言最多样化的省份之一。当地人说闽语方言的变体，其中包括台湾话。闽语分为不同的亚组——您可以在厦门和泉州听到闽南语（Mǐnnán Huà），在福州听到闽东语（Dōng Mǐn）。使用普通话不是问题。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

Fujian is well connected to the neighbouring provinces of Guangdong and Jiangxi by train and coastal highway. Xiamen and Fuzhou have airline connections to most of the country, including Hong Kong, and Taipei and Kaohsiung in Taiwan. Wuyi Shan has flight connections to China’s larger cities, including Beijing, Shanghai and Hong Kong. The coastal freeway also goes all the way to Hong Kong from Xiamen. The D-class train links Xiamen to Shanghai in eight hours.
福建与邻近省份广东和江西的火车和沿海公路相连。厦门和福州有航班连接全国大部分地区，包括香港，以及台湾的台北和高雄。武夷山有飞往中国大城市的航班，包括北京、上海和香港。沿海高速公路也从厦门一路通往香港。D 级列车将厦门与上海连接起来，车程为 8 小时。

For exploring the interior, high-speed D trains are more comfortable than travelling by bus, but not always more convenient. Wuyi Shan is linked to Fuzhou, Quanzhou and Xiamen by train. There are also daily flights between Xiamen and Wuyi Shan.
对于探索内陆地区，高速 D 列车比乘坐公共汽车更舒适，但并不总是更方便。武夷山通过火车与福州、泉州和厦门相连。厦门和武夷山之间也有每日航班。

History
历史

Xiamen was founded around the mid-14th century in the early years of the Ming dynasty, when the city walls were built and the town was established as a major seaport and commercial centre. In the 17th century it became a place of refuge for Ming rulers fleeing the Manchu invaders. Xiamen and nearby Kinmen (金门, Jīnmén) were bases for the Ming armies that, under the command of the general Koxinga, raised their anti-Manchu battle cry, ‘Resist the Qing and restore the Ming’.
厦门始建于明初 14 世纪中叶左右，当时城墙建成，成为主要海港和商业中心。17 世纪，它成为逃离满清侵略者的明朝统治者的避难所。厦门和附近的金门（ 金门 ，Jīnmén）是明军的基地，明军在将军科兴嘎的指挥下，高举了反满的战斗口号，“抗清复明”。

The Portuguese arrived in the 16th century, followed by the British in the 17th century, and later by the French and the Dutch, all attempting, rather unsuccessfully, to establish Xiamen as a trade port. The port was closed to foreigners in the 1750s and it was not until the Opium Wars that things began to change. In August 1841 a British naval force of 38 ships carrying artillery and soldiers sailed into Xiamen harbour, forcing the port to open. Xiamen then became one of the first treaty ports.
葡萄牙人于 16 世纪抵达，随后是英国人于 17 世纪抵达，后来是法国人和荷兰人，他们都试图将厦门建立为贸易港口，但都没有成功。该港口在 1750 年代对外国人关闭，直到鸦片战争才开始发生变化。1841年8月，一支由38艘载有火炮和士兵的舰艇组成的英国海军驶入厦门港，迫使港口开放。厦门随后成为最早的通商口岸之一。

Japanese and western powers followed soon after, establishing consulates and making nearby Gulang Yu a foreign enclave. Xiamen turned Japanese in 1938 and remained that way until 1945.
日本和西方列强紧随其后，建立了领事馆，使附近的鼓浪屿成为外国飞地。厦门于 1938 年转为日本，并一直保持到 1945 年。

1 景点

The town of Xiamen is on the island of the same name. It’s connected to the mainland by a 5km-long causeway bearing a railway, a Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) line, a road and a footpath. The most absorbing part of Xiamen is near the western (waterfront) district, directly opposite the small island of Gulang Yu. This is the old area of town, known for its colonial-era architecture, parks and winding streets. At its heart, though, is the tourist-heavy pedestrianised Zhongshan Lu walking street, which stays hectic until 10pm when it becomes deserted, but makes for a useful point of reference.
厦门镇位于同名岛屿上。它通过一条 5 公里长的堤道与大陆相连，堤道上有铁路、捷运 （BRT） 线、道路和人行道。厦门最吸引人的地方是靠近西部（滨水）区，正对着古浪屿小岛。这是城镇的老城区，以其殖民时代的建筑、公园和蜿蜒的街道而闻名。不过，其核心是游客密集的中山路步行街，它一直很繁忙，直到晚上 10 点才变得空无一人，但却是一个有用的参考点。

A short hop from the large island city of Xiamen, car-free Gulang Yu (Gǔlàng Yǔ) is a Unesco World Heritage Site that shows off its cross-cultural heritage in its fusion of architectural styles. The island was a turn-of-the-20th-century international enclave where consulates from Europe, America and Japan managed their affairs among banyan trees and vine-strewn villas.
古浪屿距离大岛城市厦门仅几步之遥，无车通道被联合国教科文组织列为世界遗产，以其建筑风格的融合展示了其跨文化遗产。该岛是 20 世纪之交的国际飞地，来自欧洲、美国和日本的领事馆在榕树和藤蔓遍布的别墅中管理事务。

A day or two spent wandering Gulang Yu’s museums, tunnels, trails and beaches is a highlight of a visit to Southern China, but for now it’s still mostly locals who hang out in the increasingly slick cafe scene. Breathe deeply if the weekend crowds get too heavy; there is sanctuary to be found if you seek it.
花一两天时间在鼓浪屿的博物馆、隧道、小径和海滩上漫步是中国南方之旅的一大亮点，但目前，在日益光滑的咖啡馆场景中闲逛的仍然是大多数当地人。如果周末人群太多，请深呼吸;如果你寻求庇护所，就会找到它。

History
历史

The foreign community was well established on Gulang Yu by the 1880s, with a daily English newspaper, churches, hospitals, post and telegraph offices, libraries, hotels and consulates. In 1903 the island was officially designated an International Foreign Settlement, and a municipal council with a police force of Sikhs was established to govern it. Today memories of the settlement linger in the many charming colonial-era buildings and the sound of classical piano wafting from speakers (the island is nicknamed ‘piano island’ by the Chinese). Many of China’s most celebrated musicians have come from Gulang Yu, including the pianists Yu Feixing, Lin Junqing and Yin Chengzong.
到 1880 年代，古浪屿上的外国人社区已经建立起来，有英文日报、教堂、医院、邮电局、图书馆、酒店和领事馆。1903 年，该岛被正式指定为国际外国定居点，并成立了一个由锡克教徒组成的警察部队的市议会来管理它。如今，许多迷人的殖民时代建筑和扬声器中飘荡的古典钢琴声中，人们对定居点的记忆挥之不去（该岛被中国人昵称为“钢琴岛”）。中国许多最著名的音乐家都来自鼓浪羽，包括钢琴家于飞星、林俊卿和尹承宗。

WHAT IS A TǔLóU?
什么是 TǔLóU？

Tǔlóu (literally mud houses) are outlandish, multistorey, fortified mud structures built between the 15th and 20th centuries by the inhabitants of southwest Fujian to protect themselves from bandits and wild animals.
Tǔlóu（字面意思是泥屋）是福建西南部居民于 15 世纪至 20 世纪间建造的奇特的多层坚固泥结构，旨在保护自己免受土匪和野生动物的侵害。

Tǔlóu were built along either a circular or square floor plan. The walls are made of rammed earth and glutinous rice, reinforced with strips of bamboo and wood chips. These structures are large enough to house entire clans, and they did, and still do! They are a grand exercise in communal living. The interior sections are enclosed by enormous peripheral structures that could accommodate hundreds of people. Nestled in the mud walls were bedrooms, wells, cooking areas and storehouses, circling a central courtyard. The later tǔlóu had stone firewalls and metal-covered doors to protect against blazes.
Tǔlóu 是按照圆形或方形平面图建造的。墙壁由夯土和糯米制成，并用竹条和木屑加固。这些建筑足够大，可以容纳整个氏族，他们确实如此，现在仍然如此！它们是公共生活中的一项盛大练习。内部部分被可容纳数百人的巨大外围结构包围。泥墙上坐落着卧室、水井、烹饪区和仓库，环绕着中央庭院。后来的 tǔlóu 有石制防火墙和金属覆盖的门来防止大火。

The compartmentalised nature of the building meant that these structures were the ancient equivalent of modern apartments. A typical layout would be the kitchens on the ground floor, storage on the next level and accommodation on the floors above this. Some tǔlóu have multiple buildings built in concentric rings within the main enclosure. These could be guest rooms and home schools. The centre is often an ancestral hall or a meeting hall used for events such as birthdays and weddings. For defence purposes, there is usually only one entrance for the entire tǔlóu and there are no windows on the first three storeys.
该建筑的分隔性质意味着这些结构相当于现代公寓的古代等价物。典型的布局是一楼的厨房，下一层的储藏室，上面的楼层有住宿。一些 tǔlóu 在主围墙内以同心环形式建造了多座建筑物。这些可以是客房和家庭学校。该中心通常是用于举办生日和婚礼等活动的祠堂或会议厅。出于防御目的，整个 tǔlóu 通常只有一个入口，前三层没有窗户。

It was once believed that these earthen citadels were inhabited solely by the Hakka. They are the people who migrated from northwest China during the Jin dynasty (AD 265–314) to the south to escape persecution and famine, and they eventually settled in Jiangxi, Fujian and Guangdong. While most tǔlóu in the vicinity of Yongding County are inhabited by the Hakka, there are far more tǔlóu in other counties like Nanjing and Hua’an populated by the indigenous Mǐnnān (Fujianese) people. A key distinguishing feature between the Hakka and Mǐnnān tǔlóu is that the former has communal corridors and staircases as well as a central courtyard, while the latter tǔlóu puts more emphasis on privacy, eg each unit has its own staircases and patio.
曾经有人认为，这些土堡只有客家人居住。他们是金朝（公元 265-314 年）为躲避迫害和饥荒而从中国西北向南方迁移的民族，最终定居在江西、福建和广东。虽然永定县附近的大多数土楼都居住着客家，但南京和华安等其他县的土楼人口要多得多，那里居住着土著闽南人。客家和 Mǐnnān tǔlóu 之间的一个主要区别在于，前者有公共走廊和楼梯以及中央庭院，而后者 tǔlóu 更强调隐私，例如每个单元都有自己的楼梯和露台。

No matter what type or shape of tǔlóu you’re looking at, many of them are still inhabited by a single clan, and residents depend on a combination of tourism and farming for a living. The tǔlóu are surprisingly comfortable to live in, being ‘dōng nuǎn, xià liáng’ (冬暖夏凉), or ‘warm in winter and cool in summer’. These structures were built to last.
无论您看到什么类型或形状的 tǔlóu，其中许多仍然由一个氏族居住，居民依靠旅游业和农业相结合为生。tǔlóu 居住起来出奇的舒适，是“ 冬暖夏凉 ”， 即“冬暖夏凉”。这些结构经久耐用。

Xunpu Village 蟳埔村
嵯浦村 蟳埔村

The fishing village of Xunpu (Xúnbù Cūn; sometimes Xunbu), some 10km southeast of the city centre of Quanzhou, was on the old trade route of the maritime Silk Road and was perhaps the Arabs’ first port of call when they set foot in Quanzhou during the Song dynasty. The village, now under encroaching urbanisation, is still fascinating and you’ll find some old houses built with oyster shells behind the main road in the village and older women still wearing flamboyant traditional head ornaments.
荀布渔村位于泉州市中心东南约10公里处，位于海上丝绸之路的旧贸易路线上，也许是宋代阿拉伯人踏上泉州时的第一个停靠港。这个村庄现在正处于城市化的侵蚀之下，仍然令人着迷，你会发现村里主干道后面有一些用牡蛎壳建造的老房子，还有年长的妇女仍然戴着华丽的传统头饰。

The Mazu Temple (妈祖庙, Māzǔ Miào) on the knoll in the village is the local centre of worship. It’s dedicated to the goddess of seafarers and turns very lively on the 29th day of the first lunar calendar month, the birthday of the protector. All the women in the village turn out in traditional costumes to join in the annual Mazu procession.
村里小山丘上的妈祖庙 （ 妈祖庙 ，Māzǔ Miào）是当地的礼拜中心。它供奉着海员女神，在农历正月二十九日，即护法的生日，变得非常热闹。村里所有的妇女都穿着传统服装参加一年一度的妈祖游行。

Cao’an Manichaean Temple 草庵摩尼教寺
草庵摩尼教寺

This quirky temple (Cǎo’ān Móníjiào Sì; Huábiǎo Hill, 华表山; ¥20; h8am-6pm) is dedicated to Manichaeism, a religion originating in Persia in the 3rd century, combining elements of Zoroastrian, Christian and Gnostic thought, which reached China in the 7th century.
这座古怪的寺庙 （Cǎo'ān Móníjiào Sì;华表山，华表山 ; ¥20; h 上午 8 点至下午 6 点） 致力于摩尼教，这是一种起源于 3 世纪波斯的宗教，结合了琐罗亚斯德教、基督教和诺斯底主义思想的元素，于 7 世纪传入中国。

The well-restored stone complex you see today is a rebuild dating from the Yuan dynasty (14th century). The most remarkable relic in the temple is the ‘Buddha of Light’, a sitting stone statue in the main hall, which is actually the prophet Mani, founder of Manichaeism, in a Buddhist disguise.
您今天看到的修复良好的石头建筑群是元代（14 世纪）的重建建筑。寺庙中最引人注目的舍利是“光明佛”，这是正殿中的一尊坐石像，它实际上是摩尼教创始人先知摩尼，伪装成佛教。

Manichaeism was considered an illegal religion during the Song period and the religion had to operate in the guise of an esoteric Buddhist group. Take a closer look at the statue, and you’ll find its hairstyle (straight instead of curly), hand gestures and colour combinations are distinctly different from most representations of the Buddha.
摩尼教在宋代被认为是非法宗教，该宗教必须以深奥的佛教团体为幌子运作。仔细观察这尊雕像，你会发现它的发型（直的而不是卷曲的）、手势和颜色组合与大多数佛陀形象明显不同。

The temple is 19km south of Quanzhou. From the long-distance bus station in Quanzhou, board a bus to Anhai (安海, Ānhǎi; ¥12) and tell the driver to drop you off at Cǎo’ān Lùkǒu (草庵路口). 寺庙位于泉州以南 19 公里处。从泉州的长途汽车站出发，乘坐前往安海（安海 ，Ānhǎi;12 日元）的巴士，然后告诉司机在草庵路口下车。然后寻找写着 Grass Temple 的英文标牌，步行 2 公里上坡。道路标记不清，因此建议乘坐出租车;从泉州乘坐出租车大约需要 65 日元。

Wuyi Shan 武夷山

%0599 / Pop 230,000
% 0599 / 人口 230,000

Despite a long association with domestic travellers, Wuyi Shan (Wǔyí Shān) is a mountain retreat that retains a sense of untouched natural splendour. Set high up in the northwest of Fujian, its hiking trails through protected forests and the famed bamboo rafting trip are well worth the effort to come here. Try to visit midweek or in low season (November, March and April) and you might have the area to yourself. Avoid visiting during heavy rain (especially during summer months), even if the hotels and tour organisers advise otherwise.
尽管与国内游客有着悠久的联系，但武夷山（Wǔyí Shān）是一个山间度假胜地，保留了未受破坏的自然风光。它位于福建西北部的高处，穿过受保护森林的远足小径和著名的竹筏漂流之旅非常值得来这里。尝试在周中或淡季（11 月、3 月和 4 月）参观，您可能会独享该地区。避免在大雨期间（尤其是在夏季）参观，即使酒店和旅游组织者另有建议。

The Wuyi Shan Scenic Area lies on the west bank of Chongyang Stream (Chóngyáng Xī). North of here, the busy resort area (武夷山旅游度假区, Wǔyíshān lǚyóu dùjià qū) is on the pleasant east bank and has the most facilities for visitors; from here it’s a short bus ride south to the main, southern entry to the Wuyi Shan Scenic Area. There are two train stations, but the east train station is the more used and is south of the scenic area. In the north is the north train station and airport.
武夷山风景区位于崇阳溪（Chóngyáng Xī）西岸。在这里以北，繁忙的度假区（ 武夷山旅游度假区 ，Wǔyíshān lǚyóu dùjià qū）位于宜人的东岸，为游客提供最多的设施;从这里向南乘坐短途巴士即可到达武夷山风景区的主要南入口。有两个火车站，但东站使用较多，位于景区以南。北面是北火车站和机场。

It’s a moderate two-hour walk to Great King Peak among bamboo groves and steep-cut rock walls. The trail can be slippery and wet, so bring suitable shoes.
步行两小时即可到达大王峰，周围环绕着竹林和陡峭的岩壁。小径可能湿滑，因此请带上合适的鞋子。

The walk to Heavenly Tour Peak is more scenic, with better views of the river and mountain peaks. But the path is also the most popular with tour groups. In winter and autumn, water plunges over the top of the cliff, creating a curtain of spray.
