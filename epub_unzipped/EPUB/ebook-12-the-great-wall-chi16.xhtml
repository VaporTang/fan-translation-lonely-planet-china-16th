Why Go?
为什么要去？

Coiling its way through 23 degrees of longitude, the Great Wall (长城, Chángchéng) stands as an awe-inspiring monument to the grandeur of China’s ancient history. With sections dating back 2000 years, the wall (or, more accurately, walls, because they belong to several different eras) wriggle haphazardly from their scattered Manchurian remains in Liaoning province to wind-scoured rubble in the Gobi desert and faint traces in the unforgiving sands of Xinjiang. Interspersed with natural defences (such as precipitous mountains), the Great Wall can be visited in 15 Chinese provinces, principalities and autonomous regions, but nowhere is better than Beijing for mounting your assault on this most iconic of bastions.
长城（长城 ，Chángchéng）蜿蜒穿过 23 度的经度，是一座令人惊叹的纪念碑，展示了中国古代历史的辉煌。这堵墙的部分可以追溯到 2000 年前，从辽宁省散落的满洲遗迹到戈壁沙漠中被风冲刷过的瓦砾，再到新疆无情的沙子中的微弱痕迹，这堵墙（或者更准确地说，是墙壁，因为它们属于几个不同的时代）随意蠕动。长城穿插着自然防御（如陡峭的山脉），中国 15 个省、公国和自治区都可以参观，但没有比北京更适合对这个最具标志性的堡垒发起攻击的地方了。

History
历史

Official Chinese history likes to stress the unity of the Great Wall through the ages. In fact, there have been several disparate eras of Great Wall building throughout China’s history. Work on the ‘original’ was begun during the Qin dynasty (221–207 BC), when China was unified for the first time under Emperor Qin Shi Huang. Hundreds of thousands of workers, many of them political prisoners, laboured for years to construct it.
中国官方历史喜欢强调长城历代的统一性。事实上，纵观中国历史，长城建设有几个不同的时代。“原件”的工作始于秦朝（公元前 221-207 年），当时中国在秦始皇的领导下首次统一。数十万工人，其中许多是政治犯，多年来辛勤工作才建造它。

After the fall of the Qin dynasty, work on the Wall continued during the Han dynasty (206 BC–AD 220). Little more was done until almost 1000 years later, during the Jin dynasty (1115–1234), when the impending threat of Genghis Khan spurred further construction.
秦朝灭亡后，长城工程在汉朝（公元前 206 年至公元 220 年）继续进行。直到近 1000 年后的金朝（1115-1234 年）期间，成吉思汗迫在眉睫的威胁刺激了进一步的建设，才做了更多的事情。

The Wall’s final and greatest incarnation, and the one most visitors see today, came during the Ming dynasty (1368–1644). The Ming built or rebuilt atop older stretches of wall to form an undulating, overlapping defensive barrier stretching from ocean to desert, shielding the northern reaches of their vast territory from the ‘barbarians’– the Mongols, Manchu and other pastoral people. The absolute length, including every branch, tributary and intervening natural feature, of the Ming Great Wall has been put at about 8850km.
长城最后也是最伟大的化身，也是今天大多数游客看到的化身，发生在明朝（1368-1644 年）。明朝在古老的城墙上建造或重建，形成了从海洋延伸到沙漠的起伏、重叠的防御屏障，保护其广阔领土的北部免受“蛮夷”——蒙古人、满清人和其他牧民的侵害。明长城的绝对长度，包括每条支流、支流和中间的自然特征，估计约为8850公里。

As a system of border defence, the Wall was garrisoned with troops (almost a million during the Ming Dynasty, or about 110 per kilometre) who resided in the watchtowers and could move rapidly between them to counter an invasion. As well as being a kind of elevated highway for transporting troops, horses and equipment, its beacon-tower system, using smoke signals generated by burning wolves’ dung, quickly transmitted news of enemy movements back to the capital.
作为一个边境防御系统，隔离墙驻扎着驻扎在瞭望塔的军队（明朝时期近百万，或每公里约 110 人），他们可以在瞭望塔之间快速移动以对抗入侵。它的烽火塔系统不仅是一种运输部队、马匹和装备的高架公路，还利用燃烧狼粪产生的烟雾信号，将敌人动向的消息迅速传回首都。

After the ascension of the Qing dynasty in 1644, there was less need for the new Manchu rulers to maintain a border defence that looked in part upon their own territories to the north. Although the Qing had some use for the Ming walls, and added a few of their own, the greatest era of wall building in the history of humankind had come to an end, and the battlements were eventually abandoned, their unprotected bricks and stone at the mercy of scavengers.
1644 年清朝即位后，新的满清统治者不再需要维持部分面向北方本国领土的边境防御。尽管清朝对明朝的城墙有一些用处，并增加了一些自己的城墙，但人类历史上最伟大的城墙建造时代已经结束，城垛最终被废弃，它们没有保护的砖石任由拾荒者摆布。

Ruin & Restoration
废墟与修复

The Wall’s decline accelerated during the war with Japan and then the civil war that preceded the founding of the new China in 1949. Compounding the problem was the fact that the communists didn’t initially have much interest in the Wall. In fact, Mao Zedong encouraged people living near it to use it as a source of free building materials. It wasn’t until 1984 that Mao’s successor Deng Xiaoping ordered that the Wall be placed under government protection.
在与日本的战争以及 1949 年新中国成立之前的内战期间，隔离墙的衰落加速。使问题更加复杂的是，共产党人最初对隔离墙没有太大兴趣。事实上，毛泽东鼓励住在它附近的人们将其用作免费建筑材料的来源。直到1984年，毛的继任者邓小平才下令将隔离墙置于政府保护之下。

Classic postcard images of the Wall – clad flawlessly in bricks and coiling over distant peaks – do not accurately reflect the truth of the bastion today. The reality is that vast tracts of wall have been lost to the ravages of time and civilisation, and what remains exists in a state of precarious disrepair. For logistical reasons, the Great Wall was fashioned, chameleon-like, from whatever materials were to hand. Along China’s arid northern plains this equated mostly to tamped earth. The strategically critical upland sections around Beijing, amounting to around 600km of the Ming Great Wall, were far sturdier, built from local granite and bricks, lined with watchtowers and often following the high ridgelines of mountains.
长城的经典明信片图像——完美无瑕地覆盖着砖块，盘绕在远处的山峰上——并不能准确反映当今堡垒的真相。现实情况是，大片的城墙已经因时间和文明的破坏而消失，剩下的城墙年久失修。出于后勤原因，长城是用手头的任何材料建造的，就像变色龙一样。在中国干旱的北部平原上，这主要相当于夯实的泥土。北京周围具有战略意义的高地部分，相当于明朝长城的约 600 公里，要坚固得多，由当地的花岗岩和砖块建造而成，两旁是瞭望塔，通常沿着高高的山脊线。

Restoration, beginning in the 1950s with Badaling, has tended to prioritise the wholesale rebuilding of short sections in order that they can be opened as tourist attractions. Basic repairs at various ‘wild’ tourist hotspots such as Jiankou have been ongoing for years, and typically involve stabilising the loose rubble with cement. At time of research, a new and welcome school of thought with regards to restoration was just starting to emerge – one that prioritises preserving and reinforcing the Wall in its present and picturesque state of ruin rather than attempting a less-than-authentic rebuild.
从 1950 年代的八达岭开始，修复工作倾向于优先考虑对短路段进行大规模重建，以便它们可以作为旅游景点开放。尖口等各种“野生”旅游热点的基本修复工作已经持续多年，通常涉及用水泥稳定松散的瓦砾。在研究时，一种关于修复的新的、受欢迎的思想流派刚刚开始出现——一种优先考虑保护和加固目前风景如画的废墟状态的长城，而不是尝试进行不太真实的重建。

Those bitten by the Great Wall bug then graduate to more ‘wild’ hikes along ruined battlements at the likes of Jiankou, Gubeikou and Huanghua Cheng, potentially staying overnight for sunrise hikes. While most of these places can be visited by public transport, hiring a car and driver speeds things up considerably.

4Sleeping & Eating
4 睡眠和饮食

Although the vast majority of visitors do the Great Wall as a day trip, you’ll find places to stay at pretty much every visitable part of Great Wall within range of Beijing, touristy or unrestored. Staying overnight allows you to see both sunrise and sunset from the battlements and increases point-to-point hiking options considerably. Remember that many places close from November to March.
尽管绝大多数游客将长城作为一日游，但您几乎可以在北京范围内的长城每一个可参观的地方找到住宿地点，无论是旅游游客还是未修复的地方。过夜可以让您从城垛上看到日出和日落，并大大增加了点对点的徒步旅行选择。请记住，许多地方从 11 月到 3 月关闭。

You can find official campsites close to the Great Wall at several sections, including Jinshanling and Huanghua Cheng ‘Lakeside’ Great Wall. Camping on the Wall itself is, strictly speaking, forbidden, but people do it. There are plenty of places to buy camping equipment in Beijing, including several discount Decathlon superstores.
您可以在几个路段找到靠近长城的官方露营地，包括金山岭和黄花城“湖畔”长城“。严格来说，在隔离墙上露营本身是被禁止的，但人们会这样做。北京有很多地方可以买到露营装备，包括几家打折的迪卡侬超市。

The best advice for any Great Wall excursion is to take your own food and water with you. Ticketed Great Wall sections offer a basic spread of Chinese and western fast-food outlets, while at unrestored spots such as Jiankou you can often find farmer-style courtyards (农家院, nóngjiāyuàn) to eat at.
对于任何长城游览，最好的建议是随身携带自己的食物和水。售票的长城区提供基本的中西快餐店，而在剑口等未修复的地点，您经常可以找到农家院（农家院 ，nóngjiāyuàn）吃饭。

For the Great Wall newbie, Mutianyu (Mùtiányù) is the perfect introduction. Famed for its tightly woven chain of watchtowers and excellent views, it’s easy to reach from the city, less crowded than Badaling, and is well set up for families, with a cable car, a chairlift and a toboggan ride. That said, with its gargantuan ticket hall, restaurant strip and irksome shuttle-bus system, Mutianyu is far from a ‘wild’ hike, and will only serve to whet the appetite of many to seek out more remote and rambling Great Wall adventures.
对于长城新手来说，慕田鱼（Mùtiányù）是一个完美的入门。它以其紧密编织的瞭望塔链和绝佳的景色而闻名，从城市很容易到达，比八达岭人少，并且为家庭提供了良好的环境，有缆车、缆车和雪橇。也就是说，慕田峪拥有巨大的售票大厅、餐厅街和令人讨厌的穿梭巴士系统，远非“狂野”的徒步旅行，只会激发许多人的胃口，去寻找更偏远、更漫无边际的长城探险。

From the monolithic ticket office at Mutianyu, shuttle buses (¥15 return) ply the 3km up to the start of the cable car (缆车; Lǎn Chē; one way/return ¥100/120, kids ¥50/60), where there are also three or four stepped pathways leading up to the wall itself, plus a chairlift (索道, Suǒdào; 1-way ¥100, with toboggan ¥120), called a ‘ropeway’ on the signs here, and a toboggan ride (滑道, Huá Dào; 1-way ¥100, with chairlift ¥120), making Mutianyu ideal for those who can’t manage too many steps, or have kids in tow.

The Chinese call the Great Wall the ‘10,000 Lǐ Wall’ (万里长城, Wànlǐ Chángchéng). With one ‘Lǐ’ equivalent to around 500m, this would make the Wall around 5000km long; in fact, the character 万 (wàn), meaning 10,000, was often used to broadly indicate ‘an awful lot’. The maths wasn’t too far out, though. A 2009 report by China’s State Administration for Cultural Heritage estimated the noncontinuous length of the Ming dynasty Wall at 8851km. But the Ming dynasty was just one (albeit the most significant) of 13 dynasties to have contributed to the Wall over the course of history. A 2012 Chinese government survey calculated the total length of all fragments of the Great Wall that have ever stood, including sections that run parallel with others, and sections that have weathered away to nothing, to be 21,196km.
中国人称长城为“万里长城”（万里长城 ）。如果一个“Lǐ”相当于大约 500 米，这将使长城长约 5000 公里;事实上， 万 （wàn）这个字，意思是 10,000，经常被用来广义地表示“非常多”。不过，数学并没有太远。中国国家文物局 2009 年的一份报告估计，明代长城的非连续长度为 8851 公里。但明朝只是历史上对长城做出贡献的 13 个王朝中的一个（尽管是最重要的）。2012 年中国政府的一项调查计算出，长城所有碎片的总长度，包括与其他部分平行的部分，以及风化到一无所有的部分，为 21,196 公里。

The Coiled Dragon Loop

A 2½ hours
A 21/2 小时

This scenic but manageable hike starts and finishes in the town of Gubeikou and follows a curling stretch of the Wall known as the Coiled Dragon. From the Folk Customs Village (the southern half of Gubeikou), walk up to the newly reconstructed Gubeikou Gate (古北口关, Gubeikou Guǎn), but turn right up a dirt track just before the gateway. You should start seeing yellow-painted blobs left over from an old marathon that was run here: follow them. The first section of Wall you reach is a very rare stony stretch of Northern Qi Dynasty Wall (1500 years old). It soon joins up with the Ming dynasty bricked version, which you should continue to walk along (although at one stage, you need to follow yellow arrows down off the Wall to the left, before rejoining it later). Around 90 minutes after you set off, you should reach a big sweeping right-hand bend in the Wall (the coil), with three towers on top. The first and third of these towers are quite well preserved, with walls, windows and part of a roof (great for camping in). At the third tower (called Jiangjun Tower), turn left, skirting right around it, then walk down the steps before turning right at a point marked with a yellow ‘X’ (the marathon went straight on here). Follow this pathway all the way back to Gubeikou (30 minutes), turning right when you reach the road.
这条风景优美但易于管理的徒步旅行的起点和终点都是古北口镇，沿着被称为盘绕龙的长城的卷曲延伸。从民俗村（古北口南半部）出发，步行到新重建的古北口关（古北口关，古北口关），但在大门前右转沿一条土路上行。你应该开始看到在这里跑的一场旧马拉松比赛留下的黄色斑点：跟着它们走。您到达的第一段长城是北齐长城 （1500 年历史）的一段非常罕见的石质路段。它很快就会与明代的砖块版本汇合，你应该继续沿着它走（尽管在某个阶段，你需要沿着黄色箭头从墙左边向下走，然后再重新加入它）。出发后大约 90 分钟，您应该到达墙壁（线圈）的一个大右弯，顶部有三座塔。这些塔楼中的第一个和第三个保存得相当完好，有墙壁、窗户和部分屋顶（非常适合露营）。在第三座塔（称为将军塔 ）左转，绕过它右转，然后走下台阶，然后在标有黄色“X”的点右转（马拉松比赛从这里直行）。沿着这条小路一直回到古北口（30 分钟），到达公路后右转。

Gubeikou to Jinshanling

A 6½ hours
A 61/2 小时

This day-long adventure takes in unrestored and restored Great Wall, as well as a 90-minute detour through the countryside. Bring plenty of food and water. Follow the first part of our Coiled Dragon Loop hike, but instead of leaving the Wall just after Jiangjun Tower, continue along the Wall for another hour until you reach the impressive 24-Window Tower (there are only 15 windows left these days). Here, follow the yellow arrows off the Wall, to avoid a military zone up ahead, and walk down through the fields. Jinshanling 金山岭

Location 142km from Beijing
位置距北京 142 公里

Price summer/winter ¥65/55
价格夏季 /冬季 ¥65/55

Hours 8am to 5pm
营业时间： 上午 8 点至下午 5 点

Mighty and imposing, the Jinshanling (Jīnshānlǐng) section of Great Wall has been thoroughly restored, but it’s distant enough from Beijing that it sees far fewer tourists than the likes of Mutianyu. The landscape here can be drier and starker than at, say, Jiankou, but it’s arguably more powerful, and it leaves you in no doubt that this is remote territory. Jinshanling is the finish (or the start) of an adventurous 6½-hour hike from Gubeikou.
长城金山岭（金山岭）部分雄伟而雄伟，已经被彻底修复，但它离北京足够远，游客远少于慕田峪等地。这里的风景可能比剑口更干燥、更荒凉，但可以说它更有力量，它让你毫无疑问，这是一片偏远的领土。金山岭是从古北口出发的 6 小时冒险徒步旅行的终点（或起点）。

Jinshanling has some unusual features such as barrier walls (walls within the Wall), a particularly high density of watchtowers, thousands of bricks inscribed with maker’s marks and the year 1578, and standout towers such as the Storehouse Tower (库房楼, Kùfáng Lóu) and the Big Jinshan Tower (大金山楼, Dà Jīnshān Lóu) with its upper storey and brick stairway.
金山岭有一些不寻常的特征，如障壁墙（墙内墙）、特别高密度的瞭望塔、数千块刻有制造商标记和 1578 年的砖块，以及突出的塔楼，如库房楼 （库房楼，Kùfáng Lóu） 和大金山楼 （大金山楼 ，Dà Jīnshān Lóu），其上层和砖楼梯。

In the Small Pot Tower (小虎楼, Xiǎohǔ Lóu), there is an elaborately carved Screen Wall depicting a mythical qilin (a unicorn-type beast).
在小虎楼 （Xiǎohǔ Lóu）中，有一面精心雕刻的屏风墙，描绘了神话中的麒麟 （一种麒麟型野兽）。

Info boards in English at various points cover history and architecture. You can hike (in either direction) on the restored section of the Wall here and do as little or as much as you like, up to a total of about 6km. A cable car (缆车, Lǎn Chē; one way/return ¥40/80; h8am-5pm Apr-Oct, to 4.30pm Nov-Mar) by the ticket office saves a trudge up to the battlements.
不同地方的英文信息板涵盖历史和建筑。您可以在这里修复后的长城部分徒步旅行（任一方向），随心所欲地进行，总共约 6 公里。缆车 （ 缆车 ，Lǎn Chē;单程/往返¥40/80; h 4 月至 10 月上午 8 点至下午 5 点，11 月至 3 月下午 4 点 30 分） 在售票处停留，省去了通往城垛的跋涉。

If you want to find some unrestored sections, keep marching west along the Wall, and you’ll eventually get to Gubeikou (6½ hours), although you have to leave the Wall for an hour or so in order to circumvent the boundary of a military zone.
如果你想找到一些未修复的路段，继续沿着长城向西行进，你最终会到达古北口（6 小时半），尽管你必须离开长城一个小时左右才能绕过军事区的边界。

Simatai 司马台

Location 143km from Beijing
位置距北京 143 公里

Price adult ¥40

Hours 8am to 6pm April to October, to 5.30pm November to March
开放时间 4 月至 10 月上午 8 点至下午 6 点，11 月至 3 月至下午 5：30

Built during the reign of Ming emperor Hongwu, Simatai (Sīmǎtái) is the great mountain climber, famed for the precarious steepness of its battlements as they soar up the ‘heavenly ladder’ of the Yan Mountains. Heavily restored and reopened in 2014, its once rambling charm has been diluted by cash-ins like the faux-historic Gubei Water Town, although an admittedly nice feature is how part of the Wall is illuminated (and can be walked on) after dark.
司马台 （Sīmǎtái） 建于明朝洪武皇帝统治时期，是伟大的登山者，以其城垛的不稳定陡峭而闻名，它们在炎山的“天梯”上翱翔。它于 2014 年进行了大规模修复并重新开放，它曾经漫无边际的魅力已被历史悠久的人造古北水乡等现金收入所冲淡，尽管一个不可否认的不错功能是天黑后长城的一部分被照亮（并且可以在上面行走）。

There are some unusual features at Simatai, such as ‘obstacle walls’ or ‘barrier walls’ – walls within walls used for defending against enemies who had already scaled the battlements. Small cannons have been discovered in this area, as well as evidence of rocket-type weapons.
司马台有一些不寻常的特征，例如“障碍墙”或“屏障墙”——墙中的墙，用于防御已经爬上城垛的敌人。在该地区发现了小型大炮，以及火箭类武器的证据。
