China’s Landscapes
中国的风景

The world’s third-largest country, China swallows up an immense 9.5 million sq km, only surpassed in area by Russia and Canada. So whatever floats your boat – verdant bamboo forests, sapphire Himalayan lakes, towering sand dunes, sublime mountain gorges, huge glaciers or sandy beaches – China’s landscapes offer a simply jaw-dropping diversity.
作为世界第三大国，中国吞噬了 950 万平方公里的巨大面积，面积仅次于俄罗斯和加拿大。因此，无论什么能让你的船漂浮起来——青翠的竹林、蓝宝石般的喜马拉雅湖泊、高耸的沙丘、壮丽的山峡、巨大的冰川或沙滩——中国的风景都提供了令人瞠目结舌的多样性。

The Land
土地

Straddling natural environments as diverse as subarctic tundra in the north and tropical rainforests in the south, this massive land embraces the world’s highest mountain range and one of its hottest and most forbidding deserts, to the steamy, typhoon-lashed coastline of the South China Sea. Fragmenting this epic landscape is a colossal web of waterways, including one of the world’s mightiest rivers – the Yangzi (长江; Cháng Jiāng).
这片广阔的土地横跨北方的亚北极苔原和南部的热带雨林等多样化的自然环境，拥有世界上最高的山脉和最热、最令人生畏的沙漠之一，以及炎热、台风肆虐的南中国海海岸线。将这片史诗般的景观支离破碎的是巨大的水道网络，其中包括世界上最强大的河流之一——扬子河（ 长江 ;Cháng Jiāng）。

Mountains
山

China has a largely mountainous and hilly topography, commencing in precipitous fashion in the vast and sparsely populated Qinghai–Tibetan plateau in the west and levelling out gradually towards the fertile, well-watered, populous and wealthy provinces of eastern China.
中国地形多山丘陵，西部地势陡峭，从广袤人烟稀少的青藏高原开始，逐渐趋向中国东部肥沃、水源充足、人口稠密、富裕的省份。

This mountainous disposition sculpts so many of China’s scenic highlights, from the glittering Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terraces of Guangxi to the incomparable stature of Mt Everest, the stunning beauty of Jiuzhaigou National Park in Sichuan, the ethereal peaks of misty Huangshan in Anhui, the vertiginous inclines of Hua Shan in Shaanxi, the sublime karst geology of Yangshuo in Guangxi and the volcanic drama of Heaven Lake in Jilin.
从广西璀璨的龙脊梯田到珠穆朗玛峰的无与伦比的巍峨，四川九寨沟国家公园的壮丽美景，安徽云雾缭绕的黄山空灵山峰，陕西华山令人眼花缭乱的斜坡，广西阳朔壮丽的喀斯特地质，吉林天堂湖的火山戏剧。

Averaging 4500m above sea level, the Qinghai–Tibetan region’s highest peaks thrust up into the Himalayan mountain range along its southern rim. The Himalayas, on average about 6000m above sea level, include 40 peaks rising dizzyingly to 7000m or more. Also known as the planet’s ‘third pole’, this is where the world’s highest peak, Mt Everest – ‘Zhūmùlǎngmǎ fēng’ being the Chinese form of its Tibetan name – thrusts up jaggedly from the Tibet–Nepal border.
青藏地区平均海拔 4500 米，山峰沿着南缘直插喜马拉雅山脉。喜马拉雅山平均海拔约 6000 米，包括 40 座山峰，令人眼花缭乱地上升到 7000 米或更高。珠穆朗玛峰也被称为地球的“第三极”，是世界最高峰珠穆朗玛峰——“Zhūmùlǎngmǎ fēng”是其藏文名称的中文形式——从西藏和尼泊尔边境锯齿状地耸立起来的地方。

This vast high-altitude region (Tibet alone constitutes one-eighth of China’s landmass) is home to an astonishing 37,000 glaciers – 14.5% of the world’s total glacier mass – and the third-largest body of ice on the planet after the Arctic and Antarctic. This enormous body of frozen water ensures that the Qinghai–Tibetan region is the source of many of China’s largest rivers, including the Yellow (Huáng Hé), Mekong (Láncāng Jiāng) and Salween (Nù Jiāng) Rivers and, of course, the mighty Yangzi. Global warming, however, is inevitably eating into this glacial volume and though experts argue over how quickly it is melting, Xinhua reported that some 7600 sq km (18% of the total glacier mass in Tibet) of ice has vanished since the 1950s.
这个广阔的高海拔地区（仅西藏就占中国陆地面积的八分之一）拥有惊人的 37,000 条冰川，占世界冰川总质量的 14.5%，是地球上仅次于北极和南极的第三大冰体。这片巨大的冰冻水体确保了青藏地区成为中国许多最大河流的源头，包括黄河（幻河）、湄公河（兰坎江）和萨尔温河（努江），当然还有浩瀚的扬子河。然而，全球变暖不可避免地正在侵蚀冰川体积，尽管专家们对冰川融化的速度争论不休，但新华社报道称，自 1950 年代以来，约 7600 平方公里（占西藏冰川总量的 18%）的冰已经消失。

This mountain geology further corrugates the rest of China, continuously rippling the land into spectacular mountain ranges. There’s the breathtaking 2500km-long Kunlun range, the mighty Karakoram mountains on the border with Pakistan, the Tian Shan range in Xinjiang, the Tanggula range on the Qinghai–Tibetan plateau, the Qinling mountains and the Greater Khingan range (Daxingan Ling) in the northeast.
这种山地地质进一步将中国其他地区扩大到波纹状，不断将这片土地荡漾成壮观的山脉。有令人叹为观止的 2500 公里长的昆仑山脉、与巴基斯坦接壤的雄伟喀喇昆仑山脉、新疆的天山山脉、青藏高原的唐古拉山脉、东北部的秦岭和大兴安岭（大兴安岭）。

China has earmarked a staggering US$140 billion for an ambitious programme of wind farms; ranging from Xinjiang province to Jiangsu province in the east, the huge wind farms were due for completion in 2020.
中国已拨款1400亿美元用于雄心勃勃的风电场计划;从新疆省到东部的江苏省，这些巨大的风电场预计将于 2020 年竣工。

Deserts
沙漠

China contains head-spinningly huge – and growing – desert regions that occupy almost one-fifth of the country’s landmass, largely in its mighty northwest. These are inhospitably sandy and rocky expanses where summers are staggeringly hot and winters bone-numbingly cold, but as destinations they offer sublime visuals.
中国拥有令人头晕目眩的巨大且不断增长的沙漠地区，几乎占据了该国陆地面积的五分之一，主要位于其强大的西北部。这些都是荒凉的沙质和岩石，夏天热得令人咋舌，冬天寒冷得令人毛骨悚然，但作为目的地，它们提供了崇高的视觉效果。

The Silk Road into China steered its epic caravan-laden course through this entire region, ferrying slow-moving fleets of camels heavily burdened with merchandise and bringing languages, philosophies, customs and peoples from the far-flung lands of the Middle East.
进入中国的丝绸之路引导着它史诗般的商队穿越整个地区，运送着缓慢行驶的骆驼队，满载着货物，带来了来自遥远中东土地的语言、哲学、习俗和人民。

North towards Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan from the plateaus of Tibet and Qinghai is Xinjiang’s Tarim Basin, the world’s largest inland basin. This is the location of the mercilessly thirsty Taklamakan Desert – China’s largest desert and the world’s second-largest mass of sand after the Sahara Desert.
从西藏和青海高原向北向哈萨克斯坦和吉尔吉斯斯坦，是新疆的塔里木盆地，这是世界上最大的内陆盆地。这里是无情的干渴塔克拉玛干沙漠的所在地——中国最大的沙漠，也是仅次于撒哈拉沙漠的世界第二大沙地。

The harsh environment shares many topographical features with the neighbouring nations of Afghanistan, Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan, and is almost the exact opposite of China’s lush and well-watered seaboard southern provinces. Indeed, the Continental Pole of Inaccessibility (the spot on land furthest from the ocean) is located in Xinjiang, at a point near the border with Kazakhstan.
恶劣的环境与邻国阿富汗、吉尔吉斯斯坦和哈萨克斯坦有许多共同的地形特征，与中国郁郁葱葱、水源充足的沿海省份几乎完全相反。事实上，无法到达的大陆极（离海洋最远的陆地）位于新疆，靠近哈萨克斯坦边境。

The Tarim Basin is bordered to the north by the lofty Tian Shan range – home to the glittering Tian Chi Lake – and to the west by the mighty Pamirs, which border Pakistan. Also in Xinjiang is China’s hotspot, the Turpan Basin. Known as the ‘Oasis of Fire’ and ‘China’s Death Valley’, it gets into the record books as China’s lowest-lying region and the world’s second-deepest depression after the Dead Sea in Israel.
塔里木盆地北部与高耸的天山山脉接壤——这里是波光粼粼的天池湖的所在地——西部与巴基斯坦接壤的雄伟帕米尔高原。同样在新疆的是中国的热点地区吐鲁番盆地。它被称为“火之绿洲”和“中国的死亡谷”，作为中国地势最低的地区和仅次于以色列死海的世界第二深洼地而载入记录。

China’s most famous desert is, of course, the ever-expanding Gobi, though most of it lies outside the country’s borders. In little-visited Western Inner Mongolia, the awesome Badain Jaran Desert offers travellers spectacular journeys among remote desert lakes and colossal, fixed sand dunes over 460m in height. Further west lie the famous grasslands and steppes of Inner Mongolia.
中国最著名的沙漠当然是不断扩大的戈壁，尽管其中大部分位于国界之外。在人迹罕至的内蒙古西部，令人敬畏的八丹吉兰沙漠为游客提供了偏远沙漠湖泊和高度超过 460 米的巨大固定沙丘之间的壮观旅程。再往西是内蒙古著名的草原和草原。

China’s Bayan Obo Mining District in Inner Mongolia produces around 45% of the world’s rare earth metals, elements essential for the production of mobile phones, high-definition TVs, computers, wind turbines and other products.
中国内蒙古巴彦鄂博矿区生产全球约45%的稀土金属，这些稀土金属是生产手机、高清电视、电脑、风力涡轮机和其他产品所必需的元素。

In 2010 six of China’s dānxiá (eroded reddish sandstone rock), karst-like geological formations, were included in Unesco’s World Heritage List. The list includes Chishui in Guizhou province. The rocks can also be seen outside Zhangye in Gansu.
2010 年，中国的六块岩溶状地质构造被列入联合国教科文组织世界遗产名录。该名单包括贵州省的赤水。甘肃张掖外也可以看到这些岩石。

Rivers & Plains
河流和平原

The Yangzi (the ‘Long River’) is one of the longest rivers in the world (and China’s longest). Its watershed of almost 2 million sq km – 20% of China’s landmass – supports 400 million people. Dropping from its source high on the Tibetan plateau, it runs for 6300km to the sea, with the last few hundred kilometres across virtually flat alluvial plains. In the course of its sweeping journey, the river (and its tributaries) fashions many of China’s scenic spectacles, including Tiger Leaping Gorge and the Three Gorges, and cuts through a string of huge and historic cities, including Chongqing, Wuhan and Nanjing, before surging into the East China Sea north of Shanghai. As a transport route, the river is limited (especially with the advent of high-speed rail), but the Three Gorges cruise is China’s most celebrated and impressive river journey.
扬子河（“长河”）是世界上最长的河流之一（也是中国最长的河流）。其流域面积近200万平方公里，占中国国土面积的20%，养活了4亿人口。它从青藏高原的高处落下，全长 6300 公里，最后几百公里穿过几乎平坦的冲积平原。在浩浩荡荡的旅程中，这条河（及其支流）塑造了中国的许多风景奇观，包括虎跳峡和三峡，并穿过重庆、武汉和南京等一系列巨大的历史名城，然后涌入上海以北的东海。作为一条运输路线，三峡游船是有限的（尤其是随着高铁的出现），但三峡游船是中国最著名和最令人印象深刻的河流之旅。

At about 5460km in length and the second-longest river in China, the Yellow River (黄河; Huáng Hé) is touted as the birthplace of Chinese civilisation and has been fundamental in the development of Chinese society. The mythical architect of China’s rivers, the Great Yu, apocryphally noted ‘Whoever controls the Yellow River controls China’. From its source in Qinghai, the river runs through North China, meandering past or near many famous towns, including Lanzhou, Yinchuan, Baotou, Hancheng, Jincheng, Luoyang, Zhengzhou, Kaifeng and Ji’nan in Shandong, before exiting China north of Dongying (though the watercourse often runs dry nowadays before it reaches the sea).
黄河全长约 5460 公里，是中国第二长的河流，黄河（黄河 ;Huáng Hé）被誉为中华文明的发源地，对中国社会的发展至关重要。中国河流的神话建筑师大禹杜撰地指出“谁控制了黄河，谁就控制了中国”。这条河从青海的源头流经华北，蜿蜒经过或靠近许多著名城镇，包括兰州、银川、包头、韩城、晋城、洛阳、郑州、开封和山东济南，然后从东营以北离开中国（尽管如今水道在到达大海之前经常干涸）。

Fields & Agriculture
田地与农业

China’s hills and mountains may surround travellers with a dramatic backdrop, but they are a massive agricultural headache for farmers. Small plots of land are eked out in patchworks of earth squashed between hillsides, or rescued from mountain cliffs and ravines, in the demanding effort to feed 20% of the world’s population with just 10% of its arable land.
中国的丘陵和山脉可能以戏剧性的背景包围着旅行者，但它们对农民来说却是一个巨大的农业难题。小块土地被压在山坡之间的拼凑而成的泥土中勉强开垦出来，或者从山崖和峡谷中抢救出来，艰巨的努力只用 10% 的耕地养活世界上 20% 的人口。

As only 15% of China’s land can be cultivated, hillside gradients and inclines are valiantly levelled off, wherever possible, into bands of productive terraced fields. Stunning examples of rice terraces – beautiful in the right light – can be admired at the Yuanyang Rice Terraces in Yunnan and the Dragon’s Backbone Rice Terraces in Guangxi.
由于中国只有15%的土地可以耕种，因此尽可能勇敢地将山坡和斜坡平整成一带多产的梯田。云南的鸳鸯梯田和广西的龙脊梯田可以欣赏到令人惊叹的梯田实例——在适当的光线下美丽。

One of the aims of the Three Gorges Dam is to help prevent flooding on the Yangzi River. The river has caused hundreds of catastrophic floods, including the disastrous inundation of 1931, in which an estimated 145,000 people died.
三峡大坝的目的之一是帮助防止扬子江泛滥。这条河引发了数百次灾难性洪水，包括 1931 年的灾难性洪水，估计有 145,000 人死亡。

Wildlife
野生生物

China’s vast size, diverse topography and climatic disparities support an astonishing range of habitats for animal life. The Tibetan plateau alone is the habitat of more than 500 species of birds, while half of the animal species in the northern hemisphere exist in China.
中国幅员辽阔、地形多样、气候差异，为动物生活提供了惊人的栖息地。仅青藏高原就是500多种鸟类的栖息地，而北半球一半的动物物种都存在于中国。

It is unlikely you will see many of these creatures in their natural habitat unless you are a specialist, or have a lot of time, patience, persistence, determination and luck. But there are plenty of pristine reserves within relatively easy reach of travellers’ destinations such as Chengdu and Xi’an, and even if you don’t get the chance to see animals, the scenery is terrific. Try Yading Nature Reserve in Sichuan, Mengda Nature Reserve in Qinghai, Sanchahe Nature Reserve in Yunnan, Fanjingshan in Guizhou, Shennongjia in Hubei, Wuzhishan in Hainan, Kanas Lake Nature Reserve in Xinjiang and Changbai Shan, China’s largest nature reserve, in Jilin.
除非您是专家，或者有很多时间、耐心、毅力、决心和运气，否则您不太可能在自然栖息地看到许多这些生物。但是，成都和习安等游客的目的地相对容易到达的地方有很多原始保护区，即使你没有机会看到动物，风景也很棒。四川亚丁自然保护区、青海蒙达自然保护区、云南三岔河自然保护区、贵州繁景山、湖北神农架、海南五指山、新疆喀纳斯湖自然保护区和吉林省中国最大的自然保护区长白山。

Mammals
哺乳动物

China’s towering mountain ranges form natural refuges for wildlife, many of which are now protected in parks and reserves that have escaped the depredations of loggers and dam builders. The barren high plains of the Tibetan plateau are home to many large animals, such as the chiru (Tibetan antelope), snow leopard, Tibetan wild ass, wild sheep and goats, and wolves. In theory many of these animals are protected, but in practice poaching and hunting still threaten their survival.
中国高耸的山脉是野生动物的天然避难所，其中许多野生动物现在受到公园和保护区的保护，这些公园和保护区已经逃脱了伐木者和水坝建设者的掠夺。青藏高原贫瘠的高原上栖息着许多大型动物，如赤如（藏羚羊）、雪豹、藏野驴、野绵羊和山羊、狼等。理论上，这些动物中有许多受到保护，但实际上偷猎和狩猎仍然威胁着它们的生存。

The beautiful and retiring snow leopard, which normally inhabits the highest parts of the most remote mountain ranges, sports a luxuriant coat of fur against the cold. It preys on mammals as large as mountain goats, but is unfortunately persecuted for allegedly killing livestock.
美丽而隐退的雪豹通常栖息在最偏远山脉的最高处，拥有一身茂盛的皮毛来抵御寒冷。它捕食像山羊一样大的哺乳动物，但不幸的是，它因涉嫌杀死牲畜而受到迫害。

The Himalayan foothills of western Sichuan support the greatest diversity of mammals in China. Aside from giant pandas, other mammals found in this region include the panda’s small cousin – the raccoon-like red panda – as well as Asiatic black bears and leopards. Among the grazers are golden takin, a large goat-like antelope with a yellowish coat, argali sheep and various deer species, including the diminutive mouse deer.
四川西部的喜马拉雅山麓是中国哺乳动物多样性最大的地区。除了大熊猫，该地区发现的其他哺乳动物包括大熊猫的小表亲——浣熊状的小熊猫——以及亚洲黑熊和豹子。食草动物包括金羚牛、一种长着淡黄色皮毛的类似山羊的大型羚羊、阿加利羊和各种鹿，包括体型矮小的鼷鹿。

The sparsely populated northeastern provinces abutting Siberia are inhabited by reindeer, moose, bears, sables and Manchurian tigers.
毗邻西伯利亚的东北部人烟稀少的省份居住着驯鹿、驼鹿、熊、黑貂和满洲虎。

The tropical rainforests of Yunnan province, particularly the area around Xishuangbanna, support Indo-Chinese tigers and herds of Asiatic elephants.
云南省的热带雨林，特别是西双版纳周边地区，为印度支那虎和成群的亚洲象提供了支持。

The wild mammals you are most likely to see are several species of monkeys. The large and precocious Père David’s macaque is common at Emei Shan in Sichuan. Macaques can also be seen on Hainan’s Monkey Island. Several other monkey species are rare and endangered, including the beautiful golden monkey of Fanjingshan and the snub-nosed monkey of the Yunnan rainforests. But by far the most endangered is the Hainan gibbon, numbering just a few dozen individuals on Hainan Island thanks to massive forest clearance.
您最有可能看到的野生哺乳动物是几种猴子。大而早熟的大卫神父猕猴在四川峨眉山很常见。在海南的猴岛上也可以看到猕猴。其他几种猴子物种是稀有和濒临灭绝的，包括梵井山美丽的金丝猴和云南雨林的短鼻猴。但迄今为止，最濒危的是海南长臂猿，由于大规模的森林砍伐，海南岛上的数量只有几十只。

The giant panda (xióngmāo – literally ‘bear cat’) is western Sichuan’s most famous denizen, but the animal’s solitary nature makes it elusive for observation in the wild, and even today, after decades of intensive research and total protection in dedicated reserves, sightings are rare. It’s a notoriously fickle breeder (the female is only on heat for a handful of days each spring); there are approximately 1864 pandas in the Chinese wilds, according to World Wildlife Fund. The easiest way to see pandas outside of zoos is at the Giant Panda Breeding Research Base just outside Chengdu, or at the Ya’an Bifengxia Panda Base, also in Sichuan.
大熊猫（xióngmāo – 字面意思是“熊猫”）是川西最著名的居民，但这种动物的独居性使其难以在野外观察，即使在今天，经过数十年的深入研究和专门保护区的全面保护，目击事件也很少见。它是出了名的善变的繁殖者（雌性每年春天只发情几天）;根据世界自然基金会的数据，中国荒野中大约有 1864 只大熊猫。在动物园外观赏大熊猫最简单的方法是在成都郊外的大熊猫繁育研究基地，或者同样位于四川的雅安碧峰峡大熊猫基地。

The dawn redwood (Metasequoia), a towering (growing up to 60m) and elegant fine-needled deciduous Chinese tree, dates to the Jurassic era. Once considered long extinct, a single example was discovered in 1941 in a Sichuan village, followed three years later by the discovery of further trees.
黎明红杉（ 水杉 ）是一种高耸（长达 60 米）优雅的细针落叶中国乔木，其历史可以追溯到侏罗纪时代。曾经被认为早已灭绝的例子，1941 年在四川的一个村庄发现了一棵树，三年后又发现了更多的树木。

Birds
鸟类

Most of the wildlife you’ll see in China will be birds, and with more than 1300 species recorded, including about 100 endemic or near-endemic species, China offers some fantastic birdwatching opportunities, with spring usually the best time.
在中国，您会看到的大多数野生动物都是鸟类，中国有记录的 1300 多种物种，其中包括大约 100 种特有或近特有物种，提供了一些绝佳的观鸟机会，春天通常是最好的时间。

Among China’s more famous large birds are cranes, with nine of the world’s 14 species having been recorded here. In Jiangxi province, on the lower Yangzi, a vast series of shallow lakes and lagoons was formed by stranded overflow from Yangzi flooding. High numbers of waterfowl and other birds, including ducks, geese, herons and egrets, inhabit these swamps year-round. Although it is difficult to reach, and infrastructure for birdwatchers is practically nonexistent, birders are increasingly drawn to the area in winter, when many of the lakes dry up and attract flocks of up to five crane species, including the endangered, pure-white Siberian crane.
中国最著名的大型鸟类是鹤，世界上 14 种鸟类中有 9 种在这里被记录下来。在江西省扬子河下游，扬子洪水的搁浅溢流形成了一系列广阔的浅湖和泻湖。大量的水禽和其他鸟类，包括鸭子、鹅、苍鹭和白鹭，全年栖息在这些沼泽中。尽管它很难到达，而且观鸟者的基础设施几乎不存在，但观鸟者在冬季越来越被吸引到该地区，此时许多湖泊干涸并吸引了多达五种鹤群，包括濒临灭绝的纯白色西伯利亚鹤。

Recommended destinations include Zhalong Nature Reserve, one of several vast wetlands in Heilongjiang province. Visit in summer to see breeding storks, cranes and flocks of wildfowl before they fly south for the winter. Beidaihe, on the coast of the Bohai Sea, is well known for migratory birds. Other breeding grounds and wetlands include Qinghai Lake in Qinghai, Caohai Lake in Guizhou, Jiuzhaigou in Sichuan and Mai Po Marsh in Hong Kong.
推荐的目的地包括黑龙江省几大湿地之一的扎龙自然保护区。夏季前往，在野禽向南飞越冬之前，您可以参观繁殖的鹳、鹤和成群的野禽。北戴河，位于渤海之滨，以候鸟闻名。其他繁殖地和湿地包括青海的青海湖、贵州的草海湖、四川的九寨沟和香港的米埔沼泽。

Most birdwatchers and bird tours head straight for Sichuan, which offers superb birding at sites such as Wolong. Bird Tour Asia (www.birdtourasia.com) has popular tours to Sichuan, Tibet, Qinghai, eastern China and southeast China, and also provides custom tours.
大多数观鸟者和观鸟之旅都会直接前往四川，那里在卧龙等景点提供绝佳的观鸟体验。Bird Tour Asia（www.birdtourasia.com）拥有四川、西藏、青海、华东和中国东南地区的热门旅游，也提供定制旅游。

Plants
植物

China is home to more than 32,000 species of seed plant and 2500 species of forest tree, plus an extraordinary plant diversity that includes some famous ‘living fossils’ – a diversity so great that Jilin province in the semifrigid north and Hainan province in the tropical south share few plant species.
中国拥有32,000多种种子植物和2500种林木，以及非凡的植物多样性，其中包括一些著名的“活化石”——这种多样性如此之大，以至于半寒冷的北部的吉林省和热带南部的海南省几乎没有植物物种。

Apart from rice, the plant probably most often associated with China and Chinese culture is bamboo, of which the country boasts some 300 species. Bamboos grow in many parts of China, but bamboo forests were once so extensive that they enabled the evolution of the giant panda, which eats virtually nothing else, and a suite of small mammals, birds and insects that live in bamboo thickets. Most of these useful species are found in the subtropical areas south of the Yangzi, and the best surviving thickets are in southwestern provinces such as Sichuan.
除了水稻之外，最常与中国和中国文化联系在一起的植物可能是竹子，该国拥有约 300 种竹子。竹子生长在中国的许多地方，但竹林曾经如此广阔，以至于它们促成了大熊猫的进化，大熊猫几乎不吃其他东西，以及生活在竹丛中的一系列小型哺乳动物、鸟类和昆虫。这些有用的物种大多分布在扬子以南的亚热带地区，现存最好的灌木丛位于四川等西南省份。

Many plants commonly cultivated in Western gardens today originated in China, among them the ginkgo tree, a famous ‘living fossil’ whose unmistakable imprint has been found in 270-million-year-old rocks.
如今，西方园林中常见的许多植物都起源于中国，其中包括银杏树，这是一种著名的“活化石”，在 2.7 亿年前的岩石中发现了明显的印记。

Deciduous forests cover mid-altitudes in the mountains, and are characterised by oaks, hemlocks and aspens, with a leafy under-storey that springs to life after the winter snows have melted. Among the more famous blooms of the understorey are rhododendrons and azaleas, and many species of each grow naturally in China’s mountain ranges. Although they are best viewed in spring, some species flower right through summer. One of the best places to see them is at Sichuan’s Wolong Nature Reserve.
落叶林覆盖了山区的中海拔地区，以橡树、铁杉和白杨为特征，冬季积雪融化后，树叶繁茂的楼下层生机勃勃。林下比较著名的花朵是杜鹃花和杜鹃花，每种植物的许多物种都自然生长在中国的山脉中。虽然它们最好在春天观赏，但有些物种会一直开花到夏天。观赏它们的最佳地点之一是四川卧龙自然保护区。

A growing number of international wildlife-travel outfits arrange botanical expeditions to China, including UK-based Naturetrek (www.naturetrek.co.uk), which arranges tours to Yunnan, Sichuan and the Tibetan plateau.
越来越多的国际野生动物旅行机构安排前往中国的植物探险，包括总部位于英国的 Naturetrek （www.naturetrek.co.uk），该公司安排了云南、四川和青藏高原的旅行。

By 2019 China had invested in a nationwide fleet of 421,000 electric buses, a staggering 99% of the world total.
截至 2019 年，中国已在全国范围内投资了 421,000 辆电动公交车，占世界总数的 99%，令人震惊。

The World Health Organization estimates that air pollution causes more than 1.4 million fatalities per year in China, while around 300 million rural Chinese do not have access to safe drinking water.
世界卫生组织估计，空气污染每年在中国造成超过 140 万人死亡，而大约 3 亿中国农村无法获得安全饮用水。

Endangered Species
濒危物种

Almost every large mammal you can think of in China has crept onto the endangered species list, as well as many of the so-called ‘lower’ animals and plants. The snow leopard, Indo-Chinese tiger, chiru antelope, crested ibis, Asiatic elephant, red-crowned crane and black-crowned crane are all endangered.
在中国，几乎所有你能想到的大型哺乳动物，以及许多所谓的“低等”动植物，都已经悄悄进入了濒危物种名单。雪豹、印中虎、赤鹭羚羊、朱鹮、亚洲象、丹顶鹤和黑顶鹤都濒临灭绝。

Deforestation, pollution and hunting and trapping for fur, body parts, medicine, food delicacies and sport are all culprits. The Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES) records legal trade in live reptiles and parrots, and high numbers of reptile and wildcat skins. The number of such products collected or sold unofficially is anyone’s guess.
森林砍伐、污染以及狩猎和诱捕毛皮、身体部位、药品、美食和运动都是罪魁祸首。《濒危野生动植物种国际贸易公约》（CITES）记录了活爬行动物和鹦鹉的合法贸易，以及大量爬行动物和野猫皮。非正式收集或销售的此类产品的数量是任何人的猜测。

Despite the threats, a number of rare animal species cling to survival in the wild. Notable among them are the Chinese alligator in Anhui, the giant salamander in the fast-running waters of the Yangzi and Yellow Rivers, the Yangzi River dolphin in the lower and middle reaches of the river (though there have been no sightings since 2002), and the pink dolphin of the Hong Kong islands of Sha Chau and Lung Kwu Chau. The giant panda is confined to the fauna-rich valleys and ranges of Sichuan.
尽管存在威胁，许多稀有动物物种仍坚持在野外生存。其中值得注意的是安徽的中华短吻鳄、长江和黄河湍急水域中的大鲵、江中下游的扬子江海豚（尽管自2002年以来就没有目击事件），以及香港沙洲和龙鼓洲岛的粉红海豚。大熊猫仅限于四川动物群丰富的山谷和山脉。

The Environment
环境

China may be vast, but with two-thirds of the land covered in mountains, desert or uncultivable area, the remaining third is overwhelmed by the people of the world’s most populous nation. For the first time in its history, China’s city dwellers outnumbered rural residents in 2011, with an urbanisation rate set to increase to 65% by 2050. The speed of development – and the sheer volume of poured concrete – is staggering. During the next 15 years, China is expected to build urban areas equal in size to 10 New York Cities; a staggering one billion Chinese could be urban residents by 2030.
中国幅员辽阔，但三分之二的土地被山脉、沙漠或无法耕种的地区所覆盖，其余三分之一的土地被世界上人口最多的国家的人民所淹没。2011年，中国城市居民数量首次超过农村居民，到2050年，城市化率将提高到65%。开发速度和浇筑混凝土的绝对数量是惊人的。未来15年，中国预计将建设相当于纽约10个城市的城市面积;到2030年，中国可能有10亿人成为城市居民。

Beyond urban areas, deforestation and overgrazing have accelerated the desertification of vast areas of China, particularly in the western provinces. Deserts now cover almost one-fifth of the country and China’s dustbowl is the world’s largest, swallowing up 200 sq km of arable land every month. The Gobi Desert is the world’s fastest-growing desert, consuming 3600 sq km of grassland annually.
除了城市地区之外，森林砍伐和过度放牧加速了中国大片地区的荒漠化，特别是在西部省份。沙漠现在覆盖了中国近五分之一的面积，中国的沙尘暴是世界上最大的沙尘暴，每个月吞噬了200平方公里的耕地。戈壁沙漠是世界上发展最快的沙漠，每年消耗3600平方公里的草原。

China has been undertaking a huge tree-planting programme, increasing its forest coverage from 8.6% in 1949 to 21.7% in 2017.
中国一直在进行大规模的植树计划，森林覆盖率从 1949 年的 8.6% 提高到 2017 年的 21.7%。

A Greener China?
一个更绿色的中国？

China is painfully aware of its accelerated desertification, growing water shortages, shrinking glaciers, acidic rain, contaminated rivers, caustic urban air and polluted soil. The government is keenly committed, on a policy level, to the development of greener and cleaner energy sources. China’s leaders are also seeking to devise a more sustainable and less wasteful economic model for the nation’s future development.
中国痛苦地意识到，中国正在加速荒漠化、日益严重的缺水、冰川萎缩、酸雨、河流受污染、城市空气恶性和土壤污染。政府在政策层面上热衷于开发更绿色、更清洁的能源。中国领导人还在寻求为国家的未来发展设计一种更可持续、更少浪费的经济模式。

China is, however, the world’s largest carbon emitter. There is evidence of bold thinking though: in 2010 China announced it would pour billions into developing electric and hybrid vehicles; Beijing committed itself to overtaking Europe in renewable-energy investment by 2020; wind-farm construction (in Gansu, for example) continues apace; and China leads the world in the production of solar cells. Coal use began declining from 2013, though 59% of China’s energy was provided by coal in 2018, reflecting a second annual increase in a row. There are worries that China may recently have put emissions reduction on the back-burner due to its slowing economy and growing costs.
然而，中国是世界上最大的碳排放国。不过，有证据表明他的想法很大：2010年，中国宣布将投入数十亿美元开发电动和混合动力汽车;北京承诺到2020年在可再生能源投资方面超过欧洲;风电场建设（例如甘肃）继续快速发展;中国在太阳能电池生产方面处于世界领先地位。煤炭使用量从 2013 年开始下降，但 2018 年中国 59% 的能源来自煤炭，连续第二次增长。有人担心，由于经济放缓和成本增加，中国最近可能将减排放在次要位置。

Public protests – sometimes violent – against polluting industries have proliferated in recent years across China and have scored a number of notable victories, including the 2012 demonstrations in Shifang (Sichuan), which led to the cancellation of a planned US$1.6 billion copper-smelting facility. A 2013 survey in China revealed that 78% of people would demonstrate if polluting industries were constructed near their homes. Much of the agitation is the result of health concerns, as cancer is now the leading cause of death in China, with 7500 deaths per day as a result of the disease (lung cancer being the most prevalent form).
近年来，中国各地针对污染行业的公众抗议活动（有时是暴力的）激增，并取得了许多显着的胜利，包括 2012 年四川什邡的示威活动，导致计划中的耗资 16 亿美元的铜冶炼设施被取消。2013 年在中国进行的一项调查显示，如果在他们家附近建造污染工业，78% 的人会进行示威。大部分骚动是健康问题的结果，因为癌症现在是中国的主要死因，每天有 7500 人死于这种疾病（肺癌是最常见的形式）。

SOUTH–NORTH WATER DIVERSION PROJECT
南水北调工程

With only around 7% of the world’s water resources but almost 20% of its population, China faces a worsening water crisis. Farmers in the arid north are draining aquifers that have taken thousands of years to accumulate, while industry in China uses three to 10 times more water per unit of production than developed nations. Meanwhile water usage in large cities such as Beijing and Tianjin continues to climb as migrants flood in from rural areas.
中国的水资源仅占世界水资源的7%左右，但人口却占世界近20%，水危机日益恶化。干旱的北方农民正在排干需要数千年才能积累的含水层，而中国工业单位生产的用水量是发达国家的三到十倍。与此同时，随着农村地区涌入的移民，北京和天津等大城市的用水量继续攀升。

To combat the crisis the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) embarked on the construction of the US$81-billion South–North Water Diversion Project, a vast network of pumping stations, canals and aqueducts (as well as a tunnel under the Yellow River) designed to divert 3.8 million Olympic swimming pools’ worth of water yearly from the Yangzi River to the parched regions of China’s north. The first stage began operating in 2013. Calculations, however, suggested that by 2020 only 5% of Beijing’s water requirements would be met by the diverted water.
为了应对危机，中国共产党（CCP）着手建设耗资 810 亿美元的南北调水工程，这是一个由泵站、运河和渡槽（以及黄河下的隧道）组成的庞大网络，旨在每年将 380 万个奥林匹克游泳池的水从扬子江引流到中国北部干涸的地区。第一阶段于 2013 年开始运营。然而，计算表明，到 2020 年，引水只能满足北京 5% 的用水需求。

There are also concerns about pollution in the Yangzi River becoming progressively concentrated as water is extracted, while Yangzi cities such as Nanjing and Wuhan are increasingly uneasy that they will be left with a water shortfall. In 2016 it was revealed that lakes along the Yangzi River had reported up to a 40% drop in water inflow.
人们还担心随着水的开采，扬子江的污染会逐渐集中，而南京和武汉等扬子城市则越来越担心它们将面临缺水。2016年，据透露，扬子江沿岸湖泊的入水量下降了40%。
