Why Go?
为什么要去？

For many visitors, the highlights of Tibet will be of a spiritual nature: magnificent monasteries, prayer halls of chanting monks, and remote cliffside meditation retreats. Tibet’s pilgrims – from local grandmothers murmuring mantras in temples heavy with the aromas of juniper incense to hardcore professionals walking or prostrating themselves around Mt Kailash – are an essential part of this experience. Tibetans have a level of devotion and faith that seems to belong to an earlier age. It is fascinating, inspiring and endlessly photogenic.
对于许多游客来说，西藏的亮点将是精神性的：宏伟的寺院、诵经僧侣的祈祷殿和偏远的悬崖边禅修静修处。西藏的朝圣者——从在充满杜松香气的寺庙里喃喃自语的祖母，到在冈仁波齐山周围行走或跪拜的铁杆专业人士——是这种体验的重要组成部分。藏人的虔诚和信仰似乎属于更早的时代。它令人着迷、鼓舞人心，而且上镜无穷。

Tibet’s other big draw is the beauty of the highest plateau on earth. Geography here is on a humbling scale and every view is illuminated with spectacular mountain light. Your trip will take you past turquoise lakes, across huge plains dotted with yaks and nomads’ tents, and over high passes draped with colourful prayer flags. Hike past the ruins of remote hermitages, stare at the north face of Everest or make an epic trip along some of the world’s wildest roads. The scope for adventure is limited only by your ability to get permits.
西藏的另一大吸引力是地球上最高的高原的美景。这里的地理规模令人毛骨悚然，每一个景色都被壮观的山光照亮。您的旅行将带您经过碧绿的湖泊，穿过点缀着牦牛和游牧民族帐篷的广阔平原，并越过挂满五颜六色经幡的高山口。徒步经过偏远隐修院的废墟，凝视珠穆朗玛峰的北壁，或沿着世界上一些最狂野的道路进行史诗般的旅行。冒险的范围仅受您获得许可证的能力的限制。

History
历史

Recorded Tibetan history began in the 7th century AD, when the Tibetan armies began to assemble a great empire. Under King Songtsen Gampo, the Tibetans occupied Nepal and collected tribute from parts of Yunnan. Shortly afterwards, the Tibetan armies moved north and took control of the Silk Road and the great trade centre of Kashgar, even sacking the imperial Chinese city of Chang’an (present-day Xi’an). Tibetan expansion came to an abrupt halt in 842 with the assassination of anti-Buddhist King Langdarma; the region subsequently broke into independent feuding principalities. The increasing influence of Buddhism ensured that the Tibetan armies would never again leave their high plateau.
有记载的藏族历史始于公元7世纪，当时藏族军队开始组建一个伟大的帝国。在松赞干布国王的统治下，藏人占领了尼泊尔，并从云南部分地区征收贡品。不久之后，藏族军队北上，控制了丝绸之路和喀什大贸易中心，甚至洗劫了中国皇城长安（今习安）。842 年，随着反佛教国王朗达玛的暗杀，西藏的扩张戛然而止;该地区随后分裂成独立的世仇公国。佛教的影响力日益增强，确保了西藏军队再也不会离开他们的高原。

By the 7th century, Buddhism had spread through Tibet, though it had taken on a unique form, as it adopted many of the rituals of Bön (the indigenous animist pre-Buddhist belief system of Tibet) including the prayer flags, pilgrimage circuits and sacred landscapes you’ll see across modern Tibet.
到了 7 世纪，佛教已经在西藏传播开来，尽管它呈现出一种独特的形式，因为它采用了许多苯教（西藏本土万物有灵论前佛教信仰体系）的仪式，包括经幡、朝圣路线和神圣景观，您将在现代西藏看到。

From the 13th century, power politics began to play an increasing role in religion. In 1641 the Gelugpa (‘Yellow Hat’ order) used the support of Mongol troops to crush the Sakyapa, their rivals. It was also during this time of partisan struggle that the Gelugpa leader adopted the title of Dalai Lama (Ocean of Wisdom), given to him by the Mongols. From here on out, religion and politics in Tibet became inextricably entwined and both were presided over by the Dalai Lama.
从13世纪开始，强权政治开始在宗教中发挥越来越大的作用。1641 年，格鲁派（“黄帽”教团）利用蒙古军队的支持粉碎了他们的竞争对手萨迦派。也正是在这段党派斗争时期，格鲁派领袖采用了蒙古人授予他的达赖喇嘛（智慧之海）的头衔。从此，西藏的宗教和政治密不可分，两者都由达赖喇嘛主持。

With the fall of the Qing dynasty in 1911, Tibet entered a period of de facto independence that was to last until 1950. In this year a resurgent communist China invaded Tibet, claiming it was ‘liberating’ more than one million Tibetans from feudal serfdom and bringing it back into the fold of the motherland.
随着1911年清朝的灭亡，西藏进入了事实上的独立时期，一直持续到1950年。这一年，卷土重来的共产主义中国入侵西藏，声称它正在将超过一百万藏人从封建农奴制中“解放”出来，并将其带回祖国的怀抱。

Increasing popular unrest in response to Chinese land reform resulted in a full-blown revolt in 1959, which was crushed by the People’s Liberation Army (PLA). Amid popular rumours of a Chinese plot to kidnap him, the Dalai Lama fled to India. He was followed by an exodus of 80,000 of Tibet’s best and brightest, who now represent the Tibetan government-in-exile from Dharamsala, India.
1959 年，中国土地改革的民众骚乱日益加剧，导致了一场全面的起义，并被中国人民解放军（PLA）镇压。在中国密谋绑架达赖喇嘛的流行谣言中，达赖喇嘛逃往印度。紧随其后的是 80,000 名西藏最优秀和最聪明的人，他们现在代表西藏流亡政府离开印度达兰萨拉。

The Dalai Lama, who has referred to China’s policies on migration as ‘cultural genocide’, is resigned to pushing for autonomy rather than independence, though even that concession has borne little fruit. The Chinese, for their part, seem to be waiting for him to die, positioning themselves to control the future politics of reincarnation. The Dalai Lama’s tireless insistence on a non-violent solution to the Tibet problem led to him winning the Nobel Peace Prize in 1989, but despite global sympathy for the Tibetan cause, few nations are willing to raise the issue and place new business deals with China’s rising economic superpower at risk.
达赖喇嘛将中国的移民政策称为“文化种族灭绝”，他甘愿推动自治而不是独立，尽管即使是这种让步也收效甚微。就中国人而言，他们似乎在等待他死去，将自己定位为控制未来的轮回政治。达赖喇嘛孜孜不倦地坚持以非暴力方式解决西藏问题，使他于1989年获得了诺贝尔和平奖，但尽管全球对西藏事业表示同情，但很少有国家愿意提出这个问题，并与中国这个崛起的经济超级大国进行新的商业交易。

The Chinese are truly baffled by what they perceive as the continuing ingratitude of the Tibetans. They claim that Tibet pre-1950 was a place of abject poverty and feudal exploitation. China, they say, has brought roads, schools, hospitals, airports, factories and rising incomes.
中国人对他们认为藏人持续忘恩负义的情况感到非常困惑。他们声称，1950 年之前的西藏是一个赤贫和封建剥削的地方。他们说，中国带来了道路、学校、医院、机场、工厂和收入的增加。

Many Tibetans, however, cannot forgive the destruction in the 1950s and 1960s of hundreds of monasteries and shrines, the restrictions on religious expression, the continued heavy military presence, economic exploitation and their obvious second-class status within their own land. Riots and protests in the spring of 2008 brought this simmering dissatisfaction out into the open, as Lhasa erupted into full-scale riots and protests spread to other Tibetan areas in Gansu, Sichuan and Qinghai provinces. The Chinese response was predictable: arrest, imprisonment and an increased police presence in many monasteries. The increasing desperation felt by many Tibetans has led to a spate of self-immolations by Tibetans across the region, including two in Lhasa’s Barkhor Circuit in 2012.
然而，许多藏人无法原谅1950年代和1960年代数百座寺院和圣地的破坏、对宗教表达的限制、持续的大量军事存在、经济剥削以及它们在自己土地上的明显二等地位。2008年春天的骚乱和抗议活动将这种酝酿的不满情绪公之于众，拉萨爆发了全面骚乱，抗议活动蔓延到甘肃、四川和青海省的其他藏族地区。中国的反应是可以预见的：逮捕、监禁，并在许多寺院增加警察的存在。许多藏人感到越来越绝望，导致该地区藏人发生了一连串的自焚事件，其中包括2012年在拉萨八廓道的两起自焚事件。

As immigration and breakneck modernisation continue, the government is gambling that economic advances will diffuse the Tibetans’ religious and political aspirations. It’s a policy that has so far been successful in the rest of China. Whether it will work in Tibet remains to be seen.
随着移民和飞速现代化的继续，政府正在赌经济进步将分散藏人的宗教和政治愿望。迄今为止，这项政策在中国其他地区取得了成功。它在西藏是否奏效还有待观察。

Climate
气候

Most of Tibet is a high-altitude desert plateau at more than 4000m. Days in summer (June to September) are warm, sunny and generally dry, but temperatures drop quickly after dark. It’s always cool above 4000m and often freezing at night, though thanks to the Himalayan rain shadow there is surprisingly little snow in the Land of Snows. Sunlight is very strong at these altitudes, so bring plenty of high-factor sunscreen and lip balm.
西藏大部分地区是海拔4000多米的高海拔沙漠高原。夏季（六月至九月）的日子温暖、阳光明媚且普遍干燥，但天黑后气温迅速下降。海拔 4000 米以上总是凉爽，夜间经常结冰，但由于喜马拉雅雨影，雪之国的积雪出奇地少。这些海拔地区的阳光非常强烈，因此请带上大量的高系数防晒霜和润唇膏。

Language
语言

Most urban Tibetans speak Mandarin in addition to Tibetan. Even in the countryside you can get by with basic Mandarin in most restaurants and hotels, since they are normally run by Mandarin-speaking Han or Hui Chinese. That said, Tibetans are extremely pleased when foreign visitors at least greet them in Tibetan, so it’s well worth learning a few phrases. Very few Tibetans outside of the tourism industry speak English.
大多数城市藏人除了藏语外，还会说普通话。即使在乡村，大多数餐馆和酒店也可以使用基本的普通话，因为它们通常由讲普通话的汉人或回族人经营。也就是说，当外国游客至少用藏语问候藏人时，藏人会非常高兴，所以学习一些短语是非常值得的。旅游业之外很少有藏人会说英语。

8Getting Around
8 四处走走

Tibet’s transport infrastructure has developed rapidly in recent years. Most of the main highways are now paved. Airports are springing up across the plateau and the railway line is slowly extending beyond Lhasa. In 2011 Tibet’s Metok county was the very last of China’s 2100 counties to be connected by road.
近年来，西藏的交通基础设施发展迅速。现在大部分主要高速公路都已铺好。机场如雨后春笋般在高原上拔地而起，铁路线正在慢慢延伸到拉萨之外。2011年，西藏墨托县是中国2100个县中最后一个通过公路连接的县。

Car The only way to travel around Tibet at the moment, since foreign travellers have to hire private transport as part of their obligatory tour.
汽车目前环游西藏的唯一方式，因为外国游客必须租用私人交通工具作为其强制性旅行的一部分。

Train Great for getting to and from Tibet but of limited use inside Tibet, unless you are just taking a short trip from Lhasa to Shigatse and back.
火车非常适合往返西藏，但在西藏境内用途有限，除非您只是从拉萨到日喀则的短途旅行。

Bus Lots of services, but foreigners are currently not allowed to take buses or shared taxis in Tibet.
总线很多服务，但外国人目前不允许在西藏乘坐公共汽车或共享出租车。

Lhasa ལྷ་ས་ 拉萨

%0891 / Elev 3650m / Pop 530,000
% 0891 / 学生 3650m / 人口 530,000

The centre of the Tibetan Buddhist world for over a millennium, Lhasa (Lāsà, literally the ‘Place of the Gods’) remains largely a city of wonders. Your first view of the red-and-white Potala Palace soaring above the Holy City raises goosebumps and the charming whitewashed old Tibetan quarter continues to preserve the essence of traditional Tibetan life. It is here in the Jokhang, an other-worldly mix of flickering butter lamps, wafting incense and prostrating pilgrims, and the encircling Barkhor pilgrim circuit, that most visitors first fall in love with Tibet.
一千多年来，拉萨一直是藏传佛教世界的中心，拉萨（拉萨，字面意思是“众神之地”）在很大程度上仍然是一座奇迹之城。红白相间的布达拉宫耸立在圣城之上，第一眼就起鸡皮疙瘩，迷人的白色老藏区继续保留着传统藏族生活的精髓。正是在大昭，闪烁的黄油灯、飘荡的香火和俯伏的朝圣者，以及环绕的巴尔科尔朝圣者环绕的超凡脱俗的混合体，大多数游客首先爱上了西藏。

These days the booming boulevards of the modern city threaten to overwhelm the winding alleyways and backstreet temples of the Tibetan old town, but it is in the latter that you should focus your time. If possible, budget a week to acclimatise, see the sights and roam the fascinating backstreets before heading off on a grand overland adventure.
如今，现代城市蓬勃发展的林荫大道有可能淹没西藏老城区蜿蜒的小巷和后街寺庙，但您应该将时间集中在后者。如果可能的话，请花一周时间适应环境，参观景点并在迷人的后街漫步，然后开始一场盛大的陆路冒险。

oPotala PalacePALACE
o 布达拉宫 PALACE

(map Google map; པོ་ཏ་ལ་, 布达拉宫, Bùdálā Gōng; 35 Beijing Donglu, 北京中路35号; May-Oct ¥200, Nov-Apr ¥100; h9.30am-3pm Nov-Apr, 9am-3.30pm May-Oct, interior chapels close 4.30pm)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; པོ་ཏ་ལ་ 布达拉宫 ， Bùdálā Gōng;北京东路 35 号， 北京中路 35 号 ;5-10 月 200 日元，11 月-4 月 100 日元; h 11 月至 4 月上午 9：30 至下午 3 点，5 月至 10 月上午 9 点至下午 3：30，内部教堂下午 4：30 关闭）

The magnificent Potala Palace, once the seat of the Tibetan government and the winter residence of the Dalai Lamas, is Lhasa’s cardinal landmark. Your first sight of its towering, fortress-like walls is a moment you’ll remember for years. An architectural wonder even by modern standards, the palace rises 13 storeys from 130m-high Marpo Ri (Red Hill) and contains more than 1000 rooms. Pilgrims and tourists alike shuffle open-mouthed through the three storeys, past the dozens of magnificent chapels, golden stupas and prayer halls.
宏伟的布达拉宫，曾经是西藏政府的所在地，也是达赖喇嘛的冬宫，是拉萨的地标。您第一次看到其高耸的堡垒般的城墙，您将铭记多年。即使以现代标准来看，这座宫殿也是一个建筑奇迹，从 130 米高的 Marpo Ri（红山）高 13 层，拥有 1000 多个房间。朝圣者和游客都张大嘴巴穿过三层楼，经过数十座宏伟的小教堂、金色的佛塔和祈祷厅。

The first recorded use of the site was in the 7th century AD, when King Songtsen Gampo built a palace here. Construction of the present structure began during the reign of the fifth Dalai Lama in 1645 and took divisions of labourers and artisans more than 50 years to complete. It is impressive enough to have caused Chinese premier Zhou Enlai to send his own troops to protect it from the Red Guards during the Cultural Revolution.
该遗址的首次使用记录是在公元 7 世纪，当时松赞干布国王在这里建造了一座宫殿。现在的建筑始于 1645 年五世达赖喇嘛统治时期，历时 50 多年才完成。它足以让中国总理周恩来在文化大革命期间派出自己的军队保护它免受红卫兵的侵害，这已经足够令人印象深刻了。

The layout of the Potala Palace includes the rooftop White Palace map Google map (Kharpo Podrang) (the eastern part of the building), used for the living quarters of the Dalai Lama, and the central Red Palace map Google map (Marpo Podrang), used for religious functions. The most stunning chapels of the Red Palace house the jewelbedecked golden chörten (Tibetan stupa) tombs of several previous Dalai Lamas. The apartments of the 13th and 14th Dalai Lamas, in the White Palace, offer a more personal insight into palace life.
布达拉宫的布局包括用于达赖喇嘛居住区的屋顶白宫地图谷歌地图 （Kharpo Podrang）（ 建筑的东部）和中央红宫地图谷歌地图 （Marpo Podrang）， 用于宗教活动。红宫最令人惊叹的小教堂里有几位前达赖喇嘛的珠宝装饰的金色 chörten（西藏佛塔）墓。白宫中的第 13 世和第 14 世达赖喇嘛的公寓提供了对宫殿生活的更个人化的了解。

Tickets for the Potala are limited and your guide will need to book a time slot several days in advance. Arrive at the palace an hour or so before your allotted time. After a security check (no water or lighters allowed), follow the other visitors to the stairs up into the palace. Halfway up you’ll pass the ticket booth, where you’ll buy your ticket. Note that if you arrive later than the time on your voucher (or if you forget your voucher) you can be refused a ticket. Photography isn’t allowed inside the chapels.
布达拉的门票有限，您的导游需要提前几天预订时间段。在规定的时间前一个小时左右到达宫殿。经过安全检查（不允许使用水或打火机）后，跟随其他游客走上宫殿的楼梯。在中途，您将经过售票亭，在那里购买门票。请注意，如果您晚于凭证上的时间到达（或者您忘记使用凭证），您可能会被拒绝购票。教堂内不允许拍照。

o 大昭寺 BUDDHIST TEMPLE

(map Google map; ཇོ་ཁང༌, 大昭寺, Dàzhāo Sì; Bakuo Xijie, 八廓西街; adult ¥85; h8.15-noon & 3-5.30pm, most chapels closed after noon)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; ཇོ་ཁང་ 大昭寺 ，Dàzhāo Sì;八廓西街，八廓西街 ;成人 ¥85; h 中午 8.15 和下午 3-5.30，大多数教堂在中午后关闭）

The 1300-year-old Jokhang Temple is the spiritual heart of Tibet: the continuous waves of awestruck pilgrims prostrating themselves outside are a testament to its timeless allure. The central golden Buddha image here is the most revered in all of Tibet.
拥有 1300 年历史的大昭寺是西藏的精神中心：源源不断的敬畏的朝圣者在外面跪拜，证明了它永恒的魅力。这里中央的金色佛像是全西藏最受尊敬的。

The Jokhang was originally built to house an image of Buddha brought to Tibet by King Songtsen Gampo’s Nepalese wife. However, another image, the Jowo Sakyamuni, was later moved here by the king’s other wife (the Chinese Princess Wencheng), and it is this image that gives the Jokhang both its name and its spiritual potency: Jokhang means ‘chapel of the Jowo’.
大昭最初是为了安放松赞干布国王的尼泊尔妻子带到西藏的佛像而建造的。然而，另一个图像，即 Jowo Sakyamuni，后来被国王的另一位妻子（中国文成公主）搬到了这里，正是这个图像赋予了大昭的名字和精神力量：大昭的意思是“Jowo 的教堂”。

The two-storeyed Jokhang is best visited in the morning, though the crowds of yak-butter-spooning pilgrims can be thick. Access is possible in the afternoon through a side entrance, but only the ground-floor chapels can be viewed (and then only through a grille) and there are no pilgrims.
两层楼的大昭最好在早上参观，尽管用牦牛油勺勺子舀的朝圣者可能会很拥挤。下午可以通过侧门进入，但只能看到一楼的小教堂（然后只能通过格栅），而且没有朝圣者。

oChagpo Ri Rock CarvingsHISTORIC SITE
o Chagpo Ri 石刻历史遗迹

(map Google map; 药王山, Yàowáng Shān; 41 Beijing Zhonglu, 北京中路41号; ¥10; hdawn-dusk)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 药王山 ， Yàowáng Shān; 41 Beijing Zhonglu， 北京中路 41 号 ; ¥10; h 黎明-黄昏）

This hidden corner of Lhasa features more than 5000 painted rock carvings that were created on the back side of Chagpo Ri over the course of a millennium. Throughout the day, pilgrims perform full-body prostrations in front of the images, while stone carvers at the far end of the courtyard contribute to a large chörten built entirely of the carvers’ mani stones. The best way to visit the area is as part of the Lingkhor pilgrim route.
拉萨这个隐秘的角落拥有 5000 多幅彩绘石刻，这些石刻是在一千年的时间里在查格波里的背面创作的。一整天，朝圣者都会在图像前进行全身大礼拜，而庭院远端的石雕师则建造了一个完全由雕刻师的玛尼石建造的大型雕像。游览该地区的最佳方式是作为 Lingkhor 朝圣路线的一部分。

For your first visit to the Barkhor, enter from Barkhor Sq, a large plaza that was cleared in 1985. The square has been a focus for violent political protest on several occasions, notably in 1998 (when a Dutch tourist was shot in the shoulder) and most recently in 2008. 第一次参观 Barkhor 时，请从 Barkhor 广场进入，这是一个于 1985 年清理的大型广场。该广场曾多次成为暴力政治抗议的焦点，特别是在 1998 年（一名荷兰游客肩部中弹）和最近的一次是在 2008 年。广场现在有金属探测器、防暴队车辆、灭火队（以防止自焚）和屋顶监控。尽管有自拍游客络绎不绝，但气氛却是占领或围困。

TIBET TRAVEL RESTRICTIONS
西藏旅行限制

Travel to the Tibet Autonomous Region (TAR) is radically different from travel to the rest of China; a valid Chinese visa is not enough to visit Tibet. You’ll also need several permits, foremost of which is a Tibet Tourism Bureau (TTB) permit, and to get these you have to book a tour. At a minimum you will need to hire a guide for your entire stay and transport for any travel outside Lhasa.
前往西藏自治区 （TAR） 的旅行与前往中国其他地区的旅行完全不同;有效的中国签证不足以访问西藏。您还需要多张许可证，其中最重要的是西藏旅游局 （TTB） 许可证，要获得这些许可证，您必须预订旅游团。您至少需要在整个住宿期间聘请一名导游，并在拉萨以外的任何旅行中进行交通。

Travel regulations to Tibet are constantly in flux, dependent largely on political events in Lhasa and Beijing. Don’t be surprised if the permit system is radically different from how we describe it. In fact, expect it. One of the best places for updated information is the dedicated Tibet page of Lonely Planet’s Thorn Tree, at www.lonelyplanet.com/thorntree.

Tibet Tourism Bureau (TTB) Permit
西藏旅游局许可证

Without a Tibet Tourism Bureau (TTB) permit you will not be able to board a flight or train to Tibet or cross overland from Nepal and you will not be able to secure the other permits you need to continue travelling throughout Tibet.
如果没有西藏旅游局 （TTB） 许可证，您将无法登上前往西藏的航班或火车，也无法从尼泊尔通过陆路穿越，也无法获得继续在西藏各地旅行所需的其他许可证。

How these rules are interpreted depends on the political climate in Tibet. These days you can only get a TTB permit through a tour agency in Tibet (agencies outside Tibet can arrange trips, but ultimately they book through a Tibetan-based agency). Everything must be arranged beforehand, including any trekking.
如何解释这些规则取决于西藏的政治气候。如今，您只能通过西藏的旅行社获得 TTB 许可证（西藏以外的旅行社可以安排旅行，但最终他们是通过藏族旅行社预订的）。一切都必须提前安排好，包括任何徒步旅行。

What your tour actually involves depends on the agency. Some offer all-inclusive tours, while others will arrange transport, a guide and permits but leave accommodation, food and entry fees up to you. You can book your own train or air ticket to Lhasa or have the agency arrange this. Some airline offices and online booking agencies will sell flights to Lhasa to foreigners, but others won’t unless you can show you have a TTB permit.

You need to have the original permit in your hands in order to board a flight to Lhasa, so most agencies arrange to post the permit through an agency or hostel. This can cost anything from ¥25 for normal post (four working days) to ¥180/280/380 for 36-/24-/18-hour express post. TTB permits are not issued in March due to the anniversary of several politically sensitive dates. Assuming the political situation is calm, permits normally start to be reissued in the last week of March and agencies only know the new season’s permit regulations for sure by the end of March. The last-minute nature and uncertainty that comes with this obviously complicates booking train and flight tickets; we recommend booking a fully refundable ticket if possible and taking out trip-cancellation insurance in case your permits fail to materialise.
由于几个政治敏感日期的周年纪念日，TTB 许可证不会在 3 月份颁发。假设政治局势平静，许可证通常会在 3 月的最后一周开始重新颁发，而各机构要到 3 月底才能确定新季节的许可证规定。随之而来的最后一刻的性质和不确定性显然使预订火车票和机票变得复杂;如果可能的话，我们建议您预订可全额退款的机票，并购买旅行取消保险，以防您的许可证未能兑现。

Alien’s Travel Permits & Military Permits
外国人旅行许可证和军事许可证

Once you have a visa and have managed to wangle a TTB permit, you might think you’re home and dry. Think again. Your agency will need to arrange an alien’s travel permit for most of your travels outside Lhasa.
一旦您获得签证并设法获得 TTB 许可证，您可能会认为自己已经回家了。再想一想。您的旅行机构需要为您在拉萨以外的大部分旅行安排外国人旅行许可证。

Travel permits are not needed for Lhasa or places just outside the city such as Ganden Monastery, but most other areas do technically require permits. Permits are most easily arranged in the regional capital. Agencies can only arrange a travel permit for those on a tour with them.
拉萨或城外的地方 （如甘丹寺）不需要旅行许可证，但大多数其他地区在技术上确实需要许可证。在地区首府最容易安排许可证。旅行社只能为与他们一起旅行的人安排旅行许可证。

Sensitive border areas – such as Mt Kailash, the road to Kashgar and the Nyingtri region of eastern Tibet – also require a military permit and a foreign-affairs permit. For remote places such as the Yarlung Tsangpo gorges in southeastern Tibet, the roads through Lhoka south of Gyantse or for any border area, you will likely be unable to get permits even if you book a tour. Regions can close at short notice. The majority of Chamdo prefecture has been closed since 2010, effectively blocking overland trips from Sichuan and Yunnan, but as of 2018 limited areas surrounding the G318 highway are now open to tourists. THE WORLD’S HIGHEST TRAIN RIDE
世界上最高的火车之旅

There’s no doubt the Qinghai–Tibet train line is an engineering marvel. Topping out at 5072m, it is the world’s highest railway, snatching the title from a Peruvian line. The statistics speak for themselves: 86% of the line is above 4000m, and half the track lies on permafrost, requiring a cooling system of pipes driven into the ground to keep it frozen year-round to avoid a rail-buckling summer thaw. Construction of the line involved building 160km of bridges and elevated track, seven tunnels (including the world’s highest) and 24 hyperbaric chambers, the latter to treat altitude-sick workers.
毫无疑问，青藏火车线是一个工程奇迹。它最高海拔 5072 米，是世界上最高的铁路，从秘鲁线路手中夺得了冠军头衔。统计数据不言而喻：86% 的线路位于 4000 米以上，一半的轨道位于永久冻土层上，需要将管道打入地下的冷却系统，以保持全年结冰，以避免铁路弯曲的夏季解冻。该线路的建设包括建造 160 公里的桥梁和高架轨道、7 条隧道（包括世界上最高的隧道）和 24 个高压舱，后者用于治疗高原反应工人。

Aside from environmental concerns, many local Tibetans are deeply worried about the cultural and political impact of the train. The trains unload thousands of tourists and immigrants into Lhasa every day.
除了环境问题外，许多当地藏人还对这列火车的文化和政治影响深感担忧。火车每天将成千上万的游客和移民运送到拉萨。

The authorities stress the economic benefits of the line: highly subsidised, it has decreased transport costs for imports by up to 75%. But Tibetans remain economically marginalised. More than 90% of the 100,000 workers employed to build the line came from other provinces and few, if any, Tibetan staff members work on the trains. The US$4.1 billion cost of building the line is greater than the amount Beijing has spent on hospitals and schools in Tibet over the past 50 years.
当局强调了这条线路的经济效益：在高额补贴下，它使进口运输成本降低了多达 75%。但藏人在经济上仍然处于边缘地位。修建该线路的 100,000 名工人中，超过 90% 来自其他省份，很少有藏族工作人员在火车上工作。这条线路的建设成本为41亿美元，超过了北京过去50年来在西藏医院和学校上的支出。

As ambitious as the current line is, connecting Lhasa with the rest of China was only the beginning. An extension to Shigatse opened in 2014, and a new railway line under construction will expand the line east to Tsetang, Nyingtri and Sichuan province, starting/ending at Chengdu. While this undoubtedly eases travel and provides a comfortable and romantic method of transport into Tibet, travellers should remain mindful of the impact on the culture and delicate ecology of this special region.
尽管目前的线路雄心勃勃，但将拉萨与中国其他地区连接起来只是一个开始。日喀则的延伸线于 2014 年开通，一条正在建设的新铁路线将向东延伸至采唐、宁地和四川省，起点/终点为成都。虽然这无疑简化了旅行，并提供了一种舒适浪漫的西藏交通方式，但旅行者应该注意它对这个特殊地区的文化和微妙生态的影响。

Drepung was once one of the world’s largest monasteries and its ancient prayer halls and self-contained college temples are still a highlight of Tibet. It is bigger and more sprawling than Sera Monastery, so budget half a day here, longer if you want to walk the lovely kora.

Along with Sera and Ganden Monasteries, Drepung functioned as one of the three ‘pillars of the Tibetan state’, and it was purportedly the largest monastery in the world, with around 7000 resident monks at its peak. Drepung means ‘rice heap’, a reference to the white buildings dotting the hillside. The 1½-hour kora around the 15th-century monastery, 8km west of Lhasa, is among the highlights of a trip to the city.
哲蚌寺与色拉寺和甘丹寺一起，是“西藏国家的三大支柱”之一，据称是世界上最大的寺院，鼎盛时期约有 7000 名常驻僧侣。哲蚌的意思是“稻堆”，指的是点缀在山坡上的白色建筑。在拉萨以西 8 公里处的 15 世纪寺院周围游览 11/2 小时的科拉 ，是这座城市之旅的亮点之一。

The kings of Tsang and the Mongols savaged the place regularly; oddly, the Red Guards pretty much left it alone during the Cultural Revolution. With concerted rebuilding, Drepung once again resembles a monastic village and around 600 monks reside here. At lunchtime you can see the novices bringing in buckets of tsampa (roasted-barley flour) and yak-butter tea. In the afternoon you can often witness Tibetan-style religious debating (lots of hand slapping and gesticulating). The best way to visit the monastery is to follow the pilgrim groups or the yellow signs.
藏国王和蒙古人经常野蛮地蹂躏这个地方;奇怪的是，红卫兵在文化大革命期间几乎不理会它。经过协调重建，哲蚌再次像一个寺院村庄，大约有 600 名僧侣居住在这里。午餐时间，您可以看到新手们端来一桶桶糌粑粉（烤大麦粉）和牦牛油茶。下午，您经常可以目睹藏式的宗教辩论（大量拍手和打手势）。参观修道院的最佳方式是跟随朝圣者团体或黄色标志。

Nearby Nechung Monastery, a 10-minute walk downhill, was once the home of the Tibetan state oracle and is worth a visit for its blood-curdling murals.
附近的仁忠寺 ，下坡步行 10 分钟，曾经是西藏国家神谕的故乡，其令人毛骨悚然的壁画值得一游。

If you only have time for one monastery excursion outside Lhasa, Ganden – 60 km from the city – is the best choice. Ü (དབུས་) is Tibet’s heartland and contains almost all the landscapes you’ll find across the plateau, from sand dunes and meandering rivers to soaring peaks and juniper forests. Due to its proximity to Lhasa, Ü is the first taste of rural Tibet that most visitors experience, and you can get off the beaten track surprisingly easily here. Fine walking opportunities abound, from day hikes and monastery koras to overnight treks.
Ü （དབུས་） 是西藏的中心地带，几乎包含了您在高原上可以找到的所有景观，从沙丘和蜿蜒的河流到高耸的山峰和杜松林。由于靠近拉萨，Ü 是大多数游客第一次体验到西藏乡村的味道，在这里您可以出奇地轻松地走出人迹罕至的地方。从一日徒步旅行和修道院科拉到过夜徒步旅行，这里有很多很好的步行机会。

Ü is the traditional power centre of Tibet, and home to its oldest buildings and most historic monasteries. The big sights, such as Samye, are unmissable, but consider also heading to less-visited places such as the Drigung and Yarlung Valleys, or to smaller monasteries like Dratang and Gongkar Chöde. The monastic town of Samye (Sāngyé Zhèn) is home to the the beautiful Samye Monastery, deservedly the most popular destination for travellers in the Ü region. As Tibet’s first monastery and the place where Tibetan Buddhism was established, the monastery is also of major historical and religious importance. Surrounded by barren mountains and rolling sand dunes, the monastery has a quiet magic about it that causes many travellers to rate it as the highlight of Ü.
寺院小镇三耶（Sāngyé Zhèn）是美丽的三耶寺的所在地，当之无愧地成为乌地区游客最受欢迎的目的地。作为西藏第一座寺院和藏传佛教的发源地，该寺院也具有重要的历史和宗教意义。这座修道院周围环绕着贫瘠的山脉和连绵起伏的沙丘，拥有一种安静的魔力，使许多旅行者将其评为乌的亮点。

The historical province of Tsang is either the first or last place that most travellers experience in Tibet, and the setting for two of Asia’s great mountain drives: out to far western Tibet and across the Himalaya to Nepal. The great overland trip across Tibet – from Lhasa along the Friendship Hwy to the Nepali border via Gyantse, Shigatse and Mt Everest Base Camp – goes straight through Tsang, linking most of Tibet’s highlights on one irresistible route. Lying on a historic trade route between India and Tibet, Gyantse (Jiāngzī) has long been a crucial link for traders and pilgrims journeying across the Himalaya. Tibet’s second-largest city and the traditional capital of Tsang province, Shigatse (Rìkāzé) is a modern, sprawling city, with wide boulevards humming with traffic. History
历史

The town of Shigatse, formerly known as Samdruptse, has long been an important trading and administrative centre. The Tsang kings exercised their power from the dzong (fort) and the fort later became the residence of the governor of Tsang.
日喀则镇，原名桑德鲁普策，长期以来一直是重要的贸易和行政中心。曾王从宗 （堡垒）行使权力，堡垒后来成为藏总督的官邸。

Tashilhunpo Monastery is one of the six great Gelugpa (Yellow Hat sect) institutions, along with Drepung, Sera and Ganden Monasteries in Lhasa, and Kumbum (Tǎ’ěr Sì) and Labrang in Amdo (modern Gansu and Qinghai provinces). It was founded in 1447 by Genden Drup, a disciple of Tsongkhapa, the Yellow Hat sect founder himself. Genden Drup was retroactively named the first Dalai Lama and he is enshrined in a stupa inside Tashilhunpo. Despite this important association, Tashilhunpo Monastery was initially isolated from mainstream Gelugpa affairs, which were centred in the Lhasa region.
扎什伦布寺是六大格鲁派（黄帽教）机构之一，与拉萨的哲蚌寺、色拉寺和甘丹寺，以及安多（今甘肃省和青海省）的昆布姆寺（Tǎ'ěr Sì）和拉卜楞寺并列。它由黄帽教创始人宗喀巴大师的弟子根登·德鲁普 （Genden Drup） 于 1447 年创立。根登·德鲁普被追溯命名为第一任达赖喇嘛，他被供奉在扎什伦布的一座佛塔中。尽管有这种重要的联系，扎什伦布寺最初还是与格鲁派的主流事务隔绝，格鲁派的主流事务集中在拉萨地区。

The monastery’s standing rocketed when the fifth Dalai Lama declared his teacher – then the abbot of Tashilhunpo – to be a manifestation of Öpagme (Amitabha). Thus Tashilhunpo became the seat of an important lineage: the Panchen (‘great scholar’) Lamas. Unfortunately, with the establishment of this lineage of spiritual and temporal leaders – second only to the Dalai Lamas – rivalry was introduced to the Gelugpa order.

加德满都和拉萨之间全长 1000 公里左右的公路无疑是世界上最壮观的公路之一。

The main Nepal–Tibet border crossing has shifted to Rasuwagadhi at the meeting of Nepal’s Langtang region and Tibet’s Kyirong Valley. Chinese travellers have been using the border crossing for a few years now, but it was only opened to foreigners in 2017. It’s a spectacular and little-explored route that allows you to combine a trek in Nepal’s Langtang region with a visit to lovely Peiku-tso on the Tibetan side.
尼泊尔-西藏的主要过境点已在尼泊尔朗塘地区和西藏吉荣谷的交汇处转移到拉苏瓦加迪。中国游客使用过境点已有几年了，但直到 2017 年才向外国人开放。这是一条壮观且鲜为人知的路线，您可以将尼泊尔朗塘地区的徒步旅行与参观藏区一侧可爱的佩库措结合起来。

西藏一侧的路段已铺好，但尼泊尔公路行驶缓慢，尤其是在 6 月至 9 月的季风月份。计算从拉苏瓦加迪到加德满都的一整天，并考虑租一辆四驱车进行旅行（16,000 卢比）。

Ngari མངའ་རིས་

Tibet’s far wild west has few permanent settlers but is nevertheless a lodestone to a billion pilgrims from three major religions (Buddhism, Hinduism and Jainism). They are drawn to the twin spiritual power places of Mt Kailash and Lake Manasarovar, two of the world’s most fabled and far-flung destinations. This part of Ngari is a huge, expansive realm of salt lakes, Martian-style deserts, grassy steppes and snow-capped mountains. It’s a mesmerising landscape, but it’s also intensely remote: a few tents and herds of yaks may be all the signs of human existence you’ll come across in half a day’s drive.
恩加里的这一部分是一个广阔的王国，由盐湖、火星式沙漠、草原和雪山组成。这是一片迷人的风景，但也非常偏远：几个帐篷和成群的牦牛可能是您在半天车程内遇到的所有人类存在的迹象。

Going to Ngari and not attempting a kora around holy Mt Kailash (Kang Rinpoche, Precious Jewel of Snow in Tibetan) is inconceivable. 几个世纪以来，这座山一直是朝圣者和旅行者的指路石，但直到最近，很少有人注意到它。路况的改善正在改变这一点。7 月至 8 月期间，大量印度朝圣者参观这座山，顽强的中国游客开始探索该地区。

Mt Kailash is accessed via the grubby town of Darchen (དར་ཆེན།, 塔钦, Tǎqīn), a rapidly expanding settlement of hotel compounds and pilgrim shops. Almost everyone spends a night here before setting off on the kora, and many spend a second night after the trek to grab a hot shower and check emails.
冈仁波齐山可通过肮脏的达琴镇（དར་ཆེན།， 塔钦 ，Tǎqīn）进入，这是一个快速扩张的酒店大院和朝圣者商店的定居点。几乎每个人都在出发前往科拉之前在这里过夜，许多人在跋涉后花第二晚洗个热水澡并查看电子邮件。

Darchen is 3km north of the main Ali–Saga road, about 22km from Barkha, 107km north of Purang, 330km southeast of Ali and a lonely 1200km from Lhasa.
达尔钦位于阿里-萨加主干道以北 3 公里处，距离巴尔喀约 22 公里，普朗以北 107 公里，阿里东南 330 公里处，距离拉萨 1200 公里。

The age-old path around Mt Kailash is one of the world’s great pilgrimage routes and completely encircles Asia’s holiest mountain. With a 5650m pass to conquer, this kora is a test of both the mind and the spirit.
冈仁波齐山周围的古老小径是世界上最伟大的朝圣路线之一，完全环绕着亚洲最神圣的山峰。这场 5650 米的山口需要征服，这是对思想和精神的考验。

There’s some gorgeous mountain scenery along this trek, including close-ups of the majestic pyramidal Mt Kailash, but just as rewarding is the chance to see and meet your fellow pilgrims, many of whom have travelled hundreds of kilometres on foot to get here. Apart from local Tibetans, there are normally dozens of Hindus on the kora during the main pilgrim season (June to September). Most ride horses, with yak teams carrying their supplies. There are also plenty of Chinese tourists.
沿途有一些美丽的山景，包括雄伟的金字塔形冈仁波齐山的特写镜头，但同样有益的是有机会看到和见到您的朝圣者同伴，他们中的许多人步行数百公里才到达这里。除了当地的藏人外，在主要朝圣季节（六月至九月）通常有数十名印度教徒在科拉上。大多数人骑马，牦牛队携带他们的补给品。中国游客也很多。

The route around Mt Kailash is a simple one: you start by crossing a plain, then head up a wide river valley, climb up and over the 5650m Drölma-la, head down another river valley, and finally cross the original plain to the starting point. It’s so straightforward and so perfect a natural circuit that it’s easy to see how it has been a pilgrim favourite for thousands of years.
冈仁波齐山周围的路线很简单：您首先穿过平原，然后沿着宽阔的河谷向上行驶，爬上并越过 5650 米的 Drölma-la，沿着另一个河谷行驶，最后穿过原来的平原到达起点。它是如此简单、如此完美的自然环路，很容易看出它如何成为几千年来朝圣者的最爱。

The Mt Kailash trekking season runs from mid-May until mid-October, but trekkers should always be prepared for changeable weather. Snow may be encountered on the Drölma-la at any time of year and the temperature will often drop well below freezing at night. The pass tends to be snowed in from early November to early April.
冈仁波齐山徒步季节从五月中旬持续到十月中旬，但徒步旅行者应始终为多变的天气做好准备。一年中的任何时候，Drölma-la 上都可能遇到雪，夜间气温通常会降至冰点以下。11 月初至 4 月初，该山口往往会下雪。

The kora is becoming more and more popular. A tent and your own food are always a nice luxury, but there is now accommodation and simple food at Drira-puk and Zutul-puk. Guides can even book you a room here in advance. Bottled water, beer, instant noodles and tea are available every few hours at teahouse tents. Natural water sources abound, but you should bring some means of water purification. A dirt road now encircles two-thirds of the kora, but traffic is light and it’s fairly easy to avoid.
科拉越来越受欢迎。帐篷和自己的食物总是一种奢侈，但现在 Drira-puk 和 Zutul-puk 有住宿和简单的食物。导游甚至可以提前为您预订房间。茶馆帐篷每隔几个小时就提供瓶装水、啤酒、方便面和茶。天然水源比比皆是，但您应该带上一些净水手段。现在，一条土路环绕着科拉的三分之二，但交通很少，很容易避开。

Horses, yaks and porters are all available for hire in Darchen, the gateway town to the kora. Big groups often hire yaks to carry their supplies, but yaks will only travel in pairs or herds, so you have to hire at least two. Horses are an easier option but are surprisingly expensive because they are in great demand by Indian pilgrims. Most hikers carry their own gear or get by with the services of a local porter (¥210 per day for a minimum of three days). All guides and pack animals have to be arranged through a central office (岗仁波齐牛马运输服务中心, Gǎngrén Bōqí Niúmǎ Yùnshū Fúwù Zhōngxīn; %133 2257 5733) in Darchen.
马匹、牦牛和搬运工都可以在通往科拉的门户城镇达尔钦租用。大团体经常雇用牦牛来搬运物资，但牦牛只会成对或成群结队地旅行，所以你必须至少雇两只。马匹是一种更容易的选择，但价格却出奇地昂贵，因为印度朝圣者对马匹的需求量很大。大多数徒步旅行者携带自己的装备或依靠当地搬运工的服务（每天 210 日元，至少三天）。所有导游和驮畜必须通过中央办公室 （ 岗仁波齐牛马运输服务中心 ，Gǎngrén Bōqí Niúmǎ Yùnshū Fúwù Zhōngxīn; % 133 2257 5733）。

Your guide will register your group with the PSB (公安局, Gōng’ānjú; %0897-260 7018; h24hr) in Darchen. The entrance fee to Mt Kailash is ¥150 per person and is paid at a large entry gate before you arrive in Darchen.
您的导游将在公安局 （ 公安局 ，Gōng'ānjú; % 0897-260 7018; h 24 小时） 在达尔真。冈仁波齐山的入场费为每人 150 日元，在您抵达达尔钦之前在一个大型入口处支付。

Most travellers spend a night in Darchen before the kora. Bigger places can be fully booked with large groups of Indian pilgrims during the summer months of June, July and August.
大多数旅行者在科拉之前在达琴过夜。在夏季的 6 月、7 月和 8 月，大群印度朝圣者可能会预订满更大的地方。

Almost all hotels and even restaurants offer free wi-fi. On the kora you can use your mobile phone’s 3G connection to go online.
几乎所有的酒店甚至餐馆都提供免费无线网络连接。在 kora 上，您可以使用手机的 3G 连接上网。

Supplies on the Mt Kailash kora are limited to instant noodles, beer and the occasional plate of fried vegetables, so stock up on snacks in Darchen’s supermarkets before heading off.
冈仁波齐山上的供应仅限于方便面、啤酒和偶尔的一盘油炸蔬菜，所以在出发前在达真的超市里储备零食。

Sacred Lake Manasarovar (Mapham Yum-tso, or Victorious Lake, in Tibetan; Mǎpáng Xióngcuò, in Chinese) is the most venerated of Tibet’s many lakes and perhaps its most beautiful. With its sapphire-blue waters, sandy shoreline and snow-capped-mountain backdrop, Manasarovar is immediately appealing, and a contrast to the often forbidding terrain of Mt Kailash.
圣湖玛纳萨罗瓦尔（藏语：Mapham Yum-tso，或胜利湖;Mǎpáng Xióngcuò）是西藏众多湖泊中最受尊敬的，也许也是最美丽的湖泊。马纳萨罗瓦尔拥有宝蓝色的海水、沙滩海岸线和白雪皑皑的山脉背景，立即具有吸引力，与冈仁波齐山通常令人生畏的地形形成鲜明对比。

Most visitors base themselves at picturesque Chiu village, site of Chiu Monastery, on the northwestern shore of the lake. Indian pilgrims often drive around the lake, immersing themselves in the sacred waters at some point. You’ll also see Tibetan pilgrims walking the four-day kora path around the lake, or inching round on a 40-day prostration.
大多数游客将自己定营在湖西北岸风景如画的 Chiu 村，即 Chiu 寺的遗址。印度朝圣者经常开车绕湖而行，有时沉浸在圣水中。您还会看到西藏朝圣者在环湖四天的科拉小径上行走，或者在为期 40 天的跪拜中慢慢绕行。

If you have the time, it’s worthwhile travelling around the eastern side of the lake, visiting Seralung and Trugo monasteries en route, and preferably overnighting. It’s an easy day slotted in between Hor Qu and Darchen.
如果您有时间，值得在湖的东侧旅行，途中参观 Seralung 和 Trugo 修道院，最好过夜。这是在 Hor Qu 和 Darchen 之间轻松的一天。

The lake area has a one-time admission fee of ¥150 per person (the Mt Kailash fee does not cover this).
湖区的一次性入场费为每人 150 日元（冈仁波齐山费用不包括在内）。

There are over a dozen simple guesthouses at Chiu, between the monastery and the lake, with four- or five-bed rooms all costing around ¥65 per bed per night, and sharing a single set of outdoor pit toilets. There’s not much to choose between them except perhaps the availability of food and where the big groups are staying. Most are only open May to October.
Chiu 有十几间简易宾馆，位于修道院和湖边之间，有四床或五床房，每床每晚 65 日元左右，共用一套室外坑式厕所。除了食物的供应和大团体的住宿地点之外，他们之间没有太多选择。大多数只在 5 月至 10 月开放。

Chiu, at the northwestern corner of the lake, is 15km south of Barkha junction, from where it is 22km west to Darchen or 22km east to Hor Qu.
Chiu 位于湖的西北角，位于 Barkha 交汇处以南 15 公里处，从那里向西 22 公里到达 Darchen，向东 22 公里到达 Hor Qu。

The eastern shore of the lake is accessible from Hor Qu, from where a dirt road continues all the way around the lake to Chiu. From Hor Qu it’s 8km to Seralung Monastery and a further 24km to Trugo. From Trugo a second dirt road cuts west to join the main paved road south to Purang.
湖的东岸可从 Hor Qu 到达，从那里有一条土路一直绕湖到达 Chiu。从河区出发，距离实拉隆寺 8 公里，再行驶 24 公里即可到达特鲁戈。从特鲁戈出发，第二条土路向西切入，与向南通往普朗的主要铺砌道路汇合。
