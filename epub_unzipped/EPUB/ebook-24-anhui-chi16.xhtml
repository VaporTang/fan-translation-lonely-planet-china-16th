Why Go?
为什么要去？

Fantastical mountainscapes and well preserved villages make Anhui (安徽, Ānhuī) the perfect antidote to the brashness of China’s larger cities. The main attraction is unquestionably Huangshan, a jumble of sheer granite cliffs wrapped in cottony clouds that inspired an entire school of ink painting during the 17th and 18th centuries. But the often-overlooked peaks of nearby Jiuhua Shan, where Buddhists bless the souls of the recently departed, have a hallowed aura that offers a strong contrast to Huangshan’s stunning natural scenery.
梦幻般的山景和保存完好的村庄使安徽成为中国大城市傲慢的完美解药。毫无疑问，主要景点是黄山，这是一片被棉花云包裹的陡峭花岗岩悬崖的杂乱，激发了 17 世纪和 18 世纪整个水墨画流派的灵感。但附近九华山经常被忽视的山峰，佛教徒在那里为新逝去的人的灵魂加持，它们有一种神圣的光环，与黄山令人惊叹的自然风光形成鲜明对比。

At the foot of these ranges are strewn the ancient villages of the province formerly known as Huizhou. With distinctive whitewashed walls and black-tiled roofs augmented by lush surroundings of buckling earth, bamboo and pine forest, they are among the most picturesque in the country.
在这些山脉的脚下散布着该省的古村落，以前称为惠州。它们拥有独特的粉刷成白色的墙壁和黑瓦屋顶，辅以郁郁葱葱的泥土、竹子和松树林，是该国最美丽的地方之一。

Anhui’s top sights are clustered in its southeast corner. Easy to navigate and within striking distance of Shanghai, this is rural China at its accessible best.
安徽的顶级景点集中在其东南角。交通便利，距离上海仅几步之遥，这里是中国农村最畅通无阻的地方。

History
历史

The provincial borders of Anhui were defined by the Qing government, bringing together two highly disparate geographic regions and cultures: the arid, densely populated North China Plain and the mountainous terrain south of the Yangzi River (Cháng Jiāng). The region has a long, long history, with excavation sites in the Yangzi River basin turning up some of the oldest evidence of human settlement in Eurasia. During the Three Kingdoms Period many battles were fought here (and warlord Cao Cao was a native son).
清政府划定了安徽省界，汇集了两个截然不同的地理区域和文化：干旱、人口稠密的华北平原和扬子江以南的山区（长江）。该地区有着悠久的历史，扬子江流域的发掘遗址发现了欧亚大陆一些最古老的人类定居证据。三国时期，这里打过很多仗（军阀曹是土生土长的儿子）。

Impoverished for much of history and today a primary source of China’s hardworking army of āyí (nannies), rural Anhui’s fortunes have begun to reverse. Some say the massive infrastructure improvements in the hitherto remote areas are partly due to former president Hu Jintao, whose ancestral clan hails from Jixi County. Hu comes from a long line of Huizhou merchants, who for centuries left home to do business or fill official posts elsewhere, but would never fail to complete their filial duty and send their profits back home (much of it by way of large homes and ceremonial structures).
安徽农村在历史上的大部分时间里都处于贫困状态，如今是中国辛勤工作的保姆军队的主要来源，其命运已经开始逆转。有人说，迄今为止偏远地区的大规模基础设施改善部分归功于前国家主席胡锦涛，他的祖宗来自鸡西县。胡出身于徽州商人世系，几个世纪以来，他们离开家去其他地方经商或担任官职，但总是不尽孝，将利润汇回国内（其中大部分是通过大房子和礼仪结构）。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

The historic and tourist sights of Anhui gather in the south around the town of Tunxi (also known as Huangshan Shì), which has an airport and high-speed train station. High-speed trains connect Tunxi with Shanghai, Beijing, Xi’an, Nanjing, Hangzhou, Fuzhou, Changsha and other cities, departing from and arriving at Huangshan North Train Station, 18km north of town.
安徽的历史和旅游景点聚集在南部的屯溪镇（又称黄山市）周围，该镇设有机场和高铁站。高铁连接屯溪与上海、北京、习安、南京、杭州、福州、长沙等城市，出发和到达城北18公里处的黄山北站。

Within mountainous Anhui, buses are plentiful and the best way to get around. However, as the main sights are clustered in one region, hired taxis are also an option for groups.
在安徽山区，公共汽车很多，是出行的最佳方式。然而，由于主要景点集中在一个地区，租用的出租车也是团体的一种选择。

Qiyan Shan 齐云山

Qiyun Shan (Qíyún Shān; Mar-Nov ¥75, Dec-Feb ¥55; h8am-5pm Mon-Fri, 7.30am-5.30pm Sat & Sun; p) means ‘mountain as high as the clouds’ and it’s an apt description: though not actually that high (just 585m) its peaks do pierce the low-lying, ghostly puffs of mist that regularly envelop the region. Long venerated by Taoists, the reddish sandstone rock provides a mountain home to temples, many built into the mountain itself, and the monks who tend to them. It’s a 45-minute bus trip west of Tunxi.
齐云山（Qíyún Shān;3-11 月 75 日元，12-2 月 55 日元; h 周一至周五上午 8 点至下午 5 点，周六和周日上午 7：30 至下午 5：30; p ）的意思是“像云一样高的山”，这是一个恰当的描述：虽然实际上没有那么高（只有 585 米），但它的山峰确实刺穿了经常笼罩该地区的低洼、幽灵般的薄雾。长期以来，这块微红色的砂岩岩受到道教徒的崇敬，为寺庙提供了山上的家园，许多寺庙都建在山上，以及照料寺庙的僧侣。从屯溪以西乘坐巴士 45 分钟即可到达。

Most tour groups get dropped off at a back entrance, so if you arrive by public bus you’ll likely be on your own for the 75-minute climb up stone steps to the ticket office. Just beyond, Zhenxian Cave (真仙洞府, Zhēnxiān Dòngfǔ) houses a complex of Taoist shrines in grottoes and niches gouged from the sandstone cliffs. The Unesco World Heritage Site of Hongcun (Hóngcūn; ¥104; open 7am to 5.30pm), is the most-visited and best-known of the Huizhou villages. When its granite peaks and twisted pines are wreathed in spectral folds of mist, the idyllic views of Huangshan (Huángshān; literally ‘Yellow Mountain’) easily nudge it into the select company of China’s top 10 – nay, top five – sights. Climate

Locals claim that it rains more than 200 days a year up on the mountain. Allow yourself several days and climb when the forecast is best. Jiuhua Shan 九华山

% 0566

The Tang dynasty Buddhists who determined Jiuhua Shan to be the earthly abode of the Bodhisattva Dizang (Ksitigarbha), Lord of the Underworld, chose well. Often shrouded in an other-worldly fog that pours in through the windows of its cliff-side temples, Jiuhua Shan (Jiǔhuá Mountain; Nine Flower Mountain; Feb-Nov ¥160, Dec-Jan ¥140) has a powerful gravitas, heightened by the devotion of those who come here to pray for the souls of the departed. It is one of China’s four most sacred Buddhist peaks and there are dozens of active temples here, housing a population of some 500-plus monks and nuns.
唐代佛教徒将九华山确定为地藏菩萨（地藏）的尘世居所，他们选择得很好。九华山 （Jiǔhuá Mountain;九花山;2 月至 11 月 160 日元，12 月至 1 月 140 日元） 具有强大的庄严感，前来这里为逝者的灵魂祈祷的人们的奉献精神更加强烈。它是中国最神圣的佛教四峰之一，这里有数十座活跃的寺庙，居住着大约 500 多名僧尼。

The mountain is not untouched by commercialism, however; the hawkers of overpriced joss sticks and jade carvings come together with the ochre-coloured monasteries, flickering candles and the low, steady drone of Buddhist chanting emanating from pilgrims’ smartphones to concoct an atmosphere both of this world and of another one entirely.
然而，这座山并非没有受到商业主义的影响。售卖价格过高的香烛和玉雕的小贩与赭色的寺院、闪烁的蜡烛和朝圣者智能手机发出的低沉而稳定的佛教诵经声聚集在一起，营造出一种这个世界和另一个世界的氛围。

History
历史

Jiuhua Shan was made famous by the 8th-century Korean monk Kim Kiao Kak (Jin Qiaojue), who meditated here for 75 years and was posthumously proclaimed to be the reincarnation of Dizang. In temples, Dizang is generally depicted carrying a staff and a luminous jewel, used to guide souls through the darkness of hell.
九华山因8世纪的韩国僧人金翘角（金巧觉）而出名，他在这里打坐了75年，被追封为地藏的转世。在寺庙中，地藏通常被描绘成手持一根手杖和一颗发光的宝石，用于引导灵魂穿越地狱的黑暗。

(九华山巅, Jiǔhuá Shān Diān)

Hiking the summit of Jiuhua Mountain alongside pilgrims following a stone trail (天台正顶, Tiāntái Zhèng Dǐng) shaded by pines and bamboo is the highlight of a journey to the mountain. To begin the full-day hike from the village, walk up Furong Lu for 450m and look for a sign on your left pointing up to Huixiang Pavilion (回香阁, Huíxiāng Gé).
与朝圣者一起沿着松树和竹子遮荫的石板路 （ 天台正顶，Tiāntái Zhèng Dǐng）徒步登上九华山山顶，是登山之旅的亮点。要从村庄开始全日徒步旅行，请沿着芙蓉路步行 450 米，然后在左侧寻找指向回香阁（回香阁 ，Huíxiāng Gé）的标志。

From here to the summit takes four to five hours; count on about two to three hours to get back down to the village. Follow the path along the front of Tonghui Nunnery (通慧庵, Tōnghuì Ān) as the gentle incline transitions to steep stone steps. The 750m trek up to Huíxiāng Pavilion takes around 20 minutes. Above you reach the towering seven-storey 10,000 Buddha Pagoda (万佛塔, Wàn Fó Tǎ), fashioned entirely from bronze and prettily lit at night. Continuing along, the path dips into a pleasant valley, passing Roushen Temple (肉身寺, Ròushén Sì) and Welcoming Guest Pine (迎客松, Yíngkè Sōng) en route to Phoenix Pine (凤凰松, Fènghuáng Sōng). The summit is slightly damp, with mist shrouding the area.
