Religion & Philosophy
宗教与哲学

Ideas have always possessed an extraordinary potency and vitality in China. The 19th-century Taiping Rebellion fused Christianity with revolutionary principles of social organisation, almost sweeping away the Qing dynasty in the process and leaving 20 million dead. The momentary incandescence of the Boxer Rebellion drew upon a volatile cocktail of martial-arts practices and superstition, blended with xenophobia, while the chaos of the Cultural Revolution further illustrates what can happen in China when ideas assume the full supremacy they seek.
思想在中国一直具有非凡的力量和生命力。19世纪的太平天国运动将基督教与革命的社会组织原则融合在一起，在这个过程中几乎席卷了清朝，造成2000万人死亡。义和团运动的短暂炽热借鑒了武术习俗和迷信的不稳定混合物，混合了仇外心理，而文化大革命的混乱则进一步说明了当思想占据他们所寻求的完全至高无上的地位时，中国会发生什么。

Religion Today
今日的宗教

China has always had a pluralistic religious culture, and though statistics in China are a slippery fish, an estimated 400 million Chinese today adhere to a particular faith, in varying degrees of devotion. The Chinese Communist Party (CCP) made strident efforts after 1949 to supplant religious worship with the secular philosophy of communism, but since the abandonment of principles of Marxist-Leninist collectivism, this policy has significantly waned.
中国一直拥有多元化的宗教文化，尽管中国的统计数据是一条滑溜溜的鱼，但据估计，今天有 4 亿中国人以不同程度的虔诚信奉一种特定的信仰。1949 年后，中国共产党（CCP）做出了艰苦的努力，以共产主义的世俗哲学取代宗教崇拜，但自从放弃马克思列宁主义集体主义的原则以来，这一政策已经明显减弱。

Religion in China is enjoying an upswing as people return to faith for spiritual solace at a time of great change, dislocation and uncertainty. The hopeless, poor and destitute may turn to worship as they feel abandoned by communism and the safety nets it once provided. Yet the educated and prosperous are similarly turning to religious belief for a sense of guidance and direction in a land many Chinese suspect has become morally bereft.
在中国，随着人们在巨大变化、混乱和不确定的时代回归信仰以寻求精神慰藉，中国的宗教正在蓬勃发展。绝望、贫穷和贫困的人可能会转向敬拜，因为他们感到被共产主义及其曾经提供的安全网抛弃了。然而，受过教育的富裕人士同样转向宗教信仰，以寻求在许多中国人怀疑这片土地上已经道德败坏的土地上的指导和方向感。

Religious belief in China was traditionally marked by tolerance. Though the faiths are quite distinct, some convergence exists between Buddhism, Taoism and Confucianism, and you may discover shrines where all three faiths are worshipped. Guanyin, the Buddhist Goddess of Mercy, finds her equivalent in Tianhou (Mazu), the Taoist goddess and protector of fisherfolk, and the two goddesses can seem almost interchangeable. Other symbioses exist: elements of Taoism and Buddhism can be discerned in the thinking of some Chinese Christians, while the Virgin Mary finds a familiar toehold in the Chinese psyche owing to her resemblance, in bearing and sympathetic message, to Guanyin.
中国的宗教信仰传统上以宽容为标志。尽管信仰截然不同，但佛教、道教和儒家之间存在一些融合，您可能会发现供奉这三种信仰的神社。佛教观音菩萨在道教女神和渔民保护神天后（妈祖）身上找到了她的等价物，这两位女神似乎几乎可以互换。其他共生关系也存在：道教和佛教的元素可以在一些中国基督徒的思想中辨别出来，而圣母玛利亚在中国人的心灵中找到了熟悉的立足点，因为她在带领和同情的信息上与观音相似。

The CCP today remains deeply fearful of ideas and beliefs that challenge its authority. Proselytising is not permitted and religious organisation is regulated and monitored, while organisations such as Falun Gong (a quasi-Buddhist health system) can be deemed cults and banned outright. The mass internment of up to several million Uyghurs and other Muslim minorities in northwest China for re-education in what Beijing terms ‘correct thought’ is evidence of a hardening attitude to devotion among Muslims. Alongside this mass internment, a number of mosques have been razed and signs of religious conservatism (eg long beards and the wearing of veils in public) have been banned, while the region of Xinjiang has effectively been turned into a police state, with endless security checks and high-tech surveillance creating an Orwellian society.
今天，中共仍然对挑战其权威的思想和信仰深感恐惧。不允许传教，宗教组织受到监管和监控，而法轮功（准佛教卫生系统）等组织可能被视为邪教并被彻底取缔。在中国西北地区，多达数百万维吾尔人和其他穆斯林少数民族被大规模拘留，接受北京所说的“正确思想”的再教育，这证明了穆斯林对虔诚的态度越来越强硬。除了大规模拘留之外，一些清真寺被夷为平地，宗教保守主义的标志（例如长胡子和在公共场合戴面纱）被禁止，而新疆地区实际上已经变成了一个警察国家，无休止的安全检查和高科技监控创造了一个奥威尔式的社会。

FALUN GONG
功

Falun Gong – a practice that merges elements of qigong-style regulated breathing and standing exercises with Buddhist teachings, fashioning a quasi-religious creed in the process – literally means ‘Practice of the Dharma Wheel’. Riding a wave of interest in qigong systems in the 1990s, Falun Gong claimed as many as 100 million adherents in China by 1999. The technique was banned in the same year after more than 10,000 practitioners stood in silent demonstration outside Zhongnanhai in Beijing, following protests in Tianjin when a local magazine published an article critical of Falun Gong. The authorities had been unnerved by the movement’s audacity and organisational depth, construing Falun Gong as a threat to the primacy of the Chinese Communist Party. The movement was branded a cult (邪教, xiéjiào) and a robust, media-wide propaganda campaign was launched against practitioners, forcing many to undergo ‘re-education’ in prison and labour camps. After the ban the authorities treated Falun Gong believers harshly and reports surfaced of adherents dying in custody. Accusations of torture also persist, while several reports have pointed to industrial-scale harvesting of organs of Falun Gong prisoners, including David Matas and David Kilgour’s 2009 book Bloody Harvest: Organ Harvesting of Falun Gong Practitioners in China. The Chinese government denies the charges. Falun Gong remains an outlawed movement in China to this day.
法轮功——一种将气功式的调节呼吸和站立练习元素与佛教教义相结合的修炼，在此过程中形成一种准宗教信条——字面意思是“法轮修行”。乘着 1990 年代对气功系统的兴趣浪潮，到 1999 年，法轮功在中国拥有多达 1 亿信徒。同年，在天津一家当地杂志发表批评法轮功的文章后，10,000 多名学员在北京中南海外无声示威，该技术被禁止。当局对该运动的大胆和组织深度感到不安，将法轮功解释为对中国共产党至高无上的威胁。该运动被贴上邪教（ 邪教 ，xiéjiào）的标签，并在媒体范围内对修炼者发起了强有力的宣传运动，迫使许多人在监狱和劳改营接受“再教育”。禁令颁布后，当局严厉对待法轮功信徒，并有信徒在拘留期间死亡的报道浮出水面。对酷刑的指控也持续存在，而一些报告指出了对法轮功囚犯器官的工业规模摘取，包括大卫·马塔斯和大卫·基尔古尔 2009 年出版的《 血腥收获：中国法轮功学员的器官摘取 》一书。中国政府否认这些指控。直到今天，法轮功在中国仍然是一个非法的运动。

Buddhism
佛教

Although not an indigenous faith, Buddhism (佛教, Fójiào) is the religion most deeply associated with China and Tibet. The religion has seen periods of growth, decline, persecution and grand resurgence through China’s history, and while Buddhism’s authority may have long ebbed, the faith still exercises a powerful sway over China’s spiritual inclinations. Many Chinese may not be regular temple-goers but they may harbour an interest in and an affiliation with Buddhism – they could be described as being ‘cultural Buddhists’, with a strong affection for Buddhist civilisation.
佛教（ 佛教 ，Fójiào）虽然不是本土信仰，但它是与中国和西藏联系最深的宗教。在中国的历史上，佛教经历了发展、衰落、迫害和大复兴的时期，虽然佛教的权威可能早已衰落，但这种信仰仍然对中国的精神倾向产生强大的影响。许多中国人可能不是经常去寺庙的人，但他们可能对佛教有兴趣和联系——他们可以被描述为“文化佛教徒”，对佛教文明有着强烈的感情。

Chinese towns with any history to their name usually have several Buddhist temples, but the number is well down on pre-1949 figures. The small Hebei town of Zhengding, for example, has four Buddhist temples, but at one time had eight. Beijing once had hundreds, compared to the 20 or so you can find today.
有任何历史的中国城镇通常有几座佛教寺庙，但数量远低于 1949 年之前的数字。例如，河北正定小镇有四座佛寺，但一度有八座。北京曾经有数百人，而今天你可以找到的 20 人左右。

A large number of Buddhist temples are also active monasteries; others may also provide accommodation. Some temples are free to enter, while for others there is an admission fee, especially if the temple is famed for a particular statue, pagoda or temple hall. You may discover pagodas standing alone in the middle of nowhere; these are almost always the remains of vanished temples, as pagodas were traditionally built within temple grounds.
大量佛教寺庙也是活跃的寺院;其他人也可能提供住宿。有些寺庙是免费进入的，而另一些寺庙则需要支付入场费，特别是如果寺庙以特定的雕像、宝塔或寺庙大厅而闻名。您可能会发现宝塔孤零零地矗立在偏僻的地方;这些几乎总是消失的寺庙的遗迹，因为宝塔传统上建在寺庙内。

Some of China’s greatest surviving artistic achievements are Buddhist in inspiration. The largest and most ancient repository of Chinese, Central Asian and Tibetan Buddhist artwork can be found at the Mogao Grottoes in Gansu, while the carved Buddhist caves at both Longmen and Yungang are spectacular pieces of religious and creative heritage. To witness Buddhism at its most devout, consider a trip to Tibet, where the faith permeates local life to a profound degree.
中国现存的一些最伟大的艺术成就是佛教的灵感。甘肃莫高窟是中国、中亚和藏传佛教艺术品的最大、最古老的宝库，而龙门和云冈的雕刻佛教石窟则是壮观的宗教和创意遗产。要见证佛教最虔诚的一面，可以考虑去西藏旅行，那里的信仰深刻地渗透到当地生活中。

Anyone interested in Tibetan Buddhism will find Inner Mongolia easier to reach than Tibet. The province is home to many important and historic lamaseries, including Dà Zhào in Hohhot, Wudang Lamasery and Guǎngzōng Sì.
任何对藏传佛教感兴趣的人都会发现内蒙古比西藏更容易到达。该省拥有许多重要且历史悠久的喇嘛教派，包括呼和浩特的大昭喇嘛寺、武当喇嘛寺和广宗寺。

Origins
起源

Founded in ancient India around the 5th century BC, Buddhism teaches that all of life is suffering, and that the cause of this anguish is desire, itself rooted in sensation and attachment. Suffering can only be overcome by following the eightfold path, a set of guidelines for moral behaviour, meditation and wisdom. Those who have freed themselves from suffering and the wheel of rebirth are said to have attained nirvana or enlightenment. The term Buddha generally refers to the historical founder of Buddhism, Siddhartha Gautama, but is also sometimes used to denote those who have achieved enlightenment.
佛教于公元前 5 世纪左右起源于古印度，教导说所有生命都是痛苦，而这种痛苦的原因是欲望，其本身植根于感觉和执着。只有遵循八正道才能克服痛苦，八正道是一套道德行为、禅修和智慧的准则。那些从痛苦和轮回之轮中解脱出来的人据说已经获得了涅槃或觉悟。佛陀一词通常指佛教的历史创始人乔达摩悉达多，但有时也用来表示那些已经开悟的人。

Siddhartha Gautama left no writings; the sutras that make up the Buddhist canon were compiled many years after his death.
乔达摩悉达多没有留下任何著作;构成佛教经典的佛经是在他去世多年后编纂的。

Buddhism in China
中国佛教

Like other faiths such as Christianity, Nestorianism, Islam and Judaism, Buddhism originally reached China via the Silk Road. The earliest recorded Buddhist temple in China proper dates back to the 1st century AD, but it was not until the 4th century that Buddhism gained mass appeal. Buddhism’s sudden growth during this period is often attributed to its sophisticated ideas concerning the afterlife (such as karma and reincarnation), a dimension unaddressed by either Confucianism or Taoism. At a time when existence was especially precarious, spiritual transcendence was understandably popular.
与基督教、景教、伊斯兰教和犹太教等其他信仰一样，佛教最初是通过丝绸之路传入中国的。中国最早有记载的佛教寺庙可以追溯到公元1世纪，但直到4世纪，佛教才获得了大众的欢迎。佛教在这一时期的突然发展通常归因于其关于来世的复杂观念（例如业力和轮回），这是儒家或道家都无法解决的维度。在存在特别不稳定的时代，精神超越的流行是可以理解的。

As Buddhism converged with Taoist philosophy (through terminology used in translation) and popular religion (through practice), it went on to develop into something distinct from the original Indian tradition. The most famous example is the esoteric Chan school (Zen in Japanese), which originated sometime in the 5th or 6th century and focused on attaining enlightenment through meditation. Chan was novel not only in its unorthodox teaching methods, but also because it made enlightenment possible for laypeople outside the monastic system. It rose to prominence during the Tang and Song dynasties, after which the centre of practice moved to Japan.
随着佛教与道教哲学（通过翻译中使用的术语）和流行宗教（通过实践）融合，它继续发展成为与原始印度传统截然不同的东西。最著名的例子是深奥的禅宗（日语中的禅宗），它起源于 5 世纪或 6 世纪的某个时候，专注于通过冥想获得启蒙。禅宗之所以新颖，不仅在于其非正统的教学方法，还因为它使僧侣系统之外的居士能够开悟。它在唐宋时期声名鹊起，之后实践中心转移到日本。

Beyond Tibet, China has four sacred Buddhist mountains, each one the home of a specific Bodhisattva. The two most famous mountains are Wutai Shan and Emei Shan, respectively ruled over by Wenshu and Puxiang.
除了西藏之外，中国还有四座佛教圣山，每座山都是特定菩萨的家园。最著名的两座山是五台山和峨眉山，分别由文殊和浦乡统治。

Buddhist Schools
佛教学校

Regardless of its various forms, most Buddhism in China belongs to the Mahayana school, which holds that since all existence is one, the fate of the individual is linked to the fate of others. Thus Bodhisattvas – those who have already achieved enlightenment but have chosen to remain on earth – continue to work for the liberation of all other sentient beings. The most popular Bodhisattva in China is Guanyin, the Goddess of Mercy.
无论形式如何，中国的大多数佛教都属于大乘学派，大乘学派认为，既然万物为一，个人的命运就与他人的命运息息相关。因此，菩萨——那些已经证悟但选择留在人间的人——继续为所有其他众生的解脱而工作。中国最受欢迎的菩萨是观音观音，观音菩萨。

Ethnic Tibetans and Mongols within China practise a unique form of Mahayana Buddhism known as Tibetan or Tantric Buddhism (Lǎma Jiào). Tibetan Buddhism, sometimes called Vajrayana or ‘Thunderbolt Vehicle’, has been practised since the early 7th century AD and is influenced by Tibet’s pre-Buddhist Bon religion, which relied on priests or shamans to placate spirits, gods and demons. Generally speaking, the faith is much more mystical than other forms of Buddhism, relying heavily on mudras (ritual postures), mantras (sacred speech), yantras (sacred art) and esoteric initiation rites. Priests called lamas are believed to be reincarnations of highly evolved beings – the Dalai Lama is the supreme patriarch of Tibetan Buddhism.
中国境内的藏族和蒙古族信奉一种独特的大乘佛教形式，称为藏传佛教或密宗佛教（Lǎma jiào）。藏传佛教，有时被称为金刚乘或“霹雳车”，自公元 7 世纪初开始信奉，并受到西藏前佛教苯教的影响，该宗教依靠僧侣或萨满来安抚鬼魂、神灵和恶魔。一般来说，这种信仰比其他形式的佛教更加神秘，严重依赖手印（仪式姿势）、咒语（神圣的言语）、yantras（神圣的艺术）和深奥的灌顶仪式。被称为喇嘛的僧侣被认为是高度进化的众生的转世——达赖喇嘛是藏传佛教的最高祖师。

GUANYIN
观音

The boundlessly compassionate countenance of Guanyin (观音), the Buddhist Goddess of Mercy, can be encountered in temples across China. The goddess (more strictly a Bodhisattva or a Buddha-to-be) goes under a variety of aliases: Guanshiyin (观世音, literally ‘Observing the Cries of the World’) is her formal name, but she is also called Guanzizai, Guanyin Dashi and Guanyin Pusa, or, in Sanskrit, Avalokiteshvara. Known as Kannon in Japan, Guanyam in Cantonese and Quan Am in Vietnam, Guanyin shoulders the grief of the world and dispenses mercy and compassion. Christians will note a semblance to the Virgin Mary in the aura surrounding the goddess, which at least partially explains why Catholicism (and Christianity in general) found a slot in the Chinese consciousness.
佛教观音（观音 ）无限慈悲的面容，在中国各地的寺庙中都可以遇到。这位女神（更严格地说是菩萨或准佛）有多种别名：观世音（ 观世音 ，字面意思是“观察世界的哭声”）是她的正式名称，但她也被称为观子斋、观音大石和观音菩萨，或者梵语为观世音菩萨。观音在日本被称为观音，在粤语中被称为观音，在越南被称为全岩，观音肩负着世界的悲痛，并施以慈悲和慈悲。基督徒会注意到女神周围的光环与圣母玛利亚的相似之处，这至少部分解释了为什么天主教（以及整个基督教）在中国人的意识中找到了一席之地。

In Tibetan Buddhism her earthly presence manifests itself in the Dalai Lama, and her home is the Potala Palace in Lhasa. In China her abode is the island of Putuoshan in Zhejiang province, the first two syllables of which derive from the name of her palace in Lhasa.
在藏传佛教中，她的尘世存在体现在达赖喇嘛身上，她的家是拉萨的布达拉宫。在中国，她的住所是浙江省的普陀山岛，前两个音节来源于她在拉萨的宫殿名称。

In temples throughout China, Guanyin is often found at the very rear of the main hall, facing north (most of the other divinities, apart from Weituo, face south). She typically has her own little shrine and stands on the head of a big fish, holding a lotus in her hand. On other occasions she has her own hall, often towards the rear of the temple.
在中国各地的寺庙中，观音经常出现在正殿的最后方，朝北（除威陀外，大多数其他神灵都朝南）。她通常有自己的小神龛，站在一条大鱼的头上，手里拿着一朵莲花。在其他情况下，她有自己的大厅，通常朝向寺庙的后方。

Taoism
道教

A home-grown philosophy-cum-religion, Taoism (道教, Dàojiào) is also perhaps the hardest of all China’s faiths to penetrate and grasp. Controversial, paradoxical and – like the Tao itself – impossible to pin down, it is a natural counterpoint to rigid Confucianist order and responsibility.
道教（ 道教 ，Dàojiào）是一种本土哲学兼宗教，也许也是中国所有信仰中最难渗透和掌握的。它有争议、自相矛盾，而且——就像道本身一样——无法确定，它与僵化的儒家秩序和责任形成了自然的对比。

Taoism predates Buddhism in China and much of its religious culture connects to a distant animism and shamanism, despite the purity of its philosophical school. In its earliest and simplest form, Taoism draws from The Classic of the Way and Its Power (Taote Jing; Dàodé Jìng), penned by the sagacious Laotzu (Laozi, c 580–500 BC), who left his writings with the gatekeeper of a pass as he headed west on the back of an ox. Some Chinese believe his wanderings took him to a distant land in the west where he became Buddha.
道教早于中国的佛教，尽管其哲学流派是纯洁的，但其大部分宗教文化都与遥远的万物有灵论和萨满教有关。道教最早和最简单的形式借鉴了 《道经及其力量》（Taote Jing;Dàodé Jìng），由睿智的老子（老子，约公元前 580-500 年）撰写，他在骑着牛背向西行驶时将他的著作留给了关口的守门人。一些中国人认为，他的流浪将他带到了西方的一个遥远的国度，在那里他成佛了。

The Classic of the Way and Its Power is a work of astonishing insight and sublime beauty. Devoid of a godlike being or deity, Laotzu’s writings instead endeavour to address the unknowable and indescribable principle of the universe, which he calls Dao (道, dào, ‘the Way’). Dao is the way or method by which the universe operates, so it can be understood to be a universal or cosmic principle.
《 道的经典及其力量 》是一部具有惊人洞察力和崇高之美的作品。老子的著作没有神一样的存在或神灵，而是努力解决宇宙的不可知和难以描述的原理，他称之为道（ 道 ，dào，“道”）。道是宇宙运作的方式或方法，因此可以理解为普遍或宇宙原理。

The 5000-character book, completed in terse classical Chinese, somehow communicates the nebulous power and authority of ‘the Way’. The book remains the seminal text of Taoism, and Taoist purists see little need to look beyond its revelations.
这本 5000 字的书以简洁的文言文完成，以某种方式传达了“道”的模糊力量和权威。这本书仍然是道教的开创性文本，道教纯粹主义者认为没有必要超越它的启示。

One of Taoism’s most beguiling precepts, wúwéi (inaction) champions the allowing of things to naturally occur without interference. The principle is enthusiastically pursued by students of Taiji Quan, Wuji Quan and other soft martial arts who seek to equal nothingness in their bid to lead an opponent to defeat himself.
作为道教最迷人的戒律之一， 无为 （无为）倡导允许事物自然发生而不受干扰。太极拳、无极拳和其他软武的学生热衷于追求这一原则，他们寻求等于虚无，以带领对手击败自己。

China’s oldest surviving Buddhist temple is the White Horse Temple in Luoyang. Other more ancient Buddhist temples may well have existed but have since vanished.
中国现存最古老的佛教寺庙是洛阳的白马寺。其他更古老的佛教寺庙很可能曾经存在过，但后来消失了。

Confucianism
儒学

The very core of Chinese society for the past two millennia, Confucianism (儒家思想, Rújiā Sīxiǎng) is a humanist philosophy that strives for social harmony and the common good. In China its influence can be seen in everything from the emphasis on education and respect for elders to the patriarchal role of the government.
儒家思想（儒家思想 ，Rújiā Sīxiǎng）是过去两千年中国社会的核心，是一种追求社会和谐和共同利益的人文主义哲学。在中国，它的影响体现在从强调教育、尊重长辈到政府的重男轻女角色等方方面面。

Confucianism is based upon the teachings of Confucius (Kongzi; p237), a 6th-century BC philosopher who lived during a period of constant warfare and social upheaval. While Confucianism changed considerably throughout the centuries, some of the principal ideas remained the same – namely an emphasis on five basic hierarchical relationships: father-son, ruler-subject, husband-wife, elder-younger and friend-friend. Confucius believed that if each individual carried out his or her proper role in society (a son served his father respectfully while a father provided for his son, a subject served his ruler respectfully while a ruler provided for his subject, and so on) social order would be achieved. Confucius’ disciples later gathered his ideas in the form of short aphorisms and conversations, forming the work known as The Analects (论语, Lúnyǔ).
儒家思想以孔子（孔子，第 237 页）的教义为基础，孔子是一位公元前 6 世纪的哲学家，生活在一个不断的战争和社会动荡的时期。虽然儒家思想在几个世纪中发生了很大变化，但一些主要思想保持不变——即强调五种基本的等级关系：父子、统治者、臣民、夫妻、长幼和朋友朋友。孔子认为，如果每个人都在社会中发挥自己的应有作用（儿子恭敬地侍奉父亲，父亲供养儿子，臣民恭敬地侍奉统治者，统治者供养臣民，等等），社会秩序就会实现。孔子的弟子后来以短短的格言和对话的形式收集了他的思想，形成了被称为《 论语 》的著作。

Early Confucian philosophy was further developed by Mencius (Mengzi) and Xunzi, both of whom provided a theoretical and practical foundation for many of Confucius’ moral concepts. In the 2nd century BC, Confucianism became the official ideology of the Han dynasty, thereby gaining mainstream acceptance for the first time. This was of major importance and resulted in the formation of an educated elite that served both the government as bureaucrats and the common people as exemplars of moral action. During the rule of the Tang dynasty an official examination system was created, which, in theory, made the imperial government a true meritocracy. However, this also contributed to an ossification of Confucianism, as the ideology grew increasingly mired in the weight of its own tradition, focusing exclusively on a core set of texts.
早期儒家哲学由孟子（孟子）和荀子进一步发展，他们都为孔子的许多道德观念提供了理论和实践基础。公元前2世纪，儒家思想成为汉朝的官方意识形态，从而首次获得主流接受。这非常重要，并导致形成了一支受过教育的精英，他们既作为官僚为政府服务，又为普通民众服务，成为道德行为的典范。唐朝统治期间，建立了官方考试制度，从理论上讲，这使得帝国政府成为真正的精英制度。然而，这也导致了儒家思想的僵化，因为意识形态越来越深陷于自身传统的重压中，只关注一组核心文本。

Nonetheless, influential figures sporadically reinterpreted the philosophy – in particular Zhu Xi (1130–1200), who brought in elements of Buddhism and Taoism to create Neo Confucianism (Lǐxué or Dàoxué), which remained a dominant social force up until the 1911 revolution toppled the imperial bureaucracy. In the 20th century modernist writers and intellectuals decried Confucian thought as an obstacle to modernisation and Mao further levelled the sage in his denunciation of ‘the Four Olds’. But feudal faults notwithstanding, Confucius’ social ethics have resurfaced in government propaganda, where they lend authority to the leadership’s emphasis on ‘harmony’ (héxié).
尽管如此，有影响力的人物还是偶尔重新诠释了这一哲学——特别是朱习（1130-1200），他引入了佛教和道教的元素，创造了新儒家思想（Lǐxué 或 Dàoxué），直到 1911 年革命推翻了帝国官僚机构，新儒家思想一直是社会的主导力量。在 20 世纪，现代主义作家和知识分子谴责儒家思想是现代化的障碍，毛在谴责“四旧”时进一步平息了这位圣人。但尽管存在封建错误，孔子的社会伦理在政府宣传中重新浮出水面，它们为领导层强调“和谐”（héxié） 提供了权威。

Confucius Institutes around the world aim to promote Chinese language and culture internationally, while simultaneously developing its economic and cultural influences abroad.
世界各地的孔子学院旨在在国际上推广中国语言和文化，同时在国外发展其经济和文化影响力。

Kaifeng in Henan province is home to the largest community of Jews in China. The religious beliefs and customs of Judaism (犹太教, Yóutài Jiào) have died out, yet the descendants of the original Jews still consider themselves Jewish.
河南省开封市是中国最大的犹太人社区的所在地。犹太教（ 犹太教 ，Yóutài Jiào）的宗教信仰和习俗已经消亡，但原始犹太人的后裔仍然认为自己是犹太人。

Christianity
基督教

The explosion of interest in Christianity (基督教, Jīdūjiào) in China over the last decade or two is unprecedented except for the wholesale conversions that accompanied the tumultuous rebellion of the pseudo-Christian Taiping in the 19th century.
过去一二十年来，中国对基督教（ 基督教 ，Jīdūjiào）的兴趣激增，除了 19 世纪伪基督教太平天国的动荡叛乱所伴随的大规模皈依之外，这是前所未有的。

Christianity first arrived in China with the Nestorians, a sect from ancient Persia that split with the Byzantine Church in AD 431 and arrived in China via the Silk Road in the 7th century. A celebrated tablet – the Nestorian Tablet – in Xi’an records their arrival. Much later, in the 16th century, the Jesuits arrived and were popular figures at the imperial court, though they made few converts. Large numbers of Catholic and Protestant missionaries established themselves in China in the 19th century, but the community left after the formation of the People’s Republic of China in 1949.
基督教最早是随着景教传入中国的，景教是古波斯的一个教派，于公元431年与拜占庭教会分裂，7世纪通过丝绸之路传入中国。西习安的一块著名的石碑——景教石碑——记录了他们的到来。很久以后，在 16 世纪，耶稣会士来到这里，成为宫廷中的热门人物，尽管他们很少有皈依者。19世纪，大量天主教和新教传教士在中国定居，但该社区在1949年中华人民共和国成立后离开。

Some estimates point to as many as 100 million Christians in China today. However, the exact population is hard to calculate as many groups – outside the four official Christian organisations – lead a strict underground existence (in what are called ‘house churches’) out of fear of a political clampdown.
据估计，今天中国有多达1亿基督徒。然而，确切的人口很难计算，因为除了四个官方基督教组织之外，许多团体出于对政治镇压的恐惧而过着严格的地下生活（在所谓的“家庭教会”中）。

Churches (教堂, jiàotáng) are not hard to find and most towns will have at least one. Cities such as Shanghai, Beijing, Datong, Taiyuan and Qingdao (and many other large towns) have cathedrals, most of them dating from the 19th and early 20th centuries.
教堂 （ 教堂 ， jiàotáng） 并不难找到，大多数城镇至少有一个。上海、北京、大同、太原和青岛等城市（以及许多其他大城镇）都有大教堂，其中大部分的历史可以追溯到 19 世纪和 20 世纪初。

There is growing evidence of official unease at the spread of Christianity and its association with universal human rights and values, and perennial fears of the infiltration of Western ideas that will compete with the primacy of state dogma. Christmas celebrations are periodically banned in various towns and cities to promote traditional Chinese culture instead. Online Bible sales were banned in 2018 to help limit the circulation of Christian concepts.
越来越多的证据表明，官方对基督教的传播及其与普世人权和价值观的联系感到不安，并且长期担心西方思想的渗透将与国家教条的首要地位相竞争。各个城镇定期禁止圣诞节庆祝活动，以弘扬中国传统文化。2018 年禁止在线圣经销售，以帮助限制基督教概念的传播。

The removal of crosses and spires from churches has also been reported in several provinces in recent years, while others have been demolished, or had surveillance cameras installed inside.
近年来，一些省份也有拆除教堂十字架和尖顶的报道，而其他省份则被拆除，或在里面安装了监控摄像头。

During the Cultural Revolution, many Christian churches around China served as warehouses or factories, a utilitarian function that actually helped preserve many of them. They were gradually rehabilitated in the 1980s.
在文化大革命期间，中国各地的许多基督教教堂都充当了仓库或工厂，这是一种实用的功能，实际上有助于保存其中许多教堂。他们在 1980 年代逐渐平反。

Islam
伊斯兰教

Islam (伊斯兰教, Yīsīlán Jiào) in China dates from the 7th century, when it was first brought to China by Arab and Persian traders along the Silk Road. Later, during the Mongol Yuan dynasty, maritime trade increased, bringing new waves of merchants to China’s coastal regions, particularly the port cities of Guangzhou and Quanzhou. The descendants of these groups, now scattered across the country, gradually integrated into Han culture, and are today distinguished primarily by their religion. In Chinese they are referred to as the Hui.
伊斯兰教（ 伊斯兰教 ，Yīsīlán Jiào）在中国的历史可以追溯到 7 世纪，当时它最初是由丝绸之路沿线的阿拉伯和波斯商人带到中国的。后来，在蒙古元朝时期，海上贸易增加，为中国沿海地区，特别是广州和泉州等港口城市带来了新一波的商人。这些群体的后裔现在分散在全国各地，逐渐融入汉族文化，如今主要以他们的宗教为特色。在中文中，他们被称为回族。

Other Muslim groups include the Uyghurs, Kazakhs, Kyrgyz, Tajiks and Uzbeks, who live principally in the border areas of the northwest. It is estimated that 1.5% to 3% of Chinese today are Muslim.
其他穆斯林群体包括维吾尔人、哈萨克人、吉尔吉斯人、塔吉克人和乌兹别克人，他们主要居住在西北部的边境地区。据估计，今天有1.5%到3%的中国人是穆斯林。

The mass internment of Muslim Uyghurs, Kazakhs, Kyrgyz, Hui and various other ethnic Turkic Muslims in re-education camps since 2014 is further evidence of a hard-line policy against what the state terms ‘Islamist extremism’, though this seems to include any depth of devotion to Islam. The internment camps are reportedly detaining hundreds of thousands of Muslims, though figures of up to three million detainees have been posited. In Xinjiang long beards and the wearing of veils in public places have been banned. Mosques have also been razed as the state seeks to limit their number.
自2014年以来，维吾尔族、哈萨克族、吉尔吉斯族、回族和其他各种突厥族穆斯林被大规模关押在再教育营，这进一步证明了国家对所谓的“伊斯兰极端主义”采取强硬政策，尽管这似乎包括对伊斯兰教的任何深度虔诚。据报道，这些拘留营拘留了数十万穆斯林，但据估计被拘留者人数高达300万。在新疆，禁止留长胡子和在公共场所戴面纱。由于国家试图限制清真寺的数量，清真寺也被夷为平地。

THE CHURCH OF ALMIGHTY GOD
全能神的教会

One byproduct of the recent explosion of interest in Christianity in China and the widespread number of unofficial ‘house churches’ has been the emergence of Christian heresies, with large numbers of devoted followers. Chief among these is the Church of Almighty God (www.holyspiritspeaks.org), which teaches that a Chinese woman named Yang Xiangbin is the second Christ.
最近中国对基督教的兴趣激增，以及非官方的“家庭教会”数量广泛增加，其副产品之一是基督教异端的出现，拥有大量忠实的追随者。其中最主要的是全能神教会（www.holyspiritspeaks.org），该教会教导一位名叫杨香宾的中国妇女是第二位基督。

After being blacklisted in 2000, Yang Xiangbin and the founder of the cult, Zhao Weishan, fled to the US. It was only when some followers killed a 37-year-old woman in a branch of McDonald’s in Shandong, after she refused to give them her mobile-phone number, that the organisation came to greater public attention.
2000年被列入黑名单后，杨向斌和邪教创始人赵维山逃往美国。直到一些追随者在山东麦当劳的一家分店杀害了一名 37 岁的妇女，因为她拒绝向他们提供她的手机号码，该组织才引起了公众的更多关注。

Directly opposed to the Chinese Communist Party (CCP), which it terms the Great Red Dragon, the organisation continues to aggressively recruit adherents in China, though its members live a largely underground and secretive existence.
该组织直接反对中国共产党（CCP），它称之为“大红龙”，继续在中国积极招募追随者，尽管其成员主要生活在地下和秘密中。

Believing he was the son of God and brother of Jesus Christ, Hakka rebel Hong Xiuquan led the bloody and tumultuous pseudo-Christian Taiping Rebellion against the Qing dynasty from 1856 to 1864.
客家叛乱者洪秀全相信自己是上帝的儿子和耶稣基督的兄弟，在 1856 年至 1864 年间领导了血腥而动荡的伪基督教反抗清朝的伪基督教太平天国起义。

Animism
万物有灵论

A small percentage of China’s population is animist, a primordial religious belief akin to shamanism. Animists see the world as a living being, with rocks, trees, mountains and people all containing spirits that need to live in harmony. If this harmony is disrupted, restoration of this balance is attempted by a shaman who is empowered to mediate between the human and spirit world. Animism is most widely believed by minority groups and exists in a multitude of forms, some of which have been influenced by Buddhism and other religions.
中国有一小部分人口是万物有灵论者，这是一种类似于萨满教的原始宗教信仰。万物有灵论者将世界视为一个生物，岩石、树木、山脉和人都包含着需要和谐相处的灵魂。如果这种和谐被破坏，萨满就会试图恢复这种平衡，他有权在人类和精神世界之间进行调解。万物有灵论最受少数群体的广泛信仰，并以多种形式存在，其中一些受到佛教和其他宗教的影响。

Communism & Maoism
共产主义与毛主义

Ironically (or perhaps intentionally) Mao Zedong, while struggling to uproot feudal superstition and religious belief, sprang to godlike status in China via a personality cult. By weakening the power of deities, Mao found himself replacing those very gods his political power had diminished. In the China of today, Mao retains a semi-deified aura.
具有讽刺意味的是（或者也许是故意的），毛泽东在努力根除封建迷信和宗教信仰的同时，通过个人崇拜在中国获得了神一样的地位。通过削弱神灵的力量，毛发现自己取代了那些他的政治权力已经减弱的神灵。在今天的中国，毛保留着一种半神化的光环。

Communism remains the official guiding principle of the CCP. However, young communist aspirants today are far less likely to be ideologues than pragmatists seeking to advance within the party structure.
共产主义仍然是中共的官方指导原则。然而，今天有志于共产主义的年轻人不太可能成为理论家，而不是寻求在党内取得进步的实用主义者。

Chinese communism owes something to Confucianism. Confucius’ philosophy embraces the affairs of man and human society and the relationship between rulers and the ruled, rather than the supernatural world. Establishing a rigid framework for human conduct, the culture of Confucianism was easily requisitioned by communists seeking to establish authority over society.
中国共产主义在某种程度上归功于儒家思想。孔子的哲学包括人与人类社会的事务以及统治者与被统治者之间的关系，而不是超自然世界。儒家文化为人类行为建立了严格的框架，很容易被寻求建立社会权威的共产党人所征用。

With the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1989, Beijing became aware of the dangers of popular power and sought to maintain the coherence and strength of the state. This has meant that the CCP still seeks to impose itself firmly on the consciousness of Chinese people through patriotic education, propaganda, censorship, nationalism and the building of a strong nation.
随着1989年苏联解体，北京意识到了人民权力的危险，并寻求保持国家的一致性和实力。这意味着中共仍然试图通过爱国主义教育、宣传、审查、民族主义和建设强国来坚定地将自己强加于中国人民的意识。

Communism also holds considerable nostalgic value for elderly Chinese who bemoan the erosion of values in modern-day China and pine for the days when they felt more secure and society was more egalitarian. Chairman Mao’s portrait still hangs in abundance across China, from drum towers in Guangxi province to restaurants in Beijing, testament to a generation of Chinese who still revere the communist leader.
共产主义对中国老年人来说也具有相当大的怀旧价值，他们哀叹现代中国价值观的侵蚀，并渴望他们感到更安全、社会更加平等的日子。毛主席的肖像仍然悬挂在中国各地，从广西省的鼓楼到北京的餐馆，这证明了一代中国人仍然崇敬这位共产党领导人。

President Xi Jinping has faced accusations of attempting to build a personality cult, allowing himself to be nicknamed Xi Dada (Big Daddy Xi), a kind of perennially good, sympathetic and paternal figurehead for the nation, with his citizens’ best interests always at heart. The removal of the two-term limit for the Chinese president in 2018 – which Deng Xiaoping warned against decades before – has effectively permitted Xi Jinping to stay on as president indefinitely.
习近平总统面临试图建立个人崇拜的指控，让自己被昵称为习达达（习老爹），他是国家的常年善良、富有同情心和父性的傀儡，始终将公民的最大利益放在心上。2018年取消中国国家主席的两届任期限制——邓小平几十年前就警告过这一限制——实际上让习近平无限期地继续担任国家主席。

An inspiring read, God is Red: The Secret Story of How Christianity Survived and Flourished in Communist China (2011) by Liao Yiwu, himself not a Christian, relates his encounters with Christians in contemporary China, set against a background of persecution and surging growth for the faith.
廖亦武本人不是基督徒，读了一本鼓舞人心的读物，上帝是红色的：基督教如何在共产主义中国生存和繁荣的秘密故事 （2011 年），讲述了他在当代中国与基督徒的相遇，背景是迫害和信仰的激增。

Nationalism
民族主义

In today’s China ‘-isms’ (主义, zhǔyì or ‘doctrines’) are often frowned upon. Any zhǔyì may suggest a personal focus that the CCP would prefer people channel into hard work instead. ‘Intellectualism’ is considered suspect as it may ask difficult questions, and ‘idealism’ is deemed nonpragmatic and potentially destructive, as Maoism showed.
在今天的中国，“主义”（ 主义 、zhǔyì 或“学说”）经常被不受欢迎。任何 zhǔyì 都可能暗示着一种个人关注点，中共更希望人们投入到努力工作中。“理智主义”被认为是可疑的，因为它可能会提出困难的问题，而“理想主义”被认为是非实用的和潜在的破坏性，正如毛主义所表明的那样。

China’s one-party state has reduced thinking across the spectrum through propaganda and censorship, dumbing down an educational system that emphasises patriotic learning. This in turn, however, helps spawn another ‘-ism’: nationalism.
中国的一党专政国家通过宣传和审查制度减少了各种思考，削弱了强调爱国主义学习的教育体系。然而，这反过来又有助于催生另一种“主义”：民族主义。

Nationalism is not restricted to Chinese youth, but it is this generation – with no experience of the Cultural Revolution’s terrifying excesses – that most closely identifies with its message. The fènqīng (angry youth) have been swept along with China’s rise – while they are no lovers of the CCP, they yearn for a stronger China that can stand up to ‘foreign interference’ and dictate its own terms.
民族主义不仅限于中国年轻人，但这一代人——没有经历过文化大革命的可怕过激行为——最能认同它的信息。愤怒的青年们随着中国的崛起而席卷而来——虽然他们不是中共的爱好者，但他们渴望一个更强大的中国，能够经受住“外国干涉”并决定自己的条件。

The CCP actively encourages strong patriotism, but is nervous about its transformation into aggressive nationalism and the potential for disturbance. Much nationalism in the PRC has little to do with the CCP but everything to do with China – while the CCP has struggled at length to identify itself with China’s civilisation and core values, it has been only partially successful. With China’s tendency to get quickly swept along by passions, nationalism is an often unseen but quite potent force, most visibly flaring up into the periodic anti-Japanese demonstrations that can convulse large towns and cities.
中共积极鼓励强烈的爱国主义，但对其转变为侵略性的民族主义和潜在的骚乱感到紧张。中国的许多民族主义与中共关系不大，但与中国有关——虽然中共一直在努力认同中国的文明和核心价值观，但它只取得了部分成功。由于中国倾向于迅速被激情席卷，民族主义是一种经常看不见但相当强大的力量，最明显的是爆发为周期性的抗日示威活动，这些示威活动可能会震惊大城镇。
