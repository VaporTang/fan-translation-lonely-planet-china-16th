Arts & Architecture
艺术与建筑

China is custodian of one of the world’s richest cultural and artistic legacies. Until the 20th century, China’s arts were deeply conservative and resistant to change, but revolutions in technique and content over the last century fashioned a dramatic transformation. Despite this evolution, China’s arts – whatever the period – embrace a common aesthetic that embodies the very soul and lifeblood of the nation.
中国是世界上最丰富的文化和艺术遗产之一的守护者。直到20世纪，中国的艺术都极度保守，抗拒变革，但上个世纪技术和内容的革命带来了戏剧性的转变。尽管发生了这种演变，但中国的艺术——无论在哪个时期——都拥抱着一种共同的美学，体现了民族的灵魂和命脉。

Aesthetics
美学

In reflection of the Chinese character, Chinese aesthetics have traditionally been marked by restraint and understatement, a preference for oblique references over explicit explanation, vagueness in place of specificity and an avoidance of the obvious while inclining towards the veiled and subtle. Traditional Chinese aesthetics sought to cultivate a more reserved artistic impulse, principles that compellingly find their way into virtually every Chinese art form, from painting, sculpture and ceramics to calligraphy, film, poetry, literature and beyond.
为了反映中国性格，中国美学传统上以克制和轻描淡写为特征，偏爱隐晦的引用而不是明确的解释，模糊而不是具体性，回避显而易见的事物，而倾向于隐晦和微妙的事物。中国传统美学试图培养一种更加内敛的艺术冲动，这些原则几乎融入了中国的每一种艺术形式，从绘画、雕塑和陶瓷到书法、电影、诗歌、文学等等。

As one of the central strands of one of the world’s oldest continuous civilisations, China’s aesthetic traditions are tightly woven into Chinese cultural identity. It was not until the fall of the Qing dynasty in 1911 and the appearance of the New Culture Movement that China’s great artistic traditions began to rapidly transform. In literature the stranglehold of classical Chinese loosened to allow breathing space for báihuà (colloquial Chinese) and a progressive new aesthetic started to flower, ultimately leading to revolutions in all of the arts, from poetry to painting, theatre and music.
作为世界上最古老的连续文明之一的中心线之一，中国的审美传统紧密地融入了中国文化认同。直到 1911 年清朝灭亡，新文化运动的出现，中国伟大的艺术传统才开始迅速转变。在文学方面，对文言文的束缚被松弛，为白华 （汉语口语）提供了喘息的空间，一种进步的新审美开始绽放，最终导致了从诗歌到绘画、戏剧和音乐等所有艺术的革命。

It is hard to reconcile China’s great aesthetic traditions with the devastation inflicted upon them since 1949. Confucius advocated the edifying role of music and poetry in shaping human lives, but 5th-century philosopher Mozi was less enamoured with them, seeing music and other arts as extravagant and wasteful. The communists took this a stage further, enlisting the arts as props in their propaganda campaigns, and permitting the vandalism and destruction of much traditional architecture and heritage. Many of the arts have yet to recover fully from this deterioration, even though opening up and reform prompted a vast influx of foreign artistic concepts and, to a degree, a revival of China’s traditional art forms.
很难将中国伟大的美学传统与自 1949 年以来对它们造成的破坏相协调。孔子主张音乐和诗歌在塑造人类生活方面的启发作用，但 5 世纪的哲学家墨子却不那么迷恋它们，认为音乐和其他艺术是奢侈和浪费的。共产党人更进一步，将艺术作为宣传活动的道具，并允许对许多传统建筑和遗产进行破坏和破坏。许多艺术尚未从这种恶化中完全恢复过来，尽管开放和改革促使了外国艺术概念的大量涌入，并在某种程度上复兴了中国传统艺术形式。

The most abstract calligraphic form is grass or cursive script (cǎoshū), a highly fluid style of penmanship that even Chinese people have difficulty reading.
最抽象的书法形式是草书或草书 ， 这是一种高度流畅的书法风格，即使是中国人也难以阅读。

Calligraphy
书法

Although calligraphy (书法, shūfǎ) has a place among most languages that employ alphabets, the art of calligraphy in China is taken to unusual heights of intricacy and beauty in a language that is alphabet-free and essentially composed of images.
尽管书法（书法 ，shūfǎ）在大多数使用字母的语言中占有一席之地，但中国的书法艺术在一种无字母、基本上由图像组成的语言中被提升到了不同寻常的复杂性和美感的高度。

To fully appreciate how perfectly suited written Chinese is for calligraphy, it is vital to grasp how written Chinese works. A word in English represents a sound alone, but a written character in Chinese combines both sound and a picture. Indeed, the sound element of a Chinese character – when present – is often auxiliary to the illustration of a visual image, even if that image is abstract.
要充分了解书写汉语是多么适合书法，掌握书写汉语的工作原理至关重要。英文中的单词仅代表声音，但中文的书面文字结合了声音和图片。事实上，汉字的声音元素——如果存在——通常是视觉图像插图的辅助工具，即使该图像是抽象的。

Furthermore, although some Chinese characters were simplified in the 1950s as part of a literacy drive, most characters have remained unchanged for thousands of years. This helps explain why Chinese calligraphy is the trickiest of China’s arts to comprehend for Western visitors, unless they have a sound understanding of written Chinese. The beauty of a Chinese character may be partially appreciated by a Western audience, but for a full understanding it is also essential to understand the meaning of the character (or characters).
此外，尽管一些汉字在 1950 年代作为扫盲运动的一部分进行了简化，但大多数汉字几千年来一直保持不变。这有助于解释为什么中国书法对于西方游客来说是最难理解的中国艺术，除非他们对汉语书面有很好的理解。西方观众可能会部分欣赏汉字的美感，但要充分理解汉字的含义也很重要。

Painting
画

Traditional Painting
传统绘画

Unlike Chinese calligraphy, Chinese painting is highly accessible. For this reason traditional Chinese paintings – especially landscapes – have long been treasured in the West for their beauty.
与中国书法不同，中国画很容易理解。因此，中国传统绘画——尤其是山水画——长期以来因其美丽而在西方受到珍视。

As described in Xie He’s 6th-century-AD treatise, the Six Principles of Painting, the chief aim of Chinese painting is to capture the innate essence or spirit (qì) of a subject and endow it with vitality. The brush line, varying in thickness and tone, was the second principle (referred to as the ‘bone method’) and is the defining technique of Chinese painting. Traditionally it was imagined that brushwork quality could reveal the artist’s moral character. As a general rule, painters were less concerned with achieving outward resemblance (that was the third principle) than with conveying intrinsic qualities.
正如谢赫公元 6 世纪的论文《 绘画六则 》中所描述的那样，中国画的主要目的是捕捉一个主题与生俱来的本质或精神 （气）， 并赋予它生命力。粗细和色调各不相同的笔线是第二原则（称为“骨法”），是中国画的定义技巧。传统上，人们认为笔触质量可以揭示艺术家的道德品质。一般来说，画家不太关心实现外在相似性（这是第三项原则），而是关心传达内在品质。

Early painters dwelled on the human figure and moral teachings, while also conjuring up scenes from everyday life. By the time of the Tang dynasty, a new genre, known as landscape painting, had begun to flower. Reaching full bloom during the Song and Yuan dynasties, landscape painting meditated on the environment surrounding man. Towering mountains, ethereal mists, open spaces, trees and rivers, and the effects of light and dark were all exquisitely presented in ink washes on silk. Landscape paintings attempted to capture the metaphysical and the absolute, drawing the viewer into a particular realm where the philosophies of Taoism and Buddhism found expression. Humanity is typically a small and almost insignificant subtext to the performance. The purpose of the dreamlike painting sought to draw the viewer in rather than impose itself on the observer.
早期的画家沉迷于人物形象和道德教义，同时也唤起日常生活中的场景。到了唐朝时期，一种新的流派，被称为山水画，开始开花结果。山水画在宋元时期盛开，沉思着人类周围的环境。巍峨的山峦、空灵的雾气、空旷的空间、树木和河流、明暗的效果，都在绢上的水墨中精美地呈现出来。山水画试图捕捉形而上学和绝对，将观众带入道教和佛教哲学得到表达的特定领域。人性通常是表演的一个小而几乎微不足道的潜台词。这幅梦幻般的画作的目的是吸引观众，而不是强加给观察者。

The five fundamental brushstrokes necessary to master calligraphy can be found in the character 永, which means eternal or forever.
掌握书法所需的五种基本笔触可以在永字中找到， 永字的意思是永恒或永远。

Modern Art
现代艺术

Socialist Realism
社会主义现实主义

After 1949 classical Chinese techniques were abandoned and foreign artistic techniques imported wholesale. Washes on silk were replaced with oil on canvas and China’s traditional obsession with the mysterious and ineffable made way for concrete attention to detail and realism.
1949年后，中国古典技法被摒弃，外国艺术技艺批发进口。丝绸上的水洗被布面油画所取代，中国对神秘和难以言喻的传统痴迷为对细节和现实主义的具体关注让路。

By 1970 Chinese artists had aspired to master the skills of socialist realism, a vibrant communist-endorsed style that drew from European neoclassical art, the lifelike canvases of Jacques-Louis David and the output of Soviet Union painters. The blunt style had virtually nothing to do with traditional Chinese painting techniques, instead being saturated with political symbolism and propaganda.
到 1970 年，中国艺术家渴望掌握社会主义现实主义的技能，这是一种充满活力的共产主义认可的风格，借鉴了欧洲新古典主义艺术、雅克-路易·大卫 （Jacques-Louis David） 的逼真画布和苏联画家的作品。钝的风格与中国传统绘画技巧几乎无关，而是充满了政治象征主义和宣传。

The entire trajectory of Chinese painting had been redirected virtually overnight. Vaporous landscapes were substituted with hard-edged panoramas; traditional Taoist and Buddhist philosophy was overturned and humans became the masters of nature and often the most dominant theme. Dreamy vistas were out; smoke stacks, red tractors and muscular peasants were in.
中国画的整个轨迹几乎在一夜之间被重新定位。蒸气景观被硬边全景所取代;传统的道教和佛教哲学被推翻，人类成为自然的主人，并且往往是最主导的主题。梦幻般的景色已经消失;烟囱、红色拖拉机和肌肉发达的农民都进来了。

In 2011 an ink-and-brush painting by artist Qi Baishi (1864–1957) sold for ¥425 million (US$65 million) at auction.
2011年，艺术家齐白石（1864-1957）的一幅水墨画在拍卖会上以4.25亿日元（6500万美元）的价格售出。

Propaganda Art
宣传艺术

Another art form that found a fertile environment during the Mao era was the propaganda poster. Mass-produced from the 1950s onwards and replicated in their thousands through tourist markets across China today, the colourful Chinese propaganda poster was a further instrument of social control in a nation where aesthetics had become subservient to communist orthodoxy.
另一种在毛时代找到肥沃环境的艺术形式是宣传海报。这张色彩缤纷的中国宣传海报从 1950 年代开始批量生产，并在今天的中国各地的旅游市场复制了数千张，在美学已经屈从于共产主义正统观念的国家，它成为社会控制的又一工具。

With a prolific range of themes from chubby, well-fed Chinese babies to the Korean War, the virtues of physical education, the suppression of counter-revolutionary activity and paeans to the achievements of the Great Leap Forward or China as an earthly paradise, propaganda posters were ubiquitous. The golden age of poster production ran through to the 1980s, only declining during Deng Xiaoping’s tenure and the opening up of China to the West.
从胖乎乎的、吃饱的中国婴儿到朝鲜战争、体育教育的美德、镇压反革命活动和赞歌，再到大跃进的成就或中国作为人间天堂的成就，宣传海报的主题范围很广。海报制作的黄金时代一直持续到1980年代，直到邓小平执政和中国对西开放期间才有所衰落。

The success of visual propaganda lay in its appeal to a large body of illiterate or semiliterate peasants. The idealism, revolutionary romanticism and vivid colouring of Chinese propaganda art brought hope and vibrancy to a time that was actually often colourless and drab, while adding certainty to an era of great hardship and struggle.
视觉宣传的成功在于它吸引了大量文盲或半文盲农民。中国宣传艺术的理想主义、革命浪漫主义和生动的色彩，为这个实际上往往毫无色彩和单调的时代带来了希望和活力，同时为一个充满艰辛和斗争的时代增添了确定性。

Post-Mao
后毛

It was only with the death of Mao Zedong in 1976 that the shadow of the Cultural Revolution – when Chinese aesthetics were conditioned by the threat of violence – began its retreat and the individual artistic temperament was allowed to thrive afresh.
直到1976年毛泽东去世，文化大革命的阴影——中国美学受到暴力威胁的制约——才开始消退，个人艺术气质得以重新蓬勃发展。

Painters such as Luo Zhongli employed the realist techniques gleaned from China’s art academies to depict the harsh realities etched in the faces of contemporary peasants. Others escaped the suffocating confines of socialist realism to navigate new horizons. A voracious appetite for western art brought with it fresh concepts and ideas, while the ambiguity of precise meaning in the fine arts offered a degree of protection from state censors.
罗中立等画家运用从中国艺术院校收集到的现实主义手法，描绘了刻在当代农民脸上的残酷现实。其他人则逃离了社会主义现实主义的令人窒息的限制，开辟了新的视野。对西方艺术的贪婪胃口带来了新鲜的概念和想法，而美术中精确含义的模糊性则提供了一定程度的保护，免受国家审查。

One group of artists, the Stars, found retrospective inspiration in Picasso and German Expressionism. The ephemeral group had a lasting impact on the development of Chinese art in the 1980s and 1990s, paving the way for the New Wave movement that emerged in 1985. New Wave artists were greatly influenced by western art, especially the iconoclastic Marcel Duchamp. In true nihilist style, the New Wave artist Huang Yongping destroyed his works at exhibitions in an effort to escape from the notion of ‘art’. Political realities became instant subject matter as performance artists wrapped themselves in plastic or tape to symbolise the repressive realities of modern-day China.
一群艺术家，明星，从毕加索和德国表现主义中找到了回顾性的灵感。这个短暂的团体对 1980 年代和 1990 年代中国艺术的发展产生了持久的影响，为 1985 年出现的新浪潮运动铺平了道路。新浪潮艺术家深受西方艺术的影响，尤其是反传统的马塞尔·杜尚。新浪潮艺术家黄永平以真正的虚无主义风格，在展览中销毁自己的作品，试图摆脱“艺术”的概念。政治现实立即成为主题，因为行为艺术家用塑料或胶带包裹自己，象征着现代中国的压迫现实。

Chinese individuals and companies are also purchasing non-Chinese art masterpieces. In 2015 Claude Monet’s Bassin aux nymphéas, les rosiers sold for US$20.4 million at auction to the Dalian Wanda Group.
中国个人和公司也在购买非中国艺术杰作。2015 年，克劳德·莫奈的《睡莲之歌》 以 2040 万美元的价格卖给了大连万达集团。

Beyond Tian’anmen
超越天安门

The Tian’anmen Square protests in 1989 fostered a deep-seated cynicism that permeated artworks with loss, loneliness and social isolation. An exodus of artists to the West commenced. This period also coincided with an upsurge in the art market as investors increasingly turned to artworks and money began to slosh about.
1989年的天安门广场抗议活动助长了一种根深蒂固的愤世嫉俗，这种愤世嫉俗渗透到艺术作品中，充满了失落、孤独和社会孤立。艺术家开始向西方外流。这一时期也恰逢艺术市场的热潮，投资者越来越多地转向艺术品，资金开始四处乱窜。

Much post-1989 Chinese art dwelled obsessively on contemporary socioeconomic realities, with consumer culture, materialism, urbanisation and social change a repetitive focus. More universal themes became apparent, however, as the art scene matured, and artists who left China in the 1990s returned, setting up private studios and galleries.
1989年后的中国艺术大多痴迷于当代社会经济现实，消费文化、物质主义、城市化和社会变革是反复关注的焦点。然而，随着艺术界的成熟，更普遍的主题变得明显，1990年代离开中国的艺术家回来了，建立了私人工作室和画廊。

Cynical realists Fang Lijun and Yue Minjun fashioned grotesque portraits that conveyed hollowness and mock joviality, tinged with despair. Born in the late 1950s, Wang Guangyi took pop art as a template for his ironic pieces, fused with propaganda art techniques from the Cultural Revolution.
愤世嫉俗的现实主义者方丽君和岳敏君塑造了怪诞的肖像，传达了空洞和嘲讽的快乐，带有绝望的色彩。王广义出生于1950年代后期，他以波普艺术为模板创作讽刺作品，并融合了文革时期的宣传艺术手法。

Born just before the Cultural Revolution in 1964 and heavily influenced by German expressionism, Zeng Fanzhi explored the notions of alienation and isolation – themes commonly addressed by Chinese artists during this period – in his Mask series from the 1990s. Introspection is a hallmark of Zeng’s oeuvre. In 2008 Christie’s in Hong Kong sold Zeng Fanzhi’s painting Mask Series 1996 No. 6 (featuring masked members of China’s communist youth organisation, the Young Pioneers) for US$9.7 million, which is the highest price yet paid for a modern Chinese artwork.
曾梵志出生于 1964 年文化大革命前夕，深受德国表现主义的影响，他在 1990 年代的 《面具》 系列中探索了异化和孤立的概念——这一时期中国艺术家普遍关注的主题。内省是曾伟文作品的一个标志。2008 年，香港佳士得以 970 万美元的价格售出了曾梵志的画作《 面具系列 1996 年第 6 号 》（以中国共产主义青年组织少先队蒙面成员为主角），这是迄今为止中国现代艺术品的最高成交价。

Also born in the early 1960s, Zhang Dali is another artist who gave expression to social change and the gulf between rich and poor, especially the circumstances of the immigrant worker underclass in Beijing.
张大力也出生于1960年代初期，是另一位表达社会变革和贫富鸿沟的艺术家，尤其是北京移民工人下层阶级的处境。

Major art festivals include Beijing’s 798 International Art Festival, China International Gallery Exposition and Beijing Biennale, the Shanghai Biennale, Guangzhou Triennial and Hong Kong’s one-day Clockenflap festival.
主要艺术节包括北京798国际艺术节、中国国际画廊博览会和北京双年展、上海双年展、广州三年展和香港为期一天的钟表艺术节。

Contemporary Directions
当代方向

Most artists of note and aspiration gravitate to Beijing (or Shanghai) to work. Today’s China provides a huge wellspring of subject matter for artists, tempered by the reality of political censorship and the constraints of taboo. Themes that can seem tame in the West may assume a special power in China, so works can rely upon their context for potency and effect.
大多数有知名度和抱负的艺术家都被吸引到北京（或上海）工作。今天的中国为艺术家提供了巨大的题材来源，但受到政治审查制度和禁忌约束的现实的影响。在西方看似温和的主题在中国可能具有特殊的力量，因此作品可以依靠其背景来获得效力和效果。

Ai Weiwei, who enjoys great international fame partly due to his anti-authoritarian stances, exemplifies the dangerous overlap between artistic self-expression, dissent and conflict with the authorities. He was arrested in 2011 on charges of tax evasion and held for 81 days.
艾未未因其反独裁立场而享有盛誉，他体现了艺术自我表达、异议和与当局冲突之间的危险重叠。他于 2011 年因逃税罪被捕，并被关押了 81 天。

Working collaboratively as Birdhead, Shanghai analogue photographers Ji Weiyu and Song Tao record the social dynamics and architectural habitat of their home city in thoughtful compositions. Beijing-born Ma Qiusha works in video, photography, painting and installations on themes of a deeply personal nature. In her video work From No.4 Pingyuanli to No.4 Tianqiaobeili, the artist removes a bloody razor blade from her mouth after narrating her experiences as a young artist in China. Born in 1982, Ran Huang works largely in film but across a spectrum of media, conveying themes of absurdity, the irrational and conceptual. Shanghai artist Shi Zhiying explores ideas of a more traditional hue in her sublime oil-paint depictions on large canvases of landscapes and religious and cultural objects. Also from Shanghai, Xu Zhen works with provocative images to unsettle and challenge the viewer.
上海模拟摄影师季伟宇和宋涛以“鸟头”的身份合作，以深思熟虑的构图记录了他们家乡的社会动态和建筑栖息地。出生于北京的马秋莎的作品涉及视频、摄影、绘画和装置，主题非常个人化。在她的录像作品《 从四号平原里到四号天桥贝里》中， 艺术家在讲述了她作为年轻艺术家在中国的经历后，从嘴里取出了一把血淋淋的剃须刀片。黄冉出生于 1982 年，主要从事电影工作，但跨越各种媒介，传达荒谬、非理性和概念性的主题。上海艺术家石志英在大型风景画布上以崇高的油画描绘，探索了更传统的色调。同样来自上海的徐震用挑衅性的图像来让观众感到不安和挑战。

A dark and Gothic image in the West, the bat is commonly used in Chinese porcelain, wood designs, textiles and artwork as it is considered a good-luck omen.
蝙蝠在西方是一种黑暗的哥特式形象，常用于中国瓷器、木制设计、纺织品和艺术品，因为它被认为是一种好运的预兆。

Ceramics
陶瓷

China’s very first vessels, dating back more than 8000 years, were simple handcrafted earthenware pottery, primarily used for religious purposes. The invention of the pottery wheel during the late Neolithic period, however, led to a dramatic technological and artistic leap.
中国最早的器皿可以追溯到 8000 多年前，是简单的手工陶器，主要用于宗教目的。然而，新石器时代晚期陶轮的发明导致了技术和艺术的巨大飞跃。

Over the centuries Chinese potters perfected their craft, introducing many new and exciting styles and techniques. The spellbinding artwork of the Terracotta Warriors in Xi’an reveals a highly developed level of technical skill achieved by Qin-dynasty craftsmen. Periods of artistic evolution, during the cosmopolitan Tang dynasty, for example, prompted further stylistic advances. The Tang dynasty ‘three-colour ware’ is a much-admired type of ceramic from this period, noted for its vivid yellow, green and white glaze. Demand for lovely blue-green celadons grew in countries as distant as Egypt and Persia.
几个世纪以来，中国陶艺家完善了他们的手艺，引入了许多新的、令人兴奋的风格和技术。西习安兵马俑的引人入胜的艺术作品揭示了秦代工匠所达到的高度发达的技术水平。例如，国际化的唐朝时期的艺术演变时期促进了风格的进一步进步。唐代“三色器”是这一时期备受推崇的陶瓷类型，以其鲜艳的黄、绿、白釉而闻名。在埃及和波斯等遥远的国家，对可爱的蓝绿色青瓷的需求不断增长。

The Yuan dynasty saw the first development of China’s standout ‘blue and white’ (qīnghuā) porcelain. Cobalt-blue paint from Persia was applied as an underglaze directly to white porcelain with a brush, with the vessel then covered with another transparent glaze and fired. This technique was perfected during the Ming dynasty and such ceramics became hugely popular all over the world, eventually acquiring the name ‘China-ware’, whether produced in China or not.
元朝见证了中国杰出的“青花”（青花） 瓷器的首次发展。来自波斯的钴蓝色颜料作为釉下彩直接用刷子涂在白瓷上，然后用另一种透明釉覆盖器皿并烧制。这种技术在明代得到了完善，这种陶瓷在世界范围内大受欢迎，最终获得了“瓷器”的称号，无论是否在中国生产。

Although many kilns were established in China, the most famous was at Jingdezhen in Jiangxi province, where royal porcelain was fired. Jingdezhen remains an excellent place to visit ceramic workshops and purchase various types of ceramic wares, from Mao statues to traditional glazed urns. The Shanghai Museum has a premier collection of porcelain, while several independent retailers in Beijing, Shanghai and Hong Kong also sell more modish and creative pieces.
虽然中国建立了许多窑炉，但最著名的是在江西省的景德镇，那里烧制皇家瓷器。景德镇仍然是参观陶瓷作坊和购买各种陶瓷器皿的绝佳场所，从毛雕像到传统的釉面瓮。上海博物馆拥有一流的瓷器收藏，而北京、上海和香港的几家独立零售商也出售更多时尚和创意的瓷器。

In 2010 a Qing dynasty Chinese vase sold for £53.1 million after being discovered in the attic of a house in northwest London and put up for auction.
2010年，一个清代中国花瓶在伦敦西北部一栋房屋的阁楼上被发现并拍卖，以5310万英镑的价格售出。

Sculpture
雕塑

The earliest sculpture in China dates from the Zhou and Shang dynasties, when small clay and wooden figures were commonly placed in tombs to protect the dead and guide them on their way to heaven.
中国最早的雕塑可以追溯到周商时期，当时墓葬中通常放置小泥和木制人物，以保护死者并引导他们走向天堂。

With the arrival of Buddhism, sculpture turned towards spiritual figures and themes, with sculptors frequently enrolled in huge carving projects for the worship of Sakyamuni. Influences also arrived along the Silk Road from abroad, bringing styles from as far afield as Greece and Persia, via India. The magnificent Buddhist caves at Yungang in Shanxi province, for example, date back to the 5th century and betray a noticeable Indian influence.
随着佛教的到来，雕塑转向精神人物和主题，雕塑家们经常参与大型雕塑项目来供奉释迦牟尼。影响也从国外沿着丝绸之路传来，通过印度带来了远至希腊和波斯的风格。例如，山西省云冈宏伟的佛教石窟的历史可以追溯到 5 世纪，并暴露了明显的印度影响。

The most superlative examples are at the Mogao Grottoes at Dunhuang in Gansu province, where well-preserved Indian and Central Asian–style sculptures, particularly of the Tang dynasty, carry overtly Chinese characteristics – many statues feature long, fluid bodies and have warmer, more refined facial features.
最顶级的例子是甘肃敦煌的莫高窟，那里保存完好的印度和中亚风格的雕塑，尤其是唐代的雕塑，带有明显的中国特色——许多雕像具有修长、流畅的身体，并且具有更温暖、更精致的面部特征。

Beyond its grottoes, other mesmerising Chinese sculpture hides away in temples across China. The colossal statue of Guanyin in Puning Temple in Chengde is a staggering sight, carved from five different types of wood and towering over 22m in height. Shuanglin Temple outside Pingyao in Shanxi province is famed for its astonishing collection of painted statues from the Song and Yuan dynasties. The Shanghai Museum also has a splendid collection of Buddhist sculpture.
除了石窟之外，其他迷人的中国雕塑也隐藏在中国各地的寺庙中。承德普宁寺的观音像令人咋舌，由五种不同的木材雕刻而成，高耸22米以上。山西省平遥郊外的双林寺以其令人惊叹的宋元时期彩绘雕像收藏而闻名。上海博物馆还收藏了丰富的佛教雕塑。

Jade
玉

Jade (玉, yù) has been revered in China since Neolithic times. Because of its hardness and strength, the stone was first used for making tools, but later appeared on ornaments and vessels for its decorative value. During the Qin and Han dynasties, it was believed that jade was empowered with magical and life-giving properties, and the dead were buried with jadeware. Opulent jade suits, meant to prevent decomposition, have been excavated from Han tombs, while Taoist alchemists consumed elixirs of powdered jade in their quest for immortality.
玉（ 玉 ，yù）自新石器时代以来就在中国受到推崇。由于其硬度和强度，这种石头最初被用来制作工具，但后来因其装饰价值而出现在装饰品和器皿上。秦汉时期，人们认为玉石具有神奇和赋予生命的特性，死者则用玉器埋葬。从汉墓中出土了用于防止腐烂的华丽玉衣，而道教炼金术士则服用玉粉丹以求长生不老。

The I Ching (Yìjīng; Book of Changes) is the oldest Chinese text and is used for divination. It comprises 64 hexagrams, composed of broken and continuous lines, that represent a balance of opposites (yin and yang), the inevitability of change and the evolution of events.
易经（Yìjīng;《易经》）是最古老的汉文，用于占卜。它由 64 个卦组成，由折线和连续线组成，代表对立面（阴阳）的平衡、变化的必然性和事件的演变。

Bronze Vessels
青铜器皿

Apocryphal tradition ascribes the first casting of bronze – an alloy of copper, tin and lead – to the legendary Xia dynasty some 5000 years ago.
伪经传统将第一次铸造青铜（一种铜、锡和铅的合金）归功于大约 5000 年前传说中的夏朝。

Shang dynasty ritual bronzes are marvellous specimens, often fabulously patterned with tāotiè, a kind of fierce, mythical animal design. Many Zhou dynasty vessels bear inscriptions describing wars, ceremonial events and the appointment of officials. Though now they wear a deep, dark-green patina, originally they would have been burnished a bright gold colour, so their appearance and aesthetic effect would have been markedly different from what we see today.
商代的仪式青铜器是奇妙的标本，通常带有精美的图案，这是一种凶猛的神话动物图案 。 许多周代器皿上都刻有描述战争、礼仪事件和官员任命的铭文。虽然现在它们穿着深绿色的铜绿，但最初它们会被抛光成明亮的金色，因此它们的外观和美学效果会与我们今天看到的明显不同。

Literature
文献

Classic Novels
经典小说

Until the early 20th century, classical literature (古文, gǔwén) had been the principal form of writing in China for thousands of years. A breed of purely literary writing different from spoken Chinese, classical Chinese maintained divisions between educated and uneducated Chinese, putting literature beyond the reach of the common person and fashioning a cliquey lingua franca for Confucian officials, scholars and the erudite elite.
直到 20 世纪初，古典文学（ 古文 ，gǔwén）几千年来一直是中国的主要写作形式。文言文是不同于汉语口语的纯文学写作，它保持了受过教育的中国人和未受过教育的中国人之间的区别，使文学成为普通人无法理解的，并为儒家官员、学者和博学精英塑造了一种小圈子通用语言。

Classical novels evolved from the popular folk tales and dramas that entertained the lower classes. During the Ming dynasty they were penned in a semi-vernacular (or ‘vulgar’) language, and are often irreverently funny and full of action-packed fights.
古典小说是从娱乐下层阶级的流行民间故事和戏剧演变而来的。在明朝，它们是用半白话（或“粗俗”）语言写成的，并且通常不敬地有趣，并且充满了动感十足的打斗。

Probably the best-known novel outside China is Journey to the West (Xīyóu Jì). Written in the 16th century, it follows the misadventures of a cowardly Buddhist monk (Tripitaka; a stand-in for the real-life pilgrim Xuan Zang) and his companions – a rebellious monkey, lecherous pig-man and exiled monster-immortal – on a pilgrimage to India.
中国以外最著名的小说可能是 《西游记 》（Xīyóu Jì）。它写于 16 世纪，讲述了一位懦弱的佛教僧侣（大藏经，现实生活中朝圣者玄奘的替身）和他的同伴——一只叛逆的猴子、好色的猪人和流亡的怪物仙人——前往印度朝圣的不幸经历。

Written by Cao Xueqin and one of the most famous tales in Chinese literature, the Dream of the Red Mansions (Hónglóu Mèng) is an elaborate 18th-century novel penned in a vernacular, semiclassical form of Chinese. Also known as The Story of the Stone, the lavish tale relates the decline of an aristocratic family, affording a captivating overview of the mores and manners of upper-class Qing society.
《 红楼梦 》由曹雪芹创作，是中国文学中最著名的故事之一，是一部精心制作的 18 世纪小说，以半古典白话形式写成。这个奢华的故事也被称为 《石头的故事》， 讲述了一个贵族家庭的衰落，对清朝上层社会的习俗和礼仪进行了引人入胜的概述。

The Book and the Sword by Jin Yong/Louis Cha (2004) is China’s most celebrated martial-arts novelist’s first book. The martial-arts genre (wǔxiá xiǎoshuō) is a direct descendant of the classical novel.
金庸/车路易的《 书与剑 》（2004）是中国最著名的武侠小说家的第一本书。武侠体裁 （wǔxiá xiǎoshuō） 是古典小说的直系后代。

Classical Poetry
古典诗歌

The earliest collection of Chinese poetry is the Book of Songs (Shījīng), which includes more than 300 poems dating back to the 6th century BC, gathered together by royal musicians who lived in the many feudal states clustered along the banks of the Yellow River during the Zhou dynasty. Centred on themes of love, marriage, war, agriculture, hunting and sacrifice, the poems were originally meant to be sung.
中国最早的诗词集是《 诗经》，收录了公元前 6 世纪的 300 多首诗词，由周代生活在黄河沿岸众多封建国家的皇家音乐家汇集而成。这些诗以爱情、婚姻、战争、农业、狩猎和牺牲为主题，最初是用来演唱的。

China’s greatest early poet is Qu Yuan, who lived during the Warring States period (475–221 BC) and is known for his romantic, lyrical poetry.
中国最伟大的早期诗人是屈原，他生活在战国时期（公元前 475-221 年），以其浪漫、抒情的诗歌而闻名。

The Tang dynasty is considered to be the golden age of Chinese poetry, when two of China’s greatest poets – Li Bai and Du Fu – lived and created some of the most beautiful and moving poems in classical Chinese. During the Song dynasty, a lyric form of poetry called cí emerged, expressing feelings of passion and desire. Su Shi (Su Dongpo) is the most famous poet from this period.
唐代被认为是中国诗歌的黄金时代，当时中国最伟大的两位诗人——李白和杜甫——生活并创作了一些中国古典诗歌中最美丽、最动人的诗歌。在宋代时期，出现了一种名为“cí”的抒情诗歌形式，表达了激情和欲望的情感。苏轼（苏东坡）是这一时期最著名的诗人。

Following is an example of a piece of classical Chinese poetry from the Tang dynasty by the poet-painter Wang Wei (王维; AD 699–759), entitled ‘Deer Park’. Notice how the number of characters are the same on each line (five on each line) creating a perfect balance in syllables, while the final characters of the first (人, ‘ren’) and third lines (林, ‘lin’) rhyme. The last characters of the second (響, ‘xiang’) and fourth lines (上, ‘shang’) also rhyme and resonate together (that they still rhyme after more than 1200 years is also astonishing). The meaning of each individual character is added sequentially in the line below each original Chinese line, with a translation into modern English at the end:
以下是诗人兼画家王维（ 王维 ;公元 699-759 年），标题为“鹿园”。请注意，每行的字符数相同（每行五个），在音节上创造了完美的平衡，而第一行（ 人 ，'任'）和第三行（ 林 ，'林'）的最后一个字符押韵。第二句（ 响 ，'相'）和第四句（ 上 ，'商'）的最后一个字也押韵和共鸣（1200 多年后它们仍然押韵，这也令人惊讶）。每个单独字符的含义依次添加到每行原始中文行下方的行中，并在末尾翻译成现代英语：

鹿柴

空山不見人

(empty mountain not see people)
（空山不见人）

但聞人語響
但闻人语响

(but hear people speak sound)
（但听到人们说话有声）

返景入深林

(again reflection enters deep forest)
（再次倒影进入深林）

復照青苔上。

(again shine green moss upon)
（再次闪耀绿色苔藓）

鹿苑

People are unseen in the empty mountains
空山里人不见

But the sound of voices carries
但声音承载着

The reflected scene enters the deep forest
倒影的景象进入深林

To shine once more on the green moss.
再次照耀绿色的苔藓。

Modern Literature
现代文学

Early 20th-Century Writing
20世纪初的写作

Classical Chinese maintained its authority over literary minds until the early 20th century, when it came under the influence of the West.
文言文一直保持着对文学思想的权威，直到20世纪初，它受到西方的影响。

Torch-bearing author Lu Xun wrote his short story, Diary of a Madman, in 1918. It was revolutionary stuff. Apart from the opening paragraph, which is composed in classical Chinese, Lu’s seminal and shocking fable is cast entirely in colloquial Chinese.
1918 年，执着火炬的作家鲁迅写下了他的短篇小说《 狂人日记 》。这是革命性的东西。除了开头的段落是用文言文写成的，卢的开创性和震撼人心的寓言完全是用口语写的。

For Lu Xun to write his short story in colloquial Chinese was explosive: readers were finally able to read language as it was spoken. Diary of a Madman is a haunting and unsettling work, and from this moment onwards mainstream Chinese literature would be written as it was thought and spoken: Chinese writing had been instantly revolutionised.
对于鲁迅来说，用汉语口语写他的短篇小说是爆炸性的：读者终于能够阅读语言的口语。《 狂人日记 》是一部令人难以忘怀、令人不安的作品，从这一刻起，中国主流文学将按照人们的思想和口头来写成：中国文字立即发生了革命性的变化。

Other notable contemporaries of Lu Xun include Ba Jin (Family; 1931), Mao Dun (Midnight; 1933), Beijing author Lao She (Rickshaw Boy/Camel Xiangzi; 1936) and the modernist playwright Cao Yu (Thunderstorm; 1934). Lu Xun and Ba Jin also translated a great deal of foreign literature into Chinese.
与鲁迅同时代的其他著名人物包括巴金（《家庭》，1931 年）、毛盾（《午夜》（1933 年）、北京作家老舍（《 黄包车小子》/《骆驼祥子 》，1936 年）和现代主义剧作家曹禺（《 雷雨 》，1934 年）。鲁迅和巴金还将大量外国文学翻译成中文。

Published by the Chinese University of Hong Kong Research Centre for Translation, Renditions (www.cuhk.edu.hk/rct/renditions/index.html) is an excellent journal of Chinese literature in English translation, covering works from classical Chinese to modern writing.
《 演绎 》（www.cuhk.edu.hk/rct/renditions/index.html）由香港中文大学翻译研究中心出版，是一本优秀的中国文学英文翻译期刊，涵盖从文言文到现代写作的作品。

Contemporary Writing
当代写作

A growing number of contemporary voices have been translated into English, but far more exist in Chinese only. The provocative Nobel Prize–winning Mo Yan (Life and Death are Wearing Me Out; 2008), Yu Hua (To Live; 1992) and Su Tong (Rice; 1995) have written momentous, harrowing historical novels set in the 20th century.
越来越多的当代声音被翻译成英文，但更多的声音仅存在中文。具有挑衅性的诺贝尔奖得主莫言（《 生与死都让我疲惫不堪 》，2008 年）、余华（《活下去》;1992 年）和苏童（《赖斯》;1995 年）都写了以 20 世纪为背景的重大、令人痛心的历史小说。

Zhu Wen mocks the get-rich movement in his brilliantly funny short stories, published in English as I Love Dollars and Other Stories of China (2007). It’s a vivid and comic portrayal of the absurdities of everyday China.
朱温在他精彩有趣的短篇小说中嘲笑了致富运动，该短篇小说的英文出版名为 《我爱美元和其他中国故事 》（2007）。这是对中国日常荒谬的生动而滑稽的写照。

‘Hooligan author’ Wang Shuo (Please Don’t Call Me Human; 2000) is one of China’s bestselling authors with his political satires and convincing depictions of urban slackers. Alai (Red Poppies; 2002), an ethnic Tibetan, made waves by writing in Chinese about early-20th-century Tibetan Sichuan – whatever your politics, it’s both insightful and a page-turner.
“流氓作家”王朔（请不要叫我人类，2000 年）是中国畅销书作家之一，他的政治讽刺作品和对城市懒惰者的令人信服的描绘。阿来（ 红罂粟花，2002 年）是一位藏族人，他用中文写了 20 世纪初的四川藏族，引起了轰动——无论你的政治是什么，它既有见地，又令人翻页。

Exiled author Ma Jian writes more politically critical work. His 2001 novel Red Dust was a Kerouacian tale of wandering China as a spiritual pollutant in the 1980s. Banned in China, his 2008 novel Beijing Coma is set against the Tian’anmen demonstrations of 1989 and their aftermath. Ma Jian’s latest book is China Dream (2018), a dystopian parody of the ‘China Dream’ national-rejuvenation maxim dreamed up by President Xi Jinping.
流亡作家马健写的更多政治批判作品。他 2001 年的小说《 红尘》 讲述了凯鲁阿克式的故事，讲述了 1980 年代作为精神污染物在中国流浪的故事。他在中国被禁，2008 年的小说《 北京昏迷 》以 1989 年的天安门示威及其后果为背景。马健的最新著作是《 中国梦 》（2018），这是对习近平主席梦寐以求的“中国梦”民族复兴格言的反乌托邦模仿。

China’s most renowned dissident writer, Gao Xingjian, won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 2000 for his novel Soul Mountain, an account of his travels along the Yangzi after being misdiagnosed with lung cancer.
中国最著名的异见作家高行健凭借小说《 灵魂山 》获得 2000 年诺贝尔文学奖，该小说讲述了他在被误诊为肺癌后沿扬子河旅行的故事。

The Three-Body Problem, the first novel of a science-fiction trilogy by Liu Cixin, was received with great international acclaim and was the first Asian book to garner the Hugo Award for Best Novel in 2015.
《 三体》 是刘慈欣科幻三部曲的第一部小说，在国际上广受好评，是 2015 年第一本获得雨果奖最佳小说奖的亚洲书籍。

For a taste of contemporary Chinese short-story writing with both English and Chinese, buy a copy of Short Stories in Chinese: New Penguin Parallel Text (2012). The Picador Book of Contemporary Chinese Fiction (2006) brings together a range of different contemporary voices and themes into one accessible book.
想要体验当代中国中英文短篇小说写作，请购买一本《 中文短篇小说：新企鹅平行文本 》（2012）。《 当代中国小说皮卡多书 》（2006 年）将一系列不同的当代声音和主题汇集到一本易于理解的书中。

Film
影片

Early Cinema
早期电影

The moving image first came to the Middle Kingdom in 1896, when Spaniard Galen Bocca unveiled a film projector and blew the socks off wide-eyed crowds in a Shanghai teahouse. Shanghai’s cosmopolitan verve and exotic looks would make it the capital of China’s film industry, but China’s very first movie – Conquering Jun Mountain (an excerpt from a piece of Beijing opera) – was actually filmed in Beijing in 1905.
这种动态影像于 1896 年首次传入中国，当时西班牙人盖伦·博卡 （Galen Bocca） 在上海一家茶馆里推出了一台电影放映机，炸毁了睁大眼睛的人群。上海的国际化活力和异国情调使其成为中国电影业的首都，但中国第一部电影——《 征服君山 》（京剧片段选段）——实际上是 1905 年在北京拍摄的。

Shanghai opened its first cinema in 1908. In those days cinema owners would cannily run the film for a few minutes, stop it and collect money from the audience before allowing the film to continue. The golden age of Shanghai filmmaking came in the 1930s when the city had more than 140 film companies. Its apogee arrived in 1937 with the release of Street Angel, a powerful drama about two sisters who flee the Japanese in northeast China and end up as prostitutes in Shanghai; and Crossroads, a clever comedy about four unemployed graduates. Japanese control of China eventually brought the industry to a standstill and sent many film-makers packing.
上海于 1908 年开设了第一家电影院。在那些日子里，电影院老板会狡猾地放映这部电影几分钟，然后停止它并向观众收取费用，然后再允许电影继续播放。上海电影制作的黄金时代出现在 1930 年代，当时该市有 140 多家电影公司。1937 年，随着《 街头天使》 的上映，它的顶峰到来了，这是一部强大的戏剧，讲述了两姐妹逃离中国东北的日本人，最终在上海当的故事;以及 《十字路口》， 一部关于四名失业毕业生的巧妙喜剧。日本对中国的控制最终使该行业陷入停滞，并让许多电影制片人收拾行李。

Wolf Totem (2009) by Jiang Rong is an astonishing look at life on the grasslands of Inner Mongolia during the Cultural Revolution and the impact of modern culture on an ancient way of life.
江蓉的《 狼图腾 》（2009）以惊人的视角审视了文革时期内蒙古草原上的生活，以及现代文化对一种古老生活方式的影响。

Communist Decline
共产主义衰落

China’s film industry was stymied after the Communist Revolution, which sent film-makers scurrying to Hong Kong and Taiwan, where they played key roles in building up the local film industries that flourished there. Cinematic production in China was co-opted to glorify communism and generate patriotic propaganda. The days of the Cultural Revolution (1966–76) were particularly dark. Between 1966 and 1972, just eight movies were made on the mainland, as the film industry was effectively shut down.
中国的电影业在共产主义革命后受到阻碍，电影制片人纷纷涌向香港和台湾，在那里他们在建立当地蓬勃发展的电影产业方面发挥了关键作用。中国的电影制作被用来美化共产主义并产生爱国主义宣传。文化大革命（1966-76）的日子尤其黑暗。1966年至1972年间，由于电影业实际上被关闭，大陆只拍了八部电影。

Resurgence
回潮

It wasn’t until September 1978, two years after the death of Mao Zedong, that China’s premier film school – the Beijing Film Academy – reopened. Its first intake of students included Zhang Yimou, Chen Kaige and Tian Zhuangzhuang, who are considered masterminds of the celebrated ‘Fifth Generation’.
直到1978年9月，也就是毛泽东去世两年后，中国首屈一指的电影学院——北京电影学院才重新开放。第一批招收的学生包括张艺谋、陈凯歌和田壮壮，他们被认为是著名的“第五代”的策划者。

The cinematic output of the Fifth Generation signalled an escape from the dour, colourless and proletarian Mao era, and a second glittering golden age of Chinese filmmaking arrived in the 1980s and 1990s with their lush and lavish tragedies. A bleak but beautifully shot tale of a Chinese Communist Party cadre who travels to a remote village in Shaanxi province to collect folk songs, Chen Kaige’s Yellow Earth aroused little interest in China but proved a sensation when released in the West in 1985.
第五代的电影作品标志着人们摆脱了沉闷、无色和无产阶级的毛时代，中国电影制作的第二个闪闪发光的黄金时代在 1980 年代和 1990 年代到来，带来了丰富而奢华的悲剧。陈凯歌的 《黄土 》讲述了一名中国共产党干部前往陕西省一个偏远村庄收集民歌的故事，在中国几乎没有引起人们的兴趣，但在 1985 年在西方上映时引起了轰动。

It was followed by Zhang’s Red Sorghum (1989), which introduced Gong Li and Jiang Wen to the world. Gong became the poster girl of Chinese cinema in the 1990s and the first international movie star to emerge from the mainland. Jiang, the Marlon Brando of Chinese film, has proved both a durable leading man and an innovative, controversial director of award-winning films such as In the Heat of the Sun (1994) and Devils on the Doorstep (2001).
紧随其后的是张氏的《 红高粱 》（1989），将巩俐和江温介绍给了世界。龚成 1990 年代中国电影的代言人，也是第一位从内地崭露头角的国际影星。江，中国电影界的马龙·白兰度，在《烈日炎炎 》（1994）和 《门口的魔鬼 》（2001）等获奖电影中，既是一位持久的男主角，也是一位创新的、有争议的导演。

Rich, seminal works such as Farewell My Concubine (1993; Chen Kaige) and Raise the Red Lantern (1991; Zhang Yimou) were garlanded with praise, receiving standing ovations and winning major film awards. Their directors were the darlings of Cannes; Western cinema-goers were entranced. Many Chinese cinema-goers also admired their artistry, but some saw Fifth Generation output as pandering to the Western market.
丰富而开创性的作品，如 《霸王别姬》（1993 年;陈凯歌）和 《高举红灯笼 》（1991;张艺谋）被盛赞，获得全场起立鼓掌，并获得了各大电影奖项。他们的导演是戛纳的宠儿;西方电影观众被迷住了。许多中国电影观众也欣赏他们的艺术才华，但有些人认为《第五代》的作品迎合了西方市场。

In 1993 Tian Zhuangzhuang made the brilliant The Blue Kite. A heartbreaking account of the life of one Beijing family during the Cultural Revolution, it so enraged the censors that Tian was banned from making films for a decade.
1993 年，田壮壮拍了辉煌的《 蓝风筝》。这是一部关于文化大革命期间北京一家生活的令人心碎的记录，激怒了审查人员，以至于田被禁止拍摄电影十年。

Contemporary Film
当代电影

The ensuing Sixth Generation film directors eschewed the luxurious beauty of their forebears, and sought to capture the angst and grit of modern urban Chinese life. Their independent, low-budget works put an entirely different and more cynical spin on mainland Chinese filmmaking, but their darker subject matter and harsh film style (frequently in black and white) left many Western viewers cold.
随后的第六代电影导演避开了他们祖先的奢华之美，并试图捕捉现代中国城市生活的焦虑和勇气。他们独立的低成本作品为中国大陆电影制作带来了完全不同且更加愤世嫉俗的转折，但他们更黑暗的题材和严酷的电影风格（通常是黑白的）让许多西方观众感到冷淡。

A graduate of the Beijing Film Academy and associated with the Sixth Generation, Jia Zhangke emerged as a highly acclaimed film-maker, adhering to a realist, and some say minimalist, style. His meditative and compassionate look at the social impact of the construction of the Three Gorges Dam on local people, Still Life (2006), scooped the Golden Lion at the 2006 Venice Film Festival. Jia established the Pingyao International Film Festival in 2017.
贾樟柯毕业于北京电影学院，与第六代有联系，他是一位备受赞誉的电影制作人，坚持现实主义，有人说是极简主义的风格。他沉思而富有同情心地审视了三峡大坝建设对当地人的社会影响，静物 （2006 年）获得了 2006 年威尼斯电影节的金狮奖。贾某于 2017 年创办了平遥国际电影节。

Similarly educated at the Beijing Film Academy, controversial Sixth Generation director Lou Ye has a prolific and notable portfolio of sensual and atmospheric films. The tragic, noirish experience of Suzhou River (2000) is perhaps his best-known work, but Summer Palace (2006), Spring Fever (2009) and the violent Mystery (2012) maintained his reputation as an enfant terrible of China’s censorship-laden film industry.
同样，备受争议的第六代导演娄烨在北京电影学院接受教育，拥有多产且著名的感性和大气电影作品集。《 苏州河 》（2000）的悲惨、黑色的经历也许是他最著名的作品，但《 颐和园 》（2006）、《 春热》（2009）和暴力的《 神秘 》（2012）保持了他作为中国审查制度充斥的电影业的恐怖分子的声誉。

Historical wuxia (martial arts) cinema is enduringly popular in China and typified much filmmaking in the noughties, with larger-than-life films such as Hero (2002; Zhang Yimou), House of Flying Daggers (2004; Zhang Yimou) and The Banquet (2006; Feng Xiaogang) leading the way. Epic historical war dramas such as Red Cliff (2008–09; John Woo) and The Warlords (2007; Peter Chan) belong to a similar genre.
历史武侠电影在中国经久不衰，是上世纪九十年代许多电影制作的典型代表，其中有传奇色彩的电影，如 《英雄 》（2002;张艺谋）、《匕首之家 》（2004;张艺谋）和 《宴会》（2006;冯小刚）带路。史诗般的历史战争剧，如 《赤壁 》（2008-09 年;吴宇森）和军阀 （2007 年;Peter Chan）属于类似的类型。

For a taste of Kazakh folk music from northwest Xinjiang province, listen to Eagle (2009) by Mamer, an intriguing collection of songs described as ‘Chinagrass’ by their composer.
要品尝新疆西北部的哈萨克民族音乐，请听听马梅尔的《 鹰》（2009），这是一本有趣的歌曲集，被他们的作曲家描述为“中国草”。

Traditional Music
传统音乐

Musical instruments have been unearthed from tombs dating from the Shang dynasty, while Chinese folk songs can be traced back at least that far.
从商代的墓葬中出土了乐器，而中国民歌至少可以追溯到那么远。

Unlike in the West, traditional Chinese music has long valued tone over melody and uses a pentatonic scale. Music in China is also considered to have cosmological significance with musical harmony finding a parallel in dynastic harmony.
与西方不同，中国传统音乐长期以来一直重视音调而不是旋律，并使用五声音阶。中国的音乐也被认为具有宇宙学意义，音乐和谐在王朝和谐中找到了相似之处。

Traditional Chinese musical instruments include the two-stringed fiddle (èrhú), four-stringed lute (pípa), four-stringed banjo (yuèqín), two-stringed viola (húqín), horizontal flute (dízi), piccolo flute (bāngdí), vertical flute (dòngxiāo) and zither (gǔzhēng). A lot of emphasis is placed on percussion.
中国传统乐器包括两弦小提琴（èrhú）、四弦琵琶（pípa）、四弦班卓琴（yuèqín）、两弦中提琴（húqín）、水平笛（dízi）、短笛（bāngdí）、竖笛（dòngxiāo）和古筝（gǔzhēng）。打击乐非常强调。

China’s ethnic minorities have preserved their own folk-song traditions. A trip to Lijiang gives you the opportunity to appreciate the local Naxi Music Academy orchestra.
中国的少数民族保留了自己的民歌传统。丽江之旅让您有机会欣赏当地的纳西音乐学院管弦乐团。

Chinese Opera
中国戏曲

Contemporary Chinese opera, of which the most famous is Beijing opera (京剧, Jīngjù), has a continuous history of some 900 years. Evolving from a convergence of comic and ballad traditions in the Northern Song period, Chinese opera brought together a disparate range of forms: acrobatics, martial arts, poetic arias and stylised dance.
当代中国戏曲，其中最著名的是京剧 ，已有约 900 年的连续历史。中国戏曲从北宋时期喜剧和民谣传统的融合演变而来，汇集了多种不同的形式：杂技、武术、诗歌咏叹调和程式化舞蹈。

Operas were usually performed by travelling troupes who had a low social status in traditional Chinese society. Chinese law forbade mixed-sex performances, forcing actors to perform roles of the opposite sex. Opera troupes were frequently associated with homosexuality in the public imagination, contributing further to their lowly social status.
戏曲通常由在中国传统社会中社会地位较低的旅行团演出。中国法律禁止混合性别表演，强迫演员扮演异性角色。歌剧团在公众的想象中经常与同性恋联系在一起，进一步加剧了它们的低社会地位。

Formerly, opera was performed mostly on open-air stages in markets, streets, teahouses or temple courtyards. The shrill singing and loud percussion were designed to be heard over the public throng, hence their piercing qualities.
以前，歌剧主要在市场、街道、茶馆或寺庙庭院的露天舞台上演出。尖锐的歌声和响亮的打击乐旨在让公众听到，因此它们具有刺耳的品质。

Opera performances usually take place on a bare stage, with the actors taking on stylised stock characters who are instantly recognisable to the audience. Most stories are derived from classical literature and Chinese mythology, and tell of disasters, natural calamities, intrigues or rebellions.
歌剧表演通常在光秃秃的舞台上进行，演员扮演观众一眼就能认出的风格化角色。大多数故事都源自古典文学和中国神话，讲述灾害、自然灾害、阴谋或叛乱。

As well as Beijing opera, other famous Chinese operatic traditions include Cantonese opera, Kunqu (from the Jiangnan region), Min opera (from Fujian) and Shanghai opera.
除京剧外，其他著名的中国戏曲传统还包括粤剧、昆曲（来自江南地区）、闽剧（来自福建）和上海戏。

Architecture
建筑

Traditional Architecture
传统建筑

Four principal styles governed traditional Chinese architecture: imperial, religious, residential and recreational. Whatever the style, Chinese buildings traditionally followed a similar basic ground plan, consisting of a symmetrical layout oriented around a central axis – ideally running north–south to conform with basic feng shui (风水, fēngshuǐ) dictates and to maximise sunshine – with an enclosed courtyard (院, yuàn) flanked by buildings on all sides.
中国传统建筑有四种主要风格：皇家、宗教、住宅和休闲。无论风格如何，中国建筑传统上都遵循类似的基本平面图，包括围绕中轴线的对称布局——理想情况下是南北走向，以符合基本风水（ 风水 ，fēngshuǐ）的要求并最大限度地利用阳光——有一个封闭的庭院（ 院 ，yuàn）两侧是建筑物。

In many aspects imperial palaces are glorified courtyard homes (south-facing, with a sequence of courtyards, side halls and perhaps a garden at the rear) completed on a different scale and with more defensive features and decorative embellishments.
在许多方面，皇宫都是美化的四合院（朝南，有一系列庭院、侧殿，也许后面还有一个花园），以不同的规模完成，并具有更多的防御特征和装饰性装饰。

Religious Architecture
宗教建筑

Chinese Buddhist, Taoist and Confucian temples tend to follow a quite strict, schematic pattern. All temples are laid out on a north–south axis in a series of halls, with the main door of each hall facing south.
中国的佛教、道教和儒家寺庙往往遵循相当严格的示意式模式。所有寺庙均以南北轴线排列在一系列大厅中，每座大厅的正门朝南。

The sequence of halls and buildings is interspersed with open-air courtyards, allowing the weather to permeate the temple and permit qì (气, spirit) to circulate, stale air to disperse and incense to be burned.
大厅和建筑的顺序穿插着露天庭院，让天气渗透到寺庙里，让气 （ 气 ，精神）流通，陈旧的空气散去，熏香。

Buddhist Temples
佛寺

Once you have cracked the logic of Buddhist temples, you will see how most temples conform to a basic pattern, which rarely varies. The first hall and portal to the temple is generally the Hall of Heavenly Kings (天王殿, Tiānwáng Diàn) – which is sometimes also called the Mountain Gate (山门; Shānmén) – where a sedentary, central statue of the rotund Bodhisattva Maitreya is flanked by the stern and often fearsome Four Heavenly Kings. Behind is the first courtyard, where the Drum Tower (鼓楼, Gǔlóu) and Bell Tower (钟楼, Zhōnglóu) may rise to the east and west.
一旦你破解了佛教寺庙的逻辑，你就会看到大多数寺庙如何符合一个基本模式，而这种模式很少变化。通往寺庙的第一座殿堂和门户通常是天王殿（天王殿 ，Tiānwáng Diàn）——有时也被称为山门（山门 ;Shānmén）——那里有一尊久坐不动的圆形弥勒菩萨雕像，两侧是严厉且常常令人恐惧的四大天王。后面是第一个庭院，鼓楼 （ 鼓楼 ，Gǔlóu）和钟楼 （ 钟楼 ，Zhōnglóu）可以向东西方升起。

The main hall of Buddhist temples is usually the Great Treasure Hall (大雄宝殿, Dàxióngbǎo Diàn), sheltering glittering statues of the past, present and future Buddhas, seated in a row. This is the main focal point for worshippers at the temple. On the east and west interior wall of the hall are often 18 luóhàn (arhats – Buddhists who have achieved enlightenment) in two lines, either as statues or paintings. In some temples, they gather in a throng of 500, housed in a separate hall, usually called the Luohan Hall (罗汉殿, Luóhàn Diàn). A statue of Guanyin (观世音菩萨, Guānshìyīn Púsà, the Goddess of Mercy; p988) frequently stands at the rear of the main hall, facing north, atop a fish’s head or a rocky outcrop.
佛教寺庙的正殿通常是大雄宝殿（大雄宝殿 ，Dàxióngbǎo Diàn），庇护着闪闪发光的过去、现在和未来佛像，排成一排。这是寺庙礼拜者的主要焦点。大殿的东西内墙上通常有 18 个罗汉 （罗汉——成道的佛教徒），分为两行，要么是雕像，要么是绘画。在一些寺庙中，他们聚集在一个单独的大厅里，通常称为罗汉殿 （ 罗汉殿 ，Luóhàn Diàn）。观世音菩萨（观世音菩萨 ，观世音菩萨，

The rear hall may be where the sutras (Buddhist scriptures) were once stored, in which case it will be called the Sutra Storing Building (藏经楼, Cángjīnglóu). Conceived to house the remains of Buddha and later other Buddhist relics, sutras, religious artefacts and documents, a pagoda (塔, Tǎ) may rise above the main halls or may be the only surviving fragment of an otherwise destroyed temple.
后殿可能是曾经存放佛经的地方，在这种情况下，它将被称为藏经楼（藏经楼 ，Cángjīnglóu）。 一座宝塔 （ 塔 ，Tǎ）被设想用于存放佛陀的遗骸和后来的其他佛教舍利、佛经、宗教文物和文献，可能耸立在主殿之上，也可能是一座被毁坏的寺庙中唯一幸存的碎片。

Taoist Temples
道观

Taoist shrines are not as plentiful and are generally more nether-worldly than Buddhist shrines, though the basic layout echoes Buddhist temples. They are decorated with a distinct set of motifs, including the bāguà (八卦, eight trigrams) formations, reflected in eight-sided pavilions and halls, and the Taiji yin/yang (yīn/yáng) diagram. Effigies of Laotzu, the Jade Emperor and other characters popularly associated with Taoist myth, such as the Eight Immortals, Guandi and the God of Wealth, are customary.
道教圣地没有那么多，通常比佛教圣地更幽冥，尽管基本布局与佛教寺庙相呼应。它们装饰有一组独特的图案，包括反映在八面亭台楼阁和大厅中的八卦（八卦 ，八卦）阵法，以及太极阴阳 （阴/阳） 图。老子、玉皇大帝和其他与道教神话相关的人物的肖像，如八仙、关帝和财神，是习俗。

Taoist door gods, similar to those in Buddhist temples, often guard temple entrances. The main hall is usually called the Hall of the Three Clear Ones (三清殿, Sānqīng Diàn), devoted to a triumvirate of Taoist deities. Pagodas are generally absent at Taoist temples as they are Buddhist in function.
道教的门神，与佛教寺庙中的门神类似，经常守卫寺庙的入口。正殿通常被称为三清殿 （ 三清殿 ，Sānqīng Diàn），供奉道教三巨头。宝塔通常没有在道教寺庙中，因为它们的功能是佛教的。

Taoist monks (and nuns) are easily distinguished from their shaven-headed Buddhist confrères by their long hair, twisted into topknots, straight trousers and squarish jackets.
道教僧侣（和尼姑）很容易与剃光头的佛教僧侣区分开来，因为他们的长发、打成顶髻、直筒裤和方形夹克。

Confucian Temples
孔庙

Unless they have vanished or been destroyed, Confucian temples can be found in the old town district of ancient settlements throughout China and are typically very quiet havens of peace and far less visited than Buddhist or Taoist temples. The largest Confucian temple in China is at Qufu in Shandong, Confucius’ birthplace.
除非它们已经消失或被摧毁，否则孔子庙可以在中国各地古代聚落的老城区找到，通常是非常安静的和平天堂，远不如佛教或道教寺庙参观。中国最大的孔庙位于孔子的出生地山东曲阜。

Confucian temples are called either Kǒng Miào (孔庙) or Wén Miào (文庙) in Chinese and bristle with stelae celebrating local scholars, some supported on the backs of bìxì (mythical tortoiselike dragons). A statue of Kongzi (Confucius) usually resides in the main hall (大成殿; Dàchéng Diàn), overseeing rows of dusty musical instruments and flanked by disciples and philosophers.
孔庙在中文中被称为孔庙或文庙 ，上面挂满了纪念当地学者的石碑，有些还支撑在 bìxì（神话中的龟状龙）的背上。孔子（孔子）的雕像通常位于大殿（ 大成殿 ;Dàchéng Diàn），监督着一排排尘土飞扬的乐器，两侧是弟子和哲学家。

Contemporary Architecture
当代建筑

Architecturally speaking, anything goes in today’s China. You only have to look at the Pudong skyline to discover a melange of competing designs, some dramatic, inspiring and novel, others rash. The display represents a nation brimming with confidence, zeal and money.
从建筑学上讲，在今天的中国，什么都可以。你只需要看看浦东的天际线，就会发现各种相互竞争的设计，有些是戏剧性的、鼓舞人心的和新颖的，有些则是鲁莽的。该展览代表了一个充满自信、热情和金钱的国家。

Most of the top names in international architecture have designed at least one building in China in the past decade. Impressive examples of modern architecture include the National Stadium (aka the ‘Bird’s Nest’) and the CCTV Headquarters in Beijing; and the art deco–esque Jinmao Tower, the towering Shanghai World Financial Center, Tomorrow Square and the Shanghai Tower in Shanghai. In Guangzhou the Zaha Hadid–designed Guangzhou Opera House is an astonishing contemporary creation, both inside and out.
过去十年，大多数国际建筑界的顶级人物都至少在中国设计过一座建筑。令人印象深刻的现代建筑例子包括国家体育场（又名“鸟巢”）和北京中央电视台总部;以及装饰艺术风格的金茂大厦、高耸的上海环球金融中心、明日广场和上海中心大厦。在广州，扎哈·哈迪德设计的广州歌剧院从内到外都是令人惊叹的当代作品。

For something rather different, Jinhua Architecture Park, a project of artist Ai Weiwei, is an abandoned, overgrown, mouldering yet thought-provoking collection of modern memorial pavilions (designed by such names as Herzog & de Meuron), slowly returning to nature. They can be found in Jinhua, Zhejiang province.
对于一些相当不同的东西，艺术家艾未未的项目金华建筑公园是一个废弃的、杂草丛生的、发腐但发人深省的现代纪念亭馆（由赫尔佐格和德梅隆等名字设计），慢慢回归自然。它们可以在浙江省金华市找到。
