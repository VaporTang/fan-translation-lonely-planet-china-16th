History
历史

The epic sweep of China’s history can suggest prolonged epochs of peace occasionally convulsed by sudden breakup, internecine division or external attack, yet for much of its history China has been in conflict either internally or with outsiders. The Middle Kingdom’s size and shape may have continuously changed – from tiny beginnings by the Yellow River (Huáng Hé) to the subcontinent of today – but an uninterrupted thread of history runs from its earliest roots to the full flowering of Chinese civilisation.
中国历史的史诗般的横扫可能暗示着漫长的和平时代，偶尔会因突然的分裂、自相残杀或外部攻击而动荡，但在其历史的大部分时间里，中国一直处于内部或外部冲突中。中国的规模和形态可能不断变化——从黄河（Huáng Hé）边的微小起源到今天的次大陆——但从最早的根源到中华文明的全面开花，一条不间断的历史线索贯穿始终。

From Oracle Bones to Confucius
从甲骨文到孔子

The earliest ‘Chinese’ dynasty, the Shang, was long considered apocryphal. However, archaeological evidence – cattle bones and turtle shells in Henan covered in mysterious scratches, recognised by a scholar as an early form of Chinese writing – proved that a society known as the Shang developed in central China from around 1766 BC. The area it controlled was tiny – perhaps 200km across – but Chinese historians have argued that the Shang was the first Chinese dynasty. By using Chinese writing on ‘oracle bones’, the dynasty marked its connection with the Chinese civilisation of the present day.
最早的“中国”王朝商朝长期以来被认为是杜撰的。然而，考古证据——河南的牛骨和龟壳上布满了神秘的划痕，被一位学者认为是中国文字的早期形式——证明，从公元前 1766 年左右开始，中国中部就形成了一个被称为商的社会。它控制的地区很小——大约有 200 公里宽——但中国历史学家认为商朝是中国第一个王朝。通过在“甲骨文”上使用中文文字，该王朝标志着它与当今中国文明的联系。

Sometime between 1050 and 1045 BC, a neighbouring group known as the Zhou conquered Shang territory. The Zhou was one of many states competing for power in the next few hundred years, but developments during this period created some of the key sources of Chinese culture that would last till the present day. A constant theme of the first millennium BC was conflict, particularly the periods known as the ‘Spring and Autumn’ (722–481 BC) and ‘Warring States’ (475–221 BC).
公元前 1050 年至 1045 年间的某个时候，一个被称为周的邻国征服了商的领土。周是未来几百年争夺权力的众多国家之一，但这一时期的发展创造了一些中国文化的重要来源，这些文化一直延续到今天。公元前第一个千年的一个不变主题是冲突，特别是被称为“春秋”（公元前 722-481 年）和“战国”（公元前 475-221 年）的时期。

The Chinese world in the 5th century BC was both warlike and intellectually fertile, in a way similar to ancient Greece during the same period. From this disorder emerged the thinking of Confucius (551–479 BC), whose system of thought and ethics underpinned Chinese culture for 2500 years. A wandering teacher, Confucius dispensed lessons in personal behaviour and statecraft, advocating an ordered and ethical society obedient towards hierarchies and inclined towards ritual. Confucius’ desire for an ordered and ethical world was a far cry from the warfare of his times.
公元前5世纪的华夏世界，既好战又智力肥沃，在某种程度上与同一时期的古希腊相似。孔子（公元前551-479年）的思想就是从这种混乱中产生的，孔子的思想和伦理体系支撑了中国文化2500年。孔子是一位流浪的教师，他教授个人行为和治国之道，倡导一个有秩序、有道德的社会，服从等级制度，倾向于礼仪。孔子对一个有秩序和道德的世界的渴望与他那个时代的战争相去甚远。

So far some 7000 soldiers in the famous Terracotta Army have been found near Xi’an. The great tomb of the first emperor still remains unexcavated, though it is thought to have been looted soon after it was built.
到目前为止，在习安附近发现了大约 7000 名著名的兵马俑士兵。始皇帝的大陵墓至今仍未被挖掘，尽管人们认为它在建成后不久就被洗劫一空。

Early Empires
早期帝国

The Warring States period ended decisively in 221 BC. The Qin kingdom conquered other states in the central Chinese region and Qin Shi Huang proclaimed himself emperor. The first in a line of dynastic rulers that would last until 1912, Qin Shi Huang would be portrayed in later histories as particularly cruel and tyrannical, but the distinction is dubious: the ensuing Han dynasty (206 BC–AD 220) adopted many of the short-lived Qin’s practices of government.
战国时期在公元前221年果断结束。秦国征服了中国中部地区的其他国家，秦始皇自称皇帝。秦始皇是一直持续到 1912 年的王朝统治者系列中的第一个，在后来的历史中被描绘成特别残忍和暴虐，但这种区别是可疑的：随后的汉朝（公元前 206 年至公元 220 年）采用了许多短命的秦国政府做法。

Qin Shi Huang oversaw vast public works projects, including walls built by some 300,000 men, connecting defences into what would become the Great Wall. He unified the currency, measurements and written language, providing the basis for a cohesive state.
秦始皇监督了大规模的公共工程项目，包括由大约 300,000 人建造的城墙，将防御工事连接到后来的长城。他统一了货币、度量和书面语言，为一个有凝聚力的国家提供了基础。

Establishing a trend that would echo through Chinese history, a peasant, Liu Bang (256–195 BC), rose up and conquered China, founding the Han dynasty. The dynasty is so important that the name Hàn (汉, 漢) still refers to ethnic Chinese and their language (汉语, Hanyu, ‘language of the Han’). Critical to the centralisation of power, Emperor Wu (140–87 BC) institutionalised Confucian norms in government. Promoting merit as well as order, he was the first leader to experiment with examinations for entry into the bureaucracy, but his dynasty was plagued by economic troubles, as estate owners controlled more and more land. Indeed, the issue of land ownership would be a constant problem throughout Chinese history to today. Endemic economic problems and the inability to exercise control over a growing empire, coupled with social unrest that included an uprising by Taoists (known as the Yellow Turbans), led to the collapse and downfall of the Han.
农民刘邦（公元前 256-195 年）崛起并征服了中国，建立了汉朝，建立了一种贯穿中国历史的趋势。这个王朝非常重要，以至于汉（ 汉 ， 汉 ）这个名字仍然指的是华人及其语言（ 汉语 ，Hanyu，“汉语”）。武帝（公元前 140-87 年）对权力集中至关重要，将儒家规范制度化到政府中。他提倡功绩和秩序，是第一个尝试通过考试进入官僚机构的领导人，但他的王朝受到经济困境的困扰，因为庄园主控制着越来越多的土地。事实上，土地所有权问题在中国历史上一直持续到今天都是一个持续存在的问题。地方性的经济问题和无法控制不断发展的帝国，加上包括道教徒（被称为黄巾）起义在内的社会动荡，导致了汉朝的崩溃和垮台。

Han trade along the Silk Road demonstrated clearly that China was fundamentally a Eurasian power in its relations with neighbouring peoples. To the north, the Xiongnu (a name given to various nomadic tribes of Central Asia) posed the greatest threat to China. Diplomatic links were also formed with Central Asian tribes, and the great Chinese explorer Zhang Qian provided the authorities with information on the possibilities of trade and alliances in northern India. During the same period, Chinese influence percolated into areas that would later become known as Vietnam and Korea.
丝绸之路沿线的汉族贸易清楚地表明，中国在与邻国人民的关系上从根本上来说是一个欧亚大国。在北方，匈奴（中亚各个游牧部落的称呼）对中国构成了最大的威胁。还与中亚部落建立了外交联系，伟大的中国探险家张骞向当局提供了有关印度北部贸易和联盟可能性的信息。在同一时期，中国的影响力渗透到后来被称为越南和朝鲜的地区。

Evidence from Han dynasty (206 BC–AD 220) tombs suggests that a popular item of cuisine was a thick vegetable and meat stew, and that flavour enhancers such as soy sauce and honey were also used.
汉代（公元前 206 年至公元 220 年）墓葬的证据表明，一种受欢迎的美食是浓稠的蔬菜和肉类炖菜，并且还使用了酱油和蜂蜜等增味剂。

Disunity Restored
不团结恢复

Between the early 3rd and late 6th centuries AD, north China witnessed a succession of rival kingdoms vying for power while a potent division formed between north and south. Riven by warfare, the north succumbed to non-Chinese rule, most successfully during the northern Wei dynasty (386–534), founded by the Tuoba, a northern people who embraced Buddhism and left behind some of China’s finest Buddhist art, including the famous caves outside Dunhuang. A succession of rival regimes followed until nobleman Yang Jian (d 604) reunified China under the fleeting Sui dynasty (581–618). His son Sui Yangdi contributed greatly to the unification of south and north through construction of the Grand Canal, which was later extended and remained China’s most important communication route between south and north until the late 19th century. After instigating three unsuccessful incursions onto Korean soil, resulting in disastrous military setbacks, Sui Yangdi faced revolt and was assassinated in 618 by one of his high officials.
公元 3 世纪初至 6 世纪末，中国北方见证了一系列敌对王国争夺权力，同时南北之间形成了严重的分裂。北方饱受战争蹂躏，屈服于非中国统治，最成功的是在北魏王朝（386-534 年）建立的，拓跋人是一个信奉佛教的北方民族，留下了一些中国最优秀的佛教艺术，包括敦煌郊外著名的洞穴。接踵而至的是一连串的敌对政权，直到贵族杨坚（卒于 604 年）在转瞬即逝的隋朝（581-618 年）统治下统一了中国。他的儿子隋阳迪通过修建大运河，为南北统一做出了巨大贡献，大运河后来得到了延伸，直到19世纪末，这条运河一直是中国最重要的南北交通路线。在煽动了三次入侵朝鲜领土，导致灾难性的军事挫折后，隋阳帝面临叛乱，并于 618 年被他的一位高级官员暗杀。

Ban Zhao was the most famous female scholar in early China. Dating from the late 1st century AD, her work Lessons for Women advocated chastity and modesty as favoured female qualities.
班昭是中国早期最著名的女学者。她的作品 《女性教训》 可追溯到公元 1 世纪末，提倡贞洁和谦虚是女性青睐的品质。

The Tang: China Looks West
唐人：中国向西看

Tang rule (618–907) was an outward-looking time, when China embraced the culture of its neighbours – marriage to Central Asian people or wearing Indian-influenced clothes was part of the era’s cosmopolitan élan – and was influenced by the cultures of distant nations that reached the country along the Silk Road. The Chinese nostalgically regard the Tang as their cultural zenith, with Chinatowns around the world still called Tangrenjie (Tang People Streets) to this day. The output of the Tang poets is still regarded as China’s finest, as is Tang sculpture, while the dynasty’s legal code became a standard for the whole East Asian region.
唐朝统治（618-907）是一个外向型的时代，当时中国接受了邻国的文化——与中亚人结婚或穿着受印度影响的衣服是那个时代国际化的一部分——并受到沿着丝绸之路到达该国的遥远国家文化的影响。中国人怀旧地将唐人视为他们的文化顶峰，世界各地的唐人街至今仍被称为唐人街。唐代诗人的作品至今仍被认为是中国最好的作品，唐代雕塑也是如此，而唐朝的法典则成为整个东亚地区的标准。

The Tang was founded by the Sui general Li Yuan, with his achievements consolidated by his son Taizong (r 626–49). Chang’an (modern Xi’an) became the world’s most dazzling capital, with its own cosmopolitan foreign quarter, a population of one million people, a market where merchants from as far away as Persia mingled with locals, and an astonishing city wall that eventually enveloped 83 sq km. The city exemplified the Tang devotion to Buddhism, with 91 temples recorded in the city in 722, but a tolerance of, and even absorption with, foreign cultures allowed other faiths a foothold, including Nestorian Christianity, Manichaeism, Islam, Judaism and Zoroastrianism.
唐朝由隋将李渊建立，他的成就由他的儿子太宗（在位 626-49 年）巩固。长安（今习安）成为世界上最耀眼的首都，拥有自己的国际化外国区，人口达 100 万，远至波斯的商人与当地人交流的市场，以及最终包围 83 平方公里的惊人城墙。这座城市是唐朝对佛教虔诚的典范，722 年该市有 91 座寺庙的记录，但对外国文化的宽容甚至吸收让其他信仰站稳了脚跟，包括景教基督教、摩尼教、伊斯兰教、犹太教和琐罗亚斯德教。

Taizong was succeeded by a unique figure – Chinese history’s sole reigning woman emperor, Wu Zetian (r 690–705). Under her leadership the empire reached its greatest extent, spreading well north of the Great Wall and far west into inner Asia. Her strong promotion of Buddhism, however, alienated her from the Confucian officials and in 705 she was forced to abdicate in favour of Xuanzong, who would preside over the greatest disaster in the Tang’s history: the rebellion of An Lushan.
太宗继位的是一位独特的人物——中国历史上唯一的在位女皇帝武则天（690-705 年在位）。在她的领导下，帝国达到了最大的范围，从长城以北蔓延到遥远的西亚内亚。然而，她大力弘扬佛教，疏远了儒家官员，705年，她被迫退位，转而支持玄宗，玄宗将主持唐朝历史上最大的灾难：安禄山之乱。

Xuanzong appointed minorities from the frontiers as generals in the belief that they were so far removed from the political system and society that they would not harbour ideas of rebellion. Nevertheless, it was An Lushan, a general of Sogdian-Turkic parentage, who took advantage of his command in north China to make a bid for imperial power. The fighting lasted from 755 to 763, and although An Lushan was defeated, the Tang’s control over China was destroyed forever. It had ceded huge amounts of military and tax-collecting power to provincial leaders to enable them to defeat the rebels, and in doing so dissipated its own potency. A permanent change in the relationship between the government and the provinces formed. Prior to 755 the government had an idea of who owned what land throughout the empire, but after that date the central government’s control was permanently weakened. Even today the dilemma has not been fully resolved.
玄宗任命边疆少数民族为将军，认为他们与政治制度和社会相去甚远，不会怀有造反的念头。然而，却是粟特突厥血统的安禄山，利用自己在华北的指挥权，对皇权进行了争夺。战斗从755年持续到763年，安禄山虽然战败，但唐朝对中国的控制却永远被摧毁了。它将大量的军事和征税权让给了省级领导人，使他们能够击败叛乱分子，并在此过程中消散了自己的力量。政府与各省之间的关系发生了永久性的变化。在 755 年之前，政府知道谁拥有整个帝国的土地，但在那之后，中央政府的控制被永久削弱了。即使在今天，这种困境也没有完全解决。

In its last century the Tang withdrew from its former openness, turning more strongly to Confucianism, while Buddhism was outlawed by Emperor Wuzong from 842 to 845. The ban was later modified, but Buddhism never regained its previous power and prestige. The decline of the Tang dynasty was marked by imperial frailty, growing insurgencies, upheaval and chaos.
在上个世纪，唐朝从以前的开放中退缩，更加强烈地转向儒家思想，而佛教则在 842 年至 845 年间被武宗皇帝取缔。禁令后来被修改，但佛教再也没有恢复到以前的权力和威望。唐朝的衰落以帝国的衰弱、日益增长的叛乱、动荡和混乱为标志。

Two Nestorian monks smuggled silkworms out of China in AD 550, divulging the jealously guarded method of silk production to the outside world.
公元550年，两位景教僧侣将蚕走私出中国，向外界透露了这种备受戒备的丝绸生产方法。

The oldest surviving brick pagoda in China is the Songyue Pagoda, on Song Shan in Henan province, dating from the early 6th century.
中国现存最古老的砖塔是位于河南省宋山的松月塔，其历史可追溯至 6 世纪初。

The Song: Conflict & Prosperity
歌曲：冲突与繁荣

Further disunity – the fragmentary-sounding Five Dynasties or Ten Kingdoms period – followed the fall of the Tang until the northern Song dynasty (960–1127) was established. The Song dynasty existed in a state of constant conflict with its northern neighbours. The northern Song was a rather small empire coexisting with the non-Chinese Liao dynasty (which controlled a belt of Chinese territory south of the Great Wall that then marked China’s northern border) and less happily with the western Xia, another non-Chinese power that pressed hard on the northwestern provinces. In 1126 the Song lost its capital, Kaifeng, to a third non-Chinese people, the Jurchen (previously an ally against the Liao). The Song was driven to its southern capital of Hangzhou for the period of the southern Song (1127–1279), yet the period was culturally rich and economically prosperous.
进一步的分裂——听起来支离破碎的五代或十国时期——随着唐朝的灭亡，直到北宋王朝（960-1127）建立。宋朝与北方邻国的冲突不断。北宋是一个相当小的帝国，与非中华辽朝共存（辽朝控制着长城以南的一条中国领土，当时标志着中国的北部边界），与西夏共存，夏西是另一个对西北省份施加重压的非中国势力。1126 年，宋朝的首都开封被第三个非中国民族女真人（以前是反对辽国的盟友）夺走。宋朝南朝时期（1127-1279年）宋朝被赶到南都杭州，但这一时期文化丰富，经济繁荣。

The full institution of a system of examinations for entry into the Chinese bureaucracy reached fruition during the Song. At a time when brute force decided who was in control in much of medieval Europe, young Chinese men sat tests on the Confucian classics, obtaining office if successful (most were not). The system was heavily biased towards the rich, but was remarkable in its rationalisation of authority, and lasted for centuries. The classical texts set for the examinations became central to the transmission of a sense of elite Chinese culture, even though in later centuries the system’s rigidity meant it failed to adapt to social and intellectual change.
进入中国官僚机构的考试制度的全面制度在宋代取得了成果。在中世纪欧洲大部分地区，当蛮力决定谁是控制权的时代，中国年轻人参加儒家经典的考试，如果成功了就可以获得职位（大多数人没有）。该制度严重偏向富人，但在权威合理化方面却非常引人注目，并持续了几个世纪。为考试设置的古典文本成为中国精英文化传播的核心，尽管在后来的几个世纪里，该制度的僵化意味着它无法适应社会和知识的变化。

China’s economy prospered during Song rule, as cash crops and handicraft products became far more central to the economy, and a genuinely China-wide market emerged, which would become even stronger during the Ming and Qing dynasties. The sciences and arts also flourished under the Song, with intellectual and technical advances across many disciplines. Kaifeng emerged as an eminent centre of politics, commerce and culture.
宋朝统治期间，中国经济繁荣，经济作物和手工业产品在经济中变得更加重要，并出现了真正的中国市场，该市场在明清时期变得更加强大。科学和艺术也在宋朝时期蓬勃发展，许多学科的智力和技术都取得了进步。开封成为著名的政治、商业和文化中心。

The cultural quirk of foot binding appears to have emerged during the Song dynasty. For much of the next few centuries it became a Chinese social norm.
缠足的文化怪癖似乎在宋代就已经出现了。在接下来的几个世纪的大部分时间里，它成为中国的社会规范。

The Tang saw the first major rise to power of eunuchs (huànguān). Often from ethnic minority groups, they were brought to the capital and given positions within the imperial palace. In many dynasties they exercised real influence.
唐朝见证了宦官（huànguān）的第一次重大崛起。他们通常来自少数民族，被带到首都，并在皇宫内担任职务。在许多王朝中，他们发挥了真正的影响力。

Mongols to Ming
蒙古人到明朝

The fall of the Song reinforced notions of China’s Eurasian location and growing external threats. Genghis Khan (1167–1227) was beginning his rise to power, turning his gaze on China. He took Beijing in 1215, destroying and rebuilding it. His successors seized Hangzhou, the southern Song capital, in 1276. The court fled and, in 1279, southern Song resistance finally crumbled. Kublai Khan, grandson of Genghis, now reigned over all of China as emperor of the Yuan dynasty. Under Kublai the entire population was divided into categories of Han, Mongol and foreigner, with the top administrative posts reserved for Mongols, even though the examination system was revived in 1315. The latter decision unexpectedly strengthened the role of local landed elites: since elite Chinese could not advance in the bureaucracy, they decided to spend more time tending their large estates instead. Another innovation was the introduction of paper money, though overprinting created a problem with inflation.
宋朝的灭亡强化了人们对中国在欧亚大陆的位置和日益增长的外部威胁的观念。成吉思汗（1167-1227）开始掌权，将目光转向中国。他于 1215 年占领了北京，摧毁并重建了它。他的继任者于 1276 年夺取了宋南都杭州。朝廷逃亡，1279 年，南宋的抵抗终于瓦解了。成吉思汗的孙子忽必烈现在作为元朝皇帝统治着整个中国。在忽必烈的统治下，整个人口被分为汉人、蒙古人和外国人，最高行政职位保留给蒙古人，尽管考试制度在 1315 年恢复了。后一个决定出人意料地加强了当地有地精英的作用：由于中国精英无法在官僚机构中晋升，他们决定花更多时间照顾他们的大庄园。另一项创新是纸币的引入，尽管套印造成了通货膨胀问题。

The Mongols ultimately proved less adept at governance than warfare, with their empire succumbing to rebellion and eventual vanquishment within a century. Ruling as Ming emperor Hongwu, Zhu Yuanzhang established his capital in Nanjing, but by the early 15th century the court had begun to move back to Beijing, where a hugely ambitious reconstruction project was inaugurated by Emperor Yongle (r 1403–24), building the Forbidden City and devising the layout of the city we see today.
最终证明，蒙古人不擅长治理，而不是战争，他们的帝国在一个世纪内屈服于叛乱并最终被征服。朱元璋作为明朝洪武皇帝在位，将首都定在南京，但到了 15 世纪初，宫廷开始迁回北京，永乐皇帝（1403-24 年在位）启动了一项雄心勃勃的重建工程，建造了紫禁城并设计了我们今天看到的城市布局。

Though the Ming tried to impose a traditional social structure in which people stuck to hereditary occupations, the era was in fact one of great commercial growth and social change. Women became subject to stricter social norms (for instance, widow remarriage was frowned upon), but female literacy also grew. Publishing, via woodblock technology, burgeoned and the novel appeared.
尽管明朝试图强加一种传统的社会结构，让人们坚持世袭职业，但实际上那个时代是商业大增长和社会变革的时代。妇女受到更严格的社会规范的约束（例如，寡妇再婚是不受欢迎的），但女性识字率也有所提高。通过木刻技术，出版蓬勃发展，小说出现了。

Emperor Yongle, having usurped power from his nephew, was keen to establish his own legitimacy. In 1405 he launched the first of seven great maritime expeditions. Led by the eunuch general Zheng He (1371–1433), the fleet consisted of more than 60 large vessels and 255 smaller ones, carrying nearly 28,000 men. The fourth and fifth expeditions departed in 1413 and 1417, and travelled as far as the present Middle East. The great achievement of these voyages was to bring tribute missions to the capital, including two embassies from Egypt. The emperors who succeeded Yongle had little interest in continuing the voyages, however, and China dropped anchor on its global maritime explorations.
永乐皇帝从侄子手中夺取了权力，热衷于建立自己的合法性。1405 年，他发起了七次伟大海上探险中的第一次。舰队由宦官将军郑和（1371-1433）率领，由60多艘大船和255艘小船组成，载有近28,000人。第四次和第五次探险队于 1413 年和 1417 年出发，远至现在的中东。这些航行的伟大成就是将贡品使团带到首都，其中包括来自埃及的两个大使馆。然而，继永乐号的皇帝们对继续航行没什么兴趣，中国开始了全球海洋探险。

The Great Wall was re-engineered and clad in brick, while ships also arrived from Europe, presaging an overseas threat that would develop from entirely different directions. Traders were quickly followed by missionaries, and the Jesuits, led by the formidable Matteo Ricci, made their way inland and established a presence at court. Ricci learned fluent Chinese and spent years agonising over how Christian tenets could be made attractive in a Confucian society with distinctive norms. The Portuguese presence linked China directly to trade with the New World, which had opened up in the 16th century. New crops, such as potatoes, maize, cotton and tobacco, were introduced, further stimulating the commercial economy. Merchants often lived opulent lives, building fine private gardens (as in Suzhou) and buying delicate flowers and fruits.
长城经过重新设计并用砖砌成，而船只也从欧洲抵达，预示着海外威胁将从完全不同的方向发展。传教士很快跟随商人，耶稣会士在强大的利玛窦的带领下进入内陆并在宫廷中建立了存在。利玛窦学会了流利的汉语，并花了数年时间苦思冥想如何在具有独特规范的儒家社会中使基督教教义变得有吸引力。葡萄牙的存在将中国与 16 世纪开放的新大陆的贸易直接联系起来。引进了马铃薯、玉米、棉花和烟草等新作物，进一步刺激了商业经济。商人经常过着富裕的生活，建造精美的私人花园（如苏州），购买精致的鲜花和水果。

The Ming was eventually undermined by internal power struggles. Natural disasters, including drought and famine, combined with a menace from the north: the Manchu, a nomadic warlike people, who saw the turmoil within China and invaded.
明朝最终因内部权力斗争而受到破坏。包括干旱和饥荒在内的自然灾害，再加上来自北方的威胁：满清人，一个好战的游牧民族，他们看到中国国内的动荡并入侵。

Toilet paper was first used in China as early as the 6th century AD, when it was employed by the wealthy and privileged for sanitary purposes.
卫生纸早在公元6世纪就首次在中国使用，当时它被富人和特权阶层用于卫生目的。

A Chinese woodblock-printed copy of the Diamond Sutra, kept in the British Library, is the earliest dated printed book, created in AD 868. Visit the library website (www.bl.uk/onlinegallery/sacredtexts/diamondsutra.html) to turn the pages of the sutra online.
大英图书馆收藏的中国木刻版《 金刚经 》是最早的印刷书籍，创作于公元 868 年。访问图书馆网站 （www.bl.uk/onlinegallery/sacredtexts/diamondsutra.html） 在线翻阅佛经页面。

The Qing: the Path to Dynastic Dissolution
清朝：王朝解体之路

After conquering just a small part of China and assuming control in the disarray, the Manchu named their new dynasty the Qing (1644–1911). Once ensconced in the (now torched) Forbidden City, the Manchu realised they needed to adapt their nomadic way of life to suit the agricultural civilisation of China. Threats from inner Asia were neutralised by incorporating the Qing homeland of Manchuria into the empire, as well as that of the Mongols, whom they had subordinated. Like the Mongols before them, the conquering Manchu found themselves in charge of a civilisation whose government they had defeated, but whose cultural power far exceeded their own. The result was quite contradictory: on the one hand, Qing rulers took great pains to win the allegiance of high officials and cultural figures by displaying a familiarity and respect for traditional Chinese culture; but on the other hand, the Manchu rulers enforced strict rules of social separation between the Han and Manchu. The Qing flourished most greatly under three emperors who ruled for a total of 135 years: Kangxi, Yongzheng and Qianlong.
在征服了中国的一小部分并在混乱中占据了控制权后，满清人将他们的新王朝命名为清朝（1644-1911）。满清人一旦被困在紫禁城（现已被焚毁），他们就意识到他们需要调整游牧生活方式以适应中国的农业文明。通过将清朝故乡满洲以及他们所隶属的蒙古人的祖国并入帝国，消除了来自内亚的威胁。就像他们之前的蒙古人一样，征服的满清人发现自己掌管着一个文明，他们的政府被他们打败了，但其文化力量却远远超过了他们自己的政府。结果颇为矛盾：一方面，清朝统治者费尽心思，通过表现出对中国传统文化的熟悉和尊重，赢得高官和文化人士的效忠;但另一方面，满清统治者在汉满之间执行了严格的社会分离规则。清朝在三位皇帝的统治下最为繁荣，共统治了 135 年：康熙、雍正和乾隆。

Much of the map of China that we know today derives from the Qing period. Territorial expansion and expeditions to regions of Central Asia spread Chinese power and culture further than ever. The expansion of the 18th century was fuelled by economic and social changes. The discovery of the New World by Europeans in the 15th century led to a new global market in American food crops, such as chillies and sweet potatoes, allowing food crops to be grown in more barren regions, where wheat and rice had not flourished. In the 18th century the Chinese population doubled from around 150 million to 300 million people.
我们今天所知道的中国地图大部分都源自清代。领土扩张和对中亚地区的远征使中国的力量和文化传播得比以往任何时候都更远。18 世纪的扩张是由经济和社会变革推动的。15世纪欧洲人发现新大陆，导致辣椒和红薯等美国粮食作物的全球新市场，使得粮食作物得以在小麦和水稻尚未繁荣的更贫瘠的地区种植。18世纪，中国人口翻了一番，从约1.5亿增加到3亿。

Historians now take very seriously the idea that in the 18th century China was among the most advanced economies in the world. The impact of imperialism would help commence China’s slide down the table, but the seeds of decay had been sown long before the Opium Wars of the 1840s. Put simply, as China’s size expanded, its state remained too small. China’s dynasty failed to expand the size of government to cope with the new realities of a larger China.
历史学家现在非常重视这样一种观点，即在18世纪，中国是世界上最发达的经济体之一。帝国主义的影响将帮助中国开始滑落，但衰败的种子早在 1840 年代鸦片战争之前就已经播下。简而言之，随着中国国土面积的扩大，其国度仍然太小。中国王朝未能扩大政府规模以应对更大中国的新现实。

War & Reform
战争与改革

For the Manchu, the single most devastating incident was not either of the Opium Wars, but the far more destructive anti-Qing Taiping Rebellion of 1850–64, an insurgency motivated partly by a foreign credo (Christianity). Established by Hakka leader Hong Xiuquan, the Heavenly Kingdom of Great Peace (Taiping Tianguo) banned opium and intermingling between the sexes, made moves to redistribute property and was fiercely anti-Manchu. The Qing eventually reconquered the Taiping capital at Nanjing, but upwards of 20 million Chinese died in the uprising.
对满清人来说，最具破坏性的事件不是鸦片战争，而是1850-64年更具破坏性的反清太平天国运动，这场叛乱部分是由外国信条（基督教）推动的。由客家首领洪秀全建立的大和平天国（太平天国）禁止鸦片和两性杂居，采取重新分配财产的举措，并强烈反满。清朝最终在南京重新征服了太平天国首都，但有超过 2000 万中国人在起义中丧生。

The events that finally toppled the dynasty, however, came in rapid succession. Foreign imperialist incursions continued and Western powers nibbled away at China’s coastline. Shanghai, Qingdao, Tianjin, Gulang Yu, Shantou, Yantai, Weihai, Ningbo and Beihai would all either fall under semi-colonial rule or enclose foreign concessions. Hong Kong was a British colony and Macau was administered by the Portuguese. Attempts at self-strengthening were dealt a brutal blow by the Sino-Japanese War of 1894–95. Fought over control of Korea, it ended with the humiliating destruction of the new Qing navy. Not only was Chinese influence in Korea lost, but Taiwan was also ceded to Japan.
然而，最终推翻王朝的事件接踵而至。外国帝国主义的入侵仍在继续，西方列强蚕食了中国的海岸线。上海、青岛、天津、鼓浪屿、汕头、烟台、威海、宁波和北海要么属于半殖民地统治，要么将外国租界封闭。香港是英国的殖民地，澳门由葡萄牙人管理。1894-95 年的甲午战争对自我强势的尝试造成了残酷的打击。为了争夺朝鲜的控制权，最终以新清海军的耻辱性摧毁而告终。不仅中国在朝鲜失去了影响力，台湾也被割让给了日本。

Japan itself was a powerful Asian example of reform. In 1868 Japan’s rulers, unnerved by ever-greater foreign encroachment, had overthrown the centuries-old system of the Shōgun, who acted as regent for the emperor. An all-out program of modernisation, including a new army, constitution, educational system and railway network, was launched, all of which gave Chinese reformers a lot to ponder.
日本本身就是亚洲改革的一个有力例子。1868 年，日本统治者对日益严重的外国侵占感到不安，推翻了长达数百年的幕府将军制度，幕府将军担任天皇摄政王。全面现代化计划的启动，包括新的军队、宪法、教育体系和铁路网，所有这些都给中国的改革者带来了很多思考。

One of the boldest proposals for change, which drew heavily on the Japanese model, was the program put forward in 1898 by reformers including the political thinker Kang Youwei (1858–1927). However, in September 1898 the reforms were abruptly halted, as the Dowager Empress Cixi, fearful of a coup, placed the emperor under house arrest and executed several of the leading advocates of change. Two years later Cixi made a decision that helped to seal the Qing’s fate. In 1900 north China was convulsed by attacks from a group of peasant rebels whose martial arts techniques led them to be labelled the Boxers, and who sought to expel foreigners and kill Chinese Christian converts. In a major miscalculation the dynasty declared its support for the Boxers in June. Eventually a multinational foreign army forced its way into China and defeated the uprising, which had besieged the foreign Legation Quarter in Beijing. The imperial powers then demanded huge financial reparations from the Qing. In 1902 the dynasty reacted by implementing the Xinzheng (New Governance) reforms. This set of reforms, now half-forgotten in contemporary China, looks remarkably progressive, even set against the standards of the present day.
最大胆的变革建议之一是包括政治思想家康有为（1858-1927）在内的改革家于 1898 年提出的纲领，它大量借鉴了日本模式。然而，1898 年 9 月，改革突然停止，因为慈禧太后担心政变，将皇帝软禁并处决了几位主要的变革倡导者。两年后，慈禧做出了一个决定，帮助确定了清朝的命运。1900年，中国北方因一群农民起义军的袭击而动荡不安，他们的武术技术使他们被贴上义和团的标签，并试图驱逐外国人并杀害中国基督教皈依者。在一次重大的误判中，王朝于 6 月宣布支持义和团。最终，一支多国外国军队强行进入中国，击败了围困北京外国公使馆的起义。帝国列强随后向清朝索要巨额经济赔款。1902 年，王朝的反应是实施新政（新政）改革。这套改革现在在当代中国已经被遗忘了一半，但看起来非常进步，甚至与当今的标准背道而驰。

The Cantonese revolutionary Sun Yatsen (1866–1925) remains one of the few modern historical figures respected in both China and Taiwan. Sun and his Revolutionary League made multiple attempts to undermine Qing rule in the late 19th century, raising sponsorship and support from a wide-ranging combination of the Chinese diaspora, the newly emergent middle class and traditional secret societies.
广东革命家孙中山（1866-1925）仍然是中国和台湾为数不多的受人尊敬的现代历史人物之一。孙中山和他的革命同盟会在 19 世纪末多次试图破坏清朝的统治，从华人侨民、新兴的中产阶级和传统秘密社团的广泛组合中筹集到赞助和支持。

The end of the Qing dynasty arrived swiftly. Throughout China’s southwest, popular resentment against the dynasty had been fuelled by reports that railway rights in the region were being sold to foreigners. A local uprising in the city of Wuhan in October 1911 was discovered early, leading the rebels to take over command in the city and hastily declare independence from the Qing dynasty. Within a space of days, then weeks, most of China’s provinces did likewise. Provincial assemblies across China declared themselves in favour of a republic, with Sun Yatsen (who was not even in China at the time) as their candidate for president.
清朝的末日来得很快。在整个中国西南地区，有报道称该地区的铁路权被卖给外国人，这加剧了民众对王朝的不满。1911年10月武汉市的一场地方起义被早早地发现，导致起义军接管了该市的指挥权，并匆忙宣布脱离清朝独立。在几天，然后几周的时间里，中国的大多数省份都这样做了。中国各地的省议会都宣布支持共和制，孙中山（当时甚至不在中国）作为他们的国家主席候选人。

In the 18th century the Chinese used an early form of vaccination against smallpox that required not an injection but rather the blowing of serum up the patient’s nose.
在 18 世纪，中国人使用了一种早期的天花疫苗接种方式，不需要注射，而是将血清吹到患者的鼻子上。

Qing emperor Kangxi sponsored a vast encyclopedia of Chinese culture, which is still read by scholars today.
清朝康熙皇帝赞助了一部庞大的中国文化百科全书，至今仍被学者们阅读。

The Republic: Instability & Ideas
共和国：不稳定与思想

The Republic of China lasted less than 40 years on the mainland (1912–1949) and continues to be regarded as an unstable period in modern Chinese history, when the country was under threat from what many described as ‘imperialism from without and warlordism from within’. Yet there was also breathing room for new ideas and culture.
中华民国在大陆持续了不到 40 年（1912-1949 年），并且仍然被视为中国近代史上的一个不稳定时期，当时该国正受到许多人所说的“帝国主义来自外部，军阀主义来自内部”的威胁。然而，新思想和文化也有喘息的空间。

Sun Yatsen returned to China and only briefly served as president, before having to make way for militarist leader Yuan Shikai. In 1912 China held its first general election, and it was Sun’s newly established Kuomintang (Nationalist; Guómíndǎng, literally ‘Party of the National People’) party that emerged as the largest grouping. Parliamentary democracy did not last long, as the Kuomintang itself was outlawed by Yuan, and Sun had to flee into exile in Japan. However, after Yuan’s death in 1916, the country split into rival regions ruled by militarist warlord-leaders. Supposedly ‘national’ governments in Beijing often controlled only parts of northern or eastern China and had no real claim to control over the rest of the country. Also, in reality, the foreign powers still had control over much of China’s domestic and international situation. Britain, France, the US and the other Western powers showed little desire to lose those rights, such as extraterritoriality and tariff control.
孙中山回国后只短暂担任过总统，就不得不让位给军国主义领袖袁世凯。1912 年，中国举行了第一次大选，是孙中山新成立的国民党（国民党;Guómíndǎng，字面意思是“国民人民党”）党，成为最大的集团。议会民主制并没有持续多久，因为国民党本身被袁取缔，孙中山不得不逃亡日本。然而，1916 年袁氏去世后，中国分裂成由军国主义军阀领导人统治的敌对地区。据称，北京的“国家”政府通常只控制中国北部或东部的部分地区，而没有真正控制中国其他地区的权利。此外，实际上，外国势力仍然控制着中国的大部分国内和国际局势。英国、法国、美国和其他西方大国几乎不想失去这些权利，例如治外法权和关税管制。

Shanghai became the focal point for the contradictions of Chinese modernity. By the early 20th century, Shanghai was a wonder not just of China but of the world, with skyscrapers, art deco apartment blocks, neon lights, women (and men) in outrageous new fashions, and a vibrant, commercially minded, take-no-prisoners mindset. The racism that accompanied imperialism was visible every day, as Europeans kept themselves separate from the Chinese. Yet the glamour of modernity was undeniable too, as workers flocked from rural areas to make a living in the city, and Chinese intellectuals sought out French fashion, British architecture and American movies. In the prewar period Shanghai had more millionaires than anywhere else in China, yet its inequalities and squalor would also inspire the first congress of the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) in 1921.
上海成为中国现代性矛盾的焦点。到 20 世纪初，上海不仅是中国的奇迹，也是世界的奇迹，摩天大楼、装饰艺术风格的公寓楼、霓虹灯、穿着令人发指的新时尚的女性（和男性），以及充满活力、具有商业头脑、不拘一格的心态。伴随帝国主义的种族主义每天都是显而易见的，因为欧洲人将自己与中国人分开。然而，现代性的魅力也是不可否认的，因为工人从农村涌入城市谋生，中国知识分子寻找法国时尚、英国建筑和美国电影。在战前时期，上海的百万富翁数量比中国其他任何地方都多，但其不平等和肮脏也激发了 1921 年中国共产党 （CCP） 第一次代表大会的召开。

The militarist government that held power in Beijing in 1917 provided 96,000 Chinese to serve on the Western Front in Europe in support of the Allies, not as soldiers but as trench-diggers and labourers. This involvement in WWI led to one of the most important events in China’s modern history: the student demonstrations of 4 May 1919.
1917年在北京掌权的军国主义政府提供了96,000名中国人在欧洲的西线服役，以支持盟军，他们不是作为士兵，而是作为挖壕机和劳工。第一次世界大战的参与导致了中国现代史上最重要的事件之一：1919 年 5 月 4 日的学生示威。

Double-dealing by the Western Allies and Chinese politicians who had made secret deals with Japan led to an unwelcome discovery for the Chinese diplomats at the Paris Peace Conference in 1919. Germany had been defeated, but its Chinese territories – such as Qingdao – were not to be returned to China but would instead go to Japan. Five days later, on 4 May 1919, some 3000 students gathered in front of the Gate of Heavenly Peace in central Beijing, and then marched to the house of a Chinese government minister closely associated with Japan. Once there, they broke in and destroyed the house. Over in a few hours, the event immediately found a place in modern Chinese folklore.
西方盟国和中国政客与日本进行秘密交易的双重交易导致中国外交官在 1919 年巴黎和会上发现了一个不受欢迎的发现。德国战败了，但其中国领土——如青岛——不会归还中国，而是归还给日本。五天后，即1919年5月4日，大约3000名学生聚集在北京市中心的天安门前，然后游行到一位与日本关系密切的中国政府部长的家中。到达那里后，他们闯入并摧毁了房子。几个小时后，该事件立即在现代中国民间传说中占有一席之地。

The student demonstration came to symbolise a much wider shift in Chinese society and politics. The May 4th Movement, as it became known, was associated closely with the New Culture, underpinned by the electrifying ideas of ‘Mr Science’ and ‘Mr Democracy’. In literature, a May Fourth generation of authors wrote works attacking the Confucianism that they felt had brought China to its current crisis, and explored new issues of sexuality and self-development. The CCP, later the mastermind of the world’s largest peasant revolution, was created in the intellectual turmoil of the movement, with many of its founding figures associated with Peking University, including Chen Duxiu (dean of humanities), Li Dazhao (head librarian) and the young Mao Zedong, a mere library assistant.
学生示威活动象征着中国社会和政治的更广泛转变。众所周知，5 月 4 日运动与新文化密切相关，以“科学先生”和“民主先生”的激动人心的思想为基础。在文学方面，五四代作家写了攻击他们认为将中国带入当前危机的儒家思想的作品，并探讨了性和自我发展的新问题。中共后来成为世界上最大的农民革命的策划者，是在这场运动的思想动荡中创建的，其许多创始人物都与北京大学有关，包括陈独秀（人文学院院长）、李大钊（图书馆馆长）和年轻的毛泽东，只是一名图书馆助理。

DIRTY FOREIGN MUD
肮脏的外国泥浆

Although trade in opium had been banned in China by imperial decree at the end of the 18th century, the cohong (local merchants’ guild) in Guangzhou helped ensure that it continued, and fortunes were amassed on both sides. When the British East India Company lost its monopoly on China trade in 1834, imports of the drug increased to 40,000 chests a year.
尽管鸦片贸易在 18 世纪末被圣旨禁止在中国，但广州的 cohong（当地商会）帮助确保了鸦片贸易的继续，双方都积累了财富。1834 年，当英国东印度公司失去对中国贸易的垄断权时，该药的进口量增加到每年 40,000 箱。

In 1839 the Qing government sent Imperial Commissioner Lin Zexu to stamp out the opium trade once and for all. Lin successfully blockaded the British in Guangzhou and publicly burned the ‘foreign mud’ in Humen. Furious, the British sent an expeditionary force of 4000 men from the Royal Navy to exact reparations and secure favourable trade arrangements.
1839年，清政府派出御史大夫林则徐，一劳永逸地铲除鸦片贸易。林成功封锁了英国在广州，并在虎门公开焚烧了“洋泥”。英国人愤怒地派出了一支由皇家海军组成的 4000 人的远征军，以进行赔偿并确保有利的贸易安排。

What would become known as the First Opium War began in June 1840 when British forces besieged Guangzhou and forced the Chinese to cede five ports to the British. With the strategic Yangzi River city of Nanking (Nanjing) under immediate threat, the Chinese were forced to accept Britain’s terms in the Treaty of Nanking.
后来被称为第一次鸦片战争的战争始于 1840 年 6 月，当时英国军队围困了广州，迫使中国人将五个港口割让给英国人。随着扬子江战略城市南京（南京）受到直接威胁，中国人被迫接受英国在《南京条约》中的条款。

The treaty abolished the monopoly system of trade, opened the ‘treaty ports’ to British residents and foreign trade, exempted British nationals from all Chinese laws and ceded the island of Hong Kong to the British ‘in perpetuity’. The treaty, signed in August 1842, set the scope and character of the unequal relationship between China and the West for the next half-century.
该条约废除了贸易垄断制度，向英国居民和对外贸易开放“通商口岸”，免除英国国民所有中国法律的约束，并将香港岛“永久”割让给英国。该条约于 1842 年 8 月签署，确定了未来半个世纪中西不平等关系的范围和特征。

Ping-pong (pīngpāngqiú) may be China’s national sport (guóqiú), but it was invented as an after-dinner game by British Victorians who named it wiff-waff and first used a ball made from champagne corks.
乒乓球 （pīngpāngqiú） 可能是中国的国球 （guóqiú）， 但它是由英国维多利亚时代人发明的，作为餐后游戏，他们将其命名为 wiff-waff，并首先使用由香槟软木塞制成的球。

The Northern Expedition
北伐

After years of vainly seeking international support for his cause, Sun Yatsen found allies in the newly formed Soviet Russia. The Soviets ordered the fledgling CCP to ally itself with the much larger ‘bourgeois’ party, the Kuomintang. Their alliance was attractive to Sun: the Soviets would provide political training, military assistance and finance. From their base in Guangzhou, the Kuomintang and CCP trained together from 1923 in preparation for their mission to reunite China.
在多年徒劳地为他的事业寻求国际支持后，孙中山在新成立的苏维埃俄罗斯找到了盟友。苏联命令刚刚起步的中共与规模大得多的“资产阶级”政党国民党结盟。他们的联盟对孙中山很有吸引力：苏联将提供政治培训、军事援助和资金。国民党和中共从广州基地开始，从 1923 年开始一起训练，为统一中国的使命做准备。

Sun died of cancer in 1925. The succession battle in the party coincided with a surge in antiforeign feeling that accompanied the May Thirtieth Incident when 13 labour demonstrators were killed by British police in Shanghai on 30 May 1925. Under Soviet advice the Kuomintang and CCP prepared for their ‘Northern Expedition’, the big 1926 push north that was supposed to finally unite China. In 1926–27 the Soviet-trained National Revolutionary Army made its way slowly north, fighting, bribing or persuading its opponents into accepting Kuomintang control. The most powerful military figure turned out to be an officer from Zhejiang named Chiang Kaishek (1887–1975). Trained in Moscow, Chiang moved steadily forward and finally captured the great prize, Shanghai, in March 1927. However, a horrific surprise was in store for his communist allies. The Soviet advisers had not impressed Chiang and he was increasingly convinced that the communists aimed to use their cooperation with the Kuomintang to seize control themselves. Instead, Chiang struck first. Using local thugs and soldiers, Chiang organised a lightning strike by rounding up CCP activists and union leaders in Shanghai and killing thousands of them.
孙于1925年因癌症去世。党内的继任之争恰逢 1925 年 5 月 30 日 13 名劳工示威者在上海被英国警察杀害的 5 月三十事变带来的反外国情绪高涨。在苏联的建议下，国民党和中共为他们的“北伐”做准备，即 1926 年的大规模北伐，本应最终统一中国。1926-27 年，苏联训练的国民革命军缓慢向北进发，战斗、贿赂或说服对手接受国民党的控制。最有权势的军事人物竟然是一位来自浙江的军官蒋介石（1887-1975）。蒋在莫斯科受训后，稳步前进，最终于 1927 年 3 月夺取了大奖——上海。然而，他的共产主义盟友却遭遇了可怕的惊喜。苏联顾问并没有给蒋留下深刻的印象，他越来越相信共产党人的目的是利用他们与国民党的合作来夺取控制权。相反，蒋先发制人。蒋介石利用当地暴徒和士兵组织了一次闪电袭击，在上海围捕了中共活动人士和工会领导人，并杀死了数千人。

The first railroad in China was the Woosung Railway, which opened in 1876, running between Shanghai and Wusong. It operated for less than a year before being dismantled and shipped to Taiwan.
中国第一条铁路是宇城铁路，于1876年开通，往返于上海和吴淞之间。它运行了不到一年，就被拆除并运往台湾。

Kuomintang Rule
国民党统治

Chiang Kaishek’s Kuomintang government officially came to power in 1928 through a combination of military force and popular support. Marked by corruption, it suppressed political dissent with great ruthlessness. Yet Chiang’s government also kick-started a major industrialisation effort, greatly augmented China’s transport infrastructure and successfully renegotiated what many Chinese called ‘unequal treaties’ with Western powers. In its first two years, the Kuomintang doubled the length of highways in China and increased the number of students studying engineering. The government never really controlled more than a few (very important) provinces in the east, however, and China remained significantly disunited. Regional militarists continued to control much of western China, the Japanese invaded and occupied Manchuria in 1931, and the communists re-established themselves in the northwest.
蒋介石的国民党政府于1928年通过军事力量和民众支持的结合，正式上台。以腐败为标志，它以极其无情的方式镇压政治异议。然而，蒋介石政府还启动了一项重大的工业化努力，极大地增强了中国的交通基础设施，并成功地与西方列强重新谈判了许多中国人所说的“不平等条约”。在建国的头两年里，国民党将中国高速公路的长度增加了一倍，并增加了学习工程的学生人数。然而，政府从未真正控制过东部的几个（非常重要的）省份，中国仍然严重不团结。地区军国主义继续控制中国西部的大部分地区，日本人于 1931 年入侵并占领满洲，共产党在西北重新建立自己的地位。

In 1934 Chiang Kaishek launched his own ideological counterargument to communism: the New Life Movement. This was supposed to be a complete spiritual renewal of the nation through a modernised version of traditional Confucian values, such as propriety, righteousness and loyalty. The New Life Movement demanded that the renewed citizens of the nation must wear frugal but clean clothes, consume products made in China rather than seek luxurious foreign goods, and behave in a hygienic manner. Yet Chiang’s ideology never had much success. Against a background of massive agricultural and fiscal crisis, prescriptions about what to wear and how to behave lacked popular appeal.
1934年，蒋介石发起了他自己的意识形态反共主义反驳：新生活运动。这本应是通过礼、义、忠等传统儒家价值观的现代化版本，对民族进行彻底的精神更新。新生活运动要求，更新的国民必须穿着节俭但干净的衣服，消费中国制造的产品，而不是寻求奢侈的外国商品，并保持卫生。然而，蒋介石的意识形态从未取得太大成功。在大规模农业和财政危机的背景下，关于穿什么和如何举止的处方缺乏大众吸引力。

The Long March
长征

The communists had not stood still, and after Chiang’s treachery most of what remained of the CCP fled to the countryside. A major centre of activity was the communist stronghold in impoverished Jiangxi province, where the party began to try out systems of government that would eventually bring them to power. However, by 1934 Chiang’s previously ineffective ‘extermination campaigns’ were making the CCP’s position in Jiangxi untenable and the Red Army found itself increasingly encircled by Nationalist troops, prompting the CCP to commence its legendary Long March. Travelling over 6400km, just 4000 of the original 80,000 communists who set out eventually arrived, exhausted, in distant Shaanxi province in the northwest, far out of the reach of the Kuomintang. It seemed possible that within a matter of months, however, Chiang would attack again and wipe them out.
共产党人并没有停滞不前，在蒋介石背叛之后，中共的大部分剩余人员都逃到了农村。一个主要的活动中心是贫困的江西省的共产党据点，中共开始在那里尝试最终让他们掌权的政府制度。然而，到了 1934 年，蒋介石之前无效的“灭绝运动”使中共在江西的地位站不住脚，红军发现自己越来越被国民党军队包围，促使中共开始了传奇的长征。在6400多公里的路程中，最初出发的8万名共产党人中，只有4000人最终疲惫不堪地抵达了遥远的陕西省，远远超出了国民党的范围。然而，在几个月内，蒋介石似乎会再次进攻并消灭他们。

The approach of war saved the CCP. There was growing public discontent at Chiang Kaishek’s seeming unwillingness to fight the Japanese, a perception that was perhaps unfair. The Kuomintang had undertaken retraining of key regiments in the army under German advice, and also started to plan for a wartime economy from 1931, spurred on by the Japanese invasion of Manchuria. Events came to a head in December 1936, however, when the Chinese militarist leader of Manchuria (General Zhang Xueliang) and the CCP kidnapped Chiang. As a condition of his release, Chiang agreed to an openly declared United Front – the Kuomintang and communists would put aside their differences and join forces against Japan.
战争的临近拯救了中共。公众对蒋介石似乎不愿意与日本人作战的不满情绪越来越高，这种看法也许是不公平的。国民党在德国的建议下对军队中的关键团进行了再培训，并在日本入侵满洲的刺激下从 1931 年开始规划战时经济。然而，1936 年 12 月，当满洲的中国军国主义领导人（张学良将军）和中共绑架蒋介石时，事件达到了顶峰。作为释放他的条件，蒋同意公开宣布统一战线——国民党和共产党将搁置分歧，联手对抗日本。

War & the Kuomintang
战争与国民党

China’s status as a major participant in WWII is often overlooked or forgotten in the West. The Japanese invasion of China, which began in 1937, was merciless, with the notorious Nanjing Massacre (also known as the Rape of Nanjing) just one of a string of war crimes committed by the Japanese Army during its conquest of eastern China. The government had to operate in exile from the far southwestern hinterland of China, as its area of greatest strength and prosperity, China’s eastern seaboard, was lost to Japanese occupation.
中国作为二战主要参与者的地位常常被西方忽视或遗忘。1937年开始的日本侵华行动是无情的，臭名昭著的南京大屠杀（又称南京大屠杀）只是日军在征服中国东部期间犯下的一系列战争罪行之一。政府不得不从中国遥远的西南腹地流亡，因为其最强大和最繁荣的地区——中国东部沿海地区被日本占领。

Paul French’s Midnight in Peking (2012) is a gripping true-crime murder-mystery book examining the brutal murder of Pamela Werner, daughter of a retired British diplomat, in 1937 Peking.
保罗·弗伦奇 （Paul French） 的《 午夜北京》（2012 年）是一本扣人心弦的真实犯罪谋杀悬疑书，讲述了 1937 年北京一名退休英国外交官的女儿帕梅拉·维尔纳 （Pamela Werner） 被残忍谋杀的事件。

It is now acknowledged that both the Kuomintang and the communists played important roles in defeating Japan. Chiang, not Mao, was the internationally acknowledged leader of China during this period, and despite his government’s multitude of flaws, he maintained resistance to the end. However, his government was also increasingly trapped, having retreated to Sichuan province and a temporary capital at Chongqing. Safe from land attack by Japan, the city still found itself under siege, subjected to some of the heaviest bombing in the war. From 1940 supply routes were cut off as the road to Burma was closed by Britain, under pressure from Japan, and Vichy France closed connections to Vietnam. Though the US and Britain brought China on board as an ally against Japan after the bombing of Pearl Harbor on 7 December 1941, the Allied ‘Europe First’ strategy meant that China was always treated as a secondary theatre of war. Chiang Kaishek’s corruption and leadership qualities were heavily criticised, and while these accusations were not groundless, without Chinese Kuomintang armies (which kept one million Japanese troops bogged down in China for eight years), the Allies’ war in the Pacific would have been far harder. The communists had an important role as guerrilla fighters, but did far less fighting in battle than the Kuomintang.
现在人们承认，国民党和共产党在打败日本的过程中都发挥了重要作用。蒋介石，而不是毛，是这一时期国际公认的中国领导人，尽管他的政府存在诸多缺陷，但他一直坚持到底。然而，他的政府也越来越陷入困境，撤退到四川省，临时首都重庆。这座城市免受日本的陆地攻击，但仍然发现自己处于围困之中，遭受了战争中最猛烈的轰炸。从 1940 年起，由于英国在日本的压力下关闭了通往缅甸的道路，维希法国关闭了与越南的连接，补给路线被切断。尽管美国和英国在 1941 年 12 月 7 日珍珠港爆炸后将中国作为对抗日本的盟友加入，但盟军的“欧洲优先”战略意味着中国始终被视为次要战区。蒋介石的腐败和领导才能受到严厉批评，虽然这些指控并非空穴来风，但如果没有中国国民党军队（让 100 万日军在中国陷入困境八年），盟军在太平洋上的战争会更加艰难。共产党人作为游击队战士发挥着重要作用，但在战斗中的战斗远不如国民党。

The real winners from WWII, however, were the communists. They undertook important guerrilla campaigns against the Japanese across northern and eastern China, but the really key changes were taking place in the bleak, dusty hill country centred on the small town of Yan’an, capital of the CCP’s largest stronghold. The ‘Yan’an way’ that developed in those years solidified many CCP policies: land reform involving redistribution of land to the peasants, lower taxes, a self-sufficient economy, ideological education and, underpinning it all, the CCP’s military force, the Red Army. By the end of the war with Japan, the communist areas had expanded massively, with 900,000 troops in the Red Army, and party membership at a new high of 1.2 million.
然而，二战中真正的赢家是共产党人。他们在中国北方和东部对日军进行了重要的游击战，但真正关键的变化发生在以中共最大据点首府延安小镇为中心的荒凉、尘土飞扬的山区。这些年发展起来的“延安方式”巩固了中共的许多政策：土地改革，包括将土地重新分配给农民、降低税收、自给自足的经济、思想教育，以及支撑这一切的中共军事力量——红军。到对日战争结束时，共产主义地区大规模扩张，红军有90万，党员人数达到120万，创下新高。

Above all, the war with Japan had helped the communists come back from the brink of the disaster they had faced at the end of the Long March. With their common enemy defeated following the Japanese surrender in 1945, the Kuomintang and communists then resumed fighting in 1946 and after three long years of heavy combat, the CCP finally seized victory in 1949. On 1 October 1949, Mao declared the establishment of the People’s Republic of China in Beijing’s Tian’anmen Square.
最重要的是，与日本的战争帮助共产党人从长征结束时面临的灾难边缘恢复过来。1945年日本投降后，国共两党的共同敌人被击败，国民党和共产党于1946年恢复战斗，经过三年的激战，中共终于在1949年取得了胜利。1949年10月1日，毛在北京天安门广场宣布中华人民共和国成立。

Chiang Kaishek fled to the island of Formosa (Taiwan), which China had regained from Japan after WWII. He took with him China’s gold reserves and the remains of his air force and navy, and set up the Republic of China (ROC), naming his new capital Taipei (台北, Táiběi).
蒋介石逃往福尔摩沙岛（台湾），这是中国在二战后从日本手中收回的。他带着中国的黄金储备和空军和海军的遗迹，建立了中华民国（ROC），并将他的新首都命名为台北（台北 ，Táiběi）。

Chiang Kaishek’s New Life Movement and the Chinese Communist Party ideology were attempts to mobilise society through renewal of the individual, but only the communists advocated class war.
蒋介石的新生活运动和中国共产党的意识形态是试图通过个人的更新来动员社会，但只有共产党人鼓吹阶级战争。

Mao’s China
毛的中国

Mao’s China desired, above all, to exercise ideological control over its population. It called itself ‘New China’, with the idea that the whole citizenry, down to the remotest peasants, should find a role in the new politics and society. The success of Mao’s military and political tactics also meant that the country was, for the first time since the 19th century, united under a strong central government.
毛的中国首先希望对其人民进行意识形态控制。它自称为“新中国”，其理念是全体公民，包括最偏远的农民，都应该在新的政治和社会中找到角色。毛的军事和政治策略的成功也意味着该国自 19 世纪以来首次在强大的中央政府下团结起来。

Most Westerners – and bourgeois Western influences – were swiftly removed from the country. The US refused to recognise the new state at all. However, the 1950s marked the high point of Soviet influence on Chinese politics and culture. Mao’s experiences had convinced him that only violent change could shake up the relationship between landlords and their tenants, or capitalists and their employees, in a China that was still highly traditional. The first year of the regime saw 40% of the land redistributed to poor peasants. At the same time, one million or so people condemned as ‘landlords’ were persecuted and killed. The joy of liberation was real for many Chinese, but campaigns of terror were also real and the early 1950s was no golden age.
大多数西方人——以及西方资产阶级的影响——很快就被赶出了这个国家。美国根本拒绝承认这个新国家。然而，1950年代标志着苏联对中国政治和文化影响的顶峰。毛泽东的经历使他相信，在一个仍然高度传统的中国，只有暴力变革才能动摇地主与佃户或资本家与雇员之间的关系。政权执政的第一年，40% 的土地重新分配给贫困农民。与此同时，大约一百万人被谴责为“地主”，受到迫害和杀害。解放的喜悦对许多中国人来说是真实的，但恐怖运动也是真实的，1950年代初期并不是黄金时代。

As relations with the Soviets broke down in the mid-1950s, the CCP leaders’ thoughts turned to economic self-sufficiency. Mao, supported by Politburo colleagues, proposed the policy known as the Great Leap Forward (Dàyuèjìn), a highly ambitious plan to harness the power of socialist economics to boost production of steel, coal and electricity. Agriculture was to reach an ever-higher level of collectivisation. Family structures were broken up as communal dining halls were established: people were urged to eat their fill, as the new agricultural methods would ensure plenty for all, year after year.
随着与苏联的关系在 1950 年代中期破裂，中共领导人的想法转向了经济自给自足。毛在政治局同事的支持下，提出了一项被称为“大跃进”的政策，这是一项雄心勃勃的计划，旨在利用社会主义经济的力量来促进钢铁、煤炭和电力的生产。农业将达到更高的集体化水平。随着公共食堂的建立，家庭结构被瓦解：人们被敦促吃饱，因为新的农业方法将年复一年地确保所有人都能吃饱。

However, the Great Leap Forward was a horrific failure. Its lack of economic realism caused a massive famine that killed tens of millions – historian Frank Dikötter posits a minimum figure of 45 million deaths in his Mao’s Great Famine (2010), a figure greater than the total number of casualties in WWI. Yang Jisheng’s Tombstone: The Great Chinese Famine, 1958–1962 (2012) conservatively estimates there were 36 million deaths. Yet the return to a semi-market economy in 1962, after the Leap had comprehensively ended, did not dampen Mao’s enthusiasm for revolutionary renewal. This led to the last and most fanatical of the campaigns that marked Mao’s China: the Cultural Revolution of 1966–76.
然而，大跃进却是一次可怕的失败。它缺乏经济现实主义，导致了一场大规模饥荒，导致数千万人死亡——历史学家弗兰克·迪科特 （Frank Dikötter） 在他的毛《大饥荒 》（2010 年）中假设最低死亡人数为 4500 万人，这个数字比第一次世界大战的伤亡总数还要多。杨继生的 《墓碑：中国大饥荒，1958-1962》（2012）保守估计有 3600 万人死亡。然而，在大跃进全面结束之后，1962 年恢复到半市场经济，并没有削弱毛对革命复兴的热情。这导致了标志着毛的中国的最后一场也是最狂热的运动：1966-76 年的文化大革命。

Cultural Revolution
文化大革命

Mao had become increasingly concerned that post-Leap China was slipping into ‘economism’ – a complacent satisfaction with rising standards of living that would blunt people’s revolutionary fervour. Mao was particularly concerned that the young generation might grow up with a dimmed spirit of revolution. For these reasons Mao decided upon a massive campaign of ideological renewal, in which he would attack his own party.
毛越来越担心闰后中国正在滑入“经济主义”——一种对生活水平提高的自满满足，这会削弱人们的革命热情。毛特别担心年轻一代在成长过程中可能会带着黯淡的革命精神。出于这些原因，毛决定发起一场大规模的意识形态更新运动，他将攻击自己的政党。

Still the dominant figure in the CCP, Mao used his prestige to undermine his own colleagues. In the summer of 1966 posters in large, handwritten characters appeared at prominent sites, including Peking University, demanding that figures such as Liu Shaoqi (president of the PRC) and Deng Xiaoping (senior Politburo member) must be condemned as ‘takers of the capitalist road’. Top leaders suddenly disappeared from sight, only to be replaced by unknowns, such as Mao’s wife Jiang Qing and her associates, later dubbed the ‘Gang of Four’. Meanwhile, an all-pervasive cult of Mao’s personality took over. One million youths at a time, known as Red Guards, would flock to hear Mao in Tian’anmen Sq. Posters and pictures of Mao were everywhere. The Red Guards were not ashamed to admit their tactics were violent. Immense violence permeated throughout society: teachers, intellectuals and landlords were killed in their thousands. Encouraged to smash remnants of the past, homes were raided and antiquities looted or destroyed by Red Guards, while imperial and religious sites across China were defaced, damaged or demolished.
毛仍然是中共的主导人物，他利用自己的威望来破坏自己的同事。1966年夏天，包括北京大学在内的重要地点出现了大型手写海报，要求必须谴责刘少奇（中国国家主席）和邓小平（政治局高级委员）等人物为“资本主义道路的接受者”。最高领导人突然消失在人们的视线中，取而代之的是无名之辈，比如毛的妻子江青和她的同伙，后来被称为“四人帮”。与此同时，对毛人格的无处不在的崇拜占据了上风。一次有一百万名被称为红卫兵的年轻人，会蜂拥而至，在天安门广场听毛的声音。到处都是毛的海报和照片。红卫兵并不羞于承认他们的策略是暴力的。巨大的暴力渗透到整个社会：成千上万的教师、知识分子和房东被杀害。红卫兵怂恿砸毁过去的遗迹，民居被抄袭，古物被抢劫或毁坏，中国各地的皇家和宗教遗址被污损、损坏或拆除。

While Mao initiated and supported the Cultural Revolution, it was also genuinely popular among many young people (who had less to lose and more to gain). However, police authority effectively disappeared, creative activity came to a virtual standstill and academic research was grounded.
在毛发起并支持文化大革命的同时，它也真正受到许多年轻人的欢迎（他们失去的少而得到的多）。然而，警察权力实际上消失了，创造性活动几乎停滞不前，学术研究也停滞不前。

The Cultural Revolution could not last. Worried by the increasing violence, the army forced the Red Guards off the streets in 1969. The early 1970s saw a remarkable rapprochement between the US and China: the former was desperate to extricate itself from the quagmire of the Vietnam War; the latter, terrified of an attack from the now-hostile USSR. Secretive diplomatic manoeuvres led, eventually, to the official visit of US President Richard Nixon to China in 1972, which began the reopening of China to the West. Slowly the Cultural Revolution began to cool down, but its brutal legacy survives today. Many of those guilty of murder and violence re-entered society with little or no judgement or punishment, while today’s CCP discourages open analysis and debate of the ‘decade of chaos’.
文化大革命不可能持续下去。由于担心暴力事件日益加剧，军队于 1969 年将红卫兵赶出街头。1970年代初期，中美之间出现了显著的和解：前者迫切希望摆脱越南战争的泥潭;后者害怕来自现在敌对的苏联的袭击。秘密的外交策略最终导致美国总统理查德·尼克松于 1972 年对中国进行正式访问，开始了中国对西方的重新开放。慢慢地，文化大革命开始降温，但其残酷的遗产至今仍然存在。许多犯有谋杀和暴力罪的人在很少或根本没有受到审判或惩罚的情况下重新进入社会，而今天的中共不鼓励对“混乱十年”进行公开分析和辩论。

The ghostly shadows of Cultural Revolution slogans can be hard to find in large and modern cities, but are quite a common sight in rural destinations such as the towns of Pingyao and Fenghuang and small historic villages across China.
文革口号的幽灵般的影子在大城市和现代化城市中很难找到，但在平遥镇和凤凰镇等农村地区以及中国各地的历史悠久的小村庄却很常见。

Reform & Protest
改革与抗议

Mao died in 1976, to be succeeded by the little-known Hua Guofeng (1921–2008). Within two years Hua had been outmanoeuvred by the greatest survivor of 20th-century Chinese politics, Deng Xiaoping. Deng had been purged twice during the Cultural Revolution, but after Mao’s death he was able to reach supreme leadership in the CCP with a radical program. In particular, Deng recognised that the Cultural Revolution had been highly damaging economically to China. He enlisted a policy slogan originally invented by Mao’s pragmatic prime minister, Zhou Enlai – the ‘Four Modernisations’. The party’s task would be to set China on the right path in four areas: agriculture, industry, national defence, and science and technology.
毛于 1976 年去世，由鲜为人知的华国锋（1921-2008 年）继任。两年之内，华就被20世纪中国政治最伟大的幸存者邓小平击败了。邓在文革期间曾两次被清洗，但在毛泽东去世后，他能够通过激进的纲领登上中共最高领导层。特别是，邓认识到文化大革命对中国的经济造成了巨大破坏。他提出了一个最初由毛务实的总理周恩来发明的政策口号——“四个现代化”。该党的任务是让中国在农业、工业、国防和科学技术四个领域走上正确的道路。

To make this policy work, many of the assumptions of the Mao era were abandoned. The first highly symbolic move of the ‘reform era’ (as the post-1978 period is known) was the breaking down of the collective farms. Farmers were able to sell a proportion of their crops on the free market, and urban and rural areas were also encouraged to establish small local enterprises. ‘To get rich is glorious,’ Deng declared, adding, ‘It doesn’t matter if some areas get rich first’. As part of this encouragement of entrepreneurship, Deng designated four areas on China’s coast as Special Economic Zones (SEZs), which would be particularly attractive to foreign investors.
为了使这一政策发挥作用，毛时代的许多假设被放弃了。“改革时代”（1978 年后时期）的第一个极具象征意义的举动是集体农场的瓦解。农民能够在自由市场上出售一部分农作物，也鼓励城乡地区建立当地小企业。“致富是光荣的，”邓宣称，并补充道，“如果某些地区先致富并不重要”。作为鼓励创业的一部分，邓将中国沿海的四个地区指定为经济特区（SEZ），这将对外国投资者特别有吸引力。

The new freedoms that the urban middle classes enjoyed created the appetite for more. After student protests demanding further opening up of the party in 1985–86, the prime minister (and relative liberal) Hu Yaobang was forced to resign in 1987 and take responsibility for allowing social forces to get out of control. He was replaced as general secretary by Zhao Ziyang, who was more conservative politically, though an economic reformer. In April 1989 Hu Yaobang died, and students around China used the occasion of his death to organise protests against the continuing role of the CCP in public life. At Peking University, the breeding ground of the May Fourth demonstrations of 1919, students declared the need for ‘science and democracy’, the modernising watchwords of 80 years earlier, to be revived.
城市中产阶级享有的新自由创造了对更多自由的胃口。在1985-86年要求进一步开放党的学生抗议活动之后，总理（也是相对自由派的）胡耀邦被迫于1987年辞职，并承担让社会力量失控的责任。赵紫阳接替他担任总书记，赵紫阳在政治上更为保守，但却是一位经济改革者。1989年4月，胡耀邦去世，中国各地的学生利用他的去世组织抗议中共在公共生活中的持续作用。在北京大学，1919年五四示威的温床，学生们宣称需要复兴“科学与民主”，这是80年前的现代化口号。

In spring 1989 Tian’anmen Sq was the scene of an unprecedented demonstration. At its height, nearly a million Chinese workers and students, in a rare cross-class alliance, filled the space in front of the Gate of Heavenly Peace, with the CCP profoundly embarrassed to have the world’s media record such events. By June 1989 the numbers in the square had dwindled to only thousands, but those who remained showed no signs of moving. Martial law was imposed and on the night of 3 June and early hours of 4 June, tanks and armoured personnel carriers were sent in. The death toll in Beijing has never been officially confirmed, but it seems likely to have been in the high hundreds or even more. Hundreds of people associated with the movement were arrested, imprisoned or forced to flee to the West.
1989年春天，天安门广场发生了一场史无前例的示威活动。在鼎盛时期，近百万中国工人和学生以罕见的跨阶级联盟形式，挤满了天安门前的空间，中共对让世界媒体记录此类事件深感尴尬。到 1989 年 6 月，广场上的人数已经减少到只有数千人，但留下来的人却没有移动的迹象。戒严令实施，6 月 3 日夜间和 6 月 4 日凌晨，坦克和装甲运兵车被派进来。北京的死亡人数从未得到官方证实，但似乎很可能在数百甚至更多。数百名与该运动有关的人被捕、监禁或被迫逃往西方。

For some three years, China’s politics were almost frozen, but in 1992 Deng, the man who had sent in the tanks, made his last grand public gesture. That year he undertook what Chinese political insiders called his ‘southern tour’, or nánxún. By visiting Shenzhen, Deng indicated that the economic policies of reform were not going to be abandoned. The massive growth rates that the Chinese economy has posted ever since have justified his decision. Deng also made another significant choice: grooming Jiang Zemin – the mayor of Shanghai, who had peacefully dissolved demonstrations in Shanghai in a way that the authorities in Beijing had not – as his successor by appointing him as general secretary of the party in 1989.
大约三年来，中国政治几乎被冻结，但在 1992 年，邓，这位派出坦克的人，做出了他最后一次盛大的公开姿态。那一年，他进行了中国政界人士所说的“南巡”，即南巡。 邓在访问深圳时表示，改革的经济政策不会被放弃。自那以后，中国经济的大幅增长率证明了他的决定是合理的。邓还做出了另一个重大选择：1989 年任命江泽民为党总书记，将——上海市长——以北京当局没有的方式和平解散了上海的示威活动——作为他的继任者。

Deng died in 1997, the same year that Hong Kong returned to China under a ‘one country, two systems’ agreement with the UK, which would maintain the ex-British colony’s independence in all aspects except defence and foreign affairs for the next 50 years. Macau followed suit two years later. Faced with a multitude of social problems brought on by inequalities spawned by the Deng years, President Jiang Zemin, with Zhu Rongji as premier, sought to bring economic stability to China while strengthening the centralised power of the state and putting off much-needed political reforms. Faced with a protest of up to 10,000 Falun Gong adherents outside Beijing’s Zhongnanhai in April 1999, Jiang branded the movement a cult and sought its eradication in China through imprisonment and detention, backed up by a draconian propaganda campaign.
邓于1997年去世，同年香港根据与英国签订的“一国两制”协议回归中国，该协议将在未来50年内保持这个前英国殖民地在除国防和外交事务外的所有方面的独立性。两年后，澳门也纷纷效仿。面对邓代引发的不平等带来的诸多社会问题，江泽民主席在朱镕基担任总理的同时，试图在加强中央集权和推迟急需的政治改革的同时，为中国带来经济稳定。1999年4月，面对北京中南海外多达1万名法轮功学员的抗议活动，江将法轮功运动称为邪教，并通过监禁和拘留来寻求在中国根除法轮功，并辅以严厉的宣传运动。

Read The Cultural Revolution: A People’s History, 1962–1976 (2016) by Frank Dikötter for the history; see Zhang Yimou’s film To Live (1994) to understand the emotions.
阅读弗兰克·迪科特 （Frank Dikötter） 的《 文化大革命：人民的历史，1962-1976》（2016 年）;看张艺谋的电影《 活下去 》（1994）来理解其中的情感。

One product of the new freedom of the 1980s was a revived Chinese film industry. Red Sorghum (1988), the first film directed by Zhang Yimou, was a searingly erotic film of a type that had not been seen since 1949.
1980 年代新自由的产物之一是中国电影业的复兴。《 红高粱 》（1988）是张艺谋执导的第一部电影，是一部自 1949 年以来从未出现过的灼热色情电影。

Twenty-first-century China
二十一世纪的中国

Jiang Zemin was succeeded in 2002 by President Hu Jintao, who made further efforts to tame growing regional inequality and the poverty scarring rural areas. China’s lopsided development continued, however, despite an ambitious program to develop the western regions. By 2009 an inflow of US$325 billion had dramatically boosted GDP per capita in the western regions, but a colossal prosperity gap survived and significant environmental challenges – from desertification to water shortages and soil erosion – persisted.
江泽民于 2002 年由胡锦涛主席继任，他进一步努力遏制日益严重的地区不平等和农村地区的贫困伤痕。然而，尽管中国制定了雄心勃勃的西部地区开发计划，但中国的不平衡发展仍在继续。到 2009 年，3250 亿美元的资金流入极大地提高了西部地区的人均 GDP，但巨大的繁荣差距仍然存在，从荒漠化到缺水和水土流失等重大环境挑战仍然存在。

The question of political reform found itself shelved, partly because economic growth was bringing prosperity to so many, albeit unevenly. Property prices, especially in the richer eastern coastal provinces, were rocketing and the export and investment-driven economy was thriving. For some the first decade of the 21st century was marked by spectacular riches – the number of dollar billionaires doubled in just two years – and property prices began moving dramatically beyond the reach of the less fortunate, while bringing wealth to the more fortunate. This period coincided with the greatest migration of workers to the cities the world has ever seen.
政治改革问题被搁置了，部分原因是经济增长给许多人带来了繁荣，尽管不平衡。房地产价格，尤其是在较富裕的东部沿海省份，正在飙升，出口和投资驱动的经济正在蓬勃发展。对于一些人来说，21 世纪的第一个十年以惊人的财富为标志——亿美元富翁的数量在短短两年内翻了一番——房地产价格开始急剧上涨，超出了不幸者的承受能力，同时为更幸运的人带来了财富。这一时期恰逢工人向城市迁移最大规模的时期。

China responded to the credit crunch of 2007 and the downturn in Western economies with a stimulus package of US$586 billion between 2008 and 2009. Property and infrastructure construction enjoyed spectacular growth, buffering China from the worst effects of the global recession, but the export sector contracted as demand dried up overseas. A barrage of restrictions on buying second properties attempted to flush speculators from the market and tame price rises. These policies partially worked, but millions of flats across China lay empty – bought by investors happy to see prices rise.
2008年至2009年期间，中国以5860亿美元的刺激计划应对2007年的信贷紧缩和西方经济的衰退。房地产和基础设施建设业增长惊人，缓冲了中国免受全球经济衰退最严重影响的影响，但随着海外需求枯竭，出口部门萎缩。对购买第二套房产的一系列限制试图将投机者赶出市场并抑制价格上涨。这些政策部分奏效，但中国各地数百万套公寓空置——投资者乐于看到价格上涨而购买。

Vice president from 2008, Xi Jinping replaced Hu Jintao as president in 2013. Pledging to root out corruption, Xi also sought to instigate reforms, including the abolition of both the one-child policy and the láojiào (re-education through labour) system. These reforms, however, were matched by a growing zeal for internet and social-media controls and a domestic security budget that sucked in more capital than national defence.
习近平从 2008 年起担任国家副主席，2013 年接替胡锦涛担任国家主席。习习承诺根除腐败，还试图推动改革，包括废除独生子女政策和劳教制度。然而，与这些改革相匹配的是对互联网和社交媒体控制的日益热情，以及国内安全预算，其吸收的资本多于国防。

Xi inherited a China that was a tremendous success story, but one that remained beset with problems. Despite resilient and ambitious planning (including a massive expansion of the high-speed rail network, a space program, some of the world’s tallest buildings), the Chinese economy remained fundamentally imbalanced. During his tenure Xi has sought to shift China’s economy from this over-reliance on exports by stimulating domestic consumption and at the same time launching a powerful anti-corruption drive. Xi has also worked hard on the presentation of his public persona, in a drive that some observers have likened to a personality cult. The abolition of the two-term presidential limit in 2018 only amplified concerns, ratcheted up further by revelations China was detaining up to a million Uyghurs against their will in detention centres in the Xinjiang autonomous region. Political reform found itself on the back-burner as China sought to consolidate its economic achievements, all the while confronting US President Donald Trump over an increasingly drawn-out and fractious trade war, as storm clouds gathered above the competing claims over the reefs, shoals and islands of the South China Sea.
习习继承了一个巨大的成功故事，但仍然充满问题的中国。尽管有坚韧而雄心勃勃的规划（包括大规模扩建高铁网络、太空计划、一些世界最高建筑），但中国经济仍然从根本上失衡。在任期间，习正试图通过刺激国内消费，同时发起强有力的反腐败运动，使中国经济摆脱这种过度依赖出口的局面。习习还努力展示他的公众形象，一些观察家将其比作个人崇拜。2018年取消两届国家主席任期限制只会加剧人们的担忧，而中国违背维吾尔人意愿将多达100万维吾尔人拘留在新疆自治区的拘留中心进一步加剧了担忧。随着中国寻求巩固其经济成就，政治改革被搁置一前，同时与美国总统唐纳德·特朗普就日益旷日持久和紧张的贸易战对峙，南海礁石、浅滩和岛屿的竞争主张上空笼罩着乌云。

First identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, Hubei province, but not declared a pandemic by the World Health Organization (WHO) till two and a half months later, Covid-19 went on to become one of the most lethal pandemics in human history, with almost 3.5 million deaths at the time of writing. Though the precise origin of the outbreak remains unknown, the first cases were associated with the Huanan Seafood Market in Wuhan. Several doctors at Wuhan Central Hospital, including Li Wenliang (who later died from Covid-19), were reprimanded by police for ‘spreading false rumours’ regarding the new virus at the end of December. On 21 January 2020, the WHO tweeted that it was evident there was ‘at least some human-to-human transmission’ of the virus and just two days later, Wuhan and other cities in Hubei province were quickly put into lockdown. On 30 January, the WHO finally designated Covid-19 a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC), though by then the virus had spread abroad. Despite periodic small-scale outbreaks within its borders, China ran a largely successful campaign of not re-importing the virus by closing its borders to foreign nationals in March 2020 and began a huge programme to vaccinate its citizens from early 2021.
Covid-19 于 2019 年 12 月在湖北省武汉市首次发现，但直到两个半月后才被世界卫生组织 （WHO） 宣布为大流行，Covid-19 继续成为人类历史上最致命的流行病之一，在撰写本文时已有近 350 万人死亡。尽管疫情的确切起源尚不清楚，但首批病例与武汉华南海鲜市场有关。12 月底，武汉市中心医院的几名医生，包括李文亮（后来死于 Covid-19）在内，因“散布有关新病毒的虚假谣言”而受到警方的谴责。2020 年 1 月 21 日，世卫组织在推特上表示，很明显该病毒“至少存在一些人际传播”，仅仅两天后，武汉和湖北省其他城市迅速进入封锁状态。1 月 30 日，世界卫生组织最终将 Covid-19 指定为国际关注的突发公共卫生事件 （PHEIC），尽管当时该病毒已传播到国外。尽管境内周期性地爆发小规模疫情，但中国于 2020 年 3 月对外国人关闭边境，开展了一场不重新输入病毒的运动，并从 2021 年初开始了一项大规模的公民疫苗接种计划。

One of the most astonishing recreations in living memory is the epic reconstruction of the entire city wall in the north Shanxi town of Datong, complete with scores of watchtowers.
人们记忆中最令人惊叹的娱乐活动之一是山西北大同镇对整个城墙的史诗般的重建，并配有数十座瞭望塔。

时间线

c 4000 BC
公元前4000年

The first known settlements appear along the Yellow River. The river remains a central cultural reference point for the Chinese throughout history.
第一个已知的定居点出现在黄河沿岸。纵观历史，这条河仍然是中国人的中心文化参考点。

c 1700 BC
公元前1700年

Craftsmen of the Shang dynasty master the production of bronzeware (in the form of ritual vessels) in one of the first examples of multiple production in history.
商代的工匠掌握了青铜器皿（以祭器的形式）的制作，这是历史上最早的多种生产例子之一。

c 600 BC
公元前600年

Laotzu (Laozi), founder of Taoism, is supposedly born. The folk religion of Taoism goes on to coexist with later arrivals such as Buddhism in a reflection of Chinese religion’s syncretic, rather than exclusive, nature.
道教的创始人老子（Laozi）据说是出生的。道教的民间宗教继续与佛教等后来传入的宗教共存，反映了中国宗教的融合性而不是排他性。

551 BC
公元前551年

The birth of Confucius. Collected in The Analects, his ideas of an ethical, ordered society that operated through hierarchy and self-development would dominate Chinese culture until the early 20th century.
孔子的诞生。他收录在 《论语》中， 他关于一个通过等级制度和自我发展运作的道德、有序的社会的思想将主导中国文化，直到 20 世纪初。

214 BC
公元前214年

Emperor Qin indentures thousands of labourers to link existing city walls into one Great Wall, made of tamped earth. The brick cladding of the bastion dates from the much later Ming dynasty.
秦帝契约了数千名劳工，将现有的城墙连接成一座由夯实的泥土制成的长城。堡垒的砖砌覆层可以追溯到更晚的明朝。

c 100 BC
公元前100年

The Silk Road between China and the Middle East takes Chinese goods to places as far flung as Rome.
中国和中东之间的丝绸之路将中国商品带到了远至罗马的地方。

c 100 BC
公元前100年

Buddhism first arrives in China from India. This religious system ends up thoroughly assimilated into Chinese culture and is now more powerful in China than in its country of origin.
佛教首先从印度传入中国。这种宗教体系最终被彻底同化到中国文化中，现在在中国比在原籍国更强大。

874

The Huang Chao rebellion breaks out, which helps reduce the Tang empire to chaos and leads to the fall of the capital in 907.
黄巢之乱爆发，帮助唐帝国陷入混乱，导致907年首都沦陷。

c 1000

The major premodern inventions – paper, printing, gunpowder and the compass – are commonly used in China. The economy begins to commercialise and create a countrywide market system.
前现代的主要发明——纸张、印刷、火药和指南针——在中国很普遍。经济开始商业化并建立全国性的市场体系。

1215

Genghis Khan conquers Beijing as part of his creation of a massive Eurasian empire under Mongol rule. The Mongols overstretch themselves, however, and neglect good governance.
成吉思汗征服了北京，这是他在蒙古统治下建立一个庞大的欧亚帝国的一部分。然而，蒙古人过度扩张，忽视了良好的治理。

1286

The Grand Canal is extended to Beijing. Over time the canal becomes a major artery for the transport of grain, salt and other important commodities between north and south China.
大运河延伸至北京。随着时间的推移，运河成为中国南北之间粮食、盐和其他重要商品运输的主要动脉。

1298–99

Marco Polo pens his famous account of his travels to China. Inconsistencies in his story have led some scholars to doubt whether he ever went to China at all.
马可·波罗 （Marco Polo） 写下了他著名的中国之旅记述。他的故事前后矛盾，让一些学者怀疑他是否曾经去过中国。

1406

Ming Emperor Yongle begins construction of the 800 buildings of the Forbidden City. This complex, along with much of the Great Wall, shows the style and size of late-imperial architecture.
明朝永乐皇帝开始建造紫禁城的800座建筑。这座建筑群以及长城的大部分建筑群都展示了晚期帝国建筑的风格和规模。

1557

The Portuguese establish a permanent trade base in Macau, the first of the European outposts that will eventually lead to imperialist dominance of China until the mid-19th century.
葡萄牙人在澳门建立了一个永久性的贸易基地，这是欧洲的第一个前哨基地，最终导致帝国主义统治中国，直到 19 世纪中叶。

c 1600
约1600年

The period of China’s dominance as the world’s greatest economy begins to end. By 1800 European economies are industrialising and clearly dominant.
中国作为世界最大经济体的主导地位开始结束。到 1800 年，欧洲经济体已经工业化并明显占据主导地位。

1644

Beijing falls to peasant rebel Li Zicheng and the last Ming emperor Chongzhen hangs himself in Jingshan Park; the Qing dynasty is established.
北京沦陷于农民起义军李自成手中，末代明朝皇帝崇祯在景山公园上吊自杀;清朝建立。

1689

The Treaty of Nerchinsk is signed, delineating the border between China and Russia. This is the first modern border agreement in Chinese history, as well as the longest lasting.
《尼布楚条约》签订，划定了中俄边界。这是中国历史上第一个现代边界协议，也是持续时间最长的边界协议。

1793

British diplomat Lord Macartney visits Beijing with British industrial products, but is told by the Qianlong emperor that China has no need of his merchandise.
英国外交官马戛尔尼勋爵带着英国工业产品访问北京，但乾隆皇帝告诉中国不需要他的商品。

1823

The British are importing roughly 7000 chests of opium annually – with about 63.5kg of opium per chest, enough to supply one million addicts – compared with 1000 chests in 1773.
英国每年进口大约 7000 箱鸦片——每箱鸦片约 63.5 公斤，足以供应 100 万吸毒者——而 1773 年为 1000 箱鸦片。

1839

The Qing official Lin Zexu demands that British traders at Guangzhou hand over 20,000 chests of opium, leading the British to provoke the First Opium War in retaliation.
清朝官员林则徐要求英国在广州的商人交出2万箱鸦片，导致英国人挑起第一次鸦片战争进行报复。

1842

The Treaty of Nanjing concludes the First Opium War. China is forced to hand over Hong Kong Island to the British and open up five Chinese ports to foreign trade.
《南京条约》结束了第一次鸦片战争。中国被迫将香港岛移交给英国，并开放中国的五个港口进行对外贸易。

1856

Hong Xiuquan claims to be Jesus’ younger brother and starts the Taiping uprising. With the Nian and Muslim uprisings, the Taiping greatly undermines the authority of the Qing dynasty.
洪秀全自称是耶稣的弟弟，发动了太平天国起义。随着年和穆斯林的起义，太平天国极大地削弱了清朝的权威。

1898

Emperor Guangxu permits major reforms, including new rights for women, but is thwarted by the Dowager Empress Cixi, who has many reformers arrested and executed.
光绪皇帝允许进行重大改革，包括妇女的新权利，但遭到慈禧太后的阻挠，慈禧逮捕并处决了许多改革者。

1898

The New Territories adjoining Kowloon in Hong Kong are leased to the British for 99 years, eventually returning, along with the rest of Hong Kong, in 1997.
毗邻香港九龙的新界租借给英国人 99 年，最终于 1997 年与香港其他地区一起归还。

1900

The Hanlin Academy in Beijing – a centre of Chinese learning and literature – is accidentally torched by Chinese troops during the Boxer Rebellion, destroying its priceless collection of books.
北京翰林院——中国学习和文学中心——在义和团运动期间被中国军队意外烧毁，毁坏了其无价的藏书。

1904–05

The Russo-Japanese War is fought entirely on Chinese territory. The victory of Japan is the first triumph by an Asian power over a European one.
日俄战争完全在中国领土上进行。日本的胜利是亚洲大国首次战胜欧洲大国。

1905

Major reforms in the late Qing dynasty include the abolition of the 1000-year-long tradition of examinations in the Confucian classics to enter the Chinese bureaucracy.
晚清的重大改革包括废除儒家经典中长达1000年的考试传统，进入中国官僚机构。

1908

Two-year-old Puyi ascends the throne as China’s last emperor. Local elites and new classes such as businessmen no longer support the dynasty, leading to its ultimate downfall.
两岁的溥仪登基，成为中国最后一位皇帝。地方精英和商人等新阶层不再支持王朝，导致了王朝的最终垮台。

1911

Revolution spreads across China as local governments withdraw support for the dynasty, and instead back a republic under the presidency of Sun Yatsen (fundraising in the US at the time).
革命蔓延到中国各地，因为地方政府撤回了对王朝的支持，转而支持孙中山总统领导下的共和国（当时在美国筹款）。

1912

Yuan Shikai, leader of China’s most powerful regional army, goes to the Qing court to announce that the game is up. On 12 February the last emperor, six-year-old Puyi, abdicates.
中国最强大的地区军队的首领袁世凯前往清廷宣布游戏结束。2月12日，末代皇帝，六岁的溥仪退位。

1915

Japan makes the ‘21 demands’, which would give it massive political, economic and trading rights in parts of China. Europe’s attention is distracted by WWI.
日本提出了“21项要求”，这将使其在中国部分地区拥有巨大的政治、经济和贸易权利。欧洲的注意力被第一次世界大战分散了注意力。

1916

Yuan Shikai tries to declare himself emperor. He is forced to withdraw and remain president, but dies of uraemia later that year. China splits into areas ruled by rival militarists.
袁世凯试图宣布自己为皇帝。他被迫退出并继续担任总统，但当年晚些时候死于尿毒症。中国分裂成敌对军国主义统治的地区。

1926

Kuomintang and communists unite under Soviet advice to bring China together by force, then establish a Kuomintang government.
国民党和共产党人在苏联的建议下联合起来，通过武力将中国团结起来，然后建立国民党政府。

1927

Kuomintang leader Chiang Kaishek turns on the communists in Shanghai and Guangzhou, having thousands killed and forcing the communists to turn to a rural-based strategy.
国民党领导人蒋介石在上海和广州向共产党发起攻击，杀害了数千人，并迫使共产党转向农村战略。

1930s
1930年代

Cosmopolitan Shanghai is the world’s fifth-largest city (the largest in the Far East), supporting a polyglot population of four million people.
国际化的上海是世界第五大城市（远东最大的城市），拥有400万多语种人口。

1931

Japan invades Manchuria (northeast China), provoking an international crisis and forcing Chiang to consider anti-Japanese, as well as anticommunist, strategies.
日本入侵满洲（中国东北），引发了一场国际危机，迫使蒋介石考虑抗日和反共战略。

1935

Mao Zedong begins his rise to paramount power at the conference at Zunyi, held in the middle of the Long March to the northwest, while on the run from the Kuomintang.
毛泽东在西北长征中段举行的遵义会议上开始了他的最高权力地位，当时他正在逃离国民党。

1937

The Japanese and Chinese clash at Wanping, near Beijing, on 7 July, sparking the conflict that the Chinese call the ‘War of Resistance’, which only ends in 1945.
7月7日，日中两国在北京附近的宛平发生冲突，引发了中国人称之为“抗战”的冲突，直到1945年才结束。

1939

On 3–4 May Japanese carpet bombing devastates the temporary Chinese capital of Chongqing. From 1938 to 1943 Chongqing is one of the world’s most heavily bombed cities.
5 月 3 日至 4 日，日本的地毯式轰炸摧毁了中国临时首都重庆。从 1938 年到 1943 年，重庆是世界上轰炸最严重的城市之一。

1941

In the base area at Yan’an (Shaanxi), the ‘Rectification’ program begins, remoulding the Communist Party into an ideology shaped principally by Mao Zedong.
在延安（陕西）根据地，“整风”计划开始，将共产党重塑为主要由毛泽东塑造的意识形态。

1941

The Japanese attack the US at Pearl Harbor. China becomes a formal ally of the US, USSR and Britain in WWII, but is treated as a secondary partner at best.
日本在珍珠港袭击美国。中国在二战中成为美国、苏联和英国的正式盟友，但充其量只是次要伙伴。

1943

Chiang Kaishek negotiates an agreement with the Allies that, when Japan is defeated, Western imperial privileges in China will end forever, marking the twilight of Western imperialist power in China.
蒋介石与盟国谈判达成协议，当日本战败时，西方帝国在中国的特权将永远结束，标志着西方帝国主义在中国的权力的黄昏。

1946

Communists and the Kuomintang fail to form a coalition government, plunging China back into civil war. Communist organisation, morale and ideology all prove key to the communist victory.
共产党和国民党未能组建联合政府，使中国重新陷入内战。共产主义的组织、士气和意识形态都被证明是共产主义胜利的关键。

1949

Mao Zedong stands on top of the Gate of Heavenly Peace in Beijing on 1 October, and announces the formation of the People’s Republic of China (PRC), saying, ‘The Chinese people have stood up’.
10月1日，毛泽东站在北京天安门顶上，宣布中华人民共和国成立，说：“中国人民站起来了”。

1957

A brief period of liberalisation occurs under the ‘Hundred Flowers Movement’, but criticisms of the regime lead Mao to crack down and imprison or exile thousands of dissidents.
在“百花运动”下发生了短暂的自由化时期，但对政权的批评导致毛镇压、监禁或流放数千名持不同政见者。

1958

During the Taiwan Straits Crisis, Mao’s government fires missiles near islands under the control of Taiwan in an attempt to prevent a rapprochement between the US and USSR in the Cold War.
在台海危机期间，毛政府在台湾控制下的岛屿附近发射导弹，试图阻止美苏在冷战中的和解。

1961

Mass starvation is a consequence of the Great Leap Forward. Politburo members Liu Shaoqi and Deng Xiaoping reintroduce limited market reforms, which lead to their condemnation during the Cultural Revolution.
大规模饥饿是大跃进的结果。政治局委员刘少奇和邓小平重新推行有限的市场改革，这导致他们在文化大革命期间受到谴责。

1966

The Cultural Revolution breaks out, and Red Guards demonstrate in cities across China. The movement is marked by violence as a catalyst for transforming society.
文化大革命爆发，红卫兵在中国各地城市示威。该运动的特点是暴力是改变社会的催化剂。

1972

US President Richard Nixon visits China, marking a major rapprochement during the Cold War and the start of full diplomatic relations between the two countries.
美国总统尼克松访华，标志着冷战时期的重大和解，也是两国全面外交关系的开始。

1973

Deng Xiaoping returns to power as deputy premier. The modernising faction in the party fights with the Gang of Four, who support the continuing Cultural Revolution.
邓小平重新掌权，担任副总理。党内的现代化派系与支持持续的文化大革命的四人帮作斗争。

1976

Mao Zedong dies, aged 83. The Gang of Four are arrested by his successor and put on trial, where they are blamed for all the disasters of the Cultural Revolution.
毛泽东去世，享年 83 岁。四人帮被他的继任者逮捕并接受审判，他们被指责为文化大革命的所有灾难负责。

1980

The one-child policy is enforced. The state adopts it as a means of reducing the population, but at the same time it imposes unprecedented control over the personal liberty of women.
强制实施单子策略。国家将其作为减少人口的手段，但同时对妇女的人身自由施加了前所未有的控制。

1987

The Last Emperor, filmed in the Forbidden City, collects an Oscar for Best Picture, and marks a new openness in China towards the outside world.
在紫禁城拍摄的 《末代皇帝》 获得了奥斯卡最佳影片奖，标志着中国对外世界的新开放。

1988

The daring series River Elegy (Héshāng) is broadcast on national TV. It is a devastating indictment of dictatorship and Mao’s rule in particular, and is banned in China after 1989.
大胆的连续剧 《河流挽歌 》（Héshāng）在国家电视台播出。这是对独裁统治，尤其是毛统治的毁灭性控诉，1989 年后在中国被禁止。

1989

Hundreds of civilians are killed by Chinese troops in the streets around Tian’anmen Sq. No official reassessment has been made, but rumours persist of deep internal conflict within the party.
数百名平民在天安门广场周围的街道上被中国军队杀害。官方尚未做出重新评估，但党内内部冲突的传言仍然存在。

1997

Hong Kong is returned to the People’s Republic of China. Widespread fears that China will immediately interfere directly in its government prove wrong, but its politics become more sensitive to Beijing.
香港归还中华人民共和国。人们普遍担心中国会立即直接干预其政府，这被证明是错误的，但其政治对北京变得更加敏感。

2001

China joins the World Trade Organization, giving it a seat at the top table that decides global norms on economics and finance.
中国加入世界贸易组织，在决定全球经济和金融规范的最高层中占有一席之地。

2004

The world’s first commercially operating Maglev train begins scorching a trail across Shanghai’s Pudong district, reaching a top speed of 431km/h.
世界上第一列商业运营的磁悬浮列车开始在上海浦东区的一条小径上行驶，最高时速达到 431 公里/小时。

2006

The Three Gorges Dam is completed. Significant parts of the landscape of western China are lost beneath the waters, but energy is also provided for the expanding Chinese economy.
三峡大坝建成。中国西部的大部分景观都消失在水下，但也为不断扩大的中国经济提供了能源。

2008

Beijing hosts the Summer Olympic Games and Paralympics. The Games go smoothly and are widely considered to be a great success in burnishing China’s image overseas.
北京举办夏季奥运会和残奥会。奥运会顺利进行，被广泛认为是中国在海外形象的巨大成功。

2008

Violent riots in Lhasa, Tibet, again put the uneasy region on centre stage. Protests spread to other Tibetan areas in Gansu, Sichuan and Qinghai provinces.
西藏拉萨的暴力骚乱再次将这个不安的地区置于舞台的中心。抗议活动蔓延到甘肃省、四川省和青海省的其他藏族地区。

2008

A huge 8.0-magnitude earthquake convulses Sichuan province, leaving 87,000 dead or missing and rendering millions homeless.
四川省发生8.0级大地震，造成8.7万人死亡或失踪，数百万人无家可归。

2009

July riots in Ürümqi leave hundreds dead as interethnic violence flares between Uyghurs and Han Chinese. Beijing floods the region with soldiers and implements a 10-month internet blackout.
7月乌鲁木齐发生骚乱，维吾尔族和汉族之间的种族间暴力爆发，造成数百人死亡。北京向该地区充斥着士兵，并实施了为期 10 个月的互联网封锁。

2010

A huge 7.1-magnitude earthquake in the Qinghai region of the far west flattens the remote town of Yushu in April, killing thousands..
4月，遥远的西部青海地区发生了一场7.1级的大地震，将偏远的玉树镇夷为平地，造成数千人死亡。

2011

Two high-speed trains collide in July near Wenzhou in Zhejiang province, killing 40 people, the first and only fatal high-speed rail crash in China to date.
7月，两列高铁在浙江温州附近相撞，造成40人死亡，这是中国迄今为止首起也是唯一一起致命的高铁事故。

2012

After the heaviest rainfall in 60 years, Beijing is inundated with epic summer floods; 77 people are killed by the floodwaters and 65,000 evacuated.
在经历了 60 年来最强的降雨之后，北京被史诗般的夏季洪水淹没;77 人死于洪水，65,000 人疏散。

2013

In December the total length of China’s national high-speed rail network reaches a staggering 10,000km, making it the world’s longest.
12月，中国国家高铁网总长度达到了惊人的10000公里，成为世界上最长的。

2014

Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 disappears while flying from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing, with 152 Chinese passengers (of a total of 239 people) on board.
马航370航班在从吉隆坡飞往北京的航班上失踪，机上有152名中国乘客（共239人）。

2015

Satellite imagery reveals that China has been rapidly constructing an airfield at Fiery Cross Reef in the Spratly (Nánshā) Islands, part of territory also claimed by Vietnam, the Philippines and Taiwan.
卫星图像显示，中国一直在南沙群岛的火焰十字礁迅速建设一个机场，越南、菲律宾和台湾也声称拥有主权领土。

2017

China launches its first domestically constructed aircraft carrier, the Type 001A aircraft carrier, the second carrier in China’s People’s Liberation Army Navy..
中国下水第一艘国产航母——001A 型航母，这是中国人民解放军海军的第二艘航母。

2018

The China National Space Administration launches the Chang’e-4 lunar probe to land on the far side of the moon.
中国国家航天局发射奇娥四号月球探测器登陆月球背面。

2019

China is rebuked by 22 nations that call upon it to stop its program of the detention of a million or more Uyghurs in Xinjiang province.
中国受到 22 个国家的谴责，呼吁中国停止在新疆拘留 100 万或更多维吾尔人的计划。

2019

Covid-19 is first detected in Wuhan, Hubei province, in December 2019; the city and other cities of the province are put into lockdown the following month.
Covid-19 于 2019 年 12 月在湖北省武汉市首次被发现;该市和该省其他城市将于下个月进入封锁状态。
