Why Go?
为什么要去？

China’s largest tropical island boasts all the balmy weather, coconut palms and gold-sand beaches you could ask for. Down at Sanya it’s see-and-be-seen on the boardwalks or you can escape altogether at some of Asia’s top luxury resorts. Thatched huts and banana pancakes haven’t popped up anywhere yet, but there’s a hint of hipness coming from the east-coast beachside towns, and the budding surf scene is helping to spread the gospel of chill out.
中国最大的热带岛屿拥有所有温暖的天气、椰子树和金色沙滩。在三亚，您可以在木板路上看到它，或者您可以在一些亚洲顶级豪华度假村完全逃离喧嚣。茅草屋和香蕉煎饼还没有出现在任何地方，但东海岸海滨小镇传来一丝时髦，新兴的冲浪场景正在帮助传播寒冷的福音。

Money is pouring into Hainan (海南, Hǎinán) these days to ramp up the luxury quotient. You can cruise on the high-speed rail, but cycling is still the better way to get around. When you’ve had enough of a lathering on the coast, the cool central highlands are an ideal place to be on two wheels. The good roads, knockout mountain views, and concentration of Li and Miao, the island’s first settlers, give the region an appealing distinction from the lowlands.
这些天，资金涌入海南（海南 ，Hǎinán），以提高奢侈品商数。您可以乘坐高铁巡游，但骑自行车仍然是更好的出行方式。当您在海岸上玩够了泡沫时，凉爽的中部高地是骑两轮车的理想场所。良好的道路、壮丽的山景以及岛上第一批定居者李族和苗族的集中，使该地区与低地区分开来。

History
历史

Until the economic boom of the last 30 years, Hainan had been a backwater of the Chinese empire. The first Han settlements appeared on the coast almost 2000 years ago, but the island was largely ignored by a series of dynasties, and was known as the ‘tail of the dragon’, ‘the gate of hell’ and a place best used as a repository for occasional high-profile exiles such as the poet Su Dongpo and the official Hai Rui.
在最近30年的经济繁荣之前，海南一直是中华帝国的死水。大约 2000 年前，第一个汉族定居点出现在沿海地区，但该岛在很大程度上被一系列朝代所忽视，被称为“龙的尾巴”、“地狱之门”，也是最适合用作偶尔知名流亡者（如诗人苏东坡和官员海瑞）的储存库的地方。

More recently, China’s first communist cell was formed here in the 1920s and the island was heavily bombarded and then occupied by the Japanese during WWII. Li and Han Chinese guerrillas waged an effective campaign to harass the Japanese forces but the retaliation was brutal – the Japanese executed a third of the island’s male population. Even today resentment over Japanese atrocities lingers among the younger generation.
最近，中国的第一个共产党小组于 1920 年代在这里成立，该岛在二战期间遭到猛烈轰炸，然后被日本占领。李汉游击队发动了一场有效的骚扰日军的战役，但报复是残酷的——日本人处决了岛上三分之一的男性人口。即使在今天，年轻一代对日本暴行的怨恨仍然挥之不去。

In 1988 Hainan was taken away from Guangdong and established as its own province and Special Economic Zone (SEZ). After years of fits and starts, development is now focused on turning tropical Hainan into an ‘international tourism island’. What this really means, besides developing every beach, and building more golf courses and mega-transport projects (such as a high-speed rail service around the island, a cruise-ship terminal and even a spaceport), is not entirely clear.
1988 年，海南从广东被夺走，并建立自己的省和经济特区（SEZ）。经过多年的断断续续，现在的发展重点是将热带海南打造成“国际旅游岛”。除了开发每个海滩、建造更多高尔夫球场和大型交通项目（例如环岛高铁服务、游轮码头甚至太空港）之外，这到底意味着什么并不完全清楚。

Climate
气候

The weather on Hainan is largely warm in autumn and winter, and hot and humid in spring and summer. The mountains are always cooler than the coast, and the north is cooler than the south. Hainan is hit by at least one typhoon each year, usually between May and October.
海南的天气在秋冬季节大多温暖，春夏季节炎热潮湿。山总是比海岸凉爽，北方比南方凉爽。海南每年至少遭受一次台风袭击，通常在5月至10月之间。

Language
语言

Hainanese is a broad term for the baker’s dozen local dialects of Hǎinán Mǐn (it’s known by many other names), most of which are also spoken in Guangdong. While the Li and Miao can usually speak Mandarin, they prefer to use their own languages.
海南话是面包师的十几种海南语方言（它有许多其他名称）的广义术语，其中大部分也在广东使用。虽然黎族和苗族通常会说普通话，但他们更喜欢使用自己的语言。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

Both Haikou and Sanya have international airports. Haikou, the capital city on the island’s north coast, is the point of entry for longdistance buses and trains coming from the mainland. And yes, there’s no bridge: even trains cross the Qiongzhou Strait on the ferry.
海口和三亚都有国际机场。海口是该岛北海岸的首府城市，是来自大陆的长途巴士和火车的入口。是的，没有桥梁：甚至火车也乘坐渡轮穿越琼州海峡。

Getting around most of Hainan is both cheap and easy. A high-speed rail service makes a loop around the island and buses ply the three main expressways (east, west and central). The train is quicker and costs only slightly more than buses; however, most stations are not centrally located and require a bus or taxi transfer to the main town.
游览海南大部分地区既便宜又方便。高铁服务环岛一圈，巴士往返于三条主要高速公路（东、西、中）。火车速度更快，价格仅比公共汽车略高;然而，大多数车站并不位于市中心，需要乘坐巴士或出租车前往主要城镇。

Haikou (Hǎikǒu) means ‘Mouth of the Sea’, and while sea trade remains relatively important, the buzzing provincial capital at the northern tip of Hainan is most notable for its booming construction. New and restarted projects are everywhere.
海口（Hǎikǒu）的意思是“海口”，虽然海上贸易仍然相对重要，但海南北端繁华的省会城市以其蓬勃发展的建设而闻名。新的和重新启动的项目无处不在。

Haikou doesn’t have much in the way of sights, save for its restored downtown; however, there are some decent beaches a short bike or bus ride away, the air is fresh and clean (though worsening yearly because of traffic), and some visitors find themselves quite satisfied just hanging out here for a few days.
海口除了经过修复的市中心外，没有太多景点;然而，骑自行车或乘坐公共汽车很短的路程即可到达一些不错的海滩，空气清新干净（尽管由于交通拥堵，每年都在恶化），一些游客发现自己在这里闲逛几天就很满意。

THE LI & MIAO

The Li, who today number over one million and can only be found on Hainan, were the island’s first-known settlers, likely immigrating from southern China several thousand years ago. The Li were followed by the Miao (H’mong), who can also be found across stretches of northern Vietnam, Laos and Thailand; their arrival pushed the Li into the central highlands. When Han settlers arrived in big numbers during the Qing dynasty (1644–1911), they pushed the Miao, who in turn pushed the Li even further into the mountains.
如今，黎族人口超过100万，只能在海南找到，他们是该岛最早已知的定居者，可能是几千年前从中国南方移民过来的。紧随其后的是苗族（苗族），他们也可以在越南北部、老挝和泰国的地区找到;他们的到来将李氏推向了中部高地。清朝（1644-1911）期间，当汉族定居者大量抵达时，他们将苗族推入山区，而苗族又将黎族推入山区。

Today both populations occupy some of the most rugged terrain on the island, in the forested areas covering the Limuling mountain range that stretches down the centre of the island. Since 1987 several counties in the central highlands have been designated as Li and Miao autonomous regions, which afford the minority groups a degree of independent governance.
今天，这两个种群都占据了岛上一些最崎岖的地形，在覆盖岛中心延伸的利木林山脉的森林地区。自1987年以来，中部高原的几个县被划为黎族和苗族自治区，为少数民族提供了一定程度的独立治理。

来到中部高地的游客将看到黎族文化的视觉标志，包括装饰有传统几何符号的建筑和穿着五颜六色刺绣的校服的孩子们。

博鳌 （Bó'áo） 是一个朴实无华（尽管发展迅速）的海滨小镇，周围环绕着美丽的乡村。它为骑自行车的人提供了一个方便的停靠点。西边是石头和砖砌建筑的小村庄，当地人在小巷中间晒米，在小神社里为当地的民间神灵烧香。

Officially Bo’ao is starting to cover a large area, but the ‘downtown’ blocks, where most travellers both stay and eat, are tiny. The beach is a five-minute walk away.
博鳌正式开始覆盖大片区域，但大多数游客住宿和用餐的“市中心”街区很小。海滩距离酒店有五分钟步行路程。

Avoid visiting during, or the week before, the Bo’ao Forum for Asia (BFA), an annual April meet-up of top-level officials, academics and economists exclusively from the Asia region. The town is pretty much closed off under the scrutiny of high-level security (there are even warships in the harbour).
避免在博鳌亚洲论坛（BFA）期间或前一周参观，博鳌亚洲论坛是一年一度的 4 月聚会，专门来自亚洲地区的高层官员、学者和经济学家。在高级别安全部门的审查下，该镇几乎是封闭的（港口里甚至有军舰）。

China’s premier beach community claims to be the ‘Hawaii of China’, but ‘Moscow on the South China Sea’ is more like it.
