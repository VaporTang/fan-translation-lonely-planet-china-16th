Why Go?
为什么要去？

The stereotype of Henan (河南, Hénán) as a slow-moving and impoverished province may cause affluent Chinese to roll their eyes at its mention, but Henan’s heritage takes us back to the earliest days of Chinese antiquity. Ancient capitals rose and fell in Henan’s north, where the capricious Yellow River (Huáng Hé) nourished the flowering of a great civilisation.
河南（ 河南 ，Hénán）是一个发展缓慢、贫困的省份的刻板印象可能会让富裕的中国人一提到它就会翻白眼，但河南的遗产将我们带回了中国古代的早期。河南北部古都兴起衰落，反复无常的黄河（Huáng Hé）滋养了伟大文明的绽放。

Henan is home to China’s oldest surviving Buddhist temple and one of the country’s most astonishing collections of Buddhist carvings, the Longmen Grottoes. There is also the Shaolin Temple, that legendary institution where the martial way and Buddhism found an unlikely but powerful alliance. Henan’s inability to catch up with the rest of the land perhaps helps to explain why the unusual village of Nanjiecun still sees a future in Maoist collectivism. Henan is also home to the excellent walled town of Kaifeng and the 1000-year-old craft of woodblock printing in Zhuxian Zhen.
河南是中国现存最古老的佛教寺庙的所在地，也是中国最令人惊叹的佛教雕刻收藏之一——龙门石窟。还有少林寺，这是一个传奇的机构，武道和佛教在这里找到了不太可能但强大的联盟。河南无法赶上其他地区，这或许有助于解释为什么南街村这个不寻常的村庄仍然看到了毛主义集体主义的未来。河南还拥有优秀的城寨开封和朱仙镇千年木刻印刷工艺。

History
历史

It is believed that the first Shang capital, perhaps dating back 3800 years, was at Yanshi, west of modern-day Zhengzhou. Around the mid-14th century BC, the capital is thought to have moved to Zhengzhou, where its ancient city walls remain visible.
据信，第一个商都可能可以追溯到 3800 年前，位于今郑州以西的偃师。公元前 14 世纪中叶左右，人们认为首都已迁至郑州，那里的古城墙仍然清晰可见。

Henan again occupied centre stage during the Song dynasty (AD 960–1279), but political power deserted it when the government fled south from its capital at Kaifeng following the 12th-century Jurchen invasion.
河南在宋朝（公元 960-1279 年）再次占据中心舞台，但在 12 世纪女真入侵后，政府从首都开封向南逃亡，政治权力抛弃了它。

Modern Henan has been poor and strife-prone. In 1975 Henan’s Banqiao Dam collapsed after massive rainfall, leading to a string of other dam failures that caused the deaths of 230,000 people. In the 1990s a scandal involving the sale of HIV-tainted blood led to a high incidence of AIDS in several Henan villages.
现代河南一直很穷，容易发生冲突。1975 年，河南板桥大坝在大雨后坍塌，导致一系列其他大坝溃决，造成 23 万人死亡。1990 年代，一起涉及出售受 HIV 污染的血液的丑闻导致河南几个村庄的艾滋病高发率。

The provincial Henan capital of Zhengzhou (Zhèngzhōu) is a rapidly modernising, smog-filled metropolis with few relics from its ancient past (due to Japanese bombing in WWII). The city is a major transport hub and access point for the Shaolin Temple and the left-field Maoist collective of Nanjiecun. Although Zhengzhou can largely be zipped through, don’t despair if you need to stay here for a night or two: Chenghuang Temple, good food and quality shopping malls help to pass the time.
河南省会郑州（浙江）是一座快速现代化、雾霾弥漫的大都市，几乎没有古代遗迹（由于二战期间的日本轰炸）。该市是少林寺和左翼毛派集体南界村的主要交通枢纽和出入口。虽然郑州大部分都可以快速通过，但如果您需要在这里住一两晚，请不要绝望：城隍庙、美食和优质购物中心有助于打发时间。

1Sights
1 景点

Despite a history reaching back to the earliest chapters of Chinese history, the city now only has a few sights of interest to travellers.
尽管这座城市的历史可以追溯到中国历史的最早篇章，但现在游客只感兴趣的景点。

Most worthwhile is the bustling 600-year-old City God temple (城隍庙, Chénghuáng Miào; 4 Shangcheng Lu, 商城路4号; h9am-5pm; g2) F. Worshippers leave its trees festooned with red ribbons and its entrances swirling with incense smoke. There is an opera stage in the temple and a small exhibition of opera costumes on the right side of the complex.
最值得一看的是拥有 600 年历史的城隍庙 （ 隍城庙 ，Chénghuáng Miào;商城路 4 号 h 9am-5pm; g 2） F 。礼拜者离开了树上挂满了红丝带，入口处弥漫着香烟。寺庙内有一个歌剧舞台，建筑群右侧有一个小型戏曲服装展览。

Zhengzhou’s eastern outskirts are marked by long, high mounds of earth, the remains of the old city walls (商代城墙遗址, Shāngdài Chéngqiáng Yízhǐ) F. There are a few small parks around the entrances and you can climb up to explore the mounds.

Nanjiecun (Nánjiēcūn) is China’s very last Maoist collective (gōngshè), and a visit here is a surreal trip back in time – a journey to the puritanical and revolutionary China of the 1950s, when Chairman Mao was becoming a supreme being, money was yesterday’s scene and the menace of karaoke had yet to be prophesied by even the most paranoid party faithful.

In Taoism, Song Shan (嵩山, Sōng Shān) is considered the central mountain (中岳, zhōngyuè) of the five sacred peaks, symbolising earth (土, tǔ) among the five elements and occupying the axis directly beneath heaven. Despite this Taoist persuasion, the mountains are also home to one of China’s most famous and legendary Zen (禅, Chán) Buddhist temples: the inimitable Shaolin Temple. Two main mountains crumple the area, the 1494m-high Mt Taishi and the 1512m-high Shaoshi Shan (少室山, Shàoshì Shān) whose peaks compose Song Shan about 80km west of Zhengzhou.
在道教中，嵩山被认为是五座神峰的中心山（ 中岳 ，zhōngyuè），象征着五行中的土（ 土 ，tǔ），占据天下轴线。尽管有这种道教的劝说，但山上也是中国最著名和最具传奇色彩的禅宗寺庙之一的所在地：无与伦比的少林寺。两座主要山脉将该地区压成一团，分别是海拔 1494 米的太石山和海拔 1512 米的少室山 ，它们的山峰构成了郑州以西约 80 公里处的宋山。

At the foot of Mt Taishi, 12km southeast of the Shaolin Temple and 74km from Zhengzhou, sits the squat little town of Dengfeng (登封, Dēngfēng). While tatty in parts, it’s prettily fringed by mountains, and travellers use it as a base for trips to surrounding sights or exploratory treks into the hills.
在太石山脚下，少林寺东南 12 公里处，距离郑州 74 公里处，坐落着矮矮的登封小镇（ 登封 ，Dēngfēng）。虽然部分地区破旧不堪，但周围环绕着群山，游客将其用作前往周边景点或探索山丘的基地。

Embedded in a mountainous background, with monks garbed in traditional dress and sporting topknots, the temple is less visited and exudes a more palpable air of reverence than its Buddhist sibling, the Shaolin Temple.
在登封以东几公里处，古老而荒凉的中岳庙 （ 中岳庙 ，Zhōngyuè Miào;少林大道， 少林大道 ; ¥30; h 上午 8 点至下午 5 点） 是一个被低估但重要的道教寺院建筑群，其历史可以追溯到公元前 2 世纪。这座寺庙以山区为背景，僧侣们身着传统服饰，打着顶髻，参观人数较少，比其佛教兄弟少林寺散发着更明显的崇敬气息。

1Shaolin Scenic Area
1 少林风景区

The largely rebuilt Shaolin Temple (少林寺, Shàolín Sì; incl with Scenic Area ticket; h8am-5.30pm Mar-Sep, to 5pm Oct-Feb) is a commercialised victim of its own incredible success. A frequent target of war, the ancestral home of wǔshù was last torched in 1928, and the surviving halls – many of recent construction – are today assailed by relentless waves of selfie-shooting tour groups. The temple’s claim to fame, its dazzling gōngfu (kung fu) based on the movements of animals, insects and sometimes mythological figures, guarantees that martial arts clubs around the world make incessant pilgrimages.
已重建的少林寺 （ 少林寺 ，Shàolín Sì;含景区门票; h 3 月至 9 月上午 8 点至下午 5 点 30 分，10 月至 2 月下午 5 点）是其自身令人难以置信的成功的商业化受害者。 武术的祖居经常成为战争目标，最后一次被烧毁是在 1928 年，幸存的殿堂——许多是最近建造的——今天正受到无情的自拍旅行团的袭击。这座寺庙声名鹊起，其令人眼花缭乱的功夫 （功夫）基于动物、昆虫，有时甚至是神话人物的动作，保证了世界各地的武术俱乐部不断前来朝圣。

A satisfying visit to the Shaolin Temple requires, rather than bestows, a Zen mentality (to handle the visiting hordes and looped recordings broadcast from competing loudspeakers). But if you explore away from the main areas, you could spend an entire day or two visiting smaller temples, climbing the surrounding peaks and eking out crumbs of solitude.
对少林寺的令人满意的参观需要而不是赋予禅宗心态（以处理成群结队的来访者和从竞争扬声器播放的循环录音）。但如果你远离主要地区进行探索，你可以花一整天或两天的时间参观较小的寺庙，攀登周围的山峰，勉强吃掉孤独的面包屑。

Coming through the main entrance, you’ll pass several wǔshù schools; the students practising in the red uniforms belong to Shaolin Tagou – the world’s biggest gōngfu school. On the right, about 500m in, is the Wushu Training Centre (武术馆, Wǔshù Guǎn; incl with Scenic Area ticket; h9.30am, 10.30am, 11.30am, 2pm, 3pm, 4pm, 5pm), with shows featuring novices tumbling around and breaking sticks and metal bars over their heads – an integral part of the Shaolin experience, though some may find parts of the performance uncomfortable to watch.

The main temple itself is another 600m along. Many buildings, such as the main Daxiong Hall (大雄宝殿, Dàxióng Bǎodiàn; reconstructed in 1985), burned to the ground in 1928. Although the temple seems to have been founded in approximately the year AD 500 (accounts vary), some halls only date back as far as 2004. Among the oldest structures at the temple are the decorative arches and stone lions, both outside the main gate.
主寺本身还有 600 米。许多建筑，如大雄宝殿（大雄宝殿 ，Dàxióng Bǎodiàn，1985 年重建），于 1928 年被烧毁。 尽管这座寺庙似乎大约建于公元 500 年（说法各不相同），但有些大厅的历史可以追溯到 2004 年。寺庙最古老的建筑之一是装饰拱门和石狮子，均位于正门外。

At the very rear, the West Facing Hall (西方圣人殿, Xīfāng Shēngrén Diàn) has depressions in the floor, famously (and apocryphally) the result of generations of monks practising their stance work, and huge colour frescoes. Always be on the lookout for the ubiquitous Damo (Bodhidharma), whose bearded Indian visage gazes sagaciously from stelae or peeks out from temple halls.
在最后方，西圣人殿 （Xīfāng Shēngrén Diàn）的地板上有凹陷，这是著名的（也是杜撰的）几代僧侣练习站姿工作的结果，还有巨大的彩色壁画。始终留意无处不在的达摩（达摩），他留着胡须的印度面孔从石碑上睿智地凝视着，或者从寺庙大厅里探出头来。

Past the main temple on the right, the Pagoda Forest (少林塔林, Shàolín Tǎlín), a cemetery of 248 brick pagodas, which includes the ashes of eminent monks, is well worth visiting. Not all of the pagodas are ancient; see if you can spot the one with a car, camera and laptop computer on the frieze!
经过右边的主寺，宝塔林 （ 少林塔林 ，Shàolín Tǎlín），这是一个由 248 座砖塔组成的墓地，其中包括高僧的骨灰，非常值得一游。并非所有的宝塔都是古老的;看看你是否能在楣板上找到那个有汽车、相机和笔记本电脑的人！

Between the main temple and the Pagoda Forest, paths lead up Wuru Peak (五乳峰, Wǔrǔ Fēng). Flee the tourist din by heading towards the peak to see the cave (达摩洞, Dámó Dòng) where Damo (Bodhidharma) meditated for nine years; it’s 4km uphill with lots of steps towards the end. From the base, you may spot the peak and the cave, marked by a large, white Bodhisattva figure. En route to the cave, detour to the Chuzu Temple (初祖庵, Chūzǔ Ān), a quiet and battered counterpoint to the other temple. Its main hall, with Northern Song frescoes, is the oldest timber structure in the province (c 1125).
在主寺和宝塔林之间，有小路通向五乳峰 （ 五乳峰 ，Wǔrǔ Fēng）。逃离旅游喧嚣，前往山顶参观达摩（达摩）打坐九年的洞穴 （ 达摩洞 ，Dámó Dòng）;这是 4 公里的上坡路，尽头有很多台阶。从山脚下，您可能会看到山峰和洞穴，以一个巨大的白色菩萨为标志。在前往洞穴的途中，绕道前往初祖庵（初祖庵 ，Chūzǔ Ān），这是一座安静而破旧的寺庙，与另一座寺庙形成鲜明对比。它的主殿有北宋壁画，是该省最古老的木结构建筑（约 1125 年）。

Access point for the incredible Longmen Grottoes outside town, Luoyang (Luòyáng) was one of China’s true dynastic citadels. The city was the prosperous capital of 13 dynasties, until the Northern Song dynasty shifted its capital east along the Yellow River to Kaifeng in the 10th century. o 龙门石窟佛教遗址

(龙门石窟, Lóngmén Shíkū; ¥90, English-speaking guide for west grottoes ¥100; h8am-6.30pm Apr-Oct, to 5.30pm Nov-Jan, to 6pm Feb & Mar)
（ 龙门石窟 ，Lóngmén Shíkū;90 日元，西石窟英语导游 100 日元; h 4 月至 10 月上午 8 点至下午 6 点 30 分，11 月至 1 月至下午 5 点 30 分，2 月和 3 月至下午 6 点）

The ravaged grottoes at Longmen constitute one of China’s handful of surviving masterpieces of Buddhist rock carving. A sutra in stone, the epic achievement of the Longmen Grottoes was commenced by chisellers from the Northern Wei dynasty after the capital relocated here from Datong in the year AD 494. Over the next two centuries, more than 100,000 images and statues of Buddha and his disciples emerged from over a kilometre of limestone cliff wall along the Yi River (伊河, Yī Hé).
龙门被毁坏的石窟是中国现存为数不多的佛教石刻杰作之一。龙门石窟的史诗般的成就是北魏在公元 494 年从大同迁都后由北魏的凿者开始的。在接下来的两个世纪里，超过 100,000 尊佛陀及其弟子的图像和雕像从伊河沿岸一公里多的石灰岩崖壁中出现。

The grottoes are scattered in a line on the west and east sides of the river. Most of the significant Buddhist carvings are on the west side, but a small crop can also be admired after traversing the bridge to the east side. Admission also includes entry to a temple and garden on the east side. English captions are rudimentary, although some carvings have English audio descriptions available via WeChat.
石窟分布在河的西侧和东侧。大多数重要的佛教雕刻都在西侧，但穿过桥到东侧后也可以欣赏到一小块作物。门票还包括进入东侧的寺庙和花园。英文字幕很简陋，尽管有些雕刻有通过微信提供的英文音频描述。

After you’ve seen the west side, you can take a boat (¥25) back to the main entrance to get a riverside view of the grottoes (note that you can’t re-enter the west side once you leave). On the east side, electric carts (¥10) can take you to a variety of locations. All in all, it’s a 3km walk; expect to spend at least three hours here.
看完西侧后，您可以乘船（25 日元）返回正门，欣赏石窟的河边景色（请注意，一旦离开，您将无法再次进入西侧）。在东侧，电动推车（10 日元）可以带您前往各种地点。总而言之，步行3公里;预计在这里至少呆三个小时。

The grottoes are 13km south of Luoyang and can be reached by taxi (¥30); bus 81 (¥1.5, 50 minutes) from the west side of the long-distance bus station on Jinguyuan Lu (金谷园街); or bus 53 from Zhongzhou Donglu. A taxi from Longmen station will cost ¥15. On the way back, be prepared to haggle or take the bus.
石窟位于洛阳以南 13 公里处，可乘坐出租车（30 日元）到达;81 路公交车（¥1.5,50 分钟）从金谷园街长途汽车站西侧;或从中州东路乘坐 53 路公交车。从龙门站乘坐出租车费用为 15 日元。回来的路上，准备好讨价还价或乘坐公共汽车。

A disheartening amount of decapitation disfigures the statuary at this Unesco World Heritage Site. In the early 20th century, many effigies were beheaded by unscrupulous collectors or simply extracted whole, many ending up abroad in such institutions as the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York, the Atkinson Museum in Kansas City and the Tokyo National Museum. Many statues have clearly just had their faces crudely bludgeoned off, vandalism that probably dates from the Cultural Revolution and earlier episodes of anti-Buddhist fervour. The elements have also intervened, wearing smooth the faces of many other statues.
令人沮丧的斩首事件使这个联合国教科文组织世界遗产的雕像毁容。20世纪初，许多雕像被无良收藏家斩首或干脆整件被提取，许多最终被送往国外的纽约大都会艺术博物馆、堪萨斯城阿特金森博物馆和东京国立博物馆等机构。许多雕像的脸显然刚刚被粗暴地捶掉，这种破坏行为可能可以追溯到文化大革命和早期的反佛教狂热。这些因素也介入了，使许多其他雕像的脸都变得光滑。

West Side
西区

Work began on the Three Binyang Grottoes (宾阳三洞, Bīnyáng Sān Dòng) during the Northern Wei dynasty. Despite the completion of two of the grottos during the Sui and Tang dynasties, statues here all display the benevolent expressions that characterised Northern Wei style. Traces of pigment remain within the three large grottoes and other small niches honeycomb the cliff walls. Nearby is the Moya Three Buddha Niche (摩崖三佛龛, Móyá Sānfó Kān), with seven figures that date from the Tang dynasty.
宾阳三洞 （ 宾阳三洞 ，Bīnyáng Sān Dòng）的工程始于北魏时期。尽管其中两座石窟在隋唐时期竣工，但这里的雕像都表现出北魏风格的仁慈表情。三个大石窟内残留着色素的痕迹，悬崖壁上的其他小壁龛蜂窝状。附近是摩崖三佛龛（摩崖三佛龛 ，Móyá Sānfó Kān），有七尊唐代人物。

The Tang dynasty Ten Thousand Buddha Grotto (万佛洞, Wànfó Dòng) dates from 680. In addition to its namesake galaxy of tiny bas-relief Buddhas, there is a fine effigy of the Amitabha Buddha. Note the red pigment on the ceiling.
唐代万佛洞 （ 万佛洞 ，Wànfó Dòng）的历史可以追溯到 680 年。除了同名的微小浅浮雕佛像星系外，还有一尊精美的阿弥陀佛雕像。注意天花板上的红色颜料。

The most physically imposing and magnificent of all the Longmen carvings, the vast Losana Buddha Statue Grotto (奉先寺, Fèngxiān Sì) was created during the Tang dynasty between 672 and 675; it contains the best examples of sculpture, despite evident weathering and vandalism. Nine principal figures dominate: the Buddha, two disciples, two Bodhisattvas, two heavenly kings and two guardians. The 17m-high seated central Buddha is said to be Losana, whose face is allegedly modelled on Tang empress and Buddhist patron Wu Zetian, who funded its carving.
巨大的罗撒那佛像石窟 （ 奉先寺 ，Fèngxiān Sì）是所有龙门雕刻中最雄伟、最宏伟的，创作于唐朝 672 年至 675 年之间;它包含了雕塑的最佳例子，尽管有明显的风化和破坏行为。九个主要人物占主导地位：佛陀、两个弟子、两个菩萨、两个天王和两个守护者。据说这座 17 米高的中央坐佛是洛萨那，据称其脸是仿照唐后和佛教赞助人武则天建造的，武则天资助了它的雕刻。

The Tang figures tend to be more three-dimensional than the Northern Wei figures, while their expressions and poses also seem more natural. In contrast to the otherworldly effigies of the Northern Wei, many Tang figures possess a more fearsome ferocity and muscularity, most noticeably in the huge guardian figure in the north wall.
唐人人物往往比北魏人物更加立体，而他们的表情和姿势也显得更加自然。与北魏超凡脱俗的肖像相比，许多唐人人物拥有更可怕的凶猛和肌肉发达，最明显的是北墙上巨大的守护者形象。

East Side
东区

Although the east side grottoes (东山石窟, Dōngshān Shíkū) lack comparable grandeur – many are even gated shut – there are still some gems to seek out here. The first stop you’ll come across after crossing the bridge is the Leigutai Architectural Site (擂鼓台建筑遗址, Léigǔtái Jiànzhù Yízhǐ), which takes visitors through an earlier excavation, with various Tang and Song relics on display. It’s through a pair of doors at the top of the steps.
尽管东山石窟（东山石窟 ，Dōngshān Shíkū）缺乏可比的宏伟——许多石窟甚至被封闭——但这里仍然有一些瑰宝值得寻找。过桥后，您会遇到的第一站是雷古台建筑遗址 （ 擂鼓台建筑遗址，Léigǔtái Jiànzhù Yízhǐ），它带领游客参观早期的发掘，展出了各种唐宋遗迹。它是通过台阶顶部的一对门。

Although badly faded, the delicate Thousand Arm and Thousand Eye Guanyin (千手千眼观音龛, Qiānshǒu Qiānyǎn Guānyīn Kān) in Grotto 2132 is a splendid bas-relief dating from the Tang dynasty, revealing the Goddess of Mercy framed in a huge fan of carved hands, each sporting an eye.
2132 年石窟中精致的千手千眼观音龛（千手千眼观音龛 ，Qiānshǒu Qiānyǎn Guānyīn Kān）虽然褪色严重，但却是一幅可追溯到唐代的精美浅浮雕，展示了一个巨大的雕刻手扇子中的观音女神，每扇扇子上都有一只眼睛。

Further is the eastern side’s largest site, the Reading Sutra Grotto (看经寺洞, Kàn Jīng Sìdòng), with a carved lotus on its ceiling and 29 expressive luóhàn (arhats) around the base of the walls.
再往前是东侧最大的遗址—— 读经寺洞 （ 看经寺洞 ，Kàn Jīng Sìdòng），天花板上有一朵莲花，墙壁底部有 29 个富有表现力的罗汉 （罗汉）。

At the top of a steep flight of steps, the Xiangshan Temple (香山寺, Xiāngshān Sì) nestles against a hill. It was first built in 516 and has been repeatedly restored. Look for the villa which once belonged to Chiang Kaishek, built in 1936 to celebrate his 50th birthday.
香山寺 （ 香山寺 ，Xiāngshān Sì）坐落在陡峭的台阶顶端，依偎在一座小山上。始建于 516 年，历经多次修复。寻找曾经属于蒋介石的别墅，建于 1936 年，以庆祝他的 50 岁生日。

The final stop is a lovely garden built around the tomb of Bai Juyi (白居易墓地, Bái Jūyì Mùdì), a poet from the Tang dynasty. It’s a peaceful, leafy place with windy and secluded paths. Rest your tired feet at the shady alfresco cafe, where you can get tea (from ¥98), snacks and instant noodles.
最后一站是围绕唐代诗人白居易墓地（白居易墓地 ，Bái Jūyì Mùdì）墓而建的美丽花园。这是一个宁静、绿树成荫的地方，有风和僻静的小路。在阴凉的露天咖啡馆休息疲惫的双脚，在那里您可以享用茶（98 日元起）、小吃和方便面。

White Horse TempleBUDDHIST MONASTERY
白马寺佛教寺院

(白马寺, Báimǎ Sì; ¥35, audio guide ¥20; h7.40am-6pm)
（ 白马寺 ，Báimǎ Sì;35 日元，语音导览 20 日元; h 上午 7 点 40 分至下午 6 点）

Although its original structures have all been replaced and older Buddhist shrines may have vanished, this vast, active monastery outside Luoyang is regarded as China’s first surviving Buddhist temple, originally dating from AD 68. When two Han dynasty court emissaries went in search of Buddhist scriptures, they met two Indian monks in Afghanistan; the monks returned to Luoyang on white horses carrying Buddhist sutras and statues. The impressed emperor built the temple for the monks; it’s also their resting place.
尽管其原来的建筑已被全部更换，较旧的佛教圣地可能也消失了，但这座位于洛阳郊外的巨大而活跃的寺院被认为是中国第一座幸存的佛教寺庙，其历史可追溯至公元 68 年。两位汉朝宫廷使者去寻佛经时，在阿富汗遇到了两位印度僧人;僧侣们骑着白马，驮着佛经和造像回到了洛阳。感动的皇帝为僧侣建造了寺庙;这也是他们的安息之地。

More than 500 years later, the monk Xuanzang began his ‘journey to the west’ pilgrimage from here, and served as the abbot of White Horse Temple upon his return.
500多年后，玄奘和尚从这里开始了他的“西游”朝圣之旅，回来后担任白马寺住持。

In Mahavira hall, it is astonishing to note that the main sculpture is hollow and weighs only 5kg. Outside, keep an eye out for the bronze ‘longevity peach’ – give it a good rub, then brush your hands over your head or whichever body part you think might need a little extra luck.
在摩诃毗罗殿中，令人惊讶的是，主雕塑是空心的，重量仅为 5 公斤。在外面，留意青铜色的“长寿桃”——好好揉搓一下，然后用手拂过头顶或你认为可能需要一点额外运气的身体部位。

Tucked amid the smoky incense burners and usual Buddhist halls are some unusual sights; plan on spending at least two hours here. In the back of the complex, beneath a raised hall, is the Shiyuan Art Gallery (释源美术馆, Shìyuán Měishùguǎn), displaying temporary exhibitions. Also in the back of the complex is a surprisingly chic teahouse (止语茶舍, zhǐyǔ cháshě), an excellent place to take refuge and relax with a bowl of weak tea (free: help yourself from the warmer).
隐藏在烟雾缭绕的香炉和普通的佛教殿堂中，有一些不寻常的景点;计划在这里至少呆两个小时。在建筑群的后面，一个凸起的大厅下面，是释源美术馆（释源美术馆 ，Shìyuán Měishùguǎn），展示临时展览。在建筑群的后面还有一个令人惊讶的别致茶馆 （ 止语茶舍 ，zhǐyǔ cháshě），这是一个避难和喝一碗淡茶放松身心的好地方（免费：从温暖的地方帮助自己）。

West of the historic grounds is the remarkable International Zone, featuring Thai, Burmese and Indian Buddhist temples. It’s certainly worth strolling around.
历史悠久的场地以西是引人注目的国际区 ，以泰国、缅甸和印度佛教寺庙为特色。当然值得四处逛逛。

At the opposite end of the grounds are gardens and the ancient 12-tiered Qiyun Pagoda (齐云塔, Qíyún Tǎ), encircled by worshippers. People say that if you stand 20m back from the pagoda and clap your hands, the echo sounds like a croaking frog.
场地的另一端是花园和古老的 12 层齐云塔 （ 齐云塔 ，Qíyun Tǎ），周围环绕着信徒。人们说，如果你站在离宝塔 20 米后面拍手，回声听起来像青蛙的呱呱叫声。

The temple is 13km east of Luoyang, around 40 minutes away on bus 56 from the Xīguān (西关) stop. Bus 58 from Zhongzhou Donglu in the Old Town also runs here.
该寺位于洛阳以东 13 公里处，从西关站乘坐 56 路公交车约 40 分钟即可到达。从老城区中州东路出发的 58 路公交车也在此运行。

Guoliang 郭亮
国亮 郭亮

On its clifftop perch high up in the Mountains of the Ten Thousand Immortals (万仙山, Wànxiān Shān) in north Henan, this high-altitude stone hamlet was for centuries sheltered from the outside world by a combination of inaccessibility and anonymity. Guoliang (Guōliàng) shot to fame as the bucolic backdrop to a clutch of Chinese films, which firmly embedded the village in contemporary Chinese mythology.
这个高海拔的石头村庄位于河南北部万仙山（ 万仙山 ）的高处，几个世纪以来一直因交通不便和匿名而与外界隔绝。国梁（国梁）作为一系列中国电影的田园背景而声名鹊起，这些电影将这个村庄牢牢地融入了当代中国神话中。

Today the village attracts legions of artists, who journey here to capture the unreal mountain scenery on paper and canvas. Joining them are Chinese tourists who get disgorged by the busload. For a more isolated mountaintop experience, come on an out-of-season weekday when it’s more tranquil. New buildings have sprung up but the original dwellings – climbing the mountain slope – retain their simple, rustic charms. Long treks beneath the marvellous limestone peaks more than compensate for the hard slog of journeying here.
如今，这个村庄吸引了大批艺术家，他们来到这里，在纸上和画布上捕捉虚幻的山景。加入他们的还有被公共汽车堆吐的中国游客。如果想要获得更偏僻的山顶体验，请在淡季工作日前来，那里更宁静。新建筑如雨后春笋般涌现，但原来的住宅——爬上山坡——保留了其简单、质朴的魅力。在奇妙的石灰岩山峰下长途跋涉足以弥补在这里旅行的艰辛。

At 1700m above sea level and approximately 6°C (42.8°F) colder than Zhengzhou, Guoliang is cool enough to be devoid of mosquitoes year-round (some locals say), but pack warm clothes for winter visits, which can be bonenumbing. Visiting in low season may seem odd advice, but come evening the village can be utterly tranquil, and moonlit nights are intoxicating.
国梁海拔 1700 米，比郑州低约 6°C （42.8°F），凉爽到全年没有蚊子（一些当地人说），但冬季游览时要带上保暖的衣服，这可能会让人感到骨头麻木。在淡季参观似乎很奇怪，但到了晚上，村庄就会变得完全宁静，月夜令人陶醉。

1Sights & Activities
1 景点和活动

Several kilometres before the village, you’ll need to purchase an admission ticket to the Wanxian Shan Scenic Area (¥115); the required ticket includes free transport on the park’s green shuttle buses. Bring your passport.
在村庄前几公里，您需要购买万贤山风景区的门票（115 日元）;所需门票包括公园绿色穿梭巴士的免费交通。带上你的护照。

All of the village dwellings, many hung with butter-yellow bàngzi (sweetcorn cobs), are hewn from the same local stone that paves the slender alleyways, sculpts the bridges and fashions the picturesque gates of Guoliang village. Swallowed up by new construction, the original village can be easy to miss – it’s on the right and up the hill from the rest of town.
所有的村庄住宅 ，许多都挂着黄油黄色的 bàngzi（甜玉米棒子），都是用当地的石头凿成的，这些石头铺设了细长的小巷，雕刻了桥梁，并塑造了国梁村风景如画的大门。被新建筑吞没的原村很容易被错过——它位于右侧，距离城镇其他地区上山。

Most buses from the ticket office terminate at the Long Corridor in the Cliffs (绝壁长廊, Juébì Chángláng), leaving visitors to walk through for a closer perspective on the plunging cliffs, with dramatic views from the tunnel carved through the rock. Before this tunnel was built by hand (between 1972 and 1978) by a local man called Shen Mingxin and several others, the only way into the village was via the Sky Ladder (天梯, Tiān Tī) – steep Ming dynasty steps hewn from the local stone.
售票处出发的大多数巴士终点站是悬崖长廊（绝壁长廊 ，Juébì Chángláng），游客可以步行穿过，可以更近距离地欣赏陡峭的悬崖，从穿过岩石的隧道中可以欣赏到壮丽的景色。在这条隧道由当地人沈明新和其他几个人手工修建之前（1972 年至 1978 年间），进入村庄的唯一途径是通过天梯 （ 天梯 Tiān Tī）——用当地石头凿成的陡峭的明代台阶。

The stairs have no guard rails and have fallen into disrepair, making them off limits to visitors, but the 2km walk from the village is among the area’s most scenic. To get here, take the left fork of the road heading back from the village towards the tunnel and walk for 2km.
楼梯没有护栏，年久失修，禁止游客进入，但距离村庄 2 公里的步行路程是该地区风景最优美的地方之一。要到达这里，请从村庄返回隧道的左岔路口步行 2 公里。

On the other side of the precipice from the village, across the bridge, a small row of cottages, set almost on the edge of the cliff, is Yáshàng Rénjiā (崖上人家), four family compounds that date back to the Ming dynasty. Nearby, there’s a viewing platform that sits atop a pillar of rock; step across the walkway for astonishing views into the canyon.
在村庄悬崖的另一边，桥对面，有一小排小屋，几乎坐落在悬崖边上 ，是崖上人家 ，这四个家族大院的历史可以追溯到明代。附近有一个观景台，坐落在一根岩石柱上;穿过人行道，欣赏峡谷的壮丽景色。

Alternatively, head up the valley through the strip of street stalls and hotels to get to the start of a 5km circuit. From the end of the street, it’s an additional 1.3km to the starting point of the loop. (Sadly, the mood of the area has been spoilt in parts by the addition of several constructed oddities, including a mini zip line and a drain-like slide ride from the top of the mountain.) If you start on the left-hand set of steps, you’ll first go past the awe-inspiring curtain of rock above the Shouting Spring (喊泉, Hǎn Quán). According to local lore, its flow responds to the loudness of your whoops (it doesn’t, but the site is predictably a riot of noise). You’ll also pass the peaceful Old Pool (老潭, Lǎo Tán), which is thankfully out of earshot of the spring. Further along is the Red Dragon Cave (红龙洞, Hónglóng Dòng), now closed, and after a few steep flights of stairs, the slide ride (¥30) and then the small White Dragon Cave (白龙洞, Báilóng Dòng; ¥20), which you can skip with no regrets. The last sight is a set of steps that lead up to Pearl Spring (珍珠泉, Zhēnzhū Quán), a fissure in the mountain from which pours out cool, clear, spring water. You can, of course, do the loop in the opposite direction.
或者，穿过街边摊和酒店地带沿着山谷向上行驶，到达 5 公里环路的起点。从街道尽头到环路起点再行驶 1.3 公里。（可悲的是，该地区的气氛在某种程度上被一些建造的奇怪设施所破坏，包括一条迷你高空滑索和从山顶上滑下水道一样的滑梯。如果您从左侧的台阶开始，您将首先经过喊泉 （Hǎn Quán）上方令人敬畏的岩石帷幕。根据当地的传说，它的流量会对你的呜呜声的响度做出反应（事实并非如此，但可以预见的是，该地点会充满噪音）。您还将经过宁静的老潭（老潭 ，Lǎo Tán），值得庆幸的是，它不在泉水的耳边。再往前走就是红龙洞 （ 红龙洞 ，Hónglóng Dòng），现已关闭，经过几段陡峭的楼梯后，乘坐滑梯（30 日元），然后是小白龙洞 （ 白龙洞 ，Báilóng Dòng;20 日元）， 您可以跳过，没有后悔。最后一个景点是通往珍珠泉 （ 珍珠泉 ，Zhēnzhū Quán）的一组台阶，这是山上的一条裂缝，从那里倾泻出凉爽、清澈的泉水。当然，您可以沿相反方向循环。

Take the time to visit the western half of the scenic area. Most shuttles leaving from the lower end of the Long Corridor will take you to the village under Sun Moon Star Rock (400m up the road from Nanping). From here, it’s a 1500m walk to Black Dragon Pool, which is at least 28m deep (the shape of the pool has thus far made it impossible to measure any deeper). Above the pool is a stunning 40m-tall waterfall. For a longer walk, wooden steps lead from the pool high up into the mountain. Take your own water with you.
花点时间参观景区的西半部。大多数从长廊下端出发的班车都会带您前往日月星岩下的村庄（距离南平 400 米）。从这里步行 1500 米即可到达黑龙潭，黑龙潭至少有 28 米深（池子的形状迄今为止无法测量更深的水池）。泳池上方是一个令人惊叹的 40m 高瀑布。如果想走更远的路，木台阶从高处的游泳池通向山上。带上自己的水。

4Sleeping & Eating
4 睡眠和饮食

There are hotels galore in Guoliang village, most heading up the valley, though there are also rooms along the road by Yáshàng Rénjiā. All offer identical two-star quality, with hot showers, private squat toilets, wi-fi and TVs (no toiletries or towels though). Rooms cost ¥50 to ¥100 depending on size and orientation. Prices are a bit higher during the summer but negotiable in the low season and on weekdays.
国梁村有很多酒店，大部分都沿着山谷向上，尽管沿路也有 Yáshàng Rénjiā 的房间。所有客房均提供相同的二星级品质，配有热水淋浴、私人蹲式卫生间、无线网络和电视（但没有洗浴用品或毛巾）。房间费用为 50 至 100 日元，具体取决于大小和方向。夏季价格稍高，但在淡季和工作日可以协商。

Small restaurants are everywhere you turn, with most attached to one of the innumerable village guesthouses. Expect a sampling of classic Chinese dishes and noodles, costing in the range of ¥25 to ¥60.
小餐馆随处可见，大多数都附属于无数乡村宾馆之一。您可以品尝到经典的中国菜肴和面条，价格在 25 日元至 60 日元之间。

From here, minibuses depart for Xinxiang (¥25) at 6.20am, 9am, noon, 1pm, 1.30pm, 3pm and 5pm. If there are only a few people on the bus, it may only go as far as Huixian.

Kaifeng 开封

%0378 / Pop 2.23 million

More than any other of Henan’s ancient capitals, Kaifeng (Kāifēng) makes an effort to recall its former grandeur. The walled town has character: you may have to squint a bit and sift the reproductions from its genuine historical narrative, but the city still offers up an intriguing display of age-old charm, magnificent market food, relics from its longvanished apogee, and colourful chrysanthemums, the city flower (Kaifeng is also known as Júchéng, or ‘Chrysanthemum Town’).
与河南的任何其他古都相比，开封（Kāifēng）更努力地回忆起它曾经的宏伟。这座有城墙的小镇很有特色：你可能不得不眯着眼睛，从其真实的历史叙事中筛选出复制品，但这座城市仍然展示了古老的魅力、华丽的市场美食、消失已久的远地点的遗迹，以及五颜六色的菊花，城市之花（开封也被称为菊花，或“菊花镇”）。

You won’t see soaring skyscrapers, though – one reason being that buildings requiring deep foundations are prohibited, for fear of destroying the ancient northern Song dynasty city below.
不过，你不会看到高耸入云的摩天大楼——原因之一是禁止建造需要深地基的建筑，因为担心会破坏下面的北宋古城。

History
历史

Once the prosperous capital of the Northern Song dynasty (AD 960–1126), Kaifeng was established south of the Yellow River, but not far enough to escape the river’s capricious wrath. After centuries of flooding, the city of the Northern Song largely lies buried 8m to 9m deep in hardened silt. Between 1194 and 1938 the city flooded 368 times, an average of once every two years.
开封曾经是北宋（公元 960-1126 年）繁荣的首都，建立在黄河以南，但距离不足以逃脱河流反复无常的愤怒。经过几个世纪的洪水，北宋城大部分埋在 8 米至 9 米深的硬化淤泥中。1194 年至 1938 年间，该市发生了 368 次洪水，平均每两年发生一次。

By the early 13th century Kaifeng had a whopping one million inhabitants and likely was the most populous city in the world. An important stop along the Silk Road, Kaifeng was also the first city in China where Jewish merchants settled when they arrived. Sadly, little remains of Kaifeng’s once thriving Jewish community – all that remains of Kaifeng Synagogue is a well with an iron lid in the boiler room of the Kaifeng Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital and the name of the brick alley south of the hospital – Jiaojing Hutong (教经胡同, Teaching the Torah Alley). A small Christian and Catholic community also lives in Kaifeng alongside a much larger local Muslim Hui community.
到 13 世纪初，开封拥有多达 100 万居民，可能是世界上人口最多的城市。开封是丝绸之路沿线的重要一站，也是中国犹太商人抵达后第一个定居的城市。可悲的是，开封曾经繁荣的犹太社区所剩无几——开封犹太教堂仅存一口开封中医院锅炉房里的一口铁盖井，以及医院南边砖巷的名字——教经胡同 。一个小型基督教和天主教社区也住在开封，还有一个更大的当地穆斯林回族社区。

First founded in AD 555, this frequently rebuilt temple vanished along with Kaifeng in the early 1640s, when rebels breached the Yellow River’s dykes. During the Northern Song, the temple covered a massive 34 hectares and housed over 10,000 monks. The show-stopper today is the mesmerising Four-Faced Thousand Hand Thousand Eye Guanyin (四面千手千眼观世音, Sìmiàn Qiānshǒu Qiānyǎn Guānshìyīn), towering within the octagonal Arhat Hall (罗汉殿, Luóhàn Diàn), beyond the Hall of Tathagata (大雄宝殿, Dàxióng Bǎodiàn).

Kāifēng Fǔ 历史遗址

(map Google map; 开封府; north side, Baogong East Lake, 包公湖北岸; ¥65; h7.30am-7pm Mar-Nov, to 5.30pm Dec-Feb)
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 开封府 ;北侧，包公湖北岸 ;¥65; h 3 月至 11 月上午 7 时 30 分至晚上 7 时，12 月至 2 月下午 5 时 30 分）

This reconstructed site of the government offices of the Northern Song has daily theatricals commencing outside the gates – the doors are thrown open and costumed actors play period scenes, complete with cracking whips and the sound of gongs. Drumming, kung-fu displays and Chinese-language plays are staged inside throughout the day. Drama aside, the site is one of Kaifeng’s better recreations of Song imperial life, with English explanations, martial parade grounds, a prison and several appearances by the famed Judge Bao.
这座重建的北宋官府遗址每天都有戏剧在大门外开始——门被推开，身着古装的演员扮演时代场景，伴随着劈啪作响的鞭子和锣声。室内全天上演击鼓、功夫表演和中文戏剧。撇开戏剧性不谈，该遗址是开封对宋朝皇帝生活的最佳再现之一，有英文解说、阅兵场、监狱和著名的包法官的多次露面。

Kaifeng City WallsWALLS
开封城墙 WALLS

(map Google map; 城墙, Chéng Qiáng) F
（ 地图谷歌地图 ; 城墙 ， Chéng Qiáng） F

Kaifeng is ringed by a relatively intact, much-restored Qing dynasty wall, which you can climb up at various points. There’s precious little to see from up top and it can be punishingly hot on a sunny day (bring plenty of water and a hat). In fact, the best way to appreciate the bastion is to walk alongside it at ground level, through various small parks and with plenty of local life to observe.
开封周围环绕着一堵相对完整、经过大量修复的清代城墙，您可以在各个地方爬上去。从顶部看不到什么，在阳光明媚的日子里可能会非常炎热（带上大量的水和帽子）。事实上，欣赏堡垒的最佳方式是在地面上沿着它走，穿过各种小公园，并观察大量当地生活。

Today’s bastion was built on the foundations of the Song dynasty Inner Wall (内城, Nèichéng). Rising up outside was the mighty, now buried Outer Wall (外城, Wàichéng), a colossal construction containing 18 gates, which looped south of Po Pagoda.
今天的堡垒是在宋代内城（ 内城 ，Nèichéng）的基础上建造的。外面耸立着雄伟的、现在被埋没的外墙（外城 ，Wàichéng），这是一座巨大的建筑，包含 18 个城门，环绕着宝塔以南。

Po PagodaBUDDHIST PAGODA
宝塔佛教宝塔

(繁塔, Pó Tǎ; Pota Xijie, 繁塔西街; ¥15; h9am-5.30pm)
（ 繁塔 ，Pó Tǎ;Pota Xijie， 繁塔西街 ; ¥15; h 上午 9 点至下午 5.30）

This stumpy pagoda from 974 is the oldest Buddhist structure in Kaifeng and was originally a nine-storey hexagonal building, typical of the Northern Song style. The pagoda is clad in tiles decorated with 108 different Buddha images – note that most of the Buddhas on the lower levels have had their faces smashed off. ALONG THE RIVER DURING THE QINGMING FESTIVAL
清明节沿江

Now held in the Forbidden City and widely acknowledged as China’s first shén (godly) painting, Along the River During the Qingming Festival was completed by Zhang Zeduan (张择端) in the early 12th century. These days, you’ll see versions of it everywhere in Kaifeng. Museums, parks and bridges have it in carved wood and stone bas-relief; it’s found in scale dioramas, souvenir posters and advertising; and there’s even a theme park modelled after it.
《清明节沿江 》现藏于紫禁城，被广泛认为是中国第一幅神画，由张择端于 12 世纪初完成。如今，你会在开封到处看到它的版本。博物馆、公园和桥梁都有木雕和石头浅浮雕;它出现在比例立体模型、纪念海报和广告中;甚至还有一个仿照它建造的主题公园。

The long (about 25cm x 529cm) painting depicts life in Kaifeng during the Song dynasty. The painting is packed to the gills with details of the period: boats unloading goods at a harbour, an inn crowded with customers and children playing on the streets. When the original is displayed in Beijing, queues to see it last hours. Art enthusiasts will no doubt recognise later copies of the work, some of which are equally famous – the 1737 version, presented to Emperor Qianlong, is now held in Taipei’s national Palace Museum.
这幅长画（约 25cm x 529cm）描绘了宋代开封的生活。这幅画充满了那个时期的细节：在港口卸货的船只，挤满顾客的旅馆和在街上玩耍的孩子们。当原件在北京展出时，排队观看要持续几个小时。艺术爱好者无疑会认出这幅作品的后来复制品，其中一些同样著名——献给乾隆皇帝的 1737 年版本现在收藏在台北国立故宫博物院。

Versions in Kaifeng include the scale diorama in the Shanshangan Guild Hall and the replica in the Kaifeng Museum.
开封的版本包括山山阁会馆的比例立体模型和开封博物馆的复制品。

Zhongshan Lu is a good place to catch buses (¥1) to most sights. Taxis (flagfall ¥5, pollution tax ¥1) are the best way to get about.
中山路是乘坐巴士（1 日元）前往大多数景点的好地方。出租车（旗帜5日元，污染税1日元）是最好的出行方式。

避免使用三轮车，因为它们经常敲诈游客。

Zhuxian Zhen 朱仙镇

%0371 / Pop 38,630
% 0371 / 人口 38,630

Zhuxian Zhen (Zhūxiān Zhèn), where the 1000-year-old craft of woodblock printing (木板年画, mùbǎn niánhuà) is still practised, is known as one of China’s four ‘ancient’ towns: the other three are Hankou (trade), Jingdezhen (porcelain) and Foshan (silk). An easy day trip from Kaifeng, the woodblock prints here are a sure highlight for anyone interested in traditional Chinese arts and crafts.
竹县镇（Zhūxiān Zhèn）至今仍在实行具有千年历史的木版画（ 木板年画 ，mùbǎn niánhuà），被誉为中国四大“古镇”之一：另外三个是汉口（贸易）、景德镇（瓷器）和佛山（丝绸）。从开封出发的一日游很轻松，这里的木刻版画对于任何对中国传统艺术和手工艺感兴趣的人来说都是一个亮点。

The expertly baked naan (flat bread; ¥2), sold by Muslim vendors in the street, may just be some of the freshest bread you’ll eat in your life.
街上穆斯林小贩出售的专业烘焙馕饼（扁面包，2 日元）可能是您一生中吃到的最新鲜的面包之一。

At the time of writing, Zhuxian’s charming main drag had been engulfed by enormously dusty roadworks. All the buildings were intact, but you may have to clamber over some rough sections to reach the two temples and the Yin family workshop.
在撰写本文时，朱仙迷人的主干道已被尘土飞扬的道路工程所吞没。所有的建筑都完好无损，但你可能需要爬过一些崎岖的路段才能到达两座寺庙和尹家作坊。

Heading 700m or so south off the main road along a wide stone path will take you to the mosque (朱仙清真寺, Zhūxiān Qīngzhēn Sì) F, originally founded in the Northern Song and featuring elaborately carved beams and lintels.
沿着一条宽阔的石板路向南行驶 700 米左右，您将到达清真寺（朱仙清真寺 ，Zhūxiān Qīngzhēn Sì）， F 该寺最初建于北宋时期，拥有精心雕刻的横梁和门楣。

Guanyu TempleTEMPLE
观玉寺 TEMPLE

(关庙, Guān Miào; ¥10; h8am-5pm)
（ 关庙 ，Guān Miào;¥10; h 上午 8 点至下午 5 点）

Dedicated to Guandi, the god of war and protection (among other things), this temple was originally built during the Ming dynasty (then dedicated to the god of wealth); the present structure dates back to 1708. Consisting of a single hall, there is not much to see here besides the building and a twisted pine tree – touch the top branch for fortune, the rear for longevity, or the middle for a promotion.
这座寺庙供奉着战争和保护之神（除其他外）的关帝，最初建于明代（当时供奉财神）;目前的建筑可以追溯到 1708 年。这里由一个大厅组成，除了建筑和一棵扭曲的松树外，没有什么可看的——触摸顶部的树枝可以发财，后部的树枝可以长寿，中间的树枝可以升职。

Yue Fei TempleTEMPLE
岳飞寺 TEMPLE

(岳飞庙, Yuè Fēi Miào; %2675 8556; ¥30; h8am-6pm summer, 8.30am-5pm winter)
（ 岳飞庙 ，Yuè Fēi Miào; % 2675 8556; 30 元; h 夏季上午 8 点至下午 6 点，冬季上午 8.30 至下午 5 点）

Dedicated to the Southern Song military hero Yue Fei, this temple was first founded in 1478. The weatherbeaten temple hasn’t seen much restoration, and has a refreshing sense of time and place that is difficult to find in most popular temples.
这座寺庙供奉南宋军事英雄岳飞，始建于 1478 年。这座饱经风霜的寺庙没有经过太多修复，具有在大多数受欢迎的寺庙中很难找到的令人耳目一新的时间和地点感。

Don’t be surprised to come across the ‘five kneeling traitors’ statues of the corrupt officials who persecuted Yue Fei and were instrumental in the hero’s execution. The officials were not punished in their lifetimes but statues like these were erected posthumously at Yue Fei’s mausoleum in Hangzhou. Visitors are encouraged to give the traitors a little whip (whips provided) as they pass by.
遇到迫害岳飞并在处决英雄中发挥重要作用的贪官的“五跪汉奸”雕像，请不要感到惊讶。官员们生前没有受到惩罚，但像这样的雕像被追封在杭州岳飞陵墓中。鼓励游客在叛徒经过时给他们一根小鞭子（提供鞭子）。

The artist/owner of this woodblock printing workshop, Mr Yin (尹), is a fifth-generation artisan whose family has been in business for more than 200 years. A beautifully bound book of prints, with English explanations, housed in a wooden presentation box costs ¥300 (but if you’re nice, he may knock a little off the price). Located 100m east of the Yue Fei Temple.
这家木刻版画作坊的艺术家/老板尹先生是第五代工匠，他的家族经营了 200 多年。装在木制展示盒中的一本装订精美的版画书，并附有英文解释，售价 300 日元（但如果你好心的话，他可能会稍微降低价格）。位于岳飞寺以东 100m 处。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

As it’s an easy day trip from Kaifeng, there is no need to spend the night in Zhuxian.
由于这是从开封出发的轻松一日游，因此无需在竹县过夜。

Head to Kaifeng’s west bus station, from where bus 306 (¥5, one hour, every 15 minutes) runs all the way to Zhuxian. The driver can let you off at the mosque, or you can get off at a busy thoroughfare closer to the centre of town. The last bus from Zhuxian leaves at 6pm.
前往开封西公交站 ，从那里乘坐 306 路公交车（5 日元，一小时，每 15 分钟一班）一路开往竹县。司机可以在清真寺下车，也可以在靠近市中心的繁忙大道下车。从竹县出发的末班车在下午 6 点出发。
