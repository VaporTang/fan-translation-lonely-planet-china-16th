Why Go?
为什么要去？

Rarely explored by foreign travellers, the luscious province of Jiangxi (江西, Jiāngxī) offers a bucolic entrée into semirural Chinese life. It’s a succulent, green place, connected by waterways of natural and human design, rice paddies teeming with bird life and fields draped in wildflowers. Tea seemingly grows out of every patch of land until dramatic mountain ranges, swirling with mist, rise up at its edges.
外国游客很少探索，甘美的江西省（ 江西 ，江西）是中国半乡村生活的田园风光。这是一个多汁的绿色地方，由自然和人类设计的水道、充满鸟类的稻田和开满野花的田野连接起来。茶叶似乎从每一片土地上生长出来，直到雄伟的山脉，在薄雾中旋转，在它的边缘升起。

Jiangxi has joined the high-speed rail circuit and now there’s a new breed of local, prosperous traveller. They come here for the story-book villages around Wuyuan, the remote mountain parks of great spiritual significance and the ‘white gold’ of Jingdezhen, the porcelain capital of China.
江西加入了高铁线路，现在出现了新一代的本地繁荣旅客。他们来这里是为了五原周围的故事村、具有重要精神意义的偏远山间公园和中国瓷器之都景德镇的“白金”。

The cities here are small, manageable and surprisingly pleasant, but it’s the irresistible countryside villages, with their traditional architecture and their altogether slower pace of life, which are the real highlight of a visit to this charming pocket of southeast China.
这里的城市很小，易于管理，令人惊讶地宜人，但令人难以抗拒的乡村，其传统的建筑和完全缓慢的生活节奏，才是游览中国东南这个迷人地区的真正亮点。

History
历史

Jiangxi’s Gan River Valley was the principal trade route that linked Guangdong with the rest of the country in imperial times. Its strategic location, natural resources and long growing season have ensured that the province has always been relatively well off. Jiangxi is most famous for its imperial porcelain (from Jingdezhen), although its contributions to philosophy and literature are perhaps more significant, particularly during the Tang and Song dynasties.
江西赣河流域是帝国时代连接广东与全国其他地区的主要贸易路线。其战略位置、自然资源和漫长的生长季节确保了该省一直相对富裕。江西以其御瓷（来自景德镇）而闻名，尽管它对哲学和文学的贡献可能更为重要，尤其是在唐宋时期。

Peasant unrest arose in the 19th century when the Taiping rebels swept through the Yangzi River Valley. Rebellion continued into the 20th century, and Jiangxi became one of the earliest bases for the Chinese communists.
农民骚乱发生在19世纪，当时太平天国起义军席卷了扬子河流域。叛乱一直持续到20世纪，江西成为中国共产党最早的据点之一。

Language
语言

Most Jiangxi natives speak one of innumerable local variants of Gàn (赣), a dialect whose name is also used as a shorthand for the province. Gàn is similar (some say related) to the Hakka language, spoken in southern Jiangxi.
大多数江西人说赣（赣 ）的无数当地变体之一，赣是一种方言，其名称也被用作该省的简写。甘语与赣西南部使用的客家语相似（有人说有关系）。

8Getting There & Away
8 到达那里和离开

Nanchang is connected by air to most major cities in China. There’s also a small airport at Jingdezhen. Bullet trains link most of the main destinations with other parts of the country, and will soon reach down to Longnan too.
南昌与中国大多数主要城市有空路相连。景德镇还有一个小机场。子弹头列车将大部分主要目的地与该国其他地区连接起来，很快也将到达陇南。

8Getting Around
8 四处走走

As with the rest of China, long-distance buses are on the decline, slowly being usurped by high-speed trains, which are quicker, more frequent and often cheaper than buses. Unlike overnight sleeper trains, tickets for high-speed trains are generally easy to come by – just turn up at the train station and buy a ticket for the next available train; you’ll rarely have to wait more than an hour or so. Within towns and cities, local buses cost ¥1 to ¥2 (carry exact change). Around the villages, you may sometimes have to resort to motorbike taxis if you’re in a hurry, though there’s always a bus for those who are willing to wait.
与中国其他地区一样，长途公交车正在衰落，慢慢被高速列车所取代，高速列车比公交车更快、更频繁，而且通常更便宜。与夜间卧铺列车不同，高速列车的车票通常很容易买到——只需在火车站出现并购买下一趟可用列车的车票即可;您很少需要等待超过一个小时左右。在城镇和城市内，当地巴士费用为 1 至 2 日元（零钱）。在村庄周围，如果您赶时间，有时可能不得不求助于摩托车出租车，但总有一辆公共汽车供那些愿意等待的人使用。

Known reverently in textbooks for fomenting Chinese Communist Party (CCP) rule, Nanchang (Nánchāng) now galvanises support for its attractive, tree-lined streets and easy urban charm. It’s a handy base for Jiangxi’s immediate country to the north and is now on a number of bullet-train lines.

Tengwang PavilionMONUMENT
腾王阁纪念碑

(map Google map; 腾王阁, Téngwáng Gé; Rongmen Lu, 榕门路; ¥50; h7.30am-6.30pm summer, 8am-5pm winter; mWanshou Palace)

This nine-storey pagoda, now part of landscaped riverside grounds, is the city’s drawcard monument. It was first erected during Tang times, but destroyed and rebuilt no fewer than 29 times, most recently in 1989. Each floor (including two basement floors) contains museum exhibitions of varying interest, and some of the floors have viewing balconies overlooking the river.
这座九层高的宝塔现在是风景优美的河滨场地的一部分，是这座城市的吸引人纪念碑。它始建于唐代，但被毁和重建不少于 29 次，最近一次是在 1989 年。每层楼（包括地下两层）都包含不同兴趣的博物馆展览，部分楼层设有俯瞰河流的观景阳台。

(map Google map; 绳金塔, Shéng Jīn Tǎ; Shengjinta Jie, 绳金塔街) F

This rebuilt, 58m-tall Tang-dynasty pagoda can’t be climbed, but is attractive, and surrounded by other recently reconstructed features (an outdoor stage, a small garden, a huge bronze bell) to create a pleasant area for families to hang out in. The pagoda, which is lit up at night, has been rebuilt several times throughout history, most recently in 1985.
这座重建的 58 米高的唐代宝塔无法攀登，但很吸引人，周围环绕着其他新重建的设施（户外舞台、小花园、巨大的铜钟），为家庭创造了一个宜人的闲逛区域。这座宝塔在夜间点亮，历史上曾多次重建，最近一次是在 1985 年。

The Nanchang Uprising is known as the first appearance of the People’s Liberation Army. On 1 August 1927, Zhou Enlai and Zhu De broke ranks with the Nationalist-controlled military and, together with 30,000 communist troops, held the city for four days. The communists were eventually forced to retreat to the nearby mountains where they continued their gruelling campaign, which eventually led to the fabled Long March in 1934.
南昌起义被誉为解放军的首次亮相。1927年8月1日，周恩来和朱德与国民党控制的军队决裂，与3万共产党军队一起占领了这座城市四天。共产党人最终被迫撤退到附近的山区，在那里他们继续进行艰苦的战役，最终导致了传说中的 1934 年长征。

The undisputed porcelain capital of China (and therefore the world), Jingdezhen (Jǐngdézhèn) has been producing the finest-quality ceramics for more than 1700 years. Even those unaccustomed to the ways of the wheel will be stoked by the range of glazed wares on offer at the many small galleries around town, and although little of the city’s architectural heritage remains, it is well worth a day or two getting to know more about the precious ‘white gold’.

Nothing beats staying on top of a mountain so, if you’re able to carry all your luggage, take the bus from Yushan to either the southern or eastern sections, hike to the summit, stay overnight, then hike down to the other section the following day to catch a bus back to Yushan. You may have heard of Fujian’s famous circular tǔlóu (土楼, roundhouses). Well, Jiangxi has its very own, equally marvellous rectangular versions, called wéi lóng wū (围龙屋). In the verdant Hakka countryside in the south of the province, there are estimates of some 370 such dwellings surrounding the town of Longnan (Lóngnán) alone. These unusual structures have housed neighbouring families for generations in earthen, fortified compounds; the villages of Guanxi (关西, Guānxī) and Yangcun (杨村, Yángcūn) are the most accessible.
您可能听说过福建著名的圆形 tǔlóu（ 土楼 ，圆屋）。江西有自己的、同样奇妙的矩形版本 ，称为围龙屋 。在该省南部青翠的客家乡村，仅隆南镇周围就估计有大约 370 座这样的住宅。这些不寻常的建筑世世代代将邻近的家庭安置在土制的坚固建筑中;关西（ 关西 ，Guānxī）和杨村（ 杨村 ，Yángcūn）村是最容易到达的。
