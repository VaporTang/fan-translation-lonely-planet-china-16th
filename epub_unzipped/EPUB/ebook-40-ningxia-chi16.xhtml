Why Go?
为什么要去？

With its raw terrain of dusty plains and stark mountains, sliced in two by the Yellow River, there’s a distinct Grapes of Wrath feel to Ningxia (宁夏). Outside the cities is a timeless landscape where farmers till the hard yellow earth just like their ancestors did.
宁夏拥有尘土飞扬的平原和荒凉的山脉，被黄河一分为二，给宁夏带来了一种独特的愤怒的感觉。城市之外是一片永恒的景观，农民们像他们的祖先一样耕种坚硬的黄土地。

Yet Ningxia was once the front line between the Mongol and Han Chinese empires. Wondrous art and architecture from the Xixia empire and the Jin and Liao dynasties fills museums and historic sites. Then there’s the royal tombs of Xixia, as well as rock carvings that predate the emperors, plus the chance to camp out under the desert sky or float down the Yellow River on a traditional raft. Being the homeland of the Muslim Hui ethnic minority adds another unique layer to Ningxia’s culture.
然而，宁夏曾经是蒙古和汉族帝国之间的前线。西夏帝国、金辽时期的奇妙艺术和建筑充斥着博物馆和历史遗迹。然后是西夏的皇家陵墓，以及早于皇帝的石刻，还有在沙漠天空下露营或乘坐传统木筏顺流而下的机会。作为穆斯林回族少数民族的故乡，宁夏的文化又增添了一层独特的色彩。

Best of all, Ningxia sees few foreign visitors, so it seems like you have the place all to yourself.
最重要的是，宁夏很少有外国游客，所以看起来你已经独享了这个地方。

History
历史

Ningxia had been on the periphery of Chinese empires ever since China’s first imperial dynasty, the Qin (221–206 BC), but it took centre stage in the 10th century AD when the Tangut people declared the establishment of the Xixia (Western Xia) empire in the face of Song opposition. The empire was composed of modern-day Gansu, Ningxia, Shaanxi and western Inner Mongolia, but it soon collapsed in the face of Mongol might.
自中国第一个帝国秦朝（公元前 221-206 年）以来，宁夏一直处于中国帝国的边缘，但在公元 10 世纪，当唐古特人面对宋人的反对宣布建立西夏（西夏）帝国时，宁夏就占据了中心舞台。帝国由现代甘肃、宁夏、陕西和内蒙古西部组成，但很快就在蒙古的威力面前崩溃了。

The Mongol retreat in the 14th century left a void that was filled by both Muslim traders from the west and Chinese farmers from the east. Tensions between the two resulted in Ningxia being caught up in the great Muslim Rebellion that convulsed northwest China in the mid-19th century.
14世纪的蒙古撤退留下了一个空白，由来自西方的穆斯林商人和来自东方的中国农民填补。两者之间的紧张关系导致宁夏卷入了19世纪中叶席卷中国西北部的穆斯林大叛乱。

Once part of Gansu, Ningxia is China’s smallest province, although technically it is an autonomous region for the Muslim Hui ethnic minority, who make up one-third of the population, rather than an official province. It remains one of the poorest areas of China, with a sharp economic divide between the more fertile, Han Chinese–dominated north and the parched, sparsely populated south.
宁夏曾经是甘肃省的一部分，是中国最小的省份，尽管从技术上讲，它是占人口三分之一的穆斯林回族少数民族的自治区，而不是一个官方省份。它仍然是中国最贫穷的地区之一，在更肥沃、汉族占主导地位的北部和干旱、人口稀少的南部之间存在着巨大的经济鸿沟。

Yinchuan 银川
银川 银川

%0951 / Pop 2 million
% 0951 / 流行 200 万

In the sun-parched land of Ningxia, Yinchuan (Yínchuān) has managed to thrive. The Tangut founders wisely chose this spot as their capital, planting the city between a source of water (the Yellow River) and a natural barrier from the Gobi Desert (the Helan mountains).
在宁夏这片阳光枯槁的土地上，银川（Yínchuān）成功地蓬勃发展。唐古特的创始人明智地选择了这个地方作为他们的首都，将城市种植在水源（黄河）和戈壁沙漠（贺兰山脉）的天然屏障之间。

Modern-day Yinchuan is predominantly Han, although its many mosques reveal its status as the capital of the Hui peoples’ homeland. But the most interesting sights, the Western Xia Tombs and Helan Shan to the west of the city, predate both the Han and the Hui. Yinchuan is also a handy jumping-off point for longer trips to western Inner Mongolia.
现代的银川以汉族为主，尽管其众多清真寺表明其作为回族家园的首都的地位。但最有趣的景点是西夏十三陵和城西的贺兰山，其历史早于汉朝和回族。银川也是前往内蒙古西部长途旅行的便捷起点。

The name Yinchuan means ‘Silver River’. Some say it comes from the alkaline land which can appear white, while others say that it’s because the Yellow River is clear in these parts and can appear bright, but the exact origin is obscure.
银川这个名字的意思是“银河”。有人说它来自碱性土地，可以呈现白色，也有人说是因为黄河在这些地方清澈见底，可以显得明亮，但确切的起源却不详。

108 STUPAS
108 佛塔

An unusual arrangement of Tibetan-style Buddhist dagobas (stupas), 108 Stupas (一百零八塔, Yībǎilíngbā Tǎ; ¥100) is 83km south of Yinchuan, not far from the town of Qingtongxia (青铜峡). 108 佛塔 （100 日元） 位于银川以南 83 公里处，距离青铜峡镇不远，是藏式佛教佛塔（100 日元）的不寻常布置。12 排（经过大量翻修的）砖瓶状结构可追溯到元代，排列在黄河岸边的一个大三角形星座中。

THE EAST IS RED (WINE)
东方是红色的（酒）

Wine is hot in China. A bottle of red is a status symbol for a wining and dining Chinese upper middle class: in 2013 China became the world’s largest red-wine consumer, overtaking France and Italy by glugging (or at least purchasing) 155 million cases of the stuff. Consumption per capita in China remains lower than in Europe, but sales of the tipple tripled from 1997 to 2013, and continue to grow.
葡萄酒在中国很热。一瓶红葡萄酒是中国中上层阶级的身份象征：2013 年，中国成为世界上最大的红葡萄酒消费国，超过法国和意大利，购买了 1.55 亿箱红葡萄酒。中国的人均消费量仍然低于欧洲，但从 1997 年到 2013 年，该酒的销量增长了两倍，并且还在继续增长。

In China 80% of the wine consumed is red. The colour red (hóng) is a positive and vibrant colour: strongly associated with wealth, power and good fortune, so red wine is the way to go (especially for men; Chinese women incline more to white).
在中国，消费的葡萄酒中有 80% 是红葡萄酒。红色 （hóng） 是一种积极而充满活力的颜色：与财富、权力和好运密切相关，因此红酒是必经之路（尤其是对男性;中国女性更倾向于白人）。

In 2018 China was the world’s ninth-biggest wine producer, just ahead of Portugal, with Ningxia, Shandong and Xinjiang the most prominent wine-producing regions. The first commercial vines were planted in 1982 by large Chinese wineries like Great Wall and the following years saw a flurry of investment by international names such as LVMH (of Moët fame) and Pernod Ricard.
2018 年，中国是世界第九大葡萄酒生产国，仅次于葡萄牙，宁夏、山东和新疆是最著名的葡萄酒产区。1982 年，长城等大型中国酒庄种植了第一批商业葡萄树，随后几年，LVMH（酩悦成名）和保乐力加等国际品牌进行了大量投资。

By 2019 Ningxia had about 170 wineries and some 90,000 acres of vines. There are plans to develop another 3200 acres between the base of the Helan mountains and the Yellow River into 50 small boutique wineries. By 2022 it is expected that Ningxia’s vineyard area will double and wine production will increase by four times.
截至2019年，宁夏拥有约170家酒庄和约9万亩葡萄园。计划在贺兰山脚下和黄河之间再开发3200亩土地，成为50家小型精品酒庄。到2022年，宁夏葡萄园面积预计将翻一番，葡萄酒产量将增长4倍。

Taste-wise, Ningxia wine, like most other Chinese wines, has moved beyond striving for the technical precision of a ‘proper’ cabernet sauvignon to developing its own character. Though the wines may not yet have the same full flavours of their European cousins, domestically the region is esteemed for its merlot, cabernet sauvignon and ‘Chinese Cabernet’ Gernischt grape varieties. Try Ningxia’s distinctively light, herbaceous wine putaojiu with live jazz or Tibetan music in Yinchuan at Liangyuan Bar, a fiveminute walk north of the Drum Tower.
在口味方面，宁夏葡萄酒与大多数其他中国葡萄酒一样，已经超越了追求“适当”赤霞珠的技术精确性，而是发展了自己的个性。尽管这些葡萄酒可能还没有欧洲表亲那样的浓郁风味，但在国内，该地区因其梅洛、赤霞珠和“中国赤霞珠”格尼施葡萄品种而备受推崇。在音川的两园酒吧（Liangyuan Bar）品尝宁夏独特的清淡草本葡萄酒普陶酒 ，搭配现场爵士乐或藏族音乐，距离鼓楼以北五分钟步行路程。

Like giant beehives scattered on the arid eastern slope of the Helan mountains, the Western Xia Tombs (西夏王陵, Xīxià Wánglíng; ¥68, van ¥20; h8am-5.30pm, to 6pm summer) are Ningxia’s most celebrated sight. The earliest tombs were built a millennium ago by Li Yuanhao, founder of the kingdom. There are nine tombs, plus 250 lesser tombs, in an area of 50 sq km. The tomb site is fashioned like a miniature city with towers, city walls and temple.

The examples of Buddhist art in the good site museum (8am to 5.30pm) offer a rare glimpse into the ephemeral Western Xia (or Xixia) culture, and point to clear artistic influences from neighbouring Tibet and Central Asia. There are also many fascinating artefacts excavated from Li Yuanhao’s tomb.
好遗址博物馆（上午 8 点至下午 5 点 30 分）中的佛教艺术示例提供了对短暂的西夏（或西夏）文化的难得一瞥，并指出了来自邻近西藏和中亚的明显艺术影响。从李元昊墓中出土的文物也有很多引人入胜的文物。

South of Zhongwei, the Han Chinese–dominated cities of northern Ningxia give way to the Hui heartland. Journeying here takes you deep into rural Ningxia, through villages of mud-brick houses where the minarets of the numerous mosques tower over the endless cornfields.
在中卫以南，宁夏北部以汉人为主的城市让位于回族中心地带。在这里旅行将带您深入宁夏的乡村，穿过泥砖房屋的村庄，众多清真寺的尖塔耸立在一望无际的玉米地之上。

Tongxin (同心, Tóngxīn) has a very strong Muslim feel. There are always students in residence at the mosque training to be imams and they will greet you with a salaam alaikum and show you around. Tongxin is also one of the few places in China outside of southern Xinjiang where you’ll see women in veils and covered from head to toe in black.
同心（同心 ，Tóngxīn）有很强的穆斯林感觉。清真寺里总是有学生接受伊玛目培训，他们会用萨拉姆阿莱库姆迎接你，并带你四处参观。同心也是中国南疆以外为数不多的几个地方之一，在那里你可以看到戴着面纱、从头到脚都覆盖着黑色的女人。

Of all the mosques in Ningxia, the most hallowed is the Great Mosque. Dating back to the 14th century (although the present mosque was built in 1573 and then renovated in 1791), it was the only one of Ningxia’s 1000-odd mosques to avoid the ravages of the Cultural Revolution. As such, it’s a near-perfect example of Ming- and Qing-era temple architecture. Not until you get up close and notice the crescents that top the pagoda roofs does it become apparent that it’s a mosque.
在宁夏所有的清真寺中，最神圣的莫过于大清真寺。它的历史可以追溯到14世纪（虽然现在的清真寺建于1573年，然后于1791年进行了翻修），是宁夏1000多座清真寺中唯一一座避免了文化大革命的蹂躏。因此，它是明清时代寺庙建筑近乎完美的典范。直到你走近并注意到宝塔屋顶顶部的新月形，才会发现它是一座清真寺。
