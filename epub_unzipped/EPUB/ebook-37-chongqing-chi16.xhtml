Why Go?
为什么要去？

Chongqing municipality (重庆市, Chóngqìng Shì) may be a relatively recent creation, having been carved out of Sìchuan in 1997, but with its eponymous city driving the economy of western China, it’s now one of the most important regions in the whole country. Understandably, many confuse the larger administrative ‘municipality’ with the city itself – keep in mind that the former is really a province, consisting of dozens of large towns and lots of verdant, hilly countryside.
重庆市（重庆市 ，Chóngqìng Shì）可能是相对较新的创建，于 1997 年从泗川中分离出来，但凭借其同名城市推动了中国西部的经济，它现在是全国最重要的地区之一。可以理解的是，许多人将更大的行政“直辖市”与城市本身混为一谈——请记住，前者实际上是一个省，由数十个大城镇和许多青翠的丘陵乡村组成。

Thanks to the mighty Yangzi River (Cháng Jiāng), which powers its way through here, this region has long been one of strategic military importance. The river was responsible for creating one of China’s greatest natural wonders, the magnificent Three Gorges.
由于浩瀚的扬子江（Cháng Jiāng）流经这里，该地区长期以来一直是具有战略军事重要性的地区之一。这条河创造了中国最伟大的自然奇观之一——壮丽的三峡。

Humans have left their indelible mark as well, with a panoply of ancient Buddhist sculptures, rural riverside villages and, of course, the megalopolis that is Chongqing: one of the five largest cities in all China.
人类也留下了不可磨灭的印记，有一系列古老的佛教雕塑、乡村的河边村庄，当然还有重庆这个大都市：中国五大城市之一。

History
历史

Stone tools unearthed along the Yangzi River valleys show that humans lived in this region two million years ago. The ancient Ba kingdom ruled from here more than 2000 years before subsequent Qin, Sui and Southern Song dynasty rulers took over. From 1938 to 1945, Chongqing City (then romanised as ‘Chungking’) became the Kuomintang’s wartime capital. It was here that representatives of the Chinese Communist Party (CCP), including Zhou Enlai, acted as ‘liaisons’ between the Kuomintang and the communists headquartered in Yan’an, Shaanxi province.
扬子江流域出土的石器表明，两百万年前人类就生活在该地区。古巴王国从这里统治了 2000 多年，随后的秦、隋和南宋统治者接管了这里。1938年至1945年，重庆市（当时罗马化为“重庆”）成为国民党的战时首都。包括周恩来在内的中国共产党代表正是在这里充当了国民党和总部设在陕西省延安的共产党之间的“联络人”。

Refugees from all over China flooded into the city during WWII. More followed when the construction of the Three Gorges Dam displaced over one million people.
二战期间，来自中国各地的难民涌入这座城市。当三峡大坝的建设导致超过 100 万人流离失所时，更多的人接踵而至。

In 1997 Chongqing separated from Sichuan province and became an autonomous municipality, eventually joining Beijing, Shanghai and Tianjin as one of the only municipal areas under direct control of the central government.
1997年，重庆从四川省分离出来，成为自治市，最终与北京、上海、天津并列，成为仅有的中央直辖市辖区之一。

The city was the backdrop for one of modern China’s biggest political scandals in 2012 when Gu Kailai, the wife of Chongqing’s Communist Party boss Bo Xilai, was convicted of murdering a British business associate, Neil Heywood. Allegations of corruption, extortion and espionage surrounded the case, as well as rumours that political rivals in Beijing felt threatened by Bo’s populist policies and wanted him out of the way. Both Bo and his wife were sentenced to life in prison.
2012 年，重庆共产党老板薄熙来的妻子谷开来因谋杀英国商业伙伴尼尔·海伍德（Neil Heywood）而被判有罪，这座城市是现代中国最大的政治丑闻之一的背景。围绕此案的腐败、勒索和间谍指控，以及有传言称北京的政治对手感到受到薄熙来民粹主义政策的威胁，并希望他离开。薄熙来和他的妻子都被判处无期徒刑。

Language
语言

In addition to standard Mandarin Chinese, Chongqing residents also speak Sichuanese. It’s a Mandarin dialect, but pronunciation is different enough that it’s often difficult for those who speak standard Chinese to understand. Two words visitors will often hear are yàodé (pronounced ‘yow-day’, meaning ‘yes’ or ‘OK’) and méidé (pronounced ‘may-day’, meaning ‘no’).
除了标准的普通话外，重庆居民还会说四川话。这是一种普通话方言，但发音差异很大，对于说标准汉语的人来说往往很难理解。游客经常听到的两个词是 yàodé（发音为“yow-day”，意思是“是”或“OK”）和 méidé（发音为“may-day”，意思是“不”）。

The most important city in western China and the economic engine of the upper Yangzi, Chongqing City (Chóngqìng) is a massive and enthralling urban sprawl. Chongqing makes up for a lack of top-notch sights with fantastic food and charismatic geography: its steep hills at the confluence of the Yangzi and Jialing Rivers are a prelude to the even more dramatic scenery of the Three Gorges.
重庆市（崇庆市）是中国西部最重要的城市，也是扬子河上游的经济引擎，是一个庞大而迷人的城市扩张。重庆以美味的美食和迷人的地理环境弥补了顶级景点的不足：其位于扬子江和嘉陵江交汇处的陡峭山丘是三峡壮丽景色的前奏。

Chongqing is sometimes mistakenly referred to as the most populous city in the world. It isn’t. CHONGQING’S STILT HOUSING
重庆的高跷房

Once a striking feature of the Chongqing skyline, stilt houses (吊脚楼, diàojiǎo lóu) were in many ways the predecessor to the modern skyscraper, expanding vertically rather than horizontally to save space. Their design also served to keep family units in close quarters despite the uneven terrain of hilly Chongqing. They were built on a bamboo or fir frame that was fitted into bore holes drilled into the mountain side, and their thin walls were stuffed with straw and coated with mud to allow for cooling ventilation in a city that swelters in summer.
吊脚楼（吊脚楼 ，diàjiǎo lóu）曾经是重庆天际线的显着特征，在许多方面是现代摩天大楼的前身，垂直而不是水平扩展以节省空间。他们的设计还有助于在重庆丘陵地形不平坦的情况下保持家庭单位的距离。它们建在竹子或冷杉框架上，安装在山坡上钻的钻孔中，薄壁上塞满稻草并涂上泥土，以便在夏季闷热的城市中实现凉爽通风。

You’ll no longer see any stilt housing in the city, but some survives in the villages around Chongqing municipality, particularly in Zhongshan.
你不会再在城市里看到任何高跷房，但一些高跷房在重庆市周围的村庄里幸存下来，特别是在中山。

Dazu Buddhist Caves 大足石刻
佛教石刻 大足石刻

The superb rock carvings of Dazu (Dàzú Shíkū) are a Unesco World Heritage Site and one of China’s four great Buddhist cave-sculpture sites, along with those at Dunhuang, Luoyang and Datong. The Dazu sculptures are the most recent of the four, and the artwork here is distinctly more Chinese in style – with ghosts, monsters and Confucian influences – when compared with its Silk Road counterparts.
大足石刻精美，与敦煌、洛阳、大同并列为联合国教科文组织世界遗产，是中国四大佛教石窟雕遗址之一。大足雕塑是四件雕塑中最新的一件，与丝绸之路的雕塑相比，这里的艺术品明显更具中国风格——有鬼魂、怪物和儒家的影响。

Scattered over roughly 40 sites are thousands of cliff carvings and statues, dating from the Tang dynasty (9th century) to the Song dynasty (13th century). The main groupings are at Treasured Summit Hill and North Hill.
散布在大约 40 个遗址中，有数千件悬崖雕刻和雕像，其历史可追溯到唐代（9 世纪）至宋代（13 世纪）。主要分组位于 Treasured Summit Hill 和 North Hill。

FISHING TOWN FORTRESS
渔镇要塞

Famed throughout China for being one of the great ancient battlegrounds, the 700-year-old Fishing Town Fortress (钓鱼城, Diàoyú Chéng; ¥60; h8.30am-5pm) is perched on top of a 300m-tall hill at a bend in the Jialing River. The Yangzi Basin was the last stand of the Southern Song dynasty and famously, in the 13th century, the fortress withstood the mighty Mongol armies for an incredible 36 years, during which time an estimated 200 battles were fought here.
拥有 700 年历史的渔镇堡垒（ 钓鱼城 ，Diàoyú Chéng;60 日元; h 上午 8.30 至下午 5 点） 坐落在嘉陵江弯道上一座 300 米高的山顶上。扬子盆地是南宋王朝的最后一站，著名的是，在 13 世纪，这座堡垒抵御了强大的蒙古军队长达 36 年之久，在此期间估计在这里进行了 200 场战斗。

The fortress was protected by an 8km-long, 30m-tall double wall, punctuated with eight gate towers. Much of the outer wall and all the main gates remain today; some partly restored, others crumbling away. There is little here in terms of facilities apart from a noodle vendor or two, but it’s a fascinating and peaceful place to walk around. Narrow stone pathways lead you through the forest, past Buddhist rock carvings, temples, engraved poems, bamboo groves, the wall and its gateways and some fabulous lookout points. Sights not to miss include the serene, 11m-long, 1000-year-old Sleeping Buddha (卧佛, Wòfó), cut into the overhang of a cliff; Huguo Temple (护国寺, Hùguó Sì), dating from the Tang dynasty, although largely rebuilt; and the Imperial Cave (黄洞, Huángdòng), an ancient drainage passage with steps leading down to it, clinging to the outside of the fort wall.
堡垒由长 8 公里、高 30 米的双层城墙保护，城墙上点缀着八座门楼。大部分外墙和所有正门至今仍保留下来;有些部分恢复，有些则摇摇欲坠。除了一两个面条摊贩外，这里几乎没有什么设施，但这是一个迷人而宁静的地方，可以四处走走。狭窄的石板路带您穿过森林，经过佛教石刻、寺庙、雕刻诗歌、竹林、城墙及其门户以及一些神话般的观景台。不容错过的景点包括宁静的卧佛 ，长 11 米，拥有 1000 年历史 ，切入悬崖的悬垂处; 护国寺 （ 护国寺 ，Hùguó Sì），其历史可追溯至唐代，但大部分已重建;皇家洞穴（ 黄洞 ，Huángdòng），一条古老的排水通道，有台阶通向它，紧贴堡墙的外侧。

Alternatively, you can take the much longer bus ride from Caiyuanba station (¥29, 1½ to two hours, half-hourly, 8.10am to 6.10pm).
