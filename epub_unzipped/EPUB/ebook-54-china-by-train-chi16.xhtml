China by Train
乘火车前往中国

Trains are the best way to travel long distances around China in reasonable speed and comfort. They are also adventurous, exciting, fun, practical and efficient, and ticket prices are reasonable to boot. Colossal investment over recent years has put high-speed rail at the heart of China’s rapid modernisation drive. You really don’t have to be a trainspotter to find China’s railways a riveting subculture – as a plus you’ll get to meet the Chinese people at their most relaxed and sociable.
火车是以合理的速度和舒适度在中国长途旅行的最佳方式。它们也具有冒险精神、刺激性、乐趣、实用性和高效性，而且票价合理。近年来的巨额投资使高铁成为中国快速现代化发展的核心。你真的不必成为火车观察员，就能发现中国的铁路是一种引人入胜的亚文化——作为一个加分项，你会遇到中国人最轻松、最善于交际的一面。

At the Station
在车站

As you arrive at the train station to board your train (and sometimes before you can access the main ticket hall), you will have your ticket checked against your passport. Then you will need to put your bag on a conveyor belt through a scanner that checks for prohibited items, including knives (your pocket knife may be confiscated, so leave your best Swiss Army knife at home) and aerosols (anything over 120mL). It is a better idea to buy necessities (eg shoe freshener) in China that are manufactured in low-enough-volume cans to be allowed onto trains. If an item is prohibited and you are prevented from taking it aboard the train, you can arrange for it to be sent to wherever you are going, by express delivery. That is the only option, other than throwing it away. You will also need to walk through a metal detector and security personnel will quickly check you with a wand.
当您到达火车站登上火车时（有时在您进入主售票大厅之前），您将根据护照检查您的车票。然后，您需要通过扫描仪将包放在传送带上，该扫描仪会检查是否有违禁物品，包括刀具（您的小刀可能会被没收，因此请将最好的瑞士军刀留在家里）和气雾剂（任何超过 120 毫升的东西）。在中国购买必需品（例如鞋类清新剂）是个好主意，这些必需品是用足够小的罐头制造的，以便允许上火车。如果某件物品被禁止携带，并且您无法将其带上火车，您可以安排通过快递将其送到您要去的任何地方。这是唯一的选择，除了扔掉它。您还需要通过金属探测器，安保人员会用魔杖快速检查您。

In old-style stations (such as Beijing West Train Station) you will have to find the correct waiting-room number, displayed on an illuminated screen as you walk in. Some more modern stations (such as Shanghai Hongqiao Train Station) are more straightforward and intelligently designed, without waiting rooms. Instead your platform number will appear on the screen.
在老式车站（如北京西站），您必须找到正确的候车室号码，当您走进去时，候车室号码会显示在发光的屏幕上。一些更现代化的车站（如上海虹桥火车站）设计更加简单和智能，没有候车室。相反，您的平台编号将出现在屏幕上。

Aboard the Train
在火车上

Trolleys of food and drink are wheeled along carriages during the trip, but prices are high and the selection is limited. You can also load up on mineral water and snacks at stations, where hawkers sell items from platform stalls. Long-distance, older-type trains should have a canteen carriage (餐厅车厢, cāntīng chēxiāng); they are sometimes open through the night.
旅途中，装有食品和饮料的手推车沿着车厢行驶，但价格高昂，选择有限。您还可以在车站购买矿泉水和小吃，小贩们从站台摊位出售物品。长途、旧型火车应配备食堂车厢（餐厅车厢 ，cāntīng chēxiāng）; 它们有时通宵营业。

If taking a sleeper train you will generally be required to exchange your paper ticket for a plastic or metal card with your bunk number on it. The conductor then knows when you are due to disembark, and will wake you in time to return your ticket to you.
如果乘坐卧铺列车，您通常需要将纸质车票换成印有您的铺位号码的塑料或金属卡。然后，售票员会知道您何时下车，并会及时叫醒您，将车票归还给您。

CHINA’S TRAIN NETWORK
中国的火车网络

One of the world’s most extensive rail networks penetrates every province in China and high-speed connections are suddenly everywhere. In line with China’s frantic economic development and the pressures of transporting 1.4 billion people across the world’s third-largest nation, expansion of China’s high-speed rail network over the past decade has been simply mind-boggling.
世界上最广泛的铁路网络之一渗透到中国的每个省份，高速连接突然无处不在。随着中国经济的疯狂发展和运送14亿人口穿越世界第三大国的压力，过去十年中国高铁网络的扩张简直令人难以置信。

The network currently totals more than 127,000km in length, 29,000km of which is high-speed rail track. Thousands of kilometres of track are laid every year, with high-speed trains zipping across the land since 2007, shrinking China’s once-daunting distances. State-of-the-art train stations have continuously appeared as if from nowhere, primarily to serve high-speed links. Most of these new stations are located in city outskirts and are given a name corresponding to their position in relation to the city in question eg Xi’an North, Zhangye West or Guangzhou South. The highly anticipated Xi’an–Chengdu line has cut travel times from 13 hours to around four hours, and similar stories have been repeated across China. The only cities and regions to date not penetrated by high-speed rail are Tibet, Ningxia and Macau.
目前，该网络总长度超过127,000公里，其中29,000公里是高铁轨道。每年铺设数千公里的轨道，自 2007 年以来，高速列车在这片土地上飞驰而过，缩短了中国曾经令人生畏的距离。最先进的火车站不断出现，仿佛不知从何而来，主要是为了服务于高速线路。这些新车站大多位于城市郊区，并被赋予了与其相对于相关城市的位置相对应的名称，例如习安北、张掖西或广州南。备受期待的习安-成都线已将旅行时间从 13 小时缩短到约 4 小时，类似的故事在中国各地重演。迄今为止，高铁尚未穿透的城市和地区只有西藏、宁夏和澳门。

High-speed D, G and C class trains have also put the squeeze on numerous domestic air routes and the punctuality of trains sees far fewer delays than air travel. Some bus routes have also disappeared, or cut the frequency of services, due to the competition from high-speed rail. There is even talk of extending links through Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, Iran and Turkey to Bulgaria. Down south, China is also planning a high-speed link from Kunming in Yunnan to Singapore, via Laos, Thailand and Malaysia.
高速 D、G 和 C 级列车也给众多国内航线带来了压力，列车的准点性比航空旅行的延误要少得多。一些公交路线也消失了，或者削减了班次，因为来自高铁的竞争。甚至有传言说要通过吉尔吉斯斯坦、塔吉克斯坦、乌兹别克斯坦、土库曼斯坦、伊朗和土耳其与保加利亚建立联系。在南部，中国还计划修建一条从云南昆明经老挝、泰国和马来西亚到新加坡的高速线路。

TRAIN TRAVEL
火车旅行

Trains are generally highly punctual in China and are usually a safe (and convivial) way to travel. Train stations are often conveniently close to the centre of town, though high-speed train stations may be a considerable distance from the town or city centre.
在中国，火车通常非常准时，通常是一种安全（且欢乐）的旅行方式。火车站通常靠近市中心，交通便利，但高速火车站可能距离城镇或市中心相当远。

Travelling on sleeper berths at night means you can frequently arrive at your destination first thing in the morning, saving a night’s hotel accommodation. Think ahead, get your tickets early and you can sleep your way around a lot of China.
夜间卧铺旅行意味着您经常可以在早上第一件事就是到达目的地，从而节省一晚的酒店住宿。提前考虑，早点买票，你可以在中国的很多地方睡一觉。

Some tips regarding train travel:
关于火车旅行的一些提示：

ADon’t wait to board your train until the last minute, as queues outside the main train station entrance can be shocking.
A 不要等到最后一刻才登上火车，因为火车总站入口外的排队可能会令人震惊。

AKeep passports handy when entering a station as the name on your ticket will be checked against the name on your passport.
进入车站时请 A 随身携带护照，因为车票上的姓名将与护照上的姓名进行核对。

AOn a nonsleeper, ask a member of staff or a fellow passenger to tell you when your station arrives.
A 在非卧铺车上，请工作人员或其他乘客告诉您车站何时到达。

AKeep your ticket after disembarking the train as there can be checks when exiting the platform and ticket-fed turnstiles in some newer stations.
下车后请 A 保留车票，因为在离开站台时可能会有检查，在一些较新的车站有售票闸机。

TRAIN TYPES
火车类型

Chinese train numbers are usually (but not always) prefixed by a letter, designating the category of train.
中国的火车号码通常（但并非总是）以字母为前缀，指定火车的类别。

High-Speed Trains
高速列车

The fastest, most luxurious and expensive (and now most common) intercity trains are the streamlined, high-speed G, D and C trains, which rapidly shuttle between major cities. D class trains were the first high-speed trains to appear and glide around China, offering substantial comfort and regular services. Their temperature-regulated 1st-class carriages have mobile and laptop chargers, and seats are two abreast with ample legroom and TV sets. Second-class carriages have five seats in two rows. G class trains are even faster, and have now largely superseded the D class trains on major routes. Smoking is not permitted on high-speed trains. C class trains are mostly used for rapid commuter-type hops between neighbouring cities.
最快、最豪华、最昂贵（也是现在最常见）的城际列车是流线型高速 G、D 和 C 列车，它们在主要城市之间快速穿梭。D 级列车是第一批在中国各地出现和滑行的高速列车，提供大量的舒适性和定期的服务。他们的温度调节一等舱车厢配有手机和笔记本电脑充电器，座位并排，腿部空间充足，配有电视机。二等车厢有两排五个座位。G 级列车速度更快，现在已在主要路线上在很大程度上取代了 D 级列车。高速列车上禁止吸烟。C 级列车主要用于邻近城市之间的快速通勤型跳跃。

Regular Trains
普通列车

The old-style express classes, dwindling in number, include the overnight Z class trains, while further down the pecking order are older and more basic T and K class trains. Toilets will generally be the squat variety on these. While high-speed trains are truly nonsmoking, the older trains allow smoking between carriages.
数量不断减少的老式特快列车包括夜间 Z 级列车，而排名靠后的是更旧、更基本的 T 级和 K 级列车。马桶通常是这些的深蹲品种。虽然高速列车确实禁止吸烟，但较旧的列车允许在车厢之间吸烟。

TICKETS
票

It is possible to upgrade (补票, bǔpiào) your ticket once aboard your train. If you have a standing ticket, for example, find the conductor and upgrade to a hard seat, soft seat or hard sleeper (if there are any available).
登上火车后，可以升级（ 补票 ，bǔpiào）。例如，如果您有站票，请找到售票员并升级为硬座、软座或硬卧铺（如果有）。

Soft Sleeper
软卧

Soft sleepers are a very comfortable way to travel, and work perfectly as mobile hotels. Tickets cost much more than hard-sleeper tickets but often sell out, so book early. Soft sleepers vary between trains and the best are on the D and Z class trains. All Z class trains are soft-sleeper trains, with very comfortable, up-to-date berths. A few T class trains also offer two-berth compartments, with their own toilet.
软卧是一种非常舒适的旅行方式，非常适合作为移动酒店。门票价格比硬卧票贵得多，但经常售罄，因此请尽早预订。软卧铺因列车而异，最好的是 D 级和 Z 级列车。所有 Z 级列车都是软卧列车，拥有非常舒适、最新的铺位。一些 T 级列车还提供两铺位车厢，并配有自己的卫生间。

Tickets for upper berths are slightly cheaper than for lower berths. Expect to share with total strangers. If you are asleep, an attendant will wake you to prepare you to disembark, so you will have plenty of time to ready your things. Available on some lines, two-bed deluxe soft sleepers usually have a toilet and sink. VIP sleepers – essentially three-bed compartments that one person can book in their entirety – are available on the Kunming–Lijiang route.
上铺的门票比下铺的票略便宜。期待与完全陌生的人分享。如果您睡着了，服务员会叫醒您，准备下船，这样您就有充足的时间准备东西。在某些线路上，两床豪华软卧房通常配有卫生间和水槽。贵宾卧铺——本质上是三人床的车厢，一个人可以预订全部——在昆明-丽江航线上可用。

Soft-sleeper carriages contain the following:
软卧车厢包含以下内容：

Afour air-conditioned bunks (upper and lower) in a closed compartment
封闭车厢内 A 的四个空调双层床（上层和下层）

Abedding on each berth and a lockable door to the carriage corridor
每个铺位上都有 A 床上用品，车厢走廊有一扇可上锁的门

Ameals, flat-screen TVs on some routes
A 餐食、部分航线上的纯平电视

Apower/USB sockets
A 电源/USB 插座

Aa small table and stowing space for your bags
A 一张小桌子和行李储物空间

Aa hot-water flask for drinking (plain or for tea) or making instant noodles, filled by an attendant (one per compartment)
A 一个用于饮用（原味或茶）或制作方便面的热水瓶，由服务员装满（每个隔间一个）

TRAVELLING THE TRANS-SIBERIAN RAILWAY
乘坐西伯利亚大铁路

Rolling out of Europe and into Asia, through eight time zones and over 9289km of taiga, steppe and desert, the Trans-Siberian Railway and its connecting routes constitute one of the most famous and most romantic of the world’s great train journeys.
西伯利亚大铁路及其连接路线从欧洲出发，进入亚洲，穿越八个时区和超过 9289 公里的针叶林、草原和沙漠，构成了世界上最著名、最浪漫的火车之旅之一。

There are, in fact, three railways. The ‘true’ Trans-Siberian line runs from Moscow to Vladivostok, but the routes traditionally referred to as the Trans-Siberian Railway are the two branches that veer off the main line in eastern Siberia for Beijing.
事实上，有三条铁路。“真正的” 西伯利亚大铁路线从莫斯科到符拉迪沃斯托克，但传统上被称为西伯利亚大铁路的路线是从东西伯利亚东部主线转向北京的两条支线。

Since the first option excludes China, most readers here will be choosing between the Trans-Mongolian and the Trans-Manchurian railway lines. The Trans-Mongolian route (Beijing to Moscow; 7865km) is faster, but requires an additional visa and another border crossing – on the plus side, you also get to see some of the Mongolian countryside. The Trans-Manchurian route (Beijing to Moscow; 9025km) is longer.
由于第一个选项不包括中国，因此这里的大多数读者将在跨蒙古铁路线和跨满洲铁路线之间做出选择。跨蒙古路线（北京至莫斯科，7865 公里）速度更快，但需要额外的签证和另一次过境点——从好的方面来说，您还可以看到一些蒙古乡村。横贯满洲的路线（北京至莫斯科，9025 公里）更长。

Trans-Mongolian Railway
横贯蒙古铁路

Trains offer deluxe two-berth compartments (with shared shower), 1st-class four-berth compartments and 2nd-class four-berth compartments. Ticket Offices & Buying Online
售票处和在线购买

Ticket offices (售票厅, shòupiàotīng) at train stations are usually to one side of the main station entrance. Automated ticket machines operate on some routes, but never accept foreign passports as ID. At large stations there should be a window staffed by someone with basic English skills. Queues are generally quite long, but are much shorter these days as so many Chinese travellers either buy their tickets online or use the automated ticket machines.
火车站的售票厅（售票厅 ，shòupiàotīng）通常位于主站入口的一侧。自动售票机在某些路线上运行，但从不接受外国护照作为身份证件。在大型车站，应该有一个窗口，由具有基本英语技能的人组成。排队的人通常很长，但现在排队的时间要短得多，因为许多中国游客要么在网上购买机票，要么使用自动售票机。

Alternatively, independent train ticket offices usually exist elsewhere in town, where tickets can be purchased for a ¥5 commission without the same kind of queues; we list these where possible. The number of these ticket offices is dwindling, however, as more and more Chinese buy tickets online. Larger post offices may also sell train tickets. Your hotel will also be able to rustle up a ticket for you for a commission, and so can a travel agent.
或者，独立的火车售票处通常存在于镇上的其他地方，在那里可以支付 5 日元的佣金购买车票，而无需排队;我们会尽可能列出这些。然而，随着越来越多的中国人在网上购票，这些售票处的数量正在减少。较大的邮局也可能出售火车票。您的酒店也可以为您准备一张机票以收取佣金，旅行社也可以。
