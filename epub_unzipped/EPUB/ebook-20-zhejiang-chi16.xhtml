Why Go?
为什么要去？

It’s Hangzhou, the handsome capital city, that lands Zhejiang (浙江, Zhèjiāng) on many a traveller’s itinerary. Home to picture-perfect landscapes of classical Chinese beauty (and just a short train ride from Shanghai), Hangzhou is the obvious highlight. Yet the province offers so much more. Also within easy striking distance are water towns with spiderweb networks of canals and restored Ming and Qing dynasty merchants’ homes (Wuzhen and Nanxun). Among the thousands of islands dotting a ragged and fragmented shoreline is the island of Putuoshan, one of China’s four most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites. More intrepid travellers can head west, where ancient villages retain their traditional architecture and bucolic charms. Meanwhile travellers looking for the opposite of intrepid can hole up in one of the stylish resorts nestled among the hillside bamboo groves and tea fields of naturally cool Moganshan.
杭州，这座美丽的首都，让浙江（ 浙江 ，Zhèjiāng）成为许多旅行者的行程中的一员。杭州拥有风景如画的中国古典美景（距离上海只有很短的火车车程），是明显的亮点。然而，该省提供的远不止于此。同样近在咫尺的还有水乡，那里有蜘蛛网般的运河网络和修复的明清商人住宅（乌镇和南浔）。在成千上万的岛屿中，点缀在参差不齐、支离破碎的海岸线上，是中国四大佛教朝圣地之一的普陀山岛。更勇敢的旅行者可以向西行驶，那里的古老村庄保留了传统建筑和田园风情。与此同时，寻找与勇敢相反的旅行者可以躲进坐落在自然凉爽的莫干山山坡竹林和茶田之间的时尚度假村之一。

History
历史

By the 7th and 8th centuries, Hangzhou, Ningbo and Shaoxing had emerged as three of China’s most important trading centres and ports. Fertile Zhejiang was part of the great southern granary from which food was shipped to the depleted areas of the north via the Grand Canal (Dà Yùnhé), which commences here. Growth accelerated when the Song dynasty moved court to Hangzhou in the 12th century after invasion from the north. Due to intense cultivation, northern Zhejiang has lost a lot of natural vegetation and much of it is now flat, featureless plain.
到了 7 世纪和 8 世纪，杭州、宁波和绍兴已成为中国三个最重要的贸易中心和港口。肥沃的浙江是南方大粮仓的一部分，粮食从这里开始通过大运河（Dà Yùnhé）运往北方的枯竭地区。12 世纪，宋朝在北方入侵后迁至杭州，发展加速。由于密集的耕作，浙北地区失去了大量的自然植被，现在大部分平坦、毫无特色的平原。

One of China’s most enduringly popular holiday spots, Hangzhou’s (Hángzhōu) dreamy West Lake panoramas and fabulously green hills can easily tempt you into long sojourns. Eulogised by poets and applauded by emperors, the lake has intoxicated the Chinese imagination for aeons. Kept spotlessly clean by armies of street sweepers and litter collectors, its scenic vistas draw you into a classical Chinese watercolour of willow-lined banks, mist-covered hills and the occasional shíkùmén (stone-gate house) and old lǐlòng (residential lane).
杭州（Hángzhōu）是中国最受欢迎的度假胜地之一，梦幻般的西湖全景和美妙的绿色山丘很容易诱惑您进行长时间的逗留。这个湖被诗人歌颂，被皇帝喝彩，亿万年来陶醉了中国人的想象力。由街道清扫者和垃圾收集者大军保持一尘不染，其风景优美的景色将您带入一幅中国古典水彩画中，包括柳树成荫的河岸、薄雾缭绕的山丘以及偶尔出现的石门屋和古老的 lǐlòng（住宅小巷）。

Wonderful as it is, Hangzhou’s charms are by no means limited to West Lake scenery – delve further into the city to climb ancient pagodas and discover blissfully quiet temples. Away from the tourist drawcards exists a charismatic and buzzing city in its own right, with wide pedestrian walkways, an unpretentious and exciting food scene, upbeat nightlife and increasingly cosmopolitan population.
杭州的魅力绝不仅限于西湖风光——深入城市，攀登古塔，发现幸福安静的寺庙。远离旅游景点之外，还有一座魅力十足、热闹非凡的城市，拥有宽阔的人行道、朴实无华的美食场景、欢快的夜生活和日益国际化的人口。

History
历史

Hangzhou’s history dates from the start of the Qin dynasty (221 BC). Marco Polo passed through in the 13th century, calling Hangzhou Kinsai and noting in astonishment that Hangzhou had a circumference of 100 miles (161km), its waters vaulted by 12,000 bridges.
杭州的历史可以追溯到秦朝初期（公元前 221 年）。马可·波罗 （Marco Polo） 在 13 世纪经过，称杭州为金赛，并惊讶地注意到杭州的周长为 100 英里（161 公里），其水域由 12,000 座桥梁拱起。

Hangzhou flourished after being linked with the Grand Canal in AD 610 but fully prospered after the Song dynasty was overthrown by the invading Jurchen, who captured the Song capital Kaifeng, along with the emperor and the leaders of the imperial court, in 1126. The remnants of the Song court fled south, finally settling in Hangzhou and establishing it as the capital of the Southern Song dynasty. Hangzhou’s wooden buildings made fire a perennial hazard; among major conflagrations, the great fire of 1237 reduced some 30,000 residences to smoking piles.
杭州在公元 610 年与大运河相连后繁荣昌盛，但在宋朝被入侵的女真人推翻后全面繁荣，女真人于 1126 年与皇帝和朝廷领导人一起占领了宋都开封。宋廷残余势力南逃，最终定居在杭州，并将其定为南宋的首都。杭州的木制建筑使火灾成为常年危害;在重大大火中，1237 年的大火将大约 30,000 栋住宅烧成烟堆。

When the Mongols swept into China they established their court in Beijing, but Hangzhou retained its status as a prosperous commercial city. With 10 city gates by Ming times, Hangzhou took a hammering from Taiping rebels, who besieged the city in 1861 and captured it; two years later the imperial armies reclaimed it. These campaigns reduced almost the entire city to ashes, led to the deaths of over half a million of its residents through disease, starvation and warfare, and finally ended Hangzhou’s significance as a commercial and trading centre.
当蒙古人席卷中国时，他们在北京建立了自己的宫廷，但杭州保留了其作为繁荣商业城市的地位。到明代，杭州拥有 10 个城门，从太平天国起义军手中夺取了锤子，太平天国起义军于 1861 年围攻该城并占领了该城;两年后，帝国军队收复了它。这些战役几乎将整个城市化为灰烬，导致超过 50 万居民因疾病、饥饿和战争而死亡，最终结束了杭州作为商业和贸易中心的重要性。

Few monuments survived the devastation; much of what can be seen in Hangzhou today is of fairly recent construction.
很少有纪念碑在破坏中幸存下来;今天在杭州可以看到的大部分都是最近建造的。

The very definition of classical beauty in China, West Lake is utterly mesmerising: pagoda-topped hills rise over willow-lined waters as boats drift slowly through a idyll of leisurely charm. Walkways, perfectly positioned benches, parks and gardens around the banks of the lake offer a thousand and one vantage points for visitors to admire the faultless scenery.
西湖是中国古典美的定义，非常令人着迷：宝塔顶的山丘耸立在柳树成荫的水面上，船只缓缓漂流，悠闲迷人的田园诗。湖岸周围的人行道、位置优越的长椅、公园和花园为游客提供了一千零一个有利位置，可以欣赏到完美无瑕的风景。

Originally a lagoon adjoining the Qiantang River, the lake didn’t come into existence until the 8th century, when the governor of Hangzhou had the marshy expanse dredged. As time passed, the lake’s splendour was gradually cultivated: gardens were planted, pagodas built, and causeways and islands were constructed from dredged silt.
该湖最初是毗邻钱塘江的泻湖，直到 8 世纪杭州总督疏浚了这片沼泽地时才出现。随着时间的推移，湖泊的壮丽逐渐被培育出来：种植花园，建造宝塔，用疏浚的淤泥建造堤道和岛屿。

Celebrated poet Su Dongpo himself had a hand in the lake’s development, constructing the Su Causeway (苏堤, Sūdī) during his tenure as local governor in the 11th century. It wasn’t an original idea – the poet-governor Bai Juyi had already constructed the Bai Causeway (白堤, Báidī) some 200 years earlier. Lined by willow, plum and peach trees, today the traffic-free causeways with their half-moon bridges make for restful outings.
著名诗人苏东坡本人也参与了湖泊的开发，在 11 世纪担任地方总督期间修建了苏堤（ 苏堤 ，苏地）。这不是一个原创的想法——诗人兼总督白居易在大约 200 年前就已经建造了白堤（白堤 ，Báidī）。柳树、李子树和桃树两旁种满了柳树、李子树和桃树，如今，无交通的堤道和半月形桥是宁静的郊游场所。

An often spectacular natural phenomenon occurs every month on Hangzhou’s Qiantang River (钱塘江, Qiántáng Jiāng), when the highest tides of the lunar cycle dispatch a wall of water – sometimes almost 9m tall – thundering along the narrow mouth of the river from Hangzhou Bay, at up to 40km/h. Occasionally sweeping astonished sightseers away and luring bands of intrepid surfers, this awesome tidal bore (钱塘江潮, qiántáng jiāngcháo) is the world’s largest and can be viewed from the riverbank in Hangzhou, but one of the best places to witness the action is on the northern side of the river at Yanguan (盐官, Yánguān), a delightful ancient town about 38km northeast of Hangzhou.

The most popular viewing time is during the International Qiantang River Tide Observing Festival (admission to viewing area ¥30; hSep/Oct), on the 18th day of the eighth month of the lunar calendar (the same day as the mid-autumn festival), which usually falls in September or October. You can, however, see it throughout the year when the highest tide occurs at the beginning and middle of each lunar month; access to the park in Yanguan for viewing the tide is ¥25. The Hangzhou Tourist Information Centre can give you upcoming tide times. To make it a day trip, a through ticket (¥100) is available in Yanguan to explore the charming historic temples and buildings of the town.

Like other towns on the water, Nanxun (南浔, Nánxún; ¥100; h7.30am-5pm Apr-Oct, 8am-4.30pm Nov-Mar) has arched stone bridges, meandering lanes and old wooden houses. What sets it apart is its fascinating mix of Chinese and European architecture – and comparatively few visitors. Established during the Southern Song dynasty, Nanxun rose to prominence in the Ming and Qing dynasties, when it became a key trading point along the Grand Canal from Beijing to Hangzhou. Refreshingly cool in summer and sometimes smothered in spectral fog, Moganshan (莫干山, Mògànshān; %0572 841 2345; Deqing; admission ¥80; h8.30am-4.30pm), 60km northwest of Hangzhou, is famed for its scenic vistas, forested views, towering bamboo and stone villa architecture. The tiny village of Siping (寺平村, Sìpíng Cūn; ¥20; h8.30am-5pm), 30km west of Jinhua, was founded 700 years ago, and remains a remarkable repository of brick and wood carvings. Its seven original halls, built by generations of the Dai (戴) family, are arranged in the shape of the Big Dipper – to communicate harmony between the human and natural world. The village is blissfully uncommercial, though thanks to a homestay program it’s well set up for visitors.
寺平村 （Sìpíng Cūn;20 日元; h 上午 8 点 30 分至下午 5 点） 位于金华以西 30 公里处，始建于 700 年前，至今仍是非凡的砖木雕刻宝库。它的七个原始大厅由戴氏家族几代人建造，以北斗七星的形状排列——以传达人类与自然世界之间的和谐。这个村庄非常不商业化，尽管由于寄宿家庭计划，它为游客做好了准备。

The main entrance to Siping is along the handle of the dipper. On your right will be Liben Hall (立本堂, Lìběn Táng), an early Qing dynasty structure with some wood carvings that remain impressively vivid; keep an eye out for carvings of the bats. In the cosmology of the village, this hall represents the star Phad.
四平的主要入口位于北斗的把手上。右边是立本堂 ， 这是一座清代早期的建筑，有一些木雕仍然令人印象深刻; 留意蝙蝠的雕刻。在村庄的宇宙观中，这个大厅代表着明星法德。

Just a few paces from Chonghou Hall (崇德堂, Chóngdé Táng) is Wǔjiān Huāxuān (五间花轩), marked by flowery brick carvings over the door. This is the birthplace of Dai Yinniang, Siping’s most famous historical resident – the village girl who became an imperial concubine. According to legend, when Yinniang was ill as a child, a monk in a vision told her father to construct a well near the house. He did, and after giving his daughter water from it to drink and wash, she became well – and more beautiful. The well still exists, adjacent to the house.
距离崇德堂 （崇德堂 ，Chóngdé Táng）仅几步之遥的是五间花轩 ， 门上有花砖雕刻。这里是戴隐娘的出生地，戴隐娘是四平最著名的历史居民——成为皇贵妃的村姑。相传，隐娘小时候生病时，异象中的一位和尚告诉父亲在房子附近打一口井。他照做了，在给女儿喝水和洗漱后，她变得好了——而且更漂亮了。这口井仍然存在，毗邻房子。

At the base of the dipper, and standing in for the star Alioth, is Chongde Hall. Its brick carving, ‘Nine Lions Scrambling for a Ball’, is noteworthy as much for its detail as for its seemingly impudent use of five-toed lions (usually an imperial symbol).
北斗星的底部，代表阿利奥斯星的是崇德厅。它的砖雕“九只狮子争先恐后地争夺一个球”，值得注意的是它的细节和它似乎无礼地使用了五趾狮子（通常是帝国的象征）。

The village is well signposted in English. While the halls are the most dramatic structures, many ordinary houses have fantastic carvings as well, depicting popular Chinese symbols of luck, upward mobility and prosperity.
村庄有很好的英语路标。虽然大厅是最引人注目的建筑，但许多普通房屋也有奇妙的雕刻，描绘了中国流行的幸运、向上流动和繁荣的象征。

As most people day trip here from Jinhua, the only accommodation in Siping is the 15 homes open to guests through the Jinhua Homestay (www.jinhuahomestay.com; s/d incl 2 meals per person ¥128) program (the small number keeps the village uncrowded). The program began in 2015, with six villages and counting. While Siping has plenty of heritage structures, the homes are modern (with modern amenities); bathrooms are shared. Host families are keen to have guests at their table and to take them around the village. Little English is spoken in the village, but you can make arrangements through the program in English. Contact by email; the website is mainly about the homestay ‘scholarships’.
由于大多数人从金华来这里一日游，四平唯一的住宿是通过金华民宿 （www.jinhuahomestay.com;s/d 含每人 2 餐 128 日元） 计划向客人开放的 15 个家庭（数量少，村庄不拥挤）。该计划于 2015 年开始，有六个村庄，并且还在不断增加。虽然四平有很多遗产建筑，但房屋是现代的（配备现代化的设施）;浴室为共用设施。寄宿家庭热衷于让客人坐在他们的餐桌旁，并带他们在村庄里转一圈。村里很少说英语，但您可以通过英语程序进行安排。通过电子邮件联系;该网站主要是关于寄宿家庭的“奖学金”。

Cut with sparkling streams, decorated with placid ponds and embraced by silent hills, the picturesque village of Xinye (新叶, Xīnyè; admission incl all sights ¥78; h8am-4pm) is populated by families sharing the surname Ye (叶) and an abundance of free-roaming chickens. THE BRIDGES OF TAISHUN COUNTY
台顺县的桥梁

Scenic, damp Taishun (泰顺, Tàishùn), in southeast Zhejiang, is China’s living bridge museum: there are hundreds of covered wooden bridges – many centuries old, in varying stages of preservation and decay – scattered around the countryside. The best of those that are easily accessible are the four clustered around the township of Sixi (泗溪, Sìxī), which sits on the confluence of two streams.
风景秀丽、潮湿的泰顺（泰顺 ，Tàishùn）位于浙江东南部，是中国活桥博物馆：有数百座有盖木桥，已有数百年的历史，处于不同的保存和衰败阶段，散布在乡村各处。其中最好的交通便利是四条河，聚集在泗溪（ 泗溪 ，Sìxī）镇周围，它位于两条溪流的交汇处。

From the main arrival bus stop in Nanxi (南溪, Nánxī), a village within Sixi township, it’s a short 400m walk back through the village to Nanxi Bridge (南溪桥, Nánxī Qiáo). What this level bridge lacks in grandeur it makes up for in usefulness: built in 1842, it’s more local thoroughfare than tourist attraction.
从泗溪乡南溪的主要到达巴士站出发，步行 400 米即可到达南溪桥 。这座水平桥在宏伟方面所缺乏的不足，但它在实用性上得到了弥补：它建于 1842 年，与其说是旅游景点，不如说是当地的大道。

Next head back 200m to the main village junction and turn right. In 1km you can turn right again and take a 3km detour up to Nanyang Bridge (南阳桥, Nányáng Qiáo), a level wooden cantilever bridge, built in 1870 and situated in the grassy hills above town. Otherwise veer left for Xidong Bridge (溪东桥, Xīdōng Qiáo, Creek East Bridge), in Sixi proper, another 500m away. Easily identified by its dramatic winged roof and deep vermilion staining, this Ming dynasty arched wooden bridge was first built in 1570 and later rebuilt in 1827.
接下来返回 200m 到主要村庄路口并右转。1 公里后，您可以再次右转，绕行 3 公里到达南阳桥 （ 南阳桥 ，Nányáng Qiáo）， 这是一座建于 1870 年的平木悬臂桥，位于城镇上方的草山上。否则左转前往溪东桥 （ 溪东桥 ，西东桥， 溪东桥），在泗西，再走 500 米。这座明代拱形木桥始建于 1570 年，后来于 1827 年重建，以其引人注目的翼形屋顶和深朱红色的染色而很容易辨认。

From here, the village is well signposted in English. Follow signs to gracefully arching, covered Beijian Bridge (北涧桥, Běijiàn Qiáo, North Stream Bridge), the most picturesque of Sixi’s bridges, accessed by stone steps. It was originally built in 1674 and last rebuilt in 1803. Don’t miss the 1000-year-old camphor tree just before it.
从这里，村庄有很好的英语路标。沿着路标前往优雅拱起的有盖北涧桥 （ 北涧桥 ，Běijiàn Qiáo，北溪桥）， 这是泗溪最美丽的桥梁，可通过石阶进入。它最初建于 1674 年，最后一次重建是在 1803 年。不要错过它前面的 1000 年树龄的樟树。

Just 30m west of Beijian Bridge, at the far end of the village, is the Covered Bridge Culture Hall (廊桥文化展厅, Lángqiáo Wénhuà Zhǎntīng; hhours vary) F, which has models and information (in English) on other noteworthy bridges in the area.
北涧桥以西仅 30 米处，村庄的尽头是廊桥文化展厅 （ 廊桥文化展厅 ，Lángqiáo Wénhuà Zhǎntīng; h 时间各不相同）， F 其中包含有关该地区其他著名桥梁的模型和信息（英文）。
